You can say this is an unofficial rule, but it's "Don't be a dick."

Even if it's legal to lie about some RL circumstances, it subsequently makes it harder for people who genuinely have these things happen to them to be believed. "Fool me once, shame on you, fool me twice, shame on me." Lying about a sick or recently deceased relative (or pet) for sympathy is not good gameplay, and I consider it to fall under "being a dick." - So don't do that. It's an ethical line drawn from the health of the community standpoint, and people shouldn't cross that line just to cover for Mushy behavior.

The reasons why are obvious: When people have these things actually happen to them, and they tell the truth, they might get killed just because other players lied for sympathy on previous ships. It unnecessarily adds insult to injury, and if the positions were reversed, most of us wouldn't want to deal with people harshly mocking us on top of the loss of a loved one. It's ultimately bad for the playerbase, because I've seen people quit playing other games over similar incidents.

So, lying about being busy due to work or school is one thing, and most of the playerbase has to deal with one or both of those. There are plenty of other understandable excuses, without having to resort to lying about a pet/relative/etc. Mush CAN lie; hell, they're supposed to lie in order to maintain their cover. It's what they lie about that I'm trying to rein in, here.

I'm not saying that these excuses are a guaranteed way to cover for Mush activity, and they should not be. I'm also not suggesting that an excuse about a pet or relative must be taken as fact, and provides any form of immunity against being killed. That couldn't be further from the truth.

What I am saying is: "Don't be a dick about it, and don't lie for sympathy". You may not get sanctioned for lying about something so horrific, but it IS a dick move, and the effects will definitely ripple out to affect other ships. The only effects on the playerbase as a whole are negative, since they bring out unsporting conduct in other players.

Now, this applies to the human teams: People are not supposed to be slinging personal attacks and abuse at each other, and that IS against the rules. Quit it. Be on your guard, but be willing to show some compassion as well. If you have to ask yourself why, you've completely missed the point of this post.
