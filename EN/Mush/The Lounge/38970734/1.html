Azzyquemint

Roland Zuccali here, Orator of Dumb Jokes and Spearhead of the Grand Mush Operative.

Hats off to the Human team, it's amazing that you guys pulled through after the state we left you in. No Techs, a single Pilot, no genuinely useful magebooks, just over 30 armor, no hope of a Plasma Shield, PILGRED very much still in disrepair... I thought we'd doomed you for sure.

But I digress, let's start from the beginning. I joined the game as Roland because I knew I wouldn't have a lot of real-life time, and his job is both AP-intensive yet straightforward and not time-consuming. I was pleasantly surprised that, not five minutes after I had joined (and before I even had a chance to catch up on all the radio chatter), our last crew member thawed out and I was chosen to serve Mother Mush.

I immediately bee-lined straight for Bravo Dorm, pulling two spores on the way there. And wouldn't you know it, therein lied a gold Hua, the coveted shower, and most importantly no camera. I immediately dumped a spore into her; that's what you folks overlooked, and that's what would ultimately prove to be the lynch-pin in your near-downfall. I had no bed and no AP, but I couldn't have asked for a smoother start in such an active ship.

I was kind of hoping that the camera issue would continue to be overlooked, but alas the camera was installed well before day two rolled around. By the time I recovered from my initial escapades the Antispore and Constipaspore were already finished, severely limiting us. Derek had the ridiculous idea of coordinating an "accidental" push on Hua, which ended up being so insanely ballsy we actually got away with it. He and I put the second and third spores into her right there in Bravo Bay, before the alarm was raised and the MycoAlarm was delivered to her (coincidentally only about 20 minutes before the day 3 changeover, which turned out to actually work in our favor) and Derek took Anonymush so that he could try to continue blending in.

Sometime during day 3 I decided to try out Green Jelly for the first time, knowing it was bugged to be completely private. I really wanted to spore Terrence at least once, so I slimed the camera. I was advised that it would be too suspicious, though, so I let it break and be repaired without acting on it.

By the time day four rolled around I was feeling a bit boxed in and out of options, and Hua and I were ready to act on Terrence and Raluca. But as luck would have it Derek tuned in only ten minutes before I would have started without him. With all three of us working together we just dumped one spore each into Terrence, converting him almost instantly (he actually managed to clear the dorm in the same instance we converted him, almost ruining everything). Hua tried to clear the logs, but it turns out Defacer is kind of a crap skill and doesn't immediately erase anything.

From there it was like living in a dream. We knew our next target was Raluca, and that it was now or never. I Sprinted up to the lab, intent on stealing the Blaster that was being researched. You would not believe the manic grin I had on my face when I arrived mere seconds after the Natamy had been completed (I had no idea you were even close to finishing it, seriously, the timing was brilliantly surreal); I grabbed that right out from under you, and then for good measure I also stole the armor which Chun had presumably dropped to in favor of the apron. Then I ran to the Nexus, emptied the Natamy into Raluca, then Derek and I beat her to death. I slimed the NERON Terminal without thinking just because I could, then went to a rear storage, where I completely ran out of action and movement points. For me, everything had worked out near-flawlessly, and I was genuinely happy for a job-well-done. I believe Hua came behind me, slimed the BIOS and Doorman'd the door. By the time I came back from a real-life shower about fifteen minutes later, the initial slaughter was already over.

Excellent ship all-around, very tight-knit crew on both sides with most everyone playing their parts to a 'T'. Congratulations to the Human team for such a gritty success; I genuinely thought we were guaranteed a postmortem victory.

10/10 would Mush again.
