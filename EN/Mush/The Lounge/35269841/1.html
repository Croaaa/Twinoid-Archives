What a trip!

We just finished, after 12 sometimes excruciating days, mostly due to crew going mad under the pressure.

I was Ian, on my first ship started in Gold. And at the beginning, a couple of things went rather well: We got extra hydropots soon, found tha plasma shield not long after, and I was looking forward to a long game with a glorious garden of 10 plants actually making us gain oxygen.


But oh, the crew!
There were a few pleasant surprises, like a really competent Chao, who successfully questioned and killed the mushy Eleesha on day 2. Others, however, seemed to be moving a bit but then did nothing useful. And most of all, appeared to have left the cryosleep mute and deaf.

Gio was truly inactive, so we wasted no energy on trying to communicate with him. But them... Paola kinda hung around the refectory for 2 days, eating, fixing the microwave (!), and doing everything but her job. Terrence the not-technician random walked to the Nexus and did his best to finish the Sink before the (already started) Hydropots. Raluca stood in a bay looking at the ships for a couple of days, then proceeded to the engine to marvel at that, but not repair it once. Jin Su gave a talk to 2 people, then looked out of the front window... and no amount of private messages or shouting their names in general chat would reach them.

(EDIT: Forgot about Chun, who was the best at this. Details below.)


After a long while, most of those were kinda sorted. Just when we were about to blast her, Paola's job was eventually passed on to Stephen, who was ready and willing to gung-ho the Comms terminal just in time for the D3 base. Chao "visited" the others in turn, or they eventually starved, and inactive Jin Su turned out to be our second Mush. We also got lucky with an early O2 planet, which should be all we ever need thanks to the plants (no alien ones though ), and our pilots Hua and Frieda raked in lots of scrap metal.


But then, around day 6, Raluca finally died as well, after some more zombielike shambling... and we realized that we had a problem: With no physicist technician, there was nobody left to repair Pilgred! Except for us untrained scrubs, repairing it 1% at a time!

Faced with such an ordeal, we set up the most awesome plan to achieve a most mediocre ending: We calculated how long this would take us, and found that it could work, but we would have to ration our AP and cut any unneeded activity, with everyone helping, for a chance of all of us getting home alive!

So we moved everything and everyone to the back of the ship, piling the plants and other gear in rear corridor, and prepared to get to it.
...which would have worked better if people did things like use the empty beds, or use the thread which we put up for organizing relays rather than walking to the engine and back for every 1%. But you know, that would mean reading...
So basically half the crew was happily wasting AP in spite of the situation, while Janice and I watched everyone's chances dwindle (in a constant blaze of fires everywhere), in spite of our best efforts.
Only Frieda, who had been active and helpful before, did not get out of bed anymore one day. And starved there.

After that blow, it was clear that we really had to get our shit together now, if we wanted to be done before being buried under piles of falling metal! It was day 10 or such, and people were already hurting, with Pilgred at 60%.

- Finola, who had been reluctant to abandon her glory-only research before, reacted promptly, by standing right in our main hub in RC and trying the Infernal Kube a dozen times. Yes.
After a short discussion, Chao shot her. Which cost us morale ingame, but arguably raised it (back from the depths this caused) a bit IRL.


But now we were really fighting an uphill battle. With tempers going up, and crewmembers still seeming to be doing everything, in troll-like proportions, to make it more difficult (like Kuan Ti who was helping but at the same time wasting others' points by not sharing what he did or knew, people generally not reading, acting before talking, repairing unimportant equipment next to a drone, or doing stuff like not using the beds because other rooms allegedly had "less falling metal"...), it was a tough time.

Long story short - we made it. Baaarely.
On day 12, two cycles after the time we had planned (for an utopian case in which everyone would use beds and show up), with both O2 tanks leaking and metal raining from the sky, Janice, Chao and I fixed the damn thing in the back of a broken, smoking ship that had just been hit by 2 asteroids, and got the few survivors home. - Except for Stephen, who was plated one cycle before. RIP.


But man... while I am sure that this would definitely not have ended so well without us sometimes shouting at people, for now I am just happy it is over.


Next time, I am going to pick a peaceful role, with little responsibility, like gardening.

Oh, wait...
