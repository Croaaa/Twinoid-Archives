SO.

This's already being told from others' perspectives. I'm going to focus on Derek.

I'll admit, I had a bit of a rough start. I read Derek's motive and was completely not thrilled. A matchmaker who is fine with being alone? Uhh... okay. Hmm.... I acknowledged that in the right hands that could be hilarious, but not so much mine. So I thought about it some and came to the following conclusion - Derek tells himself he's okay with being alone because he is asexual, and no one has ever been happy being in a relationship with him because of this. So he works really hard to make everyone else happy and keeps telling himself he is okay. This made for a nice cheerful bundle of hidden angst, and something I could work with, heh. It seemed natural that this desire to help those around him be happy should make him very much a white knight. And then I built the rest of his personality in a way that begged to be engaged, and to engage everything - a soldier who is done with war, and wishes for peace, who takes everyone at face value and assumes the best of everyone until proven otherwise - a stubbornly happy idiot with a habit of declaring himself to be vigilant unless kittens. Because kittens. But no need to worry, because no kittens aboard the vessel!

I tried hard to do the matchmaker angle but it's not one that comes naturally to me, and I seemed to pick the wrong people to engage on it. I wrote up a really dopey romance personality quiz that only Hua ever responded to despite directly sending it to a handful of people. So I pretty much gave up on that route, since no one was biting and it didn't fit well with me anyways.

I thought for sure Derek's good nature and eager boy scout attitude would get him taken advantage of, but Stephen was the only one who ever did, using Derek as a human lab rat. Because really, someone needed to test the acid.

Oh, wait, I'm getting ahead of myself.

I had some absolutely brilliant interactions with Janice early on. I adored the way her thoughts contradicted with what she said. I had begun wondering whether or not NERON and Janice had swapped personalities, but Derek just thought that she was a very nice lady. I was very sad when you had to quit, Cela, and not just because you and Chun were the only ones I'd managed to solidly engage with at that point.

You may have caught the bit in there were Derek declares Jin Su to not be a rapist. Yeah... that happened. Chun started the ship afraid and vulnerable, so Derek decided he would befriend her and calm her. She mentioned that Jin Su had been giving her drugs that made her forget and... well... he was very good at leaping to conclusions. After many misunderstandings and Chun attempting to stab Jin Su, things settled down. Derek was determined to never doubt Jin Su ever again and redoubled his devotion. Jin Su charged Derek with protecting Chun. Meanwhile, Chun became increasingly infatuated, but Derek kept failing to notice, until she got so obvious that even Derek couldn't fail to realize. But he was convinced that she only wanted him physically, and he wasn't interested in that. So... he ran away!

A bit before this, Derek had gotten drunk and stumbled upon room logs to suggest that Kuan Ti, who had previously pledged friendship to Derek, was probably a mush. Not one to jump to conclusions Always one to jump to conclusions, regardless of what he kept saying, Derek's response was to tell Kuan Ti he knew. And in so doing, he met Lucy the friendly mush and spilled his guts to her. Turns out, mush consciousness is separate from host consciousness. Who knew? After speaking with Kuan Ti, Derek embraced Lucy's offer of friendship and asked her ALL the questions. She accepted him in his weirdness and he took her at face value and they became close. She even encouraged him to try things with Chun - after all, he'd never know if Chun really wanted to get to know him if he kept running away from her.

So after avoiding Chun for a day or so, Derek decided to broach the subject of a relationship with her... but she needed to know that he wasn't interested in sex. He obsessed about how to tell her and in the end, blurted it out terribly, and publicly. Chun rejected him and began drinking heavily.

Meanwhile, Derek cried on Lucy's shoulder for a bit and then realized - Lucy accepted him. Well and truly. And cared about him. And she amazed him. She was kind and patient and wise, and reminded him of being a boy staring up at the stars in the night sky.... only the stars were talking back. So he told her, and what followed, well, Artie first described it as Unicorn Vomit. Seems about right. Suffice it to say, it was a love match. Quite possibly my favorite RP on mush to date, and I enjoyed it even more for the fact that it didn't get physical beyond cuddles and hugs, but was very deep. Eventually, Lucy became sad that Derek would die and be gone, while she would live on in the mush consciousness without him. So, of course, being the white knight that he is and very much in love, Derek got mushed.

Except, well, the mush companion he got wasn't quite what he bargained for....
