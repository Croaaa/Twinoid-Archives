SummerMcLovin

Finally, on my 18th ship, I and the other remaining nine crew-members make it to Eden

I wasn't intending to go as Chun until my Gold days had ran out, since there isn't much point in having more than her first two skills. However, she came up on my first full round of choices for the MMM so I took the plunge early; didn't regret it for a second. Of course, many of you will have seen this ship discussed quite a bit in the MMM thread already, but here's some more detail...

As mentioned, this was an MMM ship, and fortunately D1C1 rather than D2. I think it filled up only a cycle later, with Gioele and Raluca chosen as Alpha Mush; the latter immediately extracting a spore which ended up being the only one of the entire game. Derek and Eleesha were inactive after their first cycle and Hua only logged in twice the entire ship. My main worry at the start was having no Technician, but fortunately Raluca was Level 3 Gold and Kuan Ti took it later in the day. Other than that, we had a good mix of skills to cover all tasks. The only Mage Book to be used was our initial Botanist one for me, allowing Ian to take Green Thumb instead.

Research was very speedy with a Polyvalent Andie and Biologist Ian, Raluca quickly working out that she would have no opportunity to do anything with the MycoAlarm placement and extra Cameras. The only topics we didn't manage were Natamy Rifle and Mushicide Soap (after a fire) - that would have gotten me to top Glory position!
Projects also went without a hitch; Plasma Shield came up in the first round and we always had something useful as an option.

My jobs were standard Chun fare, relaying Research, taking care of the plants - this was no small task with a full ten-pot Garden for a few days - and leading the Mush investigation without an Eleesha. The Solid 'bug' helped out initially, giving me my first human in Stephen and bringing suspicion on Gioele (as well as keeping him pacified after lugging the Microwave all the way to Icarus Bay). Despite Kuan Ti's grand claims of single-handedly doing lots of things, the actual human chat had already formed that opinion and was about to make a move on Gioele. Before we could do this though, he went to the Bridge, the restricted Front Turret that Hua was idling in, then promptly killed himself in the shower. We were worried he had Jellied some Terminals, spiked Hua or done... anything, but it turns out all he'd done is build a Grenade. Maybe that caused the accident that killed him before Transfer, but either way it wasn't very effective.
In the next few days there were many people double-eating for morale, Comms/Astro AP, by accident, or in Terrence's case just for the hell of it I guess. Once Derek starved, the only non-confirmed humans were Andie, Kuan Ti and Raluca. We decided to eventually use the Cure on Raluca, and if we were wrong we'd kill Andie. However, Kuan Ti made some further mistakes that have already been discussed elsewhere, leading to his death, and within a few cycles we had cured our second Mush. The crew had a good laugh about his later PMs to me.
My later conversation with Raluca revealed that we had nothing much to worry about from them given our active human team; the only real enemy was Oxygen. Admittedly Gioele's Grenade did consume one Oxygen which would have put us at 0 just before the plants would have given us long enough. Fortunately a Space Capsule gave us that extra window, although we got 8 from our first safe Oxygen planet in about fifteen and the plants gave us a daily surplus from then on.

With the Mush hunt out of the way, we geared up to search for our remaining two Starmap Fragments (the first was from an earlier planet, I think the one that claimed Roland). I gave Andie two MTPasses for them to go up to Level 5, and Stephen had Apprenticed Pilot earlier to make up for Roland and Hua not being able to support Jin Su. We had an Astrophysicist book for Paola, a Babel Module blueprint from Xyloph and were ready for the long haul. This took us one expedition: Intelligent Life and Shipwreck yielded a Fragment each, we didn't even need to go to the Cristalite zone.

We were well equipped to push on to Day 15 or perhaps even 20, but given we had almost nothing to do in the middle of Day 8, we just earned our Casting Tickets and went home before anyone died of steel plates or boredom. Hopefully we get to hold onto that record for a while, but it was an enjoyable expedition nonetheless. Thanks to all of the surviving ten, hope to hear from you here if I've left anything to say!
