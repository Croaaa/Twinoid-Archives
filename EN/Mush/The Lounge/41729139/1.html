All in all a pretty average ship. The only thing that nearly doomed us was the frequency of the hunter waves, though the pilots did all they could we often had about a dozen Hunters on us for several cycles.

But! Luck was on our side for the entire journey. Just to name a few:
-Someone enters the nexus just before Mush Kuan booby-traps the console.He gets killed.
-Our first project completed is Armored Corridors. Without it, we'd have been blown up in a couple days.
-Among the next projects there's PLASMA SHIELD! Heck yeah.
-Paola is pretty active and decodes most rebel bases, even the less useful ones. But hey, not complaining.
-Jin Su is inactive, but comes online perhaps once a day so he keeps the title. Chun has to kill him, he doesn't take it like a sport and threatens to report her. Oh well.
-Unfortunately the high level of activity of the crew plays a heavy toll on our rations, which run out on day 4. Fortunately, we have Ian as one of the most active humans, and Heat Lamps in the garden. We have enough bananas to stave off starvation indefinitely.
-First expedition for oxygen. It's just me(Andie) and Janice. Janice gets lost, I instantly find her again. Also, we get two days' worth of O2.
-Stephen is the second Mush, and gives himself away when he springs Kuan's trap but still claims to find zero spores when checking the MycoScan. He doesn't realize his mistake until we jab him with the Serum. Welcome back to the Human Team.
-We are down to 4 hull, but Roland and Raluca throw scrap metal around and manage to repair it and hold out long enough for Janice to fire up the shield.
-We do another expedition. Get some steaks, some bruises, and Ian bursts into joy as we bring back a Jumpkin among other fruits.

...and then our luck abandoned us. We didn't get any oxygen from that planet, and we were down to 3 units with the reservoirs threatening to break any minute. Ian is very disappointed, but there's nothing we can do. Gioele grabs the commands and blasts us back to Sh*t-Outta-Luck (SOL).

A really good ship where the extremes of both good and bad luck happened. The RNG was very stingy with oxygen, and that's what really made us give up.
