Hi guys, Ian here from our long voyage! It was 18 days, 7 cycles I believe!

We started out the ship half a day or one day after MMM, and I am glad to say this ship has been full so many active players and Gold players! I really enjoyed myself!

Here is a brief summary of events:

After we all got out of Cryo and Mush were selected, our great Chun organised a quick Mush hunting team to delete our first Mush, Jin Su (It's always the commander!) We found him out when the Mush Sample was missing for nearly 3 days, and when Chun found it searching randomly, the only people who could have been near it were Jin Su and a few others, including me. Before Jin Su got assassinated though, we had a Griefer Derek, who killed out Stephen When I saw that, I rushed to the medlab immediately to protect our dear Chun who was together with Mush Jin Su. Glad to say that got me some brownie points (And a little 'reward' after, heh )

Soon after, me and Andie headed the research and we did it really quickly, churning out Antispore Gas and Constipation Serum, among a whole lot of other Researches quickly by alternating bumps with each other. We were on our mission to eliminate or find the last Mush, and we had pinpointed Eleesha, who was behaving so mushily.. having some morality in me, I decided to tell Chun of my intent to rush THE CURE and I got Andie to do it with me within one cycle with his stocked up missions and me drinking coffee from a Thermos. Chun sticked Eleesha with it and.. lo and behold! She was human! (At least we saved one human soul )

We quickly deduced that the last Mush had given up when his partner died so fast, and that it was either our dormant Kuan Ti, or our Giole whom kept hovering between inactivity and jeopardising our ship with taking commander title and not doing anything. We decided to kill Kuan, who was the Mush, and later Giole so that we could give our Frieda commander. She pretty much held it for the rest of the game.

With some great projects from Paola, help from everyone, and me and Andie churning out the research, we got everything maxed out by day 12. While it now seemed more like a full co-op game now with everyone being human, we now faced a greater enemy: The dreaded RNG. We couldn't get GPU due to taking plasma shields over it and the ship pretty much destroyed much of our will and resolve, and consumed lots of our AP too. The fires ravaged my plants and my 8 pots slowly diminished to 3. My bad luck with Jumpkins always got the pots destroyed everytime I planted one

Now, our crew was facing our final enemy: Inactivity. With real life taking its toll on us, most of us became inactive and got hit by fires, metal sheets, and bouts of depression. This killed off our Raluca, who decided sleeping in the dorms for a long while without reporting morale/health was a great idea, and Eleesha, who drifted in and out of inactivity. Our last casualty was Paola, who suddenly became inactive and at 2 health, tried to get to the medkit but just one step before taking it up, hurt herself and managed to put the 'fun' in her funeral.

At day 15, we decided that maybe we were taking too many casualties and should head back to SOL for an easy victory. However, many of us, back from Real Life, persisted, and decided to clamber ahead. Ahead, into the dark, dense space filled with aliens, hunters, random wildfires, and room collapses..

At day 18, we finally had a last stand. Most of us were heavily damaged, and Frieda just found a planet with the last Cristalite zone that we needed for the starmap fragment - but it was laden with crazy health depleting zones too. Our heroic entourage of Janice, Andie, Frieda and of course myself went into the planet and walked for what it seemed like forever, until we found.. THE LAST STARMAP FRAGMENT! When we got back, all the gears turned into motion. Chun, our great leader, whom was running around the past two weeks dilligently healing everyone, commanded all of us to put the starmaps into the nexus, and finish any research that may be needed to be done. I was honoured to be able to be the one to decode the fragments for Eden!

So in closing, it was a great ship. I enjoyed every second of it, through the good and the bad. I do hope each and every one of us do play again with each other, it is a great crew!
