We had Neron quarantine Chun and Hua both and Frieda first picking pilot over Astro, then we killed her for being mush. Mute Derek died of morale loss after accomplishing little, but only Eleesha died from idleness in the lab. We lost KT half way to a late onset of cryosickness (or vacation as some people call it).

As Ian, I asked Raluca to come and help pushing research and against all odds we found support in each other against the many obstacles. Stephen and Raluca had physics, not chemistry to bind them.

Stephen was soon added to my circle of friends in the laboratory but I was plagued by nightmares and as we completed the Natamy rifle together, and we had had time to go on one failed expedition, we believed the most likely and dangerous mush was Lady Janice. There were other suspects, but no one else seemed to have opportunity to turn the ship against humanity. Armed with the Natamy Stephen was sent to the Icarus Bay, moments before take-off and tried to shoot her. He was armed, but we did not have the knowledge to carry it out correctly. The expedition was cancelled, and the experiment failed. Stephen picked up shrink skill before the attack.

Meanwhile we had growing suspicions about Pilot Bergman. It was a highly unusual skill choice for her, at the same time she brought out snippets of commentary that showed a greater understanding of the journey. While I was engaged elsewhere with my seed catalogs, Raluca and Stephen carried out the second Natamy testing with great success. We were ready for the second phase in research.

The paranoia and mush hunt went on until day 7 (?) when we cured our lvl 6 JinSu from the mush fate with no beta mush onboard. Our decision was not purely based on suspicion, we also realized we needed him for the journey, as our Mr Rinaldo was active, but only intermittently 2-3 cycles per day, with limited language abilities. We were right about our Jin, except on one point; Jinnie, she turned out to be a reliable captain.

Lady Janice and Pilot Graham were constant companions on expeditions, often available just at the right time. We never needed to carry a weapon onto a planet in all the time we went out. We built up a nice selection of bumpjunkins as a reliable AP-source. But we were constantly unlucky with Fuel (HC=6) and rations. We had no luck at crystalites either, although I found one so mesmerizing that I decided to stay for a while. I brought a small shard of it back to Raluca.

Natives gave us two star map pieces but as the FOUR techs (from two tech books) were tending to the ship our Astrophysical scans were unproductive. Our main scanner Dr KT was lost to us and died. Lady J and Rinaldo were working hard, but - especially Rinaldo, with tech - were distracted elsewhere. The ship was starting to fall apart.

When most of Lady J's AP had to go towards healing and Rinaldo had no-crystalite and volcano planets we knew our journey was running towards the end. Pilots were struggling to keep up and our bountiful store of metals were dwindling.

On day 24 we decided to go home. The ship could have lasted a little longer, even with 33 hunters appearing outside. We had fuel for one more planet but no AP for scans and we wanted to bring the surviving crew with us rather than losing one or two members to accidental death.

If I remember correctly: We had Stephen, KT, Roland and Andie choose gold during the journey. Gioele was gifted gold for coffee, and we had hoped Astro. Janice and I had gold from day one. Bronze Paola with Radio was made more effective with a tech book, and so was Bronze Gioele with Paranoid (before gifting). Our third book was Radio and we had mainly weapon blueprints (with two diplomats, not too useful). The sofa was on the bridge, and oh so many times I wished for it in the refectory.
