Welcome fellow bards, you may now know why you are here, but I do. You all have heard of the Tale of the Two Strangers yes? Perhaps you have not? Even if you have not, you surely have heard of rumors surrounding them.

Perhaps they were a brother and a sister from another kingdom engaged in a forbidden love? Or were they just two friends from the farmlands of our own wonderful kingdom, who decided to go find adventure and glory for their own reasons? Perhaps one of them was from one of the three moons we have, and the other from the underworld?

Whatever their reasons and previous lives, we are sure that one of them was a male, and the other a female. What we cannot tell however, is if they were lovers, or siblings. That is where you come in, as you surely have heard of their tale, a tale that has been told 1000 times over, So many times it has varied between person and person, a tale twisted into 10000 tales as confusing as the original. Thus, we come here today to piece the truth of the Two Strangers.

May you Bards contribute what you have heard about the Two Strangers.

Here are some rules before we proceed any further [DP

1) At a decision point, we will sprout what we have heard about the two strangers.

2) Each bard can contribute up to three times at each decision point

3) Each contribution must have one of the wonderful strange magical artifacts called d100 rolled.

4) The highest roll will win and that contribution will become part of the story! However, do not leave if your idea does not make it through, if your ideas are the second or even third highest, then there is a chance I will combine it with the First in a way.

5) The writer (me) will be the deciding vote in a draw, unless both ideas are really good, in which case, I might just use them both.

6) Keep it safe. It would do our story no good if you cursed at every second word, and there was rape everywhere. This does not mean you cannot use curse words however.

7) I will update every weekend, unless troubles come up, such as my ink running out (how can a Bard write with no Ink? I do not use magic!), unless your dice roll is over 95, then I'll just write it right there to prevent the story from fading into the sands of time.

And now certain things involving this world you may not know.
MagicMagic works in two different ways here. The two magics are known as Magic of the Land, or Mana, and Magic of the Person, or Psi. Mana is something that varies among person to person at first, but just about everyone, and every race has the ability to wield it. The Mana in a person will grow, and as such, along with their power to wield it. Psi is something that is unique among a select few and the Psi in a person does not grow stronger, it is only used in a new way.

Location This world's name is Arrathor, we are currently on the continent of Komens, in the Kingdom of Lowdregon, and inside my house.

Other racesYes, elves, orcs, and such do exist there. So do beasts from one of the three moons, bristling with life. The other two moons do not appear to have life however.

ReligionThere are several religions worshiping several different gods. The gods have fallen several times, often with their slayer becoming them over time. Only one god managed to slay the gods and escape Fate's Hand, he was known as the God-King Vant Axwov.

CHARACTERS FOR THOSE WHO CAN'T BOTHER TO READ THE STORY.

Cersei: Armok's sister (and stalker/lover) who takes pride in manipulating men and causing unnecessary pain and suffering.
