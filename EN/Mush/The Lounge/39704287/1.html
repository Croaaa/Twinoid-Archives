This ship had some great potential and serious active players, we just couldn't handle post Day 10 shenanigans...

THE SHORT VERSION: Both Mushes mutated really early and were killed, had some good actives hat kept the ship afloat and we got 2 Map Fragments. Too many inactives and lack of research power meant post Day 10 was just too much for our small crew and we had to go back to Sol.

The Alpha Mush
Stephen, Raluca - Both Mutated

Actives:
Frieda, Eleesha, Jin Su, Roland, Hua, Ian, Kuan Ti, Paola (well till the end)

Did some work:
Janice (active early but then inactived later)

Tha Bad (inactives, griefers, idiots):
Derek, Andie, Gioele, Terrence, Chun (she was also a newb/griefer/idiot)

I was Frieda, played pretty actively as usual. What surprised me was the rare active Jin Su and Eleesha. Both of them were really active players that helped make the ship really well.

As it so happens I guess our Mush wanted a quick game so Stephen Mutated in the lab and dropped a Grenade on everyone there (not in that order) but forgot Ian was in the lab and blew all his AP. Jin Su and Eleesha showed up and decided they couldn't wait for Ian to move so they just knifed him down with some good crits.

Then as we are settling Raluca Mush also Mutates and attacks people but also doesn't kill anyone. She dies in short order as well. They spiked the cat but otherwise we felt in the clear. Eleesha tried to print the List and got nothing so we were Mush free!

This was pretty exciting as we had some good actives and I had some serious hope. Unfortunately humanity started getting in the way immediately. First off our Chun was a first time player, didn't speak the language, didn't care, or some combination of the above. She didn't say anything, wandered around, but somehow had played enough to NOT pick Humanity's Hope but Nurse! She didn't stay in the lab and punched random people too. Eventually she had to be put down. So we were losing 2 morale a day.

Andie died in a similar fashion. She picked up the Apron and sat on it for several cycles until Jin Su got fed up and knifed her down. I was concerned about this but ultimately JS turned out to be one of our MVPs.

Most of the other people died of starvation or depression.

After we got the Mush down we focused most of our efforts on NERON and PILGRED with good performance, and Ian got occasional relays on research. He did have a lot of work to do as we got Heat Lamps and Extra Hydropots (which ultimately saved us time and again. SO MANY FRUITS!) Eleesha picked up the extra Pilot Book, and Paola took the Radio Expert book (she had Logistics to start).

We missed several bases (including the ration one) but that wasn't Paola's fault, we had to do some running and forgot about bases here and there.

We also managed to nail a ton of GREAT NERON projects. Plasma Shield, General Patching Up, Armored Corridors, Patrol Blasters, Automatic Sprinklers to say a few (including the previously mentioned Gardening ones). We had one close call with O2 in the beginning when we got down to 7 but after that we were totally solid (found O2 planets, tons of O2 from plants)

We got really lucky finding a Map Fragment early with Cristalite, and later we found one as an artifact drop in either a Cavern or Intelligent Life.

But you know how things go. Pressure was steadily mounting. We had some communication issues with Hua which caused a bit of AP loss or cycle miscoordination. We had to spend a lot of AP on speeches till the plants got up and running, and we had to spend tons of AP on PILGRED. Our research was always way behind (never got Healing Pomade or Meridon, OR the serum ) and people were slowly getting killed by the ship.

Despite getting quite a few steaks and fruits (never a Jumpkin though in about 12 Fruit drops) people were still getting pretty low.

Then Day 10 hit (well D10C4) and the Hunter swarms and fires started going up. We fought it off for a while while trying to find another artifact planet and we finally did but the ship was in terrible shape. Many things were broken, many people were at sub 9 health. We finally decided to chance this last planet and if we got a Fragment we might try to push the Calculator through in a last gasp effort. The planet had 2 Intelligent Life and we had the Babel Module (but no Diplomat). Unfortunately all we got was an Alien TV.

So here we are back in Sol with quite a few dead and no serum, but at least we are alive.

Many thanks to Eleesha and Jin Su for being super active and quite the team! Roland, Paola, Ian, Hua and KT also get big thanks for being pretty active as well. I think we would've made it if Janice hadn't quit halfway through the game, or if we even had one more active. OR a Biologist book!

I'll see you guys in the next game!
