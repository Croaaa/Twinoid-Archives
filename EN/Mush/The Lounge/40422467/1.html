Stephen (briefly Jin Su) reporting from the angriest Daedalus to ever successfully return to Sol.

Things started off promising with my level 5 Jin Su, a level 4 Andie, a level 4 Ian, and if I recall correctly a rather high level KT (he became inactive early and soon burned to death).

However the issues we had quickly became clear. Ian wanted to use the scooter to find the pots and get his garden in order, but Andie refused to drop it because she wanted to wait to search for the mush sample hunt. Refusing to acknowledge that we had 3 folks on board and the mush sample wouldn't appear for many many many hours, she stubbornly held onto it. Not to mention how she held the knife from ship start until day 9 or so. That was the main issue with the ship, Andie did what she wanted and she was always right.

Back on the game front, Stephen became a prime mush suspect because he had one skill, opportunist, and kept talking to us about his imminent apprenticeship skill. But why did you take the least helpful skill, Stephen? Oh, so you've uninstalled all the cameras and transferred into me. Ok, that explains it.

A third mush popped up around the time of the transfer so paranoia was on. Andie stabbed people. We had to have her do it because the knife was part of her hand. She killed Gioele for some reason and he was Mush. So that was luck.

A few days later, with only a few unconfirmed humans remaining, the suspicion fell on Terrence for being the most likely conversion target. However I passed through the CBT one day and noticed that Derek had gone in and out of the room many time to drain the scooter. Derek was promptly killed and we were mush free (later we used the serum on Roland to be certain)

All through these days the main chat was filled with full blown fights between crew members. Generally people yelling at Andie for being difficult. Then Terrence lost his mind briefly and got really really really angry that we'd suspected him, but that must have been some sort of space disease because he soon regained his head.

I've never experience such an angry ship. Even the final moments were filled with tension as Andie insisted she had used her mission to research and I pointed out that I got pop-ups when missions were accepted. Suddenly I was a bad guy for questioning her honesty and was told I was useless. All in all, I think I did a fine job adjusting to my new body and keeping the ship from falling apart. It's especially difficult to care about a ship when its all tension and rage, but we managed to hang together and make day 11!

Some things that helped us survive amid the rage:

-Janice going inactive on the bridge. The combo of heals as she starved and the sofa gave folks a much needed place for morale during the mush hunt when sleeping together wasn't a safe option.

-Personally, the fact that I was forced into a body with maxed morale saved me the trouble of ever being in danger suicide.

-The fact that Chun was played by an apparent professional diplomat (she told me she actually worked in customer service) who was able to defuse so much of the tension. She calmed Andie a number of times, calmed the ship towards Andie, and most importantly, she single handedly talked Terrence off the edge when he was contemplating space suicide!

This ship was an experience...
