So there I was, blissfully solitary Raluca, toiling away in the engine room while the ship goes on around me. I never even had to ask for help with resetting the Pilgred: Gioele was always ready to come down and help out with it. As per Chao's direction, I was relaxing in the turret, so never actually had to attempt to be social. He popped down twice this way, and while Chao had warned against people doing repeat trips, I was never there, so surely I was safe.

Shortly after, Plasma Shield popped up on NERON, and considering I was clearly the ship Genius, I ran up to give it an instant completion. That's when life as I knew it changed entirely.

Suddenly the only two important voices in the universe were Frieda and Gioele, while the other thirteen people on the ship seemed like so much distant space chatter now. I had been working my way towards Designer designation, but now I had new priorities. Becoming a stronger mush, I immediately learned anonymush to hide among the crew and chose some other skills that could possibly hold future potential in the right opportunity.

It wasn't long after this that Chao cornered and tortured poor Gioele, before slaughtering him. Chao contacted me to get checked on the mycoscan, so I did a wander up and reported back only one spore. Gioele must have just been using the Pilgred as an excuse for being dirty, and not actually trying to convert me.

By this time, we were running low on oxygen, and Frieda had found one small planet found with oxygen. Janice and Chao hopped in the shuttle, and dear Frieda jumped into action. She stole the astrophysicist book and hid it, raced down to put one fuel in the engine room, ran back up towards the bridge, sabotaging both oxygen tanks on the way, stole the hacker kit from the lab, and we took off. I'm sure Janice and Chao are happy together on the planet we left them on. Obviously, Frieda was not long for this life after that.

At this point, a clear depression seemed to take over. Most everyone else stopped talking or even responding to my pleas to help with the Pilgred so we could try and make a last-ditch return to Sol before lack of oxygen killed everyone. Ian was kind enough to come down and do a reset for me, but he was to be the last one. As the RNG and morale losses seemed to take out the most active players first, while either less active or just quiet-and-waiting crew members stayed alive, Kuan Ti did come down to the Engine Room, but he only accessed the Pilgred without actually resetting it. Realizing I had to attempt it alone and hope for the best, I pushed as much into Pilgred as I could. And here the RNG attacked me to give me the lowest possible work results - I hit 98% when I could no longer put any more to it alone.

As the rest of the crew died around me, I made my way to the lab to pick up Schrodinger. We went by the Refectory where I grabbed the Mad Kube. While I let Schrodinger down to play on the floor of the Bridge, I sat in the captain's chair and played with the Kube until NERON ejected me from the ship. I like to think that somewhere out there, Schrodinger is still on the bridge, puzzling over the strange cube and exploring the powerful ship he has been left alone to run.
