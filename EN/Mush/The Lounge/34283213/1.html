I hope with all my being that he is right, but I recognize the ring of empty reassuring words people need to hear. I should know, I’ve been saying them quite a lot lately. If he’s not right though, I at least have faith that I can indeed count on him.

Day 5 Cycle 4:

We wandered the planet, finding wonderful things… 9 alien steaks, several alien fruits including one Hua has called a jumpkin and claims to have wonderful properties. No one took any damage. It was perfect – until we reached the oxygen pump right before heading back to the Icarus. It was jammed. Now it’s back to space, time to leave orbit and continue searching frantically.

Day 6 Cycle 1:

It’s hard to focus, hard to think. Dizzy. We had our first two deaths due to oxygen deprivation. The first to collapse was Gioele. I used those empty words people need to hear, and exhorting anyone who would listen. “We must succeed, to make his death mean something!” I said many things like that, but I don’t believe them anymore. We are out of food, and hunters are piling up outside since our pilots have been scanning planets instead of shooting at them – after all, suffocation will kill us first.

I vent my rage at the fates to NERON, who has been imploring us all to learn to stop needing oxygen so that he’s not left bored with nothing to do.

Day 6 Cycle 3

There is nothing I can do but wait to die. The Hunters outside will surely seize the ship once we are dead… if only there was some way I could get our research to the other rebels, perhaps they could finish the job. But I can’t. It’s reaching the end of the cycle and I feel terribly faint. Everything is far away, through a haze, muffled as though I am underwater. I manage to collapse onto my cot in bravo dorm as spots swim before my eyes, clouding my vision. Somehow they’re both made of darkness and terribly bright all at once. The last thing I hear is my little girl’s voice, laughter, like before. I smile as I exhale my last.
