I enjoy story in games, and this game has a lot of potential for it. I'm asking for opinions on how it ends storywise. I am taking Finola/Chao version of events. Mush die at some point, are not any characters mentioned after this. It begins with Frieda scanning for planets, trying to find signs of Crystalites to be able to calculate a path to New Eden. She finds stuff, so on and so forth. Hua leads all these expeditions, being the most qualified, helping the rest of the team through it. Janice comes along as a diplomat, and finds the starmap fragments. Finola is brought them to study, and develops the supercalculator to use the fragments to calculate the coordinates. Eventually all the fragments are found. First Frieda tries to find the location, due to her experience with astrophysics, being one of the most qualified. Failing, Finola then tries, due to her immense knowledge base. She fails as well. Raluca tries it due to her being a genius, but even she fails. Eventually even Janice tries it, attempting to get NERON to help with the calculations. When it has been all but given up, Stephen steps into the room. He's been doing general things, never quite having something specific to work on. Unnoticed by the others, he begins the complex process of decoding the fragments. At first he tries the way he saw the others try it, straightforward and logical. That fails and he's about to give up. As he moves, the room shudders and he is hit by a motherboard. Muttering to himself, he is then hit by the stroke of genius. He rushes back, and begins trying new ways of fitting all the data together. After a few tries, it all comes together. He begins walking slowly into the bridge with the data. He approaches Jin Su, and after a short conversation in which he convinces Jin Su he is right, the commander heads to the terminal and inputs the coordinates. The ship begins to shudder as PILGRED starts up, and then they were off to Eden.
So what are your opinions on this? I figured this would be a decent way for it to end storywise, with Stephen never really having any purpose on the ship, always looked at strangely being a complete outsider, he's the one that brings them to EDEN. Plus, the message when you calculate the location says you have to think outside the box, which is generally used to describe creative thinking, Stephen has the skill creative, and is most likely that sort of individual, so I believe he would be most likely to find New Eden. Roland has the skill as well, so it could fit him, but Roland has a purpose and I felt this just better fit Stephen.
