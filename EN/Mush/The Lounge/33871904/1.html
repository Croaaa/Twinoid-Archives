What a round that was. We started off pretty well, but between a prematurely slain, innocent Captain (Rest in peace, Jin Su, we miss you! ), both Technicians turned Mush and killed, a sporadically active replacement captain and plenty of inactivity, there was not much hope for our rag-tag party to make it to Sol or Eden... although we did find a fragment of the starmap! Some emotional moments were had, and friendships as well as rivalries (Looking at you, Paola) blossomed during our 8-day-long voyage through the stars, until it all eventually came crumbling down on *Day 8 - Cycle 4*.

I want to thank all the active players for sharing this voyage with me; it was my 2nd truly active round, and I've learned a lot about the game as well as made my first foray into Mush role-playing. We held on by our teeth, and it worked out pretty well despite all the setbacks, even though by the end it became a bit of a drag (but only because of gameplay systems). I have a feeling that I won't find a crew this good again in quite some time.

Alpha Bay 2 -- Day 8, Cycle 3Janice:
<sighs> This is it, then...?

It's hard to find the right words... this was a rollercoaster ride, wasn't it? Everything in my life up to this seems so small and irrelevant compared to what we've experienced together in this last week. And, sad as it seems... it's such a fitting, dramatic way to end, isn't it? Maybe one day they'll have a novel, a movie about us, when humanity has fought back and defeated the Mush...? At least I hope so...

I do not regret serving with any of you, listening to your problems and helping you where I could, and I can only hope that you return the sentiments.

Paranoid Paola here.

First of all my sincere apologies to Jin-Su, who as you may have gathered now was rather mercilessly killed for a series of apparent mistakes that made me add 2 and 2 and somehow get 5. This was the first time I killed an innocent in error and it was extremely painful, and I hope the last. I'm not going to name names, but this was not a unilateral decision I took, but I'm happy to shoulder the burden of responsibility. By the way the topic title was my idea as we wanted to honor you.

This was a journey filled with many joys, lows and ironies. Some of the more memorable moments for me :

* Being 100% sure that Jin-Su was mush and taking matters into my own hands and then finding out he wasn't. Although it happened early in the game it completely changed the mood of the game and really set the stage for the rest of the journey and some of the tensions and challenges we all faced together. The sillyness was over at that point, and it became serious.

* Me not realizing what 'paranoia' actually did and then believing that Janice was in the room controlling my speech somehow and having a 20 minute monologue crazy rant whilst everyone looked on perplexed. I remember thinking as a player at that point that I would want to kill me if I was playing one of the other crew.

* Scanning a planet for the first time with the last of my AP and finding Oxygen when we had 2 units of O2 left and Frieda and other crew had been trying for days with no luck.

* Long after we thought all the mush were dead I refresh the screen to see a mutated mush standing in the dorm with me. It took me about 5 seconds of just staring to process what I was actually seeing.

* All of us running around the ship doing jobs we were not trained for or accustomed to with the very last of our AP pulling the ship through one crisis only to land straight into the next. I think we dodged certain asphyxiation twice and several meteor storms with less than one cycle to go.

* Chao whining about his broken foot whilst lying in the medbay as we were dodging one of the aforementioned crisis with Janice desperately trying to hack the bridge console in time.

* Hua (our only active pilot) landing about 3 seconds before Chao kicked the ship into high gear a cycle too early without any warning at all.

* Me accusing Janice of being a mush every time she tried to help me with an act of kindness. At one point I was so sure she was trying to trick me I prepared an elaborate trap for her and wasted a whole bunch of MP on it. All because she offered to move across the ship to help me with my morale and offered me coffee to help with my duties. How you put up with me I don't know Janice!

Good times, crew. I'm sad we lost the will to make it back home, although it would have been a long long trip before we could have repaired the Pilgred.
