Ghost Gioele: My name's Gioele Rinaldo III, and I tripped on the finish line. And then Stephen went and did my daughter. Goddamn.

This is the story of the Daedalus, the ship that reached the utopian planet called "New Eden", and the sad shipowner that died the day prior to reaching it. From a set of expo events that had less than 1% risk of occurring in the sequence they did...

EDIT: This is the ship.
The Mush phase
So it all began with the PILGRED breaking down and the crew having to wake up. Grump Gioele and Stephen woke up, both having rainy clouds over their heads and making snarky remarks about it. They decide to start blaming it on Kuan-Ti, who they then find out is a vegetable. Another victim of the cryo-pods. Seems Jin Su is as well. Just to add to it, they find out that two of their crew members had been exposed to Mush and had awakened as agents of the terrifying fungus.

First day passes by, everything seems calm. Gioele has his coffee and discusses coffee blends with Chao and Finola, while in reality, he's rigged cameras around the ship and is scheming with the crew he relatively trusts (Gioele trusts no-one), planning who to kill. Kuan-Ti dies that day because of that he never finished the tutorial. Jin Su is drooling in Alpha Bay.

Then, Eleesha is killed. Apparently she went amok and punched Chao to near death. She's killed by Hua who's secretly held on to the knife since the start of the game. Or, at least Chao wanted it to seem that way as he explained the whole occurrence while being healed by Finola. Gioele, however, knew the truth. Chao had shot her twice before torturing her, and blew her ears off. She couldn't communicate, and was killed looking like a lunatic. She'd been speaking to Gioele in a private chat before the attack though ,and was able to leave one last message before she was killed.

This seemed far too fishy, especially when Chao started asking Gioele to go get items for him that he himself had been around before. It screamed "Trapper! Trapper!".

A "facial redecoration" later, and Gioele found out that Chao sure was Mush. He then disposed of the vegetable Jin Su and assumed control of the ship.

We then started to look for the second Mush. Hua had been holding on to a secret knife and had helped Chao in killing Eleesha, so it seemed obvious that she was the other Mush.

Until Ian came with some interesting insight on the case. He told Gioele that Finola had been working awfully slow in the lab, and seemed to sleep most of the time. Even so, she wasn't inactive, she spoke quite a bit, as a matter of fact. She'd also been silently active while Chao was killed. Then Gioele realized... it had been Finola and Chao that were speaking to him about coffee, and Chao had been in there before he drank it. "Oh crap." Gioele thought. "I probably have a bunch of spores in me."

So we kept our eyes on her, and sure enough, Ian caught her red-handed as she trapped a shelf. We rushed the cure and injected her with it.

Oh, and yeah, 2 spores. Close call!

The other days
From now on writing in first person.
Then there were the six other days. The days of Gioele's reign. Constantly being a bossy asshole is rough, and it always felt like the crew weren't responsive enough. I think Gioele's mindset was actually leaving impressions on my real one. It's not like me to play, act and write as such a character (Roland is my favorite), and I was feeling weird towards the end.

We kept going after the Mush threat was neutralized, and went on several expos, storing up on fuel, oxygen, artifacts and food. Then we focused on going only to planets with cristallite.

Sadly all the cristallite planets we found was nasty. Not "volcanoes, mankarogs and seismic" bad, but we got lost and hurt so often to the cristallite planets we went to. The first one hurt us bad and had a mush trap, on the second we found a fragment, but both me and Terrence got lost. Thankfully, both of us were found alive.

Then, on the third, I was killed by taking fatigue damage, and then hit twice for max damage from "accident" events, and Paola got lost. She was found, along with a fragment, though.

The day after they found the last fragment and went home. Oh, and my enemy, the one and only Stephen Seagull assumed command and went and did the thing with my daughter.

To summarize
A good ship!
I had fun and some good laughs.

I was actually sorta sad when I had to kill Chao, as he seemed to be a cool guy. Thankfully I could keep speaking to him over messages on Twinoid.

Also, a shout out to Janice, the one that helped me out so many times on the ship. You were a great teammate.

But all in all, it's been a good ship. I hope the rest of the crew will add their perspectives and details to the story. I hope no-one is mad at me for being such an ass for 10 days. Oh, and sorry for being so late with putting this up.
