Was this the end of the two Alpha Mushes? Would they survive? Stay tuned...

Act 1: The Boo-Boo
1. It was obvious we needed to convert Roland, the tough as nails Human Leader. Unfortunately he was at Bravo Dorm under the watchful eye of the camera.

4. In the end, several people took pot shots at poor Chao. Roland killed him at the Medlab.

5. Of course, our erstwhile Chef also wandered into the Medlab on the pretext of wanting to kill Chao and infected Roland whilst he was there.

Act 2: The Dumb-ass Move!
1. Now after the hullaboo had died down, things got pretty dicey. Several people started accusing Frieda as Mush instead for her wrong accusations. She denied she was Mush but refused the food test. Volunteered to be quarantined on the Bridge but...

New players can have lots of different types of great ships. A player doesn't even have to win to have a great ship.

New players tend to have great ships when at least one experienced player 'adopts' them, or maybe even the whole crew. A ship where they can communicate back and forth with at least one other player, feel like they have teamwork going on, like their play has purpose and meaning, like what they do and they themselves matter to more than just themself.

Even a mush can give a newbie that, even if the newbie is human and is tricked a lot, converted or not quite helped enough to save their ship, but still helped a lot and taught a lot.

Some of my happiest teaming up with newbies has happened on ships where newbie and I were human, and humans lost.... and when newbie and I were mush, and humans lost

Seriously though.... all most newbies need is one player to connect with. We can all be that. 'Winning too' is of course nice... but a newbie (or a pro!) can have a great time losing too, it's all in how the process happened.
