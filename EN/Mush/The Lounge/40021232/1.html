So, Kuan Ti and I were Alpha Mush. Both of us were experienced players. However, I was only a bronze at the start of the ship.

Early on, I nabbed the pilot skill. Intentionally. I figured it would be a good way to harm the crew if there was no astrophysicist on board, while having pilot would let me Alpha Strike/Escape if necessary. How this single choice would color and haunt the late-game...

The biggest threats, off the bat, were Roland and Terrence. They were both Gold Players, and experienced. Still, Kuan Ti and I were dedicated to playing the long game and attempting to pass under the radar.

This was not to be. Somehow, the crew zeroed in on Kuan Ti as potential mush. Before long, he was dead and I was partnerless.

Well, not exactly partnerless. From the start, Jin Su was an awful griefer. Literally, the worst I've ever seen. He alpha striked almost immediately, and then managed to kill both Roland and Terrence. At no point was he ever Mush.

After the deaths of Kuan Ti and Jin Su (who I suggested had transfered), Frieda was in place to be trusted and coast the whole way. Of course, I got greedy. I slipped up.

Having been victorious as Alpha Mush on a few ships, I knew that heavy activity can be an enemy. There are just too many ways to screw up.

Still, I saw Janice hanging out in the Nexus. It was a tempting sporing target. I was already dirty from 'loading fuel' so we could jump away and kill Jin Su. It seemed like a great opportunity.

I had no idea that inactive Gioele had put one of his paranoid cameras in the Nexus.

After that, I did a little bit of unheroic panicking. I quickly chose a mush skill (up until that point, I had done without. I've actually won games without ever picking a Mush skill). But this time, karma had a bit of fun with me.

Whereas I had pretended to misclick and choose pilot at the beginning of the game, in my shock of having been caught by the camera, I quickly chose a Mush Skill. I meant to choose Transfer. Instead, I accidentally chose Trapper. FAIL!

After that, I quickly grabbed the hacker kit and wrench, hid my walkie talkie in Alpha Bay 1, and ran off in the Pasiphae. Fail Number 2: Apparently, you can't take off with the Hacker Kit. It just gets dropped in the Bay.

Part 2: The Golden Transfer

So, now I was stuck in the Pasiphae. Janice had a spore. I had Trapper. What was a bronze player to do?

This was the game where I became a paying member of the community. I bought my starter kit. Suddenly, I was gold. I took two more mush skills--transfer and phagocyte.

Yet, despite having intentionally run down my AP to 0, I couldn't transfer into Janice. What was going on? Musphedia held the answer. I had concocted this wonderful plan, only to discover that you can only transfer if you're in the same room AND have 2 AP.

Long story short, I parked my Pasiphae, did some mischief (I forget what, but I bled myself down to two AP), and then swapped into a fresh, young, and beautiful Psychologist. Who wasn't a pilot. I also decided to double extract and phagocyte, and then try out the Defacer skill. Theoretically, it should have fully wiped the logs of the room.

Then, we had our Frieda hunt. J-Frieda was speechless, and followed me to Bravo dorm. Things were going so well. Then she stumbled upon my hidden itrackie in Alpha Bay. I suspect she was able to see it, in the way that people can see what they hide. But maybe she had to search for it.

Anyway, most of you were around for the rest of this. I did my best to throw you folks off my scent and prolong the deception. For the most part, it worked. I swung by the Nexus and played with the settings: food destruction on, no pilot lock, yes project lock, etc. I did my best to poke holes in J-Frieda's story. Still, I couldn't escape the dastardly "Skill Thread" that Roland put up. I always thought responding to those things was a bad idea. I was right.

Still, I was able to fool Andie for a bit with my suggestion that Frieda had hacked the Neron Bios and taken off the pilot lock to help her/me escape, which would explain how an astrophysicist could be zipping around in space.

Ultimately, though, the deception was up. Thankfully, I was safely in the Pasiphae again. And, while the ship posted a ton of guards in the various launch bays, it didn't stop me from taking off. And landing. And landing again.

This was how the Daedalus died. Killed by Janice: she didn't have the pilot skill, but she could kamikaze just fine.
