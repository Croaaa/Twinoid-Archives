Rules

The following are the laws my Forum Game universe adheres to, we use typical RNG.

When you attempt something, eg: Repair the Air conditioning. It is assigned a difficulty, in this case, it is 54 you have a base Tech level determined by rolling a d100 in you character submission.
When you attempt something, a second d100 is rolled and added to your base skill, if the combined total is higher than difficulty of the task, the task is successful. in this case, the person attempting the repair has 30, he needs to roll a 25 or higher in order to succeed in repairing the Air Conditioning Unit.

Combat works a little differently: Your base roll determines both your attack and defense, say your base is 57, all your attacks have 57 base damage, plus whatever you roll on the instance d100, but you need to pierce your opponents base defense. the remainder is the damage dealt.

Example Chao has a base of 100, and rolls a 100 on his d100. thats 200 damage, but the Makarog has a base of 150 (NPCs can exceed the 100 rule, but where they do, they don't get an additional roll.) The Makarog has 45 HP. Chao one hits the Makarog, because the remainder of the damage he deals versus the defense of his opponent is greater than it's HP.

The rest of the skills are "job" skills. (These all function similarly, you have a target "HP" that every job has, and your base skill plus a d100 determines how much you complete of it everytime you attempt that task.)
Research skill. (I mean, stuff still needs to be looked at all the time right?)
Gardening skill. (determines how well you can grow the Ark's food supply and Oxygen farm.)
Mental care skill. (Goodness knows the Ark needs it's shrinks.)
Cooking skill. (self-explanatory.)
Physical care skill. (The Medics and Nurses of the Ark.)
Tech Specialization.* (Those trying to repair the engines and other sensitive equipment require special training.)
Combat Specialization.* (The policemen, explorers and murderers of the Ark. Allows all kinds of fun ways to interact with your crewmates.)
More may be added.

*Specializations add critical chance to tasks, and allow access to special tasks, critical chance is determined by how high the skill is: 20 or below: pick a card, Black Aces win. 40 or below: d20. 60 or below: d12. 80 or below: d10. 81-99: d4. Skill level 100: coin flip.

If a critical occurs, the task is an automatic success.

Every time you perform an action that can make you dirty, a d12 is rolled, if the result is 1 or 12 you become dirty.
