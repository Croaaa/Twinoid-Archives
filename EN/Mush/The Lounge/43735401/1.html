The Pilots
We were lucky enough to have four pilots on this ship, thanks to the starting mage book! Well... technically. The only one of my missions they didn't receive was D4's, I think... which I gave to Hua while she was working as a solo pilot after Roland's demise. You were absolutely great by the way, Hua: bright, cheerful, and always ready to do your duty.

The Shrink
Thanks Janice for taking care of us! You were especially in demand that final day, and though you were stretched thin you did all you could. A solid expo team member as well - having both you and Andie meant that we were a lot more versatile in when we could head planetside.

The Quiet Do-ers
Ian, Kuan Ti and Terrence were kind of a mysterious force. KT and Terrence especially had a tendency to go inactive, then wake up and scuttle around the ship doing things, some of it useful. Ian silently tended his plants and then died of depression with hardly a peep. Poor fella. Stephen

The Mush

Derek

The End
I pretty much summed it up in my end words and false-start-AP thread, but ultimately: we could possibly have made it to D15. Maybe. With a ton of luck. But when I woke up to go to work and saw that 8 hunters had turned into 36... I panicked. I was on 5 health, and it looked like the crew had already done their healing rounds. What if I got plated the next cycle, or worse? Would Gioele be around to jump in my stead in time? (He had left final words by the time I got a break to check, so the answer could well have been "yes." ) The pilots were drained, so jumping to lose hunters was probably not an option. It would only take a couple cycles for them to mow through our plasma shield if were unlucky, cycles I would be at work, unable to coordinate for more than 15 minutes each. So I decided to make the safe move, the one that ensured everyone would make it safely to Sol. I felt a bit bad about it, but I still think it was the right decision.

And hey, I certainly wouldn't mind seeing some of you in future ships.
