From any turret, or from any fighter or the scrap collection (Pasiphae) ships launched to space, you can see everything that's out in space close to the ship.


Days 1-5, we only ever get harmless merchants and normal 'hunters' (We call all the types hunters, but 'normal hunters' are what the game calls real hunters). Hunters have 6 health and do about 1-3 damage normally (can miss or crit)

Day 6+ you can get the other types:

Trax that follow the ship whenever it moves, having 10 health and doing the same damage as hunters
Arack that prevent the ship from moving, otherwise identical to normal hunters
D1000s that have 30 health and supposedly shoot 3x per cycle (I have seen them do 5 damage every cycle they don't miss.

And asteroids.

Asteroids are special, they don't shoot but they have a 'distance' and start with 20 health. When they first appear you have 6 cycles before they hit. Asteroids go straight through the plasma shield if you have one, and they do the damage equal to their remaining health. So if you shoot an asteroid until it has 1 health and let it hit you, the hull takes 1 damage (this is unique, the hunter types all shoot the same damage range even if injured).

Our ship had 2 asteroids due to hit D6C3. I mentioned this when I went to kill hunters D5C6, was scared if I didn't I'd look like a mush. You guys had until the end of D6C2 to move or kill them, else blamo.

As it was, they got damaged some, but still hit.

Moving is not a cureall, every time you move the ship new hunters are generated. Tends to be a smallish wave, but could easily be more than two pilots could handle.
