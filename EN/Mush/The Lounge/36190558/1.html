[AP] Misery Business

This is the story about a wealthy coffee addict, a skilled pilot, and ... another... who were all very happy.

It began on day one... when everyone woke up they were all so glad. Such an active crew! Many gold users! They would get to Eden for sure if the RNG gods willed it. Yet, something was wrong. Gioele felt a corruption within his veins. Disgusting, putrid, human flesh surrounded his perfect, Mushy core. He was in a human host. Roland too could feel this, and both Roland and Gioele cried out in dismay in their dreams.

Were the other crew lost Mush, hidden away in a freezer too just like them? No. They were actual humans. To the core. Roland and Gioele again dismayed. The crew was so good - good potential Mush, but not Mush! It was unfortunate that surely they could not give all of them their own cold serenity, the everlasting embrace that is Mush. Some would not be able to share their joy. They would have to die.

All was well for the Human crew. Except for three minor annoyances... the Chun was for the most part silent, and decided to finish unnecessary projects only to go brain dead for a while in a turret. Chao and Stephen too both appeared to sustain brain damage and stood idle in Ref, both dirty, both seemingly the Mush, but were not.

Roland and Gioele both began their work immediately. They decided that unlike many ships of their past lives, where Mush were dispatched quickly for making too many obvious mistakes... they both would play their early roles well in an attempt to dispel suspicion. Fortunately for our Mushy heroes the Human members of the crew were not so careful, and made many small mistakes which could be seen as Mush sabotage. Getting dirty without saying? Taking key items away from where they should be? Finishing projects no one wanted? Such was their fate, and Mush luck.

The Mush had two primary threats, and one primary target. The Jin seemed very skilled and knowledgeable, and was likely ready to act at any moment to dispatch an obvious Mush threat. Eleesha too with her power to print The List made her a crucial target for Mush to turn. Finally, Ian, with his defensive skills would be an ideal asset for if things became bloody.

Gioele and Roland both worked diligently. Doing enough human work, doing just enough good... resetting research and projects. Killing all humans… ahem, hunters, collecting scrap. The ship had 100 hull, and 100 plasma shield, plus some decent research, and projects completed! Gioele even got the Gold Kitchen as a useless token display of non-Mushdom. By day 2 gas was finished, and by day 3 Mush could only make 2 spores a day, but it was already too late.

The other work also bore fruit, and with subterfuge they were able to keep their trails less obvious. Hua took the apron out of the lab, and Gioele used it as an excuse to feign getting dirty while relaying research. He quickly went to Alpha Dorm, picked up the soap, and dropped it. He was clean now, and if anyone were to check his life with a medkit he would be at full health without having left the room after. But spores were still created, and showers were taken covertly. By day 3 Terrence, Ian, and Eleesha were spored by traps.

The Mush sang in their dreams. "Eleesha/, we know you will join us soon. When you do... take anonymush. The humans need not panic. They will all join us soon one way or another."

When the Mush awoke Eleesha was turned by a final trap around day 3-4. She was ready to fight for her new life as a Mush, and proved to be masterful at her new found calling.

The crew went on two expos. The first only O2 was collected. Gioele insisted that Chun pick up the hacker kit, but she did not. The expo was not lost, but only because Mush was not yet ready. The second, well...

The plan was simple. Jin, Hua, and Terrence would go on expo. Roland was conveniently AFK, and Gioele was on a "mobile device" so could not move around... which was a lie. Since Chun was brain dead more or less she didn't pick up the armor or hacker kit, and so once the expo team left... Eleesha quickly loaded a fuel, hacked the bridge, moved the ship, demoralized a few people, and then switched bodies with Ian. Jin, Hua, and Terrence were dead, and many of the crew were at severe lack of moral.
