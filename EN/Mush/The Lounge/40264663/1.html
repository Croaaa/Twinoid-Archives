Relax, Janice. As attractive as you may be (yes, metal parts and all), you're a bit young for my tastes. I'm no cradle-robber .

Kuan Ti here. This ship was quite the journey. Here's the exciting bit, for those who weren't with us.

Mush Frieda's days were numbered as soon as she picked up the hacker kit without saying anything and then continued to hold onto it. The only delay there was finding somebody trustworthy who also had the AP to do anything about it as we burned through the important anti-mush research and our lucky draw on Plasma Shields.

The real problem came when we had to take care of the second mush. Roland was a huge suspect, because he was holding onto the knife and requests for him to hand it over only resulted in moaning and griping about lost AP - despite the fact that he had made the journey to the front of the ship to help with establishing comms, and a quick word to our active Chun would have allowed him to drop it off in the front corridor with no issues. Yet there were other factors; Ian had managed to get dirty over three times in the first few days (forgetting the apron for gardening or researching), as well as posting that the latest in the day he could get on was cycle 5, so he would have trouble with the garden. And Derek had gone inactive in the engine room, the exclusive domain of Raluca, while holding on to one of the blasters. Finding mushy activity wasn't hard- the difficult was figuring out who was the real mush. The Hunting group was reluctant to turn this flight into a murdership, especially when there were so many active and helpful players around.

Still, we had almost set the seal on killing Roland when... Derek started to move. He headed straight to the bridge as an expedition team launched. As the ship's only planet scanner, I sounded the alert in the mush-hunting chat when I saw that dot moving, and actually had a taste of fear when Eleesha reported that he had dropped his walkie because she couldn't contact him. He appeared on the bridge, and I was prepared to flee for my life- when he checked the comms console, checked the planet scanner... and left.

Unable to believe what had just happened, I checked the tracker to find an extra dot in the lab. Aha, I thought, he'd decided to make better use of his energy to take out the research crew, or discovered that Chun was now holding the hacker kit. But he merely checked the research console, flirted with Eleesha (who was standing there in place of a camera), and left again.

Eleesha followed him through the ship, trying to get him in a PM through whispering and find out what the heck was going on. He ate (presumably to stop starving), flirted with a few more people. Eventually, he came to rest in Alpha Dorm, where Stephen the apprentice shrink, Roland the pilot, and Terrence the tech and drone wizard were resting. And there he stayed.

We were baffled. He had just blown all his AP checking on things that he could have known just by looking at the chat, and flirting with a few of the ladies. He didn't respond to Eleesha's pm, even when she invited me into the conversation. And he still had shooter points, and a blaster on his person.

At this point, it was clear he had to go. At the very best, he was a wild card uncommunicative player. At the worst, he was a mush. We quickly made a plan- Terrence stepped into the hallway and crafted the Lizaro Jungle, while I was brought a ration. And one or two hours after he'd stayed silent in the dorm, we acted. Terrence took a shot to bring down his health, then left it to me to beat the rest of the life out of him. And we stared in disgust at the crew bar as it showed another dead human.

So I picked up the blaster Derek had dropped (I was confirmed human after Frieda's death) and returned to my lair on the bridge, soaking up Paola's logistics to find us a planet. And knowing I had done all I could for now, I went to bed in real life.

The next cycle, everything went wrong.

Roland didn't waste any time. Knowing that Terrence (who aside from having a valuable skillset was an experienced and knowledgeable player) was asleep in real life, the pilot laid into him. Terrence went down, and with his death, dropped the Lizaro. Roland grabbed it and made his way up to the bridge. He used a shooter point to put a bullet in me and with his last AP, he spiked Paola and transferred into her. Paola had been saving AP for the rebel base, and the mush gleefully spent it all trying to finish me off. It was very close, but several swings missed (perhaps from unfamiliarty with the new body).

Stephen was awake. He sounded the alert in the chat with Terrence's death message and chased Roland up to the bridge- but instead of reading the room logs, he started pounding the old body. He realized too late what had happened, but he informed chat that there had possibly been a transfer, as Roland had spiked Paola.

I woke up at this point, and was in something of a panic, as I had 4-5 health left. I confirmed the transfer - Paola had been gold yet now only had two skills, while Roland had none at all. Additionally, I sounded the alert in the mush-hunting channel. The mush had access there, thanks to Paola's also having contracted a disorder from Frieda's death, and attempted to throw the others off by claiming I was the mush, but I didn't have any fear of that being believed. Instead, I contacted Stephen, who was very apologetic about his role in the disaster - he hadn't really read the logs and missed that Paola had started laying into me after the spike. I tried a shot with the blaster but missed. Chun was on and came in to take a few stabs with the knife; she missed, and simultaneously dropped out of the mush hunting chat.

I went into salvage mode, instructing Stephen to take the Diplomat book we'd gotten from Xyloph and leave the room. He was still full health, and that would keep the mush from both wasting the book and getting the ability to declare a cease-fire. Chun had already left the room, presumably to attend to her torn tongue. I resigned myself to death, taunting back when the mush taunted us in chat.

And then out of nowhere, the commander came up from the back of the ship. Jin Su set the ship into motion away from the planet, then turned around and calmly sank a few shots into now-mush Paola with the other blaster. We were saved.

After that, it was a matter of adjusting. Paola-now-Roland came back and was... well, unhappy was an understatement. They were at 3 health and starving. But after some cheering on and use of the couch, Paoland got a ration and resigned himself (herself? We never really decided) to being a pilot. Chun had indeed torn her tongue - and gotten sepsis trying to fix it - but between my telling chat that a miss had happened and her getting a hold of the post-its, she got herself sorted out very quickly. She had healed herself of the sepsis before it could do too much, then saved AP in case she had to do several surgeries. In the meantime, we learned that though torn tongue also apparently makes you deaf to main chat as well as mute, if somebody in the room invites you to a private conversation you can read that just fine. Eleesha took over comms with her IT skills and her information-gathering savvy. I returned to planet scanning, taking the medlab bed until Eleesha no longer had a use for the sofa.

We gave the sprinter mage book to Raluca, allowing her to reach any broken object on the ship with ease. Paoland took the Diplomat book, giving us an alternate peacekeeper/pilot for expeditions if Andie was away or out of energy. Terrence had given the drones pilot to go with our Magnetic Net, and they did a better job keeping the area clear than I had ever seen before. Gioele went gold and picked up Astrophysicist to help with planet scanning. We had 8 plants producing jumpkins in a garden upgraded with both the shorter-growing-time project and the extra-fruit-chance project. Things went well, for quite a while.

Hua had always been quiet. Never spoke in chat except for the pilot thread, to report kills and pasiphae trips, but she did her job. Until day 10 or so. Then she went inactive, and ended up dying from injuries where she'd parked herself in AB2 on day 12. Stephen also went inactive, though he did so in Alpha Dorm, where his shrink skill was still useful until he died of depression on day 16ish (we'd been healing starvation damage to keep him as long as possible).

In the end, it was just too much. We had two starmap fragments, but the RNG just wasn't giving us a break. No suitable planets, coupled with double oxygen tank breaks every other cycle and fires and steel plates everywhere, and nobody had the AP to keep up with it. Gioele died from injuries, getting the room-shake/steel plate combo in the refectory. Ian was almost always nearly dead where he loitered in the garden. Raluca and Chun bore the brunt of the panic attacks. Eventually, we drew a line. If nothing promising showed up by Day 16, cycle 4, we'd head back to Sol before we lost somebody else.

One planet showed up, with two intelligent life zones, oxygen, and lots of places to get food. It could keep us alive long enough to locate Eden if we found the final piece. But nothing came of it. One alien stole a post-it, the other just handed us some steaks, and I fell into the ocean and drowned. So close, and yet so far.
