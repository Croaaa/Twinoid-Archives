An amazing voyage with a fantastic crew. One of the best experiences I have had in any online game.

We did 26 explorations on 21 different planets. Mostly from day 6 to day 16 while the extremely active Chao was commander. At least once, we managed to explore different planets in cycles 5, 7, and 1. We visited 4 cristalite sectors and about 30 intelligent life sectors, but the only star map fragment we ever found was in a shipwreck on day 8, I think.

On day 17 cycle 1 Chao was killed when he got hit by a metal sheet, a room shake, and an electrical spike at the same time for a total of 12 damage. An absurd case of bad luck as we were generally very careful with healing people all the time.

This left me as commander. I was already losing faith in our lack of expedition luck and while Chao’s death didn’t cost us that many action points, it demoralized us and it meant one fewer player who could always be relied upon to be online for expeditions. So, we went from an expedition-heavy strategy to a survival-based one where we only scanned when Janice or Frieda had full IT points. Freeing me from scanning meant that I could help a lot with other stuff.

In the last four days, we made 3-4 1-fuel-jumps per day to constantly flee the hunters. This worked fairly well. At the end, we still had full hull, half shield and 4 scrap metal to repair the hull if needed. However, it meant that we slowly ran out of fuel. And as neither fuel nor steaks got replenished and our last expedition attempt was a disaster (the best event we had was wandering), we couldn’t keep flying forever even if we we’re doing well on most other parameters. Could we have survived to day 23? Probably. Day 24? I don’t think so.

What worked well:
- IRC channel. Started on day 5 it saved us thousands of in-game posts. Not everyone used it all the time, but all the active players visited now and then. And a few seemingly shy players (Frieda and Chun) got considerably more communicative in the IRC channel. I never had any serious lag in-game, but I also rarely had much more than 300 posts in total in the four threads I kept as favorites.

- Four turbo drones. They and Kuan Ti kept the number of broken things fairly low until the very end. One time three drones used two cycles on not repairing the astroterminal, but at another we had an electrical spike in the engine room, breaking everything. I dropped three drones there and it was all fixed next cycle.
Firefighter might have helped prevent drones from breaking a few times, but I'm not sure how useful it was - we never relied upon drones to put out fires.

- Ian’s jumpkin garden with heat lamps. Having lots of jumpkins is obviously good, but it worked really well here. Most of the players were in European time zones, which meant that Janice sometimes was the only one online in cycle 3. Then it was fantastic that she can fix everything by eating enough jumpkins and even help keep her morale up at the same time.
We usually carried the plants around during the day and dropped them in the garden at day change (we never figured out whether heat lamps work if the plant is held by someone in the garden?). We still managed to lose a few plants when the fires magically appeared in the garden just when Paola hadn’t been able to pick up the plants because of connection problems.

- High activity. Obviously. But simply the 20 seconds it takes a person to log in and tell whether she (and her dorm) needs healing or not will save a lot of movement points in the long run.

- Steaks. Okay, we were usually disappointed when intelligent life yet again gave us steaks, but doing many expeditions with two survivalists brought in enough steaks to make our two chefs run out of cooking points by freezing them a few times. This meant that we could eat a lot of rations which helped compensate for our somewhat low player count.

- Thermosensor. Everyone understands the value of diplomat and babel module, but I find that the thermosensor is often underappreciated. It is actually very useful when you’re visiting 16-sector-planets (or larger) and you’re only really interested in the two intelligent life sectors as you can then usually avoid doing a second expedition.
Strongly recommended if you have a diplomat.

Mage books:
Biologist: Finola - who was bronze medic
Medic: Ian – our two biologist+medic players made great progress in research. Finola wasn’t very active, but Ian was and managed to alternate with her when she came online. They died at almost the same time early day 13 as Ian was lost and killed on exploration and Finola starved.
Robotics Expert: Eleesha – who needed a new job and gave us a nice set of upgraded drones as Terrence wasn’t reliable.
Chef: Chun – obvious choice when we decided not to give her one of the research skills

The mush:
We were fortunate enough not to have suicidal mush, but instead a pair who was trying for an early Sol return. This meant that went we got them, they hadn’t caused any real damage – the worst was that Jin Su had wasted time.

Raluca used genius to give us the plasma shield day 1 and showed that she was a knowledgeable player, but was busted when the mycoscan revealed that the cat was infected – few cycles before she would have been able to finish Pilgred.

Jin Su was a mystery. He was a gold player who had bought a skin. As mush he was an inactive and inefficient commander – which he probably overdid as many wanted him killed no matter if they thought him mush or not. He might even have been killed before they would have been able to finish Pilgred, but I am not sure on the exact timing. I believed his inactivity to be a front hiding a good player and thus called for him to be cured rather than killed when we got Raluca.
After he was cured (around the day 4/5 change), he was very active for a few hours and even gave gold days to a few players. (How he decided that Eleesha and Chao were those who needed gold most at a time when he knew there was no more mush is another mystery.) Then he completely disappeared and starved to death. It was so abrupt that I guess something happened in his private life.

When you make many explorations, strange things can happen.
- The exploration crew returned from one expedition and wanted to do another immediately. They didn’t notice that Chun had entered Icarus Bay to freeze steaks while they were away. This resulted in a second expedition with Chun randomly selected as participant instead of Janice the diplomat. Not ideal.

- Another time there was some confusion about whether we should heal minor damage before leaving. This resulted in Janice picking up the medikit at the same time as I launched the expedition. So, we went on expedition with the medikit. And as Janice went to bed while we we’re away, she was still holding the medikit for the next cycles. This meant we couldn’t do another expedition, but it also meant that we couldn’t heal her before she came back online. We were quite worried at the time, but luckily she survived the cycle change.

- Janice usually slept standing in Icarus Bay so we could use her on expeditions even if she wasn’t online. Which we did very often. One time the exploration returned and we could deduce that Janice was the one who was ill, but we had no way of knowing how nasty an illness it was. She hadn’t taken any damage on expedition so we couldn’t heal her. To be able to heal and cure her, I decided to punch her once – and immediately busted her nose. (But at least I cured her of smallpox.)

- We managed to lose the compass (early), a blaster, and a rope (and some post-its) along the way. I brought a camera on several expeditions hoping to lose it, but no luck.
Players were regularly lost and found on days 7-12. Then Ian was lost and unfortunately died. After that hardly anyone was lost for the rest of the expeditions.

- We generally got a nice set of projects completed. The only less useful one was the anti-gravity thrusters – the one time we tried it, it still resulted in a landing accident.


A great game deserves a long post.

/Gioele, tired and proud commander
