This was my fourth ship and my first as Terrence. I did a lot of homework and decided that he had a ton of potential on an active ship. Possibly more so than a caffeinated Geoele. Both together would have been better but if I had to pick one? No contest: Terrence !

Definitely my #1 lowlight. Roland (leadfarmer), Hua, and I had a lot of overlap in hours so we accomplished so much together. You want these guys on your ship.

Reflecting on the journey it's incredible how we matured. Consider this: we only had two tools, two fire extinguishers, and a medkit for most of the journey. Why is that significant? The difference between day 1 and day 30 was exactly 1 item and the rest was teamwork. A month ago I would have though an electrical surge on the bridge, 7 fires, "metal shakes", and 72 hunters was game over. Turns out: not a big deal. Just make a plan and execute. Oh and get plasma shields, obviously .

I would like to think we died from boredom. In the last couple days we took way more risks. Couple crazy expos that should have never worked (two trips in one cycle! boy, we had a lot to do when we landed) and generally playing loose. Let the o2 tank stay broken a little bit more, laughed at the hunters instead of finding a way to go shoot them down, curing Chun, 4+ people solved the kube, etc. We balanced the self-destruction well and ended with almost no fuel, o2, food, or shields.

Tons of respect for the French record holders (D33). I can really appreciate the organization that must have gone into that effort and you guys deserve to keep it. We wanted it badly but it was out of reach for us. I do hope that the tricks the community is learning continue to take the game to higher and higher levels. I'll share what I can remember once I've had some time to decompress.

Good luck, space cadets!

Keys to success:
- An active and willing crew. This does not mean experienced! Good leadership covers that.
- IRC Chat. Efficient and fluent communication is important. Mush chat is a bulletin board.
- Terrence. No really, his trait is exceedingly overpowered when utilized perfectly.

Everyone did their share of hard work and time investment but Jin Su and Terrence really stood out as leaders and organizers throughout. Those two just never stopped shining in the face of adversity. Jin Su's knowledge, experience and confidence was a perfect match for Terrence's talents in micromanaging group efforts and optimizing resource management.

There were many a time when the IRC chat was being flooded with statements from 7+ crew members and Terrence would give individual instructions to everyone while others was getting confused just trying to follow the damn thing.
On the other hand we have Jin Su who was ever-present 7 cycles a day as our central pillar: Whenever you logged on the captain would be there to give you a warm welcome and a briefing. ->

Frieda made for a good leadership figure as well and acted like an opposition to Jin Su from my perspective. They never got really angry at each other mind you - they had constructive arguments not demeaning accusations . Her RP as the caring but decisive grandma of the ship was also quite entertaining. I consider it unfortunate that we passed each other by for most of the trip because of the difference in activity cycles.

I wish to go on about our other talented and fantastic members of the crew but this is getting a bit long so I'll save it for later.

Then jackpot struck on D10 when, not only did we find three cristallite zone, but we also got three fragments within a 24 hour period. Unfortunately, we lost Hua, who was pretty active. Feel sorry for him.

Then started the looooooooong journey from D10 to D30 where we did a lot of expeditions, put out fires, repaired. My job was to finish the research and heal, heal, heal, heal, heal, heal, heal

Anyway, it all ended out well. Lot of RP by Frieda, Raluca, Chun etc made the chatting fun. Some people may have found my timezone annoying as I used to be offline when most people were active (this can be seen by Ian's screenshot where the only person missing is me ...because it was 5:30 AM my time ). Overall, a great experience. Would love to play with everyone in the ship again.
