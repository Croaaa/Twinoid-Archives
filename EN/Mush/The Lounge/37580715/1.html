Welcome to a mini-StoryGame. Rules of an SG:

1. Author (in this case, me) writes a mini chapter of a few paragraphs, ending it at a decision point (DP).

2. Readers suggest what the protagist should do at that decision point, and roll a 1d100. They are allowed to support an existing suggestion by simply saying "X's suggestion, 1d100."

3. The highest 1d100 is then written into the next chapter by the author, and repeat from (1).

I'll now begin with a prologue of my first SG (Sorry if it is a bit corny. ), a tale set in a realm ruled by dragons of old!

Oh, and feel free to start your own SG. I love to participate in them!

Dragons have ruled the world for years. A few people have tried to revolt back, and kill them. Those few people have either burnt to a crisp, gone mad, or have thier had atleast 10 yards from thier body. The dragons have ruled the world so long the the world itself has started to float, and the core of the world is seperated from the place that living things dwell. One wrong step and you'll fall for millions of miles before you burn up and die. But at this tavern, in the town raided almost every week, a group of daring adventurers meet. A dwarf prince with a promise to slay the Overlord Dragon, an elvish rebel leader and soon to be queen of Orindel, a man with the power to age forever and who has lived to see the world float higher, a young dwarvern apprentice with no special traits- that can be seen, and a hobbit with the only hope to defeating the Overlord of Dragons Tyranate- a courage like no other.
