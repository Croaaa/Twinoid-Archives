AJ_Impy

Raluca here. Truly, this was a magnificent voyage, setting off on the first day of the month and ending on the last. We started, as such things tend to, with a mush hunt, gradually identifying who the potentials were. and regrettably coming up with a couple of false positives. Gioele, we owe a collective apology to. Less so to Roland, who was so sure that he hadn't left any sign that he was a mush, and who was planning on staying incognito.

Unfortunately for him, the list of suspects shrank to the critical point. He was rightfully killed, and I used my genius to instantly produce the cure, which was administered to the last remaining suspect, Jin Su. Fortuitous, as it turned out, as once his humanity was restored, our commander was a round-the-clock rock about whom our journey was built.

We had a couple of inactives: Stephen and Chun, the latter of whom we kept alive for a few days, the former we, er, didn't. We had one active player die after we had switched to the expeditions phase, namely Eleesha, a victim of shoddy construction and poorly reported health. We had no less than sixteen hundred-post threads documenting everyone's vital statistics, and hers was the last fatality.

Once PILGRED was repaired, shortly after the Mush were dealt with, I picked up a botany mage book, helping to grow our ever-larger Bumpjunkin farm alongside Hua and Ian, safeguarding the pots through each cycle change whilst running about fixing things, repairing doors and putting out fires, The botany skill also helped on expeditions: I was one of many crewmates who reached double figures on away teams. I 'got lost' once (I was staying behind on the planet to keep Frieda company, that's my story and I'm sticking to it!) but no-one was ultimately left behind.

The quest for map pieces was long and arduous: Every single crystalite vein we visited was a mush trap, else someone got lost. We racked up a phenomenal amount of artifacts, with piles of alien can openers in easy reach of every technician, globs of circuit jelly in the nexus, ironically a few days after we'd completed all possible research, and so many water sticks that we eventually used them as chaff on expeditions after our post-its got burnt.

Romance blossomed on board ship: Kuan Ti read a psychology mage book, which promoted him up to 'tolerable' in Raluca's eyes, since spending time with him didn't make her actively unhappy. The Captain and Frieda got along exceptionally well as well, I'll let the others fill in any details they wish to add on this front.

The end came about one cycle earlier than we were planning for: There was a mad dash to try and get everyone out of single figures in health towards the end of the penultimate cycle. Unfortunately there were a couple of folks too injured to risk the turnover, so with literally seconds to spare, the Captain punched in the jump to Eden, guaranteeing that all eleven of us made it.

It was a truly magnificent endeavour. Everyone put in an amazing shift, doing their jobs and quite often someone elses'. No matter how bad it got, we remained on top, keeping everyone healthy. putting out fires, fixing the most vital broken things cycle after cycle. I won't forget putting out six out of eight fires in a single cycle, that's for sure.

So, ladies and gentlemen of the lounge, I give you the finest crew of the Daedalus yet to fly. At time of writing we have a monopoly on the Glory top 3, five out of the top eight, and every one of the Eden eleven is the highest-rated exemplar of their character. Beat that!
