Mush seems unique to other social games in that it inspires a lot of casual roleplay. Considering that, I was wondering if you all had any head canons.

For those not yet familiar with the term, 'headcanons' are basically theories or beliefs about the story behind a piece of media.

For example, Ian smoking some of his plants for *ahem* recreational purposes is, as far as I know, a common headcanon. A lot of people believe he blazes, but there is neither confirmation nor proof against that in the canon lore. (Unless you count that one text box in the intro, but I'm not sure which character that was pointed at.)

They can be inane and small. For example, Janice strikes me as the kind of lady who still likes hoolah hoops. No real reason. Has good hips for it.

Or, they can be larger theories. For example, I believe that Gioele's paranoia is a lifelong trait, and a major driving force both for his character and for how he interacts with others. For example, despite only being involved with the funding of the Ark, we know that he heard 'by chance' about the reality that Kuan Ti was building the Daedalus. He not only followed up on this, but broke into Kuan-Ti's office to learn the truth. If he's willing to break into an employee's office, what are the chances that he would stop from invading his daughter's privacy? We don't know why Paola ran away at 15, but it's possible he was overprotecting her and she got fed up. Even now that she's a rebel, he still tries to overprotect her by getting her onto the Daedalus, even though he is literally reducing their chances of saving humanity by trying to save his own flesh and blood over the more capable Nils Carson. So he's not only paranoid, but self-interested. Ultimately, he's a cowardly man who holds onto his dreams and family only when he can see that if he doesn't move to protect them, they will be lost. But he is very good at hanging on.

So, those are some of mine. I'd really like to hear yours.

I think Jin Su keeps a deliberately professional divide between him and most of the crew, and is an extremely good judge of character. Though there is no doubt that he's competant and perhaps one of the more legally-minded of the crew, he's not afraid to ignore a couple of rules here and there and he has a good sense for the 'bigger picture'.

Case in point regarding his character insight, the chain of command goes Jin-Su (defacto leader), Chao (merciless sadist who has absolutely no interest in the commander role at all), Gioele (Paranoid, Twitchy mess of a man whose only experience of leadership is running a giant corporation, not a small tight ship under stressful circumstances) and Stephen (who may very well want Jin Su dead so he can take his commander position).

In having Chao has his #2, he protects himself simultaneously from Stephen (Stephen has to push his way through Chao AND Jin to get Commander) and Chao (Chao would rather be bullying people than piloting the daedalus).
Just to hammer home just how little Jin intends to part with the Commander position, the Commander's manual says something along the lines of "It would be remiss of me not to lay standing orders in the event that something should happen to me." and cuts off there.

Remiss indeed, Commander Kim.

I do believe he'd be rather professional regarding most of the crew, and would do little more over the radio than give orders or request sitreps or organise the crew. To that end roleplaying him could be rather dull, except for the fact that I imagine he has an extremely close relationship with (having been working with her almost his entire career) Frieda as well as a more casual relationship with Kuan-Ti (ambitious, naive but with genuine potential as a leader) and a rivalry of sorts with Gioele. I also suspect that he'd be less authoritarian around Andie and Derek, Andie because she'd be awed and devoted to the point where it would be awkward, and Derek because... because... well. Derek.

So yes, Jin Su is a stoic, professional leader. A strategic genius and with character insight that allows him to raise their mood and predict their actions. Jin-Su's sense of humour would probably be very dry and very subtle.

One thing worth mentioning is that the skill 'cold-blooded' implies that he doesn't care much for his crew. However this interpretation is altered when you realise that the french name for the skill is Sang-Froid. Which translates, literally, as 'Cold-Blooded'. In french, referring to someone as cold-blooded says that they stay cool under pressure, literally that their body heat doesn't rise. As opposed to the english interpretation of cold-blooded where someone is remorseless and callous. A more appropriate translation for the cold-blooded skill would be 'Steel Nerves'. Jin Su is unflappable and calm, and always does what needs to be done, but is not a remorseless machine.

In all, my interpretation of Jin Su is of someone who is an extremely capable leader, insightful, intelligent and deliberately isolates himself from most of the crew so they seem him more as a boss than a friend. He gets his social interaction from Andie, Derek, Kuan-Ti, Frieda and Gioele and doesn't afraid of nothing.
