Hey, I finally found the thread! Chao, checking in. I guess I'll provide the recap too...

The first day was actually really eventful, although not for me. I had held off picking my skills until the Mush were selected, but once I found out I was human I picked Torturer, which I had just unlocked. I reasoned that although I didn't know if I would get to use it, the only reason I joined the ship was so I could test the skill and I figured it would be a quick ship, destined to be pulverized, that I could dabble around in before the MMM. Little did I know...

Later in the day, I find that both Mush had been killed while I was asleep. Apparently, Terrence didn't want to be Mush, so he suicided. I think Paola screwed up and removed a spore in public and so they were both outed really fast. They did manage to kill Chun, however...which was probably a good thing. I don't know what was up with her, but she never talked and went around flirting with everything that walked (and Terrence.) We clearly didn't lose a valuable member of the team there.

Now began the long period in which the only thing I remember doing was waiting for a mage book. Our leader Jin Su kept everything going smoothly, including getting O2 and helping Roland and Hua fight off hunters. By Day 10, we had maxed out on Neron Projects (even Plasma Shield) and had finished a grand total of ONE research project. That was Meridon Scrambler, which we got on Day 8! I blame this entirely on Finola, who although active until about Day 12, did nothing outside of holding on to valuable items. Her and Gioele (who died really early from depression) are the only two post-Mush crew who I wouldn't want to see again any time soon, to be honest.

Eleesha had been dutifully filling in as Comms. Officer since Paola died, and she finally unlocked the very last two Xyloph Databases around Day 10, which gave us the Ghost of Chun, as well as Medic and Shrink Mage books. I quickly snatched up the Medic book, and Kuan got the Shrink. Both of those books were extremely helpful; Kuan was crucial in helping Janice keep all of us in good morale. And once Raluca finished PILGRED, the Medic book allowed me to help us actually start doing Research. Thanks to a lot of coffee and a lot of relays (mostly from Ian and others, not a single one was from Finola), we obtained the RF Serum pretty fast.

But even by then, my role as a Medic had become less about research and more about keeping everyone alive. I lost count of how many times I healed Stephen, and Hua and Roland were also frequent customers. It still wasn't enough. A piece of sheet metal took Ian's head clean off while I was waiting for another cycle's coffee to give me the AP to heal him. Raluca frantically signed on and told us she had 1 HP left...a few minutes before the cycle changed and she died. Janice and Finola were fatally hurt without anyone even knowing they were in danger. Hilariously, Stephen had already wanted to kill Finola since he knew she was holding the Alien Bottle Opener and there were a ton of things we needed to repair any given cycle, but it turned out she had also stolen his scooter.

With the hunters and fires drawing ever closer, we set Day 16 C1 as our return date. Jin Su was slightly worried that the hunters would be able to overwhelm us if waited to d16, but we should have been more worried about O2. The tanks broke practically every cycle after d11, leaving us with none left at d15c8. We had assumed that the plants would give us O2 before someone would die of asphyxiation...and we were wrong. That's how poor Hua died the same cycle we were planning to go home and how Eleesha became the last woman standing. But the rest of us (Stephen, me, and Eleesha, though Kuan and Roland had also survived) celebrated by gathering in the bridge for one last moving speech from Jin Su, who had been leaving pearls of his wisdom on notes all throughout the ship. All in all, it was a great ship and I think our only regrets were that we couldn't save everyone.
