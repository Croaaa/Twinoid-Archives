Stephen here!

No Icarus. No apron. No Sample. No Pasiphae. A totally inactive Chun--sitting in the hallway just outside the lab. A dead mush Janice. 1 pilot.

Frankly, I thought we were doomed.

But this crew pulled together. Ian kept the plants going strong while our project team moved quickly to get the necessary projects to keep us breathing. (extra hydropots and hydoponic incubator.)

Our next order of business was to uncover the Mush so we could finish the Pilgred. But that was shockingly hard. Chao tortured his way through the crew, but no one acting suspicious. (Well, besides Eleesha, who removed a camera then reinstalled it. I killed her, but she wasn't Mush.)

I know not everyone is a fan of Mush tests. I'm not so much a fan of them myself. But it looked like the Mush was acting 100% human, so we decided to move forward.

We had a few conscientious objectors, Ian and Kuan-Ti. They both had reported gotten dirty and showered several times. Torture suggested they were innocent.

Terrence, our lone pilot, was working hard to keep the skies clear from interdictors. He also offered to buy some food from the vending machine to prove his innocence.

Raluca was role played as being wildly anti-social. But following her movements revealed that she picked up the coffee, a meal, and took a pill all in the same cycle. Unless she was a particularly clever mush hiding some food and coffee somewhere, she wasn't mush.

Frieda agreed to the mush test and was found to be not mush.

That left Terrence and Paola as the only options. Paola seemed to be ignoring any request to do a test but she was working hard on the comms. She had gotten rebel bases completed, updated Neron (which gave us the vital project oxygenated ducts) and done a lot of xyloph (including hand delivering the Mush Genome disk to the lab.)

But someone was Mush. It was a difficult decision-none of her actions had been Mushy. But by elimination she was the best candidate. I opened fire and then attacked hand to hand.

With my last AP I killed her. To my relief she had been the final mush, perfectly faking human actions.

We rushed Pilgred, worked hard on the Mush cure and related research, and returned home heroes!

(Though at day 10 cycle 7 we got hit by 2 asteroids, putting us at 2 life. Luckily I was able to take apart a blaster and patch the hole, giving us a few more cycles of life so the crew could get their golden ticket.)
