So. Story time.

Right, so. We lost one mush (Eleesha) to a NERON quarantine. Along with Andie (Multiteamsters: Unfortunate... I guess? Honestly didn't seem like a big loss.) And shortly after that I saw Ian sabotage the surgery pod. So Stephen and I killed him. And that was two Mush down. Which we assumed meant we got our two alphas out of the way.

At this point, only half of the people on the ship were alive and active, so deciding to continue the bloodbath probably wouldn't have ended well for us anyway. Whatever the case, we decided to continue the ship as though there were no mush remaining. Stephen got lucky with some trades, Hua took a risk with exploration, and on day 4 we had PILGRED, 8 Hydropots, and two jumpkins. Even with only half of a crew, our chances of Eden seemed pretty good.

Except... someone seemed to be uncooperative... At this point, we had Quantum Sensors and Oxygenated Ducts as possible NERON upgrades. Everyone who had voiced their opinion on the matter had agreed that Sensors were our best shot at doing some good on this ship. Except for two: Janice and Terrence. Janice didn't seem to be much for conversation in the first place, so her work on O2 Ducts really just seemed to indicate that she wasn't even paying attention to the chat. Terrence, however, was posting to indicate that he thought sensors were a bad idea. (SPOILER: We didn't end up needing the ducts. I guess?)

At this point, I figured either Terrence was gonna cooperate, or we may as well just turn the ship around and head back right away. So in the interest of not wasting too much time figuring out which would be the case, I decided to have some fun in a way I've always wanted to on a ship.

CHUN: You'll follow me, stephen, and the other 3 people who asked for QS. If you don't, you're standing in the way of us and Eden. If you decide to do that, then you're no longer a crewmember, you're an obstacle needing to be removed. I still have the knife, Terrence. I'm not afraid to use it if other crewmembers agree that you're no longer an asset. I don't like resorting to threatening non-mush crew, but it's well within the scope of the rules for me to do so if I have good enough reason to eliminate you. Another couple crewmembers agreeing that you're a threat is all I need. Take another count of the people who REALLY want QS and REALLY want to make the best effort we can to get to Eden and ask yourself: does it look like I can get a couple other people willing to kill to get to Eden? Because I think I can. And I think you should act accordingly. Think it over VERY carefully before you respond again. Everyone is watching.

Yep. I know. This was probably a bad idea. I knew there was an OKAY chance that it would cause Terrence to reconsider, but there was admittedly a better chance it'd just cause him to act out. Honestly, I just felt like if he actually had any plan to make it to Eden he wouldn't risk doing something stupid. And if he WAS going to do something stupid, I would have rather he just did it right off the bat rather than wait a few days. Was it fair of me to make this decision for everyone? Nope. This was totally selfish.

But... I had too much fun with this to feel all that bad. Sorry everyone.
