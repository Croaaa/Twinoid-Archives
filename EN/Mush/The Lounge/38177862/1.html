Welcome to a mini-StoryGame. Rules of an SG:

1. Author (in this case, me) writes a mini chapter of a few paragraphs, ending it at a decision point (DP).

2. Readers suggest what the protagist should do at that decision point, and roll a 1d100. They are allowed to support an existing suggestion by simply saying "X's suggestion, 1d100."

3. The highest 1d100 is then written into the next chapter by the author, and repeat from (1).

This one is set in the Mush Universe, prior to the Magellan projects launch, but after Finola is kidnapped.

========================================================================================================================

Even modern science couldn't save everyone, this was the case with the incredibly old, and the incredibly young, Mush researchers began to experiment on ways that they could keep the latter alive, thus increasing the yield of bodies harvested from the Human race.

A tiny baby boy, not even a month old, had been saved by their attempts, his mother died in a car crash, left the tiny being that could barely be called Human in the hands of the researchers.
His name is Tobius Levi, and he is first success story of the project.

When the altered Mush spores were injected into his body, they didn't take, leaving the researchers with little hope for the subject, however, one of the many scientists in the team got the idea to implant a fully formed Mush fungus directly into his cranium.

It took.

That was 13 years ago... today, the boy known as Toby, is being trained to fight against those that resist the Mush, he has shown much in the way of physical and mental development, he far outpaces Humans twice his age in knowledge and muscle development.

So it was hardly surprising when he was sent out to dispatch a group of rebels.

The group called themselves: "The Moons of Jupiter" the name was a rouse as they weren't actually located in the Sol star system.

The team of Mush operatives piled into the Prototype space vehicle know as a "Trax". There was a thick silence, as was the way of the Mush, they communicated Pheromones, not verbal speech, but even then it was quiet, the commander doing rollcall, the stink of fear on the untested but it was eerily silent nontheless. Then, suddenly, there was noise: the Trax took off with a fearsome roar, blistering the sky with trails of orange and red, it broke through Earths atmosphere and took off for the distant planet known as Xena-XII.
