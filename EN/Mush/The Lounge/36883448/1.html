Welcome to a mini-StoryGame. Rules of an SG:

1. Author (in this case, me) writes a mini chapter of a few paragraphs, ending it at a decision point (DP).

2. Readers suggest what the protagist should do at that decision point, and roll a 1d100. They are allowed to support an existing suggestion by simply saying "X's suggestion, 1d100."

3. The highest 1d100 is then written into the next chapter by the author, and repeat from (1).

I'll now begin with a prologue of my brand new SG, a tale of a prisoner!

p.s. feel free to start your own SG.

JAIL BIRD - STORY GAME BY DEADMANWATER
PROLOGUE

"It's been twelve years Mark, twelve years.."

The robbery of 1969 was set to perfection. The two robbers, Mark and Dwain had been planning for months, preparing for their most daring robbery yet. In a small country town of Dunderburg, the ill equipped bank would be dripping with cash from the wealthy farmers that lived in the area. The plan was simple, get in the back, wave their guns and shoot the security guard in the knee cap. Seeing they meant business, the customers and the bank staff would scream in panic and follow their every command. Fool proof, right?

Mark and Dwain are brothers, not only in blood, but in crime too. Mark is the younger of the two, and has only just hit 20 years of age. Dwain is 24. Both their parents were killed when 10 years ago in a freak car accident. The police say it was an accident, but both Dwain and Mark believe otherwise. The only way they have gotten by is by thieving and stealing. They are both quite skinny as running from the law can do that to you. They are both equipped with hockey suits and 9mm pistols, police issue. They don't have much practice with them as they only have 2 clips of ammo. They are both hoping they won't need to use them.

It was an early Monday morning, and nobody in their right mind gets up early on a Monday. Mark and Dwain were talking casually with their getaway driver.

"$5000 cash, you get us out of there and it's all yours"
"Are you sure you can't bump that up a biit more?"
"$5001."
"Smart ass, ok, I'll meet you out front in 10 minutes."

The deal was struck, now it was only a matter of getting the cash. They suited up, wearing hockey suits to disguise their appearance. Mark says enthusiastically, "This is going to be great man, can't wait to get this show on the road!" They cocked their guns, adjusted their masks. "We're going in."

BANG! CRASH! "GET ON THE GROUND NOW!""

Screams of panic fill the room and everyone drops to the floor, screaming and crying. Dwain drags the bank manager to the safe.
"Open it.. OPEN IT!" He yells as he points his gun at his head. The confused and scared bank manager shakily puts in the combo, the sweat on his forehead droops down his face. Mark casually kicks a civilian to the ground when he tries to look at what's happening. CLICK! Dwain smiles, and shoves the bank manager away. He opens the safe, smiling greedily, but what? It's empty? All that is in there is a $5 note. "What is this, some kind of joke?" Dwain exclaims. The bank manager looks up and smiles. "You thought you were robbing us today, but it is us robbing you. Robbing you of your freedom that you do not deserve. You really shouldn't hire a police officer for your getaway driver." Outside, tires screech, doors slam.
"This is the Police! Get on the ground now!"
The footsteps were surrounding the building, Mark ran into the room.
"What do we do man, what do we do?" Mark's voice was becoming erratic, he was clearly too scared to go to prison.
"We do what I should have done a long time ago brother, it's time we met our parents."
They both hug, tears from both eyes stream down, as Dwain rises his gun to their heads, they would both suffer no more...
BANG!

"Twelve years? Really? That makes me wonder, when will this torture end?"

Dwain looks down at his wrist, still partially deformed from the gunshot. It had been the getaway driver too, that stopped him from saving himself and his brother from the torture that prison would be. He sighed, and looked around what had been home for the last 12 years. A double bunk bed took up half the room, the other half consisted of a toilet and a sink. Nothing fancy really, nothing is ever fancy in prison. They live in Cell block C, the last of the cell blocks. It's a small country prison, that doesn't get funding, so there are not too many security guards. There are two fences, both have razor wire on-top, there's a gym and refractory, and that's all. Two square meals a day was all they got, it kept everyone skinny, thus lowering the chances of anyone ever escaping. But Mark and Dwain have had enough. Tonight is the night; the night they escape.


Hope you've enjoyed reading so far! Now the decision point is upon us, you must decide how they escape! Try to be elaborate too, don't want it too simple. Stay tuned for chapter one!

Chapter 1

Tonight was the night they would escape this infernal prison they were forced to call home for two long. They had it planned perfectly; Dwain would flail about brainlessly on the floor as if he was having a seizure, Mark would call a guard and they'd take him out and steal his keys. Foolproof, right?

"BLOUGH! ARGH! IT HURTS.. EULG!"
"Somebody? Anybody! Please! Help!"

A guard came rushing over.
"What's wrong with him?"
"I don't know, please, help him!"
"Bleh bleh blurgh eeoulp!!!"
Confused, and unsure what to do, the guard turned around and pulled out his iTrackie.
"NOW!"
Dwain spun around and grabbed the guard by his leg, pulling him inside the cell. Mark grabbed his tie and shoved him against the bed, thrusting him towards it. Dwain leapt to his feet to help take out the guard. They both lay into him, with no end in sight.
SMACK! "Ppplease, please, WACK! I have a wife an kids.." GRUNT!
The guard lay on the jail cell bed, bloodied and shaking in a fit. The rage in Mark's eyes boiled over.
"They die.. THEY ALL DIE! RAWG!"
Mark continued punching and kicking the near-death guard.
"MARK STOP! He has a family, we don't want to take away somebodies father! Think of Mum and Dad... We're here to escape, not murder."
Mark looked down, and kicked the bed.
"You live, for now."

Dwain grabbed the keys and baton from the Guard, and locked their cell. Getting out of their cell was the easy part, now they had to get out of the jail. What's the best way to cause a large disturbance in a jail and hope to escape? Why a riot of course! Dwain used the keys from the guard and started unlocking cells. Surprisingly there were no other guards in the area, but that wouldn't last long. The whole cell-block was buzzing with excitement as their doors opened. An old man approached Dwain.
"Thank you young man, for letting me have one last taste of freedom."
"You're welcome old timer!"
"I won't make it out of here, but here, take this.. But don't open it, not until you're out. Good luck out there."
The old man had given Dwain an old, partially scrunched up piece of paper. Suddenly, sirens wailed as the prisoner escape alarm went off. The prisoners yelled and screamed as the picked up random objects to use as weapons. Dwain and Mark used this opportunity to use a maintenance door to exit the area. They were finally getting out.

The faint sound of gunshots, yells and clatters of metal could be heard in the distance. They moved towards the Refectory. The old food truck should be there, and would make a nice getaway vehicle, and might have food in it.
The truck bay was completely empty. Most of the guards would be containing the riot. Mark hopped in the drivers seat, and surprisingly there were keys in the ignition. He started it up.
RRRRRGGGG. RRRRRGGG. RRRRRRRGVVRROOM DEDEVROOM!
Dwain checked the back, and sure enough it was full of food. If they could get far enough away they could survive months on the run.
"We're home free 'ol buddy 'ol pal! Hit it!"
Mark and Dwain sped off towards the gates.
"So how do we get through the gates?" Mark pondered.
"Just Ram it eh eh eh."
VVVRRROOOOOOOOOOOOOMMM!!!!!!!!!
"NO I WAS KIDDING AH AH..!?"
SMASH!

They were out. They made it out. Curiously, Dwain opened the piece of paper the old man gave him. It had some writing and a rough map drawn on it.

NoteDwain, Mark, your parents, they were good people, and close friends of mine. The police said they died in an accident, but it's not true. They were both murdered. In cold blood. There are three locations I've marked out on this rough map: A safehouse, an old weapons cache and the home of an old informant. You have all you need, now avenge your parents. Make me proud boys.

A tear dripped down Dwain's face. He always knew his parents were killed, but from what the old man describes, it made him feel sick. Whoever did this, will pay, it's only a matter of time.


We have reached our second decision point folks! What do the brothers do now? Should they take refuge in the safehouse and commit more crimes using weapons from the weapons cache, or should they track down the informant and get more information?? Stay tuned for more!

Chapter 2: Escaping Crimes Grip

Dwain stared endlessly at the piece of paper. They had what they needed, but he couldn't help but think this was not right. Why would this man give them this information? Did he have a quarrel with their parents' murderer? Dwain shut his eyes, and held the paper firmly.
"No more, we don't deserve this."
Without consulting Mark, Dwain tore the innocent seeming piece of paper into a hundred pieces, and threw it out the window.

"What the hell Dwain?!?!"

"I hate to say it, but I don't want us going down that bad path, ever again. All crime leads to is heartache, pain, suffering and an endless chase that most definitely will end up with us winding up in prison again. I can't have that happen anymore. We're leaving the country."

Mark was quite stunned at the reply, and being the one driving at the time almost lost control of the truck. They decided to head for Mexico.


They both ditched their prison overalls and strolled almost butt naked into a clothing store to purchase some new clothes. The customers in the store gave some strange looks and did their best to avoid them. While looking through the different clothes the shop owner uncomfortably confronted them.
"Erm, excuse me gentlemen, uhhh, can I help you at all?"
The man, with the name tag 'Carl' seems extremely uncomfortable with their current attire, and Dwain signals to Mark that he'll take care of this.
"Hello Carl, we'd like to buy some clothes."
"Anything you, er, have in mind?"
Dwain picked up four pairs of clothes that would fit them both nicely.
"Just this lot."
"So how are you, erm, going to pay for these today? Cash or Cheque?"
"Hard work"
"Erm, hard work? I don't understand what you mean."
"We don't have any money, so we'll work for you for however long you deem necessary for us to claim these clothes."
The shop owner was completely puzzled, and with several onlookers looming, he accepted.
"Ok, very well, but put on those two first, I don't think my customers like the view. Come to my office when you're changed and we'll sort something out."

Mark crossed his arms.
"The hell Dwain? Why can't we just grab the clothes and ditch this place??"
Dwain stopped, spun around, and stared directly into Marks eyes.
"Mark. Look at me. Looook at me. NO! What kind of people do you think we're going to become? We are going to earn an honest living, and that starts by doing the right thing. Stealing, is not the right thing."
Mark looked down, still puzzled about 'doing the right thing'.


"So it's settled then, you work for me for a week, and the clothes are yours. If you work hard, there might be some extras in it for you!"
Dwain happily accepted, and Mark, somewhat reluctantly, accepted too. It was only a week, that's not too long, right? During the week of contracted work, both brothers worked hard shifting clothes, sorting clothes and selling clothes. Dwain took pride in his work and neatly sorted the clothes evenly, however Mark seemed unenthusiastic and did not work as hard. They both spent their time either working hard or sleeping in the truck, albeit uncomfortable for them.

After the week was over, Carl seemed very impressed by their work. He gladly handed over the four pairs of clothes, then more clothes, and even more clothes, and finally handed them a wad of cash. Somewhat surprised, Dwain counted the money.
"400 dollars? Carl, are you..."
"Take it, you guys need it more than I do. I can only eat so many lollies."
"Wow Carl, thank you so much!"
"Just answer me this: Where are you guys headed now?"
"We're thinking Mexico."
Carls eyes lit up like a candle.
"Mexico?? Why I have a cousin living in Mexico!"
Excitedly, Carl ran out the back and picked up a map.
"If you're ever in the area, please visit my cousin, I'll tell her all about you! Now if we look on this map, the town is called 'Alwac-Atrrak-Aaneus', or AAA for short. Just keep an eye out for 'Zoupies Pastries'. You can't miss my cousin either, she goes by the name of Del. Have a safe travel and if you do see her, say hi for me!"
And with that, the two brothers were on the road again.

The road to Mexico was a treacherous one. The brothers considered their options. Quite frankly, Dwain had his heart set on going straight to 'Alwac-Atrrak-Aaneus' to meet Carl's cousin. Mark didn't really want to do anything, it seemed as though he had lost the meaning of life.
"So Mark, we're heading for 'Alwac-Atrrak-Aaneus'."
"That reminds me of a war movie we watched when we were small, what was it called?"
"Always Attack Aaeni"
"Can you tell me about it? I was too young to remember..."
"Well it was all about this small country that thought it could take on the might of America. It had one warship: 'The Royal Aaenia'. This ship sailed for war against America. It's humble captain was hell bent on destroying America, but his crew was not. Later on, they were surrounded by the American navy, but the captain refused to surrender."
"Then what happened?"
"He drowned with his ship."
He deserved it.

As the hours passed by Mark seemed to be more and more detached from the world, but there was not much Dwain could do. They managed to cross the boarder without a hitch, and they made their way towards the town.
"Zoupies Pastries?"
"Yep, this is the place"
Their food truck pulled in and they hopped out.
"Lets go find Del."

They walked into the bakery and their mouths instantly started salivating. The smell of the freshly baked bread and the sugar coated cupcakes was almost too much to handle. A girl from the back of the store approached them, wearing what appeared to be a disco uniform, and roller blades. She skated towards them and jumped over the counter and skidded to a halt in front of them. Her somewhat spherical Afro bounced with her movements.
"Yo waddup, can I help ya gentlemen?"
"Uh, yeah, Hi, we're looking for Del."
"Well todays ya lucky day!"
Seemingly out of nowhere she pulled out some sun glasses and popped them on.
"How can I help ya's?"
After days of travel, the brothers had finally found Del.

Chapter 3: The 'sweet' life

The moment Dwain lay his eyes upon Del he knew he was in safe, and somewhat jazzy hands. Del grew up in the small town of 'Alwac-Atrrak-Aaneus', and the locals always called it AAA. Both her parents originally owned the bakery and it was previously called 'The AAA Bakery', but Del didn't like the name. She renamed the shop 'Zoupies Pastries' after a particular pastry she made when she was young. After mixing some sand, twigs, a scorpion and some flour, her special 'Zoupies' baked in the oven. Del knew it wasn't good to eat but she was just having fun with the oven. She left the shop to play outside, forgetting completely about her special cakes. Unbeknown to her parents, who believed they were normal cakes, put them on the shelves for selling. The outcome was grief: 12 people were hospitalized from eating the cakes, as the sand and twigs caused indigestion. Her parents weren't so lucky, and managed to eat part of the scorpion. They later died from food poisoning. Her parents death didn't really have an impact on her though - she spent most of her time with her Grandma, Rayne, who the majority of the time raised her.

Although the bakery was small, Del gladly let the brothers in with open arms. There were enough rooms at the back of the bakery for everyone to have their own room - like a private getaway. Both brothers agreed to work in the bakery to help the business and have a place to sleep at night. Dwain was even convinced if they didn't know Del's cousin that she would've still let them in, the AAA community generally seemed well mannered and caring, and Dwain knew they had found a nice place, to call home. They promptly settled in and began their daily routine. The chemistry between Dwain and Del was flurrying, they got along really well and seemed very compatible. It could only be a matter of time, and something could develop.

Every week day, Monday to Friday the bakery was open, and during these times Dwain, Mark and Del would endlessly cook various pastries, breads and cup cakes. They would wake up 6 AM sharp to begin mixing vast quantities of flour, yeast and water. The shop would open at 8 AM and they would take turns at running the counter. A lot of the town members smiled to see the new faces helping out at their favorite bakery. The bakery's ovens would cook all day, finally cooling down at 4 PM. The days were tough, but Dwain and Del always managed to have smiles on their faces while they chatted about their childhoods. Mark on the other hand, didn't really seem to get along with anyone, while smiles and compliments were thrown his way, he seemed to shun them and get angry. For the next 12 days the three musketeers slaved away in relative peace, until, 'The Incident'.

It was a Tuesday night, Taco Tuesday as they called it. The abnormal family had finished their meals and were heading off to bed. They all went to their separate rooms and the lights turned out. Dwain tossed and turned in his bed. He had noticed that Dwain had been sleepy some mornings, and figured he wasn't sleeping well at night. He knew Mark wasn't really getting along too well with this life, so he decided to talk to him about it. He opened the bedroom door and came in, and to his surprise saw Mark half out the window.
"MARK! What are you doing? Get back inside now!"
"... No."
"Mark, what the hell man?"
"I'm.. I'm just going out... For.. For some fresh air."
"In the middle of the night?"
"Yes."
"Come on Mark, tell me the truth."
"No."
It was clear Dwain was not going to get any answers.
"Whatever then, I'm going back to sleep, don't stay out too long."
Mark sighed, and without a word left. A somewhat puzzled and confused Dwain headed off to sleep, unsure what to do next.
The next morning Mark was nowhere to be seen, and his window was still wide open. A look of grief and confusion covered Dwains face. He felt so responsible. A tear fell from his eye as Del walked up behind him.
"It's... It's all my fault.. He's gone."
Del hugged him from behind around his waist.
"Dwain, it can't be your fault, you're too nice and caring."
Dwain turned around and smiled. Grandma Rayne walked out of her bedroom and they both instantly separated.
"So what are you young folks upto hey?"
Both Dwain and Del stood motionless and silent at Marks door as Grandma Rayne looked in his room. A look of utter horror covered her face.
"Oh no.. The night riders have taken your brother it seems."
Right at that moment tire screeches came from the front of the store. Mark, Del and Grandma Rayne walked to the counter to see the horrors unfold. A large, muscular man with skull and crossbones tattooed on his arms kicked in the front door and walked towards the counter, dragging Mark by the hair. Del looked down in shame, as if she knew more than she was letting off. The man grinned, tipped his cowboy hat and threw Marks almost lifeless body on the ground.
"'Ello Del sweetheart, when you commin' back to the keep 'eh? Heh nevermind, silly question you hore."
Del looked up in shock and slapped the man across the face in anger.
"What the hell are you doing here Andy? I've already told you I'm not coming back to you."
"I'm not here for you. Your friend Mark here owes me and my bosses a pretty hefty debt. I'm just here to deliver that message. 50K by sunset, or we're taking it by force. We'll be waiting."
'Andy' picked up Mark by the legs and dragged him out of the store while casually whistling a song that brought a chill down everyone's spine.
Crows of the setting sun.

And so we've reached our decision point once again! What do Dwain, Del and Grandma Rayne do? Do they try to save the probably already lost Mark, pay the debts or risk an all out war with the outlaws? STAY TUNED!
