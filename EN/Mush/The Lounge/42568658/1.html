Hey, Terrence! I was your lovely comms officer-turned-astrophysicist Paola.

This ship probably had things the best "under control" that I've ever been on. The poor Mush were easily sniffed out and destroyed with no human casualties aside from a human Gioele griefer (who made the weakest attempt at sabotage I've ever seen). Most everyone stayed at least semi-active for a long time, and the ones that fell inactive died pretty quickly to plates or starvation, with the exception of Chun, Eleesha, and Frieda, who would drop off the radar and then suddenly have a burst of useful activity. We had active pilots, a Janice who managed to be everywhere she was needed with the Medikit or a hug, teams that managed to both finish NERON projects and venture into glory research, and a Heat Lamps garden full of Jumpkins that never saw a fire... at least not while I was alive. The biggest yield I saw was 18 jumpkins!

The thing that stood in the way of Eden was just finding the starmap fragments, which came excruciatingly slow. The first I think we found in a cavern while we were there for something else, and the next two Cristalite zones (neither of which I actually found) were Mush traps. I scanned tirelessly and never saw a single Cristalite zone on my terminal. I imagine that's why Stephen kept eating all the jumpkins to scan, which I was resisting the temptation to do for a reason. Ended up giving him a piece of my mind over it because of the AP waste, but the irritating thing was that like my magic touch for reestablishing the Sol connection, his grubby non-Astro hands had a magic touch for finding Cristalite planets.

When we got the Cristalite/Mankarog planet, it was day 17 and we decided to risk it, knowing the fires and breakages would only get worse. So we piled into the Icarus and... proceeded to find every other zone first. It was amazing how dramatically it unfolded. Finally there were two left: the Cristalite and the Mankarog. Success! We found a starmap! Now to cross our fingers. Maybe the Mankarogs will make us abandon the already good-as-finished expo. Or maybe, I thought cheekily, we'll find an artifact and it'll be the third starmap. Unfortunately, I got smashed instead. But at least it wasn't our Diplomat Janice, or that might have been game over for the crew right there.

I thought you guys had a solid chance, and I'm sorry to hear the ship's issues finally became insurmountable. We were so close, and I enjoyed flying with you all. After having a run of rather terrible ships, this one has been part of a trend of pretty great ones. So thanks for that.
