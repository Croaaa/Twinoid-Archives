http://mush.twinoid.com/theEnd/9607 was an interesting ship, one where you could easily lose hope of returning to Sol but we made it without mush.

Jin Su here. After full ship, I awoke to mayhem. Terrence Mush had thrown a grenade at me, and having dismantled fire extinguishers, had set fire to the dorms before taking off in the Pasiphae. Frieda's quick thinking and the luck of a Robotics Expert starter magebook meant we could upgrade the drone to deal with the fires. Success.

The other mush was Ian who became suspect after not relaying the antimush research. I tried to shoot him with my blaster points but I instead shot myself in the abdomen giving me a disfunctional liver. Painful.

After several cycles, even though Ian did redeem himself a little by doing research, he also claimed to be dirty from dealing with the plants then did research before showering. Gioele finished off what I failed to do so one mush down.

However, Terrence landed and killed Chao who was guarding AB2. We were low on oxygen and he stole some capsules taking off again. Luckily for us, there was a trader offering oxgyen for a hydropot so this trade Paola managed to do. I then moved the ship to abandon Terrence.

Frieda and Gioele scanned for planets. We managed to do 2 planets and get oxygen to keep us going.

We had lots of pilots, but only one tech - Hua who was semi-active. We started Pilgred late on, but it was slow going. 7% I think was the last reported progress. Then, in the penultimate cycle, a new trader came, D9C7, offering Pilgred. Hua had gone inactive again and Gioele spent a lot of ap shoving her into storage. Paola had been fixing stuff but we were lucky to feed her a pill for 3ap and a banana to get her clean and up to the bridge for the trade.

Neron participation finished Automatic Sprinklers at the cycle change and it immediately worked putting out 2 fires straight away.

There was a lot of inactivity on the ship, as well as some active but silent people. We lost Janice, and others either from starvation or fatal depression. Although I was Leader, and we had the pilots to defend the ship, with only one fire drone (unlike an earlier ship with the [AP] Game of Drones), it wasn't worth trying to survive another day with weekend giving some players some offline excitement - in the end, only 6 of us were alive and it wouldn't have been fair after all their hard work if they were to die from steel plates or fires.
