As Mush is coming to an end, let's share our experiences while playing this game - how you would rate this game, best moments you remember from this game, great teamwork in challenging ship, brilliant roleplay, cruel betrayals, your gameplay - anything to your heart's desire.

I'll remember this game as my first deceit game I have ever played. It made me love playing games where the opponent's identity is unknown. 7 years ago I was impudent and egocentric who liked spreading controversy and this used to be my human play on ships and even my attitude on forums. Once I was really mad when I missed my first Eden - got killed by fellow humans as human. But now I've learnt not to blame teammates for mistakes, even when I get killed, it means I could have done something better to avoid it. I have changed after all those years even though a part of my past still stays, I try not to cause any negative reactions.

As human I stayed vigilant on ships trying not to miss any suspicious behavior, trying to predict how Mush would act. Even when initially I didn't intend to Mush hunt, somehow I got included sooner or later. Whatever didn't get me killed, I didn't follow meta which I considered suboptimal. I used to even argue about the metagaming. I didn't invite people to Mush hunt for solely being confirmed human. I usually picked people which I considered smart and trusted. I wasn't afraid of asking potential Mush for information regarding Mush hunt, with taking caution.

As Mush, I liked infiltrating Mush hunt whenever possible. I followed human meta accepting everything they were saying. I was a tryhard every time because non-rampaging Mush play is strongly unbalanced and difficult. I calculated all my actions, usually playing passive way but with a plan that would make it a won game or looking for opportunities.

I hated being considered confirmed human and tried hiding illnesses and food-testing whenever possible. I analyzed each crewmember and predict their Mush play. Humans hunting for Mush usually stayed passive waiting for confirmations until there's a really small group, sometimes it being too late because of Mush striking first. When I was certain someone of being Mush and I felt I could do it myself, I killed the suspect without asking anyone else for permission. When I felt I needed help as I have job to do, I tried convincing and reasoning others but when it didn't work, I threatened them that I would go to act if they don't do anything. When I was Mush, surviving a week wasn't a challenge.

I've never got Mush Eden, but as starting Mush, I have achieved many things worth remembering:

  • simple rampage on the first day,
  • late rampage after surviving a week,
  • transfer to make humans kill each other,
  • be praised by humans for killing a human,
  • rush PILGRED and go to Sol,
  • let human send us to Sol,
  • sell human for PILGRED without any future plans (will I die first or be sent to Sol?),
  • join the ship just to bring the crew to Sol within 15 minutes,
  • transfer out of starving body to be considered "confirmed human",
  • discreetly convert half of crew,
  • manifestly convert half of crew without killing humans or occupying Icarus Bay.

So how will you remember Mush? Share your thoughts!
