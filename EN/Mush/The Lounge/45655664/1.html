The mush hunting chat was eager to take down our second mush quickly, and a little nervous. The mush did not disappear from the crew bar. We were wrong. It wasn't Kuan Ti.

With his humanity confirmed, but the hunting team's morale low, we added KT to the hunt PM. (He apologized for the lack of activity, owing to Real Life circumstances, and made a better effort to help after that. It was appreciated, by me at least.) We had lost the ability to cure the other mush, which was a big disappointment when all the other possible suspects were people we would like to keep.

So, we went on an expo. Rather Chao did, and specifically called on unproven crew members to attend. The idea was to examine them afterwards, when accidents and fatigue would make them reveal more than they normally would. Terrence immediately got lost. Then was found. Then got lost again, and this time was found dead. Our other suspect.... but he was human. I quietly threw up my hands and figured the mush would reveal themselves when they were ready, and all we could do was keep pressing forward.

Chao, on the other hand, acted. The expo had gone extremely poorly, enough to suspect a traitor. So he held the medkit and pretended to go to sleep, preventing heals. And then, the interviews began. Janice was spared, because he talked to pilot Frieda first. The expo was the first chance she'd had to get dirty, and she had taken the chance to pull a spore before she showered. Mush 2 dead.

The rest of the journey was not so easy. Kuan Ti switched priority to Astrophysicist, since his second skill had not been chosen. Eleesha read the mage book on planet finding to help. Chao and Janice kept each other's morale up, and I set to work on Pilgred, to get us our coffee to help with planet scans. Our biggest difficulty was lack of pilots. Hua and Roland rarely (if ever spoke) and seemed to only check in once in a while. Chun took the starting Pilot mage book and Lethargy, hoping to become a flying machine.

Ian had been roaming the ship, failing to report garden work, fixing unimportant breaks and eating to fuel these repairs. He'd been warned, but he was also a confirmed human. Plus, he was Ian, very annoying and energy intensive to kill. Still, food was very limited because he refused to comply. So we found another planet, to get oxygen and bring back food. A bonus, it had aliens that might give us star map pieces.

This expo went nearly as poorly as the first. I got lost. Chao got lost. Accidents all over the place. And when we returned without Chao and relaunched to find him, Chun healed those who had been hurt, but didn't fix herself. And then she took two 5hp accidents in a row. Rest in peace, Last Hope, and pray we can manage with only the two pilots.

We did, for a while. Hua was more responsive than Roland, and we had plenty of scrap. I fixed Pilgred, and we got and finished Plasma Shields with a few food-binge-fueled pushes. Another expo netted us a precious jumpkin plant, and with Heat Lamps in the garden, it was only a matter of time until we had a bumper bumpjumpkin crop to compliment the coffee. Chao commanded like he was born to it.

Then, disaster. The jumpkin was placed in the garden on C8, since it would mature for day change. And not ten minutes later, Ian cheerfully reported the plants cared for and watered, and that the new banana plant was doing fine. He had deliberately killed the jumpkin by trying to turn it back into a banana while dirty, then planted another banana in its place.

That was the last straw. Another dead human, and more reports to the SDF. Very frustrated, we continued our search for Eden, only slightly mollified by the star map fragment we obtained. And the next expo, we found piece two. But I was lost. And when they found me in the snow where I had curled up, it was too late.

The rest of the story must be told by someone there, but I quite enjoyed my trip despite the... difficulties. Special thanks to Chao, Finola, and Janice for being the RP core on the ship, but I appreciated everyone who wasn't a quitter or griefer. Even Gioele, that was a gutsy move.

Hope to play with you again!
