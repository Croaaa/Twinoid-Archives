Good game, everyone. Tragically, this time I was forced to do a lot of the "grunt work," as my mushy compatriot Chao suffered from his time in cryo-sleep.

I awoke as Janice-Mush. Chao-mush did not join me in awakening. Some of us Mush have termed the condition "freezer-burn." Personally, I find the term perjorative and distasteful-an insensitive and casual dismissal of the suffering of our fellow mush.

In any event, being all alone on a ship full of smelly meatbags, I resolved myself to stay under the radar. Early on, I communed with Neron-the only other sentient being aboard who could understand my plight. I made sure not to have Neron dedicate any of her processing power to useless things like research boosts. It was far more important for Neron to spend her time idly thinking about her sense of self and the nature of being, so as to further her self-actualization.

Unluckily for the crew, Gioele suffered a bit of a mental breakdown early on. He wasn't of Mush, but his actions helped our cause significantly. Had I been more inclined, perhaps I might have helped Gioele through his rough period. Instead, the crew killed him off.

Being the lone enlightened being on our ship, I could only do so much to sabotage our ship. Luckily, the Bravo Dorm did not have a camera installed, so I had easy access to sporing opportunities. Under the guise of "Pilgred Repair," I was able to get both Jin Su and Kuan Ti up to two spores a piece. On our Sixth Day, Jin Su joined me in Mushiness-just in time to ensure that we would not be fleeing the final hunters on our tail.

However, it would be immodest for me to not thank the two major killers of our ship. To Human Slothfulness and Inactivity: without you, I would have had to work much harder for victory.

(Good game, folks! Feel free to chime in-either in-character or out-to give your perspectives on our game )
