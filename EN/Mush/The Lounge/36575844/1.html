Alternate title : Damn you, Random Generator God.

After all these days venerating Him, praising Him, offering Him post-its as sacrificial victims, this is how he repays us ?

That ship was great, really. We had an awesome crew of 12 actives, 9 of whom returned to Sol on Day 16, burnt, injured, electrocuted, depressed, but still proud of having had such a great run.

The first few days were spent chasing our rather hopeless mush, Chao (who was inactive for 4 days, woke up to sabotage the Pasiphae, came back to bed and waited to be killed) and Jin Su (who did almost nothing, neither as mush or as commander). Still, that mush hunt was fun, even if we nearly did a mistake and cured Frieda with the serum... but at least we weren't about to kill her. (I'm still at a loss to understand why she was hungry 4 cycles earlier than she should have been, by the way... if anyone has an explanation, I'd be grateful, because it's driving me crazy not to understand. )

When we were finally mush free, and having found a first starmap fragment quite early, we decided to try and go to Eden, but the RNG had other plans. No matter how many planets we scanned after finding the first fragment and falling into a mush trap, we couldn't find another planet with cristalite. But we did find a LOT of seismic zones and volcanoes... And none of the artefacts we collected were starmap fragments (though we ended up with 3 Printed Circuit Jellies, which... well, which was totally useless).

And we had a fair number of horrible, terrible, tragic... er... tragedies, especially on the last few days :
- Janice accidentally cured Frieda of her coprolalia. Twice. Which was awful, because we loved our foul-mouthed astrophysicist and were hoping she would continue insulting us after going home.
- We lost a plant in a fire, which was heart-breaking for our poor Ian.
- Our courageous little Robo Tuktuk was severely injured while trying to heal repair Robo Molly, who had also been grievously hurt by another fire.

More seriously, the worst thing was losing Gioele today. Our great caffeine-addict pilot died during an expedition, having gallantly thrown himself in front of terrible insects to save Janice and Frieda. That was really unfair, because if someone deserved to go back to Sol, it was him. We couldn't have make it so far without him. And we couldn't go on without him either, and so we decided to go home.

So let's raise our coffee mugs to our fallen commander, and to the whole crew. Thanks everyone, we had a great time.
