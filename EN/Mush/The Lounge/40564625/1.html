The crew was incredibly hyperactive, and movements were difficult. I can tell you honestly, there were many, many heart pumping moments where I couldn't be certain whether or not I was going to get caught, watching as the moments unfolded. Knowing that any number of things could happen to stonewall us. One of the most difficult of tasks was finding out reasons to be dirty.

The ideal Jin Su is never dirty, and the ideal Stephen never needs to be dirty, but we were lucky. Fate smiled on us. Two expos in a row, Stephen got dirty. The third expo, I got dirty. Extracting spores came easier, and we were able to move freely. But our biggest issue didn't come from from that, it came from the activity of this crew, and, as the title says, the eventual reason for the Mush rampage.

Stephen jellied the AD camera once. I'd scheduled a speech to give myself reason to be down there at the time of the camera breakage... Stephen latched a spore on, but Raluca fixed the camera 8 minutes after breakage. It was the beginning of our struggle.

The second green jelly came at the worst time possible, cycle 5, which was 6:00 A.M. for me. I decided to stay up late for it and ended up having to do a solo expo... where I got an illness event. I pretended to 'pass out' and watched in horror as Eleesha came in the room and healed me once, signalling the end of our chances at a long ship. Roland fixed the camera two minutes after breakage, and Stephen barely had time to spore him the second time before it went back up.

I made my way to AD and took a bed, claiming I didn't have the AP/MvtP to get back to bridge (which was true). Janice was then... killed right in front of us, obliterating Stephen's last cover.

We rampaged at D6C8

We had to move up our time table, smashing no less than 5 AP from my bar, not letting me get the sleep AP, forcing an early phagocyte because it was with the natamy, and not allowing me to issue the mission to Stephen. I completed the conversion of Roland in BD and he joined the rampage, myself killing Eleesha, and Stephderek killing Kuan-Ti and Raluca. We were all out of AP by then because of Hua's efforts.

Gioele woke up, went on a coffee rampage, and proceeded to kill all of the MUSH, leaving 7 human crew aboard, one drooling in AD for inactivity (the Derek Stephen had transferred with). Gioele tried to lead the crew to Sol anyways, but it was doomed to fail. Pilgred was at 0%. I watched until the end. Regardless of our differences, Paola, Ian, you did well to survive that long. The odds were stacked against you.

Whatever the case, there's a question I posed at the beginning of this AP that I have yet to answer. You see, TE is the name of a meta... My meta. It stands for Towards Eden, and I would not be lying when I say that I have never been more proud of them than I am now.

Among other things, the day this ship launched was my farewell jump. The last ship I would be on in Mush for another ten months, since I knew I had to abandon the timesink. If you doubt our efficiency, you'll just have to note that of the 16 players on this ship, a solid 8 were TE players.

Now don't get me wrong! Just because we like playing together, doesn't mean we won't try to kill each other if we're on opposite teams. Gioele, in fact, the person who killed us, was TE as well. We're a family! We're just a bit dysfunctional, eh? I'll let you in on a group joke, Gioele finally gained the coveted distinction of completing 'AAA', Always Assassinate Aaeni. Don't ask me where they got it, cause I dunno.

Whatever the case, I'm proud to say we're pretty legit, and we're fantastic at shaming MMM players. TE members are known not only for the D2C8 MMM rush, wherein I proudly returned a full ship to Sol, but also for the MMM Eden trip... with Mush aboard. You're a badass, Sage. Genuine badass.

I sought permission from them for this final AP of mine for a while to reveal TE because I wanted to be able to brag about them, and I can honestly say I've never been prouder, because I know they're some of the best damn mush players on this server. Roleplayers and members of one great big family, where we all abide by the rules and expect every member to do the same. We try our best to bring a whole new standard of quality.

So, keep us in mind when you're on a ship that seems waaay more awesome than it should be. TE members are everywhere, from random jumps to group jumps, filling up MMM ships and even just regular weekday jumps. We'll try our hardest to be that extra standard of quality for people to measure up to, and bring one more level of amazing to every ship we find ourselves on. I owe my success in MUSH to TE, and my continued playing of MUSH to them as well. It wouldn't have been the same without such a fantastic group behind me.

Thank you, Mush, for bringing me all of these amazing people. Thank you, TE, for standing behind me always through thick and thin, from practically the beginning.

I'll miss you all.

Aaeni out.
