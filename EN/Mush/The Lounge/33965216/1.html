But seriously, it's all in the team. You don't need a hyperactive team or a caffeine junkie logging in every cycle. Just everyone contributing a little, whether it's to putting out fires, shooting hunters, or transporting drones around.

Find a good way to track health. Proper communication which makes it easy for everyone to know what to do if they log in for 10 minutes. I think this ship would've lasted to day 20 if I knew which oxygen tanks were broken and where the wrench was.

[7923422]

You don't need a hyperactive team or a caffeine junkie logging in every cycle. Just everyone contributing a little, whether it's to putting out fires, shooting hunters, or transporting drones around.
I wish that would be the case on our ship Muz, unfortunately that is far from what we've been through. From day 17 I had to log every hour, not cycle, since it could have been last for someone. We were loosing 8 health in average every 3 hours from day 16. And 16 health from day 18 distributed among 3-4 crew members.

I personally started having double panic attacks from day 15. That made our shrink work tough as hell, as you could easily lose 6 morale at cycle change. So most of the time I didn't wait for people to ask me to do speeches. I just did them on "just in case" basis.

Just like Muz correctly mentioned, one can not expect to be informed of someones health impediment in time, as person, who just been slain by steel plate may not be online to report it. So it's important to look after your teammates, read the logs carefully to establish if they've been hurt, and heal them without them asking. Roland was the one who really nailed this well. I believe Roland is also a perfect character for that, as he can move fast from place to place and save those, who are in trouble.

4 things become the main problem when you pass 15th day: fires, broken equipment (oxygen tanks), morale and health loss. So location of medkit, wrench, fire exringuishers should be monitored constantly. We also had a separate thread for health and morale, as Muz wrote. It's not the best idea to do this when you still have mush on board, as they can exploit this, but when they are gone - it really helps later in the game.

After mushes are dead, fixing Pilgred early also helps, as it gives you coffee every cycle. We had a person (Roland at first, me second) to stay in Refectory at all times to take out a cup of coffee.

The last hunter armada, that came to us included 31 vessel. This is when you need shields more than anything. Having Frieda as a pilot helped a lot.

Sometimes it's also good to tend for your real life morale. I remember when I got hopelessly depressed with everything on the ship broken (like 16 pieces of equipment at a time), I red "NERON commentary on Janice death", which Muz wrote. Nice roleplay can give you a lot of inspiration.

One thing that we failed to do well is throw a lot of post-its in critical arias like Lab, Rear Corridor (we held food there in later parts of the game with microwave and freezer), Dorms, Engine, Bridge, Alpha Bay 1 (where we kept lenses, scooter and sometimes wrench), Icarus Bay. Having 10-20 post-its in a room can protect other things from burning in fires. But it must be executed by a person with a good internet connection. It took me ages to throw 5 post-its in the corridor.

I must say we had a lot of bronze players on board that proved to be superb in their deeds! So it really isn't about experience or power play, but in being active and responsive, tending to needs of your crew and proper communication.
