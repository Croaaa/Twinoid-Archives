Frieda here! So of course, huge wall of text coming

Wish we could have pushed it a little farther too, but I think it was too high a chance for someone to die before Eden which just wouldn't have been fair. I'm happy to have made Day 20 and take all of us who kept on going even after we found the last starmap a couple days ago.

We visited so many Intelligent Life! 45 of them I think, and our first starmap was from one after at least 20 or 25. We finally found a cristalite planet but got mush trapped on it, then finally the second starmap on our second Cristalite.

Our planet scans kept turning up Intelligent Life so we would visit, only to get steaks or other artefacts, so finally we decided as we were getting into day 15/16 that it was getting a lot harder to keep alive and we really needed to find a cristalite planet to have a higher chance of finding that third one. Planet after planet scanned with volcanoes and seismic activity and the occasional mankarog was frustrating. Gioele then found a lovely planet with cristalite that had a bonus Intelligent Life.

I was not that optimistic going in and figured "we're just going to get mush trapped ." We explored the Intelligent Life before Cristalite and lo and behold, the third starmap piece! And of course we then promptly got mush trapped on the Cristalite following .

Upon return, we quickly got the coordinates to Eden calculated and knew we were still in decent shape health wise, so decided to push on. The next couple days were constant healing, doing the thing, morale boosting, and we could have kept doing that a little while longer but most voiced not wanting to risk it.

Despite the ship link making it look like mush were killed early on, the beginning of the ship was actually a bit of a hilarious comedy of errors that led to us nicknaming the ship "Murdership." There was initially not much sign of mush activity until Chao attacked Gioele with no provocation. Looking at Chao's activity, he didn't have anything mushy going on and looked like he was just a new player so while he could be mush he could have been attacking just for being new. We decided either way he was too much a liability if he was just going to be attacking people for no reason so we killed him. He wasn't mush and we were not too surprised.

The next kill came when Stephen started attacking Eleesha for no reason while she was tracking a mush suspect's steps. She posted quickly to alert us to what happened and then Stephen started posting that he was Eleesha and had been body swapped. Based on the room logs and Jin Su's conversations with Eleesha we determined that Stephen was lying about the body swap so he must be the mush trying to get us to kill human Eleesha. We killed him and... not mush.

Now this next part I still feel bad about. We were very confused and not sure how we could have been mistaken, we were sure a body swap had not happened. Yet Stephen was not mush. So we decided to kill Eleesha figuring we had been wrong somehow about the body swap not taking place and that poor Eleesha had been killed in Stephen's body. But nope. Killed Eleesha. Human. There was never any sort of mush body swap, Stephen was just a troll, who we promptly reported. So sorry Eleesha

Next, we decided to kill Hua without ceremony. She was inactive from the start and she and Stephen had the multi-teamster icon. Stephen mentioned something about Hua being his sister, but whether that was true or it was a multi-account, Hua was inactive anyway and we didn't want to risk her coming online and then trying to pull another Stephen. Human #4 dead.

At this point we figure the mush are laughing their asses at us, but we finally get some solid evidence to kill Roland and finally we have our one mush down. With one mush dead and not too much mush activity being seen and our rooms protected well with cameras and mycoalarms, we proceed to complete the serum which we decide to attempt to cure the second mush. We had a strong suspicion as to who it was and as long as people did not idle where they shouldn't, we felt we were safe to not act immediately to kill. Once the serum was done, we went ahead and used it on Raluca, and now we were mush free and plus a human to somewhat make up for murdership's beginnings.

Things started going very smoothly as we did planet explorations routinely and with the ship mush free we focused on researching items like the mush soap and lens. We knew the cat was infected because Raluca admitted it after being cured (her dropping off the cat in a dorm was one of the few evidences we had to go on), so we warned everyone that if they got scratched to not do any more lens research until the soap was done so you could remove a spore. Sadly Ian did not listen to his own advice and not realizing he had been infected from a trapped shelf, let himself get scratched twice and became mush. It was a bit funny that in the end the mush did end up being able to turn someone.. after the mush were all gone . It was really sad as Ian was an awesome part of our expedition team, and I hated that we then had to kill him. We missed you Ian!

Two other human deaths did occur later in the ship, a starvation death from Finola who I suppose lost interest once the important mush researches were done, and Kuan Ti, who unfortunately idled after planet scanning in a location we couldn't check on as frequently so a roomshake + sheet metal event did him in.

So that was the mush part of the story to our ship. Goes to show, don't write off a ship even if it gets off to a rocky start. If your remaining shipmates can band together, you can still go far.

We got meat quite a few times from Intelligent Life, at least 4 or 5 times. Once or twice we had a sticky note stolen, the rest of the visits got some kind of artifact. We had the Babel Module blueprint so Diplomat + Babel Module resulted in very positive outcomes with Intelligent Life, just took a long time to actually get the starmaps. I didn't keep a record so just from memory the artifacts we got were: 2 boulders, 2 invert shells, 2 bottle openers, tv, jelly, magellan map, the fuel/healing thing, 2 starmaps, 3 water sticks. We did get a couple from ruins/shipwrecks too, wish I kept an actual record as that would be interesting to see.

We had 7 pots at first, four starting and three from Ian's skill before he became mush. The extra hydropots project came up as one of our 2 last projects which was timely as we then had a fire that destroyed two of our plants plus the pots, so in the end we had 8 plants. We didn't rely on them so much for O2 though because with all the planets visited, we ended up topping off on O2 occasionally.

Aivaras, skillwise, I think what helped us a lot were:

Janice - Diplomat was extremely important. Avoiding combat meant we could safely run most explorations with just three people so it was a little easier to organize having 3 people come to Icarus at the needed time and also to heal afterward.

Gioele - Caffeine Addict + Astrophysicist. Planet scanning is the most AP-intensive activity of all jobs because you can complete your job (analyze a whole planet) and the results are no good (last analysis turns up a volcano). With all that coffee Gioele had a ton of AP so he did the majority of our planet scanning, then I would step in to help if he turned up a dud and it would be a few cycles before he could try again. On my previous Day 17 ship, I was Gioele and had the same role there as main planet scanner with coffee.

Paola - Rebel. Got all our signals decoded quickly and pushed through a few neron projects along the way so that we could end up with a few extra over just doing them through nexus. Finishing her comms job early then freed her to help finish up researches when Finola went inactive and to help take care of plants with Ian dead. She could also be a general help all around when surviving got a lot harder and filled in wherever needed.

With Roland killed as a mush, having Jin Su, Terrence, and Frieda all with pilot skills helped to divide up hunter killing duties. We managed pretty well to always have either Terrence or Frieda have AP to fly up to take out araks/tracks while Jin Su then moved the ship. If there weren't that many hunters, Jin could also just fly up to kill them all.

As for advice on surviving Day 15 and on, a lot of it was constant vigilance to heal people and keep morale up. Cycle 8 before the new day we would do a health/morale check and make sure to heal everyone to 10 minimum but preferred 12 and morale of 5 minimum though were shooting for 8-10 if possible. We would then repeat it at Cycle 1 and get people partnered up so that every guy did the thing with the three women lowest in morale (since we had 3 men/5 women). Then we would have Janice or Jin top people off on morale by having Janice hug anyone if needed and gathering for speeches. Fruit was also eaten as needed for morale, usually saving a couple for later if needed.

Then each cycle where possible or at least every other cycle, someone would walk around to each room to check for room shakes and sheet metaling and heal as needed. Almost all of Chun's Ap each day was spent taking the medkit around and healing people but it was a job for everyone to keep an eye on since she can't be online all the time. There's no way to check for panic attacks though, so that's why in Cycle 1 we tried to get people as high as possible so they could go a few cycles of panic attacks and still survive. Then everyone was online at least a couple more times during the day to give an update if they had panic attacks getting them to unsafe territory and if so organize to get them some fruit/hugs/or speech. We had an early derp where Terrence built the sofa at central corridor not knowing it could not be moved, but that turned out great in the end. Paola did not need her MP for that much early on in the ship so it was not too bad a trek to go to the bridge when she had AP to do her work and it kept Gioele near his coffee. Then in the later days it was a lot less MP to go check on everyone's health for heals with Gioele and Jin set up in central corridor.

Fires were pretty constant, without fire detector it would have been a huge pain. Typically at a cycle change we would see who was online to post about needing heals first or if anyone in their room needed a heal, then figure who should heal and who to put out the fire.

Oxygen tanks were also breaking constantly. 8 plants isn't self sufficient if you lose 3 O2 a cycle. Raluca was primarily on top of this, it seemed she had to fix an O2 tank practically every single cycle. When she was offline Paola or Gioele would fix or find a drone to drop there. Raluca would frequently also have to fix fuel tanks before loading fuel as well.

Then the last bit to keep an eye on constantly is hunters. You'll get a wave of 30+ hunters at least once each day. We had to move ship at least twice a day. Usually when we got small groups of hunters we would kill all or most non-asteroids, then hang out and move before the asteroids hit. Sometimes a few more would come while we hung out and we would have to launch to kill aracks/trax before the move, but at times we let them attack and let plasma shield take small hits and regenerate later, as long as we moved before any asteroids. When the big group comes, we'll stay for one cycle since hunters typically don't do damage the very next cycle (once they did early on the ship but I second guess myself because it never happened again), then have a pilot go up to kill araks/trax and move ship. Magnetic net helped there a lot since sometimes there were lots of trax to kill so the 2 extra shots was nice. We had the project that gave patrol ships 16 charges and occasionally used them all. While that project wasn't super necessary, it was pretty handy since we could keep our sniper's helmet/lens in one bay and there was almost always a ship with enough charges to take out whatever current crop of hunters had to be dealt with. Especially later in the game when patrol ships were getting broken too. Loading fuel to escape hunters could be tricky towards the end too with the fuel tanks constantly breaking. We made a habit of trying to have someone load fuel right as we were needing to move so they could stay in the engine room holding an extra fuel, then loading it once the ship was moved. That way we already had fuel ready for the next getaway, and the fuel tank just had to be repaired every other move.
