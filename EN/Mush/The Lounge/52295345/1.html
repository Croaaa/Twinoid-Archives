I was always wondering why are people constantly complaining about rising inactivity. I'm around for more then two years and all that time it's same - people complain how the game is getting worse and worse and yet I'm still getting mostly decent ships.

I just went through my last page of ships (admitedly, I dont jump so often, so it's seven ships in last four or five months, the current one including).
From those seven ships, two were your standard happy ending Sol return with nice chances for Eden which were stopped just by late game RNG and/or the lack of will to grind after Day 10 or 15 (which is completely understandable, not everyone's notion of fun is doing healing/hugging/fixing runs every second cycle and I don't blame such people for outvoting the Eden minority on those ships), and current ship seems like it's either gonna end the same or will be able to pull that Eden.
One ship was cut short by well executed mush rampage but we managed to finish Pilgred with like six active humans and return to Sol, after all.
One ship suffered the similar fate but there were no technicians left, so we just went for surviving as long as possible in four (again, very active) people. I was the last man standing on Day 12.
Only remaining two ships ended really soon and badly after mushes decimated us (and it was them, not RNG, crippling us). And even on those ships I remember a few very active and very enjoyable people to play with.
Of those ships, only the current one was part of any group jump I know about. And even on this one there are good players who were no part of that jump.

So I'm wondering, how it comes, that my sample of ships isn't such awful as all those forum-goers talking about waves of inactivity seem to experience? Maybe I'm just lucky. Or overly optimistic and bent to see glass half-full rather then half-empty (which I often am, in fact). But I'd rather say it's the third option - I'm active enough myself. And it works.

If you want the change, be the change. Sure, you can't do anything with ships filling on Day 3 or even 4, or the fact that all the pilots are inactive, or with half the crew going inactive right after the tutorial. But you can do quite a lot with what's left.
You can try to organize those few people who are, same as you, looking at the prospect of inactive, useless ship with desperation and helplessness. You can try to find alternative solutions and new approaches for the unique problems that the dire situation brings. You can try roleplay. And most of all, you can try to have fun.
Trying makes a whole difference here. And if some of those semi-acitves who lost hope in your "doomed ship" see you trying, some of them might join. And that's the change you want. No amount of complaining on forums can substitute for that.
