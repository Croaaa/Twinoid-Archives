Was an early waker as Frieda this most recent game, with most of the crew that was already awake lying silent in the Lab. I then headed to Alpha Dorm to rest for a few cycles after my quick burst of activity.

By the time I wake again, a few more people have stirred, some from cryo, and others from their standing positions in the Lab. On the whole, the ship seemed to be a bit less active than average, which was a shame, but it was far from being totally devoid of activity. Most hunters had been taken care of by the resident Pilots in quick fashion, and there was a decent amount of progress on NERON and research (though still rather slow).

I took the opportunity to head up to the Bridge to scan for planets, finding two mostly useless specimens and reporting my dismay, as by this point, we had almost reached half , and I was worried that without a lucky break, we would be cutting it close. I returned to bed yet again, expecting not to return for a few cycles.

I wake up to find the ship in most okay shape, with most non- concerns taken care of. The plants have been abandoned due to an inactive Ian, but with no insurance policy for our dwindling reserves, I had little reason to focus on anything but planet-searching. I manage to find one with albeit not fully scanned, and satisfied with my work, decide to rest on the Bridge, with a seemingly inactive Gioele and a sleeping Logistics Expert Paola to keep me coming.

Our last arrival, Stephen, came shortly after, and I found myself on the side of Mush, with a very active Janice to boot. Immediately after selection, Janice and I began plotting our evil plan, with a somewhat-sluggish Jin Su providing us ample time to enact it.

We decided to use the threat of the low supply to our advantage, capitalizing on the fact that by deleting my planet surreptitiously, it would be likely that we would have a scapegoat in our unwitting accomplice Jin Su when he moved us to where there was supposed to be a planet, and that with the crew distracted by organizing his demise, we would be able to watch the ship slowly suffocate.

After a few cycles of little happening except for a wave of hunters and a few units being used up, we decided to speed up our plan by having me sabotage the tanks right before cycle change and fly off with the rest of our supply in my pockets, leaving Janice as a mimic to watch the chaos unfold. Janice practiced her fake NERON messages in the Mush chat for a few cycles as we waited for the middle of the night.

As Cycle 3 began to dawn, I struck, using Phagocyte to fuel a tank-breaking spree, even lobbing a grenade in Bravo Dorm (where Janice was treating a few patients) to help Janice's alibi. I waited in Alpha Bay 2, eager to take a ship to shoot any renaming traders offering :o2:, but with cycle change, I found that my partner was late to bat.

Later in the cycle, I returned to find that Paola and Stephen had beaten me to within an inch of my life, leaving me with a measly 1 . Janice had come online since my attack, however, and switched the BIOS lock, allowing me to fly off with only left in the still-broken tanks. I made sure to swallow one last spore before I left to come back up to 5 , and waited for the ship to die off.

The next cycle, I came back to a death screen, courtesy of a rare Day 5 steel plate. I've just gotten word of the ship's end, and by the looks of it, our plan succeeded, though I'm excited to see just exactly how everything went down. If any of my crewmates are here, please let me know how it went after my unceremonious death!
