First of all, what a great ship! It was not without it's challenges, but we faced adversity extremely well. My first trip to Eden after playing this game intermittently since 2014.

Jin Su here - I'd not played the good captain before, was excited at the prospect of not only having pilot, but also being able to move the ship - I felt I could offer a lot of survivability to the crew just through being active and responsive to the hunter threat. No more last minute scrambles for the hacking kit! I could even keep morale topped up if necessary with my speeches - we had a great Janice so it wasn't needed that often.
It was a public ship. I was one of the last to join, it was already into day 3. The first good sign was not only our active roster of pilots keeping the skies clear, but also our science team led by Kuan Ti and Finola, who'd already completed several projects and researches, including the crucial plasma shield.
On day 5 with the crew at 15 (Eleesha never woke up), mush selection took place. Frieda was quick off the mark, using hacker kit to move us to the wrong planet to delay our search for oxygen. Not content with just asphyxiating us, she then grabbed apron, Schrodinger and medkit, absconding in a patroller. Without magnetic net, we just had to leave her and those precious items (and feline) behind - oxygen was the priority and we had to leave ASAP to get scanning again.
We had some great luck, quickly finding another oxygen planet, exploring it and hitting the jackpot of 24 O2 units with just a few cycles to spare before we would start taking casualties. From then on we stabilised and consolidated - it was established quickly that to survive we would have to stay active and pay attention to our health, visiting the medlab whenever we got low from a steel-plating. Ian had great success with the garden, and it wasn't long before we had 9 adult banana plants that kept oxygen levels healthy. Combined with heat lamps we were getting 10+ fruits per day, which helped fill the gap left by the medkit (as well as topping up morale).
Later challenges we faced including losing a few people, mainly due to inactivity. Roland and Raluca both had some bad luck and got killed by platings/shakes. Paola did a good job at comms, but then inexplicably starved to death. The second mush turned out to be an inactive Stephen.
Further, we had a suspected griefing to contend with. Terrence had been suspiciously quiet all ship. I'd had a private message early on that theorised Terrence was an alpha mush who infected and took control of Frieda. Checking the game log this is 100% accurate. So Terrence decided, all of a sudden (around day 10) without saying anything, that he was going to pilot the Icarus to the latest planet we'd found - here he succeeded in losing the wrench (to either strong winds or a lifeform, I forget) - he would die very soon afterwards when Neron got fed up and dropped a ceiling on him - another stroke of luck. Sadly I have to say it's not the first time I've had this scenario on a public ship - when the person who recieves the transfer says absolutely nothing about what happened, stays quiet/inactive before griefing the ship later on.
On to the good stuff. We kept firing out an expedition each day like clockwork. It was something like day 13 when Kuan Ti found a cristalite planet with volcanoes, then another promising one with seismic - we weren't willing to risk it, which cost us a day without an expedition. But the next planet we visited was well worth the wait - it had multiple artifact zones, and we found 3 artifacts including invertebrate shell, alien bottle opener (who needs a wrench anyway?) and best of all, a starmap fragment. Our incredible run of luck continued, we'd find cristalite zones on safe planets over the next 2 days, finding the last two fragments, just like that. Finola and Kuan Ti completed the calculator, and Janice was on hand to deliver the last fragment to the nexus, cheekily triangulating the route to Eden with her IT points.

For what it's worth, Frieda's parting message was "OMG that monumental fail", I'm guessing it's a reference to us not metagaming as hard as her by holding on to vital items like the medkit before mush selection. But we didn't fail, and the only monument is the one they're building of us on Eden - suck it Frieda/Terrence!

Of course people get reported and receive SDF points for holding items pre-selection. They usually don't get banned because it's minor rule-breaking and they rarely do it again after the first time. It was clear fairly early that there had to be restrictions before selection, or the game would not be fair, especially on slow-filling ships. It's the shame they had to be made official rules instead of common sense recommendations, but if it wasn't reportable, some people would do it anyway.
