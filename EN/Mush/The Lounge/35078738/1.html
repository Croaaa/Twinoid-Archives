Chun reporting in.

From the outset, things were looking to be interesting. Gioele seemed to have suffered some kind of mental breakdown after being woken up from cryo and attacked me right at the start of Day 2. Finola was using the Medlab bed to sleep in, so I ended up taking a bed in Alpha Dorm, the same dorm Gioele had decided on using. He attacked me while I slept, and if Janice hadn't recommended I take the armour, I'd have been killed on Day 2 which would have completely derailed the mission. As soon as I realised what had happened, I freaked out and fled to the Medlab to heal up, despite some pretty horrendous injuries. Once I felt safe, I announced what had happened, and not long later Roland took care of Gioele for us. Turned out Gioele was still human! That early event pretty much set the tone for me for this mission. Every decision I made was done primarily for my own survival.

I kept to myself and our research a lot. I didn't really know all too much of what was going on in the ship. I was moving from the Lab to Alpha Dorm and back again to continue research. It went a lot slower than I had intended. I noticed, however, there were a couple of incidents involving Chao and Raluca. It was after that where the Anti-Mush Committee was formed. We had all voiced our suspicions, and after pooling our knowledge and observations, we pretty much came to the consensus that Chao and Hua were the Mush. Chao had made the mistake of entering the "Raluca Cave" more than once which made us suspicious. Hua on the other hand just hadn't been moving around the ship as we would expect, and it wasn't simply a case of appearing to be an inexperienced crew member. Janice noticed the cooked ration that Hua had left outside the Refectory which was also pretty... suspect.

Apparently our suspicions weren't universal, however. It turned out that Finola had fallen for the trap and ate the ration, instantly transforming her. However, she "fought" the infection hard. I didn't realise this right away, and it was only until Janice had told me privately what had transpired. This meant that the Retro-Fungal Serum became our new priority. Also by this point a fire had broken out in the Refectory at this point and we lost 10 rations.

Unfortunately I had other, more immediate plans. After it was confirmed by our Mush double-agent that Chao and Hua were the alphas, I had to protect myself and the crew. But mostly myself. Firstly, Janice and I started deciding what to do with Finola. We needed that Serum to complete the mission, but it was clear that Finola was the only one able to make good progress on it. However, we also needed to get her out of the picture. Multiple scenarios went through my head. At the time we had discovered a planet that had some much needed oxygen and food. I thought that it would be a win-win situation if we put Hua and Finola on the mission. If either of them were killed, we'd have less mush to worry about. If not, then we'd have food and oxygen. Roland and Ian also went on the mission, which made me apprehensive about it, but as I've said earlier, I was more concerned about my own safety rather than anyone else's.

Chao was a real threat to me, despite the armour. I had reasoned that if Gioele could almost kill me while I was wearing armour, that psychopath Chao would be capable. So I had to strike first. While the away mission was in progress, I ran to the Bridge and pummelled him to death. Something inside me broke that instant, and I wanted to end the Mush threat as soon as possible. As I had exhausted myself, I couldn't finish the job, as much as I felt I needed to. Our ulterior motive for the away mission to try and end up with Hua or Finola dead didn't pan out as we had hoped. I therefore privately messaged Frieda, telling her that Hua needed to die. With my mind shattered by having been attacked by one person, and having killed someone else, my message didn't come through clearly and Frieda was hesitant at first. She caught on with what I had said and she did what was needed to be done and finished off Hua.

Then things were calm. Aside from the frequent Hunter attacks, fires breaking out across the ship, and most of the crew dying from inactivity. From this point, it was a rush to finish the Serum, and deal with Finola. I wasn't sure what I was going to do. Use the Serum on her as soon as it was finished? Kill her? I didn't know. While I was trying to research and decide what to do, the rest of the crew were trying to keep the ship from falling apart and were deciding whether we'd be able to survive for 10 days in space before returning to Sol.

One morning, I woke from having fallen asleep against the Lab's shelving unit to find that Finola had killed herself. Shrödinger had claimed another victim. And the Serum was 1% from being complete! So I finished it off. After finishing that, I finished off a couple of other research projects that had been partially completed, then turned my efforts to trying to help the rest of the crew keep the Daedalus together. Apparently I had been so concerned about surviving that I missed a suicide note written on a post-it. Raluca found it in Medlab which she had taken over as her resting spot. That was pretty sad. Your last words, written on a post-it note. Wow. That's desperation right there.

Despite my intention to help the crew put out fires, fix things, and the like, I never really had the energy to do any of it. But Raluca got beaten up pretty bad by angry machinery she was trying to fix, and the Daedalus itself trying to kill her as the hull struggled to maintain its integrity. So I did what I could to keep her alive. Fortunately that was something I was good at. Also by this point, Raluca and I were suffering from panic attacks. It seemed like I was suffering from them a lot more, though. Janice was kind enough to help me through them though, and was instrumental in keeping me from losing it.

The last few cycles were torturous. I picked up a pilot manual to learn the basics of flying a fighter craft, and went into space to try and hold off the Hunter threat. I was able to hit one of them, but it turns out that I'm not a natural at fighting. Fancy that! So I stayed in space to gather myself, and to present the enemy with another target to shoot at. Surviving that, Roland soon joined me again in space, so I landed to recover from the ordeal. Time was drawing near that we needed to jump back to Sol. I took the hacker kit to the Bridge in case our acting captain - Frieda - wasn't capable of jumping.

Day 10. Cycle 1. Roland was stuck in space. Frieda tried to help him by taking out the asteroids that were threatening the ship and buying him time to get aboard. I had jacked into the Command Console, and was ready to hit the button to jump to Sol at a moment's notice. I pleaded with Roland to get back aboard the Daedalus.

No answer but I knew he was out there still.

I ran calculations in my head. I ran what-if scenarios. I figured at the rate we were getting attacked, and the damage from the fires and internal failures were doing, we could survive one more cycle.

Again, I tried to raise Roland and get him to land. I tried so hard. I was crying and begging for him to get back aboard.

Cycle 2. Nothing. The ship was hurt badly. We couldn't keep this ship together any longer. I waited for as long as I could. Longer than I should have. Roland, you bastard! My saviour at the start of the mission. The hero who kept the Hunters off us for 10 days. I didn't want to leave you! This was the worst practical joke he had played on me! It had to be! 30 minutes remained before Cycle 3 would be upon us, but my nerves were shot. I was worn out, I was losing my sanity if I hadn't already lost it. I had to jump us out to Sol.

I don't know what happened. I didn't know if Roland knew or not. Apparently, engaging PILGRED caused Roland's patrol craft to return to the ship and he had returned to Sol with us. It was over. We could cure the Mush infection. We had a shot at saving Sol!

Mission over. I need therapy. Chun out.

I had a real blast with this mission. There was definitely a few good stories to be told about this mission, despite some very dubious moments. For myself, it was a story of panic and a downward spiral into madness. Over 10 days, I pretty much became a cross between Joan Lambert from Alien to the new incarnation of Lara Croft.

This was pretty hectic from the outset for me. First time playing as Chun. First time a ship I was on was able to reach Sol! I learnt a lot about the character and the game. The patrol craft thing at the end was particularly odd. If I had known that, I'd have jumped sooner. I wanted to keep Roland alive as he had done so much for us, and I didn't want him missing out on a Day 10 completion. Despite some rather dubious Mush play from Finola, it did create some drama for us human players. Sorry to the Alpha Mush this game. If it's any consolation, Finola told us what we had already known and you guys were marked for death before she had her... double-turncoat moment. Gioele on the other hand was an ass and clearly a griefer.

Wow! I wrote a lot!
