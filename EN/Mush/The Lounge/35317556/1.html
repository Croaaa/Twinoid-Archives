Terrence reporting in.

I'd just like to highlight that I got gold due to a very generous Paola, who gave me two of the MTPass codes she bought. And also that that gold made a very big difference for the ship, I think. Those drones became very important later on (whole bays of patrol ships breaking at once...), and Hua and I were able to swap off on shooting Araks and Trak, keeping us able to move as needed. And I had enough XP banked that I was able to unlock Shooter for Terrence around day 10, which goes a long way towards efficient piloting.

Another thing which made a big difference, from my perspective, was that we started the ship with a Sprinter mage book, which was very generously agreed upon to go to me. This allowed me to move better than the average person on the ship, as I got normal AP to MP conversion but still got my free movement out of rooms with people on them. For an example of where this helped, when the gravity sim broke on the last day, I was able to go from alpha dorm to the rear corridor to fetch the wrench and then move it all the way up to the lab, costing me only the 3 MP I had.

Jin Su's death hit me really hard; I was nearly ready to throw in the towel and start pushing for a return to Sol at that point. I also spent most of the latter half of the ship terrified of dying myself, as this was going to be the first time I actually made it back to Sol, and I really didn't want to die instead.

This ship lasted almost three times as long as my previous longest ship, and while it was great fun, I have to say I'm also somewhat relieved that it is done--it takes a lot of time and energy to keep body, soul, and ship together once you pass day 10.

And to whoever was Roland, I'm sorry. I wish we could have kept you along for the ride and cured you, too.

Hey shipmates, I was Raluca.

I showed up in MMM by random chance, just started a game at the right time I guess. My first couple days were tough. This was my first time playing Raluca, so I didn't quite have a handle on what I was supposed to do and no one wanted PILGRED to advance too far until the MUSH were killed. So I spent a lot of time hanging out in the engine room watching the chat go crazy and helping out with odd jobs. I can't really tell you much of anything about the first days, I didn't see much. I was a little intimidated at first by how expereinced everyone seemed and felt a bit ignored for awhile, but I think partially I was just used to the frenetic pace of my previous games, where a crew struggle to finish PILGRED as fast as possible to end the game while randomly killing each other in paranoia before the ship eventually was destroyed by hunters. Also, I should have enjoyed the leisurely pace while I could have.

I also didn't realize how hard it is to play Raluca since she hardly ever gets to sleep in a bed. Especially once PILGRED was done and I was trying to repair as much as possible (and suffering a ton of panic attacks)

After a few days my NERON projects came up and then I felt like I had things to do and was part of the team. Kuan Ti was helpful to me in coordinating getting projects done and he helped me finish PILGRED.

Having been through this now I have a better idea of how to play Raluca and I'd totally do it again.

I've never had a ship last as long as this, it's interesting how the strategy is different. I've never played long enough to see skills become worthless (Once all the projects are done Physicist isn't that useful anymore) and I finally got to see what people were talking about when they said the game gets harder the longer you play. The organization you need to keep a ship going is hard when it bursts into flame every cycle. I do freelance work right now, so I'm home a lot, which was good and bad. The longer the game went on the more and more I felt I had to log in and see what was going on (especially as a tech) and to make sure I wouldn't die if I was away too long. When Terrence started talking about ending the game because it was taking up so much time in real life I sympathized. But like I said before, I had never paid attention to glory before (because I'd been so used to hoping to survive) and so the longer we went the more enamored I got with the idea of lasting as long as possible (and going to Eden, and really this is the first ship I was in that it was even a possibility to do that).

I'm really happy I got to play such an active ship, and we generally worked together pretty well, we finished a lot of research that I've never seen finished before on a ship (pretty much all of it), we even managed to cure a mush (which works out better for a ship if you can swing it because the longer you go the more active players you need, it's hard to imagine not having Stephen he was indispensable as the game progressed). Everyone helped out from time to time, even Frieda the few times she was active, so that was nice.

I would probably do an MMM again, an active ship is just so much better than one that's half full.

Thanks everyone, and especially Hua. You might have been a little over communicative, but your knowledge helped keep us alive. And thanks for starting this thread. It was nice to play with y'all. I hope we get to play together again.
