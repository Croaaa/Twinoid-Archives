Thoughts of Stephen, late at night sitting in his beach chair. The rising tides are slowly taking over the part of the beach where he placed his chair an hour earlier :

This isn’t the wild party I had planned. Everyone is too busy working to enjoy the triumph of the return home. I suppose the work is needed. The serum we created will need to be produced in large scale and fast if we really want a victory over the mush. Ian and Chun barely spoke of anything else all night. Can you imagine such a nice beach and Ian didn’t even try the waves?

I hear Chao’s voice loudly ordering people around. He truly became our commander, for which I’m grateful. I can handle him better this way than his previous role of torturer. I’ve never seen such horrible and effective methods. If any mush ever attempt to infiltrate our group again they don’t stand a chance as long as he’s our leader.

As for the rest of my crew … I can’t believe I think of them as my crew. I hopped aboard with no clue what I was getting myself into, with no connection to anyone, just finding an escape and now I’m surrounded by my crew. Surrounded by my friends?

Even Raluca, she looked extremely uncomfortable sitting at the table for dinner earlier tonight, but she absolutely wanted to be part of it. She might not enjoy our company, but she still doesn’t want to be without it either. It all too complicated for me. Luckily Janice was sitting next to her often whispering in hushed tones some advice or whatever sorceries a shrink uses.

Also Eleesha who we tortured to the brink of death for a small but significant mistake she made aboard the daedalus is here now, as part of the group, smiling. I spotted her chatting with Frieda. No-nonsense Frieda, who was ready to kill Eleesha for suspected mushiness after her mistake with the list, is now chatting with her as if they have been friends for years. At least I think Frieda shouting at someone is how she talks to her friends too.

Gioele is a great host. It seems he slowly starts to trust me a little, as long as I’m far enough away from his daughter at least. I’m glad we forced him to let us use his beach mansion for this party. He raised the toast to Hua. I barely knew the girl, why did I almost shed a tear then??

And then there is Paola. She looked amazing again tonight. She spend most of the night talking into that weird radio of hers, I listened to her talk most of all. I’m not sure what exactly she was discussing, I must admit I was more interested in hearing her lovely voice than in the exact words she was saying. I don’t think she’ll ever notice me, or take me serious. Even if she never does, I’m happy I helped her survive that terrible and wonderful ship. Ugh I somehow turned into a sentimental old fool in just a few days time. Even just thinking about her now makes me imagine I can hear her voice. Wait, no. I really hear her voice. There she is, walking barefoot through the sand. Maybe I should try one more time to get her to notice me. I’ll go to her take away that radio of hers and force her to really notice me for once. Just the two of us in the moonlight on this beach…
