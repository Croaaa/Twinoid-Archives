Oooh, I remember that one! It was such a fun to twirl my moustache as villainous-yet-human Commander Seagull.
I even found my last post from this ship:
Stephen Seagull gets up from his bed and walks to the dorm shelf. He picks up a knife from it and take a book out of his pocket. It’s the Commander’s manual. Casually, he guts a binding of the book and pulls out folded piece of paper. Then he moves over sleeping Andie’s bed. You served me well, little child. But you can’t help me with what comes next. Stephen looks at Andie and his knife like if pondering something, but then just walks out of the dorm.

In the bravo bay, Stephen rumages behind the shelf for a while pulling out blaster from the place where he hid it before. Holstering it, he continues his walk through empty corridors toawrds the bridge. On his way, he unfolds the piece of paper. It’s a list of names. Some are crossed out, amongst them the names of Kim Jin Su and Gioele Rinaldo. But there are others left. Like GL Maubrick... or Kivanç Terzi. Stephen smiles but there’s only a little of joy in that expression. Still more work to do, still more work...

On the bridge, Stephen folds the paper and puts it back into his pocket. He ignores snoring Frieda on the sofa and walks over to the Command terminal. He runs his hand over the smooth surface of the console and his smile widens as he puts in the cooridnates of Sol system, Cygni rebel base. But it’s time for another stage. Yes, it is, indeed. Soon it will be done. And then... Stephen’s face hardens. The coordinates are set. And he pushes the jump button.
