And thread reopened. It turns out that the ship lasted much longer than you expected, Hua, since we just went back to Sol : http://mush.twinoid.com/theEnd/8554 .

Stephen here, proud human mush human commander of an amazing crew. This was probably one of the most exciting ship I have had in a long time. Human until D3C8, I got double-spiked and converted under Defacer by mush Ian. He was actually the main suspect at the time due to his inactivity (despite being Gold and high level), but we hadn't had time to kill him yet. Raluca, the other alpha, was also suspected since she never left her turret, but again we hadn't acted on our suspicions, too busy with getting the first anti-mush research and killing inactive Jin Su, who was a liability.

So here I was, newly converted with two active partners, and with an active human crew who raised the alarm straight away. Ian transferred to inactive Roland (whose last words are so pathetic that I almost died laughing when I read them) and took off in the Pasiphae with three oxygen capsules. Humans immediately started to rush projects, and a few cycles later Paola tried to do a Neron upgrade and immediately got Magnetic Net. Soon after that, Eleesha found a planet with oxygen. The mush cursed the RNG, and tried to think of a plan. The best idea would have been to bring Roland back into the ship at the last minute, move to the planet, and let him steal the Icarus. But instead, we decided to lose the planet, steal the remaining oxygen, have Raluca transfer to Janice and sabotage O2. So be it.

It would have been a great plan anyway, except that... Raluca apparently sabotaged an oxygen tank and a fuel tank, and stole capsules before transferring. I moved the ship, then Roland and I rushed to BD protected by new Janice's ceasefire, but I only had one capsule and Janice had none. Worse, Raluca was still alive and holding capsules, and it took much longer than expected for our O2 to reach zero.

We snuggled in our beds for a few cycles, trying to plan our next move, while humans were plotting actively and everyone exchanged various threats. We suddenly noticed that someone had loaded fuel, and guessed that they had found another oxygen planet (that was to be expected...). This time, we couldn't let them get away with it. Janice sneaked out of BD to steal the last oxygen capsules, while I declared another ceasefire, having apprenticed Diplomat. At cycle change, Hua died of asphyxiation. Janice showered, rushed out of BD again, unlocked piloting, and ran to Icarus Bay. Unfortunately, Terrence had time to join her and they both went to the planet. Janice got lost, Terrence got injured, then he got oxygen and found Janice... who was lost again when the Icarus came back. Roland and Terrence played cat and mouse, and eventually Roland managed to kill Terrence. Then, despite me telling him that someone had already loaded fuel to abandon Janice, he went back to IB and launched the Icarus again. Sure enough, humans had hacked again within minutes, and both Janice and Roland were dead.

That's when I started to feel juuuust a little uncomfortable. I had no weapons, almost no AP, no shooting points, no spores. I knew that if I left BD, I would be killed immediately, and that when ceasefire ended at day change, they would all pounce on me. I couldn't kill, convert or transfer, and I couldn't even fly away since they had Magnetic Net. Raluca was screaming blue murder at me, and Derek and Chun were sleeping next to me, armed to the teeth. So I stayed in bed, clutching the teddy bear and hoping that I would run fast enough to act before getting killed. But when I came back before day change, it was only to discover that Raluca had geniused the cure... and I was human again.

I must admit that it was one of the most unsettling situations, and I was feeling as confused in real life as poor Stephen. Human mush hunter, then mush plotting against his ex human allies, then human again, forced to deal with the consequences of his actions, having lost humans and mush partners... All within less than eight cycles. An intense day indeed...

After that, things settled down, despite our difficult situation. We had lost all pilots but one, our shrink, two techs, as well as the Icarus. We did have one pilot left and a pilot magebook, though, and of course Raluca was a tech. Expos were all the more difficult as Andie got lost on his first solo expo, and by the time I was ready to rescue him (after facing a shipwreck combat, ouch), he died of depression. But we never gave up, and managed to finish Pilgred, keep the crew alive, and even do a few expos before going back to Sol on D11.

Once again, thanks a lot to everyone, on both sides. Roland and Janice, I had great fun during that mushy day. Hua, Terrence, Andie, you gave your life to save them us. Frieda, I am sorry that you died so early. And everyone else, Derek, Chun, Kuan Ti, Eleesha, Paola (I loved RPing with you), and of course my Raluca ♥, you were all amazing.

We're back The ship just returned to Sol Day 11, Cycle 1: http://mush.twinoid.com/theEnd/8554. There's a few more details I can add from the human side, but Janice covered most of it.

First few days were pretty quiet. We had an inactive Gioele (whose death was very traumatic to poor Paola, but she bravely suffered through it!) and a mute Jin who Stephen and Derek got rid of; but everyone else was at least semi-active. Roland, Ian, and Raluca were all quiet, and Frieda took a while to join in but she did start to work eventually. For a long time, there wasn't much being said about the Mush; some (Stephen mainly) were suspicious of Ian for not helping out a lot despite being Gold, but there were no solid leads. I was active and consistent on Comms, while Projects and Research were moving at an okay pace. The only matter of concern was our dwindling reserves of Oxygen.
And then it happened. Bravo Dorm was defaced, Ian was murdered, and Roland had run off in the Pasiphae! Terrence, Stephen, and Roland had all been in the dorm when it happened. Stephen, with Kuan Ti vouching for him, reported that Ian had moved to the front of the ship, come back to BD, and defaced the dorm. Stephen claimed that once he saw Defacer, he started attacking Ian and he didn't notice when he transferred into Roland and ran away. Terrence fled the room as soon as saw the defacing too, which made things a real headache. Terrence and Stephen were both online, they both offered the same telling of what happened, with the conclusion that both of them were human.
I thought that they were both Mush, thinking Terrence and Roland/Ian had coordinated to convert Stephen. One important piece of information arrived right before the Defacer incident: I unlocked Finola's List from Xyloph, and it proved that me, Hua, Eleesha, and Kuan Ti were human. Oh, and one more person...Stephen! Since I knew that Stephen wasn't an Alpha, I was convinced he was converted during that attack. I didn't believe he had killed Ian by accident, and couldn't accept that no one had been converted. But there was no proof. Eleesha tried to help out by checking logs, but she would only post disconnect snippets, and some of her deductive info contradicted what we knew really happened. She provided more confusion than answers, honestly. We concentrated on getting rid of the Mush we were sure about, and on addressing our bigger problem, Oxygen. Eleesha helped out by finding our first O2 planet, and Terrence, Hua and Kuan Ti began to rush projects in a search for Magnetic Net, completing Plasma Shield in the process. At the end of the night, once they had failed to reach it, I tried a NERON upgrade and BAM! Magnetic Net.
So we had a planet, the net to retrieve the Pasiphae, everyone knowing that Roland needs to die...it seemed like things were looking up! Of course, we still had a compromised Commander, and I was a bit worried when we still hadn't moved or had fuel loaded when I logged off for the night. But it wasn't like calling Stephen out on it would've helped anything. When I returned in the morning, the planet had been missed, and Roland was safely under a ceasefire called by Janice (originally Raluca) along with Stephen in Bravo Dorm. One of those mornings.
Thankfully, the human team really pulled together here, and things could have been a lot trickier if even one of them hadn't shown up. Eleesha and Derek were ready to kill Janice once the ceasefire was up, but they mistimed it and Eleesha had to log off with one more cycle of peace remaining. Next to show up was Hua, who managed to find us another O2 planet, and then got ready to launch an expedition to retrieve it. We had 2 O2 left, so if we could get to the planet next cycle, no one would die. Hua and I loaded the fuel, Chun used the Hacker Kit, and we were on our way! I moved to FC to guard, though I was a little too fast for Chun and she got stuck in FC. But that helped us, since it created the illusion that there were 2 guards to stop our Mushy Commander from ruining our expedition. Unfortunately, Janice moved out of BD to grab two more units of Oxygen; Derek was unable to catch her, and now we had 0 going into the next cycle. Who died? Spoiler is in the thread title.
By now, everyone was on the same page about who could and couldn't be trusted. Before she died, Hua had contacted Terrence, Andie, Frieda, and Derek. Terrence stepped up to lead the expedition in Hua's place, moving quickly after he noticed that Janice was showering to lower her HP. There wasn't really any doubt at this point, but he confirmed that Stephen was Mush by reporting that he had copied Diplomat from Janice. Terrence and Janice became our unlikely rescue team; they found O2 on the very first zone, but Terrence had only 9 HP at launch. Janice had 3 HP, but she got lost on the very next zone. It was a really tense hour for the humans as we watched Terrence navigate a planet alone, through 3 undiscovered zones, with just over half a bar of HP. But it wasn't all bad; thanks to the ceasefires, Humans and Mush had nearly a day to fling threats and openly tease the other side.

Finally, Terrence came back without Janice. I ran to IB to kill her since he had found her earlier, but since she got lost again on the last zone, my job became to save Terrence instead. Except, Roland was waiting for him too; he got Terrence down to 1 HP before he escaped into Bravo Dorm, where Stephen had used his Apprentice skill to copy Diplomat and call a second ceasefire. I headed to BD and prepared to escort Terrence to the medlab to heal him, since Janice had hidden the medikit. Roland didn't say much, but he was more active than we anticipated; as soon as Terrence left BD, he followed him. He was able to kill him before I even arrived. That was a big mistake on my part, since I should have already been in BB waiting for Terrence in the first place. It's not like I delayed more than a few seconds after Terrence moved either; Roland was fast, and I just didn't even think that he was waiting to attack Terrence again. Sorry Terrence!

At this point, I asked Derek to load the fuel so we could leave orbit. Roland surprised everyone by launching an expedition right after the fuel was loaded. This made us pause; Derek didn't want to lose the Icarus, but I insisted that Roland bringing Janice back on to the ship would be much worse. Luckily Frieda came back online, and I convinced her to hack us out of orbit. Finally, six cycles after the defacing, we had our first dead Mush, two of them no less! At this point, we still didn't know if Raluca had Genius, so the plan was to rush Mushovore to stop Stephen from converting anyone (we had no idea he was hurting for AP), and for Derek to finish him off once the ceasefire ended. But then, Raluca returned and revealed her trump card, she was a Genius! Janice had locked Research so that she could try and sabotage the expedition, so I had to grab the Hacker Kit and change the BIOS myself (Eleesha was afk). I failed, but Andie came through with the ration I needed to finish the job, Raluca completed the serum and then the battle was over.

Mush, you played really well! Keeping Raluca alive turned out to be your undoing, but even that wouldn't have cost you if the humans hadn't worked together so well. In a refreshing change from games that have only a handful of major players, every single surviving human contributed in some significant way to fighting back against you. Well done, everyone, human and Mush alike.
Oh, and a special thanks to Stephen for all the late night RP. Paola and Stephen's strange surrogate father-daughter relationship kept things fun long after the Mush hunt!
