It's not only MMM ships that 'make it happen'. This ship started to fill about 3 hours after the recent MMM jump; it was full just before the end of D1. 2 were inactive and died; both mush were caught and died; we failed to save Chao from accident damage on D17... but D19C2 we reached eden.

But for the first few days of the ship, I didn't think we had any real chance of getting past D5 at all.

I was Finola, initially level 3. I became a diplomat part-way through the journey.

We started off with swift anti-mush research, primarily Chun and I working on it; others focused on projects. Mush Ian focused on holding sample and apron, but not before gas was made at least. But Ian never went into a room by himself, he'd never had access to a shower, he was clean; so Ian never spored. Ian tried to trick me, telling me he held apron and sample to protect them; when we finally had the sample back and broke into the mush channel he was telling his partner, Terrence, that I believed him, he was fooling me...

Terrence gave himself away by having slept beside Janice for many cycles, then going to the lab to hug the cat then back to bed beside Janice and shower. Not terribly believable... we slew him before we finished Ian... but man was it a job to kill Ian, as it always is.

Mush channel showed just 2 infections, both level 1, both from room traps. Not terribly high intensity from their conversations there, they'd only had about 40 or so hours to make spores and antispore gas had been up within 15 minutes of the ship filling up; constipaspore followed swiftly. We decided that mush were probably no longer a problem, the number of spores were not either; though we did quickly finish mushovore 'just in case', but the rest of the antimush stuff was disregarded.

It took a bit to get the sofa up, Hua was our only Tech but there were some difficulties (did she even have the skill at start of play?), so it was sometime D2 or 3 before we even had the sofa. And there was some friction between Frieda and Paola, culminating in Paola, who had been sleeping in medlab for a few cycles before and after the sofa got built, running to the bridge and punching Frieda, who had just started using the untouched, being-wasted sofa, so Paola could take her sofa. Sigh.

And before Ian started 'safekeeping' the apron, Chao had been randomly torturing people; just wandering around near the front of the ship. You were clean and the roomlog showed you'd never had a chance to be dirty? No matter; torture here, torture there. Pop, pop. You never left a room with a camera? Oh well, torture, a few cycles later torture again.

Happily our mush made themselves obvious earlyish; Chao got a clearly appropriate and challenging target to direct all his attention to in Ian! Which went far in reassuring the rest of us that Chao was himself actually human. But several of us had a temper, I rather expected our ship to break into a few different sets of quarreling people who didn't trust each other.

As for me, I made a 'non-griefer' thread very, very early. I invited 3 others to it: Chun, whose no-nonsense attitude and focus on anti-mush research warmed my heart; Jin Su, who seemed wise, patient, interested, and communicative, and Roland, who was absolutely and totally an enthusiastic newbie. These people acted like themselves before and after mush were picked; very soon after we added Gioele, who had been our 16th to join the ship and who swiftly provided us with cameras once he had gotten his eyes open from cryosleep, and Chao, who was level 5 at least (he had crazy eye!), picked skills after mush were selected, and then seemed.... utterly unstrategic in how he used torture at least. But he did have a good heart, he wasn't a griefer... and he proved not to be a mush. Later in the ship he was darn useful, as was absolutely everyone, even those who struggled with language, struggled with lag (this ship happened during the terrible lag times! we too almost lost people who couldn't eat from the inability to load the game.... but only almost).

This ship was a great lesson. You don't have to be perfect to reach Eden, not even close to it. We had waste, we had greed, we had confusion, human-on-human aggression, several people who could only log in 1-3 times a day; we lost a crystallite+O2 planet to button pressing errors, we got scared of planets that were a giant mix of good and bad when time was tight and we barely could afford to find another... when the chips were down we went to a planet the same size and slightly more lethal than the one we refused before... and got that 3rd starmap piece.

We had 6 fires in one cycle; we had a cycle when 4 people in the same dorm each got their own metal plate (the same room had a roomshake then too); that same cycle had 4 other people in other rooms also get plates and roomshakes (really, Neron? Do you actually have that many plates, really?)

We had 4 drones, 3 turboed, 2 firefighters... one of the firefighters wasn't turbo.... We had people who played fast and 'wrong' when barely awake; we had projects getting finished (by humans) which were not the projects that were selected as the best to do -

And we had heroism. We had a Roland, a newbie, who about a day before the end, when we wiser folks finished an expo that could have gotten us a starmap but didn't said: Looks like we failed, there's 7 O2 left and 2 broken O2 tanks and very little AP, next to no food left, so how about that glory research then?

And when experienced heads said, 'Yeah, lets wrap up then', our newbie Roland logs in, looks around - sees the planet we are beside, and misunderstands. Roland doesn't realize that we tried the expo and failed to get a starmap, that we have next to nothing left.... oh no. Roland does understand that we're settling down to the end because of O2 tanks and low O2.... but Roland wants to try and wants us to try and wow, so much enthusiasm and fire and spirit....

He gets me to shift gears, some of the rest of us follow.... we manage with low odds to get the O2 tanks fixed... we get fuel loaded and get away from the planet.... Oh, poor Roland.... it was only then that I understood that he thought he was fighting for us to have the strength of will to go to that planet beside us, the one we'd already finished and failed at.

And then we did find another planet. A slightly darker twin to the one wiser players had, 5 or so days before, refused to try (18 zones, 1 crystal, 1 mankarog, O2, lots of stuff that could damage or help), but now we really couldn't refuse this one (18 zones, 1 crystal, 1 mankarog, no O2, lots of stuff that could damage or help).

We tried it. We got the starmap, the third piece we needed. With 3 O2 left and almost no AP, almost everyone full, we got the calculator research finished. We got the commander terminal fixed in the middle of the burning bridge. We got the calculations settled. Jin Su managed to take us home.


Ours was a ship full of stories. I've barely touched them here, and left about 90% of the events of our ship unspoken, at least as yet. It was a ship of greatness and pettiness, of frustration, of sublime successes, of learning, of unbelievable low odds, and of luck. Good luck, bad luck, ever so much luck. I was probably the dirtiest Finola ever (who was not a mush, that is). Come on out and take your bows, fellow teammates. We sure played out an incredible trip and a huge swarm of stories!
