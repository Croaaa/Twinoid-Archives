Good evening, mush, humans, bots, and Groots. This is Ian Soulton speaking.

Our ship lunatic asylum landed on Eden yesterday after twenty days of peaceful trip pure madness through space. I hope that the first building on the planet will be a psychiatric hospital, because almost everyone, down to the shrink herself, was wildly insane.

We also found out that Chun was really a bot, whose presence aboard and creation remain quite a mystery. We tried to solve that mystery while teaching her how to behave properly (like teaching her not to strip in public, or explaining that people did not need to be upgraded), until the day she... er... did something unspeakable to Terrence. As a revenge, he "fixed" her, and she started beeping and bumping into walls. Then, for some reason, she said something about being a potential bomb, but then she got pregnant from Kuan Ti, which prevented her from exploded. Don't ask me to explain. I have no idea what happened there, and I'm waiting impatiently for her explanations.

Even Gioele, reasonable, strong, level-headed Gioele, had some disturbing hallucinations...

All this madness culminated in the Groot Wars, which nearly turned the daily gardening sessions into a bloodbath. Groot was originally a banana tree kidnapped by Kuan Ti, who believed that the plant was his best friend and refused to return him, forcing me to take drastic measures. But Kuan Ti refused to surrender Groot.

Forgetting his resentment against "Rolando", Stephen turned against "Kuan Tilando" the plant murderer, and the heated discussion soon became a shouting match, eventually ending in an epic fight in the garden.

Somehow, we managed to survive this without any murder, and after that terrible scene, all our plants were named Groot. Or Bat Groot. I do hope that we will have a thriving Groot plantation on the Eden colony.

Now, having told the story of the ship (at least the story as I know it, but there are gaping holes in it, because dark things happened on that ship), I will tell you my personal story.

The pressure grew ever greater, and Derek hadn't really wanted to keep up a charade in the first place.

So he went to Frieda, asking about how to access the Collective mind, perhaps to find her there. She had begun to instruct him when she was tragically lost on a planet.

Lost, he continued his work and tried to work out his identity issues a little. Gioele began gathering a "Family" of trusted individuals, and saw fit to invite Derek in spite of everything. Deep inside, Gio's intense demands for loyalty made Derek a bit nervous, but it was really nice to be in the know on something. Around this time, Ian, revealed his true identity to Derek... who initially took it for a joke. Once convinced that Ian wasn't kidding, and that the Crimson Society worked for good, he was eager to begin training. He already had been feeling a need to take some control over his situation (he had begun working out prior to this), and this was a way he could do it, and help others.

The Patuline Scrambler was discussed, but Derek had only just begun to speak with Paola, and protested. It was delayed.

But it wasn't too long before Finola asked for lab assistance, and it came out quickly that she was indeed working on the pheromone scrambler. Derek loudly protested, and Finola took quick action.

Being cured was... a traumatic event for Derek. Ever since Finola had made the most of Frieda's death, Derek had intended to use it at some point, but not yet, and not without mental preparation. Ian helped him through it with a bit of booze, and Gioele with a thoughtful gift as he slept it off.

Meanwhile Gioele also announced his intention to return, and the means he had to counter the numerical advantage of the hostile Mush.

Finally, Derek agreed to help Ian and Gioele restrain Finola, intending to go back to Sol as protection for Gioele. He ended up feeling rather badly about it, but stuck to his guns.
