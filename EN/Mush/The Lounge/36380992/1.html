But Mightymif, there -are- counterstrategies to item theft. Not perfect ones, but there are choices.

First off, be alert and aware. Read roomlogs, watch for oddness, and communicate with each other. Even if you don't know who is human yet or not, you can still work as a team. Noticing who is not working with the team can be informative.

It is vital to know that you have a problem immediately, ideally the cycle it goes wrong. You need to start fixing that problem immediately. Someone needs to follow the trail of the 'wrong-acting person', and different people (to spread out the possible spores) or Chun need to search along it, in case items were just hidden (or some items were just hidden). Know what is lost, then if the 'problem' is that the person ejected from the craft, rejoice because it means that one of the lost items was a spacesuit (right? I've never worn one into space, and I've never had an eject option.... so spacesuits are required for it, right?) so you only lost 2 other more useful items.

But if there's a chance that person didn't eject, you have a chance to lose nothing at all. You have days for the entire ship to relay in the Nexus, this is probably happening early game, so your entire ship can focus not on problems, but on Projects (with Paola asked to focus with food on Neron upgrades, and perhaps your core research team still focused on anti-mush research).

Get magnetic Net and move. Before you do, consider trying to talk with your 'problem', if they are a mush, if they do have the communicator still, they might well be willing to 'play'. "Gosh, I sure hope So-and-so doesn't come back and spore me! He's got to have a lot of AP now, all these cycles he's been hiding in that scrapship". If he is mush and doesn't have the communicator, his partner might let him know what's being said, he might get bored himself and want to come see what the ship is doing (after all, until he dies, he canot create a new character, assuming he's a mush and not a cheater who breaks all the rules).

See if you can catch your 'problem' landing and messing around on the ship again.

If your ship is not able to organize enough to do this - accept it. Work with what you do have, do what you actually can, and embrace the fact that if it really was crippling, your ship will die sooner, releasing you honorably from a very difficult to play situation. But struggle with it, play with it, try to keep your ship alive and moving towards success no matter what the challenges you face are.

It can make you a better player, help you see options and ways to be more efficient that you never noticed before, and ultimately improve your future ships, and possibly the future ships of everyone working with you, because they have a chance to learn new things too.
