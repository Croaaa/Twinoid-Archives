Not from the ship, but as I can see, you had GPU project. It is bugged, what GPU does is not "prevent events from happening", as it would seem to in the description but it instead delays when they occur. If you have it trigger a few times in a row, the bad events get delayed and stockpiled for the next cycle, in which they may potentially be delayed again. The next time GPU fails to stop any bed events, you'll have more of them happening at once in that cycle.

The same can be said of Trail Minimizer and hunters. The hunters you think you left behind will just catch up to you in a few cycles, and if you move the ship a lot without killing them, you can end up with 30-40 hunters every new wave, before the end of Day 8 or Day 9.

Share this info on your future ships please.

Wasn't on this ship, but offering an answer to Frontalis:

It varies hugely from ship to ship and player to player. Some factors that matter:

How active is the ship? If only a few people are very active, they may eat constantly for more AP to get everything done, they also know that there's fewer people eating, so less need to conserve food.

Is someone doing something that they really want to get done as fast as possible? Communication officer, people rushing antimush research, people dealing with hunter waves and scrap collection, and people rushing Pilgred often eat to get more done faster. Other momentary urgent needs, such as killing a discovered mush, healing suddenly wounded people, or similar emergencies may prompt some rushed meals.

Is the ship having great luck with many mature plants and lots of steaks being found on expos? If the ship has an extraordinary amount of food, people might be encouraged to eat to get everything done faster.

Is someone especially greedy? Some people, having realized that eating gives more AP, think they should eat as often as they can, or believe that as in real life, you need to eat every day to avoid bad effects (Mush allows 3 days between eating anything substantial before you start to have bad effects), without realizing (or perhaps caring) that there's only so much food.

You asked somewhat about roles and eating; that's not necessarily fixed and different people handle things differently even when playing the same role.
