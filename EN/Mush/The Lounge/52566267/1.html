Things were almost too perfect to be true, honestly, I have never seen a ship like that. There isn't much to tell about what happened during the trip itself. And NO, you can say all you want, Ian, Eleesha was not killed.

After finding and curing Kuan Ti, the trip was almost ridiculously easy. We had the three starmaps and Eden coordinates by D8. Then we went on exploring safe planets for a few days, just for fun, while finishing projects and all possible research. Initially, we had decided to last until D15 because the game usually becomes too difficult and exhausting after that... but it did not. Day after day, our AP overflew, the kitchen became a sea of leftover coffees (that's what the refectory looked like... on D19 !), while the crew worked like clockwork. Within minutes of every cycle change, we always had several crew members who leapt out bed, checked and reported incidents, and then fought over the extinguishers, the medikit or even the watering cans. Pilots awaited hunters space fairies impatiently and jumped into patrol ships as soon as they appeared. Every cycle, we expected the RNG to finally get angry, and it never did. Not that it would have mattered, given the ludicrous amount of AP, coffees, jumpkins, rations and missions we had in store.

That's one of the things that made the ship exceptional, and on the other hand, we had one of the most amazing crews I have ever seen. 13 active or very hyperactive crew members, highly efficient and yet perfectly disciplined, whether they were old TE hands or less experienced players. It was just unbelievable.

And then, as Paola said, there as the RP. Crazy, constant, fantastic RP, in which almost everyone joined. Everyone, including secondary characters, like our plants, which had all pirate names, or Mortimer and Mr Squawkers, the refectory kraken (no, not Gioele), Curly Sue the Tapeworm... And so many wonderful characters, like our two fitness coaches, our piloting team (Black Box, Hot Wheels, Red Moon ♥, Pink Tiger and our honorary member, Coach), schizophrenic Raluca, witch Frieda, and our insufferable Gioele, that even Neron hated to much that it kept locking him in the refectory (yes, BB, we love you too).
