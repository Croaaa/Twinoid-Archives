The support drone's engines glowed and hummed as the device meandered about Bravo Bay. It was one of the few animated objects on the Daedalus at the moment. And also, it was the last companion of Frieda Bergmann.

She was found sitting curled up, hugging her knees, next to a shelf. She stared into the distance over her glasses, not moving, slowly breathing. The smell of smoke, fire burning the synthetic materials the ship was made of, and the slightly fungal scent still left behind by the Mush - reminding anyone who dared consider, that the ship was in a terrible situation. However, Frieda didn't dare consider. She had done her considering, hours ago. Now she was sitting. Staring. Waiting for the unknown, the endless black void - would it be void? What would death be like? She knew it was coming. She had figured that out when she woke up from a nap earlier and found the bridge filled with fire.

Frieda had run through the ship with astonishing speed for an old lady - discovering that her remaining human companions Paola Rinaldo and Zhong Chun were asleep in a flaming dorm, and she couldn't wake them. She figured Raluca Tomescu, the ship's physicist, was also in a flaming room - but she couldn't go find out. She had to find an extinguisher. Frieda ran through the rooms of the ship though her age-old legs failed her, she lurched from room to room desperately checking the storage containers...
No extinguisher. No nothing.
The Mush must have hidden it.

And now Frieda sat, curled up, staring. Why did she come on this space voyage? Why did she let them freeze her and fly her to the Magellan system? What was happening in Sol now?

The support drone hovered about, its engines emitting a low whining. The only sound accompanying the distant roar of flames getting ever closer. Smoke leaking through the doors. The blasts of hunter rays tearing the ship's hull. Cracking... cracking and snapping and a huge-

Now Frieda couldn't hear. Everything was quiet and strangely so, like there was no sound, no air - she couldn't breathe - she wasn't breathing...
