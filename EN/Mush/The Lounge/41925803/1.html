When everyone awoke, we were met with the reality that there wasn't a single person aboard with the Technician skill. Everyone who normally has Tech was bronze and level 1, except for me who was silver with level 2. So both showers were active for the ship's duration, and the sofa BP went unbuilt.

Jin Su and Frieda were the alpha mush, but Frieda was active maybe once (went to the bridge and asked about CPU priority) and started drooling thereafter. Still, the captain was able to pull off a conversion, the final spike of which he actually performed in front of a camera. We should have guessed that was the case sooner, but we assumed he was sloppy and were thankful when his bridge grenade was largely ineffective (didn't kill anyone outright, only broke a door). Janice beat him up and Eleesha finished him off.

But the next cycle brought a precision strike steel plate on the injured Paola, the only active person on the bridge, and Eleesha immediately went to fill the void. Meanwhile we were gearing up for an expo because O2 was getting critical. Our newbie Raluca had eaten the Astrophysicist magebook so scanning was slow since she had to hide away from people to gain AP. Luckily the planet she was working on was good - O2, Forest, Intelligent Life, Predators, Ruminants, Insects... not terrifically safe, but not evil.

Janice was set to accompany us because Andie's activity was spotty at best, and that's when mushling Terrence made his move, mutating and killing her. I swatted him a bit, connecting once before I had to back off - the expo had to go on, and I wanted to be sure I had AP to launch. Roland ended up finishing him off, and we just went on the expo with Janice's slot empty. The combat damage worked out okay and we got 16 O2, but Roland went missing and we found him under a rock. It was a shame; he was probably the most entertaining Roland I've served with so far.

The next few days were the exhausting ones. There were so many inactives on the ship, I began to despair of us just dying a slow death, pecked to death by Hunters. Eleesha and I carefully checked the inactives first before looking into active mush suspects, since with only 5 actives left, we wanted to be sure. Frieda ended up being the only inactive not taking starving damage at cycle change. Her death was cheap too, as she must have been reasonably hurt by the grenade days before. Now Eleesha-certified mush free, we focused on shaking Hunters. My AP for a couple days went into sniping Aracks from the pack, but then fires started to hit and I had to burn AP on that instead. I tried to get bossy in chat about priorities and goals, but most of the other non-droolers were only active once every few cycles and couldn't hop on urgent things. Finally, Eleesha pulled Raluca and I into a PM and organized a PILGRED rush. Oxygen was finally getting back down to uncomfortable levels, and the hull was starting to become a problem. It was do or die.

We met in the engine room D8C1, and that PILGRED rush was probably one of the most exciting cycles I've had on a ship. After days of vaguely hoping for a human Sol victory but watching a whole lotta nothing happen, the three of us took PILGRED from ~47% to completion. Raluca hugged her cat (freed from the grip of the finally-starved Chun) to celebrate the victory. Late the next cycle, Commander Kuan Ti took our smoking wreck home.

Hope to see some of my other comrades check in. Especially from the mush side: you guys were sneaky enough (or we were blind enough) that you had the luxury of acting instead of being forced to react. I'm especially interested in the "surprises" that Jin Su boasted about leaving around the ship before his demise. A bluff, or not?
