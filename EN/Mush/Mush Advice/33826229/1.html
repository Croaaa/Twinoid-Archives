My answer, as someone who might have to play beside you someday - the line is 'where it matters'.

Does your ship have tons of rations and everything going smoothly, expos flowing so you guys have food up to your ears? Then pig out in the name of RP all you want - you are not playing against your team to do so because it's not going to do any harm. If you guys don't 'have more food than you can eat' and it's looking like you won't anytime soon, have your 'pigging out' be virtual food only, not coded food. Write up a few awesome RP posts about your character running to the refractory and struggling to scrape the last few bits of gravy off the side of someone's discarded carton. Of stalking someone going to eat, of your emotions and turmoil and behavior as you agonize while watching them, your frazzled self control barely held in check as you think about how soon there won't be any more food to eat and how you cannot stand it. Note that you don't even have to actually move to do this - and shouldn't. Let it be virtual movement as well. Save your coded resources for playing with your coded team.

That punch you want your character to throw can be virtual too. In my opinion, hostility rarely makes the playing enviromnent more pleasant overall - use it in tiny doses and assume that it's a Very Strong Spice, with just a hint flavoring the entire experience a great deal, and more than the smallest amount often Ruining The Dish.

On my current ship, I was 14th aboard and got Racula. I started heavily with RP, then dropped it entirely. The ship's full of pretty new players for the most part - we had one seemingly experienced guy and a griefer wasted supplies and ensured that player's death (possibly accidentally, but the griefing was real enough and laced with enough racial and sexual profanity to make it clear this was going way beyond the limits of appropriate play even for a Mush (the griefer was human).

But I had to drop my RP because I was confusing my fellow players, and they were already desperately confused. A ship on D2 with only 1 camera installed - and that one not in either dorm? No dirty thread? No research or project threads?

I tentatively tried to change my RP, to post 'putting up notes' for people to follow - but that wasn't blunt enough either and people were still highly confused - they can't all be mush, it's barely D3 now. Impossible they are -all- mush, though they're playing so newbie that unless the mush are that new, I may indeed log in one day to find myself Last Human, sheesh.

Point being though - it was appropriate and reasonable for me on this ship to drop my RP entirely. Throw it away, because it wasn't what the ship needed. Remember, we are forbidden to 'play against the team' - or to 'surrender', and surrendering to your urge to knowingly be 'bad' as a human is indeed surrender and playing against your team - if you use coded resources for it. If you keep it to words only, and if you are not confusing the heck out of everyone around you in ways that are getting the others to waste resources or get a bad feel from the hostility - then it's awesome, do it more!

There's not really a lot of roleplay liberties. Most people are here for the game first, and don't appreciate roleplay. Many even get annoyed with excessive chatter. I keep my roleplays limited to derailing unimportant topics. A lot of the hardcore players don't quite appreciate roleplaying either... and that's when it's harmless. TV shows are full of drama with crazy characters, but sadly, Mush can't go that far with good players.

Also note that a lot of people on games like this (casual, on 5 minutes a day) have poor OOC/IC separation. They'll consider your actions as actions by the player, not the character.

I'd like to see a roleplay ship or one with less.. "family friendly" restrictions, but I doubt that'll happen soon.

This Dark Mush Roleplay idea seems to have potential.

Finola wakes up from cryosleep angry at being kidnapped. She fakes logs in order to frame Kuan Ti as mush. She accuses Gioele and Jin Su of being accomplices to her kidnapping. Gioele punches her for talking back to him. Jin Su talks her into staying calm and working on the serum. Later on, once the PILGRED is completed, she works only hard enough to finish the serum. She then assassinates Chun to get the hacker kit, and flies the ship back to Sol.

Ian goes into depression at seeing his beloved plants destroyed. He believes that it was the work of the great plant deity who he has abandoned. He goes on a strict fructivorous and carnivorous diet, refusing to eat the plant-based rations on the ship and surviving on bananas and alien steaks. He refuses to do the thing with people who have ever eaten any rations, insisting that he can only love one who follows the path of the fruit.

Chun takes advantage of her position as the last hope of humanity to declare herself as queen of the ship. She makes them build a sofa for her in the refectory and gorges freely on food, fruits, and coffee. She insists that she be the only one to hold the hacker kit, rightfully so because she's the only one who can be trusted. After getting in a fight with Gioele about wasting the ship's resources (mainly coffee), she confronts him and dares him to shoot her. When the team backed up Gioele, she removes the pilot lock on the ship and enters the Pasiphae threatening to kill herself if the ship doesn't do what she wants. She holds on tightly to the hacker kit, using it as her crown, and applies brinksmanship to get what she wants.

Gioele is going through a midlife crisis after losing all the money and contacts he worked for decades to build. He gets on a strict bodybuilding protein ration and coffee shake diet plan and goes around showing off how buff he his. Being one of the strongest members of the crew and spoiled by years of being at the top, he's quick to resolve problems with violence.

Gioele gets into a fight with Stephen in the early days of the ship, accusing him of kidnapping his daughter. After trading punches and getting pried apart by Jin Su, both of them agree sent to consultation with Janice. He later gains a respect for Stephen as one of similar strength, methods, and personalities. Both of them become best friends for life and get matching tattoos. They start a gang on the ship and muscle their way into getting what they want.


Chao follows a strict shoot first and talk later policy on the ship. He claims the blaster early. He tortures anyone and everyone getting dirty. He divides up the ship and forces the dirty ones into 'quarantine' in the room with the shower. In other words, he acts just the same as he does on any other ship. Gioele and Stephen try to get him into their gang, but he just glares at them and they back off.

Terrence falls in love with Janice after all the psychology research they did together. However, she friendzones him. Terrence believes that she's really in love with NERON. Unable to do that much from the wheelchair, he spends his time on the ship in depression, yelling at the AI every time it says something, and taking depressive medicine to get Janice to comfort him.

Hua has concerns over her weight and is bulimic. She cooks food and then leaves them in her inventory until she gets hungry, so that she could throw up later. Her goal is not simply to explore planets, but find a miracle diet plant which will slim her down. She keeps a plant in her dorm, experimenting with grafting it with different weight loss fruits. She pops random pills from the medlab in order to induce weight loss. She gets along well with Gioele and Ian over health food discussions.

Kuan Ti is Mr Perfect. The ladies admire his magnificence, the men admire his intelligence. After a successful date with Janice, Terrence gets maniacally jealous of him. Finola senses this and talks Terrence into thinking that Kuan Ti is mush and that the many times he has had sex with Janice is so that he could spore her. Terrence goes mad, grabs a knife and stabs both Kuan Ti and Janice to death.
