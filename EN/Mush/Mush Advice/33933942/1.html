Being idle doesn't speed up starvation. I suppose you got that impression because you often seen inactive people die of starvation very early in the game, but it's not because they are idle. These people are players who started the tutorial but never finished it. At the beginning of the tutorial, they are hungry and they must go to the refectory and eat. If they don't do it, they starve to death in 14 cycles, since they lose 1 hp per cycle from the beginning.

Apart from that particular case, all characters are the same. Someone who has never eaten starts to starve after 24 cycles. For example, if they awake Day 1 Cycle 6, they will be hungry Day 4 Cycle 6, and start losing 1 hp per cycle Day 4 Cycle 7.

If you eat, you will be hungry 24 cycles after your satisfaction has gone back to 0, so it depends on the kind of food you ate. If you eat a ration Day 4 Cycle 6, you will be hungry again Day 8 Cycle 2.

Nothing speeds up the process, but someone can die in less than 14 or 16 cycles if they are injured (by a sheet of metal, by someone hitting them...) or were already injured before they were hungry.

Since starvation damages HP, and the person starves when their HP reaches zero, it means that once they start starving they have "hp remaining" cycles left to live.

As such, taking damage reduces the time spent starving while increasing time spent dead, and that you can indefinitely keep starving people alive through regular healing.

Starvation IS hp damage. It doesn't really 'speed up' HP damage, nor does HP damage 'speed up 'the direct effects of starvation - it just makes their death occur sooner.

The 'starvation' condition means a player loses 1hp per turn. When they run out of hp due to the condition, they die, and the message displayed is that they 'died from starvation'.

Thus, if they are 1hp before they begin starving, they will die after a single cycle from 'starvation'. The HP damage they suffered resulting in them starving to death more quickly, since starvation is nothing but HP damage.
