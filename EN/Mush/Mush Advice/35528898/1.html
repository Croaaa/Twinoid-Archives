First of all : do not take Wrestler if you are alpha mush. It is one of the few skills that are lost if you are mush, and your crew would notice easily that you are much weaker than you should be. If you hit someone and they only lose 1 hp, or if it takes you 12 ap to kill someone instead of 5 or 6, they will know that you are mush. Not to mention the fact that the skill would be completely useless.

Also, remember that you are hyperactive and often lack ap. So don't waste them by hitting and torturing everyone at random, hoping that you will find a mush that way. You should target the people you torture, and if you can, try to cooperate with Eleesha (if you can trust her) and Chun, who can help you find suspects. Having Chun's permission is also good because it can protect you if people get angry, as they often tend to react badly when they are tortured (though sometimes, when someone overreacts, it can be revealing : they may be angry because they're afraid of what you could have found out).


Laforetgrab the medkit and torture people who want to use it? You might put a stop to spore production that way, if people agree to it. Also, torture people who enter in the medlab.

People may have good reasons to use the medikit. You shouldn't hit and torture blindly only because someone is injured, just like you shouldn't hit someone just because they've been dirty. Besides, while you torture dirty, innocent people, the mush could be having furtive showers and not even be injured thanks to the Splashproof skill.

Part of your job (or Eleesha's job) is to trace your crew members' actions. If someone is injured or dirty, try to find out where they have been and what they have done. Did they eat, repair something, pick up an object ? Were they injured by a steel plate ? The way people use their ap is also interesting. If someone claims to be out of ap but didn't do anything and yet sleeps all the time, there's clearly something wrong. You can also see who goes to strange places, or who enters a room and leaves without doing anything visible. For example someone who enters a dorm and gets out immediately (especially bravo dorm) may have taken a shower. If they get out of the dorm, do nothing, and come back to bed, they may have extracted a spore. Someone who gets up and then lies down (if there's no camera) could have spiked a crew member. Someone who keeps picking up objects or lies down repeatedly is also suspicious : they could be trying to cover their mush actions so that torture doesn't reveal anything interesting.

Of course there may be ships on which everyone's behaviour seems to be irreproachable. It could mean that the mush are very good at acting human, never let their crew members see that they are dirty or injured, and try to be as active as possible while doing mushy things at the same time. But in most cases, you can easily spot people who have an odd behaviour, and you don't need to torture at random.

I was thinking about how crappy it would be to draw mush as a gold Chao, but you can get around the loss of wrestler if you get into a situation where you have to punch/torture someone, because torture is a personal action and not logged. So: torture, then punch, then torture again. First torture reveals how injured the player started. Second torture lets you calculate how much damage your punch did. If you only got 1 damage from the punch, torture a third time to get 4 total damage, and then you can claim that the punch did 3 damage and you only tortured once. Unfortunately you sink a ton of AP doing this.
