I just would’ve described the situation in discussion in a bit different way:

1) Since mush is RNG-based – there might be cases where you end up either getting a positive or a negative outcome from the same actions, depending on how the dice rolls. So yes, benefit could be ambiguous for chance-based actions for some situations.
2) Same actions might look like an effort of spoiling while in reality it was an effort of gaining a benefit – you just can’t know which one it is without having access to corresponding logs/conversations/etc, in other words – don’t have a full picture of what has happened. Gameplay actions, that is. That should be taken in account as well, of course.
3) Those first 2 cases describe actions that are, let’s say, “variables” and depend on the circumstances. BUT! There’s a set of actions that are “constants” and always have a negative outcome for your team or both teams, regardless of the circumstances. Such as, for example, pathetic suicide or volunteer curing/getting converted on purpose. Why I classify them as constants is because they’re nothing but examples of giving up playing the game (in general or specifically being a member of a team you are part of) and spoiling it for other players, or an attempt to play the game not as intended. Those things are considered unacceptable and whatever the circumstances are – giving up is still giving up. And there’re no hypothetical circumstances that are capable of changing the constant’s value, without overriding the whole meaning of what “constant” is :B

PS: The example you gave with the starting planet is just an option of environment setup. Configuration options are not beneficial or harmful on their own – they just give players a chance to get some benefits or add some risks into the game.
