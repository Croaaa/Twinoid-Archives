"As long as possible" is subjective. If you read my last post, it clarifies that you can plan a specific time to go back to Sol, with an early return as an option...IF things go badly very quickly. Yeah, you should shoot for Eden, and try to last as long as is considered sane by your crew. Of course, some people don't like it when games go beyond 10 days, and they just give up. We can't force them to keep playing, but we can sanction those quitters (and I specifically mean people who initiate harmful returns, in this case) so that they don't have the opportunity to do it to their future ships.

That wording of "as long as possible" was chosen so that people can't simply kill their Mush, finish Pilgred, and call it a "victory" with a Sol return on day 4-5 or so, when it's VERY likely they could have survived to Day 7, Day 10 or beyond that. Again, it's a form of suicide, where one person gives up, and takes the rest of the crew with them.

Would you sanction someone for going against the majority (or even all) of the crew if there were one crewmate at a high risk of dying by continuing? That depends on the circumstances of the ship at the time. Quitting the game via a Sol return is becoming a problem for some ships, and that quitting is leading to an increase in harmful return reports, to the point that we're treating it as a cross between suicide and active griefing.
