It depends really.

Pros of keeping mush alive:
Killing mush is very time consuming in APs. Some things might be more urgent, like oxygen or morale. Killing the wrong person can literally kill someone else due to morale.

Some mush cooperate with the ship. Characters like Janice who are passively useful are sometimes worth keeping alive. There was one ship where I suspected Paola of being mush, but because she did all the Xyloph DB stuff efficiently, we let her live.


Cons of keeping mush alive:
They will always betray you when things are at their worst. Always. In a lot of 'survival' games, the goal of the traitor is to survive. In Mush, the goal of the bad guys is for everyone to die. Your oxygen tanks will break down when there's no oxygen left... Icarus will get sabotaged... people on an expedition will get left behind. And this is the point where you don't have the APs to deal with them.

Betrayal in Mush can be very effective. I've killed 3 people in a row without even having full APs. It's just plain stupid to keep a high level Chao-mush prisoner. Keep the knife far away from the mush, probably in Chun's hands.


If I were playing a confined mush (like I did on my first ship), I'd play along by their rules to the end. I was mush Jin Su, didn't fly the ship in wrong directions, gave people missions. So people trusted me, because of the unmushlike behavior, even though there was practically 90% evidence pointing to me as mush.

I ended up backstabbing them when they actually tried to get an expedition down and after knowing that I was mush, nobody dared to go down out of fear of being left behind. The crew died of lack of oxygen. There's always a perfect time to backstab in the game.

I have a few ideas on getting around the spore sucker test, but they are all situational.

When you are damaged (shower if needed) and if you can get a mush ally to perform the test on you (if the crew doesn't require Chun to preform the test) then you can have them say that you were at full health before picking up the sucker, then they announce that you are damaged, then heal you and the healing shows in the log. Then they announce you are cleared as human.

This method is not really that good for a bronze mush, but if you have transfer and at least one person has been infected there are ways to get around it. You can grab the sucker and hide it and bury a bunch of items on top of it to make it difficult to search for, then transfer into another body making sure that your old body is killed quickly (get help from your allies), or if you can spore someone who has already been tested you can transfer in to their body and hopefully remain clear of suspicion for the rest of the trip as long as your old body is killed. But if you are planning to impersonate someone for the rest of the trip make sure you can have the same skills as them. If you are jumping into a level 5 character and you have only level 3 in that character you will likely be discovered. Also make sure you dont to anything that could reveal you as mush before you transfer as when your old body is killed it will be human and you want it to look like a griefer. If you do something like throw the grenade while the setting that prevents humans from throwing it is on, then observant players could realize you were mush at one time and must have transferred bodies.

Bronze mush can do quite a lot! Tbh, I bought gold when I first got mush but it turns out that most of the skills aren't very effective. If you're planning on rampaging, you only need 2 skills. And you only need one slot for the anonymush trick.

Spore sucker test can be faked with some social engineering. It's highly activity intensive. You need one person approving that you were clean and uninjured. It doesn't guarantee dirtiness so you can fake that. You can go in injured (just shower!), claim you were damaged by lurching, and ask someone to heal you to full. This will waste their APs and people get cranky when they lose APs. I went into the spore sucker test with about 8 APs and lost all of them in walking, testing and healing people.

Humans also get surprisingly edgy every time they're subjected to spore-suck testing. Some don't like sitting in a room for ~10 minutes waiting for people to approve them. Some hate that they're getting multiple injuries and people still don't believe that they're not mush. Some think it's cheating and put up a fuss about it.

Use this restlessness to sneak your way past the checks. It requires a lot of social engineering but when people no longer want to sit there. Just run into the room, declare that you're ready to be tested quickly before you log off. Ask someone to heal you. Once healed, say that the people in the room are witnesses to you not being injured. ("Janice, Stephen, Chun can vouch that I'm not injured" Then before someone actually approves you, pick up the spore sucker and drop it and ask someone to heal you. Then put up an argument if someone asks for you to be retested, saying that you don't want to be injured again.

You can even pretend to log off for a long time (which makes them more restless) and then log back on when the people are no longer in the room. Just say that you ran out of APs testing yourself and healing people. Then ask someone else to come in and certify you. They'll just go "bleh fine you're clear". Also say something about not wanting to be in the room for long in case mush decides to grenade the mush hunters gathered there.

Another trick you can do is to convince them to do it in the dorm with the shower. Just say that it's to conserve MP when they get dirty.

It also doesn't work on people who don't cooperate. We didn't spore suck test everyone, just the ones who are acting dodgy. Some people are inactive and sit somewhere all day or log on like once a day. These are impossible to test. You can pretend to be one of those.

If you've been quiet and helpful since the start, they might not even bother testing you. When I play as mush, I do my duties well and get trusted because "a person being so helpful to the humans can't possibly be mush".

If you do get past the spore sucker test, you're likely to be cleared for the rest of the game. Also the people who are certified mush free are excellent candidates to convert...

Investigation is WAY easier than spore-sucking, though, assuming you can get anyone to do it. Investigation doesn't require people to cooperate or coordinate nearly enough. It doesn't tell the Mush EXACTLY when they are going to be outed, giving them the advantage in acting first. Want to guarantee bypassing the spore-test? Make a point of only being active when most of the rest of the ship isn't. :V Want to avoid investigation?.... good luck with that!

Other things you can do about suck-test: Transfer. CLAIM a transfer. Shower to get damaged. Have important priorities that need to be done elsewhere, say you'll do it "when you get a chance". Etc. and so on. With the suck-test, the Mush control the rules of engagement - it is not, in any way, the best Mush-hunting strategy for the humans.

It's a huge drain on the ships survival to actually get it functional, and testing everyone on the ship is unreasonable and practically impossible. The food test and the shower test all have their own limitations as well. No one is likely to get killed for simply refusing to take the spore test.

(On our ship, if it didn't exist Hua WOULD have gotten killed, because she insisted on acting like a Mush at every available opportunity and rendering traditional investigation pointless)

I consider the suck-test a final, last line of defense against the "perfectly helpful Mush". The humans can't win with one of those on board, and there's very little aside from Mush-tests that can actually find one.

Back on topic
I think the whole "keeping the Mush alive" thing is perfectly acceptable. Both sides do it because in the end they think it will help them and their team come out on top, so it's not anti-team play in any way. It's just taking a risk for a possible reward. And it could lead to some great RP and twists and paranoia and stuff. So I approve.
