My way of RPing is to set up few keywords describing different characteristics, then when comes to some hard play and interact with someone I just refer to those words and build message on them.

Let`s say You like Eleesha as: professional, petty, stubborn, scrupulous (example reference - fast and inaccurate but...)

Then all You need in any of situation, look to this reference and build You behavior as described in those keywords. It lets You behave in any of situations, not only those few which You were prepared for.

Whatever you do, play in a way that you believe both you and your ship enjoy. I played on a ship with someone roleplaying Finola as a furious kidnap victim, she was pushy and rude to most of the ship. Later when I got her to actually talk in PMs it seemed like she wasn't enjoying her roleplay very much; a bit later she dropped most of it and the whole ship felt 'better' to me.

Do any of us really want to play the same ship over and over? With the same personalities, costumes that people don to play out this game, no surprises there? Moreover, the same hostilities and problems, again and again? I know I sure don't. I'll take collusion to clash any day, myself... but a very little clash is just fine and sure, it's a spice, just a very strong one that needs to be gently and carefully added into the mix. Kinda like garlic - some people hate even the faintest taste of it, but that small taste goes a long way and very much of it at all overpowers everything other ingredient in the dish.

So do bring your intepretation into your character, do go the way you want to go; I remember a ship I played Gioele on, where everyone started treating me as a pervert. I didn't like it, I countered it as strongly as I could with a reformed Gioele personality who wanted nothing more than to have a grandchild to hold on an Earth reclaimed from the mush. Amusingly, the people who were so harshly pushing sexual perversion at me in their 'roleplay' didn't seem comfortable roleplaying at all in interaction with that version of Gioele - instead of rolling with my roleplay and adjusting their own, they stopped their roleplay entirely and ceased to react to anything I posted in character.

Oh well, to each their own - but remember, it's your character and your enjoyment. You need to make sure you like your play, that's the first and biggest thing.
