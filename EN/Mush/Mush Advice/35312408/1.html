Douglas AdamsSpace is big. You just won't believe how vastly, hugely, mind- bogglingly big it is. I mean, you may think it's a long way down the road to the chemist's, but that's just peanuts to space.
Having thought about it a bit, as a player and science nerd, here is how I would explain it: when you move the Daedalus, the instructions you give to the navigation computer are along the lines of "go five units that-a-way", where "that-a-way" is one of four directions out of the infinite number of directions available in space, and each unit of distance probably represents billions of miles. Given the incredibly low precision of these navigation instructions, the odds of actually ending up someplace meaningful by random chance are infinitesimal. However, if the ship's scanner has a lock on the position of a planet, the navigation computer can say, "Aha! Those silly flesh beings are trying to go there. I'll just make a few adjustments..." and BAM!, you reach your destination. Of course, when you go into hyperdrive, the navigation computer loses its lock on all of the planets it knew of except the one you are going to, so now you have to start scanning all over again.
