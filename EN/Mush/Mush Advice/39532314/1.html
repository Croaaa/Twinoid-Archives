Pretty much this. All of the other potential techs unlock their skill very late in the game, so a lot of ships depend on Terrence taking technician. Also pay attention to who is active; these are the people you'll be able to count on for rides places, especially if you are relaying with them.

Early game will probably find you focusing on neron projects, since technician has boosted efficiency on a lot of them. Even if you take robotics expert, you won't have a dedicated use for your AP other than upgrading the drones whenever you have scrap.

Late game will find you being a repair man, even as a robotics expert. Free rides from one (occupied) room to another means that you have more AP to spend on fixes. If Raluca is dead, tech Terrence will probably be the one working on Pilgred repair.

Also notes on drones: first upgrade should be Turbo because it's SUPER handy, especially on fixing things like patrol ships or the research terminal. Don't bother with Pathfinder at all, it's still broken and just makes the drones move back and forth between a few rooms - good for finding them, but an organized crew knows where they are when you need them anyway.

I just had a very good ship as Terrence (Gold, Level 3) so feel in a decent enough position to give a few tips for him, even if you can't apply them all as Bronze. We were quite lucky to start with a Sprinter Mage Book, Lizaro and second Sofa BP, and get an Alien Bottle Opener, but most of them can be applied in most ships.

Your main tasks will be NERON Projects, Drone upgrading and organisation, repairing the ship, and Piloting. Obviously you'll need Robotics Expert for the second and Pilot for the last.

Working in the Nexus works well with your Disabled trait, since you can be pushed by whoever is going to be relaying you. This task can be done by anyone who is as active as you at the same time, but if they have the Scooter or Sprinter then all the better (my second most successful ship was as a Scooter-ed Eleesha pushing Terrence around). Technician and Pilot give bonuses for many useful projects, and if you happen to be able to get IT Expert instead of Shooter that'll give you free actions to use.

Drones depend on how well you can gather scrap (both to upgrade them and to build one from your starting Blueprint), which will do much better if you are a Pilot too. If you have Robotics Expert, you'll want to have the crew work on Extra Drone when it comes up (unless it is against Plasma Shield). Upgrades should always be Turbo first - the second action can also be a movement which I didn't realise until a while ago - then Fireman to save AP on putting out fires. If you have the Magnetic Net then you can consider giving them Pilot as well, but as pointed out above Pathfinder doesn't seem to work.

If you've played any other Pilot then you'll know what to do here. If you have Shooter then you want to be killing more Hunters, otherwise others may as well do that while you collect Scrap and/or launch expeditions.

Repairing the ship can be done manually - particularly if you are a Technician and have someone to push you around - or via Drone, including fires when they're used. A thread to keep track of Drone sightings and where they're dropped (you can also let the crew know what upgrades you give them) will be helpful, so they can work on hard-to-complete and non-essential repairs while you save your Tech points and/or AP for important things like Oxygen Tanks.
