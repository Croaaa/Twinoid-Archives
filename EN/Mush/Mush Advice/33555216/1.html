Updated quite a bit now that I've had a lot more experience with the game. Thanks for those who gave feedback.

[Decent] Anomaly Detector - NERON will warn you when important things are broken, like oxygen tanks and gravity, so it's not vital. But it'll save you some AP and remind people when certain things haven't been fixed.

[Varies] Antigravity Thrusters - Less useful the more active pilots you have. Past 2 pilots it's nearly useless. With 0 pilots, it's one of the best.

[Good] Armored Corridors - Not sure. It's not as useful with Plasma Shields, but a life saver without it. Most of the hunters do around 1-3 damage. In later stages of the game, you might be facing 20+ hunters at once, and that would save your hull from about 20 damage per cycle. The d1000 attacks thrice, so that's 3 damage absorbed. If it reduces damage to plasma shields as well, it's one of the best.

[Very Good] Automatic Sprinklers - The best part is that it reduces fires before they happen. Sometimes a fire can wipe out all your food, pills, soap, plants in its first cycle.

[Poor] Auxilary Terminal - Maybe to have Raluca research? But overall one of those things which will use more APs than it saves.

[Poor] Bay Door XXL - Useless with plenty of pilots. Non-pilots flying a patrol ship only get 40% odds so it's still not worth it.

[Good] Blaster Gun - A 6 HP hunter requires 2 shots to kill, if you're doing 3-5 damage.

[Poor] Convenient Sink - Maybe prevents showers and gives mush less lurking room, but nearly useless otherwise.

[Varies] CPU Overclocking - Costs about 12 AP to research, meaning that it breaks even after searching for about 12 planets. Then again, my last ship scanned 107 planets, so it's useful if you're going for Eden.

[Poor] Defective Piston Sensors - If an important door breaks, someone will notice. Might be a little useful later when it tells you that the door to Alpha Dorm is broken, but still, it only saves a few MP.

[Poor] Dismantler - Some rare cases where it's helpful - if the ship is immediately about to fall apart, if you have a Terrence who's upgrading drones, if you don't have a pilot.

[Good] Extra Drone - Drones are amazing things that can repair tons of equipment at 5% odds in one cycle.

[Good] Extra Hydropots - If you can live long enough, these should relieve your worries about oxygen and fires destroying plants.

[Very Good] Fire Detector - Every ship should have either this or the sprinklers. You want to put out fires almost immediately.

[Bad] Fission Coffee Roaster - Gioele with Caffeine Junkie only gets 4 AP/day for it. Almost never worth it especially since fixing PILGRED gives coffee every cycle anyway.

[Very good] General Patching Up - 15% to everything can save a lot. Note that this applies to fires, sheet metal, equipment breakages. Not so useful in early (< day 10) stages, but it kicks in fast.

[Good] Heat Lamps - Mid game it'll bring an abundance of AP, morale, and health from plants. Especially with a good Ian and Jumpkins.

[Decent] Hydroponic Incubator - Faster plants help, but some of the alien plants will grow faster than bananas. If anything, it's always nice to have food and oxygen stabilized faster.

[Poor] Large Bay Upgrade - 4 people is enough to get through most things, especially with a Diplomat. It might even be more harmful to delay the expedition a few cycles to get 6 people. In later stages, it's even less useful.

[Decent] Launch Propulsion System - It's ok for > Day 10 ships. Pasiphae and patrol ships drain a lot of AP. Otherwise it takes too long to kick in.

[Good] Magnetic Net - Pilots can then idle around outside and the magnetic net will pull them in, saving 2 on landing AP. It also saves from accidents and delays from pilots idling outside or commanders flying the ship without checking.

[Bad] Magnetic Return - If the mission fails, everyone is screwed anyway.

[Varies] NERON Participation - Good if you don't have the hardcore upgrades like Plasma Shields. If you have an active ship with a lot of people alive, don't bother.

[Decent] Oxygenated Ducts - Shouldn't be necessary. If you're not doing expeditions early enough, your ship is doomed. In later stages, plants should give enough oxygen to survive for very long periods of time.

[Best] Plasma Shield - This adds 100 regenerating health to your hull. Downside is that it's about as much work as fixing PILGRED. I'd even put this as a requirement for Eden.

[Bad] Pneumatic Distributor - Saves MP, but costs AP to research.

[Very good] Quantum Sensors - 50%-100% increase to scanning planets. You can never have enough scanning power.

[Bad] Rerouted Charges - Turrets suck anyway.

[Poor] Spatial Wave Radar - Doesn't help that much.

[Varies] Teslatron Reservoir - If you have elite pilots, sure. Shooter+Pilot can drain patrol ships early. I played Caffeine Junkie Gioele pilot and my patrol ships always used more than 10 charges.

[Good] Trail Minimizer - Quite poor early on. Past day 15, when 40 hunters appear a cycle, you'll be glad you got this.


Lab research

[Poor] Anabolics - At least it's cheap. Give the pills to Terrence, he'll need the MPs to run around fixing things.

[Best] Antispore Gas - Mush get more powerful as they gain momentum. Do this or constipaspore serum ASAP, ideally before day 3.

[Best] Constipaspore Serum - This is a better first choice if you have active mush hunters who can pressure mush into not dumping all their APs into spores. If everyone on the ship is lurking, get antispore gas instead.

[Good] Create Mycoscan - Very handy in investigations. Handy for ships that go through a lot of cristalite. Good mush hunters can nail mush early with just Constipaspore and Mycoscan.

[Good] Meridon Scrambler - The less hull damage, the better. This also buys you time to do other things.

[Poor] Mushicide Soap - Maybe ok as a passive. But you lose the soap and can't make white flag, one of the most useful items in the game.

[Decent] Mushovore Bacteria - A late game research. Good if combined with things like spore sucker or super soap.

[Glory] Mush-Hunter: ZC16H - Useless.

[Glory] Mush Language - Useless

[Very good] MycoAlarm - Plenty of places that need to be watched and there aren't enough cameras.

[Very good] NCC Contact Lenses - The lenses will kill an extra hunter.

[Decent] Narcotics Distiller - Gives only one drug a day. Might not even be a good one.

[Varies] Natamy Rifle - Good for hardcore mush hunting, bad otherwise because it lowers damage.

[Very good] Pheromodem - This alone will keep mush from letting their friends die. Once you get the modem, you can see if anyone is getting infected.

[Very good] Ultra-Healing Pomade - Past Day 15, people can easily lose 14 health per day.

[Good] Spore Sucker - Nice mush detector. Good for sucking out spores too.

Armored Corridors - Not sure. Sounds extremely helpful early on but less useful when the hunters start doing heavier damage. Not really useful if you have the Plasma shield which absorbs all damage, except asteroids. But it can be useful once the shield breaks.
Blaster Gun - Good upgrade, but 1 point will change little. Oh yes it will. It's very annoying to find yourself with hunters with only 1 hp left, and knowing that with the Blaster gun, you would have needed less shots to finish them.
Extra Drone - Poor, especially since Terrence rarely focuses on drones. Very, very useful. The more drones you have, the better. Even if they're not upgraded, they're extremely convenient to repair without using any AP.
General Patching Up - Bad. 15% is not much at all. Excellent project, but not in the first few days, because there's nothing to prevent. But it prevents all accidents (fires, metal plates, broken equipment...), which means that when the project works, nothing happens in that cycle. And when the ship starts to fall apart, it's extremely valuable to have about one or two quiet cycle(s) every day.
Heat Lamps - Good? Not sure how plants work. Good if Ian and / or Hua do their job, because with 7 or 8 plants, it means about 12 to 15 fruit a day, which is good for morale, health and to prevent famine. The downside is that the plants need to be in the garden at the end of cycle 8.
Magnetic Net - Good, but depends. Especially if your pilots or commander is a little dense. Also useful because pilots can stay in the patrol ship and wait for the commander to move the ship. It saves them 2 AP as they land automatically.
Teslatron Reservoir - Good if you have pilots with a lot of AP (like Gioele pilot) Always good. It means you can use only one patrol ship instead of landing and taking off again (which usually you can't do, because it would cost you 3 or 4 AP more).
Trail Minimizer - Decent. It only means about 10% less hunters because not all will chase you. Almost vital in later days. Without the Trail minimizer, 50 % of the hunters catch up with the ship when it moves. With the project, only 25 %. Which means (yeah, I know, that's an extreme example) that with 90 hunters, you will only have about 20 after moving.


Create Mycoscan - Poor, unless you have a spore sucker. That's not its only use. You can also use it to know who has been infected, and it can help with the investigation.
Natamy Rifle - Good for hardcore mush hunting, nearly useless otherwise. Very dangerous (as discussed in another thread) if people think they can use it for a simple test : "If I shoot someone twice and they die, they're Mush. If not, they're human." It can also kill humans in 2 shots, and inflict serious wounds. But the Natamy rifle is the perfect weapon to get rid of a Mush without spending too many AP (especially Ian, or a Mush in the same room as Ian).
Meridon Scrambler - Good. Less hull damage, better. I've never had real proof of its efficiency.

One thing I noticed regarding Armored Corridors: They also seem to reduce the damage the Plasma Shield takes, if you have both upgrades active.
