Obviously the best way to find Mush, and the most enjoyable way to play, is through the classic techniques of following their footsteps, identifying suspicious behaviour, and then murdering them in the face if they stay out of line.

When this works, it's great!

But any decent mush could conceivably just hide for the entire game and achieve a technical victory, unless the humans are willing to conduct Mush tests.

If you're playing as a human, you should know all the tools in your arsenal - but remember that unlike classic Mush-testing, you're not going to get a surprise-kill on a Mush with a mush-test to minimize disruption. Most of them require the active participation of the subject, meaning the Mush will have plenty of time to engage on their own terms, which can end quite poorly for the humans. And Transfer means that the moment a possible Mush dies, your entire possible suspect list might need to be reset.

If you're playing Mush, it's important to know the sort of pressures you'll be operating under. If it's obvious the humans are making use of or working their way towards a Mush-test, you'll want to be prepared with ways to get around.

And so, a list of Mush tests that can out the mush:
The Shower Test - (incrimination test) the most basic and unreliable of Mush tests. Test a dirty human with the medkit to make sure they are at full health - without the testee leaving the room, they must remove the dirty status (by showering), and then check with the medkit again to see if they are damaged. Mush without showerproof are hurt by showering. But experienced Mush CAN take showerproof or quickly eat a phagocyte after shower, even at low levels, and humans can get hurt showering. This test isn't full proof, but it can certainly provide a powerful piece of evidence. It can be made more effective but using Chao's torturer skill or Chun's premonition skill, although those are effective all-around.
The Spoiled Food Test - (human verification test) Force the target to eat spoiled food. If they get an illness, they are human! This test is useful for verifying the humanity of an important and central player, but the whole "letting a bunch of food spoil" combined with the "making everyone sick" combined with the "failing to get sick doesn't mean your mush" thing makes it fairly unattractive as a test. Variant: The Pumpkinhead Test - as a variant of the spoiled food test, you can make people eat jumpkins (or another food likely to cause a condition) as well. Remember that the point of the exercise is that Mush can't acquire illnesses, so anything likely to make a person 'ill' works for this test.
The Pill Test - (human verification test) Humans can eat a pill followed by a ration, or coffee followed by a banana. Everything, however, makes the Mush full. So one of the easier tests is simply to have a potential mush try to eat something twice in a row.
The Suck Test - (human verification test) Mush cannot use the spore-sucker. This means that you can confirm someone is unhurt, have them use the sporesucker, and then confirm it injured them. Unfortunately, large scale testing is fairly obvious, organizing and recovering from the test can be fairly labour-intensive, and if you test half the crew and left them injured a mush with a grenade is going to be able to give you a bad, bad time. Currently a popular test, but it's often better used as a threat to force the mush into action than as an actual test.
The Natamy Blast - (mush test) Shoot someone three times with the Natamy Blaster. If they die, they were mush. They probably won't die if human. Note that if you are targeting a Mush with hard-boiled, 3 shots may not be enough to kill them. A less... likely to be lethal version of this test is to use a medkit to make the target is at full health, and then use a medkit that heals 4 damage on them after shooting them once with the blaster. Since the blaster only does 2-4 damage to humans, they should be fully healed after one go - since it does 6-13 damage to Mush, even with hardboiled a single heal won't be enough and you will be able to tell they are mush.

Posting to share results of a Natamy test, since it was the subject of much controversy on IRC. All this stuff has probably been discussed, but I cba to read the whole thread...

We had a suspected mush on our MMM ship, with many separate pieces of circumstantial evidence against him. The plan was as follows: Check that he is at full HP, shoot him with the Natamy Rifle and then heal him again and check that he is at full HP. The healer was supposed to Finola with medic and nurse, so that he would be healed for 5 HP, more than a human should be able to be damaged by the Natamy - mush take a minimum of 6 HP unless they have hard boiled, and humans a maximum of 4.

The result was that he was shot while I (Finola) was away, so someone else treated him instead. He thankfully only took 2-3 damage, if he would have taken 4 the test would have been inconclusive. He also suffered only one wound, critical bleed (-2 hp per cycle). Next cycle I came to check on things, he was ready to be operated on so after a bit of bumbling and a septic shock I cured him.

While discussing the downsides of the test, we figured that the chances of a human dieing to the test are fairly low - headshot would obviously kill them, and some combinations of wounds like smashed legs and a bleed or a lot of the minux max HP ones could also do it (those are visible only on the French wiki for some reason). Using this test on an inactive human would be very harmful, but whether they would be a great loss is another discussion.

So there is a risk to the test and it requires active hunters and a prepared medic to do, but it is not as ridiculously unreasonable as people were saying it is. Obviously from an RP standpoint, it makes sense but is also unethical to potentially kill someone just based on circumstantial evidence, so use this information at the peril of your losing your characters humanity and/or eternal space-soul...
