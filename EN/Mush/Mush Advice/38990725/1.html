It's simpler than that. Rules aren't "just guidelines". The rules are there to be followed by everyone. People who try to justify suicide when they are the last person alive have ALSO suggested that suicide when the the ship is "hopeless" should be allowed. And where does that lead to? It leads to people bailing on ships that aren't going well, but are still salvageable. Almost everyone who gets sanctioned for Pathetic Suicide claims they did it because the ship was "doomed" in their opinion. Whether or not it was the case is irrelevant...Pathetic Suicide is not allowed.

An accidental "Putting the Fun in Funeral" would be someone dying while trying to pick up the medkit and save themselves. That's perfectly reasonable, because they weren't intending to die. You know how we can tell an intentional suicide from an accident? The intentional suicide usually involves picking up the cat a few dozen times. The rest of the circumstances don't matter, at that point; the intent was to suicide.

MT has strict rules against suicide and surrender, and that's why you have to play the game to its natural conclusion. This is an argument you cannot win, and the rules are very firm regarding this issue. When sanctions and possibly quarantines are on the line, people bad advice doesn't help matters.

As for rampaging Mush, they die while attempting to accomplish their team's goals; they may die, but if they're damaging the ship, it's allowed. Here's one example of a Mush Pathetic Suicide: Mutating in a Dorm, and taking no actions after that, as they wait to die. See the difference? We certainly do, and Mush suicides like that one are fairly easy to determine.
