First, allow me to point out that, compared to a fairly good percentage of the people on this forum posting guides, my experience is practically negligible. I haven't had glorious ships lasting to day 10+, I haven't always been active on my ships, and I've only been human for half of my ships. Actually, that last point is one I'm fairly happy with. So far, in my currently six ships, I've been Mush 50% of the time... Game's telling me something, but that's off course.

When it comes to the Art of the Patsy (Or, the Art of Turning Everybody Against Some Poor Innocent Soul who Happens to be in the Wrong Place at the Wrong Time), I've learned a few things. Of course, a lot of this will probably seem obvious, or even elementary to veteran players, but it wasn't so obvious to me at first. Becoming a master of the Art of the Patsy pretty much implies that, no matter who you are, you can always turn the ship against some innocent human and shrug it off when they find out he's not Mush. I'll go over a few basic tactics and then swap to tactics for more high leveled Mush, and tactics that require a bit more of wordplay.

Hold on, guys, we should kill the inactives first before looking for the Mush amongst us

Saying this in part of the guide kind of defeats the purpose, but it makes perfect sense. Inactives are a harm to human players. To Mush, they can be a huge benefit. While not specifically part of the Art of the Patsy, a favorite tactic of many is to spore an inactive to completion, take Anonymush, and then whistle a jolly good tune when they kill the inactive and find there's one less Mush to worry about. Even if you don't manage to infect an inactive totally, they'll still waste more AP than many would be comfortable with. AP that could be better used killing hunters, advancing tech, or pushing Neron projects. From a human perspective, it's AP well spent. From a Mush perspective, you just cost them the chance at killing at least two hunters.

The Morale Trick

I'm not sure how many people actually do this, but it's really important to keep track of. From the very start of your game, start tracking your morale. Bronze starts at 7, Gold starts at 8. Frankly, I don't care how you track it, it's just important that you do. You (are supposed to) lose one morale per day change, gain one morale from Janice, so on so forth. The important thing is that if anyone ever asks you, then you want to be able to accurately tell them what your morale should be. If you ever have to hesitate, guess what? They now know you're Mush. Mush are constantly at 16 morale points, and not knowing what morale you're supposed to be at can kill you instantly. A friend of mine demonstrated how effective morale counting is when she went back from day 1 and calculated exactly how much morale they should have, due to speeches, day changes, and stuff along those lines. Morale may not affect Mush, but it is still a killer.

Oh my God he's in my body

This is a tactic of mine that I haven't really gotten to test yet, but I'm fairly confident in its success. This is a tactic for Level six Mush, so, sadly, it won't be useful for anyone below that level. However, if you're level six... Well then. To explain it, I'm going to use three players. Players 'Jin Su', 'Chao', 'Ian', and 'Finola'. Player 'Jin Su' is Mush, and has the skill 'transfer', which allows him to transfer bodies with another human player. Player 'Jin Su' already has a small amount of suspicion on them. Ready? Tactic start.

First, Jin Su pretends he transfers bodies with Finola. (Note that these characters can change, it's just an example). Note that he doesn't actually transfer bodies. You know, he gets in the same room with them, and then starts spouting off that he's Finola in Jin Su's body. Props to you if you've observed 'Finola' from the beginning of the game and manage to mimic their writing style. Of course, the actual Finola will begin complaining. Whether the crew believes Finola or not doesn't matter. If they kill Finola first and find out she was human, big news, the tactic is a success. Now, how to get out of it? Obviously, they're thinking one of two things: You were Finola at first and transferred to Jin Su, or you never transferred in the first place. So they go up to kill you. Chao comes up into the room and begins to kill Jin Su. Extra props if there's a mycoalarm in the room. Jin Su then Spores and Transfers to Ian the inactive's body. Ian in Jin Su's body is killed, and you're in the body of an inactive. The trick here is to act inactive for a little bit. If there was a mycoalarm in the room, people will think Chao was turned into Mush and immediately took Anonymush, because of the BRIIIIIIIING sound. They'll then proceed to kill Chao. At that time, little sleeping Ian Mush can wake up.

This tactic works even if there is no inactive to transfer to. Instead of transferring to Ian, Jin Su could have transferred into Chao and then finished the kill of Jin Su. If this is late game, you've just eliminated two active players. Even extra props if, before all this, you'd left a full expo crew on a planet, killing another four players.

This tactic will probably end in the death of Jin Su as Chao, sadly, as any intelligent crew would put two and two together and kill him. But think of the twist ending!

More will come later, and more would be heartily appreciated. The Art of the Patsy is an ever growing art, namely, one that grows the more I think about ways to pin stuff on other players. Thank you very much for reading my guide, ladies and gentlemen, and keep Mushing.

Most crews don't actually kill inactives. We leave them past day 4-5 and see who dies to morale/starvation. They're costly to kill and harmless as mush.

Don't convert inactives! Play to win. Convert the most active and then take anonymush. If you're playing with trapped shelves and food, they'll usually take anonymush themselves. If you two somehow mess it up and both of you take anonymush, people usually think that a formerly inactive mush suddenly got on and picked the skill. Especially if another mush has died recently.


Morale counting is very important if you're facing experienced mush hunters. I count morale. A lot of other players do and even set up spreadsheets for it. A skilled Eleesha will know who's in what room with Jin Su/Janice; this also makes Janice a great mush hunter.


Transfer tricks can be very difficult. You'll have to start off with an obviously mushy move - spore someone, attack someone. The hard part is convincing people that you found your talkie that the transferrer most likely hid. Do something dumb like hiding the talkie in front of an inactive in a corridor and stay silent until then.

You're also relying on a smart crew that knows what transfers are. A played-straight transfer may work better on a naive crew.

Let's say Eleesha here is a good mush hunter and has backed you up against a wall. Your obvious action would be to kill yourself and transfer into Eleesha. You throw a grenade, stab a few people, run across Eleesha.

If you tell them that you've just been transferred into, you're busted because you still have your walkie talkie. If you tell them nothing, they'll just kill you. What you have to do is set things up so that people expect a transfer. If you were playing a mush that was suspected enough, it's going to be hard to convince people. You should have set up an anti-mush channel by now, ideally one that's accusing your rival (Eleesha in this case). If she seems active and genuine, even volunteering to take a mush test, convince people into thinking that she'll transfer.

Even then, you're busted when they kill her and find out she's not mush. Your best bet then is to transfer into someone else (ideally the one with the knife) and accuse your former body of being mush. Or if you can't do that, just claim that you've been transferred into again.
