It works on the Bios terminal in the nexus and on the command terminal on the bridge. It allows people who are not Neron admin (for the Bios) or commander to access those terminals. It is not character specific, but characters with IT Expert have a 50 % chance of success, whereas other characters only have 25 %.

It is useful both for humans and for mush. It allows human to act when the commander or Neron admin are not online and there is an emergency. For example, if the ship must be moved and the commander is not here, someone else can do it using the hacker kit. It can also be used to change Neron settings, for instance to activate the Plasma Shield.

Mush often use the hacker kit to move the ship during an expedition, thus killing all the people on the planet, or to return to Sol when Pilgred is finished. Changing Neron settings can also be useful, for example deactivate the Plasma shield or the magnetic net, stop Neron from announcing deaths, unlock piloting to allow a mush who's not a pilot to take off, etc.

spacekadt

Ok, to answer a few questions.

re: Neutral Play - Before mush are chosen, the crew is full of human players. You may choose to play in a neutral manner which is basically taking a personal inventory and regaining ap. You can move items around the ship to areas where they'll be needed (human action). You can start research or neron projects (human actions). Or you can do pretty much nothing and wait to see which team you're ultimately playing for (neutral actions). You CANNOT hide items that are critical to either side (or hide items at all, really) as this gives an advantage to one side or the other which is not fair to the other side.

But wait! Why can I work on research (human), but not hide the hacker kit? First, chances of any anti-mush research being completed before mush are chosen is very low these days. Most ships are filling within just a few cycles and very few ships are coordinated enough from the start to get the most debilitating researches done. Second, as a person working so hard on anti-mush research, there's absolutely no guarantee that you're not screwing yourself down the line. The only person that has zero chance of becoming mush is Chun and she's not going to get very far without some help. As far as the hacker kit is concerned (or any other items), is that it's an immediate and distinct disadvantage to whatever team doesn't know where it is. The whole point of allowing/encouraging neutral play is to keep either team from being royally screwed from the start.

re: Hiding/Holding Items - as mentioned above, it's a violation of the neutral/human play and can be sanctioned if reported. Your best bet is to either leave it where it is or move it to a room with a camera if it's truly that important.

re: Cameras - If Gioele has the paranoid skill (especially if it's auto-chosen for him.. holy crap I hate that), he starts with 2 cameras in his inventory (or one in inventory and one in shelf if he's not a gold/silver player). This means his inventory is totally full and he can't just drop any of the items. Cameras cannot be dropped or hidden, only installed. This puts Gioele in a bit of a mess, really. He can install the camera in a stupid place on the very off chance he's mush, or install it in a dorm or someplace useful on the better chance he's a human. However he chooses to do it, he's currently human and expected to play either as human or neutral.

Of course, once mush are chosen, it's anything goes as long as you're working in the best interests of whatever team you're playing for

Does that help you figure things out at all?

From "Ask the SDF thread"
Ships filling slowly
There will be the rare times ships might take longer to fill, when this happens we will expect "neutral play". Which means, we don't do anything that specifically will help either team. As an example, killing hunters is acceptable as a mush goal can potentially be to live longer as much as it is a human goal. What's not acceptable is holding or hiding the Mush Sample, apron, weapons and other items. We encourage you not to advance anti-Mush research so that whoever is selected Mush (which could be you!) will have a fair chance at winning. Neutral research examples could be - Ultra-healing Pomade or the NCC Lens. We've also asked Blackmagic to consider having the Mush Sample (needed for anti-mush research) spawn only after the ship is full. Until then, though, this is our best solution.

Go fight your fight there if you really feel the need to. But I will say it's been discussed to death both in that thread and in the SDF forum and this was the best solution after conferring with knowledgeable French players (like peanutz) and devs.
