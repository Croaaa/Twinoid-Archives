Rain

You have to enable the option to speak as NERON at the bios. I think it's called vocoder or something weird like that. It's the odd one out that isn't any of the ones that follow. You speak as NERON with the command /neron (must be lowercase) then your message.

I generally prefer the destroy food option to be off, since food only has a chance of spoiling after the first day, not guaranteed. This is especially good when you get a bunch of alien steaks or your Stephen cooks half a dozen rations at the end of the first day or whatnot.

There must be a lock on either NERON projects, piloting or research, and whichever one is locked can only be done by people with the skill pertaining to that thing. Pilot lock is a good default, since you really want people other that Finola to be able to research and people other than Kuan Ti to be able to do NERON projects. However, it's a bit situational - if your ship doesn't have enough active pilots, you may have to discuss which thing should be locked so that hunters can be taken care of.

Priority should almost always be set to astronavigation. Research is nice early days to pump out the anit-mush stuff, but I've seen more ships die of lack of finding an oxygen planet than from mush, so I've become a big fan of keeping priority permanently on astronavigation, to make finding what you need easier.

Regarding the peace core, the default option is the one you want, typically. It keeps humans from throwing a grenade, so if someone does throw a grenade you know they're mush, and it blocks human griefers from being asshats in that regard.

There's no reason to ever turn off death announcements unless you're mush.

Anyways, those are my opinions on NERON settings from when I played Janice. Yay information spewing!
