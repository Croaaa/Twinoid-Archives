The reason you have up to a week is just for that purpose - though it's also to make sure we're not getting reports of griefing two weeks after a ship ends, and the accused player has been on "good behavior" since that point. PhocksJust to be clear. It's perfectly fine to say "Let's return on day 15" but it's sanctionable to say "Let's return on day 15 for mmm" correct? Even if both situations are the same? (serum researched, mush dead, everyone is healthy, etc)

Funny how that works, isn't it? Motive actually matters in this, because you're not usually going to find too many people who abandon a very viable ship (that has already passed day 12 or so) in favor of a ship that might explode in less than a week. Though the spirit of the rule is to keep people from abandoning their ships long before they would reach their natural conclusion. Usually, they do this via suicide by cat, rather than via a harmful return.

A day 15 return that everyone agrees with? Not sanctionable, even if some of them secretly want to make the next MMM jump. Both Mush dead, most of the research complete, RFS complete, etc. and a Sol return is in general the "natural conclusion" for ships in that state. Also, when one initiates a harmful return, it's not with the consent of the crew. This is a key part of this rule.

Then again, a day 15 return could technically still be considered an early return, if say you already had two map pieces and the special research completed. Abandoning the prospect of Eden at that point is unfortunate, but if your crew doesn't think you can continue, then put it to a vote. That's why each case involving a harmful return accusation is handled based on the specific circumstances at the time of the return.

If you have a reasonable shot to keep going, you should try. If your crew thinks you can only push to a certain point, and when you get there...you can push a bit longer, you should try. On my last ship, we thought we might only make a day 10 return, but we successfully survived to day 12 before returning. I was a bit surprised that we'd pulled that off, but it's feasible that we could have pushed to day 15. We feared greater losses, and activity from a few players was waning due to IRL issues. We felt it was best to call it at that point, and as you can imagine, there was nothing "early" or "harmful" about it.

It doesn't mean that you have to push for day 20 when half the crew is already dead, and Pilgred is done on day 6. Just weigh your odds, be reasonable and realistic about what you can accomplish, and communicate. Communication is the best way to avoid any return being considered harmful.

[6826491]

If you crew thinks it's not feasible to keep going, then you don't have to. That's why you put it to a vote, and then you won't have one person forcing the ship to return when everyone else wants to keep going. That's what this rule is primarily about, after all. It's to stop those people who think, "It's day 4 or 5, the Mush are dead, Pilgred is done, and I'm done with this game...and so is everyone else, whether or not they want to be." That's the mentality we're trying to prevent here. Ships in your case, that made it well beyond day 10? Not so much in question here, unless someone forced a return without consulting the crew. Consulting the crew is the other important part of this rule, and in most harmful return cases, it's against the wishes of the crew to return just yet.

The case you presented is a perfectly good example of a crew deciding together that continuing isn't viable, and going home before suffering any further losses. That's the big difference here; it's not a harmful return if everyone agrees, and it (generally) leads to ending the voyage on a high note. Totally within reason, in the case you described.

Sometimes the RNG will be on your side, and you'll survive for a few days longer than you thought you might. Other times, it will appear that every planet has something with a chance for instant death, you never go more than a cycle without at least a dozen Hunters on your tail, and fires are destroying things left and right. I've been on more than a few ships where the return time kept getting pushed back by days, until we hit a point where we all agreed that it wasn't smart to continue. And there's nothing at all wrong with that.
