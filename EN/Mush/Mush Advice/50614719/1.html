Mimic mush (mush who lay low, spore rarely if at all and carry on like normal crew members) are the most difficult to out and potentially the most dangerous to the crew if they are clever enough. Things to keep in mind that may help spot them:

Watch the refectory. while some mush do actively eat to appear human, especially if around an active eleesha, most don't even if they are trying to mimic a normal crew member. people who go several days early on without eating (especially in the case of an active job like pilot, paola or research) should be suspect.

Proven should be a dirty word. It is used too often. Because I whisper chun that someone is acting mushy (and they turn out ot be mush) does not make me proven. I out my partners all the time if I am mush and they are not listening/inexperienced. Same goes for helping to kill the mush when outed. Are they more likely human? yes. Are they proven? not at all.

Don't get too focused on the shower. If I am mimicing a human I will avoid the shower dorm at all costs, since I know the mush hunters will be busy watching it like a hawk. Likely one or two innocent humans will get killed for beginner mistakes like not declaring dirty and showering. Especially if I can help spread the suspicion in the mush hunting channel, which leads to...

The mush hunting channel can be compromised! I often start a mush channel with chun, especially if my partner starts making mistakes I can point out. I will start inviting "proven" humans in as well. In most all cases I am considered human to them now. This goes back to the Proven rule but it needs to be said again: just because they are in the mush hunt, they are not automatically human. Experienced players end up getting this over time and will point out if someone is not actually proven in the mush hunt, but too often nothing is said. A mush being in your mush hunt is disasterous. Be wary of your mush hunt, but not paranoid.

Don't be paranoid! This is important and I lesson I had to learn the hard way. I was pointing out every potential suspecious action on the ship in public chat. Dirty eating a ration? mushy! Entering and leaving shower dorm? mushy! Dirty killing a mush? mushy! This got me killed by the crew as an annoyance, or by the mush as a threat and I rarely lived long. Some paranoia is good, too much leads to alot of innocent death. If you are suspicious of things, point them out to chun or eleesha or save them for a private chat. If I am in the mush hunt and a player is doing this, it is easy for me to point out they are trying to get innocents killed and are being detrimental. It's a satisfying kill to me, but an entirely avoidable death to the crew.

Be Prepared That mimic mush is going to act eventually. The rampaging mush acts shortly after being selected and damages the ship in the most obvious ways: fires, dismantling shower, flying off in the pasiphae, etc. This is actually fairly easy to stabilize. The mimic mush can choose precisely when to strike and damage you at your most crucial moment. Day 5 and you are down to 6 O2? You better believe that last quiet mush will sabotage your O2 or move you past that savior O2 planet frieda just found. Anticipate that and you can forsee what the mush will do before they do it.

In the above instance, did that Kuan Ti from your mush chat just pick up the hacker kit? Are you sure he's proven?
