Hello everyone! On a random ship with some very nice people, I was able to run some very specific tests on: Pilot Drones. BUT, what I found was pretty interesting, interesting enough to share my findings, so here they are!:
Note: I'm only going to be going over functions of drones that are not (commonly) known, so if you are looking for basic functions of pilot drones, I suggest looking at Mushpedia!

[Mutliple] Pilot drones are able to be held in your inventory when taking off with a patroller.
If you are holding pilot drones (anywhere from 1-3) when taking off, ALL drones will use the patroller to shoot at hunters!
A Pilot Drone in your inventory will land for you at cycle change, but only if your patroller has no charges or there are no ships outside (this including trading vessels).
However.... Whenever a Pilot Drone shoots at a trading vessel, it will NOT flee at cycle change.

Pilot drones are probably good idea if you have no problems dealing with hunters paradoxally. Also only ships with good pilots will be able to afford all upgrades for drones most of the time anyway. They're perfect for finishing 1-2 hunters or asteroids so pilots can save AP on taking off/landing. And ships with 2 or more fully upgraded drones hardly ever have problemswith repairs till D20.

Also you probably don't want more than 2 pilot drones, you want to still have at least one to be always on Daedalus, just in case there's something critical to repair.

I can only imagine them being hurtful in excessive amounts (3-4 is top amount you can have and get away with it, otherwise you would be blocking patrollers for pilots, especially post D15)

In theory on very long a trip (something like D30+, I can't tell) it's possible to encounter so many hunters that you would start running out of charges in patrollers, it's probably not worth getting them if you're aiming for that but this is realistic scenario for very few random ships.
