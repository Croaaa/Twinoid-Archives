My MMM ship with a Lethargic Chun ended. (If you are interested you can find it with the name "Derek's Virgin Journey".

I have said before that after my ship ended I'll make an assesment of Chun's Lethargic skill and current situation, and I did. I had played her before the fix, and now after it. I'm writing this because it split the community, but I think most people didn't play it after the patch, just reacted and there were some unknown facts about it. Sure, some of the things here have been mentioned, and before I begin:
No, I will not mention that it should be OP because Chun has no other useable skills (which I agree but doesn't add anything new to the discussion) and no refunds and other "worth" stuff.

With that out of the way, let's jam!


Currently the Lethargy works by requiring you to lay down in the bed and not do ANY action, since everything will make you stand up from the bed, breaking the chain. I said before that "I layed down to a bed on cycle 1. Without ever waking up or moving, I only got the Lethargy bonus on cycle 6, meaning that on cycle 2-5 I had the simple bonus of the bed." This means you have to lay down on your bed for most of the day, and only being able to act once (when your AP is full) if you want to be efficent. And herein lies the problem.

This was embarassingly hard to pull off. Not just because you NEED to lay on your bed for many cycles, but the wind up time is just ridiculous. Laying down on the beginning of the day and only getting the bonus on cycle 6 means that you will lay there for more, since at that point you have only gotten the basic +1AP and just started getting the bonus, which of course now you want to gain for the longest possible time, when your AP is full.
The double AP gauge helps with this, as otherwise you would be sleeping for only a minor gain, but this way you can have 24AP to spend at once.

Question is, can you spend it at once?

Sometimes. There are inherently AP intensive jobs like scanning for planets, being a pilot and possible opening capsules. All three you can do by yourself, and for one you need the pilot skill to be acceptable. I mentioned these three because on these she brightly shines and does it exceptionally well with the 24AP and these do not need to be done instantly, allowing you to wait as you bank the AP.
Other obvious example would be researching/projects/Pilgred. These require the cooperation of your crew, which I did not have. Why? Because with Lethargy you can't just get up and do work, you have an exceptionally long wind up time, requiring you to coordinate "hey, I'll get up on cycle 8, please relay with me that cycle". On our ship, Andie did not do that, as IRL it was a bad timing. This happened for several days before I changed my cycle for it to happen sometime else, but even then she had less Ap than me, and Chun's % is basic on everything. Loads of relays happened, but I felt that Ian-Finola (less with Andie) would have about the same boost to research. One major thing: Chun has to be in the laboratory for various researches, making most Chun players idle in the room. Is there a bed in the room so that Lethargy can be used? No. I don't think I need to add more to that.

Same with projects, although I did not do that on our ship, but I can assume not much changes.

So with these, you NEED someone else, since your base % are mediocre and quickly go to 0%. This entirely depends on coordination and your teammates's help, which varies wildly on ship by ship. It may be exceptional, or lackluster if you can spend all your AP with a skilled biologist/designer.

I have covered the Actions to spend your AP on. These can be done anytime, allowing you to plan for them. Now comes the Timing aspect of Lethargy.

You are the primary anti-mush. Communication, your main tool can still be done, so I don't think it hurts that parts as some say here. Actually, with that 24AP and a knife, you can pretty much kill any armored foe, so as a planned killer she can be great. If the mush acts first, well you have to break your chain and hope you have enough Ap to deal with, but that's the same situation with everyone else.
Secondary, Chun is expected to heal. Well damn you day 10+! This is pretty much the deal breaker here for the skill. Since most people don't check in every (or 2nd) cycle, late game healing will be pretty mandatory and instant as one steel plating means that the person may not live trough a second one and requires attention. Of course, pilots have their hands full, expedition crew is hurt and such, so you will rarely if ever have the time to be a primary healer of the ship. With Andie here, you simply do NOT have a primary healer. Sure Chun can pick Nurse for the free heals, but that still means you need to get up and break the 5 cycle chain, and after that point the AP saving isn't needed. It was pretty sweet when I went around with 24 Ap and healed EVERYONE (9 crewmembers) to full health, some requiring 2 or more heals, but it only happened once since...you know, people don't like being on 6 or less HP, requiring you to heal less, but more often.
Same thing with fires, those need immediate attention.

To recap, Chun is now able to spend a burst of AP after much preparation. This is possible in the early days, barely manageable in the late days (and we did not even go 15+ days). The wind up time is just way too much for it to be acceptable, and I can only think that on a less active ship (your normal midday starter ship), it is only harder to pull of.

Personally, I think if she required less time for the effect to kick in, it would be useable. Hell, throw out the double AP capability and give her only 2 cycles before the +AP comes and it is much useable.
Of course, it is my personal, active opinion, with me checking on the ship multiple times each day. It has been noted that this was done for the more passive players, but I honestly, with all my heart think that it just doesn't work that way. It is her level 5 skills, requring lots of XP to be unlocked, meaning that anyone using it will know how the game works. You wish to be a passive player? Pick Frieda or Paola, bank your AP, spend it on comms or scanning, back to sleep, repeat. It works great for them because they do not need anyone's else to do their job. If it was aimed at the new players with a chance to learn the game without that much commitment, then it wouldn't require multiple months of gameplay to unlock.

So....what can we do? You can pray for an active crew that allows you to bank your AP and use it on something with somebody. Sofa can also be build in the laboraty which I think is the best thing to do, but there is only one sofa, and even if Paola could walk to dorms/medlab bed and have Frieda stand there for the logistics, I can already hear the cries of players of Paola about their precious sofa.
Apart from these two ideas, I have no idea how it can be helped. Communication and coordination is the key, otherwise it'll just fall apart, just like any ship, but it is a gamble nonetheless.
