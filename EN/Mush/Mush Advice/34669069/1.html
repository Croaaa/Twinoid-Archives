While I've never actually played Chun, I have had the opportunity to observe her play on plenty of ships, and this is the advice I've gleaned:
~As mentioned above, her first skill (Mankind's Only Hope) is vital to the well-being of the ship. People will likely get upset if you play without choosing it.
~Chun is the only one guaranteed not to be Mush. This means that you will be not only central to the anti-Mush efforts, but also a major leader of the Mush hunt. Chun's primary role seems to be to safeguard items the players' do not want to fall into Mush hands and to spearhead the Mush hunt. People know they can discuss suspicions with her without accidentally giving stuff away to the Mush.
~Plan to spend a lot of time in the research lab. Chun has to be present for a number of the anti-mush researches to appear, so a regular (or constant) presence there is necessary. As a corollary, Chun will mainly spend her AP helping with research. She may also be asked to cook, if it's prior to all the Mush being found.

Chun's best skills are unique to her: Immunity, Humanity's last hope, Lethargy.

Immunity, and the research unlocked by her, are vital to a human success. Absolutely nothing can beat her inability to be infected at all. Chun can cook, eat food from, check shelves, and rest anywhere with relative impunity. This is a huge, huge bonus. Likewise, she is the only single person who can hold on to certain vital items (the hacker kit) and 100% reliably be counted on to hide Mycoalarms.

Humanity's Last Hope is right up there with Shrink and Leader as one of the best ship-wide skills in the game. Halving daily morale loss can drastically make a game easier, and quite frankly it beats out the Marginal benefit of Premonition and the small benefit of Nurse.

Lethergy is considered one of the top-tier skills in the game. An experianced chun can get more AP by resting than anyone else in the game except a caffeine junkie Gioele, and unlike Gioele she will ALWAYS use that AP for the human team. Additionally, Lethargy allows chun to explode in a MASSIVE flurry of movement, which can catch the Mush entirely off-guard.

As Chun, consider checking on the engine room every couple of days, if your Raluca is active and sane. (to make sure your Raluca is sane, heh). Progress on Pilgred should be slow and steady. If your Raluca has both Physicist and Technician skills, the reactor should get just under 10% progress every game day and should need a reset every other day (this is assuming Raluca uses only free Pilgred points on Pilgred and puts her AP into useful other things). With only Physicist or only Tech skill, progress is 5-7% per repair. Pilgred getting done 'too fast' is a danger, and too slow can be bad too.

But anyone else checking on Raluca and resetting the engine could be spiking Raluca or trapping the room, so a Chun-checkup can be a boon.
