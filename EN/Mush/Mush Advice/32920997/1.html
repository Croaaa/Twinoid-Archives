Enfilade

Warning signs of mushiness include the following:

  • Mush extracting a spore in front of a person or camera (even another mush). This act will be recorded for all to see.
  • Mush spiking a human with a camera, mycoalarm, or person with Observer skill (25% chance). This act will also be recorded for all to see.
  • Mush become dirty after extracting, even if they have the apron. People who are habitually dirty are suspicious, especially if they have no excuse to be; however, that is not hard evidence they are guilty.
  • Mush normally take 3-4 dmg from showers. Someone who is fully healed and then is damaged after showering may be a mush. This is not guaranteed! (a) Mush can use Phaogcyte after showering to heal immediately and invisibly; (b) Mush with the Waterproof skill won't take damage; (c) You can become damaged picking up the soap or showering. Finally, there is no confirmation for someone showering, so unless you can verify they were dirty to begin with, you cannot prove a shower occured.
  • Mush do not become hungry, nor do they normally lose morale.
  • Mush can eat, but not more than one food item at a time, regardless of satisfaction level.
  • Mush cannot contract diseases or disorders, although they retain any they may have started the game with (e.g., Eleesha's chronic vertigo).
  • Mush have the option of sabotaging equipment, which humans do not have.

Sagittaeri

shadddy, this is more challenging because it requires you to stalk ALL their activites, just so you can count their AP & Morale.

If it seems like they have less AP than they should (because they spent AP in mushie activities, or because they don't regain AP from eating rations), then they are a candidate. Whenever someone tells you "I have no AP left" or something similar, keep track of that, then find out how many AP they should have, based on how long they've slept on the bed, and stuff.

Same thing goes for Morale. If it seems like they have more Morale than they should have, then they are a suspect. Keep an eye on everyone who has never bothered getting a Morale boost from Janice. This method is a lot slower (because Morale goes down slowly), so it's only useful for mush that survives for a LONG time.

[7950824]

If you can spare a pill or a banana and a ration, you can do a test using these objects if the -to be tested- player cooperates.

Mush get full if they eat anything, but a human can eat a pill or a banana and then eat a ration, assuming the player is not already full. Set up so the player knows not to eat for a few cycles before the test (might as well have low satiation just in case). I think it's fine if the player wants to pick up and cook the ration themself (wouldn't that be a nasty trick, being a human about to take this test, pop the pill, then eat the ration - and the ration has spores to turn you Mush right then... course you -just- passed a human test too... better never get retested wink!)

I don't know if any sort of fruit can work for this, or if it's just bananas. But I know I ate two bananas in a row and still was not full while playing as a human before. And I've eaten a pill as a Mush and did get full just from the pill.

This test is heavy on resources (can you spare the two items for it?) but it's the sort of test I'd lean towards if I wanted to test someone.
