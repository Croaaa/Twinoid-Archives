I agree with the last part of npfan's message, and only partially with the first part, unless I didn't understand it right.

The known facts about hunters, at least the facts provided by the devs (and therefore the way hunters are supposed to work), are :

Normal hunters (those that appear when the skies are clear and the ship isn't moving) attack after two cycles. This works perfectly as intended, and without a doubt.

Escaping hunters divides their number by two, or by four with Trail Minimizer. This also works as intended. The escape mechanism was designed long before the game opened and was tested on countless ships, including by the devs. One of them said that she usually escaped hunters rather than fighting them, and if it had been bugged, she would have noticed, and so would players. We do see people complaining occasionally that they get identical bigger waves after escaping. But when something is bugged, you don't get occasional reports over a few years, you get dozens of reports over a few days or weeks.

The size of hunter waves increases with time, but also according to the crew's activity (the number of AP spent), the size of the previous waves, and other factors that are less clear (maybe the pilots' reactivity, how efficient they are or how fast they react). Chance also plays a part in the generation of hunters (and other obstacles). That is also true, at least the part about the influence of time and of the previous decisions of the RNG. Waves (not their daily number, but their size) increase regularly, although not always continuously. They will be bigger and bigger, but sometimes, due to chance, you can have more hunters one day, or several waves, even without moving. And we all know that one day without hunters is a bad sign and means that a much bigger wave will be coming the next day. Then things usually come back to normal during the following days.


Now, a few observations (and theories) :

Moving the ship almost always triggers a new wave. That is also a fact that we have all noticed (although I'm having a very weird casting ship which has almost always zero hunters after moving, and yet we're on D12, but that's the first time ever I've seen that happen). My explanation is close to Sperbi's. I think that it's an unintended consequence of the escape mechanism : the game is programmed to divide hunters by two when the ship escapes. So, when she ship moves, the RNG thinks that it is escaping. It invents a wave (that we don't see, since it's mid-cycle and invented), and then divides it. That explanation is a theory, but there is evidence for it :

  • those waves are usually smaller than the regular waves, sometimes just 2 or 3 hunters.
  • as Sperbi also said, the impact of those asteroids is 5 cycles and hunters attack at the next cycle change (which proves that they are one cycle "younger" than they seem).
  • the same thing happens when you escape hunters : they attack at the next cycle change, not after 2 cycles.
  • also similar : the way Strateguru triggers. A normal wave is generated at the beginning of the new cycle (here, at the beginning of D13C1, with the gain of AP, MP, glory, plant production, etc.). When the ship moves (either to escape or just to move to or from a planet), hunters appear at the end of the previous cycle, just before cycle change. You can also have a double wave, with two announcements. This explains why sometimes waves seem identical or bigger : in fact, they are the combination of a reduced old wave (re-generated and calculated before cycle change) and of a new one (generated after).

The consequence, as npfan said, is that if you often move the ship into and out of orbit, hunters will be much more manageable, for several reasons :

  • you get small waves after moving.
  • they are taken into account when the RNG generates the normal waves, which also makes them smaller.

Most ships that went for Eden or tried can testify of that. If the ship moves constantly, there are always hunters around, but rarely big waves. Huge waves mostly appear when the ship stops exploring and therefore moves less often or not at all.

As for the fact that hunters catch up with the ship, I am not completely sure that you have proven it. You did prove that old, damaged hunters show up. But it can mean two things :

  • when you escape, old hunters eventually catch up with the ship and increase the size of waves ;
  • or the waves increase (more or less) regularly, as intended, and include old hunters but are not especially bigger.

Numbers from several ships would be necessary to show which is true.

Aall in all, I disagree about escaping. Of course, it depends on the number of pilots, and it's also different if you explore a lot. But for a ship that mostly stays still and has two or three pilots, I have always found that the best technique in later days is to kill aracks and trax, move, and kill the rest. It always means less hunters to kill and I have rarely noticed a significance or abnormal increase, except when there are no hunters at all for one or two days. But in that case, the sudden increase is due to the total absence of hunters for 8 cycles or more, and to the RNG catching up to the average.

As for Trail Minimizer, since it merely improves an existing mechanism, it has no side-effect. It just decreases the number of hunters that eventually catch up.

Sorry for the wall of text (and I still have a couple of things to add, so second post incoming ).
