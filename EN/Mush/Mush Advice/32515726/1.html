Rain

Yep, Frieda basically stays on the bridge scanning for planets. Ideally, Paola is also on the bridge and no one else, and Paola has the logistics skill, which basically gives 1 ap per cycle to someone in the same room as her. However-! That's the ideal to strive for, not likely on current random ships. So try to snag a bed in a dorm and use your AP on planet scanning. It's a really good idea to create one thread and favorite it, and use it for reporting scanning results to the crew, because only you can see them at the terminal.

The first thing you'll really want to find is a planet with oxygen. Once you find one that has O2 and does not have mankarog, seismic activity, or volcanos, you can stop scanning, it's time to get an expedition to that planet going. When you move the ship, you lose all planets you've found, so trying to find your next planet after that one would be a waste. After the expedition, get your captain to leave orbit and start again. What you're looking for then will depend on your ship, but oxygen and hydrocarbon are never a bad idea early on.

Rain

The scanner's death shouldn't have an impact. In general, yes, the scanner says what direction and how much fuel, someone loads the fuel into the engine and then your commander (or someone with a hacker kit) moves the ship. If everything has been done correctly, you will immediately see a planet in the background.

There are a few possibilities here.

1) Is there a planet in the background? If there is and you can't launch an expedition, has a cycle passed since the ship was moved? You reach the plant at the beginning of the cycle after the ship is moved. Also, are you a pilot? Unless your NERON admin has changed settings, only pilots can launch expeditions, with 4 AP.

2) If there isn't a planet in the background- a couple of things could have happened, but something went wrong during the process of getting to the planet. If you moved somewhere else first, then all planet coordinates are no longer valid. There's also the possibility that whoever reported the planet was lying, especially if they were mush. Or, perhaps the wrong amount of fuel was loaded. Basically, things can go wrong at each step.

If the ship moves and there is no planet, there are several possible explanations :

- The astrophysicist gave the wrong heading or distance by mistake. Impossible to check (not in your case, since it was your planet).

- The person who loaded the fuel put the wrong amount, by mistake or on purpose. You can check that in the engine room, because when someone loads fuel, there is a public message in the log giving the level of the combustion chamber. You can also see if the amount of fuel left in the ship's tanks is right.

- They could have loaded the right amount but then retrieved some fuel. That message is private, so it is invisible for other people. Someone else could also have retrieved it, and you can check who went to the engine room after fuel was loaded (also check who went to the dorm and took a shower, or may have taken a shower discreetly).

- The commander chose the wrong heading or didn't change it at all. Easy to check too, since there is a Neron announcement when the ship's heading changes (unless of course the previous planet was in the same direction). Or maybe he chose the right heading but instead of validating it, he immediately clicked on "advance" (happened to me once, and nearly happened several times ).
