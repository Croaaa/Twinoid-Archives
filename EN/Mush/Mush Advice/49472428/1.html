Quantum Sensors is the better project. CPU Overclocking only affects the initial planet scan, which is 1 ( sometimes 2) AP saved per planet. However, Quantum Sensors adds a zone to each and every scan action. This is obviously more valuable for bigger planets than small ones in terms of AP savings, but it's super satisfying to have for the scanners. You'll uncover more faster, which means if the planet has a death zone you'll probably find it sooner and get to bail on the scan earlier.

That's common misconception. The truth is, CPU Overclocking doesn't help with scanning the zones of planet, but just scanning for the planet. So you just save 1 AP per planet with it.
On the other hand, Quantum sensors usually save much more as they uncover one more zone every time you scan the zones. So they are generally better.

Of course, there might be unprobable scenarios, when CPU overclocking might be more useful (generally any scenarios when you're looking for planet with other attributes then just zones - how big it is, direction or amount of fuel needed - so you want to sift through many of them without even scanning the zones).

Abinmorth

ideally you should only go scanning when you got full ap or you risk losing the planet, if the ship is moving.

with full 12 ap Astro players can do
1 scan (2ap) + 5 analyze (10ap) * 3 = they can fully analyze planets with max 15 zones if BIOS enabled and not in orbit. (20 zones with QS, 15 zones with CPU)
if scanning fails once, they can still fully analyze planets with max 12 zones (QS: 16 zones, CPU: CPU: 15 zones)
if scanning fails twice, they can fully analyze planets with max 9 zones (QS: 12 zones, CPU: 12 zones.)

Non-Astro:
1 scan (3ap) + 4 analyze (8ap) * 2 + 1 AP left = they can scan planets with upto 8 zones if BIOS enabled (QS: 12 zones, CPU: 10 zones)
if scanning fails once, they can fully analyze planets with max 6 zones (QS: 9 zones, CPU: 8 zones)
if scanning fails twice, they can fully analyze planets max 2 zones (QS: 3 zones, CPU: 6 zones)

Fazit:
If you have active Astrophysicists, then QS is always the better choice.
If Non-Astros have to do the job, then CPU is better, because you will have some planets where you fail scanning/want to discard more than once.

In my opinion you're mostly right, but only focusing on one full AP-bar - completely ignoring means to gain AP/start off with less.

Usually rations, bananas and coffee are even available in the early stages of a ship and later Jumpkins and practically an infinite amount of coffee. Also there's the possibility to have less(don't want to overspill, AP/MP conversion to even get to the Bridge,...). And I won't even bother mentioning the use of bed/Logistics while scanning/analyzing over a cycle change, which is pretty common(Paola/Frieda). Oh, and I almost forgot about IT Expert...

The process of scanning/analyzing is almost never done with 12 AP. Personally I like to have at least a ration available, if I find a slightly too big planet or fail more scans than expected.

Therefore it'd be better to calculate with the size of the planets and the proportion of discarded ones or simply: Do the Astros/non-Astros discard before scanning or not? For the former case CPU Overclocking and for the latter QS are the better choice.

I wouldn't aim for the optimized use of one full AP bar regarding revealed zones on one planet, but to keep the used AP as low as possible as mean to keep the difficulty of the game lower.
