TradeFederationYou should only report hard core griefing, though.

I have to disagree on that. You can't always be sure of whether someone is simply new and uncommunicative, or whether their actions are not just deliberate, but premeditated. There are things you just can't possibly see unless you have access to the logs. That's why it's better to report cases of potential griefing, and let the SDF sort it out.

As an actual example, I'll cite a case in I was personally involved in, but recused myself from ruling on. I should note that I am not aware of the results of the reports, nor do I know if sanctions were given to the players in question.

On one of my ships, we had two griefers, that did more damage than the two Mush players. They hacked the BIOS and turned off death notifications, turned off the DMZ, and I presumed turned on food destruction...then attempted a killing spree. I say attempted, because I had the knife, and I helped end both of their attempts to kill another human player. I knew the player they attacked was human, because he was nothing but helpful. There was no reason to suspect him of being Mush. They even threw a grenade at him - and only at him.

I didn't know about the grenade attack until after the ship was over. At the time, I suspected it was a Mush that had turned off the death notifications, but I wasn't 100% sure until I skimmed the logs, and found out one of the griefers had done so, before the attacks started. Even knowing that in hindsight, I had recused myself from the case, because I was on that ship, after all.

But all the AP wasted in the efforts to kill both of these griefers was too much for us. it demoralized the crew, and I'm sure the players as well, to a degree. It greatly diverted our attention away from the Hunters that were putting multiple holes in our hull, and it deprived us of our only technician, since we were forced to kill him. No question to me that they were griefing, but as I said...they did more damage than the Mush players.
