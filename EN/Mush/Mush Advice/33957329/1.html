Reporting someone for an apparent good reason to report them isn't bad, even if that person is innocent. You do -not- need to feel bad about reporting someone with reason. You're doing players as a whole a favor and helping to educate people who need it; you're making all of our future games a little bit better.

Reporting people doesn't 'get them in trouble', it gets them investigated.

When that investigation happens, the evidence is looked at, and determined to show right or wrong doing.

If it's right doing on all sides, there's absolutely no harm done.

If there's wrong doing, that gets verified and a decision is made to sanction or not. Apparently most players get -several- warnings before being limited in their ability to play, depending on the severity of the misconduct. This serves the wonderful purpose of ensuring these players get to learn the rules in a way they cannot possibly misunderstand or skip over. Good players will learn; that's what the warnings are for. It's only the determined bad players who are going to continue to mess up, hopefully continue to be reported every time they play in ways that break the rules and can ruin the game, and hopefully get removed as fast as possible.

If your Hua lied when she said 'was a random kill' then the investigation will probably be able to determine that - at worst Hua might get a warning to not claim it's random because that makes it look like she's breaking the rules, even though she actually was not.

If your Hua told the truth about it being a random kill - then she probably does deserve a warning that this is bad play - odds are on most ships at any given point a 'random kill' is much more likely to kill a human than a mush. Statistically it's going to cause her ships and herself problems. I know I would prefer not to play beside a 'random killer' type of player, and I would be quite willing to label such a one as 'space sick or a Mush' who needs to die immediately - and I would report them for the random kill with complete comfort.
