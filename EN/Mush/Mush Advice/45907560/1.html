Carrots (motivation) is great, but don't forget the stick (facing risk of punishment), either.

If your team is going to lose because of the purposeful, intentional, avoidable inactions of one member of your team - that member is playing against the team. Playing against the team is sanctionable.

By all means, try to come to a positive outcome. If that is impossible, report them.

If you feel that you triggered that behavior in some way, for example, you had a conversation with that player and you feel that your conversation broke rules (OMG, I shouldn't have called you that!) and led to that player deciding to play against their team.... that still does not mean that player has the right to break rules themselves. Each of you would be judged separately.

At worst, if your conscience requires it.... Report them and yourself. Explain why with both reports - I mean it, if your sense of justice requires you to admit that you messed up, then okay, do so, but explain that so it's easy to understand. If you don't end up getting a warning or a sanction, then the judges don't feel you broke rules, otherwise you'll get confirmation that yep, you were out of line, and you'll get the punishment you get for it.

Hey, laugh if you want - there are people with that sort of morale code, that applies to themselves as well.
