1) "accidents " (rooms hakes, steel plates falling on peoples, fires, electric spikes, panic attacks...) are quite rare the first days and become more frequent with time, if your ship last long enough you can face more and more accidents

3) some of thoses accidents are spread over the crew (steel plates, panic attack...) when there's lot of death you can find thoses accidents hitting you more often than when you are with a full crew

As with anything random (odds/percentage-based), you're also going to run into issues where some things will occur above average or below average during the period of a ship's life. Everything above is true, but this bell curve can certainly be extrapolated and applied to ships. How many oxygen planets have we found? Frequency and quantity of hunters? Cycle change causing lost connection with Sol? How many times you hurt yourself picking up items? How many crew members die to MIA events?

Most anything you can think of can present itself as more or less intense/frequent than average over the course of any given ship.
