No, it doesn't work that way. Leaving and entering the room twenty times won't work either. The skill only enables you to notice actions that were already visible. For example, if Andie is alone and hides the sample in a room with no camera, you won't see anything. If there is someone else in the room and Andie hides it, the action is visible and Observant might trigger the message "Eleesha has noticed something interesting" later.

As for covert actions (like spiking), they can only be revealed at the moment when they happen.

I'll describe how I like to use observant as Eleesha. Because most players consider it to be a passive skill. I see it as quite aggressive way to both hunt mush and play as mush.

First of all, observant has a 25% chance to detect spiking. So if there is no camera in the room, Eleesha can stay there to provide small amount of protection from mush spiking. It particularly works well with Ian in a room together, because his pacifist makes spiking cost mush 3 ap. This gives Eleesha a reason to attend expeditions early on, in case Icarus Bay is unprotected.

I like to hide this skill and never take it immediately, if I can help it, to trap mush, that tries to infect me later. It's only a 25% chance, but you'll be surprised how many times it worked. So what I seldom do, is take it before pushing Terrence for example. Or relay somebody in a room. Mush may not spot you took observant, and try to spike, logging it's action.

As mush, observant allows you to uncover everything human team hid, trying to prevent mush rampage. Take scooter, and scout the rooms, taking their weapons, blueprints, hacker kit. So having observant Eleesha on a mush team is good if you are about to loose your cover.

But take note, that observant may log your partner actions as well. So make sure to leave the room, before your mush partners spikes, to avoid the risk.
