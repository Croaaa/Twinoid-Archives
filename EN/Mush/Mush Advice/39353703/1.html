Typical jerkish wall of text incoming. I expect plenty of anger and disagreement in response to this.

This is one of those debates that always makes me facepalm everytime it's brought up in a ship because all it does is aid the Mush.

Some of the posts in this thread take the moral highground: fairness towards the mush, the fun factor being removed, how it's "bad mush hunting", etc. Here's the thing - on any ship, the mush will screw over the humans in the best way they physically can. It is their job to ruin the ship and set it on a course for disaster in the best way possible, anything else is playing against their team. Humans should feel no sympathy for the mush's game then.

A mush will not hesitate to (if a ship has no firefighters) take the extinguishers, set fires all over, and jet off in the pasiphae immediately after mush selection if that is the best way of screwing the ship over. Is that fun for the humans? Is that fair for the humans? It doesn't matter - it is the mush playing optimally and it's to be expected. Humans need to play the same way.

So doing voluntary food tests - is that fun for the mush? Is it particuarly fair? Once again, it does not matter since it is the job of the humans to eliminate the mush and survive in the most optimal way. Fairness and fun be damned - if the whole game was about the mush hunt it would be over when the mush are dead. This isn't Mafia, the mush are just another hazard to be dealt with like hunters. I think we wouldn't be having a lot of these moral issues if the Mush were NPCs, but no we have to go with what is fair for them or what makes the mush hunt more fun.

When the game starts and you make your little posts on your soapbox about how you wont be doing any mandatory food tests and you disagree with them in general, all it does is put the mush a bit more at ease. This is not a good thing. Mush that are at ease and not on edge are more likely to spore freely, shower more often, and infect crewmembers. The mush need to feel choked and limited on their actions, they should not feel lax.

I used to play a torturer Chao, and I would just stand in the dorm with the shower and I verbally said at the beginning of the games that I would torture anyone that entered. What did they end up doing? It would make the mush either not get dirty and spore, or they would feel trapped and try to rampage (the mush hopeless enough to do this were always so poor at their jobs that there would be very few deaths).

A mush rampage is not this terrible catastrophe, they are normally easily survivable with few deaths. And an outed mush early on greatly limits spore production and infections (additional mush are the greatest threat to the crew period, and im willing to argue that one for days).

Everyone needs to realize that the phrase "a forced mush test is reportable" is way too often misconstrued. If I kill you for not testing when I demand it, feel free to report me - but I assure you that I have listed already all the reasons I want you to test in a private message with chun or the rest of the mush hunters. No one demands tests without ANY proof - and asking for it is a lost cause on your part. I am not obliged to give you my reasons, they are in the mush hunt thread or chun is aware. It isn't a fair trial or anything of the sort. It's a simple ultimatum. So I can kill you without verbally telling you why, if you do not mush test. This seems unfair, but it is completely allowed.

That said, I never force mush tests. I have no need to. If I suspect you enough to warrant me asking you to do one, I am not going to take the risk of you panicking and rampaging - I will just list my reasons privately to chun/the mush hunt and kill you. I still believe though, that spouting out at the start that there should be no mandatory mush tests only serves to hurt the crew with no actual gain. It's a nice, morally upright view and I will applaud your sense of fairness but I will still think you are utterly foolish for anouncing it to the ship. Sorry if I offend over this but it's just the way it is.

And there are no logical reasons why voluntary food tests should be frowned upon. it temporarily proves one possible suspect to be human and makes one more set of trusted eyes that can be brought into the mush hunt, while narrowing possible suspects. Assuming them to be proven the whole game though, is absolutely foolish on the part of humans. But for a time, at the very least, they can be trusted fully. There are no gameplay reasons to be against this as a human other than moral or fun-based ones. I will never criticize you for not doing this on the ship, but I wish the reverse were true and players would stop criticizing their crewmembers for doing voluntary food tests, especially if the player is human.

TL;DR There is nothing wrong with voluntary mush tests and it should not be criticized if it is done. And if you don't want to do it, all the power to you. But, if you want to stand on a soapbox about your views of fairness or "funness" for the mush, take it to the forums instead of broadcasting it day 1 on the ship.
