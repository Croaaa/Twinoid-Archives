Alright. I'll list the things I've found out, and the things I'm guessing about. Things that have happened through play:

Don't hesitate. If you think you have the mush, kill him as soon as possible. Occasionally you'll be wrong, but if you think you know who it is kill it. They will not refrain from rampaging if they think you know them. Once, a great ship figured out both mush. They wanted to cure one, but Raluca didn't have genius. They were about to kill them when they rampaged with a transfer to inactive Derek, killing all the techs(there were 4 to 5). Another ship we lost our only diplomat when we had all but figured out the mush(through cross-referencing potential jelly suspects and the only other person had no real mushy actions).

Green Jelly: If a camera breaks early, I'd almost guarantee jelly. See who could have done it, by checking who was standing in the room for the 4 cycles before it broke. Then, check who was standing up after it broke. If you repair it quickly, there may be no mush in category two, but it will give potential suspects. If cameras break twice, compare who was in both rooms in the time before it broke. This will rarely make a lot of suspects, so you can generally get a pretty good idea for at least one of the mush.
Stuff I assume from being mush:

Look for people that get dirty around once per day. A mush will usually only want to be dirty this often to extract spores, as usually it's all done in one go. People in Bravo Dorm(or Alpha if the shower's there) will be the bane of your existence. They can report being dirty rarely and stealth shower to cover it. Watch these people, as you can't get them from being dirty.

AP counting: This can work, but if you make a mistake it can completely screw over your team. I actually allowed a mush Eden to happen because I miscounted a potential mush's AP and said she wouldn't have the AP to triple spore the ration and we had mycoscan, so if she did less she'd be caught. Figure out how much AP they seemed to have used and how much they should have used. There can be some left in the tank from not being active for a few cycles, so this will be a tedious process if you don't ask them to report it themselves.

That's a couple things I've figured. Here's it shortened:
Don't hesitate to kill suspicious subjects unless they're super helpful and could be cured.
If a camera breaks, it's probably green jelly.
Getting dirty around once a day. This is really the only times mush want to be dirty, as they only need to take spores once.
AP counting can work, but is tedious and a mistake could completely ruin you.
Verifying humans without food tests mostly involves people that get sick from expos or random events(vomiting/when you heal them there's a message). That can get you a few people, but they can still be converted, and they'll relay all your circle of trust information to their mush allies if they are.

There are two things I often do when mush hunting.
Copy all logs to a spreadsheet.
You don't have to be Eleesha to do this or make sense of it, just makes it easier. I do it whenever I am playing someone who is not confined to one area... and even then sometimes, especially with places like bravo dorm if I have access, and refectory. It takes some practice to make sense of raw logs, but it does give you a nice big picture of what people are doing. If you are suspicious about a certain person, you can reference it and get a general idea of what they've been doing.

Make a list with every non-chun person on it. Note the things they do that look mushy, the things that make them look non-mush, and general feelings you get about that person.
Any other information you think is relevant and can track is a bonus. This will help with the tendency to not notice the quiet ones so much, and again, give you an overall picture of each person aboard the ship. I don't do this so often these days because to be honest, I've gotten comfortable enough with the logs that I often don't really feel I need to.... but it does help, especially if you can't collect many logs.

Talk with people.
This one may not help you so much Streaky, given how taciturn you seemed to be shipside, but... I like to talk with people one on one to gauge them. Can just start with something like asking if they are experienced with their role and if they have any questions.... or if they have any thoughts on who the mush might be. Or heck, just to chat. Maybe they saw something you didn't. Or maybe they will look dodgy. Any which way, gives you a better idea of who and how that person is, and this is information you can use. And yes, that will give certain people a chance to manipulate you but... that's part of the fun of it, eh? ^^

Keep track of who your newbies are.
Yes, keep in mind that they could be pretending to be newbie to hide suspicious activity. Although this is a deplorable practice, some people do do it. But in general, think about the things they are doing and think 'could I see a newbie reasonably making this mistake?' I often ask in gen who our newer players are and start a pm with any who respond right off the bat. This isn't really related to mush hunting so much as mentoring new players, but an awareness of newbies will help avoid innocent deaths.

My favorite mush hunting strategies are rather off the cuff.

It seems to me that a lot of players play with pretty high paranoia and I think that really weakens the human team; of the 15 possible mush at game start, 13 are human for sure. If you're human, you have 13 other allies, you just don't know exactly which 13 save for Chun, and Chun just knows that 13 others are allies at the start.

I use that, and use it extensively in my mush hunting. Any of us could be weaker players, any of us could be mush. But I gather typically 5-6 players into a PM, myself and Chun (unless Chun was griefer) and then a handful of the others, who seem to be stronger players, active, concerned, involved.

It's an anything chat, we deal with possible griefers and I at least discuss evidence in it about possible mush; I encourage everyone to discuss what they see, and remind that we're not confirmed human here; if any group member has a concern about any other group member, I encourage them to make another PM with everyone but the worrisome player and lets discuss that.

For me that seems to work really well. The team tends to be smarter and more observant than any one player; that 5-6 member group selected mostly from interested, active, involved players tends to contain a number of better players (some better players don't choose to show themselves that way, that's fine, but many of your better players can be identified by those traits; and the ones with those traits who are new are going to be better players after they play a few games).

Sometimes that group contains a mush! And that's actually fine. Sure, Mr Mush knows what we're initially looking at and who the leaders are, but there's 4-5 leaders besides himself. We tend to be scattered all over the ship, a rampage to kill us all won't be easy, and now Mr Mush's thoughts, not just his actions are exposed. He has to talk about what he sees and doesn't see, what he thinks is mushy or not about what others (and possibly his partner) do, and he has to seem to have human priorities while he does so, with multiple smart minds able to read his words and see his actions, and judge if he's really 'one of us' or not.

For me that seems a great tool to catch mush, no matter if the mush has been brought into the group or not. And such groups tend to help limit the number of miskills the ship does, because many sets of eyes are gathering and discussing evidence, so more ideas about that behavior and more evidence in total is usually being considered.

So the attitude of 'can't trust anyone, so don't talk to the others', resisting others, just treating everyone on the ship like they are probably (instead of possibly) mush.... for me that seems like such a waste of a team that can be built, most of the players in it forming the core that will work together and lead the ship to the end of its play. Start early, not late, and if one of the leader-types on your ship is mush, spotlight them and see what they do, that's my favorite strategy, at least.

There's no one "right" strategy, but anyone who has ever been on the same ship as me knows my favourite strategies: information control, social engineering and mind games.


INFORMATION CONTROL

Maybe I'm a control freak or something, but I like being in control of who has what information and when they have them. I like to be the person who knows everything. So, I gain the trust of my crew when I need to know something from them, and I conveniently only share the absolute minimum information with them, enough to protect them from the mushies but not enough for them to really harm my mush hunt if they turn out to be alpha or beta mush.

Incidentally, this also really helps me if someone ever turns me into a beta mush. Being in control of so much information while being mush is really fun.


SOCIAL ENGINEERING & MIND GAMES

Being the person in charge of information flow has benefits here: I'm usually able to create circumstances that allow me to either trap the mushies in a lie, or to expose humans to my spore traps, usually in some complex scheme. And I do so with glee. It is really, really fun, and it's hands down the most fun thing about this game! I highly recommend as many people try this sort of strategy out as possible.

This strategy works for both mush hunters and for mushies.


LOGS TRACING

Saving room logs is a lot of work, I don't actually do it that often anymore unless I'm playing the mush hunter. But, it really helps with the mush hunt...like, a lot! A lot of times we can't remember very specific yet very crucial details of events a long time ago, like whether or not someone ate in D2. If you saved the logs, then you'll always be able to recall these information/evidences any time you need them.

Not only that, sometimes you get the opportunity to catch someone in a lie when they say things like "oh, I ran out of APs". With the logs, you can check if they actually did anything to drain their APs without going anywhere. So, yeah, don't underestimate the power of saving room logs!

My thoughts on information control.

When I'm a mush, I don't need much information to win, and the info I do need is almost impossible to keep from me (how the game overall is going, what the ship's approximate weaknesses are). Hiding information like detailed roomlogs and clues as to who is maybe mushy; keeping that from everyone mostly serves to weaken the human team. And, worst of all, keeping it from me doesn't weaken me significantly. I can get the info I need to decide on a plan and to make it work just by my normal play; I don't need the type of information the human team could be collecting.

You often need detailed roomlogs and understanding of each player's actions, when human, to catch mush and identify mush from just mushy. The mush need that only to fine-tune their ability to specifically time converting one person over another; and that's only going to work well if that person is sloppy anyway; in which case the mush don't need to be told that the person's vunerable. If I intend to convert you, I will find a way or fail to find a way without your fancy roomlog collection.

As a human, I hate seeing humans killed unless they're griefer-level of bad. It's deeply important to me, usually, to spare the humans and kill the actual mush. We need those humans if they're active and communicating even a little; they're going to be part of the win later. I don't want humans killed for weaker play or weird choices, and I get really mad on my ships when people 'mark mush' and start killing without adequate evidence.

There's times when the mush (or griefers) give you no time, when you kill them or they do terrible damage, and they generally reveal themselves by starting to do terrible damage. That's different, way different. Those players are not the sort I'm talking about, there's a huge difference between the play of someone trying to take out the ship and the 'minor weirdness' of a player who doesn't really understand stuff and who is figuring out how to play and doing weird stuff. Kill the killers; identify the harmless weird and watch them and talk to them; sometimes those harmless weird are mush trying to hide. But many of them are not! So correct, rather than kill, is my preference, all the while watching them and everyone else.

What am I going to do with lots of information as a mush? Twist it so that humans are killed for being mushy? Maybe. That's about the only use I can think of. I don't need that information to win. But as a human? I need that information, and the main way I'll be using it is to identify who isn't mush, who is playing weird versus playing harmful. Not so easy to do that backwards, when I am mush. So don't keep information from me, when I'm human. You're only hurting yourself, really.

And when I'm mush? Keep all the information you want to yourself. You're only helping me.
