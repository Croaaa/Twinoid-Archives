Problem: This ship is going well. All mush are believed or proven dead and it is still quite early in the game, your ship has a long and possibly bright future ahead.

You refresh the page and see the end screen. Someone just returned the ship to Sol. There was no discussion; your ship was not about to be destroyed. Either you were wrong about all the mush being dead, and the mush just won.... or a human has made a Hostile Return to Sol.

Now you have a challenge on your hands. You're a responsible player, you report rule breakers, you feel that's the best way to make everyone's future games better. But who do you report?

You know you have to report the right person. That's just how reports work - in order for someone to be punished, you have to report that specific person. Not just someone. The judges can and will figure out who actually broke the rule, but they are only allowed to punish, or clear, the person with the report. So if the rule breaker isn't reported, that rule breaker goes free - this time.

Here's an [AP] thread giving an example of the situation, and why this is a problem: [AP] Freida's Cowardly Retreat

You don't want that to happen to you, should your ship face a hostile return to Sol - so here's what you can do about it.

Don't close your end screen - yet.

Check the Lounge. Does your ship have an [AP] thread yet? Check to see - chances are high that if it does, the first thing people will be talking about is who returned the ship. Maybe the Commander - but maybe anyone with the hacker kit. If the thread already exists, talk to people in it; learn who to report and do so, then finish your end screen like normal.

If there isn't an [AP] thread yet, create one and check back every so often. Explain the situation, ask someone who has already finished their end screen to report what they see with the end page, who made the hack or if the commander did the return. Make your report and end the game normally.

Multiple reports of the correct person are not necessary, but don't do any harm. The key is to get at least one report of the correct rulebreaker, so the situation can be addressed.

If you're not willing or able to delay for a time on your end screen, be sure to participate (and create if needed) the [AP] thread. It's fine for you personally to not delay to make the report, as long as someone else does. You can help the process through participating in the [AP] thread.

A few questions (about the report system rather than harmful returns):

How do you handle sneaky offences? I had a ship where the lenses had been hidden by a human. We discussed it a lot, but nobody ever admitted that they knew where they were. We didn't know who had done it - is the only option in that case to report everybody for it?

Don't report everybody for it for such a small incident. Reports have the goal to eliminate the really bad players. Think about the judges who have to look through all these reports, most of them don't have always time to look through a massive amount of reports which could result that either judges don't look as carefully through each case as they should or it will last really long until all reports are handled.
Feel free to report a person when you are confident that the person hid the lenses despite being human.

BostrupIf a person was reported in his old body, but later was involved in a transfer, is it still possible for the judges to sanction the right player? What if the offence happens pre-transfer and the reporting post-transfer?

The report is made for the person behind the character during the time of the report. That means if you report someone who transferred into a new body you have to report the new character.
And if you have reported the person in their old body already before transferring then you have reported the right person as well.
