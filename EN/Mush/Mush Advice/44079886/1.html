Your ship needs you, too. Never forget that. The 16 players a ship starts with are the only players it gets, period. So you being helpful and active is extremely meaningful - new or not, your ship needs your help, and you can help it, even as a first time player who has almost no idea what to do.

If you are human When you log in for the first time each day, or after a few cycles of being away, speak up, say you're around and ask about how you can help the most right then, then start reading the messages that have happened since you left the game. Be cautious about running off to do something about the messages already posted - they may be 'old news', about already finished problems, or they might be stuff that you can still help with.

Some things are more important than other things - it may be that your ship normally always wants you to progress research....

But today a bit before you logged in, maybe a mush tried to rampage, and a human is terribly hurt. You talk to your team, and find out that your 10 AP that you have right now is best spent going to the refectory to cook two meals, carrying them to medlab and giving one to Chun so she can try to do surgery on herself to stop herself from bleeding to death, waiting in medlab to see if Chun needs a heal aftter the surgery (yep she does), then eating the other ration, getting the knife from Chun, carrying it to the room they tell you the mush is in, stabbing that mush to death, then lying down in the bed the mush had been in before you killed it so you get more AP faster for the next set of emergencies.

A very good day for your team, because you logged in and communicated and helped. You're a hero, newbie or not, and every other human onboard is glad to have you there and excited to continue the game with you and the rest of the team.

If you are mush with or without an active communicating partner : Listen to your partner if you can, and consider cooperating with them. It's just you two that are mush, and they could be clueless, hard for you to tell. You win as long as either: You never die, or: the ship or all humans are destroyed at any point.

The game is actually your friend, it's going to throw random events, many bad, at the ship, and worse ones happen every day (on average, it is random, so easy days happen late, sometimes). The humans cannot win until they find out who to kill or cure, and they can only cure one mush, and that after killing at least 1 mush and doing a few other hard things. If you never let the humans discover that you are mush, you have more time. Use that time to wait, watch, help them, and learn the game and what matters. Generally, a problem that humans are having trouble fixing, even with your help, is the problem to make worse if you can find a way to worsen it. Try to do so when there's not much time left before the problem would kill the ship or many humans - you usually will get caught making things worse, but by that point they have to decided, 'spend AP to kill this mush, and let the ship die so I lose, or spend AP to prolong the ship's life and give me a chance to maybe later kill this mush and win, but the mush isn't dead and might win instead'. Even the effort used to kill you at that point (many humans will try to kill you rather than deal with the immediate risks of losing NOW that are going on, hehe) is actually helping your team, because that bad problem gets fixed or humans lose - humans losing means mush win.
