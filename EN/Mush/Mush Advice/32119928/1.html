There needs to be a guide for playing as a Mush. As it stands, it's far too easy for a Mush to reveal themselves by taking actions that appear to be harmless. The in-game tool-tips do not tell you enough, and certainly not enough to survive. And I'm not willing to rely on Google translate for tips from the French language wiki. That just gives me a headache.

Here is my accumulated knowledge so far. I'm certain it is lacking in many ways, but it might serve to help a first time Mush be a bit more effective.

Any Mush actions you do around people or cameras or drones will reveal you;
It takes 3 spores to convert a human;
You can only carry 2 spores on you at a time;
Spore creation is limited per day, and it is a team affair. Each spore your fellow Mush create in a given day (not cycle) is one less you can create;
You can only infect someone once per day, which the infect tool tip does not bother to tell you. So even saving up your 2 spores is not any guarantee that you'll be able to convert a human who is already infected.

The Mush form is nigh useless. It cannot infect, sabotage, see the human chat channel, carry objects, do anything with a storage bin (eat, infect, pick up, anything);
Do you think that the Phagocyte skill (eat a spore, gain 4 AP and health) is great? Guess again: It cannot be used in Mush form! This skill seems to be tailor made to preserve the life of a mutated Mush, and yet it cannot be used if you mutate.

It's actually easier to list the things the Mush form can do :
"Kick off" (attack) with a 60% accuracy and no particular damage bonus that 2-3 crew members don't already have;
See the Mush channel;
See the room log;

That's it. You lose all of your human and all of your Mush skills. If you have spores saved up, they become useless.

Why anyone would ever mutate, I don't know, and yet there it sits at the top of your action list, beckoning to you as if it was a good option. You're far better off grabbing the Blaster or the Knife and shooting or swinging away, more so if you're one of the several characters with a special aptitude for shooting or H-T-H combat.

My best advice for Mush (and take it with a large grain of salt, since I've never been very successful) would be to be very patient. Build your spores up, using eating food or research as a cover for becoming dirty. You'll then have to shower, which will wound you unless you take the special skill that lets you shower without taking damage. Use the Trapper skill or the food infecting skill (don't recall the name) to infect random humans. And then try to find one of them alone and coordinate with your fellow Mush to infect them directly.

Sabotage is very suspicious to players unless it takes place very late in the game, Day 6+. So that's not a great option, unless you can blame someone else for it.

Other than that, keep quiet and play your usual role on the ship. Take any opportunity to be helpful as this will 'prove' your human nature, but don't be a very active player.

Or go whole hog and be totally active, calling out anyone you can for anything you might be able to construe as a Mush-like action, to stir the pot and keep suspicion on as many people as you can. Sometimes just saying that you think someone is a Mush will be enough to get another player to kill them, and that's a win-win for team Mush.

Me, I think that it's a shame that a Mush win essentially requires poor play on the human side. Any coordinated human team will very quickly complete researches which limit the Mush capabilities. If they do not, then the Mush don't really have to do much of anything, the humans will have proven themselves incompetent either through inactivity or inept play and the ship will run out of O2 or fall apart without a lot of help from the Mush being required.

I don't think that requiring either incompetence on the human side or a 'political win' is a good mechanic for the game. But that's just me.
