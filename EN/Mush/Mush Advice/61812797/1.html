Regarding the side note: It's not "sometimes" better, it's always better. It gives the judges an indication of what the offending message was and gives them a starting point to search through the entire wall to find the context.

There are certainly times where you can't find a relevant message (or maybe that there isn't a relevant message) and then it's fine to report any message with a very detailed explanation, but that's very much the exception, not the rule.

If at ALL possible, you should select a message directly relevant to the reason you're reporting.
