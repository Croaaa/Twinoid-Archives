We usually join ships most of inactive and semi-active people, but sometimes it's quite the otherwise. Sometimes, we get lucky, find ourselves in a thriving ship, getting a decent chance to collect scrap, invent an RFS, plant a jumpkin and find Eden. However, while we're dreaming about all these things, a grumpy Mush goes rampage, killing a few of crucial people or sabotaging sensitive equipment which puts the humanity in danger of extinction. The result of the rampage is either the loss of human team or forced rushed return to Sol in order to survive. In both cases: we finish the game and join another semi-active ship.


To resist the rampage and keep the ship going, you don't have to rush with Mush hunting, trying to kill the Mush before they kill you and sacrificing innocent souls. This guide is for those who want to enjoy their long voyages and those who want to protect their ships from an unexpected attack of the Mush (and possibly griefers as well.)

Disclaimer: This guide doesn't give any guarantees! Counters can be found. It's your responsibility to secure the ship as well as you can.

Mush usually attacks:

  • shortly after selection (first 24 hours),
  • during day changes,
  • when vital resources of ship or human are in danger (oxygen, armor, morale),
  • once they realize humans are going to kill them,
  • in random time.

It's good to be vigilant during these moments, but the Mush usually attacks when they're prepared, but humans are not. Also, the purpose of this guide is to make you feel safe even if you're unable to be active when the Mush attacks. The others are active though. So, overall, as soon as Mush is selected

Hide the items!
What? Grenade, knife, one extinguisher (or its blueprint) and while you're doing expo - Hacker Kit.
Where? Anywhere that doesn't reveal secret logs. Neither you should hide them in any of storages or the Lab, as these objects are seen through the radio and the Research Lab, respectively.
Who? Have Chun hide them. If she's inactive/dead/griefer, ask somebody trusted. The best alternative person would be you! because you know you're human, don't you? The responsibility to hide items might be split between people who don't look suspicious.

Protect sensitive skills!
The list of sensitive skills the Mush would like to wipe out:

  • Technician - no technician, no PILGRED, no home. To make Mush less interested in killing technicians, you'd like to advance PILGRED, but not too much, otherwise they'll force return to Sol. It's a thin line, how to advance PILGRED - it depends on technician resources. If you're Raluca, it's best for your interest to raise standards. Remember, without technicians you're still able to fix PILGRED with the crew, but it requires renunciation of other work from several crewmates.
  • Pilot - it usually appears when there's a horde of hunters or/and armor is low. You need to keep doing your work efficiently then. Collect scrap for hull and Mush will be less interested in attacking you.
  • Morale source - may include Chun, but she might be killed as the last. The best counter would be to provide morale to all the crew as soon as possible, so Mush will lose interest in attacking you.
  • Tracker - be careful, when Mush attacks a tracker, it usually means they want to hide their Beta, so if you're Eleesha, you better do your job. Finding the Mush is hard, but observing actions of people who could possibly get dirty might be enough.

It's optimal to have one of these people wear Plastenite Armor, but it should be provided discreetly so Mush doesn't know who actually wears it. You also might ask for protection from...

The Pacifist
This trait wasn't given to Ian in order to sleep in the Medlab alone for research and gardening purposes. He's the only one who condemns violence. Have him stay in either of dorms to protect the most important people. This will sure complicate the Mush plans for a rampage. It's the best if he was active during Mush rampage, as he can disturb them.

Transfer
When the Mush goes rampage, always take the possibility of using the transfer. You can see it if the rampaging person has no skills - it's not Mush anymore (unless they had no skills before). If you see it, you should warn other people because you shouldn't suppose them to be as percipient as you. The Mush may lie about being transferred human, so you ought to check for skills first.

Be careful when the Mush is alive as you might be transferred at any time. If you survive, you shouldn't pick your skills (except for proper ones that may help you kill the Mush) and you should tell other people. If you can't speak due to no talkie (the Mush may most likely leave it somewhere) and you can't find it, either you should:

  • whisper to the people being in the same room as you;
  • use post-it notes to write (it's the only way to let them know if you have torn tongue);
  • go to the Bridge, where you can chat without talkie.

As it's said, it may happen at any time as long as the Mush is alive. After the transfer, the Mush takes possession over your health, your action points, your equipment, your outfit, your private channels, your day limits, your lovers and your title while you're owning theirs. To be prepared in case of transfer, you ought to read the following tips:

  • Don't hold any crucial items that may help the Mush during rampage (Knife, Blaster as Shooter character, etc.)
  • Close channels that contain information which would help the Mush in rampage, such as where the crucial items are hidden. Information about suspects isn't that important to rampaging Mush.
  • If you play as Chao, Derek, Stephen, you shouldn't fill your AP bar. If any of these characters are inactive, you should sell or kill them asap.

Be very careful during expeditions. Most strategies include transfer when it goes to leave participants on the planet. When it's succeeded, you lose the crew, Icarus and resources collected on the planet. It makes much more difficult to continue the journey even if you get rid of the Mush. These are following tips how to best secure your expedition team from being left behind (more tips used, better security):

  • Have current commander participate during the expedition.
  • If one of inactive ex-commanders or Kuan Ti don't take part in the expedition, don't launch the expedition before cycle change.
  • Have Chun hold the Hacker Kit during expedition or hide it.
    • If all of above tips are fulfilled and the expedition doesn't contain possible events where you may lose an item, you can consider taking the Hacker Kit to the expedition.
  • Have a non-commander guard Front Corridor when the Hacker Kit and commander aren't by the Bridge side. More guards, better defense. Chun serves the best purpose.
    • It's recommended to deplete your action points before guarding to prevent possible transfers (don't forget to leave some AP for guarding and pacifism makes it cost more).



Phagocyte
Nothing might be as frightening as the Mush eating the whole spore limit to cause serious damage to the ship. It's effective during day changes as the limit is doubled so it's recommended to be very active at the time. To make day changes safer, it's advised to complete Constipaspore Serum research, before the day ends for the best. The Mush will have to forget about taking advantage of this skill while planning a rampage.



Pyromaniac
And there goes the purpose of why you should hide an extinguisher. But even if it's been found, the Mush will most likely dismantle them or steal them hijacking a patrol ship or Pasiphae, leaving Daedalus on fire. Most human plans include rushing Sol, but if you want to enjoy the next weeks on board, these are ways how to deal with the fire:

  • If Magnetic Net hasn't been refused as a project and the Mush holds extinguishers outside, rush projects or upgrade NERON until you get this project. You may refuse time-consuming projects to win the race with fire once you eliminate the Mush.
  • If you suspect Blueprint: Extinguisher hasn't been taken by the Mush, you should search for it around Daedalus. Remember that there are 3 random blueprints (except for grenade and sofa BP). If you're certain the BP hasn't been spawned, contact Xyloph to receive blueprints. Then build it.
  • Take Firefighter - extinguish the fire manually. It's really tough as you'll be able to extinguish average 1-2 rooms with full AP bar. In addition, it makes you dirty and it'll get you hurt average every second attempt, so call for medical help and protection because you're very vulnerable to attacks after trying to rescue the ship.
  • Take Robotics Expert - use scrap to upgrade your drones to Fireman and Turbo and let them extinguish the fire. It's more powerful than Firefighter, as the risk of injury is limited to upgrading and a single drone is more efficient.



You may print Mage Books to obtain proper skills. You may also install Automatic Sprinklers to make the fire spread much slower (single fire can be put out, I doubt it would work for several rooms). This way you might continue your voyage despite fire ignited randomly. Don't forget to deal with the Mush, leaving behind once extinguishers aren't needed.




Summary
Those were the frequent ways of Mush leading to human extinction. Prepare for them so you'll raise your chance to survive and that the triumph won't stop quickly in Sol. Don't forget to keep doing research. You won't be overwhelmed by the Mush then and you won't have to care about who the Mush is. Let them live until their time comes, just don't let them spread.
I would be grateful for your feedback.
