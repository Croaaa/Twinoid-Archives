To add to the above: Check this lovely sticky topic for tips regarding specific characters and game mechanics.

Constant communication is often your best friend - most experienced players will be more willing to trust and advise you if you ask before you do..."things". Regardless of what it is, ask until you're sure about what needs to be done, and after that, keep communicating. Ships thrive with consistent communication. I have a personal theory that the tooltips are designed to get you killed, and that forces you to be social, so you don't end up screwing your crew over unintentionally.

if you go around with the "Click ALL the THINGS!" mentality, you'll probably end up quickly dead. Ask first, THEN act, and you'll probably live longer.
