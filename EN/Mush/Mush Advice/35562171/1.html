Mais pourquoi est-il aussi mÃ©chant ?

Since there is a random factor it is difficult to be sure whether half of the hunters were lost or not. It always works like that. When you escape, half the hunters are lost (or 75% with TM). The randomness is not in the number, but in the types of hunters. For some reason that I don't understand, you can have asteroids in the waves that catch up with the ship. But they are not the asteroids you have just escaped, because their impact is always 5 cycles later. For example, if an asteroid spawns on C1, its impact is on C7. If you move during C3 and get a new asteroid on C4, its impact isn't on C7 but C1 (5 cycles later). Which means that it's a different asteroid from the one you left behind.

As Veren said, that is also a proof of the existence of ghost hunters (and a proof that moving the ship triggers new waves). If you move and have no hunters, if you have asteroids when you stop, their impact is 5 cycles later.

It is only one of several proofs, actually. There's also the fact that if you have new hunters on C2 and C3 after moving, which is totally impossible if you don't move.

As for the fact that those "ghost hunters" exist, for one thing, anyone who's been on ships doing lots of expeditions can confirm it : if you move constantly, you have hunters constantly, but in very small numbers. Sometimes, early in the game, Neron can even announce the presence of hunters, but there are actually none outside. That only happens if the ship has moved. It means that initial wave (ghost or not) was so small that there was less than one hunter left after moving.

There is another proof : for ghost hunters, Strateguru works at the end of the cycle during which you move, and not at the beginning of the new cycle. You can see it in those screenshots :
New wave : the Strateguru message appears after the gain of AP, MP, etc.
Reduced wave : the Strateguru message appears just before the new cycle. That happens when you're escaping hunters, but also when you get "ghost hunters".
