You can't be sanctioned for just forgetting to read the logs. That's something that can happen to anyone, and people should be presumed innocent if there is no proof, not presumed guilty, or it would be unfair. It is the same for any silly mistake that we all did at some point. If a commander abandons a pilot by accident, they probably blame themselves enough and don't need to be sanctioned as well. But if they do the same mistakes repeatedly (moving the ship without checking for pilots outside, for the presence of aracks, or for the completion of rebel signals), then it becomes obvious that they either do it on purpose, or don't care, or don't understand.

As for Chao failing to report and / or to see that Paola extracted a spore, it isn't reportable in itself, but if he never reads the logs or the conversations, then it can be sanctioned if it is clearly deliberate (and if you play a game knowing that you won't understand a word, then it is definitely deliberate).

On the other hand, even if Chao isn't reported, he can get killed if that mistake makes him a suspect. Someone failing to report a mush extracting a spore can either have missed the logs or be a mush themselves, hoping to protect their partner. It isn't evidence enough to kill, but there can be other reasons for suspicion. And Terrence doesn't need to be reported if he had sufficient reasons to think that Chao was a mush (remember that it is always preferable to communicate with other people before killing someone).

Neutral play lasts until the very second Mush are chosen, and then it's basically a free-for-all (bearing in mind that suspicious actions can still get you killed, as in my example with Eleesha. The point of not holding items in your inventory is to give both teams as fair a start as possible. That is why items may be moved to other rooms, but not held. You used to be able to hide items before selection, and we've ruled that holding non-personal items in your inventory is just another form of "hiding" them; that's why it's a Neutral Play violation, even for Chun. She must abide by Neutral Play, too.

In the case of my Eleesha, she took those items the moment she woke up, but if I'd been a moment sooner, I could have secured them, and she might have lived a bit longer. (Or perhaps not, since it's been established that she griefed on other ships.) But not everyone will be fortunate enough to refresh right after Mush selection happens, and you only have three spots in your inventory...so hoarding critical items right after selection is difficult for a Mush (or griefer) to accomplish alone. Still, it's as fair a start for both teams as the rules allow.
