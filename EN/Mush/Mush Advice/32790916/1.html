Tracker gives her the ability to see things that happened before. As in, she enters a room and knows what was done in the room in the previous cycles. Secrets are an exception I think. Not exactly. With Tracker, she can see the last 16 cycles, while others only see the last 8. But she can see exactly the same thing as other people. She can also see where people came from and where they went, which is quite useful to follow someone's steps.

With her third skill, she can also reveal actions that would otherwise be invisible (see here for details).


This is useful as she can know about suspicious behaviour by patrolling rooms. Most of the good things are secret, so it may be a waste of MP. Even if she doesn't see secret actions, she must patrol rooms. It enables her to see if someone doesn't do their job (she can check the plants, see how researches, projects, the PILGRED are going), check if someone had a good reason to get dirty (if they ate, repaired something, dismantled an object, repaired PILGRED...), find out who was active when a new Mush appears... Eleesha uses a lot of MP but it's not a waste at all.


Each day the list changes and is more specific, becoming clearer who is the Mush and who isn't. This gets more useful as days pass. More exactly, there are 8 names on the list at the beginning, and every 8 cycles, there is one name less. If you can't print the list, it's because some of the crew are not awake yet and the Mush haven't been designated.

If you see that someone got ill, for example, you know you can trust them and offer to investigate together.

Eleesha is a difficult character for a beginner, I think. Try to find someone experienced on your ship (someone you can trust, if possible), it will help you a lot.

Ooh, an Eleesha question! I'm not exactly an "experienced" Eleesha player since I've only done 2 ships as her, but I like her character so much that I've basically chosen her as my character for the next month or two.

Everything everyone said so far pretty much sums it up. Eleesha is one of the most challenging characters to play for so many reasons. On top of what everyone have already said, I want to add the following:

1. To understand suspicious behaviour, you have to learn about the WHOLE game. It is absolutely essential to understand how the other roles are meant to be played. After playing two ships with Eleesha, I feel like I can play as anyone, because I know what everyone is meant to be doing! This also means you have to understand EVERY mush skill in the game, and find out which ones are secret/discreet, which ones are covert/furtive, and which ones just never shows up.

2. To understand suspicious behaviour, you ALSO have to understand human psychology, and make a lot of judgement calls about strangers' personalities. Are they new to the game, or are they intentionally being ineffective? Are they inactive, or are they sabotaging an operation? Are they naturally selfish and opportunistic, or are they working against the team? You basically have to chat these people up and psychoanalyse them, hoping to catch them in a lie, or if you're good, set them up to trap them in a lie.

3. The small good news is, you don't have to rely on instincts and your understanding of everyone's roles and personalities alone to find the mush/human. The tabulatrix and the Xyloph database both can give you a list which allows some degree of certainty on who you can trust (some, not total certainty, because mush has skills to swap bodies, and they also have skills to appear invisible in the indicator).

4. The effectiveness of Eleesha does actually go up with your activity level! The more you log in, the more you get to check your Tracker (the minimap) to see if there's anyone in a suspicious location, make a note of it to check it out yourself later. You'd be surprised how much one can learn from the minimap, especially when you keep an eye on it often.

5. Eleesha is "next-in-line" to inherit the Comms Manager title, should anything happen to Paola, or if she's inactive. This is a hugely AP-draining responsibility, even if you're Paola herself and have all her skills. It is an incredibly important role, so you'll have to somehow juggle between this and mush hunting (another very important AP-draining role). This forces you to plan for your every move (AP, MP, AP->MP conversions, scooter pickups/dropoffs, where you sleep, etc), usually many cycles ahead, inadvertently making you one of the most efficient characters on the ship.

The bigger the challenge is, the bigger the reward. So, you can understand why I love playing her. When you manage to figure out who's mush and/or who's not, it feels really good. Downside is, Eleesha is not a character who can get lots of Glory with, if that's what you care about. However, if you ever become a mush Eleesha, you know exactly how to play your shipmates, because you know all the tricks.

I'll work on a complete guide to all things Eleesha at some point.

For now, it's true I can only speculate, but my speculations are based on what I've seen so far with the list. I believe the list is produced in the following method:

1. Select one of the two Alpha Mushies (dead or alive) at random, and put them in the list.
2. Fill the rest of the list with random people in the ship (dead or alive, and even Chun).
3. Randomise the order of names in the list.

Assuming I am right about how it's produced, then the list can be used in the following way...

If both Alpha Mush is still alive...then the list can be used to narrow suspects down to track ONE Alpha Mush.

If one dead Alpha Mush is in the list...then the list is unfortunately, useless.

If one dead Alpha Mush is NOT in the list...then you immediately know that everyone not in the list are not Alpha Mush. They could be Beta Mushies, so be careful.

The usefulness of the list can be very situational, so it really depends from ship to ship, on when you want to print it, and how much of it you want to rely.
