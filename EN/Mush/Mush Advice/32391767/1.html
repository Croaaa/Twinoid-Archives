Ephel

I think once every 3 hours for most characters is a waste.
I do it even more often, but it's because I like to check what's happening and I don't see it as a very important thing to do.

Still, it really depends on role.
If you're Jim Su you should log often to be able to react to things and move the ship: if you've got a good planet and someone has finally killed the last hunter-thing-that-doesn't-let-you-move you want to move the ship before more of them gets to you next cycle. Same goes for avoiding asteroids.
If you're Ian you mainly want to check the plants at cycle 8 to treat/water them and at cycle 1 to plant new fruits... that can be done in the same session if you're in a good time-zone. You could also want to log in somewhere in the middle of the day to use some of your AP and avoid wasting them, but if one day you cannot, it's ok.

Obviously, if Ian is more active than that the whole crew has an easier time (he could notice a fire in the lab and put it down, help Finola resetting her efficiency with research while Chun is logged off)... but while an active commander is needed (and an active Comm. Officer is also very important, at least active enough to decode rebel signals), it's not necessary that every single member of the crew checks in every cycle.

I think having a mean presence of "checking in and doing things every 2-3 cycles" is the sane way to play this game, difficult times excluded (for instance, Eleesha in the Mush-hunt days could log in a lot more). That's 3-4 times a day... I would say 2 times a day is the bare minimum to at least not waste your AP, but you wouldn't get much fun from that.
