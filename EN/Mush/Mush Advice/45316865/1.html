Because the game never really solidly defines what "winning" is outside of having two highscore boards (and a couple win conditions, sort of). One that sorts people by the number of awards they get by having the most glory on any given ship, and one that sorts people by the amount of glory they have at the end of any given game.

Not that anyone has ever used these boards to define who the best players are. But there isn't much to define "victory" outside of that.

Yeah, there's also the same kinds of awards you'd get in die2nite for doing various activities. And it will keep track of how many times you've made it to Eden or Sol. But as far as I know, even a PILGRED rush back to Sol will still give you the Sol award.

It's pretty easy to say the Human victory is "Make it to Eden or Sol with no mush on board" but does that mean that literally every other outcome is a mush victory? I'd like to think a real mush victory is when the mush are the only ones left on board. But there's really nothing in game to reflect that's any better than just ripping out the O2 tanks at the right time and letting everyone suffocate. At least from what I've seen.

So some people like to think that having the most glory is just as much a win as anything, I guess. Because in reality, it's no more pointless than a lot of the other awards you would get for anything else in this game, now that I think about it.

It'd be nice to have some way to give mush a reason to try to win with some finesse, and it'd be cool if there were some better way to score each team as a group more reliably, but for now people are left to make their own goals, to some degree. Which leaves us with some of the same problems people have on die2nite, but the SDF does give us a lot more ability to define stuff like this as detrimental to the spirit of the game at least.
