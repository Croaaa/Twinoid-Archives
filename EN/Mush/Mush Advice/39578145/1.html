"Being annoying" isn't enough of a justified reason to kill someone on its own. Killing someone "for being annoying" is usually considered an overreaction, and may be sanctionable.

That said, you can voluntarily prove yourself to be human, and if they persist after that, the rest of the crew will likely be on your side. That still doesn't mean you should kill them, though it is a common enough Mush tactic to accuse someone else of being Mush. If you do decide to kill them, discuss it with other members of the crew, and be certain that you all agree that it is for the good of the ship. Seriously, vote on it, so it's clear that you agreed to that course of action with a clear head, and not just out of anger. After you kill them, make sure to lay out the evidence and explain your reasons for doing so (even if you think they're obvious) in their death announcement. At the very least, if you must act alone, create a PM to yourself, and make your explanations there.

What does all this accomplish? It shows that it was not a random killing, nor an act of griefing. They get to see your reasoning for their death, which may (or may not) reduce the chance of them reporting you. As far as the SDF is concerned, it shows us that you thought things through, even if the conclusions drawn might have been wrong after the fact. When we look at logs, we like to see these sorts of conversations. They also greatly reduce the chance of us ruling against you, should a report be filed.
