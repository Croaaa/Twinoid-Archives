Choose your priorities. When the situation is hectic, with too many hunters, fires, broken pieces of equipment, you feel as if you have to do everything at once, but you don't. For example you can have 30 broken things, but as long as the vital pieces of equipment work, you're fine and you can ignore the rest.

So you've got to choose the most urgent problems and deal with them first, depending on your AP, on the crew (who is online and with how many AP), and on your resources. And don't forget the drones.
- Hunters : if you have Plasma shield and it's still holding, or if the ship's armor isn't too low, you can wait a little before moving the ship. Also remember that they don't fire on the Daedalus immediately, so you usually have 2 cycles to move, more if you can afford to take a little damage.
- Morale is an emergency if someone has less than 5. But maybe you can tell them to take a pill, eat fruit, cuddle the cat or do the thing.
- Fires : can be ignored for one cycle if they're not in the engine room, bridge, or dorms, or if they're not going to spread to those rooms. Can be ignored longer if you have firefighter drones or Automatic sprinklers.
- Repairs : when it really is hell on board, only the most vital things must be repaired. Oxygen tanks (though you can ignore them for one cycle if your oxygen is high, or you can simply drop a drone in the room), gravity simulator, command terminal and emergency reactor if you need to move the ship, one fuel tank and combustion chamber (but only if you need to load fuel), patrol ships (must be repaired by drones).

If other people are there, try to give them precise orders. It will help them not to panic and waste their AP on useless actions, or worse, do nothing because they think the situation is hopeless.
