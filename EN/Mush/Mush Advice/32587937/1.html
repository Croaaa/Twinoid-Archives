I was already planning to write a guide for Chun.

At level 3, she gets Premonition. When she uses it, she loses one morale, but it allows her to see the last action taken by the targeted player. It's not quite as useful as Chao's Torture, since it can only see the very last action taken. However, it can suffice if you manage to catch a Mush right after the have done something incriminating. Just be aware that if you don't have Humanity's Last Hope, AND easy access to Janice/Jin Su's speeches, you may not have the morale to make good use of this skill. Still...take it anyway, if you're Gold. It's more useful than Sneak.

At level 4, she gets Sneak. What this skill does is grant her the ability to bypass anyone in guard mode (including mutated Mush.) Effectively, you can enter through one door, and leave through a different one, while the room is being guarded. You can bypass any number of players this way. There are two downsides to this skill. First, you have a permanent penalty to the hit rate of your HTH attacks. Second, there are very few instances where you'll need to use this skill, and in the cases that you would need it, you're probably in desperate need of your ability to hit your targets! My advice? Wait for Lethargy, this skill slot should not be wasted on Sneak. Sneak is mostly useless, and does more harm than good, in my experience.

At Level 5 comes her crown jewel skill: Lethargy. What makes it so great? First, it doubles Chun's max AP gauge, to a whopping 24 AP. Second, if she's in a bed for four consecutive cycles, she gains 3 AP per cycle, for every consecutive cycle after that. So the first four cycles, she gets her normal 2 AP for being in a bed. Then she gets 3 AP per cycle, until the chain is broken. Once the chain is broken, she gets the normal 2 AP again for being in a bed, and she has to wait four cycles to start the process over.

Sound good so far? Here's the really great part: It does not matter if she gets up to take care of research/fires/even going on expos during the course of a cycle, as long as she's back in a bed before the end of every cycle, the chain won't be broken. Getting a second Sofa BP is common enough. Usually the first one gets built on the Bridge. But if the second one can be built in the Lab, and early enough in the voyage...you will be raking in AP, and still be at your post for research.

Even better, it stacks with Logistics, too. If for example you are no longer required to stay in the Lab for research, and the second Sofa is then built in the Medlab, then you can get a steady 3-4 AP per cycle if someone with Logistics is there with you! That's almost the equivalent of eating a non-upgraded ration each cycle! Under most circumstances where you'll have a second Sofa, the Lab is still a better choice for its location...but I was just illustrating the possibilities.
There are two major downsides, however: You'll be confined to the Lab for what could be most of the voyage...if your comms officer is inactive, or isn't very efficient. And if you don't have a Sofa there, this skill will go to waste early on. But the potential benefits outweigh the risks, and on an active ship, there is much you can accomplish.

I need to clarify something regarding Sneak. It's not be as useless as I initially thought, but if you have access to Lethargy, then Lethargy is always an optimal choice for a Silver/Gold player. The 25% hand to hand attack penalty is applied only to people attacking Chun. I tested it on my current ship, and Chun doesn't get a debuff from Sneak. Her base hand to hand attack is still 60%, whereas people attacking her have a base chance of 45% instead of 60%. Ranged weapon hit rates are unaffected.

From this, I can conclude that as a Gold player, you could reasonably trade out Premonition for Sneak, if Chao has Torture. Premonition is a skill you have to get insanely lucky with, if you want to catch a Mush with it. Between Nurse, Sneak, and the Armor, Chun could be quite difficult to kill indeed. (Nurse is great for free DIY Surgery!) Having Ian in the room just adds on to the possible difficulty to kill her.
