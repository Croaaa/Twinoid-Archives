I understand your concerns. I've been a Moderator here since January 2014, and I was among the first group of judges when the SDF was created. If I feared for my long term sanity/mental stability, I wouldn't have accepted the position. Seriously speaking though, newbies will always be cycling through here, and that is both a blessing and a curse. Some will enjoy the intrigue, the thrill of the hunt, the social aspects, and will stick around for a long time. Others will not find the game to their liking, and quit the first day. That's just par for the course in a free to play game, and it's fair to say that the demanding nature of this game isn't suited for everyone.

Sanctioning people who aren't interested in the game is a waste of time and effort. They usually don't realize how demanding in terms of time or social activity a ship can potentially be. Many ships last more than a week, and some last a few weeks. Most newbies just aren't prepared for that level of commitment to a single game, but even that isn't a bad thing. Passion for the game is what drives people on long voyages, and newbies can't be expected to have that passion, as many of us do. I'm of the opinion that passion needs to be nurtured, rather than expected to magically exist on its own.

My first ship was a disaster. I chose Jin Su, and you can see, it was a clear Mush victory. I wasn't used to the chat system, or how active I needed to be to be a good commander. I barely communicated, and I did things that would easily get newbies killed now, like idling anywhere except under a camera. Needless to say, it took until my third ship to have a real handle on the game...but the point is: I got better. Honestly, if I had been sanctioned for my inactivity on my first ship, I probably wouldn't have come back to the game. That's the danger in sanctioning for inactivity; you might turn away people who have a rough start, but would come to love the game.

It is not an exaggeration that since my second ship, I've been on a ship literally every day since early beta, and I'm always on a ship within hours of my last one ending. Since that first ship, I can't recall a single instance in which I went inactive, not once. Sure, I'm an extreme example of this, but my point still stands: Punishing me for not being active on my first ship would have made me stay away from the game.

In the case of newbies who actively grief their ships, we do indeed sanction them for it. It's fairly easy to determine who is truly a new player, with a "click ALL the things!" mentality, versus someone who say, cooks 12+ rations D1C8, before the ship is even full, or loads 9 fuel before there's even a planet analyzed. It's the griefers we focus on stopping, because it can be proven that their behavior was deliberate.

People may go inactive for a lot of reasons, and some of them are understandable, in context. We greatly discourage people from using a fake story to explain inactivity, just for sympathy...don't do that. It makes it much harder for us to believe people when they actually have an ill relative, or have to attend a funeral, or a number of other reasons to explain their inactivity. Again, it's difficult to prove whether someone is XP farming, and most people who go inactive don't do it to farm XP. So in effect, players are asking us to punish the majority of newbies, for a crime a tiny minority of the playerbase actually commits. Personally, I'd prefer that the SDF focus its efforts on judging cases of active griefing, and whenever it can be proved someone was XP farming, we'll sanction those players, too.

By the same token, experienced players know (usually by the end of their first few ships) that games can last for weeks. If people know that in a few days, they're going to be gone for a weekend or longer...then they shouldn't join a ship yet.

I could have sworn I weighed in on this already - must have had a similar discussion elsewhere and thought it was here when I was reading through. At any rate, I can see both sides here. For the players (myself included) it gets old really fast when it's the fifth ship in a row where your Jin Su/Eleesha/Janice is drooling in the corner. I love this game and I love helping new people learn, but I can't do anything if they don't sign in. I really wish there was a way to curb that, and THEN we can work on the glory farmers.

On the other hand, I can see that the Mods get enough reports already - both legitimate ones and the "moooooom he looked at me funnyyyyyy" sorts - that take up their time and energy. And I agree that a newbie could very easily get overwhelmed if they are chosen as mush on their first voyage, or if everybody is really active and expects them to do things they know nothing about... or bored, if enough other people are inactive. And I wouldn't be one to quit then, but I can't blame other people for thinking of their health or other obligations first. An official Sanction would just hurt them further.

What I really want is... well, a tagging system. Have a way for the ship to flag people that stay marked as inactive for more than four cycles, or ever become highly inactive. This in itself would have no penalty, but if you get more than a certain number of "tags," you would be restricted from joining a new ship until you talk to a moderator, like the Multi-Teamster flag. Mods could track the number of times they needed to "reset" a person for inactivity blockage, and be able to determine if they're having a problem. Maybe set up somebody to talk to them, or have a forum they get directed to for advice, to see why they keep joining if they don't intend to play. People who show an actual problem with joining and going inactive can then be blocked permanently for passive griefing.

I love the idea of glory/XP being awarded for doing things, rather than just surviving. Even if you have the base line of "+1 glory because you didn't die this cycle," having more ways to earn it that actively help the ship out would help enormously just by giving the sense of "reward" to encourage people. Even better would be making the more skilled characters (ie Eleesha) harder to gain glory and experience with unless you are doing things in their jobs, so the people who just want to spam buttons and get XP/Glory leave them for people who plan on doing it properly. Heck, you could even separate glory and personal achievements, rather than having them in a hybrid fashion like they are now.

TL;DR - The system right now won't make anything better until it is changed, and sanctioning inactives is more likely to hurt than to help. The best we can do is continue with community jumps; maybe have them more often, even, so it's more regular to be on a mostly-active ship than a fully stocked MMM.
