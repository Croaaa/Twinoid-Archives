I like this idea, really I do.

But it can't work.

Even with the (rather good) idea of having a weighted system where it simply creates a higher chance of being Mush it can't work.

The reason being, this will create Mush Players. As in players who try to just play as Mush every time. Why is this a bad thing? Think in parallel to griefers.

Griefers can be any character, and indeed they are, but who are they the most? Chun/Jin Su and to an extent Janice. Why? Because they have a surprising amount of starting power, or value to the rest of the crew. Jin Su griefer can deny a crew missions, jump the ship without a consensus, make it impossible to move without a Hacker Kit and - if he has pilot - take the Pasiphae and other items into space to screw over the crew. One character has a massive amount of power. How about Chun? Taking items from lab and hiding them, attacking other crew members and not having mankind's last hope. That last one is huge. Janice has access to Neron and all the horrid hijinks that can lead to (although I consider her more dangerous as a mush but that is besides the point).

So what do griefers have to do with this? It's the point that griefers choose characters to help further their role. They are playing for a preplanned purpose. Now what about Mush players? Players who play wanting to be Mush and picking characters based on the prospect of being Mush. You see evidence of the bias already - certain people already mostly play certain characters (like Roland) because it's easy to get them. Most players have no problem waiting a few hours or a day to get that one character they really like. You'll just have to check back a few times and you'll find them.

There is a big problem with this. Certain characters give a natural bonus to being a Mush over others (which is a debatable topic for another thread) meaning that most alpha Mush under this plan will be the same crop of 4 or so characters.

Imagine that, every ship your Jin Su/Terrence/Janice are Alpha Mush (just using this as an example). Almost every time it's the same few characters. Why? The same reason the griefers all picked the same few characters, because they are the best at what the player wants to do.

Oh and the pregame? Have fun with that. Every single one of those Mush players who elect in the program will just rush for the weapons/hacker kit/apron and hide them, no work will be done by them because they'll all be watching and conserving all their AP for when the Mush are picked. It would be hellish.

Besides, all those Mush players will get their Mush level up stupidly high. With only a very small group of characters they'd choose from, there are no other XP sinks as an incentive since they'll have no use for the other characters - instead there is no reason NOT to pump all their XP into their Mush skill / Primary Character.

I'm certainly not saying every player would do this or behave like this, but it's very easy to see this being the majority. I think it would cause way too many issues if they implemented a system like this. I still think it's a nice idea though.
