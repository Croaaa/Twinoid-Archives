MycoScan will be avoided by most of experienced mushes, unless you have super-active ship where every move is watched, and every missing ap is noticed and they have only some few traps. If they want to convert humans, they will focus on turning one human after another, and everyone will very likely see 0 on scan. Or they even convert just one human, take anonymush and wait for any victory as you have no tools to check that anymore. Scan is good thing to narrow possiable mush tactics, preventing traps in bays/corridors/etc, after all more direct ways became impossiable.

MycoAlarms can be used in two ways:
1) Just secure more rooms, using them as cameras in Icarus, Med, Lab, Nexus, etc
2) To make traps for mushes, hiding them in rooms, where traps can happen, or to hide somewhere and ask a human to work as a bait for spiking
In situation like yours - second way can be very useful, if mushes get caught by such alarm. For the first way it's probably too late, but still not having 2 mushes coming to Icarus, making trap + 2 spiking another player is really nice.
Still, if mushes are not greedy and already have converted enough for victory, alarms will be useless.

But research don't win games, it just make mush progress slower/more risky. Is up to you, to locate them.

If you have idles in unprotected rooms, better to kill (trade only if offer is already here, don't wait for it) them very long ago.
- It will make sure there is no anonymush hiding behind them.
- If any of them is already converted or alpha mushes, you can go for pheromodem/bacteria, that are much more useful
- Also those people with 12ap/12mp are too great to transfer to rampage and kill every important human left. Especially if those characters can take skills like Diplomat/Shooter/Apprentice/Wrestler.

MycoAlarms are important if you're going on expos with mush still alive, because Icarus Bay is a great place for spiking. A mush Hua especially can extract spores in Rear Corridor on her way to the bay, spike her target and immediately launch so that she can blame her dirtiness on the expo. You could also move cameras, but it's just another thing to coordinate (and another free dirty excuse unless the mover is recently confirmed or Chun). And they are extra important if you don't have extra cameras from Gioele, because that means two of three important non-dorm rooms (Lab, Bridge, Nexus) are going to be open for trapping or direct spiking.

The scanner can sometimes be useful for determining what sort of game your mush are playing, but IMO should either be researched early enough that the mush are unlikely to have already converted anyone, or after the alarms.
