This is something I threw together in order to explain gardening to people. I usually offer it as a Gardening 101 class during the beginning of a ship, and blast people who are interested with info via a PM group. Ya'll are welcome to do the same, just give me credit.

Also, any input/corrections/additions are appreciated, for sure. ^^

~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

First off, plants should be moved to the lab, so that gardeners can use the stainproof apron and Ian can use extra AP to help with research. The only reason for plants to be moved back to the garden would be if the crew decides to do an upgrade which makes the garden work better, like heat lamps via NERON upgrades.

Plants become thirsty at the day changeover, but they can become diseased at any cycle change throughout the day. For this reason, it is important for the plants to be checked during Cycle 8 each day, preferably at the beginning of the cycle. Ian and Hua should save their botanist points for this time too, if they can be online then.

*Actions*: Watering plants takes 1 AP. Treating a diseased plant takes 2 AP, as does planting a fruit in an empty pot. Grafting a fruit onto a mature plant takes 3 AP, and changes the type of fruit produced. All of these actions only take 1 botanist point, so these points should be saved for the most costly actions that need done in a given day. When trying to graft a fruit onto a plant, the plant must not be thirsty, dried out or sick, and the person doing it must not be dirty. If any of these things is the case, the plant will die and you will lose the fruit.

*Young plants*: Young plants will not produce oxygen or fruit. For this reason, they do not need watered when they are thirsty, since they won't give oxygen anyways. They *do* need watered when they are dried out, but not before then, so only every other day. Once they are matured, they need watering every day.

There could very well be a time when a ship's primary gardeners (ideally, Ian and Hua), do not have enough AP to care for all plants in a given day. If this happens, they need to make an announcement in general chat letting the rest of the crew know what needs done and how many more AP are needed in the garden that day.

Scientific Authority

The reason to do it at the end of the day [cycle 8] is to saved Gardening points. Plants may get a disease at any cycle turnover. Treating a disease is 2 AP or 1 Gardening point, Watering a plant is 1 AP or 1 Gardening point.

So if you "waste" your Gardening points at the beginning of the day and your plants get a disease later on that day, you'll need additional APs. That's why, Ian's working hours are actually cycle 8 in most cases to use his Gardening points wisely.

Planting a banana for example is 3 AP or 1 Gardening point. So, this (being the most expensive action) could be done right at the beginning of a new day.
However, maturing of a banana tree takes 36 cycles. So, best time to plant a banana would be cycle 3. It would mature four days later on cycle 8 and thus has no chance of getting a disease on its first day as matured tree.
