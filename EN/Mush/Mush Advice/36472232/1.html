Many ships have multiple needs, so move among those needs as you see fit and see need. Reset Nexus if there's not already a great deal of nexus resetting happening (unless your ship is trying to have most of the Projects be done through Neron Upgrades on the Bridge - ask if you're not sure). Check up on plants and tend them, especially if you can be there C8 - but watering can be done any cycle for full benefit, it's just disease that should be fixed C8. Plant bananas if that's still possible to do. Maintain inventory, pay attention to if and when items go missing, and if you trust what you see in the roomlog, help gather expo equipment into Icarus Bay. Be available to heal (but make sure your ship as a whole knows about healing needs.... that can be a way to catch a mush, if someone needs healing). Move drones where repairs are needed (possibly help with repairs), see if your crew wants the freezer moved to Icarus bay, be ready to help fight fires...

The list is long. Generalists make the ship live very much longer - they do like 80% of the work on a high-functioning ship!

We all face the 'never enough AP' issue.

Having the Pilot skill essentially turns you into a specialist, unless your ship has so many pilots that not every pilot is needed - in your case, it sounds like every pilot is needed.

Do all you can, within reason, to make sure you have a bed. Discuss the importance of your having a bed with the ship, especially if hunters are such a big issue that all of the pilots together are having trouble keeping up with the hunters.

Discuss with your ship, should you be taking a ration with you when you fly? Many more experienced ships want their pilots to use their AP as efficiently as possible - since Take off and landings probably cost you 4 AP, that leaves at most 8 shots for you to fire, but your ship has 10. Carrying a coffee or a ration to eat while on your ship can mean another dead hunter, sometimes two.

However lack of food, or an even more essential job needing the food more than pilots can happen, based on your ship's situations and personalities aboard.

Since take off and landing will consume 4 AP (or 3, if a certain project is done), and that cost never changes, you ideally have a LOT of AP when you fly. Otherwise, you're still spending the same fixed cost but getting less potentially productive use from it.

If research is going well, find out if *lenses* are possible to make (or already are made). If so, consider using them, they make you miss a lot less. Be aware of the possibility of getting spored from room traps when you pick up those lenses - check the roomlog and think about what you see, who it is, what people have done, and who has moved through the room since the last object was dropped, picked up, or ship was flown.

Ahh. So if you are in a dorm with a bed, you have a couple of options. One is to accept that you have to spend 3 AP at a time to get to/from nexus. One of those AP will be turned into 3 movement, the other two will advance a project, and just dart back and forth every couple of cycles or so. Do not use a bunch of AP at a time, because you need most of that AP.

Another alternative is to ask whomever is doing plant tending to bring the plants by the dorm (often crews will carry the plants after the first few days, because of the risk of destruction in a fire). Watering a plant needs 1 AP, and every plant needs to be watered every day. That's a great way to 'bleed off' one AP at a time if possible.

Another alternative if there is scrap and hull damage is to have someone bring scrap to the dorm, and the wrench, do a hull repair with the extra scrap.

Healing is a possibility, especially if the person brings the medkit to you.
