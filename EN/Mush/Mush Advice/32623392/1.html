Whenever a signal is active and waiting to be decoded, anyone can access the Comms console and see its progress. If one or more of the six signals is active, you will see its percentage, instead of "???" or "Signal Lost". If you see a percentage displayed on any of them, then you might want to wait to move. Of course, it's always far better if the Comms officer can keep the ship apprised of the current signal's status, so the Commander will know the moment it's safe to move the ship...

Yes, there are two results in the Xyloph DB that will always cause signal loss. These are unavoidable, but if you get them both early, it won't set you back too far. Hunters firing on you can slowly reduce the signal, to the point it is lost. It doesn't happen too often, but it has happened to me. Oh, and the infernal microwave. Using that can (but not always) cause signal loss. It's a good idea to ban its use until everything important with Comms are completed. (Or just dismantle it completely!)

Regarding fuel: Always check the tank before filling it. There is no good reason not to, because you will miss your target, as you had guessed. When a planet is found, its distance away from you is the exact amount of fuel you will need in the chamber. That simplifies things a bit, doesn't it?
