a) Killing Hunters
large waves of hunters can do huge damage, every cycle they are ignored. needs pilot upgrade. turbo is also useful for more shots. Pilot drones can be useful if you have trouble with hunters, but they also have several disavantages :
- They only shoot once per cycle (twice with Turbo)
- While they are outside, the patrol ship doesn't gain charges and can get damaged
- You lose the patrol ship and the drone if you move the ship without Magnetic Net, and even with MN, drones don't land, their patroller follows the ship like trax (and so they don't gain charges)
- If they're shooting hunters, they're not repairing things
- When patrol ships start to break all the times (random breakage, fires, electrical spikes), you don't need to have the working ones unavailable because the drones have taken them.

One or two pilot drones can help, but not more, and only if you really need them.


b) Guarding the Oxygen Tanks
if the oxygen tank breaks during the night, when the techs are sleeping (or when you are lacking techs), then you already lost a lot of oxy until the problem is being dealt with, I think drones can be useful for this. only problem I see here is that it is hard to keep the drones from wandering off. Turbo makes the drone more effective when repairing, but it also increases the risk that the drone wanders off. The advantage of the English server is that people don't all sleep at the same time. On most ships, there is always someone online who can deal with emergencies. Besides, why would you wait for techs to repair the tanks ? If they are asleep or if you lack techs, then you must repair the tanks yourself. They're not that difficult to repair if you've got the wrench, and that way you don't lose oxygen, especially when it's a bit low.

The problem with drones is that even if they manage to fix the tank at the first cycle change, you still lose one additional O2 because they fix it after the loss.

The main problem with b) and c), as you said, is that drones don't stay in the same room unless they've got something to do there. You can't keep them in one place, and if they have been upgraded to Turbo, they run twice as fast.

Ironically, the bug of Pathfinder upgrade enabled drones to stay in the same room, but it was impossible to force them to choose a particular place.


I think the best strategy is to have as many drones as possible (original drone, drone BP from Terrence, Xyloph or random BP, Robotics Expert magebook, extra drone project... and, only in castings, a series of transfers to get as many BP's as possible). And of course, if you have a Robotics Expert, you have to upgrade them to Turbo and Firefighter.

As for the best use for drones... well, it is to have them repair pretty much everything, except for important equipment such as O2 tanks, gravity simulator, or the pieces of equipment required to move the ship (command terminal, combustion chamber, emergency reactor, fuel tank), which should be repaired manually if you have to move in a hurry. If there's no rush, drones are enough, and they come in particularly handy when electrical spikes and fires start damaging whole rooms.

Incidentally, I think that Sperbi's theory is correct : drones start with the success rate of a non-tech and it increases, but it keeps increasing after each repair. Players start with 25% for an oxygen tank, and once they've fixed it or done something else, their success chance drops back to 25%. I think that the drones' efficiency keep on increasing and never goes down.

It's easy enough to check. At the start of ships, it takes them hours to fix a single item. On my current ship, the new drone took almost a whole day to fix the scooter. Later, they fix almost everything in one cycle, including the most difficult equipment that have a success base of 3%. This means that you need to keep your drones busy as early as possible, so that they become super efficient when the ship starts to fall apart.
