Hello mushlings, and welcome to the fourth iteration of this thread! This post and thread should help clarify what is reportable/sanctionable or not. If you have any questions regarding the rules or which behaviour you could report, please, ask here.

If you have a question regarding a sanction you've received or wish to speak to us personally, send us a message.

Rules of English Mush


When reporting rule breaking on your ships, please, you must type in a reason for the report! In If you leave it blank, the judges have to pick through the whole ship's logs to find out what went on.

We've found a glitch in reports where a reported private message of any kind (to your self, blank or with others) will send in a blank report. We have the logs but the judges don't know why the report was made so it's very important that we do not report private messages of any kind, find a random post in main chat to report, if you have to. Do not report PMs. We'll let you know when this is fixed!

In addition, do not just type, "Griefer". More information will only help us.


Anonymity
Many find anonymity to be one the best features of this game and choose to keep anonymity at all times. There is no Twinoid fame in Mush, no long time member hero worship and you are free to role play the character you've chosen without anyone knowing who you really are. Because most of us do want to keep our anonymity and not know the identity of others on our ships, we strongly suggest everyone preserve anonymity because it's more enjoyable to play that way.


Spreadsheets, other tools, and external communication
We won't be sanctioning for spreadsheet use. That said, there will plenty of time for total organization in private ships when they come. Spreadsheet encouragement on public ships is often futile as not everyone will use them and remember, the mush can see them as well and destruction of important documents is a valid mush strategy. Lists can also lead to tensions as the super organized might pressure the under organized. You can post your link once and please, don't expect new players or those who would rather stay in game to use these tools.

A note on IRC use - while it's very convenient its use is discouraged on public ships. No one will be sanctioned for using it nor scolded on the forums (/me puts her whip away) it must be used very carefully. IRC logs cannot be used to contest a sanction because like screenshots, they can be tampered with. All pertinent information gleaned from an IRC conversation must be summarized in game. This is the only way for the SDF to see the evidence of the ship's happenings.


Inactivity or Idlers
Inactivity (new players, players not communicating or playing) will not be sanctioned, do not report them. We do not want to sanction new players who might not be engaged. Learning to work around these players is part of our game. You *can* succeed on as ship that has many deaths and/or a fair number of inactive players. Luck and team work amongst those left will be in order but it can be done. Thoughtful communication and patience can go a long way.

There are a couple, very specific, instances of inactivity or perceived inactivity which can be reported. If there is proof of idling for XP farming without actually playing, this can be reported. Proof though would include an admission of this type of activity. Widespread idling for XP gain isn't happening and it shouldn't be assumed that someone inactive is engaging in this activity. Over reporting this will result in a sanction for the unnecessary reports if there isn't solid proof of this behaviour.

The other type of inactivity that can be reported is surrender or giving up. For example, a player has had a disagreement on the ship and stops playing. This is not allowed, ships must be played out.


Ships filling slowly, and Neutral Play
There will be the rare times ships might take longer to fill, when this happens we will expect "neutral play". Which means, we don't do anything that specifically will help either team. As an example, killing hunters is acceptable as a mush goal can potentially be to live longer as much as it is a human goal. What's not acceptable is holding the wrench, apron, weapons and other critical items prior to Mush selection; and you may be sanctioned if found doing so. These items may be moved, but not kept in your inventory. We consider holding onto items in order to have an advantage after Mush selection as a violation of Neutral Play.

The main point of Neutral Play is to give neither side a distinct advantage at the moment of Mush selection. If you have both blasters, and you are selected as Mush, it gives you and your team a significant advantage. The same can be said if you are selected as human. A griefer could pick up critical items before Mush selection, then hold onto them; this gives them a personal advantage, but more importantly, you'll probably have to kill them in order to get those items back. It is better (at the very least, it makes for a more interesting game) for neither side to have a distinct advantage after Mush selection, and that is why these Neutral Play rules exist.

Note: Neutral research before Mush selection IS allowed. Neutral research examples include - Ultra-healing Pomade or the NCC Lenses.

It is at the discretion of judges in this matter, if they feel the game has been spoiled for humans or Mush, they will give a sanction.

Mush Tests updated Jan 29/14
While Mush tests are allowed it is not just to kill someone who refuses to take one. The player might not have AP, might not want to be sick if the test would make them so or the player might just not want to partake in the test because they don't like them. Refusing to do the test alone is not a valid reason to kill someone. If this is the only evidence you have that someone is Mush, it's not enough and could be seen as an unjustified killing which will net a sanction.

Communicate, Communicate, Communicate!
This isn't a rule, per se, but it's crucial to the success of any ship. As simple as it sounds, following this bit of advice is something that may keep you alive longer. As a social game, other players are dependent upon you for information. People who wander around and don't communicate are often the first to be suspected of being Mush. Note that they're usually the first to be assassinated. Communicate more, and you have an easier time earning the trust of your shipmates...and trusted people are less likely to be killed over a misunderstanding!

Remember, Mush is a teamwork game! You can't actively work against your team be it mush or human! If you play as a human and you sabotage the humans (killing for no reason relate to the advancement of the human cause, taking both showers apart, cooking all the rations and letting them spoil, etc) this is griefing and it will be taken very seriously. If you are mush, you cannot denounce your fellow mush or impede the mush cause, you must work as a team.

Have fun!
