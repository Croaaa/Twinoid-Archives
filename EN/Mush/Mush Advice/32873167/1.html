A good rule of thumb is if they are responsive in chat. If they are doing dumb things and aren't talking. Kill them.
If they are doing bad things and are talking. Explain why they shouldn't be doing those things. Then kill them.

If they are just being inefficient, then try to correct them. An inefficient human is better than a dead one.

The thing to remember is that very easy actions CAN damage or kill crewmates in the long run. It's a bit awkward.

I indicate common catastropic mistakes(Crimes, on the ship) at the start of my voyage generally now, so ignorance is less an excuse. I also metagame in that I see whether they are searching up a walkie talkie, which I'm still deciding on the ethics of.

There is essentially no way to know definitively that someone is ignorant and not a griefer. Likewise, a mush not using their abilities is very resource inefficient to uncover.

I'm still trying to get a handle on when it's best to kill someone. Think Mill's Harm Principle. 100% identification is not necessary or useful(Due both to efficiency and griefers/crazies), moreso "what was the damage to the ship? what does this indicate about expected future damages?".
