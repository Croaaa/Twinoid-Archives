This last Alpha Mush is active during the reveal, and their AP and MP gauges are full. Are there situations in which the Mush may surrender, beg for the Serum and their survival... or MUST they be antagonists to their last breath, perhaps even finishing in a suicide attack? Mush may never surrender, nor are there any allowable circumstances in which they may volunteer for the serum. Begging for survival/surrendering is strictly sanctionable, and we take such cases very seriously, because while it grants a boon to the human team...it's also playing against the Mush team. It also effectively ruins the Mush hunt. If anyone on your ship is entertaining the notion of offering the serum to a remaining Mush, please do us all a favor and remind them of the rules. If a human player offers to cure a Mush if they out themselves, and the Mush complies even after being reminded of the rules, report them both.

I was on a ship where someone suggested that we offer to cure the remaining Mush, and said that if nobody reported it, the SDF wouldn't find out. I had to quickly put a stop to that line of reasoning, and a Mush was still cured, but without a surrender involved. I didn't want to see both players get a sanction.

That said, the serum may even be used on a mutated Mush, so if they're quick, they might stop you before you can kill anyone. But the Mush are expected to avoid being cured, however they can. Picking up the serum and using it on a human is one possible option, though you'll almost certainly be killed for it afterward. You can go out fighting; or take a few items, a ship, and play the "You'll never cure me!" game.

Of course, as Impet suggested, it's possible that you're already a suspect, and your options are limited. But it's also possible they don't have a suspect, and will be waiting for someone to present themselves as a target...in which case, waiting for the right moment to point the finger at someone else can also be a viable move. Doubt can lead to inaction, and stalling for time is a very valuable strategy here, because the longer you survive, the more desperate they might become.
