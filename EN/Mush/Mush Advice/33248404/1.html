spacekadt

It was a beta ship but I also just ended a day 13 Sunday jump ship that made it back to Sol. Gold isn't required, but a crew that works together and deals with the levels of activity they have is crucial.

For the first 10 days, you really need to try to get in as many expos as you can. Get the o2, the fuel and the food so you can make it as far as you can.

Get the Ultra Healing Pomade researched as soon as possible. Get the medkit and Chun to the lab and research the hell out of that puppy until it's done. If you wait too long to get this done, you'll end up without the medkit in the crucial areas of the ship at the worst possible times.

If you have a good research crew, it's also a good idea to get the Super Soap researched nice and early as well. Not only will it help with spores from mush, it's important if you want to visit multiple Cristalite zones looking for pieces of the star map.

Whoever seems to be the most common expedition person should read the chef mage book when it pops up in comms from the database and if you can have Gioele or Stephen move the freezer to Icarus Bay, this will make your life easier. There's no need to shuttle things back and forth after that and the person with the chef skill can freeze up to 4 steaks with no AP usage.

Kill hunters when you have the AP, but don't be afraid to load a fuel and outrun them. Especially after day 5 when you start seeing asteroids. Do not take pasiphae out unless everything is dead. The pasiphae will take damage from anything outside.

Get a decent amount of metal stockpiled if you can and the oscilloscope is fairly crucial. The 10% hull upgrade is leaps and bounds better than 5% and is probably part of what made the difference in both of these ships. Once the mush are dead, start dismantling cameras and mycoalarms if you have a tech on board. You can get more scrap metal from these.

Once you get to day 10, it's best to start splitting up where the plants are. Keep a few in each dorm and wherever the sofa is. This way, if there's a fire, you only lose a few at most instead of possibly losing your entire food/o2 source. At the same time, make sure there's someone active that will agree to tend them in cycle 8 in each room. You really can't have Ian running around between bridge and both dorms in c8 just to check to see if things need doing. You know you're going to have to water x number of plants and there may be some disease as well... so either save up AP to deal with it or make sure you have a few botanists (mage books are awesome if you can get the right ones).

You may want to consider building the sofa somewhere like central corridor instead of bridge to make it easier to keep up with health and morale later in the ship. Yeah, it's 4mp required each time Paola or Frieda need to go to the bridge, but they're often better at their jobs when they can save AP and do things in batches anyway.

Again, starting around day 10, you need to do constant health/morale checks. At some point, you'll see a pattern of constant room shaking and sheet metal falling on people. This is the point where you need to take a much more active role in managing health instead of just having people report. If someone's not online to check in during a cycle (i.e. asleep irl, at work, etc), take the medkit and go look at the room logs. If you see a room shake, everyone took some damage so give everyone a heal. If you see a sheet metaling, it will tell you who and you should give that person 2 heals if you can.

Hopefully you have an active shrink (or two if you can score that mage book) and/or active Jin Su that can help people with morale. And don't forget that you can do the thing and use Schrodinger for morale.

Tip for Raluca: if you suspect that mush are still alive, do not let anyone touch your cat. Be firm in the first days that until the mush are dead, you trust no one. Not even your best friend if you happen to be on the same ship as them. You don't have to be a dick about it, but there's a valid way to say "I'm sorry, we're early in the ship, we have a shrink, we're expecting morale from comms, and the mush are alive so I really don't trust anyone with Schrodinger yet." Once you're fairly confident the mush are dead, drop Schrodinger in a dorm so he's easily accessible to anyone that needs emergency morale.

For everyone else (well, and Raluca): Save Schrodinger for an emergency. The world doesn't end if you don't end up petting him on the voyage. The world may end if you pet him on day 3 to get up to max morale instead of waiting for the day 5 rebel base that gives you +6 morale. It *hurts* when you get to day 10 and you're at 4 morale just before day change and you're worried about a panic attack and have no way right at the moment to get your morale back up... don't you wish you hadn't already pet the cat?

So now that this has become an essay... yes, it does require a decent amount of active people (though BY FAR not an entirely active ship - you can get away with half of the crew being active if they're active enough), but it doesn't require everyone to be Gold players or have all 4 skill slots.

Okay, I have made it to Sol for the first time, as a mush no less, so I feel like I should share the stuff that I wanted to know from people.

http://mush.twinoid.com/theEnd/1164

I think we made it on day 7. The four things that managed to keep everyone alive

-Expeditions every chance we got, we didn't waste time scanning and going out. O2 was never a problem and we didn't even have oxygenated ducts. This is what keeps the crew from suffocating to death.

-A captain that's quick to act. Moving the ship away from danger, issuing missions, going after planets, these have been extremely important. The captain alone is 50% the reason the ship didn't get blown to hell. The rest of the crew are the other 50%.

-An active crew. The captain alone can't take the ship to victory. People were doing their jobs, specifically Frieda scanning a lot and Paola handling comms very well, Raluca working hard on Pilgred. But most people made themselves useful, from Stephen to Janice. We only had 4 inactives, Chun, Chao, Ian and Giole.

-Pilots, lots of them. Hua, Terrence, Frieda and Me (Roland), not only lots of pilots but active pilots. It is what kept the ship alive for so long. We were not only quick to shoot hunters down but quick to return to ship.

Of course, it's important to note that although we made it to Sol, it was a mush victory. I was one of the Mush but I wasn't one of the original Mush, I got turned I think on day 4 or 5. The mush in the ship were doing a fantastic job.

I think the best thing a mush can do to win is to cooperate with the rest of the crew. Mush are actually some of the best people to keep the ship functional. They don't have to worry about morale or starvation.

Two other things that really helped our mush victory was having Raluca and Jin. I think Jin was one of the original Mush, and we turned Raluca later on. If we didn't have them as Mush, we would not have made it back to Sol.

There was also some luck into it. Of all the ships I've been this is the one that got the luckiest with malfunctions, expeditions and hunter attacks. We found safe planets with O2 very quickly, we didn't get attacked by a lot of hunters.

Was the crew perfect? No, I wasn't perfect either. But we were functional enough to guarantee our survival for a long time. I'm sure we could still live to cycle 10 or so.

So after a long long long Eden attempt, I think the following are very important to get Eden:
- 2 pilots dedicated full time to shooting hunters. This is why Jin Su and Terrence aren't great for this. Flying patrol ships requires a lot of skill. Even if you kill all the hunters with one pilot, collecting scrap can easily drain 12 AP.
- Diplomat skill. We visited 19 planets and couldn't get our third starmap. When the choice is between starmap, fights, and mush trap, removing one option really helps. Alternatively, get the white flag done.
- Intelligent life is a fairly dangerous sector in the long run. You'll end up losing valuable items like compass and blasters and lose a lot of later fights. Avoid unless you have the babel module. Heck, one of them stole our babel module.
- Active Raluca and/or technician. Once you get the mush, you want PILGRED up ASAP for the +14 AP/day (+28 with Caffeine Junkie). Plasma shield is absolutely a must for any long term ship.
- Responsible botanist. Alive Janice and Chun.
- Paola really helps too. Feed her early and often. If you're going for Eden, you want to get all the rebel bases except the first.
- Don't underestimate your drones. Drones can fix things at 3% odds. When the whole engine room is busted or a fleet of patrol ships get taken out by an electrical surge, this comes in very handy.
- Don't linger. Things get really bad past day 15. 39 hunters popping up in one cycle is common. The whole ship comes crashing down. Entire rooms fall apart (this includes bridge and engine room). You won't get perfect planets and you won't get to replenish APs. Just go to those planets with cristalite and seismic.
- Astrophysicists are very important in getting star fragments. We had 4 on our ship (5 if you want to count Gioele as two people). We had most of the upgrades and two liquid Magellan maps. And we still couldn't get the right planets. This is where Diplomat makes a difference.
- Active commander. Probably an IT expert who's online when he's not to hack the command terminal.
- Caffeine Junkie Gioele really helps a lot. 40 AP/day is nothing to sniff at, especially if you get him a pilot magebook.
- Medics recommended. Past day 12 it becomes a full time job to keep people alive. That's about when Finola and Chun become no longer useful.
