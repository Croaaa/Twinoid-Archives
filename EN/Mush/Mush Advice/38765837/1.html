Playing as low level Mush on a good ship is pretty hard. You miss a lot of really good skills like Green Jelly, Slime Trap, Nimble Hands, etc. A good thing to do is be a Trapper in the back and help relay everywhere. As Derek you are a generalist and might be able to get away with being AP counted less.

On ships with a low quality mush hunt team you can reasonably spore once, maybe twice a day and trap things. One higher activity ships you need to be real careful someone isn't counting your APs. If people are asking you were you went exactly and what you've done, its time to settle in for the long game and play well when online.

When you are Mush I think one of the best tactics is to be a good player (do jobs, be helpful, talk clearly and honestly, well except about being Mush) but be bad at coordination. Make sure you are only active at varying times because people will be watching your logs sometimes. If you are always online people will find it easier to work with you AND you'll be expected to help with emergencies. If you aren't always "online" fires and repairs might need doing and you can just stay AFK a little longer and make the Humans do their own work. When people are asking for relays, don't be there so they can't relay a bunch of points. Wait for them to log, then go relay, maybe do some other stuff.

Generally if you can stay uncoordinated but still show you are doing a lot of work, most people will generally trust that. Especially if you have crappier human players that AFK in corridors or wander around a lot doing nothing. I will log in multiple times per day, but not actually do anything for many of those.

And then on active ships where things are going the distance, be very helpful because the ship can be screwed at key moments past Day 5. Eventually someone will forget good Hacker Kit security, or too many asteroids are coming in and you can make a quick kill on the commander, or any number of things. When O2 gets real low you can withdraw 3 capsules and sabotage. If you have 2 active Mush this works great because a lot of ships will get to 8 O2 before they can reliably find an O2 planet. 8 O2, withdraw 3 O2's each, sabotage both modules right before cycle change, one person dies per cycle except you 2. Then go hide next to Ian or something.
