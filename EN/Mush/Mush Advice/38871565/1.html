Hello everyone! The most recent update added two new characters, Andie and Derek. This guide here will be for Andie, and is a work in progress, so any input from other players is welcome.

Recommended Activity Level - Mid to high. You'll either be the head of research, a Pilot, or both, so be ready to relay or to knock out hunters when possible.

Special Trait - Androgynous - Can flirt or do the thing with anyone on the ship, and cannot get pregnant. Gives you the ability game-play wise to fill in for a lack of males or females, given the situation. RP wise it opens up all sorts of avenues for LGBT play. Why does Roland keep dropping the soap in Bravo Dorm?

Skills

  • Pilot - Allows you to take out a patrol craft, the Pasiphae, and the Icarus without causing damage to the Daedalus. Allows for 80% accuracy firing from the patrol craft as well. This also allows you to set a stance while in a patrol craft. A valuable skill for the ship, especially in the late game, and allows you to help out in the late game when research is finished. Provides a bonus to the NERON projects Anti-gravity Thrusters, Bay Door XXL, Blaster Gun, Large Bay Upgrade, Launch Propulsion System, Magnetic Net, and Trail Minimizer.
  • Devotion - Gives you an additional 3 AP for every mission you receive. This means you will receive 6 AP for every mission, so make sure you let the commander know you need missions! This gives you a huge amount of AP to work with each day, and helps to minimize the impact of performing multiple roles.
  • Polyvalent - The reason you chose Andie. This skill should always be taken, no matter what. It gives you the bonuses of Botanist, Diplomat, and most importantly, Biologist, without their associated special actions or points. From Botanist you get to read the effects of food consumption, gain one additional fruit for each Harvest event in an expedition, can view the properties of plants, and have a bonus to the NERON projects Extra Hydropots, Heat Lamps, and Hydroponic Incubator, but you do NOT get the points. From Diplomat, you remove the possibility of a Fight event on expeditions, but you do not get the ability to call a Ceasefire. From Biologist you get everything; the ability to readth e properties of pills, the 4-6% research bonus, and the bonus to NERON projects Convenient Sink and Oxygenated Ducts. If you're Andie, then Finola will not be on the ship, meaning you should take this skill no matter what.
  • Confident - Gives the option for other players to Chit Chat with you for 1 AP, revealing as many actions they performed as you have morale and giving the other player 2 Morale. This can be performed once per day. This reveals ANY action you have performed, including mush actions, so it can be used as a voluntary substitute to Chao's Torturer ability. This is also a backup morale booster if all other options are lost, and can give Janice a way to gain morale outside of speeches and doing the thing. A powerful skill that requires no effort on you part, but doesn't replace the usefulness of Polyvalent.
  • Expert - This skill raises the successful completion chance of any chance based attempt by 20% (someone confirm if it's multiplicative or additive please?), but also increases your chances or injury or getting dirty by 20%. A good skill to combine with roles that have 0 risk of getting dirty or getting injured, but a risky skill to take, and requires 250 EXP to get.

Bronze

  • Pilot - Should only be taken as a bronze player if you know there is an active human Ian and/or Paola on board with Biologist. Allows you to do what all Pilots do, kill hunters and protect the ship.
  • Devotion - While it is a powerful skill, 99% of the time as Bronze you should NOT take this skill. If you chose to wait until mush selection however, and a Biologist or Medic book appears, and Janice has Diplomat, it might be worth taking, but in almost all cases Polyvalent is a better choice. If you've got two other Biologists on board that are active, and there's a Pilot book as well, it may be tempting to take this skill, but it's highly situational. Almost every time as a bronze player you should not take this skill.
  • Polyvalent - 9/10 times this should be taken. For 55 EXP, it's the cheapest Diplomat, it serves to replace Finola who will always be absent, it allows you to view medicines, and it gives an extra fruit on expeditions. Possibly one of the more powerful skills in the game, and puts a lot of responsibility on Andie. You'll be head of the research team and a required member of expeditions if there's no other Diplomat on board.
  • Confident - Good skill for morale gain, but not worth taking as bronze. If you're the only person to raise morale on the ship then there's going to be more issues than Confident will fix. Polyvalent and Pilot are usually better choices.
  • Expert - High EXP cost for bronze (551 cycles of EXP gain), coupled with the fact that, like Creative, it is meant to be used in combination with other skills, means you'd be wasting your EXP as a bronze player to take this skill. Save that EXP for another character, like Caffeine Junky or Apprentice.

Silver

  • Polyvalent + Devotion - Use missions to be the most powerful researcher on board. Usually the best choice, unless you are lacking in Pilots. Might not be worth taking if the commander is inactive however, as you won't get your missions in that case.
  • Polyvalent + Pilot - Divides your AP between two AP intensive roles, but gives you flexibility to fill roles as needed. Mush not a threat? Then you can take out some hunters. A viable replacement for Polyvalent + Devotion, just make sure you get a bed.
  • Polyvalent + Confident - Let's you power through research, but serve as a way to gain morale as well, all without splitting your AP between two roles. Most ships, however, don't last long enough for there to be widespread panic attacks, so a Shrink and a Leader Should be enough to take care of morale loss. Still, if a Pilot book is available, may be worth taking.
  • Pilot + Devoted - Makes you the single most powerful Pilot in the game, rivaling Roland in flying prowess, at the expense of crippling expeditions and research if no Diplomats or Biologists are on board. Make sure if you go this route that you let your crew know before hand, as not taking Polyvalent might get you killed.
  • Pilot + Confident - Making yourself an OK Pilot and giving you some ability to raise morale if Biologists are covered and there is no Shrink, but in most cases if Janice is inactive that means no Diplomat, so only take this if you KNOW there's a way to make up the research loss and a Diplomat book available.
  • Pilot + Expert - Pretty good combo, but carries the same inherent risks of slowing research way down, not to mention the EXP cost of getting this far.

Gold

  • Pilot + Devoted + Polyvalent + Confident - Let's you fill all the major roles, gives you the AP to do so, and let's you run the ship. A powerful combo, and typically is the best way to go.
  • Pilot + Polyvalent + Confident + Expert - If Expert gives you an additional 20% success chance (again, someone please confirm if it's multiplicative or additive), then you're guaranteed to hit as Pilot, and still let's you fill in all the other roles, and gives the Commander more reason to spread out his missions as needed, but not as versatile as the previous combination and more costly EXP wise.

Tips for In-Game

Assuming you've got Polyvalent, you're going to be at the head of the research department. If you've also got Pilot, then you'll need all the AP you can get. Typically you should claim the med lab bed, and have Chun watch over you. If you don't have Confident, then nobody else should be allowed in your room unless they are confirmed human, or have an injury. You should also update your crew mates on the properties of pills and fruits, as for now Astropad doesn't treat Polyvalent like it does Biologist and Botanist. Also make sure if you've got Devoted that the commander is well informed of the consequences of not giving you his mission every day without good reason.

Mush Play

No personal experience as a Mush Andie, so any advice is welcome. I do know that it is a bad day for the humans if both you and the commander are Mush, as those missions can be saved for a rampage that would make Derek weep in a corner. Only problem would be that your AP is the most valuable on the ship, so expect to have an excuse as to why you're not getting that bonus from the food you will probably receive to knock out ASG and Constipaspore by Day 2.

Since OP says any input is welcome, and since I saw that people loved it in Stephen's thread...

Andie's relationships with the crew
These are propositions only, based on how I comprehended the characters' bios. Don't just take them and follow, better think your own ways and check them here for some extra ideas They're quite gender-neutral, too.

You're a generally nice person. So your attitude towards most people will be pretty much the same. Therefore, your roleplay process will depend greatly on the response of other characters.
That's why these advices are only relevant to the most narrow and bio-faithful cases (I believe?).
Andie is a great character for dynamical play and displaying personal growth. And also for having fun while fooling around with overly formal attitude, or overly friendly attitude, and for being generally hyperactive in the chat. You're the one to be poking people with a stick. Insane shenanigans are also pretty easy to develop. As it was said at the beginning of this thread, everyone can make their own Andie...

Your Devoted relationships with Commander - whoever he/she is - are going to be special, because this person gives you your vital missions.

One important note for those who are not playing Andie but have them on the ship. About crew's general relationship with Andie and the attitude towards them. As Andie was a charming person during their SDF years, it would be pretty natural if a significant part of the crew developed friendly, pleasant relations with them. Especially if on your ship Andie is Confidaent.

1. Derek.
Obvious, huh? The one and only really familiar face in the crew where most people know each other already, and some have been friends for years. And the most anticipated meeting in ages for your orphaned soul. If he is active, he is your golden mine of emotions and drama, or fun and being all chirpy. Father figure or something more? You decide! But don't expect fast and easy resolution, as Derek doesn't remember you at all. Maybe the accident with Finola is to blame. And... What if he is mush and you are not? You're not going to recover soon from this one...

2. Jin Su
If he is active, he is your Commander. Period. You are not very rebellious (yet?), and he is a former SDF, so you gotta listen to him with your mouth open, radiating excitement. Besides, he's a heroic figure. Jin Su would surely love a devoted minion by his side. Keep yourself polite, even if you wanna scream from joy being around him, as he's a superior. ...Or scream from joy.

3. Gioele
If he's your Commander, you're going to serve him with diginity. Gioele probably wouldn't enjoy your company much. He would like you to stay polite and formal...and a little boot-licking, that millionaire! If he isn't the commander... you can ignore him and his whining and screams. Official position interests you more than money.

4. Paola
A total opposite of you, which could mean attraction of opposites, or constant cat fights. You're going to meet with her at the Comms terminal at trading events, wish it or not, so listen carefully: Paola is a rebel to the bone. She might be the one to cast disillusioning on you, and you might hate her for that. She bears entrielly different values. (But she is as sociable as you.) While you were studying hard, she was setting her pirate radiostation, ignoring uni attendance. She seems to know no boundaries or authorities. (Heretic!) How are you going to handle it? Maybe you'll end up same as her, and you'll be BFFs. Maybe you'll keep arguing about moral values and devotion. Both can be exciting.

5. Janice
She has potential to be your cushion...or reverse? She can be the one to hug you and dry your tears after you discover that little red book in the Laboratory... You have lots in common. She's formerly a Diplomate, like you. You digged into communication of living things between each other. She specialises on developing bonds of trust with binary applications. She's the world of new knowledge for you in the field you fancy. Besides, if you're Confident, she's going to talk with you lots, as she's always the one lacking moral support from others. You'll need each other.

6. Frieda
Another bridge buddy. She might be interesting for you, as she has unique cultural background coming from 21th cent. Lots of stories to tell during those night shifts. You'll feel respect for her, being older and stuff. She'll probably care for you, no reason for her to be angry with you. Not your key character though.

7. Eleesha
Another one who might tell you lots about the truth and play a significant role in your personal development. Especially since she's honest and straightforward. (So tackle her for conversation if you're the one initiating roleplay, Eleeshas are generally passive.) You might not believe her at first - just a shady yellow-press journalist, no? You might even verbally attack her for badmouthing your precious SDF... Prepare to not only know about Mush, but also about all the crap Federation has done.
