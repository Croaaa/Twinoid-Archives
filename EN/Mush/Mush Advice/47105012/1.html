This is the answer that was given by the admins when they were asked the question :
http://twd.io/e/RhAX0g/125

They didn't explain everything, but basically, what happens at each cycle change is based on several factors :
- the crew's activity (number of AP spent)
- their "capacity to face obstacles" (nobody knows what that means exactly)
- what happened during the previous cycles
- the duration of the ship

This means that a crew that has a lot of inactive players will have a little less obstacles. A crew with normal activity (or higher than average) will have more problems. That includes injuries, breakdowns, fires, electrical spikes, panic attacks, and hunters (not their frequency, which is roughly once a day, but their number).

But as Sperbi explained, concretely, it just means that less active crews have a chance of surviving despite the lack of activity / skills / crew members. It doesn't mean that if your crew is mainly inactive, things will become extremely easy. In the end, having an active crew is the best way to have long ships and have a chance at Eden.

Also, if you have no hunters for one day or more, since the number of hunters is based on the previous cycles, you'd better be prepared for a big wave, inactivity or not.
