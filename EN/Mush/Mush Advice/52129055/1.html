I really wish someone told this to me when I started playing mush:

When you join a game the system will choose your skills for you if you don't have more skills unlocked than the available skill slots.

As a morale giver if you arrive shortly before cycle change don't wait for it to change, update the page just in case someone wasn't keeping you posted on morale, you know until they have a panic attack that has brought them to 0.

Be careful of storage rooms as mush can trade you at any point. And if a Pilgred trade is present then you have a very serious problem.

Anyone can repair an O2 tank pretty handily, the wrench helps a lot but if you have decent AP get on it just in case the Tech can't get there before cycle change.

If you have any trade offers at mush selection you can check 5 rooms at once by seeing the "Items we have to offer" bit. This only works if there are no attacking hunters.

Always have tabs on Ian, ESPECIALLY if he's inactive. The mush LOVE using him as a shield.

If you can kill a trader in one cycle(the only way to actually kill them instead of scare them off.) then you have a chance of getting lunchboxes as scrap. Which may help stave off starvation from loss of Icarus.(Not a massive chance though, just a last ditch method)
Also anabolics remove the starving status. So they aren't always the worst use of AP.

It's not always obvious but try not to store items in the hallways. At least not until after the mush are dead.

No one or combination of things can guarantee success or failure, except surrender.

* Young plants should not be watered until dry, and not treated for disease until mature.

* It is actually better when other people than Ian water plants, or at least help, so he can save his for more expensive actions.

* People will die from lack of oxygen before plants produce oxygen in C1, so having 1 left in C8 is not enough. Same goes for returning expeditions.

* Anything you consume, even coffee and pills, will stop starvation.

* While Janice is around, and if you are not worried about spores, it is actually more effective to eat rations cold and have her hug you than to spend 1 AP for 1 by cooking.

* You can "do it" only once per day, so it's best to meet at midnight and do it twice.

* An IT Expert's points can not be used for hacking.

* Success % increases are shared between techs trying to dismantle the same object, and retained as long as you do nothing else. (iirc)

* Working on Comms will always produce a Chef Book sooner or later.

* Some Mush skills, especially Fungal Kitchen, have unexpected privacy levels. Make sure to learn those so you know when you are or aren't safe.

Dzanek

Adding some of mine:

* Never use Knife in combat if you're actually wrestler or even solid. You won't cause more damage and you risk getting injuried. The only positive effect is critical hit that is rare. Safe punching is enough to cause huge damage using few AP.

* Don't use Blasters without shooter skill. Don't spend AP to shoot with Blaster without Lenses if you can actually do this as wrestler. Blaster causes few damage, it works with free well but not worth AP. Causes injuries to your target, but even yourself. About other kinds of blasting weapon it's another matter.

* All your daily actions (infect, extract spores, do the thing, establish comms, etc.) are put for your character, which means that after using transfer you can use the limit of new's body, if he hasn't made certain daily actions since the beginning of day.

* Camera doesn't detect transfer and doesn't show contaminating food with spores, but they're detectable with MycoAlarm. However, MycoAlarm doesn't detect extracting spores, human secret actions nor demoralizing. Moreover, MycoAlarm won't show which Mush did act, only when.
