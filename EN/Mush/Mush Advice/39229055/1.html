You can always lie about anything. But you are always going to have people out there who are counting every AP you spend, tracking every movement you make, and analyzing every choice of yours. It's just how the game works: it encourages you to be super paranoid. (For the record, you can always claim level 2 gold, and nobody will be able to check that unless they figure out who you are and check your account. Even then, they can't see what you spent your XP on.)

I have only been on a handful of ships and have never been a mush, alpha or otherwise, so take this with a grain of salt. But I feel like you could probably get away with acting human. Work with your partner, and bide your time until it is right. Convenient timing mistakes are good, but if you make too many, you might get eliminated for the good of the ship, even if they have no reason to suspect you as mush.

I would suggest always having a backup plan. An idea of what to steal when you fly off, who to kill if you are forced to rampage, where to rest so that if the hunt is called on you, you are right where you can do the most damage. And you've mentioned that you are really active in the chat? Use that. When they're closing in on you, lie. Point out ways it couldn't have been you, cast suspicion on others, get indignant at being accused. Even when you are outed, you can still play mind games. Are you the only mush with a braindead partner, or one of two active, clever mush? First time mush just making mistakes? Have you made betas? Have you sabotaged critical things? Only you know the truth. All they have are guesses.

Clearly you aren't interested in being a lame mush, so I think you'll do fine. Just be the mush that would make the game challenging and fun for you as a human player, and you're golden.
