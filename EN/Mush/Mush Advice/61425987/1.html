First of all, don't make Antispore Gas as the first research. It's not practical, it's a flawed meta. You may ask for an explanation, but it might take a separate post. If you're Finola, convince people to help with Constipaspore Serum or MycoAlarm. Then the second of these. The next should be Mycoscan. Once Mush dies, immediately go for Mushovore Bacteria. You may even consider Natamy Rifle, Spore Sucker or Mushicide Soap (but this one is glitched and people might feel unfair upgrading their soap with living Mush). Don't go for Antispore Gas. It's a waste unless you suspect you might have already got some Betas on board, this sure would annoy Betas, but generally, it's useless.

As silver, don't imagine any combos without Medic. About taking another skill:

  • Biologist - great boost early game, although it comes quite useless late game when the research is done. With this skill, you're able to complete all the basic anti-Mush research on the day of selection. It's highly recommended when there are no other biologists on board and even the starting Mage Book doesn't satisfy you.
  • Diplomat - it greatly improves chances of Eden if you actively participate during expeditions. Simply take it when there are no other diplomats onboard and printed Mage Books from Xyloph still didn't satisfy you. Keep in mind you may still save a slot in case the other diplomat dies (mainly because of Mush).
  • Nurse - a decent perk in the late game, it's useful as your main task would be healing once research is done. You may consider taking this if you've already researched what's been useful, better than being a reserve diplomat.
  • OCD - simply when your apron is gone but you really consider spending XP for this skill?

Playing as Mush, your gameplay should depend on your partner's capabilities.

  • If your partner is going to actively make and use spores, the strategy is either:
    • classic: give humans a reason not to kill you and do research, but don't complete a research that would be a burden for your partner. Remember that the research doesn't affect you as you're not making spores. Rushing Antispore Gas and showing determination is a very good idea. A single partner shouldn't make more than 2 spores a day although it might be a trouble if you get a Beta, but getting one is already a win. Another perk is gaining trust by humans as they're relying on the gas as a child to a lollipop.
    • explicit: reveal yourself! You're better off letting them know you're Mush than being an obstacle to your partner as a researcher. But not literally. Simply you should help your partner. Remember that dying you let the humans make Mushovore Bacteria which is a quick research. Your aim is to bring the greatest advantage for your partner after intending to be revealed. You may use diplomat skill and spike a human after removing a camera before day change then spike again on the next day. Reading technician book to dismantle Mush Genome Disk, Tabulatrix and a few of cameras? Whatever you do, you should be remembered as not helping human even once.
    • stable: don't help human, don't reveal yourself... yet. It depends on the human if you get killed sooner or later. You may just stay in the Medlab as no one disturbs you and triple-contaminate morale pills. Once you're found dirty, you're screwed, but who would come to visit you? You may also pretend to be AFK but it's lame, although still better than helping with research.
  • If your partner is a decent player but unable to find opportunities to make spores throughout the game or the spore production is very limited and slow either:
    • improvise: co-operate with your partner so one of you can have an opportunity to make spores and potentially convert somebody even for the cost of sacrificing one of you.
    • mimic: simply pretend to be human and look for opportunities. Rush with research as it doesn't affect any of you and sound genuine to humans. Remember that you must act if there are too few non-confirmed people onboard.
  • When your partner is inactive or not smart enough to survive this ship simply go mimicking human waiting for opportunities.
  • Also, rampage is also a thing in all cases.



Tricks and tips:

  • You may consider bringing the apron with you on expo if there's no risk of losing an item. Keep in mind that others could use the apron and the Mush might find an excuse to get dirty while gardening.
  • You might use private channels to co-operate with other researchers and find a cycle to burn all your Action Points for relaying.
    • You may also keep research progress in a private channel, as it's more useful for Mush than human. Also, once research's done is not announced unless they look at the bottom bar. They could fail to make spores (since when it costs 4 AP?) or even fall under MycoAlarm trap without realizing its existence.
  • As determined Mush hunters usually focus on counting Action Points of those who had an opportunity to get dirty rather than the rest, you as Mush may "borrow some AP" to your partner. Make some additional research progress as your partner claims to have participated in it or water plants while you're relaying research so your partner can claim to have watered them instead. Your partner shouldn't forget to leave logs accessing research lab and grabbing the apron.

I've been Mush 38 times. ASG was being picked very frequently as first research but this hardly ever affected the Mush gameplay. Half of the games my Alpha partner was either inactive or unable to find an opportunity to wash from dirtiness. I didn't have any reason to produce more than 2 spores a day unless I had accumulated AP prior selection and was going to make some infection on the first day... before any research had a chance to be done. When I had an active partner and we both were doing spores: either we were able to make spores before any research was done and later we didn't have any opportunity to make more as we didn't use more than 2 spores a day. The most crucial thing is that even if we were actively using spores and once we wanted to extract more but we hit the limit - we just waited for another day to make the next spores. The Action Points we were planning to spend on extracting spores - we spent them on doing human stuff so we would be better hidden and more trustworthy to humans. So all you do making ASG is just delaying the spore extraction and make Mush less likely to be detected regardless if they want to do what they have to or play risky by extracting more in one day.

ASG is not a free research. It's being done at 6-9% base efficiency. At the same efficiency is being done working on Constipaspore Serum and MycoAlarms. It means that if you complete ASG, at the same rate CS or MA could be completed but you refused them so ASG was made instead. However, CS and MA are more worth doing as the first and second anti-Mush research than ASG.

CS is a better research than ASG because:

  • When there's s rampage and Mush eats freshly produced spores, ASG decreases AP gain by 4 points. Taking Mycelium Spirit, the Phagocyte rampage is even more efficient. CS prevents any AP gain from freshly produced spores on phagocyting. Even with Mycelium Spirit - no matter how many spores you're able to produce, CS doesn't give any AP gain from this. Once knowing this, ASG followers had arguments that Fertile skill is countering CS as it bypasses the expensive cost of a spore. They're right, but it doesn't mean that ASG is a useful research, because we're dealing with 2 Alphas and even if both of them took Fertile+Phagocyte, which is rare for two randoms on a ship to have a high-level skill, they can gain AP from consuming only 2 free spores at most, so ASG doesn't limit it at all. Thus, for Phagocyte rampage, ASG is worthless when you have CS.
  • CS makes spore production 4 AP instead of 2 AP. It means that making more than 2 spores a day requires being really incapacitated in doing human stuff. It helps investigators to find the Mush. With ASG done over CS, the Mush will make spores at a cheap cost. If you think that extracting 2 spores for a Mush in somehow detectable, then what about a single spore with CS? If there are no investigative people to deal with the Mush, the ship is screwed either way.
  • With CS, 4 AP cost is forever. With ASG, it wears off over a day. About Fertile, it could also wear off over a day, but for 2 fertile Alphas, there are only 2 free spores, although Mycelium Spirit would be a better skill in ASG presence for mass spore production. ASG is still worthless as you help the Mush in hiding themselves due to the limit as they'll use the AP, which were supposed to be spent on extracting spores, for doing human stuff.

MA is much more powerful research than ASG whereas:

  • MycoAlarms are the only devices that can prevent triple-contamination of food, especially fruit, pills, anabolics and coffee, which guarantees conversion upon consumption unless Chun consumes it. Those are really needed in Lab and Medlab as stocks of consumables are usually stored there.
  • It guarantees that all people on board will be protected from directly spiking, especially those inactive in corridors that are a subject for Anonymush.
  • Unless the Mush knows the location of MAs, once they're hidden, the Mush will be most likely give up trapping, contaminating food and directly spiking people. The ship has 27 rooms. 6 of them are turrets, 5 storages, there's also a garden where no one goes. Once you clear 3 corridors of items, you have 12 rooms left. With 5 cameras, you only put MA in 5 of 7 remaining areas and it's a great risk of the Mush to expose themselves if they go trapping rooms. If there are only 3 cameras, there's even more reason to make MycoAlarms as there are plenty of areas where the Mush can convert people.

Inferring, CS and MA are the most optimal research for start, as they prevent an effective rampage and stealth converting no matter how many spores they can produce. It's inevitable to make them extract spores, but with these pieces of research are done you make them impossible to use these spores.

Once you make CS and MA, consider creating Mycoscan in order to prevent any kind of stealth conversion, as people may frequently check themselves after performing actions in unsecured areas, as there are some unsecured areas which can be contaminated only by a trap. ASG doesn't help as converting a person with a trap requires several days, while the double amount of spores will be being produced. You should consider inventing a Spore Sucker to remove spores if those happen to be in human's body.

ASG is only worth doing if you suspect there's more than 2 agents of Mush but with MA done it's nearly impossible to successfully convert somebody.
