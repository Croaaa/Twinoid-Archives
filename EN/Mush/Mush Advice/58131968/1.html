Because too many crewmembers go straight to bed or stand in the lab without doing anything, or want to do their job and not anyone else's. Meanwhile, the ship deteriorates, morale plummets, and let's face it, it's boring.

So... come on, people, you've got a ship to save! Wake up, play... and have fun.

First things first: Communication
- It is always helpful to start a "what should I do" thread for those who don't know how to spend their AP. Emphasize the priorities of the moment (for instance, insist on hunters if the ship is in danger of exploding) and give a list of useful actions.
- Report what you did. For instance, if you install a camera in the dorm, report it. It's always annoying to go to a room with a camera in your inventory and discover that there's already one there.
- Ask if there are new players, and guide them, encourage them to ask questions. Someone who fixes a door in Front corridor and finds a hidden walkie on the bridge has done the tutorial and needs advice.
- On the other hand, if you're not sure what to do, ask your crewmates instead of doing things at random, or worse, of doing nothing.
- A silent ship is not fun. Even if you have to talk to Neron, to the cat or to the drone, try to liven things up and hopefully other players will reply.
- A bit of RP is always welcome, even basic RP: Finola is mad at Kuan Ti and Chao for kidnapping her, Stephen helped Paola run away from home a few years ago and then he spent time in Gioele's mines, Andie worships Derek and has no idea about the SDF being controlled by the Mush... Interact with others and, once again, have fun!


The most immediate threat: Hunters. It is everyone's job to save the ship.
- Of course, if you are a pilot, grab a coffee and ration, jump into a patrol ship, and try to kill as many hunters as possible. Unless you have an incapacitating injury, and in that case, say it, so that your crewmates know that they must take care of hunters.
- If there are no pilots or they can't deal with hunters, then everyone should try to destroy them. Use turrets, and always report which ones are empty.
- If there are too many hunters, you can also hack and move the ship (remember that IT Experts have a bonus on hacking).
- Once all hunters are destroyed, pilots should collect scrap as soon as possible.
- If the hull has had too much damage, it should be repaired. Anyone can do it, no need to be a tech, you just need the wrench and a piece of scrap metal. If you have the oscilloscope blueprint, build it in AB2 before repairing the hull.


The other immediate danger: Morale. Especially if Chun and/or Janice are not awake and you spend one or two day changes without them.
- If you are a shrink, go to the dorms (try to alternate regularly, and tell people where you are).
- Check the lab logs and if some people spent day change without Chun, comfort them once or twice. And do not comfort those who are sleeping in your dorm while ignoring the others.
- If there is no shrink (or you are the shrink), you can cuddle the cat when Raluca wakes up.
- If Andie has Confident, you can confide to them once a day.
- Jin Su or sometimes Kuan Ti can give speeches (try to do it in crowded rooms, it's more efficient).
- If a biologist, nurse or medic is awake, they can give you pill properties and tell you if there are pills for morale.
- In an emergency, you can eat the bananas instead of planting them.
- If none of the above is possible, then you can do the thing with someone else. Try to keep medlab empty for that purpose (and for surgery).


Important to do on the first day: Gardening. Again, you don't have to wait for a botanist to take care of plants.
- Move the banana trees from the garden to the lab, as well as the empty hydropots (in the garden and in storages).
- If there is alien fruit in the refectory (in seasons with starting injuries), check their properties (if you are a chef or botanist) and plant them if they're good.
- If nobody can see their properties, plant them anyway to avoid their destruction, in the hope of getting good fruit and having oxygen faster than with banana trees.
- If there are no alien fruit or they're not good, plant the bananas as soon as possible.
- Check the plants on cycle 8, water and treat adult plants, and water young plants if they are dried out.


Less urgent: Astro
- If the ship is already in orbit, everyone can analyse the planet (1 AP or IT point per zone, or 1 AP or IT points for 2 zones if you're an astro).
- If the planet is interesting, prepare the future expo: bring all the equipment to Icarus Bay (compass, drill, ropes, weapons, spacesuits, post-its, and useful items built from BPs if you have them).
- Write a visible thread with clear instructions, taking into account the planet zones and the crew's skills.
- If the planet is dangerous, load 1 fuel and be ready to leave.


Miscellaneous
- Move items to the right places (apron in the lab, soap in Bravo dorm, expo things in rear corridor or Icarus Bay, extinguishers in RC and FC, freezer in IB...). Remember that you are not allowed to hold anything before selection.
- Start research (anti-mush research are forbidden): NCC Lenses (you need to hold the cat), Ultra-healing pomade with the medikit in the lab, Narcotics distiller. Don't forget to wear the apron.
- Projects: discuss with the crew before starting anything and remind beginners that only one project must be chosen.
- Technicians should dismantle the alpha shower and can also dismantle the microwave or freezer if the crew really need scrap.
- As long as there is no comms officer, anyone can accept or refuse trades. Of course, you should discuss it with the crew first.
- Install cameras in both dorms and discuss the location of the other camera(s).
- If you have an injury, read this before performing surgery: http://twd.io/e/CGJo0M/0
- Wait for the others to wake up before choosing your skills, depending on what the ship needs: more techs, more pilots, more morale sources...


Remember, the key thing is to adapt your goals to each ship, depending on the problems it's currently facing, on the other crewmembers' skills and activity...

As I said in the "Communications" part, it is always useful to sum up all of this at the start of a ship, in order to motivate people, to organize the crew, and to guide players who don't know what to do. It allows to keep the ship in one piece, it keeps everyone busy, and teaches them that they should not stick to "their" job and ignore what's happening around them. And it helps avoiding slow, boring starts that end up with the ship exploding.
