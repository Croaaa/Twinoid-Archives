[6826491]

Yes, it's primarily there to prevent people from bailing on their ships. But bear this in mind: it's better for players to not have any suicides in their ship history, because that suggests that they might be willing to suicide when things look grim.

I'll give you an example: A player has been the last person standing on two prior ships, and committed suicide by cat both times, just so they wouldn't have to wait to die. They die on a future ship by accident, just because they got scratched while picking up the cat, and again while caressing it. Now, on its face, it could appear that they committed an intentional suicide on this third ship, and looking at their ship history would show they indeed committed suicide by cat twice previously. Would YOU be more likely to believe the third suicide was completely unintentional, knowing that they had committed suicide on two previous ships?

It actually doesn't matter that the previous suicides were conducted only as the last person standing. Why they did it previously doesn't actually matter, either. The rules would still have been broken in those cases, reported or not, and it still reflects badly on the player if they get reported in the future.
