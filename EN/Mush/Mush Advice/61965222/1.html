Returning to Sol pre-selection is against the rules. It's a little like individual suicide, except that it is collective: if it was allowed, people would end their ships prematurely at the slightest difficulty. Suicide wasn't against the rules at the beginning of the game, and players used to kill themselves as soon as they thought the ship was "doomed"... which it rarely was. I saw people commit suicide because someone had established comms too early, because Jin Su was not a leader, because they did not like the ship... There was no way to establish a clear limit, because it's impossible to define clearly when a ship is doomed.

It is the same with ships that fill slowly. In fact, sometimes a ship that fills faster and seems active can be disappointing after mush selection, and the reverse is also true. All it needs is a few motivated crew members to change things for the better. I had very slow filling ships on which I felt a little hopeless and soooo bored, but in the end more active people woke up and it changed the ship.

First of all, I advise you to read this tutorial, which gives a few guidelines about how to organize a filling ship where some characters and skills are missing. The important thing is to relay that advice to your crewmembers, making lists of things that need to be done, using red, bold characters and icons, to make important orders more visible. Also don't hesitate to send PMs. Too many people wake up, go straight to bed and wait until selection, but if you ask them directly, some of them will listen and do useful things.

What I also do is tell my friends who's awake on my ships and what characters are needed (on my Twinoid Nexus, in my casting...). You can also use the Random jump thread. It helps filling the ship faster and with more active players.

It might feel like it is against (individual) players, but in fact it is one of the rules that have healthy in-game environment in mind.

Imagine, if this action would be allowed. Even, if we put up various rules about when it is allowed, we wouldn't be able to define all the circumstances. People have various ideas about what a "doomed" ship is, how many (and which) characters have to be active to call a ship "active", how active player has to be to actually "be active", when it is "too late" for ship to be saved... Even, if we put up standards and numbers in work, there would still be people who would push for them to be more flexible, using the same arguments as you are.

If we wouldn't put up any rules concerning pre-selection Sol return, slippery slope effect would probably follow. People who would do that for the first time in really dire situation, would slowly relax they own criterions and we would end up in situation similar to what Peanutz described above - people feeling like they are entitled to return to Sol whenever they see slightest problem.

Worse part of that is that the real vicitms would be people who like to fight the worst odds and never give up. AKA the main player group that the game is intended for. Sure, we can say that finishing Pilgred is possible only in situation when every person agrees - but I don't expect you waiting for your inactive crewmember to respond. And what if he comes online cycle after you returned to Sol, just to be disappointed that he wasn't able to play? Maybe they had accident or sudden change of daily routine, and managed to join the game after all? Like it or not, SDF has to protect the right of those players to enjoy the game - simply because no one else can and would.

All in all, if we don't want the game environment deteriorate to the point where the game is not enjoyable to people it is intended for (people who want difficult experience and fight the odds), we must stop potentially unhealthy trends in its beginnings. And this is one of them.
