First of all I see you are new to Mush, Welcome! For the next bit I need to explain where I come from and my usual modus operandi for when I play Mush:

I like RP. I grew up with pen and paper games and MMOs. I enjoy games like Mafia and a terrible little game called Space Station 13 - which I see many similariities in between it and Mush. While I don't initiate RP on my ships, when I see another player attempt to talk in character, I will follow suit and do so as well. I think it's fun and adds a bit to the ships.

However.

Mush is a social game. So I believe it has the infrastructure to embrace RPing - to a strong extent. But at the end of the day it is a game. One with winners and losers. If you want to roleplay out a little chat where you gradually bring your suspicions to my attention I'm going to view it as indirect and potentially harmful to the crew. Simply because by taking your time with what you're trying to say, or saying it in a passive or indirect way could potentially buy the Mush time to do what they do. When it comes to many ship functions and events (fires, hunters, illnesses, etc.) I think it is fine and largely acceptable to RP reactions or announcements of those so long as you don't delay in the process. But anything involving mush are a different story.

If I, as Chun, get a whisper from you and you give me this elaborate story about you fixing some orange juice in the kitchen and noticing Stephen has a strange musty smell and following him for a few cycles and seeing him enter one of the storage rooms and blah blah blah, my eyes are going to glaze over and I'm going to want you to get to the point. I'm also going to be less likely to clue you in on future mush-hunt things simply because you could drag down the conversation.

At the end of the day people play this game for different reasons. Some like the ship management aspect where everyone has their own jobs. You see very effective Friedas or Rolands never say a word but dutifully go about their jobs. This is an example. Others play for the thrill of the hunt. These are players that are/would be fond of games like Maifa where you must sniff out a subversive element amongst you. The players who play more for the hunt are generally (not always) less tolerant of flowery prose and indirect accusations - they will tolerate RP in a public setting, but the second it detracts from the hunt or causes you to because suspicious due to the roleplaying they will be all over you.

This is not always the case of course. One of the best RPers I've seen in game was a Gioele who became the captain early on, lead the mush hunt, and kept the ship going for a crazy long time - while never breaking character. This is very much the exception instead of the rule though.

My recommendation? RP away. Feel free to do it all you want in the general chat and even favorited threads. If someone whispers you and RPs with you through a whisper go ahead (this will not happen often though outside of castings, I assure you). But if you whisper someone else to bring something to their attention - do it in a direct OOC setting. Think of whispers as mostly OOC and you'll be fine.

The only other thing to say is to not let your actions be defined by your character. This is very important and will save you much grief in later ships. Just because you play Derek (or any shooter character) does not mean you grab a blaster off the bat and run around with it the whole ship because it is your "job". Same with playing Stephen and actually cooking. Some actions will get you nailed by the mush hunters quickly, so make sure your actions are not dictated by the persona you are adopting for your character. Keeping that in mind (and the Whisper = OOC thing) you should be fine with whatever roleplaying you wish.

I am a big fan of roleplaying in Mush (it's honestly the main draw for me), but I agree that the central part of the gameplay (finding mush, reporting on your job progress) needs to be kept clean and tidy. Sometimes if I need to be extra direct, I'll use ((out of character notation)) so I don't blur the two more than I'd like.

There's an art to communicating useful game information while staying in character. I always love when other players really hit that sweet spot. In-character banter is also lots of fun, though some sourpusses get annoyed at all the messages they have to clear as "read." Sometimes you have to adjust to the crew and keep the essential threads (piloting, research, etc.) free of RP clutter, other times people don't mind. Just try to stay aware and courteous. I've had multiple Commanders who didn't like all the reading on RP-heavy ships, so we made "commander threads" for them to favorite, kept up-to-date with whether the ship needed to be moved and in what direction.

Personally I don't mind RP at all in Health and Morale Threads as long as the important information is (more or less) clearly visible.

Example:
"I picked up the Apron to avoid dirtiness and worked on Antispore until it was at nine percent and then dropped the Apron again. Now I need one relay."

"I picked up the Apron to avoid dirtiness and worked on Antispore until it was at 9% and then dropped the Apron again. Now I need a relay."

You can use formatting to highlight important information and keep the important numbers arabic(easier to distinguish and easier to read).

Some threads even beg for some RP in my opinion. Most variations of "Dirty..." are plain boring. You can always tell them how you got dirty. Sure, don't write a whole book about this, but 2-3 nice sentences fitting to your RP instead of "dirty cooking" are very nice.

It's also possible to RP in discussions. A Frieda, who's torn between CPU Overclocking, because she wants to discover many planets since her childhood and Plasma Shield as she experienced hunters damaging her equipment in the past...The only other thing to say is to not let your actions be defined by your character.This means for my example, that you should end up with your real opinion (and ultimately vote for the Plasma Shield) instead of "I'm Frieda, I'm Astro, I want CPU Overclocking!".


Finding a good way to RP can be very difficult as you don't want to switch into efficiency mode(only post numbers and short sentences), but don't want to drag down your ship(team,...) by (slower) RP either.

I found that avoiding numbers works fine with everyone as long as the numbers are not in any critical range.

Lets say we have an expedition event and I lost 3 points of full health. When the medic asks me how I am, I can say "I'm only short 3 health, don't waste your AP on me" or I can say "Just some bruises, take care of the others first." Both works fine as long as the medic knows the game a bit.

But if I loose 5 health from the event and I was seriously wounded before so I'm down to 4 health now, I'd not RP around the numbers - unless I've already RPed with the medic about health&wounds and found her to appreciate AND understand.

Which brings me to something else that was always important from p&p RPGs to MMOs: Never establish a general level of RPing for yourself and fanatically stick to it, always tone the level of RP up and down according to the other players. And if dealing with a player who's just not into RPing, don't force it upon him, rather assume you are mind-absent and just give him the bare information.
