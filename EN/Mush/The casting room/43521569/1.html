[7950824]

A few notes about casting ship rules, and reports made on them:

1) Your casting, your rules ... but Twinoid's rules over all

You are allowed to make your own rules that technically 'break' normal Mush rules; like cooperation between mush and human teams. However, you should not make any rules that break Twinoid terms of service, like... oh, lets say creating a 'troll ship', where personal insults and lots of profanity is allowed or even encouraged. If your casting rules permit the breaking of Twinoid terms of service and we become aware of it, we'll probably have to do something about your ruleset as well as the reported person.

2) Consider the punishment you desire for those who break your casting's unique rules, and if you want (the leaders of your Casting group) to handle it personally within the casting only, or if you want it going past that point and affect the rulebreaker's overall game play, on and off the casting.

If your casting has complex unique rules and a player is accepted, participates, and does not follow your unique rules, is it enough for you to ensure that the player does not play within your casting in the future?

If that resolution is acceptable to you - I'd advise explaining to your casting group how you'd like your ruleset enforced - if it's enough for you to have group members report to the group's director that rules are being broken by someone, and that person to be removed after endpage names are revealed (or sooner if you know who it is who breaks your rules), so no SDF reports should be filed.

Remember that SDF sanctions affect a player inside and outside of your Casting. A generally acceptable player who simply does not belong in your casting and should never have joined that ship.... maybe they can be removed without any other sort of punishment needed?

3) If you do need to report a player for making a Casting-specific violation and there is concern that the judges may be unaware of the specific ruleset being used, that casting's Director should message the mod inbox (http://twinoid.com/tid/groupDiscuss)

Things the Director should let the mods know:

The name of the player who made the report (this allows us to identify the proper report)

The ruleset agreed for the specific casting ship (this allows us to inform the judges)

With that a mod can make sure the judges will be aware of that report's 'special needs'. I am unsure if that will ever be an issue or not - but if you believe this may be an issue - this is how to fix it.

The main problem with that is that it's still something we have to look through. And in the single case we've seen, it probably took more time to figure out what to do with it simply because there was no rules post at the start of the ship. If we had had that, it would have been open and shut. Instead, I alone took 3-4 days to debate with myself on what it deserved if anything.

Ideally, the castings ships will all have the respect they deserve and people will follow the rules. But if a rule is broken and it gets reported, we have no idea what to do with it.

Let's say your ship is cooperative. Mush are expected to help humans. Roland is mush and he's playing a typical mush role... or he's just generally being unhelpful to the humans so Paola reports him. As a judge without a rules post, I'm going to wonder what kind of crack Paola was smoking and clear the report. As a judge with a rules post, it takes 30 seconds to find the post, a few more seconds to read the rules, 10 seconds to select "clear", hit submit and wait for the page to update..

TBH, I don't want you guys to report castings stuff... as much because castings people should be abiding by the rules of the castings as I don't want more reports to deal with. But it's already happened once and I'm sure it'll happen again... I'm just asking you guys to make it easier for us to know what your rules are for that ship. (and it's never bad to remind the players once they're on the ship as well!)

The whole debate is moot if you guys just make sure there's an early post on the ship with the rules. As long as you do that, we're happy.
