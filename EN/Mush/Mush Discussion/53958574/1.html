How do you think the game mechanics would need to change to make Mush a fun and accessible game in a real-time setting?

My questions to you are:

1) Would you still enjoy Mush if it required real-time attention to finish a match?

3) What do you think would have to considerably changed from the current formula?
4) If you dislike the idea of Mush being real-time, do you have ideas on how to minimize idleness and other downsides of Mush's current timing?

Let me know your thoughts! This subject has kept my brain busy for a long time, now.

ividyon

So, do you believe it's necessary to design the game in a way that people can freely drop in/out of a session, and/or matches can be fun and completable even without the full cast?

Mechanics such as people joining in the middle of a match to take over an idle/disconnect character come to mind.

I don't believe it's necessary in the strictest sense of the word. You can certainly design a game that intends to have 16 players play from start to finish. Or you can design it so people can freely drop in and out, or that the full cast is not necessary to complete the game if people disappear. There are pros and cons to each method. To commit 16 players from start to finish, you're going to have long waits until a game starts and infrequent ships launching. It's not just a matter of getting people ready, but people knowing they need to commit their attention and focus reduces the player pool at any given moment.

To make what might be an unfair comparison, I've always wanted to play Artemis Spaceship Bridge Simulator, but I don't have the people to play it with, so that game has been inaccessible to me. Granted, that game is designed to be played locally with friends you can see with your own two eyes, but Mush doesn't exactly have hundreds of people joining ships in a day.

I'm not really making strong arguments on this point, but ultimately, it can go either way, there's no wrong answer, it depends on what the goal is for the game. I'm not sure how much I like the idea of say, Raluca playing for 33% of the game, quitting, and having someone take over Raluca. I feel like that destroys the social element of the game and the infused identity of the players into the characters, and switches the focus to a much heavier emphasis on roles and mechanics. But hey, at the same time, if it's done right, it could work.

As for AP, my idea was simply that gauges are much more limited, however refill fully upon every cycle end, instead of regenerating over time.

I think I see what you're getting at. My problem with this is that I believe that AP is valuable in Mush because of the time it takes to generate, which leads to a strong desire to be efficient with it. If someone is getting 2, 4, or 6 AP every 2, 5, or 10 minutes or whatever scale might be proposed, it becomes less valuable of a resource, and it makes accomplishing objectives seem trivial. Just wait a few minutes and relay back and forth with me and Plasma Shield will be complete detracts from the relatively grandiose sense of accomplishment we have currently.

I've always had a personal distaste for many RTS games which lack a certain amount of depth, because the winner essentially boils down to who can click the fastest. Harvest resources -> Build bases -> Build units -> Assault. The only thing that bottlenecks the process is how efficient you can be with what you're clicking, where you're clicking, and how fast you're clicking.

This is why I suggested you could go the way of Yohoho! Puzzle Pirates. If you've never played it, a group of players gather on a pirate ship. There are different sized ships with a different capacity for players, ranging from something like 6 players to 64 players (it's been a while since I played). There would be different stations scattered across the ship: Bilge, Carpentry, Sails, Navigation, Guns. Everybody mans a station. The Bilger plays a Match 3 type game to pump out water that the ship is taking on. If he performs well, more water is removed and the ship is not as affected by the bilge water, which slows down the ship. The Carpenter plays a tangrams type puzzle to repair the hull. The Sailor plays a Dr. Mario type game to generate sail tokens for the Navigator to use. The gunner plays a puzzle which is similar to some game I've never played but I think was popular in Japan, essentially loading the cannons so they can be refired. The sole Navigator plays on a turnbased chessboard against another ship using sail tokens and cannons to plot out moves and overtake the other ship. Then it leads to a boarding process and all players participate in a group swordfight, playing a game like Super Puzzle Fighter Turbo to ultimately win the battle, and whichever ship performed better in Battle Navigation has an advantage in the Swordfight phase.

Sorry for the longwinded explanation, but my point is, when reducing the time scale of a game like Mush from weeks to a couple hours, I think it might be important to switch the focus entirely from a game of spending accumulated energy to accomplish things, to something more entirely skill-based in an effort to retain meaningful gameplay and accomplishment. Do you want to spend a couple hours performing relays and seeing what random stuff pops up in Astronav so you can explore a planet with random outcomes and be done with the whole thing (I'm not even thinking about how the Mush work in the whole thing right now). I think that only really works as successful gameplay in the grand scale we currently have. Wouldn't you rather play a research minigame with a relayer for a while, and the better you perform, the more research gets done? Same for NERON projects, astronavigation, planet exploration, so on and so forth.

Just my rambling thoughts.

The main draw in Mush for me is RP, which actually benefits nicely from the long format it currently has. I am a busy person and have other things I have to do, but I can drop in whenever I want and respond to things as they appear.
