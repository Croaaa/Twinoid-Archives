NinetailedThere isn't anything else visibly wrong, but I'm wouldn't know how to spot most of the standard hazards.

Look for NURON announcements in the general chat channel (book icon). This will inform you of things such as fires, but only includes notifications about a scant few breakages, such as damage to the Oxygen stores. Your on your own for discovering all other breakages and damage.

Broken items will be seen to be smoking if you move to an area where there is one or more.

Check the logs (the icon is an arrow pointing down at a disk, whatever that's supposed to suggest...). Check them often. This tedious activity is required by design. You can be in a room with a player who breaks an item right in front of you, but if you don't check the logs you won't know about it. But doing this can clue you in to who the Mush players are, and it can let you look at logs from prior to your entry to the area. Are people taking items they probably shouldn't be? Are they hiding items? Are they breaking items? Just be aware that some of these activities don't show up in the logs if there isn't a camera present.

Read the game help. The icon is a far too inconspicuous book (another book? They couldn't spring for a different icon?) in the lower right hand of the screen. This will give you an inaccurate and incomplete overview of what each character can do. The game appears to be intended by design to frustrate all but the players who enjoy the masochism of flailing about, learning how stuff works by blind trial and error.

Don't make the mistake of assuming that this is any kind of Star Trek like "five year cruise" with a bunch of hyper competent officers who actually know what they should know. Your cruise will probably be less than 10 days, and perhaps up to ~15 if you are really good and your crew mates are also really good. And the game instructions don't provide even the barest amount of information that someone with the skill of, say, Pilot ("has mad pilot skills" is really one of the pieces of 'information' provided to the players about what a Pilot can do) would actually have. And a great many of the skills have this little tidbit of valuable information: "Bonus for developing certain NERON projects." So instead of Star Fleet's hyper-competent officers, you and your crew will more resemble imbeciles flailing about, trying to learn things that a person who is a specialist on a spaceship should have known for years. Aaand, sometimes doing the very thing you're supposed to be good at is actually very damaging to the welfare of the ship. Poor Stephen the cook... You're a good cook and a good brawler. You don't know who the Mush are when the ship starts up, so you go to the Refectory (kitchen) and cook up a pile of food. There's 16 people, so you cook up 16 meals. Hey, you're being helpful, right? And then you get assassinated for doing so. Why? Because cooking a pile of food on Day 1 is a very bad thing. But don't look for any game instruction on why this is so, it does not exist. And, contrary to all logic, the ship was only provisioned for about 10 days even on starvation rations. I shit you not. So if you happen to draw Stephen as your character, be careful not to get yourself killed for simply doing your job.

I hope you find this helpful.
