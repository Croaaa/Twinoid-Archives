'What do you mean by that?' said the Captain sternly. 'Explain yourself!'

'I can't explain myself, I'm afraid, sir' said Astral, 'because I'm not myself, you see.'

'I don't see,' said the Captain.

'I'm afraid I can't put it more clearly,' Astral replied very politely, 'for I can't understand it myself to begin with; and being so many different beings in a day is very confusing.'

'It isn't,' said the Captain.

'Well, perhaps you haven't found it so yet,' said Astral; 'but when you have to turn into a "chrysalis" (you will some day, you know) and then after that into a "butterfly", I should think you'll feel it a little queer, won't you?'

'Not a bit,' said the Captain, 'but if you mean what I think you might mean I think I'll exchange the star chart for this' he stated matter of factly, holding up something that seemed to be a weapon.

'Well, perhaps your feelings may be different,' said Astral; 'all I know is, it would feel very queer to me. Is that good enough to express that I am who I say I am?'

'You!' said the Captain contemptuously. 'Who are you?'

Which brought them back again to the beginning of the conversation. Astral felt a little irritated at the Captain's making such very short remarks, and she drew herself up and said, very gravely, 'I think, you ought to tell me who you are, first.'

'Why?' said the Captain.

Here was another puzzling question; and as Astral could not think of any good reason, and as the Captain seemed to be in a very unpleasant state of mind, she turned away.
