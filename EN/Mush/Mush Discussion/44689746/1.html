I had few crappy ships lately, too. And yes, when the half or more of the ship is inactive, it's hard to win, especially, when the key characters and skills are blocked by that. Sometime it's depending on RNG, but the chance is always there. As long as people keep trying and don't surrender.

But what made those crappy ships crappy weren't the inactives. It were the people who were nominally "active" but didn't do the things they should as actives. People who started killing inactives on day 2 "in case some of them were mushes" wasting APs on that and giving huge blows to crew's morale. And then feeling like there's no chance to win, when whole crew needs to organize to just get thier morale back to the sensible level instead of taking care of sudden wave of hunters or scanning for planet with oxygen.
Guys, there's mostly no reason to kill inactives, unless rest of the crew is superactive and able to counter the negative results of that murdering easily. They will die of starvation or morale loss on day 5 or so anyway. And if not, then you have your mush. Instead, use your APs for research, planet scanning, expeditions, Neron upgrades, piloting, real mush hunt, even Pilgred. You know, things that will let you survive till that day 5. The game is not so hard, if you just want to manage that. You need only four or five really active people for that and I didn't see a ship where that would be a problem.

The saddest thing is, that people who are opting for surrender or some inactives-killing rampages are not a newbies, griefers or those silent types who just walk around and do stuff and you're not even sure if they know what they are doing. They show good understanding of game mechanics, some experiences and when I look at their profiles afterwards, these are players who have twice or thrice as many ships under their belts as me. And not alway are those some murder ships or fail ships. Some of them were fairly successful and those players were able to survive till day 15 or so several times. I'm not sure, why it's so. Maybe they were just used to be pampered by those superactive players who were able to pull whole ship almost single-handedly. And now, when most of those players are jumping randomly less, they don't see reason to even try to be more active themselves.
