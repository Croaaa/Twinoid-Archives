So I decided to do a little math behind whether NERON should be advanced, or you should wait for Paola's updates. So, starting off, I make two assumptions: first, all projects can be labeled as useful, or useless; second, all projects are either equally useful or useless.

Let's say there are 28 projects, 15 of which are useful, 13 of which are useless. The likelihood Paola turns up a useful project is thus, 15/28.

The likelihood that, once the current project is finished, NERON will turn up 0 useful projects is 13 choose 3 divided by 28 choose 3. The likelihood NERON will turn up 3 useful projects is 15 choose 3 divided by 28 choose 3. (For those not in the know, n choose p will result in the total permutations of taking p items from n, where order doesn't matter.)

Since we have 4 total possible events (NERON choosing zero, one, two, or three good projects), and 2 figured out, we only need to figure out one more, as the probabilities for all events will add up to one. I'm much less clear on the math with the last one, though. Let's say we want to probability that NERON chooses one good project. I think that the probability is (for good project g, bad project b, and total projects t) (g * (b choose 2)) / (t choose 3). Plugging in numbers, it comes up with good results, which is probably the biggest indicator for me.
