Q&A thread is the fastest way to find who's on your ship. If something's not in the wiki, someone has to ask about it. If it blows my cover, it's a fair price to pay.

Some people tend to talk about past accomplishments. It's a small community. Certain accomplishments are rare. Day 15 players can be narrowed down easily. If you've got an active NERON, it might be Wyrmie. You can also tell who's who by their stance on mush tests and so on.

High level skills indicate someone who's played the character a lot. If someone just got a lvl 3 skill on day 2, you won't find that character on their profile. If they're level 4, it's usually well after 15 days with that character. Level ups are a big investment, few people grab a lvl 4 skill on a character they've taken just once. The favorite character thread in officer's lounge narrows down both who's gold/silver and taking a high level character.

People who are good at a certain character (lvl 4+) are often a little bossy when those characters are played not so well. This isn't concrete, though, because people can be good at a character even without playing them.

Talkative, experienced players are often IRC active. Mibbit shows the countries which people are from, and failing that there's always IP tracing.

Some characters like Jin Su and Caffeine Junkie Gioele are expected to be online almost every cycle. Paola is expected to be on around cycle 4, Ian is expected to be on around cycle 8. Combined with weekday work hours you can narrow down time zones based on when people are online. On long enough ships they'll just tell you when they're working and when they're sleeping.

People also have a lot of AP to dump after a RL nap. The same techniques used to track AP wastage by mush can be used to track sleeping times.

Roleplayers often break from RP if they're checking Mush first thing in the morning. So look out for a suddenly cranky Kuan Ti, an excitable Raluca who puts up smileys, a Chao who's suddenly nice.

If someone is relaying research, they'll usually be online for a while. Check who's posting on the forums or suddenly logged on to IRC during those periods.


Things like how people write inventory threads really don't make a difference IMO. I can't remember who and how anyone writes threads. Some people might even do a thread on one ship and avoid it on the next. And it's unlikely for two people to end up on the same ship, unless they usually get on in that period where ships fill slowly.
