You know, I just realized that, statistically speaking, Eleesha's List is more indicative of the people less likely to be Mush than of those who are, if printed early. Not that it's very convenient since a Mush on a knife is worth two on the list, but still.

Consider this situation: There are two kids in a playground. You know for sure that one of them is a boy. What's the chance that the other is a boy as well?

The answer is not one in two, as one could rush to answer, but one in three. Because, for a couple of kids, all possible pairings of genders are:

Boy-boy
Boy-Girl
Girl-Boy
Girl-Girl.

Four different scenarios, all of which have an equal probability. Now, the only information you have is that one of them is a Boy, but you don't know which one. So, you end up with one of three different scenarios, since one of them being a boy excludes the scenario when they're both girls. And out of these three scenarios, there is only one where, with one of the kids being a boy, the other is a boy as well, leading to a probability of one in three.

Now, let's consider the List. Let's say that we have a noob Eleesha who prints the list as soon as Much are chosen, and it has 8 people on it. We know that one of the Alpha Mush is on that list, but that's all the info we have, since it could contain both. But that's the point: there are 15 potential Mush on board. We know that one Mush on the list is an Alpha Mush, but what about the other seven?

Let's see the chance of the list containing both Alpha Mush: since it contains one Mush for sure, the chance of another Mush being in the list is 7/14 (1/2) if Chun is not on the list, and 6/14 (3/7) if she is.

BUT! There's the deal. If Chun is on the list, there is a bigger chance of the other Mush NOT being on the list than of it being there. Thus, paradoxically, Chun's being on the list (and thus narrowing the field of research) actually makes each individual on the list LESS likely of being an Alpha Mush.

Let's do some more calculations:
In the case that there is only one Mush in the list (4/7), everyone on the list has a chance in 7 of being the Mush, for a total of 4/49 chances. Let's add to that the chance of being Mush when two Alphas are on the list (3/7), which are 6/49. Sum them up, and the average chance for each crewmate in a list that includes Chun to be an Alpha Mush is 10/49, slightly more than 20%.

Now, what if Chun isn't on the list? The chance of each listed member to be a Mush is 1/8*1/2+2/8*1/2=1/16+2/16=3/16, or 18,75%. So, really, Chun's presence on the list or lack thereof doesn't make much difference, but it does, slightly, make the list less accurate.

...where was I going with that? Maybe nowhere, but I really like these kinds of probability calculations.
