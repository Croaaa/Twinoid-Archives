GOALS OF THIS EVENT: MMM is awesome for current players. However, I would like to find something to increase the odds of new players getting great ships, too! Thus, came the idea of short community events, where the goals are to:

1. Attract experienced players with fun "random" ships.
2. Inject as many experienced players as possible into fun "random" ships.
3. Help new players experience Mush nirvana.


GOALS OF THIS THREAD: For this event to be a success, it would be prudent to have a healthy debate on the possible ideas that can be implemented. Eventually, out of these discussions, I would like to cement the following:

1. A variety of doable and fun themes for this event.
2. A definitve set of rules/guidelines/parameters for each theme.
3. An idea of when these events will be run, how often, and how it might affect MMMs.


POSSIBLE THEMES: A short description of the themes. Whatever the themes are, they should aim for a short-ish ship, if they can get unanimous agreement from the crew:

1. Standard:
. Humans: Must find and kill all mush and return to Sol as soon as possible, with as many humans alive as possible.
. Mush: Must stop as many humans as possible from returning to Sol alive. Bonus points if you trick the humans into killing each other (instead of killing them directly), or converting them!

2. RP-Lovers:
. Everyone: Have fun with your RP-ing!
. Humans: Must find and kill all mush and return to Sol as soon as possible, with as many humans alive as possible.
. Mush: Must stop as many humans as possible from returning to Sol alive. Bonus points if you trick the humans into killing each other (instead of killing them directly), or converting them!

3. Starmap Dream:
. Humans: Must find and kill all mush and return to Sol as soon as possible, with a Starmap piece & Super Calculator researched. Use the Armor and Ian's Pacifist trait wisely...
. Mush: Must stop or slow the humans from returning to Sol with a Starmap piece & Super Calculator researched. (Intentionally suiciding in the Icarus forfeits your efforts)

4. Sol's Mush Expert:
. Humans: Must find and kill all mush and return to Sol as soon as possible, with all Mush-related (i.e. glory-giving) research finished. Use the Armor and Ian's Pacifist trait wisely...
. Mush: Must stop or slow the humans from returning to Sol with mush-related research.

5. Cat and Mouse:
. Humans: Must find and kill all mush and return to Sol as soon as possible, with as many humans, dead or alive, as possible. For extra challenge, bonus points if you can convince your crew to not research Constispore and Antispore Gas.
. Mush: Convert as many humans as possible. Bonus points if for each convertee you brought back to Sol alive.


BASELINE RULES: Every theme will have to adhere to the following:

1. SDF rules cannot be broken during the course of the event, full stop.

2. At the start of every ship participating in the event, a chat thread explaining the event must be posted. A premade text will be provided by the organisers.

3. If you need to return to Sol early, or do something that is otherwise breaking the rule, convince your crew and get a unanimous decision. It's part of the gameplay, so if you fail to convince everyone, roll with it.

4. Be friendly and instructive to new players. The most important thing is that everyone must have fun!

5. Things like Mush Tests and underhanded Pasiphae tactics aren't very fun. Those are highly discouraged on these events.

6. Some of these themes require you to kill someone early. If you have to choose from a group of people to kill, always kill the new player last.

I'll preface this with: I enjoy MMM a lot. In fact, I'm in one now, and I'm still enjoying it. However, there are lots of valid arguments against doing them too often considering these overachieving ships go for D20 achievements.

In this thread, I would like us all to come up with cool, short and fun jump ideas, and then we see if any of them could be taken further. We'll always have to work within what SDF is willing to allow, but I'm hoping we'll be able to work something out with them if there's a really good idea too good to pass up!

2. Starmap jump, where the goal for the human is to bring Starmap piece + Supercalculator research back to sol as early as possible

3. Speed runs, where the goal for the humans is to bring as many mush-related researches back to Sol as early as possible.
