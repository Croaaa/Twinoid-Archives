I think everyone forgets a major option one has for totally safe RP in this game, and a major boundary I think we should all observe in our RP.

We have only so many AP, moves, items, charges, and whatever else have you. Only so many - in a game where the mechanics are extremely tight and there are already coded in traitors, where the environment's set to progressively make survival harder, and in a game where "playing against your team" is breaking the rules.

Any waste, any use of resources - even your own movement points, for a purpose that you know is not helping your team win in some way - I say the game's so challenging that purposeful waste is absolutely against the rules.

It seems to me that that people are generally falling into two schools of thought. Those that believe at the core this is a team 'versus' game (where RP is optional colouring) and those that believe at the core this is a role-playing game where the meta-game is largely irrelevant and only sets the stage for some fun role-play.

I'm not sure anyone can say either approach is invalid, only that the two camps don't play very well together

I think the two camps could meet perfectly well (and I intend to be in this 'meeting camp') - if the roleplayers restrict their RP actions to that which actually only does good, not bad, for the ship, to the best of their knowledge.

Play a personality that's skilled, disciplined, determined, and going to do what needs to be done to have a chance to achieve humanity's last hope.

Be that desperate mother out there working 3 jobs so her baby doesn't starve, because that baby is worth all the pain and the heartbreak and if you fail your child you've truly failed in the one thing that matters - you'd *die* before you did that no matter how hard it is and how tired you get.

Be that father working through cancer to provide every cent of money to his family he can, because anything less is cowardice and there's no one else that's going to provide for your family, the one thing that measures the true worth of any man.

Be that child begging on the streetcorner and bringing home anything she gets to her parents that she loves so much, because she loves her parents and has no other way to help them.

Of course I don't mean -be- those roles.... but be the Mush equivalent of those personalities and similar. Be determined people who fight their weaknesses to serve and succeed, who would die before they let their team down because that team is the most important thing in life.

So you were kidnapped onto this ship? Assume you saw enough news to know that the alternative would already have you dead or completely destroyed, your body nothing but a host for Mush now. Your only chance of saving what your character loves best is to work with these people, get them to work, get the cure, and get back. Your ship gets a shot at Eden? Then roleplay the anguish and the promise that we're going back to Earth soon with that cure. After all, if the ship can get to Eden, it can get back from Eden, and then it can go to Sol. It's just one more expo, the best one yet for sure. That's nothing to fight over, for a roleplayer the game doesn't 'end' - the stuff after the end just happens off camera.

The roleplayers who want to play as humanity's last hope and failing? Stop it. Play as humans, fighting your weaknesses to -win-, not using them as an excuse to lose.

That's where the two groups meet. That's where it all comes together. That's what I want to see, and do myself.
