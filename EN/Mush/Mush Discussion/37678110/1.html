To my knowledge, there is only one way to be 100% sure to be on the same ship:

1) Both join the same casting when they're introduced
2) Level up the casting to the point where it can launch with only 16 people (they all end up on the same ship). The best way to level up is to launch ships. I believe someone calculated that with 8 ships launched per month, every month, it would take 2 years to reach that level.
3) Have only 16 people set themselves as ready for the trip, an you and your friend are 2 of the 16. Easier said than done, if just one more person sets themselves are ready, one or the other of you might not launch.

If you just join a casting and launch with 32 people, then your chances of being together are 50%.

The other tips people have mentioned can help, but if you end up on different ships, do not grieve or suicide. This is detrimental to other players and can get you quarantined.

NERON

Getting two people on the same ship is simple, just time-consuming and tedious. There are two methods:

Method one Go to the character selection screen and keep refreshing the page. The characters are occasionally reshuffled every... 10-15[?] minutes, eventually you'll get the same list of people on the screen. Once that happens, select a different character for each player.

This can take up a few hours but I've used this method a bunch of times and it's only 'failed' me when one of the parties lost patience and/or needed to go IRL.

Method two Method two takes less time but fails to work occasionally. Have person A pick a character from a list of four. This guarantees your ship will have at least three empty slots for player B to choose from. Player A stays in the lab and gives player B updates on the roster. If a character on the ship is awake from player B's choices, you will not be on the same ship. Keep on refreshing like above.

The fuller the ship, the more likely you will end up on the same one but keep in mind, the ship can fill up without your lists ever syncing.
