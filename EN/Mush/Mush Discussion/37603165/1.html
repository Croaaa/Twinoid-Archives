The basic guidelines about reporting work perfectly here, as they do in almost every circumstance including the question 'should I report this?': If you feel that something should be reported, report it. Make sure you include important details in the report (or at least that the message you issue the report on contains those details so judges know where to start).

If the reported behavior is sanctionable, problem solved. If the problem behavior was not sanctionable, but it's easy to understand why you would feel it was questionable, nothing will happen; problem solved. The only way you will have a problem from reporting something you feel is suspicious is if the judges cannot even understand why you'd think it was rule breaking - if that happens you will probably be issued a warning about your report.

That is -perfect-, because that allows you to ask for help in understanding what was wrong with the report you made. A mod who is able to know all the pertinent details will have a nice chat with you about why what you found suspicious for rule breaking actually is in the rules and is an acceptable playstyle choice, and you'll come out of the experience utterly unharmed, but far better educated.

I think I've issued around 30 or more reports to date. I've been warned about a couple of them and came away from those warnings exactly as described. Hasn't done anything to my willingness to keep reporting as appears needed (my last ship, I had reason to report 4 of my shipmates ), but I completely understand what I should never report again.
