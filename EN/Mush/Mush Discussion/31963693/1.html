My argument has two parts. First off, I want to look at the supposed rule this action would be violating.

The rule is that you aren't allowed to play against your team.

The word team implies that there is an opponent, as the definition of team is a group of players that make up one side of a competitive game or sport. But at that point, everyone is on the same side. There are no teams. Or, at the very least, teams have not been selected. Yet.

Now at this point the argument I've heard most is "But you can be turned mush at any time, so you should never be allowed to play as though you're going to become Mush simply because it's POSSIBLE that you'll become Mush."

This brings me to part two: the method by which one becomes Mush matters.

The argument I'm seeing most is that preemptively playing as though you're going to be turned Mush is the same as surrender. I would argue this would be true but only if being turned Mush is a lose condition.

Now, after the mush are selected, there's no argument. lf you get infected after Mush are selected it's because they trapped you. They outplayed you. They managed to find you and spore you 3 times. However, for the two mush selected after the ship is full this isn't the case. No matter how well they play as a human, the RNG can still select them to be mush. They can dump all of their AP into research, they can move all the items in the ship to their optimal positions, they can shoot down all the hunters they want, and it won't make them any less likely to become Mush. A game in which you can play the best you possibly can and still end up "Losing" because of nothing but luck is a very poorly created game.
