wydamn

I'm an occasional goonstation/LLJK player (which is not dominated by SA goons as much as it used to be, and is pretty pubbie-friendly). There is a lot more physics focus (I wouldn't call it realistic), and mush is much more strategy focused, like more traditionaly strategy games.

In many ways, Mush is more like what Space Station 13 should have been, with much more focus on trust dynamics and the resulting paranoia and team building that comes from that. In SS13, you might get your day ruined by a traitor, but the traitor might not care to disturb your explorations of the chemistry system or device building or other recreational gameplay (ie: just playing with the game's systems rather than playing the game). SS13 has lots of different distractions and side-games, for example the goonstation servers have different teleporter locations such as the Ice Moon or the Solarium which requires a prepared and coordinated away team to explore and discover its secrets. While that's all pretty great, it kind of dilutes the traitor's role. In many ways, Mush is a much more pure game experience.

But you know, different strokes for different folks and all.

I dunno, it seems like the lounge has turned into exclusively the AP section of forums, heh.

I first came to Mush because I heard it was similar to SS13. There are definitely some things in common, but they are very different beasts.

Also keep in mind SS13 has been in development for a looong time. As far as depth goes, SS13 wins hands down. Certain jobs (Chemistry, Genetics, Electrician among many others) all require some decent understanding of math and logic systems, or you can just be an assistant/security and bash peoples heads in with toolboxes I recommend goon servers for anyone who's interested. RP is not required, and if you're a traitor/antagonist, pretty much anything goes. (there is even a game mode where the goal is to detonate a nuclear device and destroy everyone)

Mush is more like a long distance board game with random people, and more of a focused experience. Different flavors of similar theme (treachery and team work, in spaace)

I think anyone who enjoys the treachery mechanics of these games, should try out another game called Salem (can be played in web browser and is free). A very fun new lying game set during the witch burning times.

JD_Ender

Salem is a free to play browser game with no affiliation to any TV series or anything check it out its pretty fun. Antagonists include Witches, a team of Mafia, Arsons, and Serial Killers (depending on the type of game you're playing, custom or classic). There are also Neutral type characters, most notably a jester role, where your goal is to convince the town you're guilty and get hanged, taking horrible revenge from beyond the grave.

Every day the townie players talk and discuss clues, trying to find these antagonists and hang them (through a democratic voting process). At night the antagonists are free to conduct murders/framings/witchy business etc. while the good guys conduct investigations, heal eachother, spy etc.

It's basically a race to find the baddies before they locate and murder off the good townies, games typically last around 15-30 minutes.
