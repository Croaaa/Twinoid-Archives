Example...

...that kind of thing.

Ok, lets get some proposals going.

- - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - -
Records are held by the first player to claim them unless all players are referring to the same ship, if this is the case then the record is shared.

1. Mush Records
1.1 Survival.
1.1.1 Longest living mush.
- The player who has spent the most cycles as mush aboard a single ship.

1.1.2 Longest surviving mush.
- The player who has spent the most cycles as mush aboard a single ship AND the game ended with successful infiltration.

1.1.3 Longest surviving alpha.
- The player who has spent the most cycles as an alpha mush aboard a single ship AND the game ended with successful infiltration.
- - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - -

I would not be surprised to see that 2 or all 3 of these records would be handed by the same player who would be referring back to the same game but I still think there is value in having all 3.

- - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - -
1.2 Conversions.
1.2.1 The most Conversions.
- The most conversions aboard a single ship where you played as an alpha mush (title maybe shared by alphas).

1.2.2 Destroyed by conversion.
- The most living mush players aboard a single ship when the game ended where you played as an alpha mush AND the game ended because there were no humans aboard (title maybe shared by alphas).

1.2.3 Destroyed by conversion in haste.
- The highest number as a result of...
"(Number of living mush when the game ended) / (the number of cycles in the game)"
The game must ended because there were no humans aboard.
This title maybe shared by all mush aboard when the game ended.
