Oh please... I have read those complaints since the beta tests. Crewmembers don't talk, half the ship is braindead, players do stupid things, crews are inactive... People have been saying that the game is dying pretty much since it opened. In the so-called "good old days" you are regretting, we were already reading complaints about ships being destroyed too early, being doomed from the start, not having a chance at D10.

Yes, there are less players. That is inevitable. You can't force people to play the same game over and over for more than six years, it's only natural to get tired of it after a while. A couple of new skills or new zones on expos won't change that. Even a whole new character wouldn't change that. People play a game for a while and then most of them move on, that's how things work.

The game won't be changed anyway. The main developed left MT a few years ago and there will be no more updates. We have already asked if it was possible to reduce the number of ships, and it can't be done.

So what can we do? Be positive. It has always been true, and it has always been one of the main problems of the game: people complaining, expecting "perfect" ships with 16 active members, considering that a ship is doomed if it has no shrink or no Pasiphae or a few inactives or whatnot. If you look at your ships negatively, you can't expect them to be good. If you are optimistic, you have a good chance of having a good ship. And most of all, you have to really try. It's as simple as that. Try to motivate the crew, give them directions, do a bit of RP, and above all don't give up before the game has even started. I have almost never had a ship fail as a human, no matter how many inactives or bronze or silver crewmembers, no matter what obstacles we encountered (pyromaniac mush, no techs, no showers, no morale sources, no pilots, 11 inactives out of 16...). But I have seen plenty of ships, in the forums or in reports, fail because the crew had given up.

Yes, it is still possible to have good ships, even great ones. I have just finished a D15 ship with an active crew that got along great. There were old players who had never stopped the game, old players who were returning, newish crewmembers willing to learn, some gold, some bronze or silver. We didn't get Eden because we weren't lucky, but we had a lot of fun.

All you need is to lower your expectations (there has no such thing as a perfect ship), not look at the negative things (why depress yourself looking at numbers?), and just try to play as best you can.
