The actual rule is that no ship item may be held before mush selection. Ship items are defined by items you did not personally enter play already carrying, and were not given during the tutorial. Here is summary about Neutral play, enjoy.

Short version:
- You can install cameras before mush selection
- You don't have to install them if you don't want, if somebody like Chun is saying stuff like - Stephen you are generalist, you should install cameras before selection - you can ignore her if you like
- Gioele is not forced to take Paranoid or install cameras if he took it

Long version:

Neutral play.

1. What's neutral play?
There are some special rules, that are applied to everyone before mush selection. After the last 16th player joins the ship and you see yellow mush icons to appear - neutral play is over.

2. Do we just play as humans before mush selection?
No, it's not like that. As human you should do everything to help your human team to win. Before mush selection you don't. For example - as Pilot you can ignore hunters, as Paranoid Gioele you can wait with installing cameras. While nothing stops you from doing such actions, you cannot be forced by anyone to do it.

3. What items can you move before mush selection?
You can move any items, you want, just be sure you dropped them after picking up. About holding items for long see next question:

4. What items can you hold before mush selection?
You cannot hold any items that are not "yours" - like weapons, apron, hacker's kit, armor.
Personal items:
- Talkie, Tracker, iTrakie,
- Items that you got from your skills: Drone BP (Robotics expert), Extra pots (Green Trumb), Extra cameras (Paranoid)
- Any stuff you have bought from vending machine
Also it's ok to take a ration of a cup of coffee for yourself, but holding pills (like +3ap +3morale pills especially) is not right.
While by lore our dear cat belongs to Raluca, he don't not counts as personal item for her.

5. Is Chun allowed to hold these items before selection, she will be human anyway...
No, she is not allowed. The purpose of neutral play is to give both teams a fair start, not to make sure mushes don't get some stuff.

6. What other actions are not allowed before mush selection?
You cannot make actions, that will clearly hurt human team, or just annoy everyone before you became a mush. There are some examples, but use common sence:
- Eating bananas/alien fruits, when humans want to plant them is wrong
- Dismantling objects, nobody wants to be dismantled is wrong (like second shower or tabulatrix)
- Guarding corridors to make players suffer is wrong
- Throw people from the room without any reason is wrong

7. What actions are allowed then?
You can do any ship maintenance, that game mechanics allow you.
- You can work on projects
- You can scan planets
- You can work on research, that is not against mush (examples: NCC lenses, Anabolics)
- You can kill hunters and collect the loot
- You can build any items from BPs
- If you think it's a good idea - you can even hack commanders terminal or NERON bios to use them before selection
Also you can do a few things, that clearly hurts mush team.
- You can dismantle one of showers
- You can install cameras

8. But you said nobody can force you to do these...
Yes, that's right - all actions above are just allowed. If you don't want to do anything before selection - don't do that. You can stay in lab doing nothing, to be ready to spend all your ap/mp/free points for your team as soon as you'll see who you are.

9. People say I should take a shower, if I got dirty before selection.
Again - it's up to you. While it's not right to just become dirty with playing with fuel to have advantage as mush, if you got dirty while doing stuff like dismanteling or installing cameras - you can wait for selection before taking shower.

10. People say I should select my skills or/and use them (like go to dorm as Shrink)
And again - it's only up to you. Not selecting skills can be good idea for most humans as well btw. Hua/KT/Eleesha can suddenly take Tech after mushes rampaged to kill all teches, or JS can select Pilot between Leader after having a full picture.

12. What should I do, if somebody is breaking neutral play?
If he just don't know about such rules or say they are different - tell him about it and give him link to this post. If he is breaking on purpose - you can report him. If it's clear, that he wants to use weapons he got to make some kills just after selection or wants to steal them with Pasi - you can kill him just before selection.

You know, the reason the Mush Sample doesn't spawn until the moment of Mush Selection is explicitly because people would find it, rush CS and ASG, and both would be done long before the ship even filled. If you think the Mush are severely disadvantaged now...

Each of the bullet points you just listed are a natural part of the game, and the devs aren't likely to do anything to change them. You play with the cards you're dealt. If the ship fills slowly, and most of the potential pilots are only bronze without Pilot, you might be the last to wake up and see the hull under 50 or worse. So what? You just deal with it as best you can. I've had ships like that, and we still killed our Mush and made it back to Sol. Other times, the Mush were able to turn the screws against us, and we lost.

FYI, one of the reasons the devs aren't going to "do anything" about this dynamic is simple: You're never going to have a perfect ship, for either team. There's far too much randomness (including the quality of the players, and their level of knowledge/opportunity to take advantage of mistakes) for that. Yeah, every ship starts with exactly four hunters, guaranteed. It used to be even more random...at the start, you could have six or eight hunters from the ship's first cycle, or none at all. Even then, more could show up within the next few cycles, and it was even worse to not have any pilots. So they chose to start with just four hunters, and let the rest show up depending on time and AP spent.

Neutral Play rules exist to give both sides as fair a start as possible. Yes, this means the humans can even finish the Mycoscan before the ship fills (as the only anti-Mush research you can possibly conduct during Neutral Play - it'll be even more important next season.) It also means the humans can place cameras in the usual spots, because as has already been stated, the Mush can gain a significant advantage if the dorms are unprotected at the moment of Mush selection.

As I already stated, we're NOT changing the Neutral Play rules, no matter your objections to them. If the Mush are winning more than average on your ships, then use that experience to become a better hunter. If you're consistently losing as Mush, that's not the fault of the rules. Either way, learn from their mistakes, as much as your own. The win rate for both teams is close to 50/50, with a slight advantage in favor of the humans...so if you're consistently losing on either team, you're an outlier.
