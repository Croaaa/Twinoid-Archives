I've recently noticed that almost all games end up with a mush rampage, and it happens more and more early in the game. Now they rampage almost right after getting out of the cryo. But why? To be honest, I think I can understand that. People don't like to play as mush because it's:

1) Hard. You're outnumbered and it's almost impossible to destroy the ship against a good and active crew. Not the main reason in my opinion.

2) Absolutely not rewarding. Now, that's a thing. While humans getting their glory point every cycle just for that, the mush is inevitably losing it, and twice more than humans, where's a logic in that??? So the game says: Hey if you want points, do something and quickly!

But what are the options?

a) Contaminate. Alright, but how many infections can you make per game, really? 1-4 at best, maybe? With the first research being done quickly, it's getting almost impossible to produce and carry spores. Yeah, and how many points do we get for spiking? One. Wow, that'll sure compensate half of your initial points lost while waiting for a chance to spike someone.

b) Convert human. Same as above. While waiting a chance to convert someone you're already near to 0 points. The reward for this action - 8 glory. Yes, we're bathing in points here.

c) Sneak to Sol or Eden. Welp, we're getting significantly more here, but c'mon... It's the end of the game! At this point, we are at 0 points! Besides, it's almost impossible to do. And that pity 16-32 points we're getting for this are nothing!

d) Humanocide. Now, here at least something. We're getting 3 points for every slain human. With the right character, we can go on a killing spree. Add here initial 120 points, no wonder it's the most preferred option.


Also, put aside all that points stuff, being mush is absolutely not rewarding from a social perspective. What do you see in chat while looking at the last words window or in the end game results when mush? The words of approval for the tactics you used? Or maybe gratitudes for making an investigation quite exciting and challenging? Nope. Never you see anything other than "Nicely done!" said to your murderer or any other words of approval said to the human team. At best you can receive something like pity for impossibility to cure you if you were socially active. That's. It.

Nobody cares that behind the mush is an actual player. The player that is forced to play the main role in this show, actually! The name of the game is "The Mush", after all. But nobody cares one bit. This is understandable, too. But c'mon! Can't we be more sympathetic to those players? It's like virtual racism because of this totally wasted mush-rewarding system, that simply doesn't exist.
Those mush players which are trying to play low, undetected, trying to multiply, plotting, and making this game exciting for humans doing it on sheer enthusiasm, nothing more. The game isn't encouraging to play as mush at all! That explains why it almost always ends up in a rampage. We ourselves are forcing the mush to rampage because it's easier to start a new game as a human rather than trying hard to make the game fun for others. What for?

Remember how boring the game gets after all mush were dealt with? Yep. And now remember how stressful to play as mush. So why should the mush play any differently when there's an option to end this unrewarding nightmare?

In fact, I think this is literally every online mafia-based games' problem. Being a bad guy isn't rewarding. So we have to endure it and wait for when it's over to try once more in order to actually enjoy the game. Isn't this stupid? Why this can't be solved? Even when it's easy to do. Just let the players receive more coins, points, XP, or any other stuff for playing a bad guy, and that's it. But no. Let's make it miserable and humiliating for these players because they're playing a bad role. Why? Because of being bad is bad, kids? I have no idea.

For me, being Mush is less frustrating than being human. I can split my human gameplay into active and passive regarding Mush hunting.

Playing actively, I put top priority into hindering discreet conversions. I make myself aware of every potential sinister activity going on in the ship. My actions and observations of serious suspicion are not revealed in public. Along with this, I'm aware I attract a lot of suspicion as a guy who hides objects, walks with medikit, frequently visits the shower dorm, etc. I share my informations with very few people I trust but I'm afraid the remaining crew might act vigilante and kill me meanwhile.

Playing passively, I just do my job depending on the character I am, trying to minimize any suspicious activity. I don't visit any side rooms and try to avoid visiting Bravo Dorm for no reason. I gather information only from logs and chat I read while doing my job. I'm afraid of limited knowledge as I'm not sure if suspicious activity is monitored. I'm afraid of Mush ability to kill or fully convert a human and this human might be me. Even when we are supposedly Mush free, I'm not certain if Anonymush is actually stealing our victory.

As human. I don't follow certain part of metagaming which I consider suboptimal. I usually end up disputing with other people which might question my reliability. As Mush, I try to be reliable whenever possible, acting in a way the majority would like me to see. I try to be fully aware when I act suspicious achieving Mush goal. I'm not afraid of dying because I can't be surprisingly backstabbed.

Achieving human goals is strongly dependent on the team you have and your decisions are also dependent on them. As Mush, you have much more freedom to what you want to do. You just make a thought and there we go: discreetly converting half of the crew. Maybe non-discreetly, such as watching how the crew bar grows yellow over time. Going to Sol on a whim - only possible for a Mush. Because crew annoys you? Because you want to ruin their dreams of the ship with Plasma Shield, jumpkins and numerous gold projects bought? You can kill certain human that really disgusted you beforehand. This is what you can't do as human because of the remaining crew and SDF rules.

So summarizing, being Mush is less stressful because you can't be killed undeserved and you are allowed to do more things than as a human.
