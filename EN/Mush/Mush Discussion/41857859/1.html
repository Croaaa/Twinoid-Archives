Reports ARE useful. They do help us to weed out griefers, especially the serious ones. The fact that you don't see as many griefers as there could be is proof of that. The SDF takes griefing seriously, more than the general playerbase may even realize. We're all unpaid volunteers, from the judges to the mods, and we're players recruited from within the community, just like you are.

However: If you don't think reporting helps, then you are part of the problem, honestly. If you see a crime in progress and nobody cares to report it to the authorities, the perpetrator can get away without investigation, much less punishment. The same goes for Mush. We don't investigate ship logs unless a report is filed. More to the point, we can't, because the logs aren't generated for us unless a report is filed. If you want to help us stop the griefers, then file reports when you see rules being broken; that lets us in the SDF do the jobs we volunteered for.

As for inactivity, we've had MANY threads like this, with suggestions not too dissimilar from your own. However, these threads tend to turn into little more than complaints. While some of the points made also tend to be valid, many of the same suggestions get repeated time and again, and MT has been very clear that the measures they take are the measures we work within.

Inactive players are a fact of the game. It doesn't mean that they are wanted by anyone, but this is a free to play game, and most of the newbies who don't like their initial impression of the game won't tend to come back to the game. That's just the way it is, and you are supposed to work around them; that is one of the reasons you have three characters who can push them into a storage and trade them. You can make it back to Sol with only half the crew being active. It's even possible to make it with fewer, though it's far more challenging to do so. Newbies will give up on the game, for a myriad of reasons: The AP/MP system, the chat, the paranoia of the game, the ease of getting killed for ignorant mistakes, or what have you...but some players need time to learn to love these things, and some of them have eventually become the community's most experienced players.

One of the reasons we don't sanction for inactivity is for this very reason: If we sanction the people who give up on the game on their very first day, it's not going to give them the slightest incentive to try it again. On the other hand, there are MANY players who go inactive on their first ship, give the game another try, and then actually stick around. Alienating these players would only serve to weaken the playerbase in the long term.

I'll use myself as an example. I went inactive on my very first ship: Ship #52 on this server. From my second ship onward, I kept playing, kept active, joined the SDF as a judge, and later became a mod. If I'd been sanctioned for that first ship, I would have stopped caring altogether, and the 70+ ships I've played since beta would not have happened for me. I've been to Eden three times, and each one of them was a random ship for me. I generally don't join community jumps. You can see my ship history; I've dealt with many inactives on my ships. Is it frustrating to have half the ship go inactive? Hell yes, as a player, it can be almost as frustrating as those who actively grief us. But many of the successful ships I've had were such great successes, because we achieved a victory while the odds were so stacked against us.

MT is unlikely to use the suggestions you've made, for reasons that only they know. They've stated before that they won't rehash solutions they've tried before, when they've determined that said solutions were ineffective. They want the players to treat inactives as obstacles to work around, and persevere where others would give up. Also consider that Mush players have the most difficult circumstances when they face a human crew that is hyperactive, hence the overwhelming rates of Mush losses on community jump ships. (On the other hand, there have been a few notable Mush victories, as well!)
