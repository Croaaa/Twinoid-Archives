So, with the many ships I've now been on, how people treat the Comms tasks (besides the newer players that aren't sure) still seems to vary. Some players start spamming out Xyloph for example as soon as communications are up.

As far as my Logic Goes:

1) Establish first Link as soon as 16th crew is awake
2) Immediately complete Neron V2.0
3) See if you are able to complete Neron V3.0
- Go to Bed, use D2 Rebel base message to determine what cycles current ship will receive rebel bases -
4) Ensure you have enough AP to do D3 base, use all Excess on Neron V3.0 (or 4.0 if you were lucky).
5) Prioritise all Rebel bases except D2.
6) Once Neron is at V4.0/V5.0 (depending on each ships status), finally start doing Xyloph DB entries

My reasoning for leaving Xyloph till last is:

- Blueprints can easily be destroyed in fires, getting them later when you are more likely to have the materials to build them is better
- Skill Books can be destroyed in fires, or also learned by a malicious mush directly before a rampage, loosing a member of your crew and a book that might turn the ship around is extremely bad
- Loosing Signal and Strength whilst still needin to complete rebel bases is risky, if the signal is down and the commander doesn't realise the new Signal is available (as no NERON notification was received), they might move the ship and loose the base
- Assuming your first 3 Neron Projects don't include Corridors, GPU or Plasma Shield, you have roughly a 1/10 chance of getting an exceptionally useful Neron Project (and everyone loves D1 Plasma Shield)
- If you get an awful Neron project like Convenient Sink or Spatial Wave, there's tons of traders around on D1-5 that might give you the oppertunity to trade it for something else, without the risk of loosing one of your 6 great projects instead

Overall, I don't personally understand why anyone ever tries to rush getting the Xyloph DB's, with the rare exception of the AFK-not-in-lab Chun preventing research.

People overhype that 25% neron participation xyloph. Generally I recommend if you start as a D1 Paola you should do one Neron upgrade, and then start cracking xyloph. With a bit of luck you should hit your first disconnect as you run out (or close to) of AP. That way, as you sleep, the rest of the crew will try to reconnect the signal. This is the best all around strategy.

If your Neron projects team is very fast at rushing projects, then you might want to put a bit more priority on upgrades. If your ship is having problems of any kind (no pilots/astrophysicists, a mush in the pasiphae, a griefer chun, inactive eleesha, etc) then I would recommend ignoring Neron and just banging out xyloph projects. It really depends on the situation and there should by no means be one set optimal strategy that is used every time.

That said, if you are worried about it taking too long for your crew to reconnect after a xyloph break, then your ship likely has bigger problems than your AP usage being unoptimal.

I'm in full agreement with JerkSteve. Comms officer job is highly situational. You can NOT make a step by step guide. That 25% NERON boost is not reason enough not to touch XYLOPH DB. It all depends on the strengths and weaknesses of the active/inactive crew to make a fair judgement. It also depends on the skills, level and Bronze/Silver/Gold status of your "Paola" avatar. There is nothing wrong with getting all 6 bases and all xyloph unlocked if you so desire. People that tell you otherwise, simply do not belong on the bridge.

Things to keep in mind are:

Always keep 4 AP before going to bed. Worst case scenario after you wake up, 6ap are enough to decode fully a Rebel Base with Xyloph Bonus or Rebel skill.

Note the exact cycle the Day 2 Rebel Base contacts your ship. All the rest will do so on that exact cycle each following day.

You can work both NERON and Rebel Bases, or NERON and XYLOPH DB, but always start with NERON. That way if you disconnect, you are not at a loss.

If the chance of hitting a disconnect are bigger, ie. few entries left 2 of which are disconnects, try to do those around C7/C8 and get crew up to Bridge on stand by for re-connection. If you do it on C8, you can try immediately another attempt on day change.
