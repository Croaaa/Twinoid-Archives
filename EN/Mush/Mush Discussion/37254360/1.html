Until castings happen, the only way you can try to get on a ship with your friends is to join at roughly the same time and hope you get the same ship.

If one of you joins, you can check the crew roster on the cryo thingy, then compare it with the choices your friend has. Overload, I think you might want to look into the aspect of playing on your school's network - especially if other people are playing mush games as well on the same network. Playing from two different computers is not a problem, I actually play from a work computer and my home computer, so logging into two different IPs on one account should be fine. What might be an issue ( and something im curious about now that it's brought up) is if another person logs into a separate twinoid account from the same network (say your school) and plays on the same ship with the same IP. That could possibly cause issues, but it would need some official clarification.

I know motion twin is very good at locking down people playing on the same ship from one IP, I've seen it often where they will have a special status that essentially forces them to be inactive if it picks up they are running multiple accounts on one ship and I assume it's based on the IP address of the user.

In that case, the issue will be both of you playing from school. The game could (possibly, I don't know and am making an assumption here) pick up two separate accounts on the same IP address accessing the same ship. That would likely get you flagged as a multi-teamster.
