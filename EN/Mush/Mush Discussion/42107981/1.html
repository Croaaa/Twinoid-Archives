I'm not an expert on how well ads help out F2P games, but MT isn't in a position where it would help out that much. Free games on phones can make a good amount of money on ads because it's on a phone, so people can't block ads that easily (or alternatively, the people who are casually playing the game wouldn't have the knowledge that one could do that). The lack of blocked ads plus the large amount of people trying the game equals a good ROI

Over here in desktop computer land, it seems that essentially 100% of the people who know that ads can be blocked will block them (I'm not one of them, though). MT also has an issue with not having a large-enough audience. So the end result of ads would be a negative ROI, as I can only imagine two scenarios of how adding ads would play out

First, they go the simple route, and just toss in some Google ads. This wouldn't help MT that much, since publishers can't ask for clicks, and Google heavily favors advertisers to use CPC bids, rather than the old CPM (aka impression-based) system. Unless you're willing to randomly click on interesting ads (not just any and every ad, since then your clicks might be considered invalid and end up not being counted), then MT has very little to gain from Google ads

The second (and less likely) route is to go whole hog into ads and actually try to attract sponsors. You would then end up with skinned pages and potentially some promo consumable/collectable items in the game itself. This would be a real waste of time and money, as MT would need someone on board who can wrangle deals like this (or outsource it somehow?). More money would come in this way, but there's a huge potential for failure. A company could do a test run and end up not paying for a sponsorship ever again due to not getting the results they wanted. This would then leave MT in a worse spot than before, as they would have invested time into something that can't help strengthen their long tail


MT's style of doing business would not gel well with an ad-focused mentality, I feel. The way they price things shows that they are not seeking a high conversion rate with their games, they just want to have a decently-sized audience that affords enough paying players to keep things sustainable. Throwing in ads would do nothing, unless they make serious investments in something that won't make their games any better
