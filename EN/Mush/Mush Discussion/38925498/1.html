I suspect this has been discussed before, but I'm only a couple months old on the game, so I haven't seen it, and wanted to air my concerns.

I'm so thrilled for the idea of castings. I've met some cool players in my time here whom I'd love to play with regularly, and I am indeed tired of getting ships full of people who don't even try to speak English, who log in and go braindead instantly, all the usual complaints. I think castings are a great concept, and I'm in no way saying that they shouldn't happen.

That said: What happens to the newbies when the playerbase migrates to castings? One of my profoundest joys in the game is getting an intelligent, talkative new player who's eager to learn whom I can help puzzle out its mysteries Even when I'm mush, I try to guide new players in the right direction. Twinoid needs a playerbase for revenue, if nothing else! The more people there are who have a good experience of the game, the more people there'll be buying gold subscriptions.

But when castings come around, it seems likely that new players will much more often be on ships with *only* new players, or at any rate with a much smaller population of experienced players to guide them. Now, for all I know, casting ships aren't exclusive with public ships, but even if you can be on an open ship and a casting ship at the same time, I suspect many players less interested in anyone's fun but their own will be putting a lot more attention into their casting ships than they will their publics. Has anyone had a brilliant idea to address this concern? It'll be terrible for the community and for the company if the influx of new players who get hooked dries up. The best feeling there is in an endgame screen is seeing a first-time player say they had a blast and knowing they're gonna go hop straight into another ship, and without active player guidance, I feel like those farewell messages will drop off sharply.

Put my mind at ease! Tell me that castings aren't going to mean a huge deficit of shared wisdom for newbies.
