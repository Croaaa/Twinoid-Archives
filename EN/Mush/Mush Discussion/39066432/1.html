So, my take on this,...

I play a gold lvl 6 Terrence A LOT.

I don't like the Scooter at all. It only takes a valuable slot in my inventory. In order to trigger the conversion you must be completely out of MP and that is something very, very rarely that happens if you use my play-style.

My logic is this, - There are many ways to gain AP during a single cycle, but no way to infinitely gain MP. You can stack coffees, pills, regular food items, bumpjunkins, saved up commander missions, combine all that with a bed. MP only pills and Derek. So, in essence, a Terrence could probably do more work, put more AP to good use in A SINGLE CYCLE than any other character.

I am usually hyper-active. If something needs "doing" I try to scramble and go do it. I'm often in a position when lots of my crew are asleep and I'm all by my self with 0AP/7-8MP. I just like to go do stuff right away as they happen. I'll gladly sacrifice food to "Further our goals". My work gives me the liberty to have access to PC 12+hrs a day so it's not a problem spending 10-15 every cycle. What I usually do, is "befriend" a trusted human, or simply ANYONE after mush are gone and have them push me around. My TAG-TEAM strategy does wonders. My push-partner holds the Scooter, I do all the work. One "Terrence AP" is more valuable when put to work than converted. Push partners are usually happy to do so, they get to claim "helping" while saving almost all of their AP. The idea is, If I can spend 6-8 AP on repairs, relays, IT points, Tech points, dismantles, Hull patching all in a single cycle, all that is needed is 1 other person with Scooter to spend 1 ap conversion and push me.

It's a good deal for them overall after taking a look back at all the work accomplished. Often times this can be combined with the other person's job too. Which makes it even better.

Thanks for reading.
