So, I've been playing some Mush games. Not a whole lot, but I think it's around ten. And what I've been noticing is that most of the times, the humans rather lose to the environment than to the Mush.

Of course I had games, where it came down to a rampaging Mush at the end, which was fun, but only the environment makes it possible. In most of the games, the Mush are rather inexperienced or inactive and the humans lose anyway, because of all the fires/hunters et cetera.

The Mush need more possibilities to act sneaky and do subtle damage to the humans without them noticing it. It should not be possible to lose for the humans if the Mush are inactive, that's the whole point. I think it would make the game far more entertaining, than for the humans to simply struggle for survival with or without Mush.

I have a lot of ideas on my mind, but I wanted to see the thoughts of the community about this first. I'm sure a lot of people feel this way.

I think it's definitely possible for Mush to win on a super-active ship. But they need to be careful, and clever, and patient. Probably the best early-game weapon you have, as Mush, against an active ship is existing. Remember that they can't win so long as you are alive, so play the long game. Shower-proof is NOT "needed" if you're playing careful. You just need a cover story. And since something as simple as picking up items can hurt you, it shouldn't be THAT hard to, over the course of the first 4 days, save up 2 spores per person.

Then, depending on your characters, you wait for the perfect time to strike. 4 spores means you can convert someone instantly on day change AND do a transfer, which can utterly ruin even a well-prepared ship. It's about biding your time and waiting for the perfect moment to strike, and playing politics in the meanwhile.

In my opinion, there's two reasons why you see Mush pose so little of a threat on an active ship:
a) People don't want to be Mush - The first and biggest one. Every single human victory I've been involved in was, ultimately, because one or more of the Mush simply didn't want to be Mush. This is magnified exponentially if you've got a "good looking" ship, and people don't want to feel guilty about ruining it. No matter how powerful you make the Mush, this guarantees their loss. I've seen many Mush suicide or just go inactive for this reason. I've even seen Mush go turncoat and betray their partners because they felt so bad about being the bad guy.
b) Mush are inexperienced. For many Mush, this is the first time they have EVER been Mush. They don't have mush levels, they don't know the details of how to play as Mush, and they've got no useful previous experiences to fall back on. Even if they HAVE been Mush before, it was probably on an inactive cakewalk ship. It's one of the most important roles in the game, and very few people have any idea how to play it, and usually haven't invested experience in it.

Until there's a way to deal with those two problems (and I have proposed solutions here, they are solvable, although I doubt they will be solved) you're right - Mush are going to have a tough time of it, and worthwhile Mush adversaries on an active ship are going to be hard to come by.
