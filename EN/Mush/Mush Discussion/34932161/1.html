I mean it's usually just better to take a character, roleplay him, and use the character's reputation to fortify whatever social goals you have.

It's not just background. Chao has a strong reputation as a griefer favorite on most ships, which adds a meta . The personal skillset definitely strengthens it. Jin Su's Commander trait attracts the leader types. Raluca's Antisocial will keep her segregated from the rest of the ship, at least in the mush phase. I've seen a really good mush hunter play Raluca and yet she had a hard time leading the ship from the rear.

I disagree with Impet that all characters are equally good socially with the right player. It's like going to an interview or business meeting with a t-shirt and flip flops. You can pull it off with the right charisma, but a similarly skilled fellow player with the right 'suit' (character) will do better with less effort.

A good villain doesn't do things loudly, a good villain knows the spotlight makes his life hard.

I've done mush Jin Su on my first ship. A very dominating mush Jin Su. Jin Su is best played loud. I basically just made the ship run around doing pointless errands, telling Ian to hunt mush, Roland to both scan and fill up fuel tanks, Frieda to fly out there and shoot down the arack, Stephen to hold on to the hacker kit as soon as Frieda kills the Arack (and runs out of AP to land).

It was a great way to play mush, creating a lot of chaos, yelling at people who question why I was dirty when I was doing the important role of leading the ship. And getting that final backstab in while they were suffocating in the orbit of an oxygen planet.

It's also why a lot of mush like to target Jin Su. Not simply because he can move the ship and give missions, but because he has the most political influence over the ship. Need a final decision between arguing people? Jin Su's word is final. Jin Su says that we put the camera in the medlab instead of the bridge? Yes, sir!
