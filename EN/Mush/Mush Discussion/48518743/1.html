Just to clarify, it is just the condition they use for their statistics. They do not consider, as some people say, that a mush return to Eden is a mush defeat, or that a ship that explodes is a human victory just because a human has the gold nova.

The problem with using other conditions is that it's subjective. There have been many, many, many debates in the French forum about win conditions, and every single time, it has been impossible to get people to agree. For instance, some players consider that a return to Sol without the serum is a loss, and prefer to let the ship explode (I know, it's a little too radical). Others say that if the ship explodes early because the crew was bad (mush and humans alike), it's not a mush victory, rather a human defeat. Also, I suppose that the devs have been using that win condition for their statistics since the beginning, and the game has evolved a lot, adding new possible conditions (Eden didn't exist at first). The way people play has evolved too.

The glory factor isn't the perfect criterion, but it is one of several possibilities, and they chose this one. It isn't totally illogical either. Of course, if a mush has low glory, it doesn't mean that they lost or played badly. But when a mush has the gold nova, it rarely means that humans survived and won.

Besides, it still gives them an indicator, because they see that figure increase or decrease. I know that Drzachary found completely different statistics, but I guess that the variations in the number of mush gold novas is an indicator of the evolution of the difficulty for mush and humans.

So, the first results are in, for Season 2.

With a confidence interval of 95%, margin of error 5%: Mush win 61%.
343 ships have been counted (spread across the season.) Casting ships were omitted.

I do believe this to be a far more scientific measurement of win ratio than using criteria like glory score, simply because this measurement, while not perfect, is not as vastly disconnected from the idea of winning. (Mush glory is driven more by ship length than winning, and Human glory is of course dependent on which character you have, and whether or not you shoot hunters.)

The reason I think this is important is that changes are made, presumably, to balance the two sides ... and the balancing has been done in the wrong direction. On a personal note, I like the changes, as I like it when the mush win, and change is *fun.* Still, having proper data might help MT to achieve their listed 50/50 goal.

A little over half the ships exploded, so maybe more resilient Daedaluses could be an avenue of balancing!
