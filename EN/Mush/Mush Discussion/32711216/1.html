I love this game. I really, really do. But inactives are destroying the funness, and worse, making Gold-paying players feel like they're wasting their money.

So, to improve on activity, may I suggest we have some form of a "MENTOR" system?

Who should be mentors?
Any super active players who chooses to be one.

Super active?
Players who have never been idle, even once, in their last 80 consecutive cycles. Also, this person makes at least 5 posts in chat every day.

What should mentors do?
At the start of every ship, Mentors must offer their help to all "new players" (and sell the game a bit ), and answer ANY questions that was asked of them. Mentors must also engage actively with these new players (instead of just answering questions), therefore making the game more fun for them, and showing them the best part of this game early on: the social aspect.

New players?
Players who has less then 80 cycles under their belt are automatically new players.

How do we keep these mentors accountable to their jobs?
Have a feedback system everytime a newbie dies (beyond Day 2). Ask if a Mentor offered their help, and if they speak to them?

Sounds like a lot of work. Why would anyone want to be a mentor?
Because we'll reward them with something. Say...allow one of the Gold skill slots to be "unlockable only if mentor". This will encourage life-Bronze players (people who will never pay) to be mentors and guide the new players to Mush salvation, thus ushering in more potential Gold players.

That's a nice reward! But, what if someone stops being helpful once they're a mentor?
Then we revoke their mentor status, and thus that wonderful skill slot (if they're not Gold).

What about paying Gold players? This sounds unfair...
Well, Gold players don't have to be a mentor, since they get the skill slot automatically anyway. HOWEVER, If we really need to reward them further...maybe this: at the start of Cycle 1 from Day 2 onwards, Gold players who are ALSO mentors gain an extra 4 EXP (i.e. 1.5x faster to gain high level skills).

One last thing...
Since we're making a distinction between super actives (never idle in last 80 cycles, and posts 5 times in chat every day) and everyone else...we should reward them with something, too, to encourage even more activity of long-time players. Say...a Klix for every day they are super active? Is that too good?

Okay, I'm done. What does everyone think? The reason why I want to reward super active players and mentors is because they help with bringing new players in (and thus more gold players), so I believe they definitely deserve the rewards.

I find one easy fix is to get to the before they become inactive. If they get as far as "Hello", it means that the game isn't an instant turn off for some of them. They'll likely log on again later, if only just once.

Strike up a conversation with them at that point. Roleplay a little and encourage them to roleplay. The game mechanics themselves are extremely boring - you get 1 AP and 1 MP every 3 hours and most of the game is actually chatting. It's absurd to punish people using glory or whatever - when you first start playing a game, you look at fun, not at score. What's fun is the emergent gameplay, and the community feel even though each community changes per ship.

Make friends with them on the spot, so they feel a little guilty about quitting. Most of the game is chatting, so chat with newbies! Encourage them to roleplay a bit. Dig up interesting bits from their bio:
"Hey Finola, I'm a great fan of your work. Sorry for the way we dragged you onto the ship, but you're stuck with us now."
"You know, we could really use your help developing some Anti Mush serum. Nobody else has your skill with biology."

"Stephen! I thought I'd never see you again after you dropped me off at Mars. How have you been? What are you up to on the ship?" (as Paola)
"Oh! Don't cook anything for the ship yet, it'll go rotten by tomorrow and we need to ration our food supplies. You used to be a mercenary, right? Maybe you could go into one of the turrets and shoot down a few hunters?"

Or if you want to get straight to the point, just something like "Chun, let's go hunt some mush! You're the only one I trust on this ship!"

The idea here is to pose a rigged question to a newbie. You want to trick them into committing to something. Once they're committed, they'll log in every now and then, even if it's to apologize later for not having time to play the game. After they log in 5 times, they'll log in more often even without any prodding.

The other bit is that you want to get them chatting and out of their shell. Mush is a highly communicative game. If they don't like chatting, they won't enjoy the game.


On my first game, I took Jin Su. Was a poor choice for first character, as I later realized. Everyone was asking me for their orders. It was my first time, and most of the people who asked for orders were playing their first time too.

It got me committing as a leader of some spaceship. By day 2, I found out little things that needed to be done. By day 3, I figured everyone's roles and learned first hand why it's so important to have cameras everywhere.

It was a poor ship. I mean, hell, no cameras in the dorm.. how noob can anyone get? But by day 3, I was already bossing everyone around. Roland... you fill up the fuel tanks. Frieda, you scan for planets. Eleesha, you waste AP hunting mush. Stephen, you hang around the bridge with the hacker kit in case I need to sleep. Paola, make daily announcements on our status. Janice, keep me comfortable in the dorms.

And then the magic happened. Everyone started enjoying the game, even though they didn't know what to do at first. We went from newbie ship to a point where a quarter of the ship was online every cycle. We had fun banter during our final hours. We had newbies saying "I'll be afk for a cycle", because they realized how important their role was.

Of course, as mush, all the activity wasn't good for me, so I decided to stab everyone who tried to get oxygen in the end. But it was fun from an OOC perspective.
