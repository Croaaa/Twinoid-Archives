Happy three weeks of live Mush everyone!!!

We've been working hard at hammering out our guidelines. There are some recent changes to the rules. Proposed further guideline changes were becoming more complex so we've decided to reset right back to the basic set of rules we started with. http://twd.io/e/qLXA0g/0 We've learned a lot from the French game and will be starting our game much in line with theirs. If we see separate English Mush specific issues, we will be tweaking the rules to reflect them.

Here are issues that have come up and our decisions on these matters.

Anonymity
We recently said we would sanction for breaking anonymity (revealing Twinoid user names) on our ships but have since decided to reverse this. We almost all agree that anonymity in Mush makes the game more fun! But, it is not fair to sanction those who break theirs. It also creates an atmosphere of overt rule breaking for something quite innocent, which is never a good thing.

Many find anonymity to be one the best features of this game and chose to keep anonymity at all times. There is no Twinoid fame in Mush, no long time member hero worship and you are free to role play the character you've chosen without anyone knowing who you really are. Because most of us do want to keep our anonymity and not know the identity of others on our ships, we strongly suggest everyone preserve anonymity because it's more enjoyable to play that way.

Spreadsheets
Because of our previous restriction on breaking anonymity, spreadsheets were out. Using Google Drive at least the owner is known so we said "no" to them and that we would sanction. We were then faced with others sites where anonymity was preserved but I found it too complicated to say yes to one site and no to another. Since changing our stance on anonymity, we have less reason to disallow spreadsheets and other sites used to inventory list. So, we will not sanction for them now.

That said, there will plenty of time for total organization in private ships when they come. Spreadsheet encouragement on public ships is often futile as not everyone will use them and remember, the mush can see them as well and destruction of important documents is a valid mush strategy. Lists can also lead to tensions as the super organized might pressure the under organized. You can post your link once and please, don't expect new players or those who would rather stay in game to use these tools.

Inactivity
Inactivity (new players, players not communicating or playing) will not be sanctioned, do not report them. We do not want to sanction new players who might not be engaged. Learning to work around these players is part of our game. You *can* succeed on as ship that has many deaths and/or a fair number of inactives. Luck and team work amongst those left will be in order but it can be done. Thoughtful communication and patience can go a long way.

Ships filling slowly
There will be the rare times ships might take longer to fill, when this happens we will expect "neutral play". Which means, we don't do anything that specifically will help either team. As an example, killing hunters is acceptable as a mush goal can potentially be to live longer as much as it is a human goal. What's not acceptable is holding or hiding the Mush Sample, apron, weapons and other items. We encourage you not to advance anti-Mush research so that whoever is selected Mush (which could be you!) will have a fair chance at winning. Neutral research examples could be - Ultra-healing Pomade or the NCC Lens. We've also asked Blackmagic to consider having the Mush Sample (needed for anti-mush research) spawn only after the ship is full. Until then, though, this is our best solution.


Remember, Mush is a team sport! You can't actively work against your team be it mush or human! If you play as a human and you sabotage the humans (killing for no reason relate to the advancement of the human cause, taking both showers apart, cooking all the rations and letting them spoil, etc) this is griefing and it will be take very seriously. If you are mush, you cannot denounce your fellow mush or impede the mush cause, you must work as a team.

Have fun!
