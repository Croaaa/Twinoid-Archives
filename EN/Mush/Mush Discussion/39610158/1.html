Almost every time someone is referring to a room they do so using its abbreviated form. It may appear to save you time while typing but I have a different opinion about them.

From what I understand abbreviations in general have been used for two or three reasons:
when you pay by the amount of letters you actually type (telegraph, newspaper ads, sms)
when something has a really long and complicated name that is impractical (Chemical formulas)
for some really common expressions that we are actually taught at school and are hardcoded in our brains. (i.e., etc.)

The names of Daedalus rooms don't fit in any of the above categories.
The most complicated being Centre Storages but still not enough to justify such butchering, in my opinion.

Every time you have to go through the process of thinking how a room should be abbreviated and when you are reading someone else's you have to decode that gibberish in your mind.
I just passed by Rear Alpha and Bravo Storages, and the engine room. No luck.
We need to check: CAT, CBT, CAS, CBS, Alpha Bay 2, Icarus Bay, RBT and RAT.
For me the second part of that quote is a mild form of torture.

And it gets worse, some people even go as far as to abbreviate names!
Why would you do that to something that's 8 letters tops?
