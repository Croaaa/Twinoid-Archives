Dying on this game isn't like dying on most games, at least for me it isn't. As soon as you die, you are free to start a new game, pick a new character, and totally forget whatever happened on your last ship (keep the useful lessons though ).

If your ship thinks you are mush and haven't killed you yet, maybe they mean to cure you. A live mush is a dangerous mush - let go of the embarrassment you feel and play to learn. There's things you can do. Your ship might not have killed you yet because they realize that you're new, and they don't want to smoosh a new player - if you think that's rude, smoosh them for it .

if they haven't killed you yet, maybe they're not actually certain that you're mush. they might be new too... and too, as human I recently failed a food test, because I'd miscalcuated how long I needed to go without eating beforehand in order to be able to eat two things before satiation kicked up high enough to make me full.... whoops! and the ship actually gave me more time, and didn't even press me to test again. So weird things happen, every single game of this is very, very different, and there's even a chance that if you're a specialist role without hope of a backup (like, if you are Raluca and there's no techs...) that the ship might be perfectly happy to 'give you more time and try to limit the harm you can cause' because the cost of your death is scary high for that ship. Or, if you're Ian, the cost of killing you is scary high.

Do you know about the wikipedia, and the list of useful links on this forum? There's a guide to playing each character, guides to being very dangerous as mush, there's a list of everything that can make you dirty, there's explainations of the differences between humans and mush (and how mush tests/human tests work). If you haven't found these resources yet, just ask - but know that every ship you start anonymous; no one knows if you are very new or a pro... or even a pro pretending to be very new. So let go of any embarrassment you feel; relax, dive in, if you don't like one ship remember every ship is very different and getting killed is actually a kindness, it frees you to go find a ship that you're more likely to enjoy than the one you are on.

If they do, they get sanctioned for a bad report. It's actually a great system here, I think. Here's how it works:

If you play 'reasonably', like don't swear, don't massively insult others, you're following the 'observe netiequitte' rule.

If you at least -try- (note you don't have to succeed, especially if you appear new in your actions) to support your team, no matter what your team is, then you're following the 'don't play against your own team' rule.

If you don't tell people you're mush, especially if you don't tell people who the other mush is; if you don't just 'give up' and beg to be cured, then you are not breaking the 'do not surrender' rule.

From the sounds of it, you're uncomfortable and unsure, but you sound like you are following the rules. The judges do not appear to appreciate abusive reports, so anyone reporting your play really needs to feel convinced that you are not -new-, which is very much allowed in the rules, but that you are actually -breaking the rules-. And even if you have broken the rules, the punishment you get usually starts as a correction/explanation, or can turn into one. If you end up sanctioned - talk to a mod about it. Make sure you understand -why- you were found to break a rule, so you never risk breaking it again. That's what is cared about.

From what you've said so far, I don't see anyone on your ship reporting you. Don't be too scared, remember that being new is not punishable - but reporting someone for being new is punishable - so enjoy as much as you can, and learn as much as you can!
