How does everyone feel about this? Now I'm not sure if anyone has already found this, but I don't think anyone has, so imma post this regardless. Actually, there have already been dozens of debates about "the truth about Andie". Every time someone notices that Andie is referred to as "he" (i.e. at least once during each season with Andie and Derek), it starts a discussion, with some people adamant that they are a man despite what the devs explicitly said. You couldn't know that (although it's always useful to try a forum search before posting, and see if the question has already been asked), and at least you accepted the explanation, while other people just contradict the devs' own decision.


We all know this has always plagued the minds of players: What is Andie, a boy or a girl...or something else? as for "the underlying gender prejudice that led to what you wrote."...maybe i don't fully understand or know, but what exactly is that which you're speaking of (and is it even ok to be talking about whatever that is in mush discussions? What LAbare meant (or the way I see it) is that Andie is deliberately presented as ambivalent in game and in his biography, which clearly says "he/she". The problem is that some people do not accept this. For them, Andie has to have an official gender and generally has to be a man. But why ? Why should they have to have a definite gender ? Why should the question have only one answer ?

MT deliberately chose to make Andie ambiguous. They could have decided that they would be a man who's attracted to other men, since players had been asking for a long time why there were no homosexual relationships in the game. But they decided to go one step further and to let the players decide. Andie can be a gay man, a lesbian, they can be a man or a woman (or even a man and a woman), they can be attracted to man or women or both.

Basically, this means that Andie was meant to offer us many more RP possibilities (which already existed but were only RP, not GP). So, if someone says "no, Andie is a man", that means they deny all those possibilities. The underlying idea is that someone must either be a man or a woman, they can't be both, and they can't be whatever you choose them to be, because that doesn't or shouldn't exist. Or worse, someone can either like men or women, not both.

Don't get me wrong, please. I'm not saying that it is the case in this thread, I'm just explaining why such debates are not as innocent as they seem to be, at least from some people. And from others, it's unconscious, but it's still there, in a corner of the collective mind.
