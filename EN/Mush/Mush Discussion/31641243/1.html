Would like to discuss strategy, but first a quick explanation (feel free to ask for clarification or correct me if I'm wrong on any of this, of course):
There are 31 NERON projects which will appear as options for completion at the NERON terminal in the Nexus. Each project will appear exactly once. You're given an option of three to choose from, and once you complete one, the list refreshes with new projects, meaning the other two are lost as options for your current ship.

NERON projects can also be completed by the comms officer at the comms terminal by doing NERON upgrades. For each NERON version upgrade, a random project which has not yet been available and is not currently available in the Nexus will be completed. From what I understand, the comms officer can do as many of these as they like, given that their pool of projects isn't exhausted, although there are diminishing returns on AP investment after the first few.

Once all projects have either been an option in the Nexus or been completed via comms, no more NERON projects are possible. Period. End of story.

~~~~

So here's where the strategy comes in - how quickly should NERON projects be completed at the nexus? It seems like early on, your comms officer is going to be focusing mostly on xyloph database entries and rebel databases, and in general won't really be able to do NERON upgrades. Meanwhile, the focus for the first couple of days should probably be research to get the important anti-mush stuff up, and mush hunting.

But perhaps, if all goes decently with these things in the first couple of days, then the focus could switch to NERON projects until extra hydropots are done, then slow down to let some upgrades come in via comms? Are there any other projects which people see as vital to get completed early? And lots of the NERON projects are super useful, so would like to know what people think is a good balance between getting the better ones quicker and getting more overall.

[6826491]

Ultimately, both will save AP, but even if you find it early, Neron Participation is limited in its usefulness. On the other hand, Plasma Shield is effective from the moment you finish and activate it: An instant 50 shield points, which can easily keep you alive for a few cycles, against anything except asteroids. The AP saved on not repairing the hull (given the chance of failure) is combined with the AP saved by pilots who can safely ignore small groups of Hunters, which also means 1) more time in a bed for them, and 2) saving AP not being spent in multiple takeoffs and landings, just to kill 2-3 hunters.

Back to Neron Participation, if you take it early, it might advance projects at a rate faster than your comms officer can keep up with...further reducing your pool of possible projects. I see this as a major drawback, unless your crew purposely delayed Nexus projects, and had your Comms officer heavily focus on Neron upgrades.

My conclusion: Plasma Shield is still the optimal choice for both short and long term survival.
