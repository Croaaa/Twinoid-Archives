I am speaking of the players that one CANNOT speak with at all (because they do not have a walkie-talkie), the ones that starve in day 2 (because they never ate), and the ones that idle endlessly in the front corridor. To the Mush, that is one less potential player who can do anything as an opponent, and to the humans they are a loss of AP and Morale, and often much more if the idler is Chun/Jin Su/Paola/Janice. The point is, the newbies who don't complete the tutorial at all really add nothing to the game.

Personally, I would like to see tutorial ships. However, to stop players from getting "locked in" to a ship that is full of very new players, I'd like to see something a little different than a full offline tutorial.

My suggestion

First, Tutorial ships would be in a different queue than normal ships. Players in a tutorial ship would be able to join a full ship at any time by entering their menu. The option to join a full ship would be directly next to the "Advanced Mode" button, and suggested at the completion of the "first steps" tutorial.

Second, anyone may join a tutorial ship in addition to a regular ship. Yes, this means you would be able to join two ships at once. This allows experienced players to join Tutorial ships explicitly for the purpose of introducing newbies to the game. Players doing this would know full well that they are getting into a ship predominantly made of newbies/inactives.

Third, gold/silver status, XP, and progress towards casting tickets do not apply to tutorial ships. Everyone starts and remains in bronze status. Likewise, one may not use Klix. Character level can apply and Cosmetics may be equipped, however.

Ideally, this would allow newbies to familiarize themselves with the game on a ship that is intended to be used for that. Experienced players can join them and give advice/teach without fear of losing out on cash for gold days/Klix, and as soon as a new player feels comfortable with the interface they can move on to join a full ship armed with the basics. Similarly, full ships would no longer have the routine 1-5 players who join and never once finish the tutorial.

Win/win for all sides.

I have obviously no idea how it is on other ships. My ship, despite what I said about not getting responses, is actually a ship full of generally nice and helpful people. Maybe it really is extraordinary to get a ship like that, but for me, a couple of key player made it a success.

As experienced players, if you want to have a good ship, approach new players. If they post in chat, reply. If they do something wrong, explain why that wasn't a good idea without being abrasive. They are most likely not stupid, they are inexperienced, which is not the same. In a lot of cases, *you* are the one who should've known better and should've told the newbies beforehand what they should and shouldn't be doing.

This won't prevent ships with a lot of inactive players, but having 1 or 2 active players more will make it definitely better.
