I wouldn't.

And I'd also say this won't happen. The main reason is community. All the examples you wrote above are games that are played individually. Yes, there are some clans and guilds in every game, but most of them are still games that you can play without ANY communication with any other player (although, I'm not sure about Monster Hotel, didn't try that one). That leads to several active clans in each such game, several posts in Q&A thread every now and then and otherwise, forums are mostly dead.

On the other hand, Mush is a social game (same as Die2Nite) and thus community around it is thriving in comparison to all those non-social games. And community is a big asset for developers. Just remember how they decided to stop providing any support for English server in October. And now, half year later, we still get new episode. That's something I don't think would happen if the community wouldn't work.
Now, coming with complete re-do of whole game (or creating some paralel game on Twinoid) carries a risk of splitting this working community and losing both casual and paying players. Of course, in time new community would most probably emerged again. But there's no guarantee it would be bigger and with more paying players. Being a developer I wouldn't take such risk.
