As Sperbi says, if you see rules broken, or believe you are seeing rules broken, please report it. Every ship, every player that breaks rules, please.

If you do not report a rulebreaker, there's every chance that player will go on to break the same rules (and maybe others too) on future ships, without end.

If you do report a rulebreaker, that rulebreaking behavior is very likely to end one way or another (some people didn't realize they were breaking rules, others knew but choose to stop breaking them when they are sanctioned, others choose to continue to break them, continue to be reported for it, and are stopped by the system).

If your ship has many rulebreakers on it, report them all. If your ship has 4 rulebreakers, I honestly don't mind if all twelve lawful players report all four of the rule breakers. Sure, that's 48 reports for just one ship, but if each one is a legitimate report, rules really were broken.... lets get it fixed. The system can handle that happening, and it won't result in an unfair increase of punishment.

To clarify, in case it is unclear:

It is not allowed by the rules to hold most items before mush selection.

It is not allowed by the rules to force human testing on random others, or to kill people 'just because they refused to prove they were human'. Note that if there's other evidence a player is mush and a human test is offered, then the player is killed - that may well be a legal kill as long as that other evidence is reasonable. The key parts of this are 'random' and 'just because'.

It's also not allowed by the rules to just be a complete jerk on your ships and play in a way that really makes the ship a totally miserable experience for others, be that through badgering and putting down the game as a whole, the players as a group, or even just badgering one specific player a great deal, especially without reason. Any judgment calls on such things are determined by judges, but the coded-in name for that rulebreaking is 'spoiling', 'passive negativity', or 'active negativity', depending on exactly what is happening and how severe each judge interprets the behavior to be, and yes, ruining ships through a bad attitude is rulebreaking.

So if someone is ruining a ship you are on, report them. It won't save that ship, granted, but it does help improve the future.

If someone is ruining a ship you are on, you can also, openly or through PM with select others, discuss the problem and come to some consensus about what to do about it as a ship.

On one of my early ships, long before I became a mod, we faced a griefer who hacked and moved the ship pre-mush selection, killing a pilot out in space, and then using some rather pointed hostile phrases intended to be generally offensive and directly violating the language rules of T&C. In addition to reporting them, I responded by creating a PM with every other player alive aboard and explaining that mush or human, I wanted the whole ship to band together to remove this player from it, and that I personally would not stop until that offending character was dead or mine was. That I happened to be low AP at that moment, but that I was going to do nothing else on that ship but act to remove that person until they were gone, and I hoped everyone else agreed and would do the same.

They did; that player was dead within the cycle. Rather surprising, as most of the ship were quite new players or inactives; the mush were active and the ship died an early death with a mush win. Problem solved regardless, for that ship. The griefer was dealt with.

If you have a ship with someone trying to force human testing on everyone, or a small group trying to do this to everyone, talk about it, openly or privately, in addition to reporting them if you feel they are breaking rules. Maybe you can get your whole ship to understand and agree that widespread human testing is not a way they want to play (note that it is legal for any individual, or even -all- individuals, to human test, if they choose to. Ship fill is random, and it's possible to for someone to be mush on a ship where all 14 humans are eager to prove they are human. There's coded impediments to make this.... problematic for the humans, but sure, it's legal to try if every individual involved wants to).

If 3-4 people on a ship are trying to forcibly test or kill everyone else aboard the ship, and the other 12-13 organize and do not agree to participate or 'allow' anyone to be forced.... That ship could still end with a human or a mush win and I bet you no one is getting forced if people choose to resist.

One of the neatest things about this game, I think, is that it throws you, over and over again into a situation with 15 strangers and some serious problems. No one is coming to help, even if the situation is utterly unfair and harder than it was ever meant to be. You 16 solve the problems or be destroyed by them. You have a huge role in determining how events play out. You can allow others to control you and the outcome of events. You can cooperate with evil intentions or believe you are helpless, but you actually are anything but - you have the exact same power as anyone else on that ship, give or take a few random events. You by yourself have only so much power, but there is nothing to stop you from team building, negotiating, organizing, and forming a group to handle the problems that you face, be they coded in or socially created.

This game is all about the power that you, personally, have, and how you can use this power to achieve your goals, through coded and social means

You are not helpless here. You do not have to obey anyone. You do have to work towards a win for your team, and the judges need to be able to understand how what you do works towards that win without breaking other rules (in case you are reported), but you can be part of the answer to any problem your ship faces, be it coded or social. Almost every problem on these ships needs teamwork to resolve. If your ship has a need, figure out how to meet it, or be defeated by it. Remember you are part of a team, and it is sensible to attempt to involve the resources and strengths of the team to resolve all of these problems.

And report rulebreakers, please.

Good luck and hope to play beside every one of you, as time passes!
