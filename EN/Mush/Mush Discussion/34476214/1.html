There's some information asymmetry here. You don't know how many games they've played, or what their past behaviors are.

If it was her first game, maybe she just played and... thought the game wasn't her thing. It happens a lot.

Reporting a behavior doesn't mean that they will be banned, or that they're a horrible human being. You're asking for an unbiased third party with access to more data to review what happened. It's the equivalent to calling a consultant or auditor into your company :-)

Report it. If it's part of a history of similar behavior, it will be sanctioned.

Compare to real life:
Someone accidentially steps on your foot. It's not appropriate to obsess about it. On the other hand... someone observes the same individual stepping on 12 feet consecutively, you can start visiting their intentions.
