My take on the gold/inactive ships issue - I'm gold. I went gold my second ship. I -love- this game. I probably spend.... off and on of course, a good 3 hours a day interacting on my ships, checking the forums, researching something on the wiki. I do that even on the less active ships, and I usually find at least one other person, often a newbie, to enjoy interacting with and passing the time together playing and planning and learning.

I've been on ships I've enjoyed more and those I've enjoyed less, but my gold time hasn't, for the most part been wasted at all. All I've minded, and that only a tiny bit, is when I join a ship late in the day or end a voyage early in the day - it's sad to get only a fraction of the exp that I could have gotten for that game day.

However, I accept that I'm playing a super variable game, and some of those variables are the other players. I jump random, and I've gotten a wide range of ship 'qualities'. I've seen (according to Scout) way more than the normal share of griefers, I've seen tons of inactives and actives both. I've enjoyed a lot of every ship I've been on, and I'm glad to enjoy the gold privilages even on the 'less successful' ship.

Is it frustrating to be on a ship with a lot of inactives? ehh. I don't believe I can control others. But I can control myself - and I find it more frustrating to be more limited and less efficient - I would enjoy being bronze on a 'poor ship' even less than I can enjoy being gold on that same ship - because gold lets me do more and be more, as well as profit more from the time spent in that game.

I bought gold for my birthday, and because I was on what I thought was an active ship. Here are some thoughts that I have about timing

a) Do wait until it's a new cycle, since it's switched on immediately
b) If you do decide to upgrade on an active ship, make sure it's active day 5 not active day 1. Those day 1s really make you feel optimistic and can be a huge let down
c) Also, It's good to go gold Mush. I ended up on a ship with sliver and got gifted gold, so I had 2 human traits and 4 mush - which was much easier than the next ship where I had 4 human skills, because all my skills to pretend to be human were huge AP drains.
d) You really do level up much faster with Gold. So if you're "close enough" just go for it, and you'll get your 4th skill quickly - if you want all 4.
