What do you think about different game endings? (who is considered winner)
I realize I am more strict than others, about what I consider a win.

- Humans die, Mush survive. (conversion, death by lack of health/morale/oxygen)
Mush win - even if the mush get assasinated by neron afterwards (they gain their satisfaction from watching the humans suffer, and don't mind if they die after reaching their goal)

- Mush dead, Humans dead. (ship destroyed by hunters or humans die after mush have been eliminated)
draw - everybody loses.
Ship destroyed by hunters is a special case here. Some consider it a mush win, but I personally think it is a really cheap win to do nothing and just wait until the ship falls apart on its own. (*)
I think the mush get their satisfaction from witnessing how the humans die.
they won't be witnessing anything if the hunters kill everyone, while the mush are sleeping too.
of course if the mush personally fly out and crush the last point of hull, I would count it as a mush win.
* (maybe Humans just need to get better at killing hunters and fixing up the hull to not allow the mush those cheap wins)

- return to Sol/Eden with mush still on board
draw (nobody can say what happens afterwards)

- Human return to Sol with no mush and no Serum
draw (even if they eradicated the mush - the human goal is to develop the serum and bring it back to sol. if they don't have the Serum, then they have failed the mission)
- there is exception to this. I think its better to have a cured mush even if you lose your serum by curing them. if you researched it once then you should be able to do it again.

- Human return to Sol with Serum and without mush
Human win. with the serum developed, there is hope to save humanity.

- Human return to Eden without mush
Human win. when making new Eden it won't matter if the Serum is available or not.

Sorry for bumping an old thread, but that topic is interesting imo. Surprising that some people only see Eden as victory...although I can understand them.

At the beginning of the game, the crew has no idea about the state of Sol system. After some days, contacts with rebel bases are established. And there are multiple of them. For most people, these are just bonus-giving sources. Who reads those rebels messages, really?.. But plotwise, it's us who give them info and bonuses as well (see Sirius base message). We transfer them our research results, and in exchange receive data for upgrades.

Later in the journey, there are two bases which give morale: Kaladaan and Cygni. Both inform the crew that Rebellion is not only alive and kicking, but actually prepares for the human victory. Even more, Cygni says that it uses pheromodem. Lots of ships do not research pheromodem. I guess that means Cygni people made it themselves using known researches and/or our data. That gave me the impression that we mostly research our stuff for ourselves, for the in-ship usage.

What can we see from all those rebel messages?
1) Rebellion is slowly but surely taking over. There's a whole planetar system belonging to Federation which actually is fully controlled by rebels (Cygni). Therefore, we can return into the Sol system without fear - but before that we need to discover that from decoding rebel signal, right? It also explains why we need to last longer: the more days pass, the more Rebellion succeeds, the safer our return is.
2) It is able to carry its own researches. It's unknown to which extend. Maybe they can even research the Serum, being provided the necessary data (like Chun's genome).

What's for sure, once we've got Serum, we can transfer the data about it to rebels. So, having Serum (and connection up!) should count as soothing factor even in case Daedalus went boom afterwards. And it steals the taste of victory for mush, as well! How about that?

But to where actually Daedalus returns? There's Sol(ar) system, and there are more distant colonies, some of which are rebellious. These colonies, as I now discovered, refer to the neighbour stars. Do they count as parts of Sol, since they're established by it? Also, no matter the answer - is Xyloph the one and only possible destination port, or can we say that Daedalus would choose the more precise destination about where in Sol it lands? Only knowing that we can guess if it's a win or surrender... Because seriously, what are we gonna do, arriving right onto half-ruined Xyloph? Inject our only portion of Serum into some random officer?..

What I can't argue with, Eden is the ultimate victory...
