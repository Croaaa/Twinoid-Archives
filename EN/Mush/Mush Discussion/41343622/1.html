The strength of the skill isn't how many AP it allows you to get, but how many extra AP it adds to the crew. Concentrating a lot of AP on one person isn't a good thing. Sure, if the Frugivore Ian has an Aerial-like activity level and has managed to read a magebook that makes him efficient at a late-game job, it's not much of a problem, but in more normal situations you may not want to depend too much on one player. I can easily imagine ships where I would not want all the jumpkins reserved for a Frugivore Ian who is online an average of 3-4 cycles per day while I would be fine with a Caffeine Junkie, Astrophys Gioele with a similar activity level drinking all the coffee.

The opportunity cost of having Ian eat all the jumpkins is higher than the opportunity cost of letting Gioele drink all the coffee as the base value of a jumpkin is higher than for a coffee, but you get the same +2 AP per item in either case. Caffeine Junkies use coffee 100 % more efficiently than other people do while Frugivores use jumpkins about 50 % more efficiently. A Frugivore with full morale and health who eats a jumpkin uses it only about 25 % more efficiently than another person who could have benefitted from the morale and health. If the other person also happens to have a skill that makes him more efficient at whatever job needs to be carried out, it can easily make sense to let that other person eat the jumpkin even if Ian is online.
That's less relevant in the caffeine case as Gioele doubles the benefit from coffee, can't waste any side effects and will often be efficient at a late-game job as he often has astrophysics. (Yes, Ian has access to firefighter, but it seems to be very rarely picked and firefighting is hardly a full-time job for a frugivore).

[One could argue that Frugivore is a better skill if your garden produces a worse alien fruit than jumpkins as the opportunity cost of the frugivore eating them all will be lower, but that does of course not mean that it's a better situation for the crew to be in.]

While the maximal benefit of Frugivore is higher than the maximal benefit of caffeine junkie on extreme ships that's not the only thing worth considering in assessing the strength of the skills. I care more about the usefulness on normal ships than on the few extreme ships.
It's my experience that there are a lot more ships that finish Pilgred and keep flying for a while than there are ships that get a full mature jumpkin garden and that those ships who get both Pilgred and jumpkin garden usually get Pilgred finished days earlier.
