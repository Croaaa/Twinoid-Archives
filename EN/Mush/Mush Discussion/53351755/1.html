Some Mush teams manage to convert a large number of humans and then decide to try and infect Eden. The problem with that is you have to keep at least one human alive until the very cycle you click the button, or else the game ends. You also have to make sure the last human(s) can't fight back.

My question for discussion is this: What if you are that human?

You have no hope of overpowering the mush, giving up/cooperating is against the rules, and so is intentionally suiciding. You're effectively locked out of the game for several days, and even going inactive on purpose is frowned upon... I suppose you could save up your AP and hope you get lucky, but if you fail to kill the mush keeping you jailed in one sitting and before anyone else notices, then the effort was all for naught. Naturally the mush is going to do everything in their power to make this strategy ineffective.

If you find yourself in this situation, what are you expected to do? Flail endlessly into the void until random chance puts you out of your misery? It hardly seems fair that there is a way for others to force you out of being able to play the game, and it's perfectly legal.

I've never been in this position myself, nor do I expect it to happen to me. The question came to me as I was reading a particular AP thread, and my curiosity got the better of me. So I figured I'd start a topic rather than wrack my brain trying to figure out what even can be done.

Well, it depends.

First of all, it depends on the fact whether you KNOW that all the others are mushes. If you don't then it's not much special situation. You just do your job, look for suspicious behavior (if mushy Eleesha didn't announced The List unprintable) and coordinate both with other "humans" (people who got confirmed and you don't have evidences of them being potentially converted, afterwards). You still can realistically expect the ship converted as a whole by Day 10 or so, but if there are no evidences, there's no real reason to go all paranoid on everyone around you who's able to keep their semblance of humanity.

If you know, then make yourself as much nuissance, as possible without outright killing yourself. Attack your guards. Find out patterns of activity of people who come to heal/comfort you and save APs to attack them or break through guard as they need to drop their guarding down for a moment to let the healer/shrink inside your prison cell. Hell, you can even give false claims of your health/morale to force people to come to you. Keep your eyes peeled for opportunities to steal anything important or weapons. If you're a pilot, hijack patroller, Pasiphae or even Icarus. If you're a technician dismantle things that mushes need...
There are always things to do, you just have to watch for opportunities and think outside the box.
Granted, if you're not much active or active only at predictable times, this all is much harder to do. But after all, that's probably why you ended up as a chosen pet.

But the truth is, last human is basically holding the whole Mush Eden goal as hostage. Mushes need to devote considerable amount of time, resources and organizing to keep them alive. Keeping themselves hidden eleviate part of this burden by human taking care of his own well-being, but makes it harder by the need to keep their own human facade. Add the amount of work needed to convert whole ship and you see that mushes did a really great job.
This all considered, I generally feel like mushes kinda deserve a human pet that will play according to rules and won't spoil the fun by giving up and either suiciding or start truly cooperating.
And if you don't feel like the last human deserve their fate, then consider the fact that if they did better job at mush hunting, they wouldn't be in their situation.
