Heyas Slash! We have a questions thread, Q &amp; A #12 (Read the first post!) and you can ask everything you think of there, and chances are active interested people will trip over themselves fighting to be the first to answer you.

Every ship you play will be very different; sometimes there are very quiet ships. I'm sorry if you're on one now, because I think you'd prefer a highly active and talkative one - your next ship could be exactly that.... or not. Every one is different. Do your best and keep playing, and ask the community for all the help you want, we're here even if we're not on your specific ship this time.

You asked two specific questions:

what are bronze silver and gold?

Those are types of players with some different options when they play.

Players who have never spent money on the game (or never had someone give them the right sort of gift) are always bronzes. Bronze players start the game with a Trackie and a Talkie, always unless doing tutorial (if tutorial, they get the items at the end of tutorial). They get 1 XP a cycle played, and they have just one skill choice (though they can read a skill book and have potentially 2 skills on a ship).

Silver players are players who at one time have spent money on the game, or were given the right sort of gift, but the time the money bought is over. They start the game with a single combined Trackie/Talkie item (so they get both functions for only one item slot), and they can take up to two skills (A skill book can give them a third).

Gold players are players who currently have bought gold, or used a gift of gold days (there are a few kinds). If they were actually only given gold days, they will revert to bronzes when that time is over - otherwise they stay gold until the time is used, then revert to Silver. Some gold players keep paying for more gold time, and a few even give gold to promising bronzes on ships they like, and share the options they have. Gold players get 2 XP a cycle change, and have up to 4 skills, as well as the option to read a skill book for a 5th skill. Additionally, they have a max health and max morale that is 2 higher than bronze or silver players (16 instead of 14) and start with one point higher morale (8 instead of 7). they also have the combined iTrackie device.

You also asked:
How do you assassinate someone?
That is done through the 'kick off' action, you can see when you click on another player; if you use that to remove their last health points, or sometimes if you carry the knife, blaster, or one of the other guns that have to be built from blueprints or researched from the blaster to remove their last health point. The options to use those weapons vary, but all are clearly aggressively named actions. Most (every?) weapon has a chance of doing an instant kill, if it does the cause of death is not assassinated, but if you don't do the instant kill but remove their last health point, you will have 'assassinated' them.
