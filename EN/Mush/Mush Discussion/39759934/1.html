Well, here's my 2 cents, which seem vastly different from yours.

Assumes bronze players, but not necessarily level 1, so skills beyond the lvl 1 one will be accounted for in assesment:

Difficulty 1 Task can be explained in approximately a paragraph to a new player during play.
Chao/Derek: Both of them are a gun for Chun to point at people, then fire. Chances are they'll wrestle someone or fight fires, Chao especially has pretty low AP anyway so not much action. Simple instructions and anyone can do it, as long as they listen.
Paola/Frieda: While their tasks are technically important, they aren't very complicated. Furthermore, someone will pretty much go build a bed for you and you never have to leave your workplace ever again, never deal with dirtiness (unless you eat) etc. Takes very little to tell Frieda to ditch any planet with Mangarok/Seismic/Volcano and to tell Paola whether to click the NERON Button or the Xyloph Button and on which day to click the Rebel Base button. Plus Comms just ends completely at some point.

Difficulty 2 Some independent tasks/duties, or some danger involved.
Roland: Level 1 you're a Sprinter, you move stuff around the way people ask you to. If you've picked up other skills like Pilot, you just do that. Not much to say or do. You need to learn or know one task and complete it, but there's plenty others that could do them too. Also you're not really a target for anything.
Gioele: Install cameras once, or push people around. If you've made it all the way to level 4 but are still new, Astro is still easy to be instructed in. Gioele merely places Difficulty 2 because he is second in command, and thus an interesting conversion target for Mush if they can manufacture Jin Su's Death.
Stephen: Pretty much same as the above, with the slight difference that you'll have to be good enough to convince people you're not sporing food all the time you stand in the refectory, and that people will expect you to freeze alien steaks in time before they spoil and things like that. If you're high enough to have Apprentice you really aren't new anymore and don't need help.

Difficulty 3 Palpable danger or independent work of some complexity
Ian: Ian is the first person to actually be in charge of their own station, the Gardens. They can be a huge boon and they require input and effort every day, aswell as expeditioning to fully bloom. I would always suggest to be the Botanist as anyone else is unlikely to be, but Researcher Ian is also equally ranking here, as research has some complexity, and involves some danger from the Mush. Also managing your dirtiness from the gardening, or at the very least knowing about the Apron and relaying is required.
Finola: Finola researches and heals people, she's the greatest asset to research and as such needs to be competent, but most of all is a big mush target.
Hua: Hua can be many things, and as such I put her right in the middle, as her tasks can be easier or more difficult depending on what skill you have.
Kuan Ti: You need to know how NERON works and how to not get spiked to hell and stay clean.
Terrence: Resident Robot Expert or Tech, most likely. Special movement rules require smart management and dirtiness can be an issue. Also probably the one person most in charge of managing the ships Scrap Income and how its used.

Difficulty 4 High value mush targets or resorts crucial to survival or victory
Raluca: Technically an easy task. You fix PILGRED, sometimes relay NERON. Having to stay out on your own, generally being dirty, and being a really high value target for mush conversion complicate things however.
Andie: Can be a lot like Finola, but can be so much more. Diplomat is really high value for expeditions and especially an Eden win, and thus is a high value target. Versatility requires expertise.
Eleesha: Primary investigator due to her skills. Smart use of skills is required as a Mush Hunt is the single most difficult task, unlike any of the mechanical ones, as it is a social one. You need to know how and what to search and what is suspicious, and you need to know how to not be suspicious yourself, as not only are you a high value target, but humans are likely to be rather afraid of you specifically being turned, and your AP use is hard to track, sometimes adding to that.

Difficulty 5 These people run the ship, are integral to operations and are #1 targets
Jin Su: De Jure Commander, as in starts with the title, needs to decide on missions. Also present on every expedition, and all in all has a lot of crucial skills, be it speeches for morale or a pilot that needs to wisely manage his AP between Hunters, mission-giving and expeditions. Also the single highest target for Conversion by Mush, and 2nd highest on the kill list if Gioele is Mush. Should also never ever be dirty if he wants to live.
Chun: The de facto Commander. Chun's word is the only one with guaranteed integrity, any decision goes through her and what she says goes. Primary decider over life and death and confidante to everyone. Needs superb knowledge of the game and its mechanics and a knack for people, plus knowing how to help out and where while doing the most to lead the ship and find the Mush, while potentially being tied to the Lab for a while. Also keeper of the Hacker Kit.
Janice: Primary Morale Source and tied for highest value kill target with Chun. Also NERON Admin, which opens another can of worms when it comes to unexpectedly disrupting the ship. Should always, always be in 100% safety when not on the move to desperately comfort someone, which in itself should be a calculated move.
