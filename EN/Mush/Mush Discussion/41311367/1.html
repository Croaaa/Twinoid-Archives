ZaphodBeeblbroxIf you are killed because you refuse the food test or any other humanity test, that is reportable

That's news to me.

My understanding of the relevant rules is that no one may be 'forced' to take a human test. It's most certainly not sanctionable to refuse to take one. However, it is possible to receive a sanction for attempting to force others to take food tests or any other humanity tests or mush tests.

There is an extremely narrow range of circumstances under which 'mush testing' is appropriate. Essentially, if you have enough proof that someone is a mush that you (and ideally others) are ready to kill that person, then you may elect instead to offer a 'mush test' instead of outright killing them, if you feel it is appropriate. It is inappropriate to 'offer' or 'require' 'mush testing' because someone is a bit suspicious. Pressure to 'mush test' is generally inappropriate.

That pressure is also often dangerous. If your target is a real mush and they believe themselves caught, this will often trigger a rampage. Granted, the rampage typically proves them mush - but possibly at high cost.

Furthermore, refusal to participate in any form of 'mush testing' is absolutely not permitted to be considered as evidence that someone is mush. The results of a mush test can be used to -clear- someone, but refusal to cooperate with 'mush testing' is not permitted to be considered as proof for or against a person being mush. If you did not have a lot of evidence against the person before they refused to prove themselves human - you still do not if they refuse to give that proof.

People have in the past been reported for killing others without sufficient evidence, with or without also trying to mush test the individual in some way (these kills are sometimes called 'random kills' or 'nearly random kills'; ships with players doing a large number of them are often called 'murderships'... and they rarely lead to human wins). The line between the behavior of those "Actively Griefing" and those participating in the killing or wide-scale 'forced testing' of multiple suspicious players becomes exceedingly fine. I myself don't recommend risking the sanction, because the damage you're doing to your ship usually is barely distinguishable - and forcible mush testing, especially forcible mass mush testing is not "O.K.". Even if you can be distinguished from 'any other griefer', you're still breaking rules by randomly killing with or without attempting forcible mush testing too.

My advice: If someone (or a group) tries to pressure you or someone (or everyone) on your ship to participate in mush testing - please report them, the way you would any other rule breaker. You're welcome to discuss the rules, including offering links or short quotes, upon your ships as well if you think it's useful to the circumstance. That may or may not help your immediate situation.

Note, if there is significant evidence that you're probably mush and a mush test is offered to you (or forced upon you - I'm now referring to being shot repeatedly with the Natamy Rifle), and then you are killed when you refuse the test or fail it - this describes a legal kill. If there is enough evidence to kill or cure you, but the players with this evidence choose to offer a test before killing or curing you - this is the extremely narrow range of circumstances when a mush test is appropriate to offer.

The flip side - if a person decides to prove themselves human, they may within the rules unless the method they choose can be shown to be harmful to their team. The case will be much clearer if the player has done additional 'harmful to their team' activities. Hopefully your ship will not be filled with 13 players (all but Chun and the mush) all eager to prove themselves human the first chance they get; that might be a difficult circumstance.... except the means to do that much human testing early in play probably doesn't exist and may -greatly- harm the ship over time if tried.
