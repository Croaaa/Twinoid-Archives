crawlkill

Stephen can be very useful when he's a) already level 5 and b) identifies and copies a critical skill early on in the game. He allows for some fascination combinations, like having two Paranoids. Apprentice isn't at all unbalancing, though. There's no single skill that, when doubled, fundamentally changes the ship.

Apprentice should probably be his first skill, not his fifth, so that his "uber-generalist" role is emphasized. I'd even like to see Chef be a passive default for him, since the only thing most ships ever use it for is to freeze alien steaks. He's a bad case of "really bad until he becomes really interesting" in terms of character progression. I doubt if any newbie would ever blindly blunder far enough along to find the point at which he gets interesting--they just get to feel useless when they play him, not realizing his potential.

But even that potential doesn't make him any better than any other character. Everyone with a skill he might meaningfully unique-copy has other skills that make them better than him. He could copy LogEx from Jin Su, but he wouldn't be a Leader, a Pilot or a Strateguru (or a shooter). Ditto with Paola: her whole skillset sucks, but she starts out comms officer and her LogEx is really important to running the ship, so on him, it's much less valuable, unless he ends up comms. He can copy Shrink, but then he won't be a Diplomat. He could copy Polyvalent, but he won't be Devoted or a Pilot (and two Devoteds on one ship would be terrible anyway).

Apprentice is an awesome skill, and the rest of Stephen's skills range from situational (Solid) to weak (Cook). But it doesn't unbalance a ship in any way. Apprentice should be his first skill, to make people excited about playing him straight away. If you're a Bronze with Apprentice, it makes you highly teachable, which is valuable for Twinoid. If I regularly saw bronze first-timer Stephens who started with Apprentice, you'd better believe I'd devote more attention to em than I already do.
