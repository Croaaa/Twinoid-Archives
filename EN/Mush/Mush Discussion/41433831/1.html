I can't fix the wreck problem, not fully, and not quickly. I can try to fix it, partially and slowly, and I am Maybe this post can even, in a tiny way, be a part of that process. When you play well, you're also part of helping fix the problem, and so too, every time you report when you see rules broken, that is part of fixing the problem as well.

What I can do in this thread now:

1) offer some of my personal thoughts on what you're really buying if you choose to spend money here;

2) try to show some comparisons to real life 'wrecks that we pay for' but still get good value from (and a lot of that perception of good value is where our focus is); and

3) I can discuss some strategies that I have used to get enjoyment from ships that could be called wrecks.

1) I've chosen, for now, to be a gold member. I've been one for most of the time I've played. That's my choice, and for me I feel like I'm getting my money's worth from the game - which is why I continue to pay.

I feel like I could play for free or pay, and get almost all of the 'core' of the game either way; full access to whatever ship I happen to jump on and whatever happens upon that ship. What my money adds for me is more options and freedoms for my personal playstyle - and I have that, until death, upon any ship I join, even the 'wrecks'. After death, I may join a new ship as rapidly as I choose to - and I may continue to do this unless the whole game has technical difficulties or I end up quarantined for my actions (which I can then discuss and possibly resolve with a reversal of that decision - but if I try to learn and follow the rules I can expect to never be quarantined).

So my money doesn't buy good ships, it buys more choices and faster progression for me on whatever ship I connect to, as well as a bit more survivability while I'm there. But it doesn't buy a good ship or a bad one - that I get for free just for deciding to play. It's true, I don't get to choose to turn off my 'gold time' if I decide it's being wasted - but I knew that when I bought it, and I knew the rules prohibited surrender before I bought that gold.

I even have an option to 'sort of turn gold on and off when I want to', there's something called MT passes, and you can purchase those and redeem a small number of them for a small number of gold days. I don't think it's the most cost economical of the options, but this choice isn't best for 'thriftiness', it's best for giving a player who chooses it 'the greatest possible control over when their gold days are used or not while playing'.


2) If we purchase tickets to a sporting event or concert, the event may go better or worse than we hoped. The game could end up being filled with bad calls from the umpires and/or bad plays from the teams and/or terrible injuries could happen; our chosen team could have a tragic loss under very upsetting conditions. The game could be described as a wreck, though our money is still spent upon it. It's even possible that another guest watching the game could choose to break laws and throw things onto the field or otherwise be disruptive; even many of them could. There's legal processes that try to stop this behavior as best and fast as possible.

There are some things which we can spend money on and then be sure of getting a controlled and expected return for that purchase, but events open to the public as a whole are usually never among them. This game is very much 'open to the public', and its designed to focus on teamwork - or lack of that, as a primary factor in successful or unsuccessful win conditions. The type of game this is, and that it is a multiplayer game is indeed a factor that you would wisely consider before you decide to pay, or to continue paying for the game.

3) I dream of going to Eden, especially on 'random ships'. For me, a great ship, a really great ship, is one that seems to be a band of strangers who interact with each other aboard the ship and come to some agreements, develop respect and appreciation for various things about each other, who work out compromises and together figure out what needs to be done and how to do it given the current challenges and options, and "Against the Odds" manages to become a great team that can smoothly and enjoyably worth towards all the needed goals.

Heh, I smile just thinking about my past ships which were like that; and to be honest, that sort of ship is what the good of Eden is to me. For me I guess it's a 'win' if I get to play on that sort of ship, even if we don't make it to Eden or even get a chance to seriously try to.

A lot of ships never get to Eden; many never even get slightly close. From very early in play, it becomes clear that a dream of Eden for this ship is almost impossible; that dream needs to be scaled down.

But do you know why you play, you personally? Before you discovered this game, I bet you were not dreaming of a chance to meet the wincon of whatever team you end up playing in this game. Playing this game is a way to get what you actually want from the game, and for each of us that's likely to be slightly different (sometimes very different).

Assuming you want a goal that can be included in "positive cooperative play" (well, what most griefers want cannot be included in that!), there's probably nothing wrong with seeking your real goal on every ship you play.

Even the 'wrecks'. For me.... maybe this ship in particular cannot become a full team of people who work together wonderfully.... but is there even one other player that I can try to work with and build high quality teamwork and goal setting and achieving with? Sure, those goals need to be scaled into line with what a small number of people can accomplish together - but that's a very acceptable thing to do, both in the game rules and in satisfying why I play.

On most of my ships I've been able to develop what I want from my play here, though sure, I've had to be flexible and willing to adjust the scale of what I wanted from the ship, based on what was possible under that ship's conditions and with those people.


Do you know why you play this game, what you hope to feel as you play it, and can you see ways to scale that based on your ship's actual playing conditions? If you can and try to do so... that may make you feel like 'every ship is different' more than 'many ships are wrecked'.
