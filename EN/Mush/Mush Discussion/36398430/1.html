Yes, you can steal the pasi, and you can take any items you think are most likely to get the ship dead when you do.

You do not have to - but you can, and it is not cheating. If you think that's the best way to win, do so.

Another thing you can do, if you wish - is steal the ship, regain your AP on it, make some spores if you wish, and come back down and spore people/attack people/break things/hide things/whatever you think of, then run back to the pasi and take off again. Until they move the ship - and they may decide to try and get you and the items back instead - you are in play and able to take actions, whatever you think of that might help your team win.

There actually is no such thing, in the rules, as 'spiteful mush play' towards humans. You are there to try and make the humans lose, and you have a lot of ways to try and do this. Some are fast, some are slow, some are harder to recover from than others - the point is, as mush, you are allowed (and supposed!) to try and get the humans to lose. That is why you are there, that is the whole point of it all.... Mush cannot grief humans (except by breaking netiquette... play to kill the ship or convert people, but not to insult or harass players), and someone who looks like a griefer to humans is only proven to be a griefer when they actually are proven to be human....

As to your other question, asking for the cure, that's actually the first thing you've asked about that actually is wrong to do, and it is wrong because that is working against the mush side (even if you are the only mush alive right now) and it is surrender to the loss of your side - both are explicitly forbidden by the rules.

Now, if the humans force the cure upon you, while you make choices that try and get a win for your side, that is legal for you and for them. But it is so easy and so fast for a human to cure you, they need 1 AP and to be in the same room with you. So don't ask for it in any way that leads back to you asking for it.

Ironically, you probably could have 'played well' to ask for it, then transfer, so the request came from your old body. That might have fooled your humans into using the serum on the wrong body.... and no one would consider that surrender. But I personally would not believe a mush who claimed to 'want the serum, but only for time to build up AP to act', because if your ship already has the serum.... it takes almost no time to use it. Now, if they don't.... then it gets a lot more grey area.

Remember it's for fun, and enjoy the fear of the hunt if you can .

You are not in trouble if you make mistakes, as long as they are real mistakes.

My first time as alpha mush, I knew my partner wanted to do the slow route. I agreed, then changed my mind. I realized I could demoralize Chun to death for 8 AP (she was bronze, had joined late, and people connected to Sol early) and I knew were Janice was - I intended to make spores, demoralize chun, eat spores make more spores eat spores kill Janice, then survive as long as I could, burning as much human AP as possible to try and cost humans as much as I could. I was Gioele, I'd never been alpha before, I wasn't sure what to do and killing Chun and Janice seemed deadly to me.

The Chun part of plan worked great, I demoralized her to 0 with about 5 minutes left on the cycle change, but when I got to the dorm where Janice had announced she was... Ian was there. Total change of plans, as I couldn't kill with Ian there, so.... I ran to the other dorm.

In there were Hua, who I knew was out of AP that cycle, Jin Su, who had reported about what he had done and had to be very close to no AP that cycle, and Roland, who had reported some confusion about using a turret to fight hunters, but the ship had stopped him after one shot, so I knew he was high AP. I decided that of the three, Roland was the most dangerous to me at the moment given his high AP, so I killed him.

Then I decided I'd better explain to my partner what I was doing, and started to on the mush channel, and it was only as I was typing that so he'd know what had happened and be able to use the confusion most effectively... that I scanned down the mush chat to see that Roland was my partner.

It's a mistake I'll never make again, and I feel very fortunate that if I was reported for playing against my team, it was understood that it was an inexperienced error that would never be happening again, and I never faced sanction for it.

So, Fearpls! Try to enjoy what you are doing, whatever happens, and if troubled thoughts of 'what if' bother you - remind yourself that you're doing better than Impet did her first time as alpha - and you are doing good overall.
