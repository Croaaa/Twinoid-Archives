Summary the main problems of Andie:
1) Newbies.
If you take Finola it's very easy to understand - even if you are first-timer, you always have a skill for research, so you work in lab. Things are much more difficult for Andie - you need to watch your team skills, you need to at least read their posts and better ask them, what do they need and so on. Not to mention, that not every Andie has lvl 3.
2) Benefits
For research everything is simple - you go to lab, spend ap, get profit. You just need a person to do some resets, but it could be anyone, if Ian/Chun are not very active. To get all profit Andie's skills provide you need - active astronavigator to find good planets, active commander to move ship in time, some more active people for landing party. If you have those people idle - 2/3 of Polyvalent is wasted. If your team says something like "oh, we found O2 planet on D2, but let's wait D5 for expo as we can store just 32 O2" (my rage was so great when I heard that, that we finished the ship in bloodbath on same day with my mush partner instead of returning to Sol) - 2/3 of Polyvalent is wasted as well. Ofc in that cases Finola is better.
3) Counting benefits.
A lot of people see expoes like bonus and research like must have.
I can understand them - if only half of their ships do an expo and that expo is just collecting O2 not to die they cannot see how the life becomes easier with items like Magellan Liquid Map or even just Jumpkins. Another problem that every expo is also risk and only possibility to get something nice. So, they see Andie replacing Finola as -good things we know +chance for things most of them don't understand.

Short version:
Finola is quite same useful on every ship - bronze, silver, gold, experienced or not - she do her job.
Andie requires a lot more skill, both from the player who takes her and all other crew members.
