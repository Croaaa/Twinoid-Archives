There are two things that increase difficulty : time and activity.

Activity is the number of AP used by the crew, but it's not that simple. Apparently it is also influenced by the crew's capacity to react to the different dangers and by what happened during the previous cycles or days.

Difficulty also increases with time. There are two stages, one on day 6 and one on day 12, that are visible when you access the command terminal (it says something like "we are travelling in infinite space" after day 12, and I don't remember what it says in English after day 6). It doesn't mean that difficulty increases suddenly on days 6 and 12, that steel plates suddenly start falling every cycle or that hunters are multiplied by two. The increase is gradual.

One "fun" thing to note regarding hunters: Frequent waves can be to your benefit. The longer you go between new waves appearing, the greater the numbers in the next wave will be, once it arrives. This can mean 19-22+ hunters arriving on a day 4 wave, if you only had the initial wave and no others in the interim...I've seen it happen on a few of my previous ships. 12-14 hunters on a day 3 wave is fairly common, under similar circumstances.

Ultimately, having them not arrive all at once (forcing you to kill just what you need to move, while forgoing any scrap collection) is optimal. There is also the benefit to having more scrap to use for BPs and/or drones early on, not to mention that the waves are that much more manageable. Too bad we don't really have any control over how they arrive, but all Pilots should keep this mechanic in mind.
