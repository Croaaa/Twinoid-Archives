I got into my first ship, and i was Jin Su, commander of Daedalus ship which was the hope of mankind salvation. Ironically, i was one of Mush agents. And while crew greeted their captain, i was welcomed by Paola, our communication officer, on a private mush channel.

We started small, hiding items, sabotaging, earning the trust of our fellow ship mates. Then when we made sure we are alone, and far away from curious eyes and cameras, we started sporing. By the end of our first day, we had Janice join us. She was all alone in front corridor, and was an easy target.

I trapped food with spore, Paola infected poor Schrödinger. Janice helped us infect too. So it was pretty soon we welcomed our friendly cook, Stephen. We lacked a bit cooperation about going anonymous, so they figured out there were 3 of us. The thing is that was only partially right, since in truth we were a crew of 5 at that point. Fiona joined the cause.

And after that, the plan was made. We were lacking oxygen, food you name it. And they had no special suspect, as we maintained our cover, helping them here and there. So i was pressed to take us to the planet that had oxygen, and i agreed. Scans were made, fuel was put in combustion chamber. Supplies was being readied for expedition. I put a list of four humans that will go. It included a pilot, chief security (for obvious reasons) and two more (that could benefit the team). I couldnt be the pilot as i "had no ap or mp". When they launched on Icarus, with promises of supplies was the moment plan set in motion. They saw shocked, Daedalus exiting planet orbit, jumping away , leaving them and Icarus to their deaths. Raluca tricked, put the fuel by my orders, signing their deaths, not knowing. The same moment we left orbit, a blast echoed in the ship, Paola blown up and blasted two people to a total of six dead. Crew was shocked, Raluca was pointing me as a mush, calling to arms, i was telling i made a mistake, and after they read the logs of Paola bloody rampage, they knew she was a mush. So i told them she tricked me to move from orbit, and they believed me. I managed to get them to move from engineering where Raluca was rounding up survivors, to me in the alpha doorms to boost morale with my speech.

Unfortunately Janice and Fiona were not there to help. If they were we would take them in a blink.
We lost our brave Paola who put up a fight. But they could not be saved. They were suffocating, depressed at suicide point and their commander was an alien. Janice and Fiona came. Then in the last stage we all changed to our true shape which made them full of dread. We killed a few more, then weak humans turned to suicide, rather then face us. We taunted them on ship channel. As we were eating Shrodinger Raluca painted the wall with her brains. We were victorious, in 4 days and maybe the first successful mush.

Thanks for my mush team especially Paola. Thanks to our human players which made all this possible.

Absydian

An excellent mush story! If only they were all so bright we might have had a challenge, but I figured I'd share my story here in the hopes of finding a few names of the crew involved in our awesome ship.

We start out, a string of "Hello world!" messages appear on our chat screen indicating that indeed most of the players here have not played this before, like myself. We chat, talk about other MT games and the one or two people that know what they are doing attempt to explain the complexities of the game whilst the rest of us pretend to understand. Y'know, the usual first game kinds of stuff.

Obviously, everyone is wondering around, using up valuable AP/MP exploring the ship, eating the food, making out with whatever lifeform will have them when the discussion soon turns to the inevitable "Who is the mush?!?". This is brought about by mostly by the way the one 'known' mush disappears leaving (apparently) 16 healthy human players.

At this point, our veteran points out that there is some kind of anonymous mush skill that causes this (?!?!?). So everyone goes round accusing everyone of being a mush for any slight comment they make but before anything kicks off Stephen attacks Terrence in the kitchen claiming that he spored his food!

Everyone rushes to his aid, a few get caught up in the excitement and attack Terrence, but the intelligent ones consult the event log and realize that it tells a very different story...

Stephen dies soonafter.

Paolo tries to defend him, but soon becomes the second mush to bite the dust.

Hurray! The Daedalus, and therefore all of humanity, are saved! After Terence is healed and the remainder of our dwindling AP is spent randomly firing guns in the general direction of the hunters, the crew are soon alerted by Neron that a crewmate has suicided.

PANIC!

"What is this morale stat and how do we improve it!?!?!?" The veteran player is inundated with a tidal wave of questions and tries to control the hysteria with some sound advice, which we newbs soon condense down into: "Copulate with everything that moves!"

Fortunately, due to the chief scientist carrying out some thorough beta-tests involving the blaster; Schroedinger was no longer with us, and was not a victim of the ensuing mass orgy.

When every last camera had been taken down from the dorms, and every last AP spent on flirting and "doing the thing", Neron kindly alerted us to the fact we had less than a day's supply of oxygen.

PANIC!

AGAIN!

After the veteran had imparted more wisdom to the plethora of questions, we started acting as a team; scanning planets, taking care of the plants, communicating, safe in the knowledge that all the mush aboard were exterminated.

With only 7 cycles to go before asphyxiation, 5 planets scanned, non of which contained oxygen, I composed a message. A message that would get the crew motivated, a do-or die plan which could, very easily go wrong, but I didn't know better at the time... So, I turned on caps lock and wrote my simple, yet (un)effective strategy:

PANIC!

I wanted to head for the nearest unscanned planet, and hope that it would have something that would save us from our imminent demise. With the crew not knowing anything, but going on the pure instinct that dying was probably a bad thing, they agreed that doing something was better than doing nothing.

With the veteran drowning in a torrent of panicy, capitalized noobishness, everyone sort of... well, we fell apart, disagreeing with parts of the plan, not knowing which way to go, not knowing who needs to be where and do what.

With only five cycles left all seemed lost, but then, out of nowehere, over the walkie talkie came the ecstatic voice of Frieda:

"WOAH WOAH WOAH! OXYGEN IN SIGHT! WEST, 5 FUEL! THERE'S STILL HOPE!"

Everything came together; the flight commander went to the bridge, the technician went to the fuel container and extracted fuel; a pilot, a botanist, a security officer and a psychiatrist who just sort of happened to be in Icarus bay at the time, all prepared to venture into the unknown on a last ditch attempt to save humanity. Could we pull it off?

2 cycles left before our imminent doom and the Daedalus set a new course to a promising planet; but we weren't their yet... A tense 3 hour wait followed where the anxious crew tried to defy their real-life tiredness and lunch plans in an attempt to see the Daedalus through another day.

1 cycle's worth of oxygen left. Could this rag-tag team of newbs pull off the seemingly impossible and retrieve the ever so important oxygen? Roland took off with his three comrades and...

"This module is not open yet, come back soon!"

Now confusion is mixed with the unfading panic to obliterate our shredded nerves, but the game still says "expedition in progress." Another 90 tense minutes pass by until finally:

Oxygen supplies are at 25 cycles! Hurray! We did it! against all odds (if I could be bothered to calculate them) we saved the ship!

Now we just need to worry about the incoming swarm of hunters, the fires that keep breaking out, the various pieces of broken equipment, the hull that is held together by blue tac and fairy dust, the multiple sicknesses that we picked up from the alien planet, the dire food shortage and the ever-present morale issues... but other than that, long live humanity!

I hope you enjoyed reading our ongoing saga. Great thread, I want to hear more stories about this awesome new game.

-Abs
