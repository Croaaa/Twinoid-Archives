If you don't understand how you gained those points or believe there was a misunderstanding, there's an appeals/discussion process.

If you understand how you gained those points and intend to continue behave in that fashion, then predictably you're likely to gain an ever increasing number of them, until you or others who have power over your play decide that you're not going to play anymore.

If you understand how you gained those points and have no intention of behaving in a rule breaking fashion again - you have nothing to worry about. If you do accidentally break a rule in the future, start talking about it fast. Explain what happened to the ship and why it happened, which also will be part of the log for the judges to see as they figure out what happened and if it is something that should be punished or not - should you be reported for the error. Continue to behave well into the future and it doesn't matter how many points you have.

Bear in mind, if it's been more than 72 hours since you got the sanction, it's too late to get the ball rolling on an appeal. Also bear in mind that it can take some time for us to review the case, and no appeal is guaranteed to be granted.

On the other hand, even if the ruling is upheld, it's good to understand what you did to get reported in the first place, and to learn how to avoid doing that in the future. We know the automated report messages are...vague, to say the least - but that's why you should find out from us what went wrong.

As an example, a Mush may have been reported for violating Neutral Play, while they were still human. They might think they played their mush role well, and that could very much be the truth. However, they're still responsible for holding an item or items prior to Mush selection. If they don't ask about it in the Mod inbox, they might never know what it was they did wrong.
