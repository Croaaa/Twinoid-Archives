Yes, the slogan is a parody of Alien. But Mush is based on many other things, such as "Battlestar Galactica: the Board Game" or the French board game "Novembre Rouge", itself derived from the popular party game "Les Loups-garous de Thiercelieux" based on "Mafia".

The game contains many other direct or indirect references to other movies, games or science-fiction stories (or even mythology with Daedalus, Icarus and Pasiphae). For instance, Neron's eye is a reference to HAL 9000 and its behavior is the same as GLaDOS. There are also some references to other Motion-Twin games: for instance, the action takes place in the AlphaBounce universe, some drugs are similar to those in Die2Nite, etc.

rumataRead the thanks part of the Help, Bugzilla was involved in it deep. This is probably more credit than I deserve. A bit more background about that: I was part of the first beta-testers of the French version. During the development of the game, the admins told us that Jin Su would be able to make some motivational speeches to improve the morale of the crew. They asked the players to write some short speeches and I was among the 12 players whose texts were selected. The fact that these texts were written by different authors explains why these speeches use very different styles: some of them are funny, others are more dramatic, etc.

Some other bits of trivia: the early beta was more like a pre-alpha version. The game has evolved a lot during that rather long testing phase, with many new features developed or re-designed according to the feedback of the players. For instance, there were no nice isometric views: all we had for the rooms and corridors were hand-drawn pencil sketches in first-person perspective. The map of the Daedalus was in 2D and looked very different from what we have now, although all rooms were already connected in the same way (except for the front turrets that had only one exit). Also, at some point the characters were named agent 1, agent 2, ... agent 16.

There are more things in the crewmembers themselves... I wanted to ask that for a while:


First off, it is obvious that references exist in the crew from Stephen Seagull who, himself, is a nod to the Fishmaster (which, btw, was also referenced in Magicka).


Then we have Wang Chao, the trigger-happy killer with a bad attitude. Considering that "Chao" is his first name, and probably pronounced similar to "show" (as in Lorewalker Cho), we have a "Cho Wang", which is not too far from Lo Wang.

And the new character Andie bears some similarities to Schrödinger from Hellsing. - Down to the fact that his/her gender is undefined (probably until you look ).
This one may be far fetched, but being that you will find the latter in a picture search for "Schrödinger", MT might have been inspired.

And there may be more...


Finally, I just found that the "NCC" is a congress of contact lens manufacturers (as well as, of course, the letter code of the Enterprise).

And the Lizaro Jungle is a weapon from Mini Troopers.
