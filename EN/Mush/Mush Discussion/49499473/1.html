Mushpedia says
First look for a planet. If it has 6 to 12 zones, proceed.
- If fewer than 6, delete it. Generally a very small planet has low chance of having what your ship needs, but in times of desperation this rule can be flexible.
- Conversely, an extremely huge planet may not be worth proceeding to analyze if you need to get to a planet quickly, and is more likely to have dangerous zones as well as resources. Keep in mind that an average expedition covers 9 zones.
The 9 zones limit(not considering Sprinters) is merely a practical thing due to the number of zones an expedition can cover. Which is a rather weak argument not to analyze bigger planets. Starting a 2nd expedition has almost never been an issue for me.

There are other things more important factoring in like fuel(2 zones for 9+1 fuel? yeah...) and current oxygen level(smaller planets are faster to scan in times of need) and how many zones you get per scan(20 zones under best conditions(Astro+priority+QS) are just one AP-bar(including scanning), while it's ~2 full AP-bars for a non-Astro(assuming priority, but no QS).

I'd agree with Mushpedia, that the best planets are roughly between 6-12 zones, but you very often have to settle for some kind of compromise. The need of oxygen, the amount of fuel(basically the ships ressources) and the time(planets get bigger(and therefore more dangerous) and you'll find 90% 10+ planets(bit exaggerated, but you get the picture)) should influence your decision as well.
