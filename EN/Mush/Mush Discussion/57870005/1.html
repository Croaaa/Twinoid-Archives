But if the ship explodes, mush hunt or not, then the game is over.

Why generalize ? It all depends on the situation of the ship. Ideally, the crew should work both on projects and research, but they can't always do that. Ships without Finola often need generalists to work in the lab, because there are no biologists, or when there are, then have other jobs to do (Andie cannot replace Finola entirely, especially when he's also a pilot and has to help fighting hunters). So, instead of the usual lab team, more people have to come and help, and that means less relays on projects. But as Aceb said, other crews are more efficient on projects.

Neglecting research is not good, because without Antispore gas and Constipaspore serum in particular, contamination is too easy for the Mush. But neglecting projects is not good either. Some ships desperately need projects. For instance I've seen Plasma shield save several ships that were in a very bad shape, and other projects can have a huge impact too. Sometimes the crew even needs to put both projects and research aside in order to focus on something else, for instance scanning.

You just need to adapt your strategy to each ship and its crew, depending on who is active, on the crew members' skills, on the needs of the ship, on the most urgent problems. And it's good to have someone who gives instructions to the crew, telling them who should work on what depending on the priorities of each day. If nobody tries to organize the crew, some people don't know what to do and spend their AP on the wrong things at the wrong moment. Others stick to their character's role because they consider that each character has a fixed job, without being concerned of the current situation (for example lots of people consider that only astros should scan, even when there are no astros or when the ship badly needs oxygen). Those players need someone who analyses the situation and tell the others exactly what to do.
