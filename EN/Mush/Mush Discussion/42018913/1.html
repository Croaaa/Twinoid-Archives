We have discussed this before, PatrykSzczescie - the rules trump anyone's opinions. Whether or not you think it is "fair" is besides the point. The cured Mush have the right (within the rules) to say nothing, and the remaining humans MAY kill the cured Mush if they stay silent. Both actions are considered legal. That doesn't mean the humans must kill the cured Mush, but they sure as hell have the option.

If we forced the cured Mush to always reveal who the other Mush were, there are a number of players who would consider that to be "unfair", too. Many of them, as humans, don't want the Mush hunt to be ended so quickly. That is why this compromise in the rules was reached. That's as fair as it gets, because the cured Mush has the option to say everything, or say nothing. The humans have the option to kill the cured Mush, if they say nothing, or if they say anything intentionally misleading; telling lies or half truths could lead to a sanction on top of that.

The point is, it is fair, all opinions aside.
