There's a feel I get from mush games, that it tries to emulate your characters being 'cut off' from most outside help.

There you are, with 15 other strangers, or fewer as people start to die.

Help isn't coming. Despite the problems that you face.... You are help. You 16, with 2 of you traitors and everything falling apart around you, so much pressure on you.... you are the only hope.

There's no one that can help you. No matter what else goes wrong, once you're on that ship, it's you and those other 15 people for good or worse. Probably worse, if you and they can't pull a win out of the air... there's not going to be a win.



Real life throws stuff like that at people sometimes, rare emergency situations. It's actually one of the things I love most about mush, because of that simulation.... it's rare to find especially with 'known unknown' traitors also thrown into the mix.

While mechanically the idea of replacing inactives sounds sweet, it actually goes against that feeling of 'help isn't coming, we're the only help there possibly is', that is so bittersweet and so important to be ready for, if you're ever stuck in real life, and ooh.... well. You and these strangers get whatever you face fixed, or you're all dead.

The_Lioness

I suggested this in the Suggestions/Ideas and Opinions 2 thread but I will share my idea here as well.

I have learned a lot from more experienced players, yet think players should be grouped on ships based on number of games played. People who have played under 10 games could have a specialized explanatory game format with USEFUL tips and hints. It could go something like 0-10, 11-20, 25-50,50+ It's bad for newer players to get chewed out by less forgiving Mush veterans, yet the first few games are important for establishing future play - you want them to come back, right?

I also think there should be a system to ping people for inactivity. Not as a punishment, but to disperse the amount of regular inactives per ship. This would help with inactives who are people who play a few games and never play again, as they'd never get past the 10 game minimum to progress. The more you play, you get grouped with more experienced players and encounter fewer inactives.
