It is just a rumour and has never been proven. Actually, I have seen several quiet cycles after GPU triggered, which tends to prove the opposite. At least it proves that GPU doesn't just delay the events, or there would always be something in the next cycle, which isn't the case.

The rule you mention is true (cycle events are partly based on the previous cycles, so if nothing happens, it increases the chances of having bad events). However, it is wrong to say that nothing happens when GPU triggers, because it only triggers when something happens : first the RNG decides of cycle change events, then GPU cancels them. That's why it triggers much less often in the first days, and then more and more often. So you can't say that GPU increases the chance of bad events, since it doesn't work that way.

Besides, GPU cycles mean no fires to extinguish, no one to heal, no repairs, no extra comforting, in other words less AP spent. It should make things a little easier, since difficulty is also based on the number of AP spent by the crew.


What do you mean by "double steel-plating" ? It is impossible to be steel-plated twice in the same cycle. As for having two or more people being steel-plated at the same time, that's perfectly normal on long ships.

There are reasons why it is difficult to prove.

Well...what to prove in the first place?

Everyone seems to agree that calm cycles increase chances of something happening the following cycles.

So if gpu triggering is treated exactly like a calm cycle, then the chances for accidents are the following cycle indeed slightly increased. But probably not very noticable, otherwise it were not still be in state of rumour.

On other side if game would check something happening when gpu triggers, then the cycle after Gpu triggering should be even nicer than without gpu triggering (because RNG thinks we got already bad stuff). Right - you follow my logic?

Sooo conclusion: check just Gpu triggerings which happen in a row and observe the following cycle afterwards, if you notice more calm cycles, then gpu would be great since it would make game believe something happened and is furthermore decreasing chances of accidents the following cycles then.

Unfortunately this is not the case according to my (few, but well observed) experiences.
I think that game is still considering a gpu cycle exactly like a cycle at which nothing happened. And yeah, especially in early days it is really regular that there are calm cycles in a row, so even after gpu triggering it may very well follow nothing.
In late days you should maybe pray if you have 2 or more gpu events in a row. Just one gpu event will still not be a really noticable difference for the following cycle though.
The problems are just the triggerings in a row I think.
