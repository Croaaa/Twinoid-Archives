Wikipedia as well, it is not a perfect source. In this case it is not actually extremely clear, if it states that one may not hold 'vital items'... depending on the definition of vital item, that is incorrect. The actual rule is that no ship item may be held before mush selection. Ship items are defined by items you did not personally enter play already carrying, and were not given during the tutorial. So you may legally hold your trackie, talkie, itrackie; if you have a skill that places items in your inventory such as paranoid or green thumb, you may continue to hold those items before mush selection without breaking any rules. All other items are forbidden, by rule, to be held before mush selection; all other items are what I consider to be 'ship items'.

Now, here's evidence that it actually is a rule, that holding items before mush selection is forbidden: This is a stickied post, called SDF Rules, Explanations, Quarantine! listing many (but not all) of the rules: SDF Rules, Explanations, Quarantine!

I'll quote a trimmed portion most relevant to this question:

Meadow

What is valid to report?

You can report players who do not respect the rules of Mush .
Below is a list of not exhaustive things you can report other players for:
~ Hiding or holding any items before the ship is full and Mush are selected.

I'll also offer the best and lawful alternative I've been able to think of, if you really, really want to hold something after mush selection and want to try and make sure that you, and no one else gets ahold of it -

You may move the items you want to control after mush selection to the room you intended to stay in and drop them there, then pick them up after mush selection, even immediately after it, without breaking any rules. You are not required to post that you have seen or moved these items.

Doing this gives other players a potential chance to take or move these items instead of you having them for sure.

If another player does move items that you are trying to control, you may respond to this in any legal fashion you wish.

But that possibility, that another player may move those items before mush selection or possibly grab them after mush selection before you can is why doing this does not break any rules, because this action does not give one side or one player the advantage of true control over any item from before mush selection.

Raluca can pick up the cat as soon as she spawns, declare her usual turret off limits, move the cat there, and drop it. She is discouraged from holding the cat pre-selection, but moving is allowed. There is no rule stating that the cat must stay in the Lab, or in any other room, pre- or post-selection. We also acknowledge that if the cat dies, Raluca is uniquely harmed by ts death. This ruling is designed to factor in the special circumstances of that situation, while still maintaining Neutral Play for the whole of the ship.
