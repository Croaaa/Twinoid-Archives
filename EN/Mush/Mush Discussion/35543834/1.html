There are a few safeguards against this:

1. Have Chun keep the Hacker Kit in her inventory, assuming she's active. She can still provide it in an emergency, if impending doom creeps up on you. Assuming Chun isn't the one to initiate a harmful return, that only leaves the Commander to be aware of.

2. A person who initiates a "harmful return" can be reported. I would define it as a jump to Sol that is decided on their own, without consulting the crew, AND when the ship is in no imminent danger, OR has not suffered any recent setbacks to prompt such a quick decision. Basically, it's a premature end to the game, when the game could have continued for much longer.

Someone who just wants to end their ship so they can join a community jump (as Andre stated) may initiate a harmful return, and if reported, it may result in sanctions and even quarantines for those involved. The SDF also considers a harmful return as a form of suicide, when one person just gives up, but instead takes the rest of the ship along with them.

Also note that there are exceptions to this...there may be pressing circumstances that cause (for lack of a better term) panic, and this usually stems from the death of one or more active crewmembers in a cycle. I've been on more than one ship where the death of a highly active member caused us to say, "Let's cut our losses before we lose anyone else!", followed by a quick jump to Sol. But even then, the crew online are to be consulted, and as Impet stated, you should talk about it in advance, so the crew knows when such a jump could be initiated. It's fine if you even plan a specific cycle to jump well in advance, and set the conditions for an early return if things go very badly before then. We highly encourage that discussion, because it's an important safeguard, too.

If it's been well discussed in advance, no pressing threats or recent losses have occurred, and a return to Sol is made anyway, consider reporting it, and the SDF will look into it.
