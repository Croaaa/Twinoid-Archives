Most experienced players dismiss glory because it is so obvious that it's poorly balanced/completely broken. Certain characters (Roland, Janice, Hua) have personal glory conditions that trigger often in the course of simply doing their expected jobs, and thus they have the potential to rack up a bunch of points. Other characters have personal glory conditions that trigger only once (such as Paola), or in cases that are so rare you're unlikely to get it on most ships (Eleesha, Andie). Even when the original Mush dev was still working on the game, it seemed clear that fixes to this system were not very high priority. And now he's gone, so I will continue not holding my breath.

It it bothersome? A point system that should really just be ignored for sanity's sake? Of course. But there's nothing we can do about it. I'm like Lauxnos in that I gauge my personal performance on a ship by how many other players threw me a "like" at the end. Even that system is imperfect because people often don't give mush the love they deserve, but we're dealing with a social strategy game (and RPG, for some). Hard to quantify a good performance with points.
