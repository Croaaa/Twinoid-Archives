I agree with you and sympathize with you, EdelRose.

The game requires a lot of play to pick up, and that is poor design. The tool tips do not provide complete information about the function of whatever action might be taken, and that is poor design. The Mush seem to rely upon human play incompetence rather than smart play to win, and that is poor design.

In short, the game appears to be poorly designed.

There is no reason why a player of a character with a specific skill should have to guess about how it works. Skilled people know how their skills work in real life. There is no reason that a Mush should have to learn how their abilities work via trial and error, especially considering that any given player is only Mush 1 ship in 8. And while I can't say that myco-infected humans know how their skills work in real life, in a game setting it's not such a terrible stretch to presume that the Mush should know how their skills work.

The game would be drastically improved by a comprehensive rules description. And also improved by a change to how Mush operate so as to offer some chance to 'win' as a Mush player without requiring that the human players suck.

There isn't that much to do that you can't try out most of what the game has to offer with every character.

Only the comms officer can advance communications, but everyone can access the computer to see "oh, this is where the comms officer does communications deciphering and where everyone establishes the connection to Sol".

Repairing Pilgred is practically a Raluca job, but as for Neron research and lab research, results will vary based not only on the character, but their chosen skill set. Playing Jin Su once, I checked Neron and there was a project I only gave around a 5% contribution for, but after leveling up and selecting skills, my contribution in that project skyrocketed to 15%. Check back frequently and see if you can help out on a project by project basis.

My problem with this game is that it practically requires frequent checking in for discussions (like Die2Nite), but AP production is way too slow and actions way too expensive. Firing the turret may only cost 1 AP, but when it takes an average of 2.5 shots to make one hit, and 2.5 hits to kill one hunter, that's upwards a day of waiting for enough AP to kill ONE hunter. Yes, taking a ship out is better, but 2 AP to launch, 2 AP to land and 2.5 shots to kill one hunter, I'm spending about the same amount of AP one a single hunter unless I plan to stay out in my iron coffin for 2+days or until the ship needs to move.

There's just no feeling of accomplishment. It's like pre-endgame in Die2Nite when everyone needs to stay in and build. I hate those phases in town because of that same lack of feeling of accomplishment. Otherwise, scavenging may be slow going, but every couple of hours you log in, if not for forum games, you have auto-searches giving you random items, and any single search can be a game changer for either your or your town's plans.

This game, I just feel like I'm doing a whole lot of waiting for an accomplishment that never comes. Only game I do more waiting in is Kingdoms, but that game can last a well over a month, so while there's still a lot of waiting, at least in Kingdoms there's enough time to reach milestones and accomplishments.
