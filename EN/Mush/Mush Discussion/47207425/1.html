So over on 'Ask the SDF' I asked the question of whether Eden sleepers are allowed. Kyubey posted this:

Re: Mush sleepers. Can a beta mush play exactly like a human (never spore, never infect/sabotage/break anything, etc) in order to help the crew reach Eden?

You can, if using this strategy gives you reasonable chance to get to Sol/Eden undetected in any of cases you stated.

You cannot do it on ships, when this strategy will get you killed. If you get cured instead of killed, it's much worse fail btw. In these conditions trying to play as human can even earn you a report for surrendering:

- If humans are sure that there are mushes (Eleesha is active human with Tracker or logs of your convertion are visiable in modem)

- If humans suspect there are still mushes (alphas converted you using defractor in dorm duing day change, humans are not sure who exactly is beta, but think it's very likely one was made) and they have no emergecy to rush PILGRED (if humans suspect that there is a mush, but have no pilots and 30 hunters are following, maybe they'll have to rush PILGRED anyway - but it's unlikely for Eden ship)

P.s. A lot of players nowadays think, that doing nothing mushy is unbeatable, even for alpha-msuhes. They are very wrong. It's valid strategy only in some circumstance, that you/other mushes should create for you first. So it's no point to forbid it.

Rather than hijack the SDF thread, I started this one to discuss whether it should be allowed.

I submit that it should not be allowed for mush to play completely human in order to infiltrate Eden. I would argue that it destroys player agency (for both sides,) and turns the end-game into a slow, frustrating, slog which is not what a game should do.

My supporting arguments:

1. For the mush:

I say this because the mush sleeper can do nothing to affect his fate. No room for skilled play, no room for social finesse. Just pure waiting for the humans the humans give up before all other humans get proven by either lame meta-gaming tests like morale-counting or food testing - or RNG-driven process of elimination (and by elimination, I mean you, little mushroom.)

2. For the humans:

Humans continue to needlessly display skill, not because they haven't satisfied all other win conditions, but because the final condition is completely, utterly beyond their control. No amount of sleuthing helps. You just have to resort to the legal forms of mush testing (which I hear a lot of ships do) or wait while the RNG provides the process of elimination. This could mean days of players wasting opportunities pursuing other ships where more fun is happening, simply because one player is forcing the game to drag out, and they're handcuffed to them and not allowed to suicide.

Edit: My logic only applies to random ships of course. Casting ships may agree to whatever 'artificial difficulty' rules they'd like. Randoms don't get that choice, so the ethics of right/wrong apply differently.

EriolAelfwine

You describe it as if it's an easy task to accomplish and I don't think it is. Let's imagine you are an alpha mush in a ship aiming for Eden and therefore active. You need:

1- Convert someone and take anonymush. That's one skill slot less and converting in an active ship can be difficult sometimes. And you need to do the conversion before you are a suspect.
2- Remove Eleesha from the ship. The list can reveal you.
3- Avoid expeditions completely. If you get ill in a zone event you are dead (Mush can't get ill). Hard if you are a diplomat, a pilot, a botanist, etc.
4- Fake your morale points.
5- Fake your hunger status. Remember everytime you eat and when you should be hungry. An active ship will be checking who is eating and when.
6- Avoid jumpkins and justify it. Mush can't get the pumpkin head and it will reveal you.
7- Fake any AP gain from food that the crew gives you. Hard if you are Paola, and Astro, a technician trying to rush Pilgred/projects or fixing that O2 tank that just broke and nobody has AP for it, a researcher or a pilot (counting shots remaining in a patroller after using it is a very common thing). You can use phagocyte for that but that's another skill slot.
8- Avoid taking too many showers. You can take splashproof but that's another skill slot.
And all of this while you get the trust of your crewmembers.

In my opinion, if you haven't converted anyone or rampaged before basic research (ASG, CS, Mycoalarms, Mycoscanner and Mushovore Bacteria) and before securing the weapons, mush are playing in a countdown. The more time it passes, the more confirmed humans will be aboard and the harder will be to do something to prevent them winning. I can't see why that's not a valid tactic. Specially since humans win most of the time.

PS: Same happens if you are beta mush, but just avoiding step 1.
