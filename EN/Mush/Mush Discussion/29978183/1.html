Rain

Looking for people who are dirty often or for long periods helps. Mush get dirty when they make a spore, and unless they have a certain skill they take damage when they shower. Although I imagine with the abundance of exp available at the moment, many mush will have these skills, they will still tend to get dirty more. If they're smart, they'll do it when they are doing legitimate activities to get dirty and say it's from that. If they're not, they'll just walk around the ship dirty until someone notices and questions.

Also, looking for people who are not doing their job... or who are doing it rather poorly. Someone who is in a part of the ship their character has no reason to be falls under this. This could be because they don't know what they're doing, or it could be because they're mush. If they're not asking for help or responding to offers of it, I tend to lean towards the latter.

Keeping an inventory thread is also a good way to find or at least hinder mush. This is more difficult to do, since it requires cooperation among the crew to keep it updated, but if you know that there was a grenade in the rear bravo storage 12 hours ago, two people have been there and now it's missing for no good reason, you have some suspects to look at, at the very least.

Enfilade

From what I've gathered, there are two categories of activity to be mindful of, along with a third to be wary of.

  • Active hindrance: This includes scampering off with, hiding, or destroying vital equipment; wasting oxygen, fuel, and food reserves with little to no benefit; hacking titles without cause; removing cameras without consulting with crew; abandoning crewmembers (either on expo or in patrol craft) outside of dire circumstances (such as imminent asteroid collisions); and violent actions without cause.
  • Mush-skill-related stuff: As Rain mentioned, creating a spore makes Mush dirty, which is why people are usually paranoid about cleanliness. There is also a skill that make Mush good at sabotaging equipment, so keeping track of who goes where is important. Likewise, a Mush with the Traitor skill makes expeditions more likely to encounter disasters, so watch out if someone is too eager to go or if there's a trend of failed missions. Another skill allows them to infect food, which makes the refectory a potential target - be careful about eating unattended precooked food.

The third is the grey area of unhelpful or pointless expenditure of time and energy. The whole point of the game, much like D2N, is to optimize AP to neutralize threats, gather resources, and advance projects. Anything that diverts humans from those tasks is a boon for the fungus. Was that pilot really out of AP, or did they want the hunters to chip away at the hull/let others use the lower-quality turrets?

You may have the lucky moments where you catch an agent in the act or when they misspeak on the public channel; more likely, it will require investigative casework based on tripping them up in lies or habitual negligence that can be proven via logs, previous actions, or technology.

Also, big point: don't assume that people you trusted early in the game will remain loyal. Those blind spots turn into huge disasters, because if others trust your word, that young mushling gets a free pass. Verify everyone, even Chun: she's guaranteed clean, but if she's gullible, you don't want her vouchsafing a potential enemy.

To the above, I'd add that cameras are your friend, as are the alarms that a certain research project will provide. The alarms are mostly self-evident in their use. Hide them well, and suspect anyone who picks them up and moves them elsewhere. Cameras will act as witnesses to some mush player actions, so that the actions show up in the room logs. This can greatly help in your detective work, but it's not foolproof, especially long after the fact. While it's true that drones and even the cat can be witnesses too, it's hard to depend on those being present at the time of the incident.

Certain skills can make specific characters far more effective as mush hunters, however...one must also bear in mind that they can be turned at worst, and potentially misled at best. That said: Nothing stalls an investigation more than an inactive investigator, and it gives the mush more opportunities to cover their tracks or wreak more havoc.

I would definitely suspect anyone who removes a camera from a room without the crew approving that decision. I'd do the same for anyone who consumes too much food or meds, because one mush tactic is to create as much wastage as possible.

Even if you manage to find and kill both mush on the first day, or even in the first few hours, that doesn't automatically preclude the possibility that they managed to turn someone else. Keep advancing your anti-mush projects and research, and be vigilant until the very end. Mush will exploit any weaknesses you provide them, and there are many opportunities for exploitation.
