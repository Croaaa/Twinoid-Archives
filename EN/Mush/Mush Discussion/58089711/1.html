For me, the signs of a good ship are:

> A lot of activity from the moment people wake up (+/- some RP but that's not mandatory)
> Teamwork
> A decent balance between Gold, Silver and Bronze players
> Newbies willing to learn

Even after the Mushes are selected, I love a constant level of activity. I always think that if everyone's active enough, a lot can be achieved. If activity drops, so do the chances at Eden or at least a long lasting ship.

What has already been said, also when the Mush doesn't play too aggresively (i.e. rampage as soon as they refill Action Points after selection) nor too passively (i.e. playing human without doing anything for the Mush cause).

With early rampage it results in either getting much damage to the human team, quickly finishing the voyage or forcing return to Sol once the Mush gets killed, or the Mush failing, getting them killed and continuing the ship Mush free, such as the PvP never existed.

With Mush doing nothing as the Mush and playing completely human, investigators start seeking for illnesses and starvation, getting more and more confirmed humans. When everybody gets confirmed except for the certain person, the Mush ends up getting killed or RFS'd without doing any harm to the human team.

Mush team making part of a good ship tries to convert the ship or trick the human team into thinking they're genuinely human. They don't get exposed cheaply doing covert Mush actions under cameras or secret ones with presence of other people. If they decide to expose themselves, they're doing for the Mush cause, to help the other Mush members survive or/and make them easier to play for the Mush cause.

I agree with a lot of this but to add something that hasn't been explicitly mentioned, if forced to choose I will take a friendly, semi-competent ship over an unfriendly min-maxed one. In general, "good play is good" but sometimes on a ship there's that one person who demands perfect play and throws epic tantrums if the ship wants to build a different project, research in a different order, etc. than they think is best. Not only is it not fun but from what I can tell, it doesn't actually help the ship succeed. When everyone else is afraid to talk or do things in case it triggers another fit, it creates a huge problem, and I'm becoming more and more inclined to just treat them as griefers, because I honestly believe they're just as detrimental to the human team, even if they spend all their AP on useful activities, because they cause other people to stop caring and in some cases stop playing at all.

Conversely, I love lots of chatter and weird RP on ships. I usually don't start it but I'm happy to join in once I see that others are on it too.
