Silver is essentially what you are talking about, although with 2 less skills and a few HP/Morale, it is a permanent bump up in power/utility, it never expires, the cost is reasonable, and having 4 skills is not really needed for most characters, but is lots of fun if you are leveled up. Mush, like Die2Nite, has a 'Pay2Win' element, and that is staying in Gold Mode, Like Hero Powers, its Over Powered by design, and a way to show off, like the custom costumes, although you still need the other characters to help you actually win. Everyone going Silver is the base, enough to cover the production costs of the game, but Gold is designed to generate 'reach revenue' from those that are not cost sensitive. The hope is clearly that you spend more over time than you were expecting to, more even than a reasonably priced Lifetime Gold would earn (im thinking something in the 40-60 USD range, which would equal a full retail game in the US). There is no lifetime option, because they think they can earn more over time from Mush lovers, and they probably can, IMO.
