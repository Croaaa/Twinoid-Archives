Annette

We've been discussing this with the judges and French mods. as well and understand inactivity is pretty frustrating. That said, inactives will always be part of the game and part of the challenge is to work with what you have. There are mechanisms to make up for inactive important characters, their jobs are taken over by others. You *can* succeed on as ship that has many deaths and/or a fair number of inactives. Luck and team work amongst those left will be in order but it can be done. Thoughtful communication and patience can go a long way.

As for sanctions for inactive players, warning someone who doesn't play the game as it is (and who will likely stop playing within a couple of ships) isn't going suddenly motivate them to actively play, they've lost interest and will quit naturally.

Then, there's the question of new comers who don't know the game well and are inactive because they are confused and still learning to navigate the game and communicate system. I don't see how warning them would make them feel more eager to play more actively.

If you are playing a long ship and you need to be inactive for a day or so, just let your ship know, they can look after your chores if needed. As well, if you know you are going on vacation and can't look after your duties, kindly don't start a new game at all.

We will, however, sanction those who clearly cease playing or logging in (ex: they have a disagreement and state that they are going to hurt the ship by not playing, etc - it will have to be a pretty obvious action though), games should be played to their natural end.

The idea of tutorial ships is a good one and will be passed onto Blackmagic, as is the Massive Mush Move! All that would take is a day and a time and it will be a good thing for the future.

For now, let's see how the launch goes tomorrow! Good luck everyone!
