So the game's over. But really, who won?

Lets say the ship gets back to Sol with the cure, and no living mush aboard - or goes on to Eden with the cure (or even without... once you get to Eden there's surely time to make it there, heh, as long as no Mush aboard still live).

I think everyone would agree that's a clear human victory.

Lets say the ship has living mush to the end, and the cure is never made, ship gets destroyed or all humans die of injuries and/or suffocation. Clear mush win, right?

But many games end differently than either of those two categories. Sometimes the humans kill all the mush, but never succeed in a win later. Who won that ship? I have trouble seeing that as a draw - the storyline is that Sol system is taken over and is hopelessly lost unless the ship returns with the cure.
Failure means everyone dies or is taken over by the mush (I don't mean -characters on the ship, I mean absolutely everyone) - Storywise, I don't see how that is any less of a mush win than the scenario where Mush are still in play when the ship dies.

Mush sometimes die off, but have left trapped rations, cat, or shelves behind. Sometimes newly converted mush return to a previously mush-free ship. That's considered a victory for mush, if that ship fails later, right? Well, the struggle to kill the mush, a struggle that uses AP and resources, that contributes to the later fall of the ship should it fall.

So, is this seen as a win/lose/draw scenario? If the mush die early and the humans die in failure a bit later, is that seen as a 'no win' scenario?

If so, why? How do you measure the value of the waste that a mush creates through the need to kill them, and say 'not good enough, you actually lost too' to that Mush, who did indeed take action that affected the survivability of the ship which did indeed later die?

Well, that's my thoughts on the matter. What does everyone else think?

I've been on a few ships in which griefers caused more harm (both directly and indirectly) than the Mush players. I agree with others who have said elsewhere that they can be considered neutral outcomes, because that sort of "victory" was as a result of actively throwing the game. A ship that manages a return to Sol or Eden, having dealt with its Mush threat, as well as active griefing, should of course count as a well-earned human victory.

With that in mind, I would like to think of this in terms of degrees of victory and defeat. Glory aside, it really comes down to who fulfilled their goals. If we're agreed that the primary Mush goals are to:

1. Cause the ship's destruction, through sabotage or the death of its crew, or
2. Engineer a return to Sol or Eden, with Mush aboard

Then that means the Human team's primary goals are to:

1. Get to Eden
2. Failing that, get to Sol, AND
3. Make either return with no living Mush aboard

And the secondary goal: Research the Retro-Fungal Serum, and cure one of the Mush, if possible.

So I propose that even if a ship researches the serum, cures one Mush, AND gets to Sol/Eden with one or more Mush still aboard, it's a Mush victory/Human defeat. If a ship kills its Mush, but makes it back to Sol/Eden, it's a Human victory/Mush defeat. If a ship fails to return to Sol/Eden, it's a Mush victory/Human defeat.

These can be given some scale, though:

All Mush killed or cured, serum researched, and a return to Eden: Decisive Human victory
All Mush killed or cured, Serum researched, and a return to Sol: Major Human victory
All Mush killed, serum NOT researched, and a jump to Eden: Major Human victory (the research may still continue there, and there are no Mush on Eden)
All Mush killed, serum NOT researched, and a return to Sol: Minor Human victory.

Mush victories would scale a bit differently, because a return to Sol/Eden is quite possible for a barely-active Mush, and returns to Sol/Eden potentially involve helping the humans a little too much:

Preventing a return to Sol/Eden through converting/killing all of the crew: Decisive Mush victory
Jumping undetected to Eden, with the serum NOT researched: Major Mush victory (this brings the Mush infection to Eden, where it now has time to grow)
Returning to Sol undetected, with the serum NOT researched: Major Mush victory (this can take some effort on the part of the Mush team)
Returning to Sol/Eden undetected, with the serum researched: Minor Mush victory (you could have this result by doing almost nothing)
Destruction of the ship by Hunters: Default Mush victory (you could get this result by doing nothing at all)

And jumping to Sol/Eden while there are clearly one or more Mush showing in the Comrades bar is a clear Human defeat, in my opinion...it's basically giving up.
