What is the rationale behind tying morale changes into more things? Is it because you find gaining morale to be too difficult? Are these suggestions meant to make gain/loss more "logical"? I'm guessing that you find the mechanic underused at present and want morale to be more fluid.

I like the examples you give, and were changes to be made, yours are good places to start. However, consider that adding more ways to gain morale, especially via actions that humans are already incentivized to perform, will reduce the effectiveness of other elements of the game. The Shrink, Leader, and Humanity's Only Hope abilities suddenly become way less important, though more reliable; bananas lose one of their key reasons for being grown; and comms become less vital for morale purposes. Furthermore, Finola's trait becomes something everyone has to deal with, and the mush Demoralize skill becomes more difficult to succeed with.

I guess my suggestion would be to consider all of the morale mechanics in play and figure out how expensive it is to earn one morale point. You'll find that the cheaper methods often have drawbacks or opportunity costs that limit their usefulness/abuse.
