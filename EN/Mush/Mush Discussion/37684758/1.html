Cycle changes happen whether you're there or not. If you aren't there during the cycle change, you will still earn any AP/MP that you would have earned had you been logged in at the time of cycle change, and you'll always be on the same cycle as the rest of your ship.

There are 8 cycles per day, each lasting a total of 3 real-world hours. If I understand correctly, casting ships may be able to modify that to some degree, but that isn't something to worry about quite yet.

Cycles happen in real time, whether you are there or not. The "New Cycle" button only acts as a manual update to the next cycle. Whenever you push it, refresh your browser or simply open a new tab of Mush, you will be at the current time on the ship.
Cycles are three hours long, so there are 8 of them in a day. Apart from around Daylight Savings Time when you shift the clocks forward or backwards, they're at midnight, 3am etc. For example, if the Mush day starts at midnight and you go on at 2am, you'll see it as still being Cycle 1 with one hour to go.

Each cycle, you'll get one Movement Point, one Action Point and an Experience Point. If you are lying down you'll get another AP, and if you are a Gold player (after paying money) you will get an extra XP. There are a few things that can also happen depending on what else happened at cycle change.
The only way you'll lose out on your basic MP and/or AP is if you are at the maximum (usually 12, but could be more with a Gold Project or less with Disorders).

As far as hunger, there's a hidden stat called "satisfaction." Food will give you satisfaction, and you lose one satisfaction per cycle. After your satisfaction reaches zero, you have 24 cycles before you gain the status "starving," after which point you will take damage every cycle until you eat. Source (might I suggest looking throughout mushpedia?)
