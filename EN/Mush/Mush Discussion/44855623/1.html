There's a lot unknown about the exact nature of the code. Some players have been part of data mining experiments. You might enjoy talking to the Department of Mush Research casting guys, sounds like you're already one of them

My answer is based on my experiences, and imperfectly answers the questions you are actually asking, but does perfectly explain what I have observed. The fragments of your question which this answer doesn't touch: This is because I don't know those parts - and as far as I know, no player knows those parts.

But I go with D. Here's my reasoning and supportive, if circumstantial evidence.

When equipment breaks 'without cause' (wasn't an event like fire or electric spike) it is possible for more than one item to break. The more time in play passes and the more AP spent, the greater the odds that something(s) will break each and every cycle.

It is hidden from players whether the number of already broken things affects the odds of other things breaking - however I believe from my observation it does not. Reason:

Certain objects (fuel tanks, I'm looking at you. Yeah, both of you) break more often than other objects. A LOT more often. If you run to fix your broken fuel tank(s) every time they break, they are likely to rebreak within 1-4 cycles as the game goes on (once they start to break, that is). Most ships will leave broken fuel tanks alone until there is a need to load more fuel. I have not noticed that something else breaks every 1-4 cycles on those ships that do not fix the fuel tanks, but I sure have noticed the #*&(#$*%& fuel tanks rebreaking very, very soon after some eager player repairs them again and again and again each time they break on the rare ship that does that (Remember what I said about AP spent increasing the total ship difficulty too ).

Late game, often many, many, MANY items are broken. Players just cannot keep up. Yet - certain items (Gravity sim, I'm looking at you. Really suspiciously, so behave!) break really rarely. Even in the end days, they break really rarely. Which is good, these 'almost never break' things tend to be vital for normal ship function and incredibly high difficulty to repair. If something was being randomly selected to break, then when most everything was broken, we'd expect to see the rare to break stuff breaking more often. I flat out don't see this - and I watch for it.

Circumstantial evidence: Destroyed items. When a fire breaks out in a room, it has a chance to break or destroy all breakables and destroyables present. Each one saves independently, you cannot fill the room with destroyables to try and 'sacrifice that' instead of what you want to save from the flames- still the same chance to lose every item in the room that can be destroyed. Since 'every item gets its independent chance' is used elsewhere in the game, I expect that it is used more than just once.

Frankly, even in French for French speakers, that message puzzled us for a long time and still does. BM is famous for his cryptic messages (http://twinoid.com/ev/34996109 ).

The "crew's capacity" might mean their skills, or it might mean their rapidity of reaction (if broken things get repaired immediately, for example). Or their efficiency in dealing with obstacles (breakage, fires, injuries... maybe hunters too).

As for the breakage code, or difficulty in general, there are a few things that can be noted. Some items have a higher chance of breaking than others. Tanks break very often, if not all the time in the end, while the gravity simulator rarely breaks more than once (not counting fires or electrical spikes). Patrol ships break fairly often too, less than tanks, but more than other items. Same for turrets. But it may be more noticeable because there are several of them.

Also, some items can't break at all (PILGRED, narcotics distiller, beds, Icarus, cryo module), but they are probably still taken into account, because there are sometimes Neron announcement about them breaking.
