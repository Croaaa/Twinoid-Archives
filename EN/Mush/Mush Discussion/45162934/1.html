So in talking with a few players recently, it came to my attention that some of you may not know much about how the report process works.

For once, I'll leave the "TMI" version to someone else: SDF Rules, Explanations, Quarantine!

The short and sweet of it:

If you're on a ship, and you think a person or more than one person is breaking the game rules, little rules or big ones, the thing to do is click on a post and select Report Post.

You can select a PM or a normal wall post. It is great if you can select a post that tells us something about what rule is being broken. There's also space to type an explanation - do that, tell us what you think was done. You don't have to tell us how it hurt you or the ship, but you can - just tell us what you suspect or know they did that was against the rules,

from
Frieda may have held the knife from before mush selection, when we killed her she dropped it but we never saw it all game - no idea when she picked it up
to
Jin just killed 3 people, and when we killed him he was human. Either he played against his team or he was a transfer, but I can't tell.

Notice those examples have something in common: The person making the report is not sure if they are right or not to make the report - not even sure if the person they reported broke the rules or not.

That is OK. It's not a lot of work for our judges to check reports like that. In a couple of minutes, we can make sure that it was good play or bad, and vote appropriately. Innocents won't be troubled, the guilty will have more attention and education - maybe they're unaware of the rules, maybe they've just been 'getting away with it', but the thing is - without a report, there's no sanction process.

If you don't report someone who you suspect broke rules, you have decided to act as their judge and vote them cleared, without even seeing all the evidence. Whoops?

I'm fine with that - you're allowed to. But certain players do complain sometimes about rulebreaking. As I study our sanction system more and more closely, I become more aware that -wow- sometimes players are just 'turning a blind eye' to even sometimes rampant rulebreaking; sometimes on a ship that someone has griefed, if it should be reported is discussed between players, and the players talking all agree that it should be reported, and ... No one reports it.

Impet has to go sit down now. Her head is all befuddled. She doesn't seem mad at anyone, she's staring at the handcuffs she wears.... which only come off when somebody makes a report.

Her fingers wiggle, a hopeful motion.

After a moment, the faintest of Ooooooooommmmmmmmms sounds from her direction.



Seriously though. About the only time I'm going to look at a player and think 'wow, you should not have reported this' is when the report is clearly fraudulent. Like the example about Frieda and the knife? If Frieda hadn't actually dropped the knife. A report like "I don't like Jin.". Or a report like "I want to waste your time, ha!".

Those kinds of reports might get 'bad attention' from the SDF on the person who made the silly, meaningless report. The rest of you, observing something you think is actual rulebreaking - please speak up. Really, it's something the Judges and mods agreed to have something to do with, to try and help make things better.

But we don't get free reign to run around like Batman or something. Our job is to -do nothing- if none of the players involved think a report should be made. So if you think a report should be made.... make it.

Don't even be scared that 'gosh, the whole ship probably reported it anyway already'. Don't worry about that - our system handles that just fine. Think of poor, restrained me, forbidden to say anything about that rulebreaker who actually broke rules, who doesn't get reported at all...
