Follow me if you want to live!

I haven't tested it personally but my understanding is if you delete a planet then it is lost, you can't delete it and travel to those coordinates and expect to find it. I imagine this is so that you have to take some risk if you delete a planet in hopes of finding a better one. The game doesn't want you to be able to scan 3-4 different planets and then choose which one is best. If you find a couple planets that would be okay to explore but are not the best, if you want something better, you have to take the risk of deleting one and ending up with a worse planet.

You can't go back to previous scans once moving and I'm sure it's not so much a matter of "can't" but for balance. The game is set up so that the players have to balance the ap needed to scan for and analyze a planet with the timing of moving to a planet and timing of needing to leave a certain point in space. The longer you sit in a certain space, then the more hunters you might accumulate or you might have asteroids looming. The game now forces you to find a suitable planet during the limited time you have during which you can afford to take damage from any hunters you can't destroy and the time limit of when asteroids hit. If you can't find a planet by the time you have to move then you are forced to load one fuel and just move into empty space to start scanning over again.
