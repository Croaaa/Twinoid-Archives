So, there is this problem with getting on a new ship and 80% of the crew is idle. No way to get out of the game, but the gold time is running. Those people, who are idle sure wouldn't be idle, if they had paid money - but those who did are the ones suffering because of that.

Here are some possible solutions for this problem:

1. Report the game for being fruitless:

This one is difficult and leaves room for exploiters since it would be needed for the active minority to have priority over the inactive majority. The system would need to take into consideration the number of actions made by each character, eventually even the amount of posts (also exploitable) and give those the most active the most voting power on the report.

2. Commit Suicide:

A character should be able to actively commit suicide under certain circumstances (for example if morale is less that 5). This wouldn't work with Mush, though and different characters would need different waiting times until they can do that.

3. Manual control:

A player can report the game afterwards and a mod would have to manually check the game logs for whether or not the reporting player is telling the truth. If yes, the time used should be added to the account of all who reported.

This is a complex issue and it would require a lot of attention to find a really suiting solution, but when the matter is money, it is just necessary for it to be solved. Of course, there is always starvation, but it is also not as quick as being able to leave a game at will would be.

Indeed. The inactives problem is a very difficult one to solve, and while it's great that people have a number of suggestions, a lot of people come up with suggestions without taking adequate thought into how much these ideas can be exploited. If someone comes up with a proposal that definitely took a lot of serious thought, isn't too convoluted, and cannot be easily exploited, maybe the developers will consider implementing the idea.

We've all heard of some variation of separating players. The admins have tried that and found it did not work. Anything that involves some numerical threshold is very likely to be exploitable. Finally, the SDF works hard enough as it is handling complaints, and we already know how hard it is to get the admins to do something on demand, so try to avoid ideas that involves more manual intervention.

Best advice I can give is go into mush irc chat and find someone else who is joining a new ship, and try to land on the same ship as them.
