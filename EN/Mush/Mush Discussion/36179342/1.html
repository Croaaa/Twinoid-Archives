Hi everyone !

I'm here today to present an application developed for the French community of Mush: Neron/Root/Journal, also known as NRJ.

Created first by Meowcate and re-worked by FlokS, NRJ is a website which allows you to write down the everyday point of view of your character, secretly and in a role-play manner. Your identity is protected for the duration of your ship as no-one will be able to read your diary until the end of the ship.
Every day, a new page opens on new adventures. It's up to you to give them a place where they'll be able to live forever.

The greatest point of interest of NRJ is that all the individual diaries from a same ship will form a big diary of this ship, where you'll be able to follow how everyone - humans and mushs alike - lived the same events. Once the Honours list of the ship is available, you'll just have to use the code of the game (theEnd) to link your journal with the ones of others players of your ship.

If you want to try the concept, you just have to connect at www.nrj-reloaded.fr. No new account creation required, NRJ uses a securised connection with Twinoid's API. NRJ is an approved Twinoid app, it has been verified and follows the rules fixed by the administrators of Twinoid. Just be warned that this being the French sided app, it is required to write your diaries in French. Every diary written in another language will be deleted !

Right now, this app is only in French. We would like to know if there would be enough people who have an interest in this app before we consider a possible translation of the website.

So, are you interested ?

On february 27, 2014
FlokS
Goinfre Felinophile

I have never heard of any control of the character through an astropad security breach, and I don't think that is possible.
Anyway, there is absolutely no risk of losing anything on mush through my app. The worst that can happen is that you lose your logs. The authentication process is done by twinoid themselves. All the data we get is your username, your twinoid ID and your language ('fr', 'de', 'en', etc..). And since the app is separated from the game itself, there would be no point to hack into it. Anyway, I personnally think it's safe, but since I'm no internet security expert, I cannot be 100% sure, but at any rate, the site is safe from XSS and SQL injections. And we also spent a good amount of time making sure that no other than you could access your own unfinished logs, even if they had infos such as the log's ID (which they should get directly from the db, not easy task, believe me ^^).

So, to make it short, there is no security concern about the game itself, there should be none with the security of your logs, and even if there was, the only thing that could happen would be a delete, which can be reverted in a matter of minutes since I make regular snapshots of the database.
