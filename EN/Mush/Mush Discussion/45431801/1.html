It's been a little while since I compiled a set of statistics for some people I was interested in, but I want to say a little about the statistics I saw then.

What I did was I took each person's last 30 ships (I did it by hand, and not for very many people), and checked their history to see if they were human or alpha mush, or ever converted or cured (I believe that info always shows in your history, even though most things only have a chance to be shown)

If you started play as human, I considered that one of your human ships (even if you were later converted). If you started play as mush, I considered that one of your mush ships (Even if you later were cured)

If you changed teams after game start, I considered that a lose for you, but if you died as a member of your team, I counted you as capable of winning with your team.

I counted a ship as having a human win if it ended with a return to sol or on to eden ending and had no living mush - serum was not considered (Even more fact to check by hand!). I counted all other endings as mush wins. Any casting ships were ignored, not even treated as one of the 30 ships, but any other sort of coordinated jump ship was excluded (too hard to identify to exclude).


By that standard, most of the players I expected to be 'pretty good' who I checked at that time had similar stats - 'pretty good' players seemed to average around 80% winrate, as human or mush. Other players (mostly randomly selected) had a lower winrate, between 70% and 30% of wins for their team when they are on that team.

Of course, these are skewed statistics - one player alone NEVER makes their team win. Give any of those 'good' players a ship with 15 inactives with them, and that ship is not getting a human win. However, the 'pretty good' players seem to pull off really consistent rates of wins as human or mush.

My current statistics:

When I start as alpha mush: 80.0% winrate.
When I start as human: 50.0% winrate.

At a glance, my personal stats suggest either I'm slipping as a human, or maybe it has gotten harder to win as a human... without getting much easier to win as a mush? Oh well. These statistics are not ideal, but they're something. Luck is also a factor too, and lots of statistics is far more convincing. Also, holy moly, I've been alpha mush 10 times in my last 30 games. Wowsers.

Too_Ton's statistics, measured the same way:

When starting as alpha mush: 85.6% winrate

So I can definitely see how/why Too_Ton would think humans are rarely winning these days. But there are still plenty of players whose human ships usually win. Even if your win rate is lowish, there can be extenuating factors, and it may not be the new code, even. Runs of luck, good and bad, do happen.
