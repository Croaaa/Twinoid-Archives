Being MUSH is very situational. As we all know, each ship brings its own personalities, skill levels, and random death thrown at you from every direction. Some people comb through every room logs keeping tedious notes and other people don't even notice when you remove the bravo dorm camera in front of them. What will kill you on one ship could very well be the key play to mush victory on another. The key to being a Mush is to always be flexible and be ready to adapt your plans to what is happening around you.

For me, a true mush victory is having the ship destroyed on my terms. For example, if I want the ship to be destroyed via hull damage I will focus all my efforts towards that. This can include sabotage of NERON projects, hiding fire extinguishers, and killing (or having the crew kill) pilots. With a specific goal in mind, it becomes easier to determine how to spend your limited AP. As a human, you have a specific role to fulfill on the ship. It should be no different as a mush.

Here are some basic strategies and tricks to help out. This is by no means an all encompassing list... and as stated above, what works on one ship might get you killed on the next.

The slothful boss: Take an active role on your ship by being motivated to get the human mission accomplished. The goal of the slothful boss is to be engaged with the crew on a social level, but not a productive level. Be sure to always have tasks for players to do. Try to make insignificant work seem important to bleed away your fellow players AP. Lastly, try to stall or drag out decisions on the ship to the last minute possible. AND ALWAYS make people come to you, being a higher being on the food chain, you should never go to those lower life forms.

The Mute: Be silent on your ship from the start. Run around the ship and do whatever you want. People will assume you're a foreigner with no English skills. As long as you occasionally do something productive from time to time, people will tend to leave you alone.

The Newbie: I really think this in an unethical approach to game play, but it is a strategy people play. I say it's unethical due to playing off the helpful nature of our community to want to help new players enjoy the game as much as we do. Breaking this trust makes players reluctant to help future new players. But enough of my rant there. The strategy requires you to act new, ask several questions, and make "Newbie" mistakes. The goal is to conceal your mush activity by claiming ignorance. This works best for bronze players, but can also work for silver players if they hold off on skill selection.

Mr. Klepto: This comes straight out of the tutorial. Steel and hide stuff. To hide random equipment could prove counterproductive and be an AP drain. Try to focus your efforts onto a single goal. This could be from sabotaging expeditions by slowing hiding the gear.

Jabba the Mush: This is resource consumption strategy. The goal is to take a needed resource of the crew (rations in this example) and blow through them like they are nothing. Anyone can simply cook all of them in the microwave, but you quickly out yourself as a mush and could be killed. With an active Stephen and a little help, all the rations can be refrozen. This strategy is a slow play move. Eat as often as you possibly can. Every time you eat a ration, be sure to hide one. You will burn through rations pretty quick using this strategy forcing the crew to go on expos for food rather than O2.

Mr. Fix-it: This really only applies to techs. Instead of hiding items, just dismantle them. It costs more AP, but the item is destroyed in the processes.

These six plays are just simple guides and not in the weeks tactical plays. If I revealed my tactics, well... then that trick is out of the bag and could rarely be used again. I got a little technical with the last strategy there, but it's easier for me to explain in that situation. Either way...these are just basic plans. You have to adapt them to fit your ship.

Mushepdia (http://mushpedia.com/wiki/Ending_the_... has a great list of deaths/endings for the game. This list may inspire you on how to come up with creative ways to ensure the crew does not return to Sol.

Best of luck to all my Mush Brothers & Sisters out there.
