Let me start out by saying that I love the idea of this game and what it aims to do and have also supported the game with a purchase for that very reason. However, as a (well informed) newcomer I have a few issues I would like your oppinions on as well as some suggestions (however unlikely they are to reach the French developers). I'll divide it into thematic sections and keep it "short" and sweet for ease of reading - starting with what I find the most important:



Section - Pay To Win

Problem:
Gold mode grants too large of an advantage in the form of two extra skill slots. Certain cash shop items restore large amounts of AP leading to scenarios where you can pay to win a fight.

Solution:
1) Keep gold mode as is but allow bronze and silver players to work towards the 2nd or 3rd skill slot (respectively) on each new ship voyage - potentially gaining the extra skill slot a set amount of days into the game through ingame actions/work. Alternatively allow the 1 extra slot to be purchased with experience points (once per game).
2) Reduce or remove instant AP generation from store-bought items. Movement Points are much less problematic as they cannot be easily and directly used to kill another player. Alternatively disallow player harm a set ammount of time after the ingestion. These items are (judging from similar pay to play based titles) best kept mostly cosmetic/fun or entirely team based.

Reason:
A ship can make or break depending on certain characters' available skills especially with multiple inactive people. Bronze mode serves as a good demo for the game but is very limited in its choices (especially as Mush oh dear - more on that later). Silver mode is acceptable as-is but the discrepancy between 2 and 4 skill slots is jarring in most cases and will increase as new powerful skills are added to game. Being able to unlock one of the currently Gold Mode Only skill slots through ingame actions could make Gold Mode more of a 'pay for convenience + small bonuses' while still retaining much of an incentive to buy it. Which leads to..



Section - Subscription Price

Problem:
The price of ~10€/month (no discount with longer intervals) is currently too high for a text-based browser game that gives you an average of 30 minutes to 1 hour of game play per day.

Solution:
The "simple" solution is to reduce the subscription price. Personally I would pay every month if it was a more in-tune 5€. I realize that a company is here to make money but a space ship without fuel is unlikely to get to its destination (ok, that was a lame metaphor).

The hard solution is to add many more things to do in-game in order to warrant the price. More on this further down.

Reason:
This would not be that much of a problem if there were a lot of things to tinker with in-game. I realize that it's a coffee-break game but having many things to do is not necesarrily a bad thing as long as you're not expected to play 6 hours a day.



Section - Gameplay, Simplicity vs Complexity

Problem:
1) There is a lack of detail and use in the way the non-text based part of the game plays out.
2) There is a lack of choice in the way characters can do their work.
3) There is a lack of need for most characters to move around on the ship - potentially turning the game into a less fun one-room-existence for some characters and limiting the already strained Mush-player's shenanigans.
4) There could be more random differences between each new ship you play.

Solution:
1) Integrate the isometric view of the ship more in how our daily actions play out by allowing us to move furniture around, block doors, access cameras from consoles to see other rooms, rip up/put down floor tiles, sever/fix electrical cables in rooms, hide items in specific spots of rooms, write messages on the walls, etc. there is much, much room for improvement.
2) Allow people to remodel their work environments/rooms to add their personal touch, add small choices in how research/repair/sabotage/piloting/etc is done such as requiring the player to choose how they will do a task and at what cost (think stuff like "-1 fuel to grease engine parts to increase engine repair efficiency".). Add more job-specific (including Mush) small tasks as read above.
3) Create random events where Neron requests characters with certain skills to report to a room in order to assess/help with a problem possibly gaining a small bonus to the ship/character in question. Anything to make the easily seen sporadic movement less suspicious.
4) If having a semi-random Daedalus is out of question then swap around some of the existing rooms. Variety is the spice of life. Add more random events such as 'corrupt AI Core, holes in the ship (giving a use for spacesuits on board), radiation, NPC Mush invasion, etc'.

Reason:
Having options helps keeping people interested in the game and striking a balance between too simple vs too hard will keep allow new players to still easily get started.



Section - Mush Game Balance

Problem:
Bronze and Silver Mush players are severyly underpowered and often unable in adding the excitement and sense of danger that is necesarry for the game to not become too predictable. Some times even leading to Bronze and Silver Mush players suiciding or going inactive.

Solution:
Let being chosen as Mush become an honor instead of a chore. Grant all four skill slots to anyone chosen as mush. Potentially even add more skill slots for mush only although that may require certain Mush skill rebalancing. Mush is central in the enjoyment of the game for the human crew.

Give Mush players more options in how they can go about their deeds (as mentioned in a previous segment). Cut power lines, electrify doors, mess with the AI, maybe even being able to remove certain entries from a room's log once a day.

Reason:
The heart of the game lies in the crew cooperating in order to make it to either one of the human victory endings, however without mush players the game runs the risk of becomming predictive and stale. A bronze player is a little too severely limited as a mush and the same goes for silver players.



Kudos if you've actually read this far otherwise here's a..

TL;DR: Wonderful game, but lacking in depth, a little too expensive.

Please flame away and/or present good counter arguments. I realize that none of what I wrote about may change but it serves as food for thought I guess.

Pay To Win
Thought this way at first when I first saw it. We had a thread in the gold players forums discussing what players were best played gold... and the result is that half the characters work just as well bronze. Chun and Jin Su can be extremely effective bronze. Stephen is still almost useless gold with his first 4 skills.

Characters like Terrence with Technician and Pilot aren't really that great gold. The game has limited APs, so it's diminishing gains, only there to cover up for a missing character.

Only a few characters like Finola, Raluca, and Gioele have skills boosting each other.

Vending machine stuff can only be bought once, so it's only a one shot 8 AP hit from that piece of space candy. And there are downsides to a lot of vending machine stuff too. But right now it's possible to prove you're not mush by buying something from the vending machine, which is kinda lame IMO.

Subscription Price
IMO, it's very cheap. It's not worth a cent on inactive ships, but very very fun on active ships. And it's not 30-60 minutes a day on those active ships! I like it because there's little actual grinding, but the game's fun enough that you can talk about it all day with someone. But YMMV.

Also silver is just a single purchase and also unlocks the secret forum, so you don't lose as much from forking out, unlike many other games which treat you like dirt after you stop paying.

Lack of detail
I agree it can be customized further. But too much choice makes it annoying, so would have to watch for that.

Yeah, it does get tedious on long going ships and I'd agree that events would be more interesting than getting sheetmetaled twice a day. I'd rather see it more FTL-like TBH, FTL was what brought me here.

Game Balance
I'd disagree here. The objective of mush is to well... be the bad guy. The mush are telling the story. Everyone has to do this round of storytelling.

The difficulty curve is a little high for the mush, IMO. But it's manageable. I think most people just get a little impatient while playing the mush. There are few passives you really use as mush, mostly showerproof and phagocyte. Trapper, traitor, and fungal kitchen are fun too. But even gold players don't have enough skill slots for everything.

You can even do a really good job without using any skills at all. The best mush are not the ones who sabotage the oxygen reservoirs and suffocate everyone secretly or hack the terminal and leave the Icarus behind. The best ones are the ones who convince humans to kill each other, convince Hua to go down on the planet solo and die, convinces Roland to run back and forth, feeds false evidence to Chun to confuse the mush hunting group.

Think bronze/silver are underpowered? Convert a gold player to your side. That's one massive advantage mush have - they can choose who's on their side.

psibertussure, can't wait to hear your realistic suggestions (hypothetically speaking)

Here's my suggestions:

A reworked legacy
I think the game needs clear incentives added, and I'd like it to be clear exactly what happened to a previous ship. I think the old glory system should be replaced with a new Glory system that combines "likes" with straight up "days survived", and "goals accomplished". I'd like to see some shared goals for each team, with different and obvious values, for the various end conditions, as well as personal goals. As an example, Roland could have his primary goal be "Cool Under Pressure" - Putting out fires and destroying enemy ships would both contribute to this goal, but he also needs to live long enough to achieve it. Everyone has the goals "Return To Sol" and "Reach Eden", although these goals are worth much more for the Mush, with the Mush also having "Destroy the Ship" as a valid (but significantly less valuable) goal. Giole and Paola could have a goal that the other survives, while Jin Su could have a bonus to going to Eden and a smaller goal to explore at least 10 planets or something. All humans have "Research the Serum" as a big goal. Players who died while inactive get no points towards any goals.

I'd also like to see Mush rewarded instead of punished for playing the long game. Mush goals would include things like "Prevent the Serum from being Researched" and "Prevent the humans from killing any Mush", but they wouldn't be punished for the ship surviving - rather, they would ALSO have the goal of the ship surviving, so long as they were in control, since it increases their odds of seizing the Pilgred or infecting humanities last hope at Eden.

In the end, every character that accomplished every one of their goals and survived until the ship succeeded should have the same amount of Glory - until "likes" are applied, which I think should be added directly to the Glory score. Because in the end, the ultimate goal is to have fun and insure the other players have fun too.

Tilting the Odds
I think there could be a lot more that is done with the ticketing system, things that would both encourage purchases (benefiting motion twin) while making the game more enjoyable. One of these would be to spend casting ticket to increase or decrease your chance of becoming Mush. (Someone watching your profile could see you spent them, maybe, but they'd have to know to look in advance and even then they couldn't tell if you chose to be more or less likely to be selected Mush).

There are many players who simply don't want to be Mush, and the game is more fun for everyone if the player's who do are Mush more often. It's not a guarantee. But it would be great to be able to spend a casting ticket to triple or third your chance of being selected Mush, maybe spend up to three casting tickets per ship.

Rewarding Good Behaviour
Finally, it would be nice if on long-lived ships, players with 4 or more likes didn't just get tickets, but also got a coin or two. This would encourage players to upgrade for the good of the entire team, and it would encourage players to spend more regularly since they can earn some of what they spent back by succeeding. It encourages more player dedication and a greater willingness to purchase because it lets the players see what they are missing out on, and it also rewards players who don't otherwise pay but are a positive influence on the game as a whole.
