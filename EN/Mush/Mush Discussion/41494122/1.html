For a while now, in the MMM chat I've said over and over that the tutorials suck. Any experienced player knows the tutorials suck, and along with that, idlers are a problem. So I've found an elegant solution that doesn't deter new players from joining, and stops idlers as well.

First off, tutorials need to be written by experienced players, and have the ability to be turned off in My Account.(If that isn't already a feature or what not.) My personal idea is to explain each new character's first ability in detail, and show the new player what each character's specific role is. This is in an environment NOT in a ship already, which is where idlers stop being a massive problem. Someone shall figure out they don't like the game right away, and no one else has to experience them (Although the rare one that gets through this filter.)

The current tutorials are trash, and idlers are one of the few problems that need fixed. Along with these tutorials (character specific, since each character is different) there is a link to Mushpedia for more information at the end of the tutorial. 'Be sure to check Mushpedia if you have any questions, and good luck in your real game! Be sure to ask questions, other players will be more than happy to answer!'

For example, everyone knows Roland is a Pilot most of the time, but with Sprinter, the new player doesn't know that. So, the tutorial tells them what their ability Sprinter allows them to do, and that Roland is typically a Pilot for the ship. There could also be a basic tutorial on top of this, for people that want to gain general knowledge, but it tells the new player who usually does this job. (Technicians or drones repair things, everyone cooks for themself after a certain day but not immediately, who scans for planets, etc.)

There needs to be do's and don'ts already laid out for newcomers in the tutorial rather than 'Get your cook on: go to the refectory and cook something!' While this can be remedied, a better tutorial would give experienced players an easier time explaining things to a newb if they already know they should look at mushpedia.

I'd like to hear other people's ideas, since I'm sure more than one of you is going to dismiss this entirely. Mostly because of my poor organization skills for a thread :p
