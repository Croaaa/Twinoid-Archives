SummerMcLovin

While more skills and the easier inventory from the combined iTrakie can help, Mush is mostly a game of communication and coordination, both of which can be boosted by high activity and knowledge of the game. A friendly master player who doesn't need sleep, has Gold Mode and the best skills unlocked for that character is obviously great, but you can still be an asset with much less. I would rather have a friendly Bronze new player than a silent and/or inactive high-level Gold player.

I don't know if you are only looking at the game so far or are already on your first ship, but either way it is good to take a read through Mushpedia, and nobody will have a problem with you asking additional questions on the forum (in the Q&A thread preferably) or in the chat system for a ship. Your crewmates will appreciate you thinking about your actions rather than rushing in and making mistakes.

More specifically for low level and particularly first time players, there are some characters that you may want to leave until later on:
Chun, Chao and Eleesha have a somewhat difficult role of coordinating the Mush hunt.
Roland definitely needs to be at least level 2 to play properly, and the same is true to a lesser extent for Gioele, Jin Su, Terrence and Paola. I'd advise you wait until at least your second ship before going them.

Good characters to go first for their simple tasks and Level 1 skills are Ian (if you can be active at 2200 and/or 0100 Paris time depending on the ship), Hua, Frieda, Kuan Ti, Raluca and Janice.

I really would recommend buying Gold mode at least once to unlock Silver, but if you can't or won't then you can still get by with Bronze.

SummerMcLovin

Ships tend to take a while to fill up, so you should have an opportunity when everyone is on the same side to make it clear that you are looking for some assistance. The Wiki will help a lot, starting off with the Game Basics and Human Play pages. You should go through the basic parts of the other three columns of pages (Characters, Studies, Daedalus), particularly the character you want to play and the role that they have - e.g. Frieda, then also read Astrophysics and Exploration.
Also, you probably want to ignore most of the "helpful tips" that the game gives you. If you end up as Mush, definitely read the Mush sections of the Wiki, and ask your partner. The Mush pages are helpful even when human because you can get an idea of what they might be doing on your ship.

"Mode" would be the description of Bronze/Silver/Gold, probably. Levels are how much experience you have put into a character, unlocking further skills. You will automatically have your skills selected if you have enough slots to take all of them (so you need to be at least Level 2, or you will be stuck with your Level 1 skill straight away). Pretty much every character will have a good enough skill at Level 2 (which only costs 5XP per character), but even if you have a different one you will still have AP to use in general tasks like relaying Research, NERON Projects and gardening.
As an example, the "fat asiatic guy" was probably Jin Su: he is a Leader at Level 1. If you upgrade to Level 2, you can pick between Leader and Pilot (which is usually more useful), or automatically get both as Silver. At level 3 and beyond, you can pick your skill(s) at both Bronze and Silver, but would need to be Level 5 to have a choice as Gold.

I suppose nowadays, Aceb is probably right that it doesn't particularly matter about specific skills. There are a lot of long-time players who have built up enough experience to have at least one of each important skill on each ship. It is when people get killed or go inactive that things can change.

Yeah, SummerMcLovin basically said all this already, but the game is totally worth it F2P. The thing that makes the game unique is that the interactions you have with people have a big impact. I'll bet a lot of people would prefer a team player to a selfish gold player. Also, I'm not an expert at the game or anything, but I feel like the absolute biggest factor in how your game goes is how much thought you put into each decision. Think things through, plan moves out, and check the wiki/ask about anything you're unsure of during your trip. Doing these things will make you an invaluable part of any crew.

That being said, I'd bet my lucky blaster that you'll end up wanting to at least buy a few days of gold and drop down to silver if you keep playing. Besides the other benefits, the extra skill slots add a lot of depth and possibility to each character, which makes each game all the more interesting as well as giving you more ways to help out your crew and interact with the Daedalus.
