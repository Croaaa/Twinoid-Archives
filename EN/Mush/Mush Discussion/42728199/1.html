Ethics are a wonderful topic for long long discussions. I for example am really bothered by the way the terms human & inhuman are coined and am kind of curious about the ethics of the mush. But this is about human actions against an (fictional) alien race, using the bodies of humans as hosts, trying to prevent the humans to find a way to stop them and resorting to killing them if necessary. So I'm not sure if you'll find a moral code applying for that situation.

Let's turn the question around. You are human, maybe you still have family or friends or a lover at Sol, you have a mission to find a 'weapon' or 'cure' against the mush forces endangering your and your loved ones lives. There are mush aboard your ship, paranoia takes a toll on you and you're beginning to feel claustrophobic. And then one of those mushes steals your body, leaving you in an exhausted body of a stranger. You know they are up to no good, you have no power to stop them and no walkie-talkie to call for help, but hey you still have some credit points with the intergalactic space vending machine. Are you going to ask yourself whenever buying and energy drink is fair towards the creature now inhabiting your body? I doubt that.

Even if you find a ethical codex that says be nice to the race that wants to take your body and destroy your life, there isn't much morality left on the Daedalus. You may get killed as a mush suspect, turn out to be human and after your death be blamed by your murderer and his accomplices for the whole incident. Happens all the time. Is that fair? Ethical correct?

In this game mankind's survival is at stake and not just because two mush are riding with you. What you are pointing out is a really small ethical problem, if it even counts as one.
