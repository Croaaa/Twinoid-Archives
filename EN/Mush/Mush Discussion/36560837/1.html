Note I do not say it's as serious as the issue of these things - it's as serious as the debates about those issues.

Wherever you have an issue that people care deeply about (and many of us are very interested and involved in mush, thus caring rather deeply about various things about it), and whenever there are two or more 'strong and reasonable perspectives', then lines form.

Most people find they fall more or less strongly into one perspective or the other; the other side's values and reasons are not especially convincing, sensible, important, or properly thought through - but this is perceived on both sides of the issue, each about the other.

Issues like this are both simple, seen from one perspective, and complex when seen from multiple sides. But this situation is a debate, it is a discussion. The people who feel either side is clearly right and the other clearly wrong, ideally we talk to each other, we explain our views in as easy to understand ways as possible and listen to the other sides' explanation, and then you ask questions and provide a way to understand the conflict you see, if what the other side says doesn't seem to be including all the relevant factors or is wrong for some other reason.

Even if one side is clearly, logically wrong, it might be clearly, emotionally right from a set of emotional values, especially for those who feel that way. Stuff like this is especially not simple, and calling it simple probably doesn't do anything to make it simpler.
