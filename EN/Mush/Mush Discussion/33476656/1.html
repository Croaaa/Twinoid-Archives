Your example isn't fit here though.
Arguing that something happened so the odds of it is irrelevant is valid, but that can also be a clue. For example if you happen to bump into that classmate at multiple locations in a short timeframe, it might give you a hint that he/she is secretly stalking you.

Same here: someone reporting this odd string of events doesn't prove anything, but since it is a very specific one it might point towards a mechanic in the background (that was indeed confirmed).

To give a simpler example, consider a 6-sided dice.
The odds of rolling 6 ten times in a row is 0.0000016%.
The odds of rolling 6, 6, 2, 5, 6, 4, 3, 1, 4, 3 in a row is 0.0000016%.

If you'd roll 6 ten times you'd think the dice is manipulated.
If you'd rolled 6, 6, 2, 5, 6, 4, 3, 1, 4, 3 you wouldn't suspect a thing.
