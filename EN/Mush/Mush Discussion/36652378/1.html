ringhloth

I think that the biggest factor in surviving and having a good ship is just taking the lead yourself, and organizing the crew. More people will be more active if they have a job to do, they enjoy doing it, and they like the crew they are with. You enjoy the game, you play it more often.

Try and be welcoming in chat (so don't be pushy about people doing a job; give someone a job, don't force them to do it), and don't go crazy when hunting for the mush. Oftentimes, the mush hunt can do more harm than good. Once D3, D4 comes around, I like starting a mush hunting channel that's pretty inclusive. 5-6 of the most active and least suspicious people. ALWAYS ask before shooting. A cornered mush isn't as dangerous as people think, I think. Just take a few precautions beforehand.

Don't give up. There's only one point where a ship is doomed, and that's when it's destroyed. An oxygen planet can always be right around the corner, PILGRID could be surprisingly far along, you can run from hunters for at least a little while, a great project could pop out of the comms console, some great mage books can be printed out, or a previously inactive player could come back. Giving up, even while still active, will make the ship only more likely to fail.
