Like Annette said.

You create a casting, it's like a groupe of people you invite. Depending on if you're gold or not, you've a number of tickets per month. ( +1 per game who last 10 days at least )
When enough people have put tickets in the casting ( 50 I think ) the casting is working and you can launch a game.
To launch a game, enough people have to be ready, ( being ready cost 1 ticket, you can cancel it and you'll have your ticket back ) when 32 people are ready, two ships are launched and you're in one of them. You don't choose your character.

The casting gain level through launchings and people can put tickets in the casting too. Each level you gain a new mode or something to extend your casting.
( more people each lvl can join, cycles of 4 hours, cycles of 1 hours, 3 mushs, no mush, etc etc... )

Kayin

Castings have a limited space in terms of how many players it can hold depending on casting levels (from 32 to 256), while Twinoid groups are limited to 100 players. You also need to pay 3 tickets (blue or gold) to open a casting, and players need to pay 1 to enter.

A Casting is only operational after paying 50 tickets (entry fee nor creating it are counted in those 50). Blue tickets will be used first, they can stack up to a certain number depending on your subscription. You can gain them each month or with a D10 same cycle you entered your ship. Golden tickets are given through your subscription, amount depending on how long you subscribe for.

A group have levels, and it depends on casting experience points. 1 ticket = 1 exp, one casting ship launched = 25xp. Last 15th level is around 7k exp... so do the maths.
