coro
iGenesis New personal best: 185 860 pts (192 490 pts) on Level 9, without any golden statues. The missed point opportunity at the end of Level 8 was an accident.

Your movement is good, you just need to learn to incorporate these three habits into your playstyle:

  • Prioritize enclosures with the boxes on them (I do not learn game terms, sorry). Unless you have a better opportunity to get some good unit generation going, try to take over enclosures that could potentially contain gold... It's no good to get one of those gold statues when there aren't any neutral enclosures left
  • "Double tapping" when sending sheep to capture a neutral enclosure that's near some pesticide. You need to send a support volley of sheep from the same enclosure to cover for any sheep lost while walking through the pesticide zone
  • Abusing pesticide in late game (when you need to capture more than 10 enclosures to move on). This abuse can come in many ways, but the easiest to grasp is the "bait and strand" maneuver. When you come into possession of a pesticide enclosure, you should then concentrate some sheep there and try to attack the largest enemy enclosure. The intent is not to have control of both, you want to swap. I suggest concentrating attacks on whatever enemy enclosures sent penguins to take over your pesticide. If the big enclosure is the only one that did so, great! You just baited an attack that halves the amount of penguins you need to kill there. And once the enemy controls a pesticide enclosure, you need to take over all other enemy enclosures. Do not continue to take over neutral enclosures, as this lowers your overall unit count, and leaves you open to the enemy sweeping in and taking over everything before you can react. Once the enemy is stuck with pesticide, try to kill off its ability to generate more units. You then win by default, and can take your time taking over the remaining neutral enclosures
