As we know, the level of competition can vary quite widely between leagues. Last season, League ID 1 was very competitive, with multiple M-T developers and top KadoKado players among its membership. On the other hand, a few leagues were so inactive that players with 0 points managed to be promoted to the Silver League. (Though to be fair, some of these leagues were created late into the season.)

Every season, M-T sets target percentages for the number of advancers and decliners in each league. This season's targets are 30% (top 15) for Bronze->Silver and Silver->Gold, and 4% (bottom 2) for Silver->Bronze.

Now, to eliminate unfairness caused by varying degrees of competition between leagues, why not just toss every Arkadeo member into a single general classification for each tier?

So under this proposal, the 1920 current Silver players among 39 leagues would be consolidated into ONE ranking, and the top 30% (576 players) would advance to Gold, while the bottom 4% (76 players) would drop down to Bronze.

The end-of-season rewards ("winnings") could be mathematically adjusted such that M-T pays out the same number of s.

What's wrong with this idea (other than M-T's inefficient code potentially making this infeasible?)

coro

The current league system rewards activity moreso than skill

Of course, one will hit a ceiling where their skill level will prevent them from advancing further, but it's possible that that person's league won't keep refilling with players better than them, allowing for the "less skilled" player to have a moment in the spotlight and enjoy a season in the higher league level. By having an overall ranking, there is a lower chance that someone could luck out and be able to level up beyond what they could have normally achieved

Not everyone is lucky, skilled enough to take advantage of that luck, or willing to pay to overcome the random factor of games on here. By splitting up the userbase into smaller pieces, you lessen the stress of having to perform at your best every season. If you notice that you've got tough competition this season, then you just try to get enough points to not fall down a level

The current league system might have issues when the userbase on here gets really small. If the values for the number of people who level up or down are the same across all leagues (regardless of the number of people in a league), then there won't be any problems

Alternatively, they could keep a user in a league for a certain number of seasons, before they do some "spring cleaning" and remove them for inactivity
