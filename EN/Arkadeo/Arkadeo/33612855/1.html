Arkadeo was released to the world on 1 April 2013, but was actually in private beta since December 2012, when I was invited to join.

Beta testers had unrestricted access to the first 13 games (Pulsar, ID1 through Green Witch, ID13) in the Level-Up, League and Mission modes.

Since then, this is what M-T has accomplished:

A 14th game, Overdrive, has been released. Yay.

A 15th game has been in beta since Season 15, though reported bugs have not been recognized or fixed.

Clan Mode is still nonexistent despite numerous assurances that it was coming.

Critical bugs for multiple individual games, and the site as a whole, have not been fixed.

Admins are no longer active on the French forums. Even Augures (representatives) haven't been hearing much from the admins.

Technical support is nonexistent. Support tickets opened months ago have been left unanswered.

KadoKado, Arkadeo's six-year-old cousin, is conservatively at least 20 times more popular. (One game, Iron Chouquette, has more active players in one period than Arkadeo has in all leagues combined.) Though KK is on the decline, too.

"New" games that were promised months ago have not been released. This includes teaser screenshots (Garlic Bob) and trailers.

Clearly, this is very little progress achieved in a year's time.

I have a feeling this site may not survive for much longer. I strongly doubt Arkadeo has attained M-T's revenue or ROI expectations and this is why the admins have been neglecting it.

When M-T shut down other sites like PiouPiouz and Awako, I've lost years' worth of progress practically overnight. Remember that there's no such thing as "too hard to integrate" into Twinoid and other current M-T frameworks; if M-T makes enough money from a game, they sure as heck will keep it alive.
