The following changes/additions are scheduled for April:

Level-Up Mode will no longer be bound to the season calendar.

League Mode will be extended from 2 weeks to 3 weeks.

Clan Mode will finally launch with cooperative missions and score challenges.

Any game played in "scoring mode" (whether in League, Missions or Clan) will count towards your league placement.

No word regarding new games. The most recent addition, Badabloom, was released almost 5 months ago.

The admins had assured early beta testers like myself that Clan Mode would "reinvent" multiplayer and be well worth the wait. Since the first testers were all invited from KadoKado, I'm sure none of us were expecting a blatant ripoff ... though there's a chance M-T may add some "twists" to the established formula.

My guess is that after Arkadeo completely flopped (there are now fewer active players on this entire site - ~7000 per season - than Iron Chouquette alone), M-T is half-assing something just so they can label Arkadeo as a "finished" game and relieve themselves from questions or obligations to update the site.

In my opinion, lengthening the seasons only puts a bandage on the real problem: no one wants to play every single game every single period. It's a tedious chore. Given Arkadeo's decline, I doubt the game library will ever grow large enough to preclude non-payers from reaching Elite.

It also remains to be seen how payouts will be affected. They should be multiplied by a factor of 1.5 to reflect the lengthened seasons. But I could easily see the admins keeping the current rates, because the addition of clans would open up another revenue stream for players.
