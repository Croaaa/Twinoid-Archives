In Season 6, there will be
1 Elite League with 15 players, and
5 Master Leagues with 248 total players.

(Why 248? Prixivium will lose 15 players to Elite and 2 players to Platinum, leaving 23. In addition, each of the 15 Platinum Leagues will promote 15 players to Master.)

So in Season 7, the Elite League will satisfy
15 + 5x - y <= 50
where
x is the number of players promoted from each Master League to Elite at the end of Season 6, and
y is the number of players demoted from Elite to Master at the end of Season 6

y cannot be greater than 14, because there are only 15 Elite players in Season 6, and it doesn't make sense to demote everyone. Even 10 demotions out of 15 would be unnecessarily harsh in my opinion.

x cannot be greater than 9. 10 promotions per Master League would produce 50 new Elite players when there are already 15.

From the grid of possible values, I believe the most likely configuration is (x,y)=(8,5). So if this holds true, at the end of Season 6, 8 players from each of the five Master Leagues will advance, and 5 Elite players will lose their spots.

Although the likes of Machades, Rukawa, et al will be gone, a race for the top 8 among 49-50 Master players (vs. top 15 out of 40 this period) will be very, very competitive. And this will only get worse in Seasons 8 and beyond.
