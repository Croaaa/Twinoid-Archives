Rule 1: You are playing The Game.
You, along with everyone else in the world, always is, always has been,
and always will be playing The Game. Neither awareness nor consent is required to play.

Rule 2: Every time you think about The Game, you lose.
Loss is temporary; as soon as you forget about The Game you stop losing.
The objective of The Game is to forget that it exists. Good luck.

Rule 3: Loss of The Game must be announced.
Every time you think about The Game, and hence lose, you must say so.
This is the only rule that can be broken, but do you really need to cheat..?
Tell it here in this thread. If you was the last person to post, don't say that you lost it. This is to avoid spam.


What is The Game?

The Game is an abstract mental game with millions of active players.
The objective of The Game is to avoid thinking about The Game.
Play is continuous and never stops. You are either losing (when you are thinking about The Game), or not losing (when you are not thinking about The Game).
Players who lose The Game (by thinking about it) must tell other people that they have lost.

How do I play The Game?

There are two common interpretations of Rule 1. The first is that everyone in the world is, always has been, and always will be, playing The Game. The second is that people start playing The Game once they know what it is. The Game is played all the time; you cannot quit, pause or take breaks.

Most of the time, playing The Game does not require any active input and you go about your life as normal. However, according to Rule 2, every time you about The Game, you have lost. Loss is temporary; once you stop thinking about The Game, you stop losing, but you will lose again the next time you think about The Game.

According to Rule 3, every time you think about The Game, and hence lose, you must tell other people that you have lost. Some interpretations state that you must tell as many people as possible, while others only state that you must tell whoever you are with, or talking to, at that time.

An example of play is as follows. After you stop browsing this website you stop thinking about The Game. Tomorrow, you meet a friend who tells you ''Hey! I found an awesome Flash game I think you'd like.''. This makes you remember reading about The Game on this website. As you have thought about The Game, you have lost, and you must tell your friend something like ''I lost The Game!'', or ''Crap, you made me lose The Game!''.



What happens when I lose The Game?

When you think about The Game, you lose The Game, and you have to tell people here in this thread that you have lost. Some people refuse to announce their loss after thinking about The Game, and as loss is a purely mental event, there is of course no way to prove whether somebody thought about The Game or not. This makes The Game a game of true sportsmanship; a personal mental challenge. If you cheat, you are only cheating yourself... But don't say it if you was the last person to post.



How do I announce my loss?

Tell it here in the thread. If you was the last person to post, don't say it. The tread will just get flooded then. But you will still loose.

Can I think or talk about The Game without losing?

Once you begin to lose The Game regularly, you may find youself in some curious psychological situations that are hard to interpret within the rules of The Game. For example, you may discuss The Game so much that you sometimes enter such a discussion without realising that this means you should have lost. But whether you have lost or not depends on what is really meant by 'thinking about The Game'. For example, it could be interpreted as 'thinking about the concept of The Game itself' or 'awareness that you are a participant playing The Game'. If you think or talk about The Game, but don't realise this means you have lost, and hence don't announce it, does this mean that you are unknowingly cheating? The only interpretation that would prevent such cheating is the seemingly paradoxical interpretation that you only lose The Game when you realise that you have lost it...

Another interesting situation occurs when somebody asks about or discusses The Game, without yet knowing what The Game is. If someone asks 'What is a hexagon?', are they thinking about hexagons even though they don't know what one is?

How do I win The Game?

Under most interpretations, The Game can never be won, as the rules include no winning conditions. Some people interpret the rules that you are winning The Game whenever you are not thinking about it. As The Game can never be won, you have two options; to lose as little as possible, or to make everyone else lose more than you!

Will The Game ever end?

The short answer is no.

What if I don't want to play The Game?

Everyone is playing The Game, and that your consent is not required to be a participant. You can of course ignore the rule and not announce your loss every time you think about The Game.

I've had the rules of The Game (which I lost) explained to me a little differently.

As they were explained to me:
1 - You aren't playing The Game until someone explains to you the rules of The Game. Once you know the rules of The Game then you are forever a player (You can choose to ignore your losses but they will always be there waiting for you).

2 - When you think about The Game you are losing The Game. When you are not thinking about The Game you are winning The Game. Therefore, it is impossible for you to know you are winning The Game because as soon as you realize you are winning The Game you have thought about The Game and are therefore now losing The Game.

3 - Once you lose The Game (or at least realize you have lost The Game) you must immediately announce that you have lost The Game. You only need to announce it once and if you hear someone else announce they have lost The Game you do not need to announce you have lost The Game since, theoretically, even though you have probably thought of The Game and are now losing The Game everyone else around you would have heard the announcement, would now be thinking about it, and already be losing anyways, so repeat announcements are unnecessary. (An alternate view is once someone has announced they have "lost" then all those around them have "won" that "round" and it ends. In this instance each person individually starts a new "round" the moment they forget about The Game).

It is in my observation from watching (and playing with) these friends that The Game "ends" when someone thinks about The Game and announces they have lost The Game and, for you, The Game "begins again" when you forget about The Game; it is, therefore, theoretically possible to lose The Game several times with the same group of friends, even over the course of the same day, if you continuously forget about The Game before anyone else does. Of course, once The Game "ends" (as in you are either losing The Game or are following the alternate scoring method of thinking/not-thinking about The Game) it was rather common for these same friends I learned The Game from to start writing "The Game" on slips of paper and slipping those notes into D&D manuals and other books and places only to watch in amusement as said friend opens the book and shouts out in frustration that they have lost The Game.
