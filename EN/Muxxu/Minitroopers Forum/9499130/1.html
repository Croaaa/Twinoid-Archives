Tunkson
Take a look at your guys. I know you leveled them all up, but look at them again. Consider their abilities and try to make them mesh with one another. Having a "bazooka squad" sounds cool (a wave made up solely of bazooka guys), but that will backfire when someone gets up close (like a spy or someone with a knife). Same goes for having a lot of snipers. So try to always have a wave that has a mix between close, mid and long range fighters.

Basically, consider that you're building team players, not individual strongarms. Make their skills mesh with one another, and you should do better than you're doing now.

One other thing you should look at is what skills and weapons your guys have. If you have someone who has lots of defensive skills (tuck n roll, BPV, FMB, etc), change their fighting style to defender. If you have a soldier with a machine gun, change his fighting style to hunter. For example, I have a soldier who has a BPV and heavy armor, and, I believe, tuck n roll. His problem is that he has only one weapon (sniper rifle), so once those two shots are up, he's just a sitting duck, however hard to take down. So, he gets the defender style.
(By default, I make all my medics defenders. They're not there to do the job of soldiers, they're needed to heal people up.)

Just a couple of pointers, really. But some skills are useless for raid members, while useful for personal squads (for example savior), so consider that as well. Also consider that higher raid phases mean more saboteurs, meaning more of your troops losing weapons. Take that into account as well. (Meaning, make sure your troops have at least 2 weapons on them so then they don't become sitting ducks like my tank soldier.)
