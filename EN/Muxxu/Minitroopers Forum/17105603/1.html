The English Server Cup

This is a round robin tournament.
Each army in the tournament will be created throughout the competition.
All players are welcome

All players enter 1 army into the tournament.
At the beginning each army has only one trooper at level 1 (the army may have any amount of credits and recruits).
Once the tournament begins each player may buy troopers and start leveling up.
They do this under a set of restrictions and as they face off against each other in the tournament.

Each victory earns the player 1 point.
The winner is the contestant who has the most points when the tournament ends.

Prizes
The winning player will receive their choice of either 10 new recruits or ownership of the prize army.
The top 3 contestants in the tournament will all receive recognition in the wiki Hall Of Fame.

10 recruits
The winner may choose to have 10 recruits instead of the prize army.
All 10 recruits will be sent to any one army link of their choice.
There is no guarantee on the quality of these recruits or their date of arrival.

Round robin
For an example of the round robin layout see this link 5 player round robin example.
There may be any number of players in the tournament.
There will be one round each day.
The total number of rounds will be set by the number of players.

Less than 15 players
If sign up closes with less than 15 players then a second round robin tournament immediately after the first will be started. The second round robin will continue with the same armies and from the previous round.
The two scoreboards will be added together for the grand total.

Some Rules.
Army Restrictions
All skills are aloud.
The level and the number of your troopers is restricted.
When presenting a replay for any round the total combined number of levels for all of your troopers in your army may not exceed the number for that round.

Examples...
Round 1: one trooper at level 1 (1*1=1).
All replays for round 1 must show armies of only one trooper at level 1.

Round 2: Replays may show armies of one trooper at level 2 or two troopers at level 1.
Round 5: Replays may show armies of any combination of troopers/levels adding up to 5.

At round 5 an army made up of two troopers, one of them at level 3 and the other at level 2 would be allowed, (3+2=5)
...or five level 1 troopers, (5x1=5)
...or any combination that equals 5 or less would also be allowed.

As a formula it looks like this...
(total number of levels in your army) < (current round+1)

Money will be a problem in the later game and so players are encouraged to pre-prepare an army beforehand (as long as the new army can still enter round 1). Gaining a small income by using the 'recruit of the heart' thread would be good.

Replays
Each 'face off' between two contestants must result in 3 replays being posted on the forum that day.
All 3 replays must have been generated that day.
Each victory over your opponent earns you 1 point, each defeat earns the opponent 1 point.
The contestant who initiated the battle (on the left side of the battlefield) is responsible for posting all 3 replays directly after the challenge.
Each replay must be a direct challenge (not BAT/MISS).

Challenging but not posting the replays
There may not be less then 3 replays posted.
If no replays are posted then both players will be declared inactive that day (tie, 0 points each).
Challenging your opponent and then choosing to not post all replays is considered rule breaking.
If available, any player may reveal a replay from their history page which was not previously posted in the forum by it's challenger. If this happens and it is revealed that the challenger did not post a replay then the challenger will be punished.

Rule breaking
Punishmint for rule breaking will depend on it's severity.
Punishmint may range from deducting points or tournament disqualification to being banned from all future tournaments.

Inactive players
Inactivity is not rule breaking and has no punishment, any player is free to go in and out of inactivity throughout the tournament.
If you are in a 'face off' with a player who is inactive you must still challenge that player and play on as normal.
If both players in a 'face off' are inactive then it is declared a tie with no points awarded to either player for that round.

Bugs
It is irrelevant whether or not there are any bugs present in a replay. Victory is decided by the large word that is displayed at the end of a replay after repeatedly tapping the spacebar, all bugs are overlooked.

Time/Days
There will be one round a day. The exact start and end time for each day is determined by the minitroopers server turnover.
Which is the same as the time in Paris France.

Passwords
Please use passwords! All contestants are responsible for their own security.

Other Things
The starting trooper does not need to be created as the recruit of any other perticaler army.
Each player my enter only one army, you may not swap armies once sign up has closed.
Power level is irrelevant.

Some Game Play Recommendations
1. Consider creating the starting trooper as a recruit for your own army. Use the 'Specialized Recruiting' thread if you need to.

2. Without choosing a new skill, a player may at any time click the level up button to see the next set of skills available.
By doing this without choosing any skill the trooper will remain at the same level. Going back to the HQ page the trooper can then continue to take part in the tournament at it's current level.
Any player can use this method to design the starting trooper up to level 2 (but must stay at level 1!).

3. Once the tournament is underway it is recommended that each player improves their army after the 3 battle replays have been posting up in the forum. By using this method your army will be ready for your next opponent in the coming day. If your opponent logs in tomorrow, before you do, it will not give them any advantage over you.
