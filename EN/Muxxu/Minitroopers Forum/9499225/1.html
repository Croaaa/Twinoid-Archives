pickupsticks
Loader is most useful for a weapon with a large clip. With your basic pistol you won't see much difference between slapping in a full clip with loader, and reloading it manually with nimble fingers.
If you manage to pick up a heavy weapon then loader could prove much more useful... more so if your trooper dies quickly! Being able to keep reloading with nimble fingers is helpful in extermination missions where you have a munitions trooper providing a steady stream of supplies, but in a pvp fight the survivability of your trooper is one factor to consider for which of the two skills is most useful.

I've seen people say on the forum that nimble-fingers also affects the operating time of aiming/firing the weapon, although I've yet to see anything in any of my own troopers to confirm this.

With the revolver and semi-auto I'd probably take Loader, as the reload is a little quicker than with nimble fingers... and getting the next shot off quickly before the enemy can return fire seems to be most important. If your guy is still alive after he's emptied the clip for the second time then he can still benefit from Take Cover. If you've got a munitions officer in your army then it would be a more marginal decision.

Final thought: without battle ready, sometimes you have a really bad start with a weapon only having one shot loaded - loader really helps to overcome this quickly.

My two cents...

"Nimble Fingers allows trooper to operate weapon twice as quickly as well as loading two rounds at a time."

Good for assault rifles (small clip with faster firing), double barrelled shot gun (big damage, naturally slow to fire, needs two rounds for maximum pain), dual pistols (relatively quick firing, two bullets loaded and then fired are good when the enemy is in yo' face), Magellen (fast firing and large clip for a sniper rifle).

Loader probably is better for the minigun types (large clips). It might also help with rocket launcher, seems to me that the reloading of a rocket launcher takes a bit of time to reload.
