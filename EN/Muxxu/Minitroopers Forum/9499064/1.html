Tunkson
It's not complete randomness, that's what you perhaps aren't seeing.

And while skill isn't directly involved, knowledge of what weapons do what and how, along with other acquired skills, can make a huge difference.

For example, have trooper A have a sniper weapon, a motorcycle, and the "juggler" ability (juggler switches between weapons instantly). Then pit him against someone with a knife, a motorcycle, and the "rush" ability (rush makes your troop attack directly if he can). Who do you think will come on top?

Or here's another one:

Troop A has:

Gatling Gun
Heat Sensor
Tuck n Roll
Nervous
Saboteur

Troop B has:

Shotgun
Full Metal Balaclava
Charge
Adrenaline
Soldier

Who of the two do you think stands more of a chance against a horde of rats? The soldier? Wrong. Want to know why? Because when fighting a pack of rats, your armor doesn't matter. What matters against rats is range. As soon as they close in on you, or vice versa, it's over.

So it's not only about complete randomness, it can very often come down to how you align the gained skills on your troops, and which skills you assign to them in the first place.

Although, it can't be helped if you're unaware of certain things and would rather call the game crappy rather than learn the details. Ever took a bazooka with you into an extermination mission with 5 troops? That's why your team dies. Friendly fire.

And if you insist on still complaining after that, consult yourself to this.
