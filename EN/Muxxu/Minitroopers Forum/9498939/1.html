Hm, I don't think those conditions are necessary for winning extermination missions past a power rating of 50. For one, I don't have any vehicles other than a bike and I've only been having trouble with these rodants only once I reached a power rating of 60. 50+ didn't pose much of a problem.

My munitions guy doesn't have the benefit of a tank, but is luckily a sniper and so he is naturally going to be one of the last to be killed when up against the rats.

Also, I don't really suffer from the bazooka friendly fire issue that most people complain about. My bazooka wielder is actually a very valuable asset to my team. He has a bike, on point and cold blooded so he is normally the first to fire, often taking out the first wave of rats before my other troopers have even moved. After that, he normally switches weapons to his pistol. So I benefit from the fire power of the bazooka and am also spared of the friendly fire.

I try to apply some logic when forming my army. The luck is a bonus

Btw, the power rating is not all that determines how many rats you fight. If you defeat a particular number of rats, the number of rats increases in the next extermination mission. So someone could have a power rating of less than 60 but could be fighting more rats than somebody with a power rating over 80.
