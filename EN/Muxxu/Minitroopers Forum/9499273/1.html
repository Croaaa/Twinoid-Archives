My thoughts....
Extermination missions require:

1) distance
2) ammo
3) life

You need to keep the rats at a distance or they will pummel you. Set your troops to "defender" where possible to keep them as far back from the advancing rats as you can. I have most success when I set the majority of my troops to target the closest enemy. Take down the rat that is closest and open the distance back up again. Also, the further back they are when you nail them the less chance they have to hurt you if they get back up again when the next wave comes in.
If you can use paralyzing ammo then do so. Use a weapon like a Beretta - your aim is to "tag" the rat and stop it moving, then select another target: not to kill it. The others can mop up the paralyzed rats.


Without ammo you will suffer. Get a munitions specialist and deploy them in your first wave, along with as many "rapid reload" troops as possible. The longer you can keep wiping the rats out and then reloading before the next wave advance, the better. Be thoughtful about your "favourite weapon" selections. Sometimes it's better to have "none" set so that a trooper will manage his own reloading schedule. This doesn't always work.
Keep comm's officers in your reserves if possible, so that you can benefit from their help for as long as possible without them being in danger.
Set all your troops to target the rats' heads. Rats don't wear armour. Headshots can save you precious ammo in the long run.

Avoiding getting killed is a Good Thing. The rats are only half the problem, it's the friendly fire that will make you cry. If you can get your troops to spread out and keep away from the rats then you're less likely to see them injure each other. Avoid rockets apart from at the beginning of the fight or in the last throw of desperation. Limit your heavy weapon usage.

Oh, and
4) luck

You'll be needing some of that too
