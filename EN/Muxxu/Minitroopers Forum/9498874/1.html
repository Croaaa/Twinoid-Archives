Hi Tiger, nice to meet you.
well I can not say what the problem is but I can guess. One thing it may be is the relationship of movement to range. Both of these things are big factors to a shotgun guy.

Lets say that we have two equal shotgun troopers both at level 1, trooper A and trooper B.
The game starts and both are out of range, the troopers will take turns one at a time...

Your turn gos like this...
Time stops.
You: Am I in range?
Computer: No
You: Move
Computer: you are still not in range
Computer: wait for your next turn
Time starts.
*repeat*

...both A & B repeat this and start moving towards each other, slowly.

The first trooper to get within range of the other will be the first to get shot at, why? This is because the other guy will finish waiting first and this is the tricky bit...

A finishes waiting and take a turn.
Time stops.
A: Am I in range?
C: No you are one step short.
A: I move one step.
C: You are now in range.
C: wait for the next turn.
Time starts.

meanwhile B finishes waiting and take their turn.
Time stops.
B: Am I in range?
C: Yes
B: I shoot A.
C: Bang!
C: wait for the next turn.
Time starts.

Because trooper A is shot first he will probably die first.
It is the reverse to what you might think, the faster moving trooper dies first.

Watch your old replays and see if this is happening with you Tiger, if it is then you have a movmint/range relationship problem and will win only about half of the games (at best).

If you only have one trooper right now then I recommend starting again. Talk to me about it and I'll show you how to start with any level 1 trooper you like.
