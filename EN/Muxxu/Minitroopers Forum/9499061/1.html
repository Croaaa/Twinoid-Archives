The 5 week league is drawing to a close. While it would be nice to go forwards from there and have another five week league, after promotions and relegations between the divisions, that's going to be an awful lot of work for the league administrator.
I won't have the time, as I'm studying again now that summer is over, so unless someone else wants to step up and take over then the 5-week league is going to be parked until holiday season.

Some of the problems of the five week league include:
- people dropping out
- people forgetting about their matches
- confusion regarding start-times for each week
- difficulty keeping track of "fair play" with only first challenge of the week counting for scoring
- possible confusion where league matches co-incide with other competitions
- lots of work for the scorekeeper!

In order to address these issues but still have some kind of league play on the go, I'd like to propose a new competition with conditions of play as set out below.
This thread is NOT a sign-up thread, this is to discuss the mechanics of the competition. There may be some glaring errors that I've overlooked that will make the suggested conditions unworkable, so please have a read, have a think, and post any criticisms and suggestions. NB: I might not take all suggestions on board.
When things are looking pretty settled I'll make a new thread for the actual competition and we'll get going.

PROPOSED CONDITIONS FOR ROLLING LEAGUE
1) Any "infiltation" match can be counted for scoring purposes. Missions do not count. Being challenged does not count.
2) All participants in the league have a Rolling League Rating (RLR)
3) A player joining the league starts with a RLR of zero.
4) Challenges may be sent to any other league members.
5) Defeating an enemy with equal or lower RLR will score an increase of 1 point to the RLR.
6) Defeating an enemy with a higher RLR will score an increase of 5 points to the RLR.
7) Challenging and losing does not affect RLR.
8) To claim an increase of RLR the fight must be posted into the league thread, along with the following details:
Challenger (RLR score) vs Target (RLR score) - Result link - Winner - New RLR score for challenger.
For example:
Zooxx (0) vs doom squadron (0) - LINK - Zooxx wins
ZOOXX New RLR = 1
9) Posting failed challenges is fine for entertainment value, but neither player will change their rating.
10) All players are encouraged to maintain an up to date league listing and post it to the thread periodically, however it will be possible to check the last winning match any player has posted in order to look up their RLR if people don't post an up to date list for a while.

_ _ _

The idea being that people can fight as often as they like, choosing their opponents and being rewarded for defeating "better" players. The higher up the league you climb, the more of a target you become and the fewer "better" players there are for you to score points from.
Of course after a while the most active players will have huge scores, which might put new people off. Maybe all scores should be reset to zero each month and then we can have winners on a monthly basis?

Over to you lot: what do you think?

RLR
0-9: Beginner
10-24: Novice
25-49: Intermediate
50-99: Expert
100+: Master

The amount of points you get per win depends on the rank of your opponent. So, say an intermediate challenges another intermediate and wins. Result: 2 points. An Intermediate challenges a novice and wins. 1 point. Challenges a beginner and wins. 0 points. Challenge an expert and win, 3 points. Challenge a master and win, 4 points.

If we look at the (to my mind, fairly unlikely) situation where player A wins every time they challenge player B, and then player B wins every time the challenge player A:

With 3 points for a win, and each player doing all 3 challenges in a row the score goes
0,0 / 1,0 / 2,0 / 3,0 / 3,3 / 3,4 / 3,5 / 6,5 / 7,5 / 8,5 . . . and so on
With 3 points for a win, and each player doing one challenge each
0,0 / 1,0 / 1,3 / 4,3 / 4,6 / 7,6 / 7,0 . . . and so on

With 5 points for a win, doing all 3 at once:
0,0 / 1,0 / 2,0 / 3,0 / 3,5 / 3,6 / 3,7 / 8,7 / 9,7 / 10,7 / 10,12 / 10,13 . . .
With 5 for a win, taking it in turns:
0,0 / 1,0 / 1,5 / 6,5 / 6,10 / 11,10 / 11,15 / 16, 15 . . .

Could be open to abuse if two players collaborate to soft-play for each other, and farm easy points.
