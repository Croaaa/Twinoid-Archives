I started out setting everyone to headshot (even when facing people with helmets on) but now I'm experimenting with different aiming points situationally. That's what got me thinking about why there is an "anywhere!" option on the list at all... maybe choosing to aim at something costs you a few fractions of a second, which could make the difference between getting a shot off or getting shot in the face.
