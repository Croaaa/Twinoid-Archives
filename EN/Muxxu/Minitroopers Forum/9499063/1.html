This is a game for people with access to multiple armies of different types.
It's not a "scored" game, it's just for fun and bragging rights.

This is the way it works.

Everyone who wants to play declares a team. A team is made up of several different armies, with the following condition:
A team must contain armies with different numbers of troopers, starting at one and working upwards.
Examples of valid teams:
Six armies with 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6 troopers.
Three armies with 1, 2, 3 troopers.
Four armies with 1, 2, 3, 4 troopers.
Examples of teams that are not allowed:
Six armies with 1, 2, 3, 3, 3, 4 troopers. (no repeats allowed!)
Three armies with 2, 3, 4 troopers. (must start at 1!)
Four armies with 1, 2, 4, 5 troopers. (no skipping numbers!)

When you declare a team, any other player who has declared a team may choose to challenge you.
Challenges are serious business! The teams are matched up and the 1 trooper armies fight each other, then the 2 trooper armies fight each other, then the 3 trooper armies, and so on.
This goes on until there are no more matchups allowed.
Example:
Army with 1, 2, 3, 4, 5 challenges army with 1, 2, 3.
There will be three matches as part of the challenge.
The 1 vs 1, the 2 vs 2 and the 3 vs 3.
The 4 and 5 are not involved.
Each match is played on the same day and is "best of three" so the challenger should keep starting fights until they win two (they win!) or they lose two (they lose!) or they give up (they lose, and they are mocked for being a quitter!)
The team that wins the most matches is determined to have won the challenge.

You can change your declared team at any time by posting in this thread with the up-to-date list. Please take care when choosing someone to challenge that you make sure you have their most up-to-date team list.
Also, double check before the challenge starts that all armies have the correct number of troopers - if people buy extra troopers then they will have to change their selected armies in order to keep their team within the conditions of the game.

Everything clear? Good. Let the game begin.
