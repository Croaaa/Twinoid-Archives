Team-JayI would still say that Twinoid is the broken perk, whenever it is used (which can be anytime) it cancels everything else out.
I have never seen a trooper with no initiative use a Twinoid while another trooper still had some initiative to use. The very act of using the Twinoid needs a turn and so it is not really anytime.

What I have seen is fights where many troopers all had initiative at the same time, such as an army with all on point troopers. Some of these troopers may start shooting but others may do other things as normal within the same initiative time, such as using Twinoid. This would give the illusion that the Twinoid can be used anytime.

KomperaTake the pill, get a huge initiative boost, which is then reduced by the poison effect...
Poison will only do damage as the game clock is ticking, the initiative boost stops the clock (and the poison damage). Even if the poison damage would be giving an initiative penalty it would not start that penalty until there was no initiative left.

My newest thought about poison is that it's duration is in 'real time' even tho it's damage is in game time. When game time stops the poison damage stops, when it starts the damage starts, but it seems that if you just keep stoping the game time then the real time duration runs out and the poison is removed with out any damage to the trooper. I have only seen this two times so far.
