To answer this question you have to take into consideration the original MyBrute and the new MyBrute on Muxxu.

Battles and XP:
Original MyBrute gives you 3 fights daily (that's all, go away, come back tomorrow). You got 2XP for winning and 1 XP for losing. Upon gaiing enough XP you automatically level up.
Muxxu MyBrute gives you up to a minimum of 3 fights per day and a maximum of 10 fights per day (or 6-20 if you spend Muxxu Coins). You get 1XP per fight regardless of winning or losing.
MiniTrooper gives you 3 fights daily (that's all, go away, come back tomorrow). You get 2[T] for winning and 1[T] for losing. To level up a troop you have to spend X amount of [T] and you need more and more for each successive level.

When you level up:
Origonal Mybrute gives you a random ability.
Muxxu MyBrute lets you choose between two randomly generated abilities.
MiniTroopers lets you choose between two or more randomly generated abilities (default 2, but some of those powers generate more options on level-up).

Alternate modes of play:
Original MyBrute offers a tournament you can join, who's rewards for winning are [?]. I don't know because I've never gotten strong enough in it to win. a fight. XD (I tried playing religiusly but leveling up when your that far behind is so tedious it got boring).
Muxxu MyBrute offers a tournament you can join that offers you 1XP per battle won and, upon becoming champion, lets you reset your character and either choose to get the same rewards when you level up or a different tree of rewards (thus if your skill tree SUCKS you can opt for a new one each time you win a tournament).
MiniTrooper lets you spend 5[T] to unlock a second game mode that gives you 3 chances to earn 4[T] every day.

Recruits. Motion Twin games not on Muxxu always give some benefits to people you convince, con, trick or otherwise into clicking your link and making an account under your banner.
Original MyBrute gives you 1XP when you gain a pupil.
MiniTrooper gives you 100[T] for the first army to join under your banner, 50[T] each for the next 3, 10[T] each for the next 10 and (reportedly) 5[T] for the next 20 and then no more (that's 450[T] or 450XP from only 34 recruits).
Muuxu MyBrute doesn't offer the ability to gain recruits so you get nothing.

Recruit Rewards:
Original MyBrute gives you 1XP every time a pupil levels up.
MiniTrooper unlocks a 3rd game option upon gaining but a single recruit. This game option offers multiple phases which award 4[T] for each phase completed, and you can try every day from Phase 1 to get as far as possible. Total [T] gain is immeasurable and you doesn't require any of your troops actively play (though I'm betting if they did their first troop would get stronger and thus you'd get more wins per day).
Muxxu MyBrute offers nothing because you don't get recruits to begin with.

For number of battles: Muxxu MyBrute > MiniTrooper = Original Mybrute. (Point to Muxxu MyBrute)
+ For XP: This one's a toughie. Original MyBrute and MiniTroopers gives you 3-6XP daily but Muxxu MyBrute gives you the chance of earning 3-10XP daily (but you have to win at least 3 fights before your 3rd defeat to get it all) and the potential 6-20XP if you spend real life money. Since the minimum XP for constant failure is the same I'm going to give the point to Muxxu MyBrute for the chance of gaining more XP in a single day, even without spending real money.
+ For level-up options: MiniTrooper >= Muxxu MyBrute > Original MyBrute (Point to MiniTrooper)
+ For alternate game options: MiniTrooper > Muxxu MyBrute > Original MyBrute (Point to MiniTrooper)
+ For recruits: MiniTrooper > Original MyBrute. (Point to MiniTrooper)
--Note-- Original MyBrute would score higher for giving XP for EVERY pupil to join, but for sheer speed 34 recruits is a LOT faster to get than 450 recruits.
+ For recruit benefits: MiniTrooper > Original MyBrute. (Point to MiniTrooper)

Hands down MiniTrooper gives you more bang for your buck and you don't actually have to spend that buck to get all of it.

What I'd like to see added to this game is a way to keep score between factions the way Natural Chemist does for each school of alchemy, and a way for factions to battle other factions (similar to tournaments?) for those scores to be derived from. (alternate game mode, maybe don't get any [T] from it). I also wouldn't mind a way to fire or remove a bad troop so you aren't saddled with a crap soldier forever if luck doesn't go your way. You lose all the [T] you spent on that troop and have to pay the highest cost to recruit a new troop instead of the [T] you spent to recruit him originally, so it should be a carefully considered action.
