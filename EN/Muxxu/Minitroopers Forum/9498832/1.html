Basically what you're talking about is a game of Risk, which would be a nice addition to the available game options.

But, the difficulty is in either you have to sign in, which means the game is on a time limit and points are awarded for total number of zones controlled;

Or (and this is maybe preferable), there's a set amount of zones and one zone for each faction (6 in total) are the faction's Home Base that can never be attacked or taken over. All troops start there and every day you can either move or attack (so at the start everyone would only be able to move). You'd have to rely on some intelligence with your faction members or at least get in touch with a few of them for strategic planning. Note: Either your army starts pre-spawned (so you can either move or attack on your very first day) or, if the game must be purchased with [T], you get a free spawn upon purchasing the game so, again, you can either move or attack your first day).

Premise in this case is you move to control zones. A faction controls a zone if it has at least one army in the zone. To conquer a zone you either move into it (if it's empty) or you attack one of the armies currently occupying the zone (or the lone army if only one is there). If it's the case of the latter when the last (or sole) army is defeated then you can move your army into that zone to gain control of it immediately (and this is the only time you get to attack and move in the same day; if the other armies in your current zone or other adjacent zones of the recently conquered zone have already attacked or moved then they have to wait for the next day to join you). If your army is defeated and you are attacking, then your turn ends and you are done for the day. If your army is defeated and you are the defender then your army goes back to home base.

A leader board can be maintained, then, to list which faction is "leading" based on the number of armies on the map (armies in the home base are considered inactive and thus don't count) and the number of zones a faction controls.

If we want to go for a tangible reward to make the game more appealing, would say it should cost X amount of [T] to spawn your army at home base (maybe 5[T] like the Missions).
Defeating an army (as an attacker) could award you 3 [T] (low enough so you have to win two as an attacker to gain a profit and high enough to keep the reward appealing and players logging back in to fight some more).
Either armies in their home base or armies not even spawned don't count towards their faction's scorecard or even register as an attackable army, so in this way inactive accounts or people not interested in the game won't be falsifying the scorecard.
