There are a number of things I can say about this.


Caine's army losses the game when the spies do not infiltrate. The spies do not infiltrate when there is a tank.
When fighting a tank there is only a 2 in 11 chance of first wave infiltration (for each spy). This is because the tank uses up two deployment points, so the spies only have the other two deployment place in the first wave where they might infiltrate (assuming the are no Scouts).


Some of the dominant factors in the above battles are Twinoids and Frenetic combos but it seems to come down to tanks and spies.
Spies win games, Tanks stop Spies.


Seems even, however, Caine's army has room to grow, it is not at 12 troops.


_______Nevermore__Caine
Power_____177______158
Troops_____12_______10


Caine's spies are what is keeping him in the game, they are the difference between winning or losing.


Here is one of them...
Spy, On Point, Twinoid, Pump action shotgun, shotgun, Smart, etc, etc.


Apart from avoiding the spy, as the tank does, there is almost no way to fight this set up.
This spy has smart and two shotguns, 100% sabotage will be less then unreliable when the army does hit 12 troops.
If this Spy takes the first turn then they can use Twinoid and the battle is over.
The total initiative for the spy is 320 (Spy helmet 120 + On point).
To take a turn before this spy would need both On point and Suspicious +400 initiative total (or one of them and an unreliable motorbike).
But if the spy hits level 21 (this has happed once before Link) then even this combo would not stop him the spy.


Spy > all over things.
