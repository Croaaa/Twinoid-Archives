Spies deploy based on the opponent. 100% of the time if you see large scale raids with spies or if you have spies vs rats then you'll never see your spies on your side. I wrote alot already about this in the minitroopers hangout but I'm bored atm so...

Myself with 3 spies in my 10 unit army and here's what I've observed.

-Spies will "spy deploy" randomly in a match.
-"Spy deploy" is the action which your spy will insert itself in your opponents command order and deploy based on where they placed themselves.
Fun Fact: If you opponent has Scouts or Spies your chances of "spy deploy" in the first wave increases, not by random or random number generation, but by simple probability that your spy will cut in line within the first 4 units.
-Your spy can insert himself last in your opponent's army (see bottom note).
-Spies that can and will "spy deploy" in a match will equal the amount of units your opponent has.
-The ratio seems to be 1 "spy deploy" per 4 enemy units rounded down.
Example: The opponent has 5 units. Only 1 spy will deploy, it may deploy the first or second wave.
-A spy will deploy with the rest of your army if he is not in the ratio of your opponent's to spy deploy 100% of the time.

If you ever rats with your army or face someone with 12 troopers then you have a possibility to auto-lose by this logic.

If you fight anyone with 1-3 the ratio of spy deployment becomes 0. If they have 4 and he "spy deploys" himself behind the last unit then he will also not deploy. A spy will always come in with an incoming wave and your army queue count will reflect your spy waiting for an incoming wave, essentially he becomes a MIA unit. Your unit will never deploy and if your main army dies with your spy still behind enemy lines then you will lose. Same logic applies to 8 and 12 if your spy deploys behind the last unit, it's just with 4 there's a 1/5 chance that he'll be last rather than a 1/9 and 1/13 chance. But infact there's a chance that your spies will not deploy at all if they all "spy deploy" behind the last units.

Heads up for people wanting to counter spy usage.
The simplest way to have a chance for an enemy spy to not deploy is to have your main army (without scouts) is to have it in multiples of 4. Yes the chances are slim, but that's about the same amount of chance a vehicle deploys if you own one, slim. Even if you have your own spy, just make sure your army has a multiple of 4, so if you have 1 spy then have 9 units. Like I said scouts and other things such as units with survivor and a doctor with savior, which puts the target of savior directly last in your queue order and therefore putting the spy inside a guaranteed deployment will mess this up. But this tip is for the smaller armies who don't understand yet.
