Minitroopers and mybrute have in common the fact that they don't tell you exactly how their game works. People can have subtly different ideas of how certain game mechanics work. I made this thread to find out if anyone else is interested in trying to work through this for Minitroopers.

Firstly just wanted to bring up "critical" and also "accuracy", and other mechanics as well, for example, hitting sore (red) parts bonus damage, hitting the head (and other sensetive parts), and killshots.

Doomys suggests this definition in a thread where someone asked what the "critical" stat does: Critical damage is when you hit twice at the same spot. (left arm, head, chest, Right leg, etc.)

It sounded all well and good and that thread was buried but does that really tell us exactly what the critical stat on a gun does? Certainly hitting a sore body part gives bonus damage but I don't think this is what the gun stat "critical" refers to.

"Critical" is expressed as a multiplier. if you hit something twice with a sparrowhawk that would mean a sparrowhawk will multiply its damage by 50, if that is purely what critical means. That certainly seems wrong. Even very low weapons multipliers of things like 5 and 10 would be massive overkill if it worked like that.

We should keep in mind body part can be varying degrees of "injured" as they become brighter red, which possibly means it hurts more to be shot there. I have seen very rare examples of huge damage, which I attribute to shooting someone in a very injured head. What do you guys think?

Perhaps "critical" is a multiplier of the chance to get a "killshot"? (which to me, seems like a concept completely different from the bonus damage you get from hitting a sore place a second time, but you know, I might be totally wrong here, a killshot as we see it COULD POSSIBLY just happen when we hit for sufficient damage to kill in one of the especially sensitive body parts)

So what I'm speculating is that if your bullet is already heading for the gut, lung, head or heart, the critical multiplier would make that bullet more likely to trigger the text to pop up and for the guy to drop dead instantly.

In that case, where does accuracy come into this? Accuracy obviously helps you to not miss, would it be safe to assume it makes you more likely to hit your favourite target? This would indirectly help out with killshots in a couple of ways (making sure the bullet hits + making the bullet more likely to hit where you want it).

Another thing we could discuss is where you aim at. I heard someone suggest that aiming at the gut makes you less likely to miss, which would make sense in a literal way in that there are body parts all around the gut, whereas if you shot at a hand there aren't many body parts surrounding it. Do you guys think that aiming at the extremities (hand, head, feet) makes you more likely to miss?

A counterpoint to this is that the troopers generally just shoot random body parts anyways, so it doesn't matter that much. Perhaps it would be most relevant to snipers then, who hit their favourite target the most often.

I'm pretty sure I noticed that a shot to the head when the head wasn't already injured yet already gets some bonus damage as well, but I might be wrong here.

Do you guys think there are many differences between the four possible killshot locations? The fact that the head is protected by the helmet would help to balance what I thought i perceived above.

On the contrary, maybe the head is the biggest and easiest to hit of all the killshots and THAT is what the helmet block balances. If that is the case, maybe every killshottable body part can take non kill shot bonus damage.

The purpose of this thread is for people who want more than the copout kind of answers that you might get in the short Q and A threads. I want to try to really work out the mechanics of this game with you guys. Please let me know if you think I'm wrong or right about anything, if I'm looking at anything in the wrong way, or if you have any ideas about this sort of thing.

Replays are especially welcome to demonstrate any points/opinions you have on specifics on how anything works.
