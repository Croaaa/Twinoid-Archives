1) Desert Eagle has longer range, better accuracy and is armour piercing. If I have any of the following upgrades: battle ready, trigger happy, explosive rounds then I take the Eagle. Ideally set trooper to defensive stance, so that he will pull back out of range of miniguns and other pistols and fire from safety. A weapon with a minimum range limit such as Assault Rifle means the trooper has to step away from a rat or a melee trooper before firing? Perfect. No more chasing, just shooting.

5) Dodger stops you getting injured, means you can fight more effectively. Also it prevents your aim period from being interrupted and target lost if you were to get hit. Lucky charm is of more value for snipers/rocketeers who are at the back of the field and not exposed to bursts of fire, but dodger still better I think.

Hi Cripto,
it is now certain that there are three ways to deploy the light lank, the heavy tank is still a mystery but we are assuming they are similar.

The three ways are normal deploymint, Talky-Walky deploymint and support deploymint (Comms Officier). If you change the order of your troop deploymint in the Command my troops page then you can increase the chances.

All troops with light tanks in all waves have 'normal deploymint chances', but tank troops that are in the first wave can 'only' use normal deploymint (chances x1).
Troops in a later wave uses the tank are moved into the first wave for that battle, so they to have 'normal deploymint chances'.

If a tank trooper has the starting position in the second wave then they have other deploymint chances as well.
If you have a Comms Officier they may use the support order in your first wave, this will bring in the waiting tank trooper and let them reroll the tank chances (chances x2). It is the same if you have a Talky-Walky in your first wave.

Normal deploymint in your case is scout dependent, Talky-Walky deploymint needs the trooper to stay alive and support orders are not reliable. The chances can be almost dubbed.
