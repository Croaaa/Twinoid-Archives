This just popped into my head, and I thought it could be cool.
First, a pool of 2n+2 randomly generated soldiers (n=agreed upon team size) is presented to each side, and a called coin flip decides who picks first. The soldiers would also be within a determined level range (i.e. 5-7), and there are always two extra soldiers in the pool. Finally, the two drafted teams would fight it out for 4-6 tokens. I was also thinking that this could be a mission from time to time.

Roluth's Draft Game For Dummies (if I understood correctly):

You do battle with another player using troops which are randomly created for the purpose of that battle (i.e. not your usual armies).

Both armies will have the same number of soldiers, and you and the other player decide between you how many that will be.

Instead of you each being given a random army, there is a pool of randomly generated soldiers with random weapons and skills that you take turns to draw from to create the army that you think will give you the best chance of winning the battle. The randomly generated soldiers would all be within a certain level range (e.g. Level 5 up to Level 7).

The pool is large enough that there are enough soldiers to form two armies of the size you both agreed, plus an extra two. This means that when you have both finished chosing your armies, there will be two soldiers left over. These are the ones that neither side wanted and play no further role in the game.

The rest of the soldiers, which are now divided into two teams, do battle to see which player made the best choices and will be victorious. The prize for winning would be a small number of tokens.
