Hacksaw
Every day the list changes, and it also changes when you recruit one of the 5 listed.

There are some abilities worth waiting for, "Smart" being one of them. It all comes down to preference, and I'd also suggest variety as far as weapons go. (as in, a team of nothing but snipers could be in trouble.)

I will note that all troopers will always have one of:
- Pistol
- Shotgun
- Assault Rifle
- Sniper

Just in case you try and find a trooper with a better starting weapon, sorry, but will always have one of those at least.

In my opinion, "Shotgun" seems to be really bad, and I'll avoid any troop with that as a starting weapon regardless of their other weapon/ability. "Pistol" is another one I usually try and avoid only because it seems to be fairly weak, though if their second slot is a good weapon, I'll accept a pistol to bias them towards using the alternate, better weapon. Right now I'm looking for a trooper with "pistol" and "dual pistols" mainly for flavor and partially for variety (and partially because I've seen some pretty bad-ass "dual pistols" wielders).

Guys, try to Say THE BEST skills and weapons lol"The best" is subject to opinion. MOS-TEK deals more damage per shot, and sometimes that's the better option.

In some cases a weapon or skill isn't as useful on it's own but is better when combined with other weapons or skills.

Loader is somewhat wasted on a weapon that only holds one or two rounds. Trigger happy is pretty useless with guns that hold only one round, and for guns that hold 2+ rounds, it works even better when combined with Nimble Fingers since in that case, you will always load 2 shells at a time. MOS-TEK + Trigger Happy + Nimble Fingers is (or was) a popular combo for massive first round damage.

Battle Ready is great if you have one or more weapons with a huge clip (such as the Assault Rifle or the Minigun).

Speaking of gats, there's some room for debates. The Minigun has better accuracy and a bigger clip, but the Commanche Auto (another gat) deals more damage per hit and has a quicker recovery time.

If you participate in the "recruit of the heart" thread to gather yourself some recruits, you'll probably find yourself leveling up those recruits on your own to make them effective in your raids. Might be a better idea to use your best intuition leveling up your raiders (be sure to bookmark their HQ links so you don't lose them) to get a look at and feel for different skills and abilities before actively recruiting and leveling your main army. That will also give you first-hand experience with different weapons and skills.

Hacksaw
By itself the Infernal Tube may not be the greatest, but with the right skill set (Unshakable + Cold Blooded, for example) and it becomes a dangerous weapon indeed. Throw On Point into the mix or get the trooper tagged by a commando for a Solo Mission and it has the potential to knock out an entire enemy wave in one shot.

Choose the Infernal Tube if you're looking for raw power.
Choose Bazooka M1 if you're looking for accuracy.
Chose Bazooka M25 if you're looking for speed between successive shots.

I'm wary to recommend the Rocket Launcher because of it's low accuracy and high revovery. The only other difference between it and the M1 appears to be that the Rocket Launcher is NOT a heavy weapon (which becomes a moot point of you get Sturdy, which I'd recommend for the +3HP even if you don't have a heavy weapon on the trooper).

Bazookas and sniper rifles both can result in some rapid kills, and thus some rapid wins, but both types of weapons are on the slower side of firing and reloading. Best when used with other troops to provide cover fire, especially if said allies also have the Bait skill to draw enemy aggro.
