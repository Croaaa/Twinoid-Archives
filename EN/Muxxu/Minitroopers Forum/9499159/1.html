Tunkson
There is no such thing as "better", it depends how it is used.

Comms officers can make the whole team benefit, but, so can soldiers. By having a soldier, you can send him into the line of fire to draw enemy fire as a decoy so then those in the back, especially those with bazookas that need a long time to get ready, have the opportunity to fire. Such a decoy is missing with comms officers.

The command that comms officers relay don't happen every time either. I'd say you may have one command every 30 seconds to a minute. If you have a tight fight, the battle may be over long before then.

What you should do instead is look at your army and determine which class would suit yours more. (In my experience, soldiers are by far the strongest class, since they seem to be initially immune to headshots whereas comms officers are not, and they also do more damage with some skills than other classes do.)

Tunkson
Not all the time, but majority of the time. There are ways of achieving this. Certain skills, certain positions, allow for that. A lot of players choose their favorite target to be "the closest" or "the biggest threat". So all you need to do is make your soldier's fighting style "hunter", and he'll jump into the fray, or at the very least pulling ahead of others, thus drawing the enemy's fire in the process.

That why I like my second soldier so much. He has a motorcycle, so he rides out in front of the others. Having glue grenades to lower the enemy's initiative, and having a machine gun to run interference on the enemy troops, pretty much makes him the first target for the enemies. So while he's being targeted, my snipers or bazooka guy can do their thing from afar.

The point is, one cannot downplay the importance of any class out there. It depends on how you'd use that particular class. Some people could put a comms officer to better use. Some people could put a soldier to better use. If it came down to a one-on-one fight between a soldier and anyone else out there - and in tight matches, it does - my money's on the soldier. Always.
Not to mention the initial immunity to headshots. Comms officers and several other classes get killed from it, but soldiers just get their helmets knocked off and receive maybe 2 damage at the most.
(And don't let him get things like a bulletproof vest, full metal balaclava, and/or heavy armor. Then you're just looking at a walking tank.)
