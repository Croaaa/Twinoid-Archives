Just so you know, the percentage indicates how many enemies have at least 1 sabotaged weapon. Check all your reports and it will add up. How exactly the number of sabotaged weapons is calculated is a complete mystery and does not appear to be related to that percentage in any way. In short, the percentage seems to be just something they throw in at the end after it's been calculated to show the % of enemies sabotaged. (NOT the % of weapons btw). The only case where this seems to not apply is against rats, where there are no weapons to sabotage, and the % gives odd values that are low, but not quite 0.

My theory is it somehow calculates how many weapons are sabotaged based on how many "sabotage points" you have and some other mysterious factors, picks that many weapons at random, then the troopers affected have a chance to block that with their abilities (smart, suspicious etc.). Finally it produces a % of how many enemies have at least 1 sabotaged weapon for user convenience. By "sabotage points" I am referring to how many levels worth of sabotage you have, similar to comms. points.
