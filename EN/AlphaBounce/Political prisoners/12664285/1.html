I may have found the in-game explanation what there is no sun in this system:

It's not a real planetary system at all!

What it is, is a sophisticated three-dimensional simulation, which is projected directly into the prisoners' brains, while they're really kept in a comateuse state in a large prison-hospital facility!


The prisoners subjected to this treatment aren't really criminals either. They're just dangerously anti-social, and this "punishment" is really a form of therapy.

After a while, if the patient has grown to appreciate the benefits of following orders and being a productive member of society, a representation of Earth will suddenly appear in his (and only his) simulation, offering jim the chance to return to a normal life.

The FURI organization was planted into this simulation on purpose to act as a permanent temptation to the patients. Those who choose to follow FURI in its arnarchic ways can enjoy the illusion of liberty that they promise, but will never be allowed to leave the simulation again.

And the parallel universe was introduced as an alternative option for those patients who did not give in to the temptation of FURI, but decided they did not want to return to a normal life on Earth.


We've been given access to this forum to train our social competence, based on a system of helping each other out with advice and companionship.


This also explains how each new prisoner can be sent to fulfil the same missions over and over again. We can tell each other in the forum how we fared on the various missions (again, strengthening out social competence), but the missions themselves will always be there for next prisoner to solve.
