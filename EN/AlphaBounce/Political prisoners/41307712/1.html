You're going to need a healthy chunk of cash to buy the Alphabounce IP. Don't buy the game itself, as the cost to rip it out of the Twinoid system and make it completely stand-alone will be expensive and you'll probably get a negative ROI on that. Sure, you'd then have a copy of the codebase and user data that's under your complete control but you might lose most of the current audience as they probably enjoy the perks that Twinoid affords as a network of games

If you could buy the IP off of them, the agreement should be to put an ad for your version (which I assume would be "Alphabounce 2") on this existing game in exchange for a license to use the Alphabounce name on MT's existing products. You can then use your connections to make the new game and do what you want with it


Or to put it a different way: I doubt MT is selling, and you really should just try to make your own game if you truly think it's lucrative to do so. A new game would do much better than a refresh of an old one, and you don't need the Alphabounce name (in my opinion).
