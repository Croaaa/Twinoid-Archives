I enjoy this game, and would like to continue playing it. However, I am not particularly interested in plopping down 50 € for a couple of weeks of play time. I haven't played long, but from what I can see, this game is fairly well thought out and has good potential. The only part I disagree with is the payment method.

The game itself has a fixed cost to distribute divided per player. With the current payment method available, however, each player pays an amount that scales based on how much they want to play. In essence, it's like "milking your customers for all they've got." But, as any successful business knows; the real profit comes from balancing your expenses with an even fee provided to all your customers.

When a player REALLY likes playing this game, they pay obscene amounts of money to play, right? But what about the player who only has mild interest?

Either they:

A. Patiently play 3 plays at a time each day, never paying.
B. Get bored and forget about the website.
C. Pay maybe once or twice, then proceed with A. or B.

Currently, the game looks like what it basically is - a money hoarding scheme. Essentially, it is my opinion that the game's payment structure could be converted to something like this:

Option A: Pay for your plays like currently, but change the pricing to be somewhat lower.

Option B: Memberships ranging from 1 month to 1 year, as described below (example figures):

Membership A gets 125 plays a month, at 2.5 € a month.
Membership B gets 325 plays a month, at 5 € a month.
Membership C gets 750 plays a month, at 10 € a month.
Membership D gets unlimited plays, at 20 € a month.

Then, you can offer 2/3/6/12 month plans at a price that is progressively cheaper per month based on the length of time. For example, a person paying for a 12-month plan could get Membership C at 9 € a month.

Buying plays outside of a membership would be slightly more expensive than membership based pay - for example, 2.5 € might only pay for 100 plays.

Basically, I think it would be beneficial to both you and your customers to change your payment scaling to a system similar to this. For one, the customer can still buy additional plays if they go over their monthly allotment. But it would also encourage more longer-term players that won't be driven away by the restrictive pay-your-way system currently in place.

The summary:

By appealing to a wider audience via more customer-friendly payment options, you profit less per player, but have more players to profit from.
