Nope, keeping track of statistics wouldn't take up much space on a server at all. Just add a simple variable to each of our accounts for each individual statistic. The number of explorations also would be irrelevant as that's just a simple value.

If you're still concerned about size, when implementing these statistics, they may not need anything more complicated than an 'int' type variable, which is 16 bits, or 2 bytes. It appears as though there are roughly ~20k people who use this, but lets be generous and say ~50k. That would be roughly a grand total of ~100 mb total accumulative memory usage per stat. Well thats pretty big you say.

Take a look at the 23 different percentages of planets explored at the bottom of "My Page." Those are probably 2-3 times larger double values, but even if they were small ints, those alone would take up ~2.2 gb.

So even if we had a whopping 50 different stat values, that would take up ~4.8 gb of space on a computer somewhere. But ints only give us 65,535 values, so lets be really generous and take a 32 bit, or 4 byte double variable (which gives us 4,294,967,295 values) and double all these values, giving us a grand total of 9.5 gb for 50 statistics per 50k people...
