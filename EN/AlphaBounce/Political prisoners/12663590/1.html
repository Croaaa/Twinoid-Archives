Wow, I am such a math geek. As I was deliberating over the age old FURI vs. ESCorp question, I decided to find out exactly which was better: the Syntrogenic Accelerator or the Lithium Retrofusor. Being the over-obsessive nerd that I am, I decided to actually work out all the statistics on an Excel spreadsheet, plot the data on a graph, and observe the results.

The process was a bit lengthy, so I won't post it unless someone asks (might help if someone wants to double-check my math, too ). But after all was said and done, I came to this preliminary answer:

The Retrofusor is superior ONLY IF it will save more than 25% of the total distance from start to finish. I.E., if you want to travel 200 sectors, and the Retrofusor will bring you more than 50 sectors closer to your destination, then it's better than the Accelerator. Note that this is assuming you can travel the full length of your motor's capacity every time, and that you don't screw up any levels.

I'm still working on how to get a more accurate answer, but that's what I've got so far. If I'm boring any of you, or if I'm just spouting old information, please feel free to call me out.

Here's another way to look at it:

If you are at [-100][0] and you need to get to [200][0]... 300 sectors. This is considering if you move at one square a day in a straight line (which, of course, is almost never the case).

4 / day = 75 days.
3 / day = 100 days.

(Retrofuser), then 3 / day = 50 days.

From my understanding, the retrofuser can be used any number of times you want for free. Sure it dumps you back at [0][0], but that's generally a big time saver if you're doing cleaning missions (which are generally scattered everywhere) or simply trying to return to known space after procuring a star or some other object.

This is what it comes down to: as glamorous as another play sounds, it doesn't progress you in the story of the game as quickly as the retrofuser. If you are playing the game simply to play the game and bust up some bricks, then the accelerator is for you because it allows you to play one more level a day for free. If you want to work on progressing the story line and accomplish goals in the game, then the retrofuser will be what you want because you are better able to cope with flying all over the universe completing tasks.

Make sense?
