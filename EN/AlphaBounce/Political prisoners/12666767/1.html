It needs some time to get used to, but once you get more comfortable with it you can keep the balls going for quite a while.

About losing one most of the time, there's a relatively easy way to get around that. Do NOT fire the balls by clicking, but rather move the pad just enough outside of the screen for one of the balls to shoot. The second one should shoot automatically after a few seconds, but it won't be in sync with the first ball, meaning that they should be returning to the bottom of the screen at different times, most of the time, so you don't have to rush to get both.
Using this "technique" if you want to call it that, you can start well and unless you get unlucky with the level layout, keep both balls for a while, hammering away at the bricks. No such feeling as the one you get when you hammer away at tough brown bricks with two balls, breaking them twice as fast as you would with a single ball.
