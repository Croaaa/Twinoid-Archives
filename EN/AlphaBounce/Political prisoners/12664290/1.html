I also had a PC shareware version once, that came with a level editor.

It had much more realistic physics than AB. The direction in which the ball bounced was not determined by the location on the panel where it hit, but purely on the angle of the impact. The panel itself was linked directly to the mouse, and could be moved both horizontally and vertically (in a limited area from the bottom of the screen).

As the panel shifted sideways, it also tilted slighly, so you could affect the angle of ball deflection. And you could accelerate or decelerate the ball by moving your panel into the ball, or pushing it away after the initial impact.

The choice of powerups was quite limited, but the were all commulative. I think it had Laser, Glue (Control), Larger Panel, Larger Balls (5 size levels in total), Multiball all existing balls split in 3), and Powerball (balls would smash through blocks and continue flying for a couple of seconds after collecting).

It also had new balls (of various sizes) dropping from blocks. These would fall like powerups, and behave like balls as soon as the hit the panel. One level (in the shereware version) was full of these dropping balls, Larger Ball and Multiball powerups, so after a few seconds of playing, you'd end up with upwards of 20 balls on screen.
