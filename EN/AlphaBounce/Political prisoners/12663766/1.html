You have to finish Salmeen Part 2 to get legs installed on your ship, for starters.

When you have the legs, to land you have to approach your landing spot - avoiding obstacles (usually rocks) and bring your ship in slowly to about 1 to 1 1/2 the height of your ship above the flat ground. Your legs will automagically extend and plant themselves once you're in close enough and not moving too fast.

Once you're on teh ground, you can collect any minerals that are on the surface and/or visit an alien if he has a house there. Aliens will give you (generally useless advice), minerals, , or certain mission objects (well, mission objects depending on how far along the storyline you are) and some of the surface aliens are merchants.

What sort of trouble are you having?

You need to find very-close-to-level surface on the ground. It can be off-level a little, as the legs that sprout out of your envelope can make up for that when you touch down. But not by much.

You have a fuel gauge in the upper-right corner. When the red runs out, you are out of fuel and will crash-land, which is considered failure.

You burn fuel when you are holding down the first mouse button. (Or whatever the keyboard equivalent to this is.) Flames jet out of the bottom of your envelope while the button is pushed down, and your envelope accelerates directly away from the flames. If you've ever played a Lunar Lander game, it works the same way. (You could probably find a free one on the net and play it a bit in a web browser, if you're not familiar with lander-type games. That would really help here.)

Your envelope swivels to point its engines directly away from your mouse pointer, so that burning fuel will increase the envelope's speed in the direction of your mouse pointer. On a keyboard, you probably have Rotate Left and Rotate Right keys instead. Just remember to keep your engine on the bottom.

Let the ship drop a bit, tapping on the fuel slightly from time to time in order to keep your fall speed from becoming too great, as it takes time to slow down the envelope. You don't want to get close to the surface at a high fall speed, or you won't have time to slow down to a safe landing speed.

Look for a very level place on the surface to land. Note that there will frequently be things like rock formations and plants and buildings that look like they are simply background decoration. They are not decoration. They are obstacles, and colliding with them will make the envelope explode, which fails your landing attempt. Your level surface needs to not have any of these background objects, and needs to be wide enough that you can comfortably fit the entire envelope in there without colliding with any of those background objects. Don't land on the buildings -- land on the ground.

Angle the ship left or right and use the engines to center yourself over the landing area, then use them again to cut your sideways movement to nothing. If you're drifting sideways while landing, you're more likely to collide with something.

From that point, it's simply a matter of controlling your descent at a slow enough speed that the envelope's legs will pop out just above the surface and stop your fall. But you have to do all of this without running out of fuel, so use it conservatively. You don't need to move quickly left/right when looking for a landing place, so long as you find one before you run out of gas.

After you land, you can click on the spacesuited icon in the lower left corner (even if you don't have it yet, provided you're on a planet with a hospitable environment), which will make your pilot hop out. You can walk him around the surface and look for whatever it is you wanted to land here for in the first place. Then walk him back, stand him directly under the engines, and tap the mouse button to make him jump up. He should land in the envelope, and you can just mash the mouse button to take back off again.
