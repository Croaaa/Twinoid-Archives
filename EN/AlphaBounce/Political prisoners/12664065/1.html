I used to work as technician of a small freighter ship called the Lost Cause. The ship was not in the best of condition or had the best technology, but it had enough for what we needed, and it kept our costs down, as our entire company of a half dozen furrs worked on the ship.

Suddenly we where attack by pirate scum. As quickly as the battle started, it was over. For whatever reason, they decided to abandon us with our tails between our legs with heavy damage. A couple days latter, we managed to make it to the Elysium Space Station.

Once docked, our captain went to HQ to fill out the paperwork and the rest of us went to work repairing our ship. After working for nearly 30 straight hours, I realize how long I've been working and that I haven't seen the captain. I check with the rest of the crew and they too haven't seen him or been able to contact him and starting to get worried. Eventually we decided to go looking for our captain in case he was in trouble.

When we returned to our ship, we found a dozen heavily armed officers. One of my crew mates asked if they found our captain yet. The officer in charged chuckled and said "Why yes, we have found your captain. He's in custody!" After all of us stood their stunned for a moment or two, I asked what the charges where. The officer in charged listed off failure to pay Docking and Entry fees, assaulting an Elysium citizen, and then we found out the cargo was "contraband" (we refused to ship contraband) and a few other chargers. One of crew mates asked when the trial will be, and one of the officers said probably in a year the earliest! Again, all of us where stunned that we where stuck here for a year or more. One of my other crew mates asked if they where aloud into the ship to get their personal belongings. The officer said no that the ship is being impounded until the trial as evidence. The crew mate then asked if they could be escorted in to get their belongings and the officer in charge said no, only forensics are allowed into the ship till the trial is over. Another crew member asked if we could talk to our captain, and the officer in charge shook his head no. Then he told us to leave, and if we are caught loitering around the area, we will be arrested and that the ship will be guarded and instructed to shoot anyone trespassing near the ship first, then ask questions.

When we left the ship we all pooled together what we all had on us and found it depressing little. With the inflated market of the Elysium system, it would only last a couple days if we tried very hard. We decided to look for a lawyer first to try to help us out, but we soon discovered that there where no private lawyers on Elysium they all work for the government, and refused to defend us against the government event if we where able to afford to have them review our case. We tried to contact some family and friends to try to get them to forward us some money, but we where told after paying for the call that we needed a permit for interstellar communication, and that the office will be closed for the weekend.

We decided to check out a hotel for the night, and the filthiest flee ridden lodges was beyond their budget. And because it seemed that the entire station had a no loitering law, we had to stumble throughout the station station with a patrol man following us from a distances (probably looking for some reason to arrest us).

We decided to look for a job to try to make some money, but none of the business would hire us without a work permit which cost more money then any one of us had. None of the other ships that where docked needed to hire more crew and security was too tight to try to sneak onto a ship to get somewhere else.

By Sunday afternoon, we spent our last pennies on a single package of Twinkies to share (we already sold everything we had on us except for our clothes for a fraction of what they where worthy). As we finished taking our bite's worth of the Twinky, we where surrounded by at least two dozen armed officers! One of the officers informed us that we where under arrest for vagrancy and proceeded to roughly arrest us. One of my crew mates opened her mouth to say something but she was stunned by a shock-stick.

Once we arrived at police station, we where stripped naked, and shoved into cells that was too small for me to lay down unless I curled my self in a ball, and too short for me to stand straight. The walls of the cell was solid and the lights never turned off. There was no way of keeping track of how long I was in that cell without food, water, or bathroom facilities. When they came to get me, they where furious at the mess I had made due to a lack of facilities. They proceeded to drag me in front of a magistrate still naked. When I tried to defend myself I was told to shut up. I made the mistake of trying to defend myself again, a few minutes later, and I was roughly gaged. I was then charged guilty of insufficient funds to pay the air tax. I was sentenced to work off my debt which included all the fees and charges, which would increase semi-annually at a rate of 30%!

I was then dragged down a maze of condors, many of the walls seemed like they could have been one way mirrors so people could watch me, but I couldn't see them. When we got to the end of the condors, The guard locked a collar around my neck and got a tag out of a machine there and attached it to my collar. When I asked what is going on, the guard answered, "You've just been sold. The State recouping its expenses in dealing with dangerous criminals like you..."

The next thing I know, I'm locked into an envelope, and here I am. Since our arrest, have not been able to get any word of the condition of any of my crew mates.

I am quite sure the pirates, the Elysium government, and ESCorp was working together to pull this off, and I am still looking for someway to get revenge and to prevent other innocent people from falling into this trap!
