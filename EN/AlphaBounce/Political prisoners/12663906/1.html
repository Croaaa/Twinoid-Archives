I believe, what Mr No Help is referring to, is the ability to place a marker IN THE GAME. Not to do with any 3rd party site or information.

I can totally agree with this to a great extent. All the 3rd party information is excellent, but, there are some features which would be extremely helpful here.

Having the ability to mark a location on your map, like a way point set up, would be extremely valuable. When you find a mineral rich grid, it weould be nice to be able to mark it so that if you are going through the area again, you can drop in when thee minerals have replenished. The concept is used in many games. The best example I can think of is the Baldurs Gate game, where you could mark your own reference points on the mini map.
By allowing maybe 20 of these points to be placed on the map, you could then have a guide to where you want to be. And, if need be, only put a reference number on the map, and have a section added to the main menu thingy for a notepad, where you could explain your markings....

Would be even more helpful for things like knowing where houses that need a key are located as you find them, so if you ever find the key, you know where you have to go to get them.

Basically, a way within the game to keep track of information that you think you may find useful later on.


The second big thing that really needs to be done, is a ZOOM OUT!!!!!!!
There is nothing worse than thinking you are going the right way, only to find you have gone too far in one direction.
To give a CLASSIC example. You travel from Soupaline out to Spygnosis along axis -2 but, after doing some wayward exploring, you want to be able to fast track your way bcak over to the other side of the origin. Trouble is, you are on axis -32, but you cant remember what axis you traveled out there on. Being able to zoom the play field out, would then let you see where your previously explored area is, so you can plot your best route.
