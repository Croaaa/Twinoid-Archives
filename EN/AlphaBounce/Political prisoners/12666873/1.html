Reading your posts, I find that you really overcomplicate something quite simple.

Consider the difference in the x- and y-coordinates, then divide that ratio with how far in both coordinates you can with your generator and radar. If you're near the origin and going more straight than diagonal, you can consider explored levels.

For example:
[0][0] --> [80][120]
For me, I can go in x:y, 0:8, 1:7, 2:6, and so forth.
80:120 can be reduced to 2:3, and 3:5 is closest to that. Also, I could travel 3:5 whether I'm traveling through explored or unexplored area.
80/3, 120:5 --> 26.67, 24
Since 24 is the smaller number, let's consider after going 24 x 3:5, I end up in [72][120]. Now it's 8:0, which is perfect if I was in an explored area, meaning I would only need 1 . But, if I was in unexplored area, I would need 2.
