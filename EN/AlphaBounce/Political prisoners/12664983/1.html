FURI was here
ESCorp is a loser

Ok, but seriously now.
You shouldn't care much about each side other than the gameplay value they have. Storywise, ESCorp is the big evil empire and FURI is the small rebellion. That's the basics and all you need to care about.
Now, on to gameplay:
To get to FURI, you need to cross the Karbonis Belt (an Asteroid Belt easily visible on this map) BEFORE doing 52 clean-up missions. But it doesn't end here. You need a specific upgrade, the Space Suit, because you'll need to land on a planet that doesn't have a breathable atmosphere. That planet is Nalikors, located at approximatelly [66][154].
To stay with ESCorp, just don't cross the Belt until you've done 52 clean-up missions.

On to the benefits and disadvantages of each side:
About FURI:
The upgrades you can get by FURI only are:
Saumir's Twinner: You start each life on each level with TWO balls instead of one. Both a blessing and a curse, but mostly really a blessing.
The Syntrogenic Accelerator: Gives you 1 more per day. While the trip you have to do to get everything is pretty long, you'll do it anyway for another mission which both FURI and ESCorp get, so you can couple the two missions together.
The Asphalt Project ball: This ball goes through about 4 or 5 bricks (less if it has to go through brown bricks or anything that requires more hits to destroy), and then acts like a normal ball, until it returns to your pad, at which point it can go through another so many bricks. It's located pretty far away, but many would say that it's the best upgrade in the game. Oh, and it combines well with Saumir's Twinner, I like to say that FURI doesn't give you just the best ball, it gives you two of the best ball.
Another general advantage is that FURI's equivalent to ESCorp's clean-ups, liberations, are much easier to complete. They only require you to play one level, and give you 75 minerals. ESCorp's clean-ups give 10 minerals per level cleared, so you could say that FURI gives more for each level; but then again, ESCorp gives more for each mission.
There is only one disadvantage I can think of for FURI. Remember that permanent extension you got by gathering 7 parts from Lycans? Yeah you lose it. There's a bright side to it, in that you can evade some stuff easier, but yeah, still.

Now, as for ESCorp.
ESCorp's upgrades are as follows:
YET ANOTHER permanent extension. It's probably the last thing you'll get from ESCorp, as it's mission is the last one you unlock.
The Lithium Retrofusor: It instantly teleports you back to [0][0], and has unlimited uses. It's really useful for bringing you back from far-away locations (but then again, at a point where an ESCorper would have used the Retrofusor, I followed a path that got me tons of minerals, bought the first Surface Engine upgrade from the place where it's available at the lowest price, and a missile).
The AR-SRX, 4th missile upgrade: Just like the 3rd missile, but it now covers a 5x5 area from the brick it hits. You can basically spam these to win an otherwise impossible level, but if you need to break through Silvers, the 3rd missile can do the job. The 5th one just does it better.
The FORA 7R-Z mines: When you start a level, these attach to some bricks, and when these are destroyed, they explode similar to an AR-SRX missile explosion. All good things end here though, because you only get 1 for free, and have to buy the other 3; and oh boy do they cost a lot. Still, even with 1 of them I guess this is a good upgrade, I can't see it doing you any good.
As for ESCorp's disadvantages, well, there aren't any other than CLEAN-UPS CLEAN-UPS CLEAN-UPS.

There's a small other quest from which I'd say both sides benefit almost equally (ESCorp gets some and FURI gets less but also a good amount of minerals), so I'm not covering it.

Missions for each side are unlocked by doing Clean-Ups/Liberations. The last mission each side gives you is unlocked at 100 of their specific missions.

So yeah, if you are staying with ESCorp I hope you like doing clean-ups.

Ok so they both give alot and good items but how do you get them? Like the item for 1 more from FURI what mission do I have to do for that? And how fast after you joined FURI do you get the mission?

To unlock the mission for the Syntrogenic Accelerator you have to do 20 liberations. The mission itself requires you to fly almost a full circle on the Karbonis Belt, to collect 7 parchments from specific locations.

While it IS a time consuming task, the mission to collect the 12 Karbonis Tablets also requires yout to do the same thing, and is shared by both factions, so don't think you'll be getting out of your way to do it. Conveniently, once you gather the prizes for both missions are picked up at Nalikors once you've gathered every item.

You will unlock the mission that requires you to clear Holovan first, after doing 5 liberations. Even if you don't complete it, the mission for the Syntrogenic Accelerator will be unlocked at 20 liberations and you may proceed t ocomplete them in any order. The choice is yours, but you may want to do the Syntrogenic Accelerator one first to get the most out of it, and to gather enough minerals for drones (you'll need, from my experience, at least 3 for Holovan; not to say it's impossible with less, but just saying).
