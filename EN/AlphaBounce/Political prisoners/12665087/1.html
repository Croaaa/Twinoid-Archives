the problem lies in the coding for the paddle itself...and a bit of a combination with that from the curvyness of the paths for W and K powerups. The paddle is programmed to rebound the ball at specific angles (basically anything equal or less than what it can reflect the ball at on it's own) ... with the K and W, the ball's path is curved, and thereby while it may look like it should hit the paddle, it's on it's way up or at so much of an angle that the paddle thinks that the ball is coming from below ... it's coded this way for just in case the K were to actually dip down below the paddle and come back up ... if your paddle was in the way and the paddle were coded to reflect at the angle no matter where it hit the contact circles, you'd have the K / W ball bounce off the bottom of the paddle and end up doing one of two things, either completely glitching itself into/around the paddle, or fall off the bottom of the screen.
