The missile map can be bought from a couple of merchants spread across the system.

The exact locations can be found on various fan-created info sites (which, again, doesn't answer your original question).

The locations of merchants in general is marked on your star map. Without outside information, I guess you'd just have to try out a few...

As to the antimatter nuclei, the ABWiki states:
The locations of the Antimatter Nuclei are only revealed to you in-game at certain landing sites. There are various ETs at different landing sites who tell you where the four are, however only a few of these have been logged. Landing is expensive (it costs a hydrogen capsule every time you land on a square), and also very hit-and-miss with most planets being very large. As if this wasn't enough, many of the ETs who have been logged are on planets outside the Karbonis Belt, and so for people who are planning to stick with the ESCorp this delays collecting the Antimatter Nuclei a LOT. All of these factors combined unforunately means that it's nearly impossible to find the Antimatter Nuclei without using a spoiler of some kind, as finding these people is almost as unlikely as finding the Nuclei themselves!
So, yeah, this might get a bit difficult without outside help...

Question: Are the Nuclei marked on your star map if you have the mission?

If so, you'd only have to get close enough to see them on the screen, which still leaves a lot of space to cover, but is not nearly as random as needing to hit the exact right coordinates by pure chance.

Oh, sorry Layze, I did not read your post carefully enough

Before help files existed, a long time ago, there were bold explorators (Marie84, Turok, Jessicaeup and later Acopis, me and some others) who visited the space to discover planets and various objets .

The planetes nearby the origin (Soupaline, Lycans, Tiboon, etc) are very easy to find. Some others are revealed more or less precisely by a mission briefing (Spignysos, Holovan, Khorlan ...).

Once we know enough planets and were able to land, we could transport ETs towards other planets and thus find some others. I think D-Tritus was discovered this way (from Lycans or Holovan).

In the meantime, we were also able to speak to ETs and get hints for sereval objets. The four antimatter nuclei, 4 out of 5 pink crystals and most missiles were discovered this way .

The remainders were discovered little by little by explorator's long runs. This is especially true for farthest planets (Chagarina, Volcer, Balmanch, Folket which were found quite lately .

The same thing holds for the last unknown missiles - actually, you can now find all but one in the help files.

Only I know exactly where the very last unknown missile (#35) is . But in order to find it, I spent about 4500 to have a view of all the squares located at most 450 sectors away from the origin .
