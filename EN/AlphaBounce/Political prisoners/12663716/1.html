Well, the FURI is not exactly the "enemy". Since there isn't much given on the backstory of AB, we can't tell who is more ethically correct in order to classify them.

Anyway, from a storyline perspective, FURI is a rebel organization against ESCorp (the company who uses "prisoners" for mining purposes).

Everyone starts out as prisoners of ESCorp. There is also an asteroid belt around the origin, you can see this using any map of AB available on the Internet. ESCorp doesn't allow prisoners outside the asteroid belt, if you leave that, you are kicked out forever from ESCorp, but you can go to Nalikors (a planet to the south-east of the origin).

Alternativelly, once you do 52 clearing missions you become a spy for ESCorp (which allows you to leave the asteroid belt too). Problem is, you can't leave ESCorp from that point onwards, so you are stuck with them.

You should complete all of the starting missions of ESCorp before defecting to FURI, if you plan to do so. Usually, the last ones are Invasion of Balixt and the MAS-Z Missile.

Once you are stuck with ESCorp or FURI, you can get some items exclusive to each of them. These are:
For FURI:
Syntrogenic Accelerator (IIRC): Gives you one more per day.
The Asphault ball: Last ball, from videos I've seen, it's effect is, I believe, to send afterimages of the ball that destroy more bricks in a straight line (so, for example, if you send the ball vertically upwards on the last brick of a full column of bricks, the ball itself will take out the first brick and the afterimages will take out a few of the above bricks as well).
Permanent Multiball: You begin each new life with 2 balls instead of one, as if you had picked an [M] powerup. Best way to add to the Asphault ball.

ESCorp:
Don't remember the name: Sends you back to [0.0], unlimited uses.
Mines: Adds mines to every level that act like explosive brick, you can have up to 4 and they are expensive as hell.
An extra envelope extention: permanently makes the paddle somewhat larger (like an [E] powerup).
