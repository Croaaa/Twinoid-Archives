I use the online databases to plot out a path where I can accumulate as many as I can soze I can have enough for the next upgrade I choose to buy.

Another option is to do Transport Missions ... land on a planet and take your charge to wherever he wants to go. Some of them pay quite well and while you're travelling, at least you're expanding the areas explored by radar so you can move faster around the universe when the time comes. Also, with all this non-cleaning exploration, you're leaving a sector here and there that WON'T have to be explored in the future should you ever be assigned a zone in that area.

Always nice to discover that your newest assigned zone has already had half of the squares explored beforehand. That just happened to me ... I had one zone assigned near Moltear ... once I arrived there, I saw that all that trekking back and forth beforehand (on transport missions and Escorp missions) reduced what would have been 25 unexplored squares only about 10 or 11 to complete the zone.

Transport missions and well planned-out runs are the only two suggestions I can think of. And it's always nice to find a guy that pays for his trip with .

Oh. And I always meant to suggest this (I know it's kinda unrelated to the thread) ... but when you're zipping between planets on established paths - ALWAYS widen your path with whatever radar you have by staying on one side of the explored path or the other. Or even zig-zagging your way along. Eventually, your paths will become wide enough that they merge with other paths and before long you won't be slowed down by unexplored territory anymore. Y'know what I'm saying here, right?
