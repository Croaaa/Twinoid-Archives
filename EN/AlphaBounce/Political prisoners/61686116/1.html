I had bought the game several years ago and trust me it's not worth it.

Don't get me wrong, the game would be fun if you've never played the online version before and it is fun for the first week you play it, but other than that it's not. I gave up on the game weeks after I started playing it because I found it too boring and tedious.

Here are the pros and cons of the game as compared with this version:

Pros:
-You can play as much as your heart desires because plays aren't limited
-The radar is permanent. Therefore, you can backtrack to anywhere in the game that you've played before (including the radar space around it) without needing to replay levels
-The menu screen has badass music

Cons:
-The envelope is too large.
-Way too many powerup drops. In my opinion, the frequency of powerup drops in the online version is perfect. But the DSI version showers you with these where you'll commonly collect 5 or more at the same time
-The game is way too easy for 97% of levels and ridiculously hard for the other 3% with practically no levels at the "not too hard and not too easy" sweet spot. In other words, you'll either complete a level quickly or need to replay the same level 5 or more times.
-The Flameball is overpowered and you get it early in the game, it's basically the volt ball we know of but much stronger. This ball isn't even a powerup, it's something you can automatically use on every level
-There is little motive to the game. Unlike the version we know and love with various missions along the way, all there is to the DSI version is collecting items, exploring planets, and finding Terre. In other words, the game becomes tedious quickly. I'd much rather do cleanups on the online version because at least the levels are more fun to play
-Being a perfectionist is overwhelming and practically impossible. There are so many items in the game located at random coordinates that it would be hard to keep track of everything
-There are too many items and customizations. Have fun spending half the game trying out new equipment on each of your envelopes simply to find out what they even do because you can only have a handful of items on each envelope at a time. Figuring out the perfect combinations are a pain when you have hundreds of items and don't know what half of them do

^The above is based on my memory. I haven't actually played the game in years. Also, I was planning to post a vent about the DSI version to the Alphabounce forums for years but never got around to it.

EDIT: Actually I wouldn't say that the game is not worth it but more that you get what you pay for. You'd get more entertainment buying 100 SHC than you would buying the entire DSI game
