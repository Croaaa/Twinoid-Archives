My suggestion? A grid that's formated in such a manner as to place the coordinates in the same manner as AB does. Then, in each sector, place a color for a mineral count. Perhaps even make two maps. One for lower count and one for higher (for the players that are outside the belt).

The maps could use a very common system for color-recognition. ROYGBIV, I'm thinking. The lower map could go something like:
0-10 R
11-25 O
26-50 Y
51-100 G
101-150 B
151-200 I
>200 V

The higher map could use something like:
<101 R
101-200 O
201-300 Y
301-400 G
401-500 B
501-600 I
>600 V

Alternatively, one could combine the maps using a sort of logarithmic function.

0-25 R
26-50 O
51-100 Y
101-200 G
201-400 B
401-600 I
>600 V

The overall design is, of course, subject to change. Unfortuantely, I don't know enough Perl (still learning!) to create such a map. So, any suggestions? Also, any takers? It doesn't seem like it would be that difficult to code. IIRC, there's a database with all of the mineral locations already parsed. The GoogleDocs one, I believe. Though, I don't believe it has the lower numbers. It would be a start, though.

Also, one could code a logarithmic function that selects a specific color depending on the number of minerals. It could then run through the entire range of colors where 1 is pure red and whatever the max is is pure violet. It could then use half of the max as indigo. Half of indigo as blue. Half of blue as green... etc. The range between each of these colors would, of course, be based on a logarithmic function. Then, the last section could be a 1:1 ratio on the color scale. Sorry for repost; but, you can also easily take screenshots. For everyone using Windows... that little PriScr key just to the right of F12? If you hit that, it pastes a copy of the screen into the clipboard. Mnus the cursor (mouse), of course. The you can just head over to MSPaint (Start>Program Files>Accessories>MSPain t) and go to Edit and then to Paste (or ctrl+v if you're me). This will post your screen onto MSPaint.

When you put your mouse over the newly pasted image, it should turn into a cross. You can now click and drag it. Move the image so that the corner of the game area is in the top left portion of the drawing area. Now, go to Tools and the Attributes and change the x and y settings to 400 and 360 respectively. This should give you ONLY the play area. : )

Then, of course, go to file>save as and save it as a bitmap (or jpg if you're stingy on harddrive space).

Finally, upload the image to your favorite image hosting service (www.photobucket.com and www.flickr.com seem to be popular.) And then you can link the URL into the contirbution information. You'll be helping your fellow players and you'll feel better about yourself!

I despise OSX and refuse to give information on how to do this process. : ( And, for Linux users.. well... you guys should know this stuff.

For the record, I don't believe I've played this game without using Linux once. : ) I'm a proud Linux user so I'm not here to flame you guys.

For Mac OSX users

You have two choices:

1.) Pause the game. Launch the application called Grab. You'll find it in your applications folder on every Mac. Click on its icon in the dock, when it will appear there. Then, select "Capture/Timed Screen". Or press "Command-Shift-Z".

A window will appear. Click "Start Timer". Unpause the game just before the timer will end. It will take a screenshot of whole screen and show it in a window.

Get back to your browser window quickly and pause the game again, so you won't lose a game.

Then, you can save the screenshot.

2.) Press Shift-Command-Space-3 and it will save your screenshot as picture on desktop.

Then crop the game screen area in any graphics editor.

Just cap the screen before launching the ball and paste it into your imaging software, save as PNG. Don't bother cropping, you can do it in batches with free software like FSResizer. Make sure you save losslessly (best as PNG) so that the compression won't mess the pixels up, so maybe one day fprog will put together a piece of code that will parse whatever data needed straight from the image.

Cropping options I'm using at FSR: start at 200x240, size 398x228. Start will differ on various machines but size should be ok. It is the res of max level size plus 1 pixel of background on each side.
