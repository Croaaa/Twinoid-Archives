On 15 August 2015 [French time], after 21 months of play(*) and 1994 sectors explored, I completed my PID Map. [Inventory screenshot]

(*) I took a long break between July 2012 and March 2015

I'm not sure what the record is (or if anyone has kept track), but I've read a few times that it's been believed impossible for a non-paying player to beat this game within 2 years. I'm confident this can be proven false.

What I did

I estimated travel distances between each pair of planets and used a free software library to optimise my travel route using the shortest path algorithm. This may not have been the best approach, since I didn't account for transport missions, among other things.

Note that my objective wasn't to minimize my exploration count or the number of s expended. One can artificially depress his exploration count by revisiting old sectors while pretending that generator/radar upgrades don't exist. Minimising HCs didn't make sense when I could expend 2 s to earn 7 s from FURI supporters.

Most players greatly underestimate the amount of tedium required to complete this game. They get overly ambitious and start exploring every sector of Lycans, only to burn themselves out before the game even started.

Even with the bare minimum, I've played enough Alphabounce for a lifetime.

ESCrap vs. FURI

In my opinion, the dumbest thing you can do as a free player is to stay with ESCrap. In the 15 months (1285 s) it takes to clean up 52 zones, I've already finished half the game.

My final journey

Unfortunately, my Terre is in an unfavourable location (-904 +677), with little opportunity to reuse previous traveled corridors or revisit planets. I came up with 3 possible routes:

Route 1 (blue) is the most direct path with a V5 Generator + V2 Radar. From the wormhole, I simply go southwest 225x, 3 sectors at a time, until reaching my destination.

Route 2 (red) involves sacrificing 6-10 to make a detour for the OX Delta ball, but I'll have the added benefit of navigating a well-traveled road (so I can use the MineMap.)

Route 3 (green) involves taking advantage of the Balmanch - Chagarina corridor I previously carved out to skip 6 sectors per .

I think Route 2 makes the most sense. Hopefully this journey won't take long with OX Delta and all 4 envelopes.

1) Remember that there is no permanent progression in Regular Mode, so any serious 100% definition must include Nightmare Mode.

2) A 100% "speed run" would not be very interesting from a route planning or optimisation perspective. Your first objective remains unchanged: reach as quickly as possible to earn the final radar & generator upgrades. Once you have them, you'd harvest s via Launcelot's mining route until you have enough s to complete everything else.

3) Other than extreme tedium, there is no additional challenge from completing every sector of every planet. If you can reach , you have the necessary skills to complete every planet. Especially when you can use capsules.

4) There aren't really any "optional" missions prior to the faction split. I completed every mission besides Star Dust, Holovan Land of Peace, and Unknown Prototype; that's 30 out of 33. You can argue that the Ambro-X, Zonkerian Medallion, and various extra s aren't strictly required to beat the game, but their benefits far exceed the costs to obtain them.

5) I'd say it's possible to reach with <1500 explorations, but it's highly dependent on your 's unique location. This is a random element beyond any player's control, which is why I prefer to use PID map completion as a consistent measuring stick.
