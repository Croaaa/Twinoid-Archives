Hi fellow prisonners.

Okay for those that always wanted a Perl script to plan paths through the galaxy, here's your chance. I've created one just for you and me.

In fact just for ME, but... you can use it too .

All you need is ActivePerl to run it and since you already want to use it, why not contribute some map data?!

I take anything in Excel, CSV, TXT, HTML, XML format
or even better my own Perl array format [x,y,money,info],
or anything that some 4-5 regexp can handle.

Here's the script:
j2k.sourceforge.net/svg/escorp ...

For instance, enter @pos=(-11,-19); $max = 24;
and you can see anything at 24 squares around that location.

Like locations with many minerals such as:

(-11,-18 ), (-13,-15), (-14,-16), (-7,-16),(-1,-15)...


and since you can't do math , it will tell you that within that range 1754$ can be collected. Where are the missiles and also what marchands you should look for and what items you should buy since your nearby.

Also, since it's just a script, you can add your own regexp, conditions, search criteria and whatever you may need to. I also added the cheapest Escorp and cheapest FURI items under 10000$ and the 2nd cheapest and 3rd cheapest. All the missiles, all the bonus P, all the collectable items, black holes and any of the data availables on other maps or Excel sheets.
Planets area are missing tough.

Feed back, comments or simply just Enjoy!

I update it daily, but maybe not on my website.

Fred.

Oh and big thanks to MacLambert and cie that created those Excel sheets.

It's Perl not Pearl, and it stands for Practical Extraction and Reporting Language.

You don't need to know how to code anything,
just change the variable @pos and $max
and $withBonusP value at the top of the script, and simply run it.

@pos = (2,3); <<<< where you are [2,3]
$max = 12; <<<< maximum distance from that point you want to see items
$withBonusP = 1; <<< want to see BonusP
$withBonusP = 0; <<< Do not want to see BonusP

It's just a big matrix with values, some for loops and if/else. Nothing very complicated.

If you know any other programming language, you should be okay.

Have a nice day.
