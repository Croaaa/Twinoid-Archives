This is one of my oldest and most ambitious projects . If you speak French you ought to read these threads:

La spirale infernale v1 (now abandonned)
La spirale infernale v2

The idea is to draw a spiral starting from the origin and get a view of every AB level (by "view", I mean view of its coordinates on the AB grid on the left of the AB page). Therefore, I can spot any item undiscovered yet when my spiral passes nearby. I discovered many unknown shops and missile #35 this way

The spiral v2 is made as this :

- start at [0][0]
- with one's own and , draw a first square between [-16][-16] and [16][16] (only the 4 edges, not the area inside)
- draw a second square between [-32][-32] and [32][32] (only the 4 edges, not the area inside)
- draw a third square between [-48][-48] and [48][48]
- etc ...

The current last square drawn is the 36th, from [-576][-576] to [576][576]. It took me almost 1 year and 20000 to go up to this point . I've currently stopped it due to technical reasons and because I've restarted doing more cleanups to get the 3rd red rank

Now, some words about how the picture is made. When building the spiral, I take a screenshot of AB background (the grid itself) every 2 . For this, I use the excellent ABScreenMaker, and a hidden feature of it also builds the whole map from the screenshot library (and removes all the unwanted ). The outcome is a huge picture...

Size: 23440 x 21042 pixels
Weight: .PNG version (high quality): 672 MB,
.JPG version (low quality) : 66 MB,
Overview (very low quality) : 2 MB (this is the picture you've seen already).

All of this (including the ~9000 screenshots taken for the spiral) is shared online on the LW space I'm advertising about in this thread. Please read this ad to know how to log in. Once logged in, browse the "spirale infernale v2" folder and enjoy
