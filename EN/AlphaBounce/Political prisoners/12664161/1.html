Ball killers

I think [B] is the most harmful. The only benefical use i could find is to make a Halo ball remain someplace longer... But then, when it comes back it will be a lot faster.

[Z] is very dangerous to me if I grab it in the middle of a ball "lifetime". I don't handle fast balls very well... A skillful player could handle it and speed up the completion of a level though.

[Y] can be horrible in some levels. However it can be a real friend in some others. For example, think about a level which is blocked by a horizontal line of blue (unbreakable) bricks except for one space. It would bounce terribly fast up there, and then, when it came back, it would be slow enough to bounce it back with your paddle. It's a bit tricky, but if you can do it, it's a great technique. Some people don't know this, but it IS different if you grab it when your ball is slow from when it is fast. The "average" speed of the Yo-yo-ball will change slightly.

[W] same as above, it's tricky to handle, but if you do, it gets almost as useful as [K]. If you grab the power-up when the ball is very high, it may stay up there for a loooooong time, depending only on your luck. Then if you have a very enlarged paddle, it will be a piece of cake to bounce it. When it goes lower, just follow it like the "automatic-player" on the demo, and again, if your paddle is huge, doing this would be a lot easier .

[H] Believe it or not, a Halo ball can screw your level. If it gets inside an almost unescapeable (sp?)bricks "cage", it will speed up a LOT. And then, the problem is that ALMOST. That means, there IS a possibility that it comes back. And when it does... Well, good luck bouncing it back... There are some levels, with breaks BELOW unbreakable bricks, that are not good places to have halo balls.

Mineral or Item Killers

[G], also a ball killer in some levels. Try to avoid minerals with a glacial ball... But it can save you from some deadly things in some cases.

[Q] and [O]. Think before grabbing them... They could ruin a "crowded" level or a quest item. The worst thing that could ever happen to you, is one of these hidden in a very low brick. So you won't have time to think, if grabbing it or avoiding it, and it could even be coming down in the same place your ball will hit, because when you hit a low brick, unless it was with a lot of "angle", the ball won't have time to detour enough. A quest item level is designed just like that... I played it FIVE times to get the item... ¬¬
