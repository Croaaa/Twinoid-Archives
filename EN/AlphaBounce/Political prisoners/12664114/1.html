The kinetic energy preserver, more commonly know as speed lock, is the one invention that made space ball drilling possible.

It's purpose is to prevent the kinetic energy of a moving object to reduce, in particular by transfers during collisions.

Although popularly considered a form of perpetual motion, it is actually a over-unity device: The nano-variations of space continuously apply impulses to any object, but the device prevents those opposing movement, effectively continuously accelerating said object.
Nano-variations seem more common in extra-system space and some rare spots, but this is still a widely unknown phenomenon.

The invention was one of mankind's major achievement, but the device itself is pretty simple, small and reliable, thus it is manufactured in the form of cheap expendable drilling balls that move indefinitely once launched.
Technology research focused on driving apparatus and additional effects. Because the vehicles and tanks holding these are essentially containers, they are named respectively "envelope" and "capsule".

While the acceleration of drilling balls is an unsolved problem, the over-unity behavior proved an invaluable source of unlimited energy.
This made possible the use of matter synthesizers which now equip all standard envelopes, and popularized the use of the most abundant matter available as space fuel: Hydrogen.


The momentum storage, more commonly know as range bungee, was a later development that significantly improved space mining.

It's purpose is to evade an object's momentum to and recover it from the universe's reverse side.

This allows excellent maneuvering: instead of expensive and slow braking and propelling, the device simply stores or restitutes directly speed in the direction needed.
However it has severe limitations:
-It has short range, in other words it allows to maneuver only close to the point where the device was activated. For that reason it could not be used on drilling balls and instead equips envelopes.
-It operates only along the object's greatest dimension. So because envelopes need a flat top for drilling ball bounces, they can move only left-right.

The practical result is that envelopes operate in a small zone, and for convenience space was divided in a grid of such size.
