moneymaker1
Easiest first

The ESCorp gets your shorter as before when you leave the Belt (that can't be seen on the F.U.R.I.'s members' pages because of a glitch hehe), and there is (for the moment at least, we don't know) no way to come back to the ESCorp.

The following contains spoilers, please don't read if you don't want to know

If you join the F.U.R.I., you will have to go out of the Belt before your 52nd cleanup (duh!), and then go to Nalikors and meet the F.U.R.I. chief to enroll. From that point, you will have "delivery missions" (don't know the exact term, I haven't translated the F.U.R.I. part ), with a 25 for each prisoner you free. Each mission is only one block but they're farther apart (and you have to travel more often between missions, compared to ESCorp cleanups which are all grouped).

After joining and doing a delivery, you will have a misison to completely explore Holovan. Once you do this, you'll have to meet this guy on the planet which will give you a ball duplicator (when you play you start as if you had a [ M ] power-up).

You will then have, after 20 of those deliveries, a mission to pick up parchments located around the Belt, and then by visiting an archenior, you will obtain a machine giving you an extra every day

When you reach 100 and have the 3rd drilling ball, you will be asked to go steal the 4th one from en ESCorp convoy, which has highly destructive powers!

Now for the ESCorp!

So after your 52 cleanups you'll get the Spy mission, then explore all of Nalikors and you'll have 1500 and you'll be allowed out of the Belt while still ESCorp

At 58 cleanups, you'll have the Cilorile Guardians mission, explore all of Cilorile and you'll have access to the FORA 7R-Z mine (acts as a level 3 missile in the game when the ball hits it, randomly placed at the beginning of a play). You'll then be able to buy 3 extra mines, just like extra missiles, from shops (very expensive, the nÂ°3 mine has been seen at around 150k if I can remember correctly).

At 63 cleanups, you'll have "The Fight" mission. The point of this one is to land on planets and have fun telling the ESCorp where those nasty F.U.R.I. supporters are. You get a 20 gift when the mission starts and then 5 for each enemy spotted

At 73 of them, you'll have to go to [83,-123] to get the Lithium Retrofusor, which is quite nifty a click and you'll be back to the origin for free using this machine! You can use it as many times as you wish

At 87, you'll have a mission asking you to find 5 pink cristals (made the mistake on purpose since there is the same in French: crystal instead of cristal ). Once you find them (4 of them: [310,-50], [340,-112], [284,-15], [281,-75]), you'll have the last missile which you probably had a chance to try a few days ago with the glitch

At 100, you'll have to go to the origin to get the other envelope extension

That's it for all the known missions

Up to you to choose, but keep in mind that there are probably undiscovered missions for the F.U.R.I. that compensate for all those newly found ESCorp ones

Phew! Well now I'm off to read all what people wrote elsewhere while I typed this

Also, progress is made faster with 4 free SHC / day (I bought permanent ones also, 2 or 3 times, but if you're a casual gamer that does this for fun, 4 SHC / day go a long way).

Now I'm doing the FURI "Free a prisoner" missions so I can go get that last awesome ball. I'm sure it'll be worth it (only 35 more prisoners to free ).

So point is that based on your needs, you might find each faction suited for you. Since I like a little challenge (shorter pad, mastering two balls simultaneously most of the time) and I don't end up paying too often for SHC, I think the FURI is more suited for me
