I've been fantasizing for a while now, about how I and/or other people could build our own space brickbreaker RPG after AB is gone. Crazy thoughts about things I'd do better, things I'd do differently, or reimaginings of game concepts. The player, the lore, the factions...

The DSi game, now gone, did this a lot of course. I heard that for example there's actual equipment in there, and you can select which ball and paddle you want to use.

You know how the letter powerups like Extension, Control, etc. were made for French? Why not reimagine these powerups, this time in English, and trying to make some interesting original ones, including the missing U? I don't know what the DSi game powerups are, or powerups in other brickbreakers, so I'd love to hear about them too.


So to start, here are several ideas I've been having about a powerup collection.

Autopilot - The onboard AI takes over for a few seconds, catching all incoming balls and powerups, while you can point to where you want balls to be aimed.

Barrier - A barrier appears beneath the paddle, preventing balls from falling off the screen. It has a life meter that decays slowly over time and also when hit.

Fire - Affected balls cause more damage to hard bricks. Additional effects could include burning gaseous bricks permanently. A second Fire could overcharge it to have a chance of low-power explosions. Analogous to F in this game.

Glue - Affected balls will stick to the paddle for a second before shooting off again, and will collide with other balls and projectiles for the same effect. Analogous to C in this game.

Ice - Affected balls will freeze bricks without activating them, and destroy them the next hit. A second Ice could overcharge it to destroy bricks in one hit. Frozen bricks would explode when hit by Fire balls, for a combo. Analogous to G in this game.

Laser - The paddle can charge up small lasers to be shot vertically at will. A second Laser could overcharge it to produce continuous lasers that destroy bricks where aimed. Analogous to L in this game.

Overdrive - The game speeds up steadily, up to 2x normal speed. Balls will also deal more damage to bricks as speed increases, and you gain more points. This effect lasts until a life is lost. Analogous to Z in this game.

Phantom - Affected balls will dig into the first few bricks without dealing damage to them, before becoming solid again. The effect must be recharged by hitting the paddle. Analogous to H in this game, and it also reminds me of the Asphalt ball.

Thunder - Existing balls will unload a damage-dealing charge in an area around the next hit, decaying bricks and destroying projectiles. The effect must be recharged by hitting the paddle. A second Thunder would overcharge it to spread this charge continuously around them as they move. Analogous to V in this game.

Wormhole - Each existing ball will be loaded with a single wormhole, that will be released the next time they hit a brick, removing everything in an area. Analogous to Q in this game.

Zen - The game slows down for a time, highlighting incoming balls and projectiles. Analogous to X in this game.

Ideas I've been having about the story for a while.

I think the two faction system is perfect. However, I'd change what they each are; while this game's message about humanity (represented by ESCorp) is great, I think it makes for a one-sided moral dilemma.

So here's how it goes: You're one of many miners sent by the company to a sector of the galaxy assumed uninhabited. Not long after you arrive and start the operation, however, you end up caught in the middle of a conflict between two alien empires. The fate of the rest of the crew is unknown, and you're taken prisoner by one of the factions.
Your great skills are taken advantage of, and you're made to collect resources for the war. You hear stories, about the local systems, about both factions; you learn a lot from prisoners, guards, merchants, hubs.
Time passes, and you become more accustomed, more capable and with more equipment. One day, the other faction attacks and you're caught in the middle of it again. You can choose to earn your freedom by siding with your captors, or by accepting being rescued.
Forced to join a fight you have no business in, you start to adapt and relate to the side you chose. The opposing faction has commited so many atrocities, after all; haven't you heard? In the bottom of your heart, however, the thing you want most is to find your way back to Earth.


I think such a story would keep the core of what makes AlphaBounce special. while twisting it a little to make it more interesting and widening the scope for more lore and world development.
I like the prisoner situation AlphaBounce presents, most of all. A similar situation is also present in Skyrim. ESCorp/FURI, Imperial/Stormcloak. Nice stuff. But I didn't want you staying a prisoner forever.

They were made for French, because it's a French game. Most fit just as well in English, like Zéle/Zeal, Ouverture/Opening... And there's the obvious copies from other games, which are a necessity, like Extension and Multiball. But:

B - Blindage: Means armor. They had to change it to Boost (a strange choice) so it made sense in English
C - Colle: Means glue. They had to change it to Control so it made sense in English
S - Sauvetage: Means rescue. They had to change it to Safety so it made sense in English
