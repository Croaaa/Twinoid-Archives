zAlbee and FalconSadist put it right on the money ... if you're playing with an optical mouse, not a track ball mouse, your wrist will move in an arc, and not always will you be holding the mouse and have the optics directly horizontal.

I've caught myself getting into the game quite the lot and find myself very slightly nudging my mouse when the ball is about to hit the pad ... sort of like "I'm gonna move this pad and make it move more" ... and yep, sure as anything the mouse cursor moves up ...

The reason I mentioned track ball mice, is because, while they're not seen very often any more (from what I've seen as easily purchasable in most stores (mainly due to the fact that most people are getting laptops and track balls aren't that great to carry around)) they can be calibrated to your wrist placement and the movement of your thumb so that your natural arc of your thumb's movement becomes a straight horizontal line across the mouse's movement.
