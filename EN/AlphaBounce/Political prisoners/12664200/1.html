1. is it at all possible to join FURI after you finish the majority of ESCorp stuff?
2. where is this "beyond the belt" place?
3. is it only possible to get there by being sent there via mission or can you take a black hole or simply take a long time and just travel there?
4. can you only join FURI at a certain point or is it at all possible to join early or later?

1. You will be able to finish up to the Balixt mission and the MAS-Z Missile mission. After that, and with the exception of Salmeen, Space Suit and clean-up missions, all others will be at least partially outside the Belt, so you will either have to defect or wait until you finish 52 clean-up missions.

2. The area in which you start is surrounded by an asteroid belt called the Karbonis Belt. Karbonis was a planet, but it exploded, forming the belt. That's the area you aren't allowed to leave. The place where you need to go to join FURI is Nalikors, a planet found at around [65][150]. To join you will need to able to land and have the Space Suit.

3. I don't know if any black holes help make the traveling faster, but otherwise, it's just a loooong, looooong road. If you choose to stay with ESCorp you get a mission on Nalikors, but by that time it will be too late to join FURI.

4. You can join FURI at any time before you complete 52 clean-up missions. However, you will also need the ability to land on planets and the Space Suit in order to join FURI, and it's strongly recommended that you do all of the ESCorp missions that you can (excluding clean-ups) before leaving the Belt.

Some further clarification:

1. is it at all possible to join FURI after you finish the majority of ESCorp stuff?
As others have mentioned before, you can join FURI as long as you don't finish the 52nd cleanup. That's all the requirement is as far as missions. So, yes, you can join FURI when you've completed the majority of ESCorp missions.

2. where is this "beyond the belt" place?
The Karbonis Belt is about 110 sectors from [27,6].

3. is it only possible to get there by being sent there via mission or can you take a black hole or simply take a long time and just travel there?
When you use a black hole that leads you out of the Karbonis Belt, once you play a couple of levels, you'll be considered an Interstellar Fugitive. You join FURI as soon as you meet some alien in Nalikors, but, as previously mentioned, you need the spacesuit. The other way to become an Interstellar Fugitive is to travel all the way out of the Belt.

4. can you only join FURI at a certain point or is it at all possible to join early or later?
As answered in #1, you can join FURI at any point before completing your 52nd cleanup mission.

Hope this helps a bit more.
