It is not a question of time, but rather a question of intensity of the ball by report to it's speed.

When the ball passes near the conglomerate at a slow speed, the ball can remain near the conglomerate longer, but transmits less electrical power in the conglomerate. When the ball passes near the conglomerate at a higher speed, the ball can remain less longer near the conglomerate, but transmits more electrical power in the conglomerate.

This means that the electrical power transmitted to the conglomerate is always conversely proportional to the speed of the ball.

As for the time passed near the conglomerate, just try to make rebound the ball so he can stay the longest amount of time near the conglomerate, to maximize the damage given to this one.

As for the iron conglomerate, it takes the same time to make it degrade a bit and to destruct a basic conglomerate.
