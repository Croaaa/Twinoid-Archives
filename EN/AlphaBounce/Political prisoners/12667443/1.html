On march 21, 2012
[1416686]
I'm interested in your theory about how levels are generated.
There is no theory behind my statement.
I know the exact algorithm used to create levels in alphabounce

As Zatronium said they use a self written pseudorandom number generator. As a seed, the coordinates are used (the seed is 10000x+y). This way every Player gets the same level for the same coordinates.

Since flash uses 32 bit numbers, the seed will be an int, as long as abs(10000x+y)<2*10^9 there will be no problem creating levels. So you are at least safe in the area -200000<x<200000 and -100000000<y<100000000. Even after that the generation wont fail because actionscript will switch to double on values outside this range. But there will probably arise some problems.

For generation, they additionally use the distance from [0][0] and the angle (arctan). Furthermore the planets and the belt play an important role.

After generation a level is checked against some rules. If it fails, the level is generated again.
If this process fails more than 12 times, a level full of bricks is generated. (Everyone should know them)
