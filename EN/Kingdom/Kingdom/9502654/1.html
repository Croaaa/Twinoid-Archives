MagicDragon
I wouldn't want to penalize players for expanding slowly by reducing life expectancy. What I would suggest instead is make the starting life expectancy be in the 70 to 85 range depending on several factors I'll outline a bit later. What I would suggest is that certain events trigger a reduction in life expectancy.

Every single time you experience bankruptcy, you should lose a week of life expectancy. For every famine, you should receive a loss of a month in life expectancy. If your Capital is taken over violently, you should lose six months of life expectancy (you should have no penalty if you spawn under the control of a master). If you revolt against your master and lose, you lose 5 years off your life. A second failing attempt against the same master should result in an automatic reduction in health (or death if your health is bad). By doing this, it adds an extra element of risk to the game as well as revolting.

I also think there should be ways to extend life expectancy. What I would suggest is simple. One thing I would add is that for every 2 years where you take turns where you earn trade with every turn of the production grid should reward you with three months of additional life expectancy (not to exceed a cumulative extension of five years). I also think you should be awarded additional life expectancy with the promotion to Marquis (two years), Prince (two years), King (five years) and Emperor (seven years). If you never experience anything that would cause famine, you should be able to live for up to an additional 21 years. The first two potions you buy should add ten years apiece. All subsequent potions should extend that life span for no more than five (with a cap of six potions consumed), so that a player can extend their life span by no more than 30 years.

I mentioned earlier a range of starting life expectancy in a range of 70 to 85 years. I think that range should depend on several factors. What would determine where start in that range would be how populated a map is. A sparsely populated map would net you a longer initial life span. Other factors would be the percentage of players on the map who are a certain age. If the majority of the players are young, you'd start closer to the younger end. If a larger number are old, it's skews to the upper end. The last factor would be the state of health of the majority of players who haven't purchased potions on their current game. I think they can design an age generator that would put you within a certain expectancy point depending on those factors.

But at least it'll add a bit more predictability to individual lifespans in game.
