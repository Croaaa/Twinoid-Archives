I think a better idea should be some kind of Random Number Generator (RNG) that determines age and health dropping. How I think it should work is that at each threshold, it should be re-rolled. Initial health should allow for a number of turns that equals 30 to 45 years of life span, ensuring all players who are active reach the age of 50 at minimum with excellent health. The next roll to good health would have an RNG between 15 to 20 years worth of turns. The last would allow for a span of 10 to 15 years worth of turns in bad health.

Using potions in this system should supersede the RNG and offer the player a set life extension. The first potion gets you an additional 20 years of life. The second extends your life by 15 years and all subsequent potions grant you an additional decade. This benefit would work regardless of what state your health is when you first take a potion.

To simplify matters in case I was unclear above. All players should have at least 30 years if life with excellent health, 15 with good and ten with bad. This would allow a minimum life span of 75 years (drop to good at 50, bad at 65, and death at 75) without the use of potions. Some lucky players would live as long as 100 years without potions. I think this is a reasonably fair system.

Note that this system still gives players who buy potions something of an advantage. To be blunt, I don't really see the problem with this as they are paying to extend their game. There should be a tangible benefit. It has pissed me off that I bought potions that only added a few years of life span to my game. I think the amount of extension you get should be a set amount as outlined above.
