Well, the optimal thing to do is to balance your troops out (especially if you dont have LOTS) across multiple nearby locations, to raise their trade evenly. I suggest though, to place the majority of your troops in a recently conquered location (still in the negatives) until its trade turns positive before leaving, a decent amount is 4 soldiers (trade will go up to 7) so leave behind that many until the trade stabilises, and then add more troops later on to make it rise again. The more villages you have rising in trade, the quicker your trade will rise. Just remember you need to have a fair amount of gold, say 300-1000+ (depends how many you have really) to invest in your soldiers and hence your trade, before doing this. Excess troops in one location doesn't really work well, unless you can't afford to capture more villages.

Also, the sooner you put at least one troop in every village surrounding yourself, the better. This means if someone wants to take over your village, on the exit-trip after doing so they will have to take over the barbarian village containing your soldier to escape, giving them a heavy negative trade penalty.

When you're attacking someone, you gain attacker status and your catapults get +200% bonus,
so make sure you have--
At least one catapult + one soldier for every ballista they have. This is so even if you get unlucky, and your catapult only just loses to the ballista, your soldier can finish it off without taking too much damage, or, your soldier does a little damage and gets knocked out, but your catapult will finish the job and stay alive.

One catapult for every three soldiers they have. When siege weapons have a 200% bonus, they tend to be able to KO at least three soldiers safely before they run the risk of being destroyed.
2 soldiers for every catapult they have.

Then you can't lose, really.

If you're defending, kind of the same thing, but reverse ballista's and catapults.

And of course, extra units always help...

You can also provoke generals if they aren't "defending", this will make you have Defender status and they will have Attacker status. This is useful if you have an army of ballista's and you want to kick over some idle nearby generals, or if you want to kick over their army of ballista's with a bunch of soldiers.

Observe your opponents and judge what you need accordingly. If your nearby players are not so active or are friendly it's safe to assume that they won't attack you if you have less than ten troops in your capital. If your neighbour is aggressive be sure to leave some protection at a choking point so you have more time to prepare for the assault on your capital.
