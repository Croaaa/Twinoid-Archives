Igorus
You have to place as much soldiers as you can in a village. The trade will go up faster that way. The closer the village, the faster the trade will go up. If you have 2 villages or more try to distribute soldiers from one village to the new one (like 50%), add soldiers from your capital whenever you can. Always wait till your soldiers arrive in the village before taking turns. Taking turns while your soldiers are walking, is losing trade.

Scorpian
Igoris79 nailed it. The trade rises at a rate based on the number of soldiers in a village compared to its current trade. It then maxes out when the trade is equal to one less than twice the number of soldiers assigned to defend that village. You can use this calculator to get a better idea (it's been linked several times before in this forum): http://www.smoggstudio.com/kingdom/index .php

Just enter the current trade into the first box (included the minus sign if it's negative) and the number of soldiers assigned to the village in the next. Tour = turn, and Commerce = trade. Simply hover over the graph to see how many turn you'll need to take to get your trade to a certain rate. Feel free to try with different amounts of soldiers to compare the results.

Launcelot
Use the calculator mentioned above and keep it as a guide. How many turns trade will need to reach an X value depends on 2 factors, troops positioned in the village and distance from capital. The more troops the faster. The closer to the capital the faster.

As a rule of thumb, get a new village, put troops, increase trade, control your costs then proceed to take another village. Everytime you get a new village, your economy will have to take a certain load, depending on how far the village is. What you need to do is make sure your economy is strong enough everytime you attempt to expand further, then take your time and fix trade.

Getting too many villages at once in fear of someone else getting them before you, often results in bankruptcy and eventually losing just what you rushed to get. Unless you have a really good trade that enables you to expand quicker, you should avoid doing that.

Never tried that, but I can tell you this. If you have troops about half the number of current trade and the village is close to your capital, it takes forever to drop. To give you an example, currently one of my villages has 64 trade. I used to have around 70 troops in there which resulted in a quick increase of trade. That village is right next to my capital. So at some point I decided to cut the number of my troops and use them elsewhere, so I left 31 troops. When I did that, trade in that village was 65. So it dropped by only 1 point and I must've taken more than 150 turns since.

Naturally trade drops in the same pattern just as it goes up, affected by number of troops and distance. What you need to remember is that trade will continue to increase until it reaches 1 point below twice of your troops. So with 31 troops, trade will continue to increase until it reaches 61, then it will remain stable. Then if you take troops, it will start to drop, so in other words it begins to drop if troops are less than half of the trade in it.

I have a feeling that it drops somewhat slower than it goes up though..
