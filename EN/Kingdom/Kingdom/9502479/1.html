If you don't like the capital assigned to you, what you need to do is NOT take any turns, do nothing at all. Just log off and in about 20 minutes you will be free to try again for a different capital. By doing so the game understands that you don't want to play, which is why you won't see any message that you've died, you'll just be given a new option for a map/capital like nothing ever happened.

If you do take turns before you decide that you don't want to play, then you'll have to wait until you die from inactivity, which is approximately 5 days.

However, before you decide if you want to try a different capital, or still want to give that capital a chance, what you can do is check the health status of those powerful players surrounding you and see if they are close to dying. If their health is "bad" ** then you could take your time and begin to create your kingdom, even if you're a follower (sometimes that's not a bad thing really, depends on the kind of person that your master is; if he's willing to give you just a couple of his villages before he dies, so that you can begin to raise some trade and make buildings, then you're all set), then wait until they die which shouldn't take long and then start taking his abandoned villages.

But you'll have to really like the position of that capital, otherwise no need to wait just do what I said in the beginning. Personally, I like capitals located in the corners of the map, any of the 4 corners. Less villages surrounding you, less borders that need your attention.

** there is an option to buy a life potion and increase health status, but nobody willing to take a potion takes turns while their health is bad because that means any turn can be their last, so basically if you see that their health is bad for some time, that means they're not willing to buy one. If their health is good, then it could be that they've taken a life potion, or they may take one if it gets bad, so you can't be sure.

Here is my basic strategy, which usually works well.

Find a neighbor who is likely to die soon and invite him to come conquer you. Promise to be a good little vassal if he will garisson and protect you. He only cares about prestige by this point, so it should be a gimme. Make yourself easy to conquer by either taking just enough turns to stay alive or quickly building a general and getting your soldiers out of your capitol.

Next offer to help him invade your other neighbor - he does the invading, you take the town afterwards. He gets the prestige with no negative trade and still gets some taxes, you get to grow. Do everything you can to make it worth his while to help you add villages and increase his prestige.

A week later he dies of old age, you are big enough to survive on your own, and there is a huge area of ungarissoned savage villages just waiting for you.

I virtually never try to go it alone from the start. Even in a good position I'll look for someone to conquer me so I have backup if needed. The taxes lost are far better than loosing your kingdom from a successful attack.
