Barracks let you have troops stationed in your capital for free, as soon as you move them to a general or a village, they cost gold.


Level 1 - 5 troops free
2 - 10 troops
3 - 20 troops
4 - 50 troops
5 - 100 troops


Example; If you have barracks level 2 and 12 units stationed in capital, you pay 2 gold/turn. ALL kind of units count as 1, soldiers, warmachines, upgrades soldiers, all are 1.

In my experience (tested numerous times), reputation earned is affected both by number of troops your general's army has killed, and also reputation your general already has. This means that with the exact same number of kills, if you use a general that has only the initial 1 point, he will earn more points than a general with 10 points already. This is why in every new battle you fight, you should try and use the general with the lowest reputation at the given time, so that total reputation is distributed as equally as possible to all your generals. You do that and you'll eventually maximize reputation.


The way it works makes sense in that, a general whose skill is completely unknown to anyone, will impress people a lot more than another general who's already famous. So the more famous he gets, the harder his reputation increases.
