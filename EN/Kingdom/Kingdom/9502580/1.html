1) Don't know.

2) For a village with -X trade, X soldiers will increase trade to zero in 50 turns and +X is 100 turns. Doubling the number of soldiers doubles the speed. Trade does not rise in a straight line. It is a function of soldiers/trade and rises fastest when the ratio is close to zero and slower the farther it is from zero, positive or negative.

3) I always do farm then granary. Next is market and barracks but the order depends on the situation, finally everything else. I always make sure I am wasting grain so I never risk famine.

4) You did not ask, but I usually end level 1 with peasant:merchant:lumberjack:worker around 4:2:1:1. Moving into higher levels the fraction of peasant drops until it is about 30% by level 5. That is more than you need, but I never want to worry about even a slight risk of famine. Merchants are 20 - 30% depending on how many vassals and combats. The rest depends on the situation.

To answer the 1st question, what your friend can do is look on the map selection screen see if your map is there. If it's not, he doesn't have to choose, he can wait a few minutes then try again. The system cycles the map selections and your map is likely to appear on his screen sooner or later. That's what I did when I first started.

Now suppose he makes it to the same map as yours, there is another problem called map location. If he pops up in the other side of the map, chances are you'll never meet so what will be the point of being on the same map. That problem can be dealt with making a few attempts until he's given a closer location. Luckily, when you choose a map and you're spawned somewhere on it, but you don't like your location, you can just simply follow a simple procedure: DON'T start taking turns on the production grid, in fact do nothing at all and you will be dead in about 25 minutes, free to restart on either another map, or the same map in another location. You can repeat this process as many times as you want until you are satisfied.

About trade, if you're interested there is a tool we use which helps a lot with trade and troops that are used for it.

MagicDragon
The more soldiers you place in a given village, the higher your trade will grow. The maximun amount of gold earned is one less than twice the number troops stationed in the village. Having 5 troops stationed in your new village will eventually earn you 9 gold per turn. If you double the number of troops in that village, trade will grow to 19. Do keep in mind that starting trade for any newly conquered village starts in the negative equal to the square of the number of nodes it is away from your capital (-1, -4, -9, -16, and so on).

How trade works check this out http://www.smoggstudio.com/kingdom/ this is a very handy calculator that gives you trade prediction depending on the amount of troops.

For Commerce type the current value of the village's trade if it's negative type it as negative i.e. -4, for Garnison type the amount of troops you have placed in it. Hit Valider and a graph pops up. Mouse over it starting from bottom left, as you follow the graph it tells you what trade is going to be at X turn. You will also see that in all cases, there is a certain amount of turns, starting from X to X that trade makes sort of a jump in a very short time (time is turns here). The more troops, the higher the jump, play with the graph for a bit see how it goes.
