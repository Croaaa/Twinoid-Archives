Staying a follower as opposed to revolting against a powerful master has another advantage in that you can stay at peace with him/her. If you successfully revolt, and he marches an army over to take your capital back, you will lose ALL your territories and ALL your units that are in each of those territories, in an instant!

Not only that, but if you try to take back one of those old territories you'll have to defeat all your old units just to capture them. There's supposed to be a way to re-recruit your old soldiers... but it is broken right now.

So consider very carefully whether you want to revolt... because you could easily end up utterly destroyed.

Owyndaw
The interesting bit about Kingdom is that you can't crush anyone (or be crushed); meaning that diplomacy is really important. Let me explain...

Kingdom can be seen as two games. The first is a City growth game; with buildings and population being the things you add. Choices you make assist in the second game; expanding across your surroundings. How far you expand affects how far you can go in the first game. If someone attacks you, they may capture your Capital, but this only resets the second game. The first game continues; if you have a strong Capital, you keep a strong Capital. You can lose all your villages, your Trade and even your Generals (which you might consider quite a pain!), but you cannot be crushed. Your Capital can produce a large army again and you can set off in conquest again.

For a Master, you can never get rid of your neighbours, just wait for them to die (with them waiting for you to die). Trying to be an oppressive ruler would lead to malcontent Followers, they can organise together and take your Capital quite easily. This locks everyone in battle, and may hold everyone back. Only with good diplomacy can everyone win. Interesting indeed!
