Scorpian
They're both about equal. If you experience a famine, you start losing citizens. Eventually, you have so few that you can actually afford to feed who's left and recover. If it's bankruptcy, you start losing troops. Eventually, you have so few that you can actually afford to pay who's left and recover. Some will probably say that getting new citizens after a famine is harder, but I say that fixing your trade after bankruptcy is equally as hard.

imo, your best bet is to just avoid both of them. Trying to figure out which is worse is like trying to compare death by burning with death by drowning. Both are horrible, and I would rather not choose between them at all. :P

byzanthia
Bankruptcy is worse. Once your trade hits negative, you'll lose soldiers, which in turn lowers your trade, and before you know it you lost all your soldiers and territories and it takes you 10-15 years to recover.

On the other hand, a famine will never wipe out all your citizens. You'll have food appear sooner or later and you'll recover fairly quickly - as soon as you have enough food, citizens will start appearing again.

So a bankruptcy is much harder to recover from.
