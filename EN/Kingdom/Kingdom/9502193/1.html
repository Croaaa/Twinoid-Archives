After upgrading your palace the first thing you should be doing is upgrading your granary and then your farm, and then workshop. By having your granary done first, you can keep your resources of food high, hence making more residents to appear. The farm makes your peasants more efficient, and then the workshop makes your workers even better --

Basicly, if you want to have an epic-looking capital, try to maximise the efficiency of your grid output as soon as possible. The more residents you can churn out sooner, the better. Compare my 13 workers rank 4 workshop to someone with only 6 workers and a rank 2... takes me far far less turns to make what I need to make.

Adding onto what AppleWaffle has said, you also need to take into account your most immediate geographical threats to your kingdom. If your neighbours are suddenly producing a fair number of catapults you'd be sure to either a) strike first so they lose their attacking bonus b) start building ballistics c) be diplomatic or d) be diplomatic and backstab them while their generals are off conquering distant capitals.

I have not been playing long. If the original post is asking for a guide to playing better, I don't think this is easy to do.

The biggest challenge is learning how to play your grid - what to match and how that affects the rest of the grid. If your grid has a lot of one tile, that might influence your choices, e.g. a lot of coins on the grid might lead you to add Traders or a Market.

The position of other players on your map might affect your choice of playing style; spread yourself far and thin, or keep small and strong. Do you attack a strong neighbour, or be friends and avoid them?

The Help is quite a good guide for how to play, and should be read (I read it before registering). It gives the basic skills required. What it does not give is the detail, such as how many food do you get for matching 3 on your grid, with a certain number of Peasants and a Farm; this might help, but is it really that important?
