A suggestion.

When a player enters a new position it is sometimes hard to tell if a position with a master is playable or not. Sometimes the master intends to control around the capitol and there is no chance for a player to develop. Other times the master may control the surroundings but be willing to give them up to a vassal. You can only find out which by playing and then possibly being stuck with the position for days if you guess wrong.

I can see this being a good feature to control the master but it has to be frustrating for the player who is not able to play for a week or more because he starts playing a position only to find his master will not let him play. I suspect some early players are discouraged and lost because of this.

Some possible solutions:

1) Allow free suicide as long as the player has not done some action that commits to play or affects the play of others. For instance suicide could be permitted as long as a general has not been recruited or only a limited number of soldiers built. There should be a waning before the action that crosses the line.

2) Have a way for a master to post to the capitol rather than a player. Any person who starts up in the position would be able to see the message and know the masters plans before deciding whether to play or not.

Either one makes it easier for masters and may have to be limited or handicapped in some way, but it really reduce the frustration of new players.
