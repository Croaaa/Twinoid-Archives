It depends on the amount of troops you put in it. The more troops, the faster trade rises. Use the following page:

http://www.smoggstudio.com/kingdom/

Where Commerce type the village's current trade, in your case -65.
Where Garnison type the amount of troops you put in it, then click Valider.

A graph will come up. Roll your mouse over it starting from the bottom left and watch how trade reacts to the amount of turns you take. You will see that at turn X trade will be X. For a quick look you can also check some important stats on the right, of which to me the most important is Commerce nul dans which gives you the amount of turns you will need to take for negative trade to reach 0. This is a very important statistic because no matter how many troops you put in a village, it will start to make a difference once the value is near 0. Until then, it won't matter as much whether you put 50 or 300. Once it rises to near 0 then it will make a huge difference so that is why that statistic is important, you know when to expect the big jump of trade.

And that is the reason why it's harder to take a distant village, because it starts from a very negative value so you need a lot more turns until you can trully affect trade using your troops.

Launcelot
If you find someone willing to take it from you, in which case you just remove all your troops from it and let someone have it, either a friend of yours or your follower. That is called donation. Otherwise you will have to go bankrupt and let the game ditch that village for you. When you go bankrupt you start losing your most costly villages, leaving the most profitable for the end if your financial crisis is still unresolved. You will lose some troops as well, basically those you have in those costly villages if you have any. But not to worry about your troops, because when you decide it's time to take the village back, you will be offered the option to get back your lost troops.

That's all you can do, nothing else.

I have three distant villages hitting me with negative trade. It's not a pleasant experience. I do want to add one comment to what Launcelot has shared. If you have strong followers with good trade, you would be in a better position to endure the negative trade of more distant villages.
