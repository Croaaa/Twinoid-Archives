I'm not an Emperor but my numbers, including citizens which is what you're asking about, are pretty much similar to an Emperor so I can give you my answer.

The recommended peasants-citizens ratio starts at 50% at level 1 farm and level 1 granary, in my opinion you should maintain that ratio for level 2 as well, level 3 you can drop to 42-45% and level 4 and 5 you'll be good with 32-35%. I'm a Prince currently, 95 citizens in total, 35 peasants. My farm and granary are level 4 and going to upgrade to 5 soon. My ratio is 37% as we speak and I am dropping it because I'm making other citizens. Still wasting food but I don't mind for now because once I upgrade to level 5 granary means it's gonna go full fast, which means more citizens will drop. But once that is done I will be in perfect balance with a ratio of 33% (that is peasants 1/3 of total population).

But as you know, food is random so there is no really such a thing as perfect balance, but if you follow these ratios per level you should never come close to famine.
