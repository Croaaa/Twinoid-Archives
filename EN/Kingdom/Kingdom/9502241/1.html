If you want to restart, you can buy a poison potion - you need to spend REAL money to get tokens to get the potion. You might like to think what you have done that might justify starting again. Each 'game' lasts until your health goes to bad and you die yet you are probably a long way from that.

You have a strong neighbour, but they may not last too long and their kingdom will just disappear then. You still have plenty of time to learn the basics. It seems that beginners need to do well enough to gain access to more complicated maps in their next game, so quitting this one might only give you access to simple maps next time. I expect that it will not be too difficult to recover from the 'mess' that you think that you are in.

Learn how to play the grid. Careful selection of matches, and checking where tiles go after that match, can mean that each turn on the grid matches more than 3 tiles.

Think about the balance of citizens you have. At the start about half of them should be peasants making food. If you don't have enough food, a random citizen dies, which can be useful for rebalancing the population.

Think about what buildings you have or want to have. These can be chosen to help your citizens do their jobs better.

Finally, the aim is to build a big kingdom to get lots of points, so you can start in a harder kingdom in your next game. Decide what that will look like. You can go for a lot of villages, or a lot of soldiers in your villages to get a lot of trade. Eventually you can go for both.

Make sure you read the help, check the Forum, and ask questions so you understand as much as possible.
