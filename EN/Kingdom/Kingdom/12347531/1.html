Hacksaw

To die you need to "waste" a certain amount of turns. The amount goes up the more turns you take (granted very slowly, I'm 90 years old and can waste a little over 160 turns).

To see how many turns you can waste before you die, mouse-over your remaining turns and a pop-up box will tell you how many turns you have left to waste. You earn 1 turn every 30 minutes, so if you have, say, 0/120 turns to "waste" before you die, it'll take you 60 hours (roughly 2 and a half days) to die plus however long it takes to get to the 100 turn cap.

Plus however long it takes for famine to occur (after having wasted all turns) and after that however long it takes until citizens are dead. That alone can take another couple days. In my longest game I idled 9 days (remaining turns + wasted). I didn't let my kingdom collapse but if I wanted to do that with a full level 5 granary and 100 citizens it would've taken another 2 days (9999 food / 100 per turn ~ 100 turns / 2 = 50 hours ~ 2 days) until food was gone, then I'd lose my first citizen. But because everytime you lose a citizen, a bit of food is magically restored (cannibalism?), we can assume that it'll be approx. 4 turns in between losing every citizen. But with 100 citizens and 4 turns in between, that alone will take another 400 turns = 200 hours = 8 days! Then I'd die.

So when you get to be 150 years old, you'll get 2 days for 100 remaining turns, 7 days for wasted turns, then if your food is maxed out at 9999 and you've got 100 citizens (more than likely you will), then it's another 10 days until you're gone. Total: 19 days no turns taken.
