While as we discussed on the other thread up there, aligning more than 3 citizen icons simultaneously does NOT bring more citizens, this ONLY applies to the icons being on the SAME row, or column.

XXXX (row of 4 citizen icons)
^ this will not bring 2 citizens, it will still bring 1..

X
X
X
X (column of 4 citizen icons)
^ this will not bring 2 citizens, again just 1..

There is however one way to get more than 1 citizen in 1 turn, as long as the turn you take, causes a 2nd alignment automatically...

---X (a)
XX (b)
---XX-X (c)

^ Here we have 3 rows of citizens. The top row (a) has 1 citizen, the 2nd row (b) has 2 citizens and the 3rd (c) has 3 citizens. I used "-" to place the icons at the exact positions. So in total we have 6 citizen icons on our grid. What will you do? This is the kind of situation I wanted to talk to you about and for which I made this thread. So what you do is this.. You don't have to start from the (a) row to bring the icon down to (b), you can.. but you will waste a turn. You will start from (c), bring the icon on the right to the left, you will align row (c) and that will cause the icon at row (a) to drop down to (b), which will automatically align (b). That will get you 2 citizens in 1 turn.

So again... you CANNOT get more than 1 citizen in 1 turn if you try to align more than 3 icons on the SAME row or column. But you CAN if they are different rows/columns, and your alignment causes another one automatically, like in the example.

I hope I was clear, anyone doesn't get it I'll try to explain again.
