No if the follower loses the battle (his revolt) first, his general attacking his master's capital just simply flees the battlefield but it's not exactly a flee in that he doesn't lose reputation points, the battle just ends and that general loses his injured troops as well (similar to having fled minus the reputation issue), and the master is announced victorious (and earns reputation as well if there was a general in the defense).

Word of advice; if your follower is strong enough and you're worried, try to get yourself some mounted archers, place them in your capital together with ballistas and at the same time put catapults in your follower's capital. What happens is your catapults ensure the revolt is crushed a lot sooner while your mounted archers in your capital ensure this battle will last 5 to 6 times more. Note, you don't have to actually win this (aka kill his general), you only want the revolt to be crushed as soon as possible (because you win either way), so your mounted archers are perfect to delay your follower's attack and you'll suffer the least amount of damage. Also make sure your wall is upgraded as much as possible (presumably more than your follower's wall). Do all that and usually your problem is solved. I said usually because if your follower is too strong and can also get a good number of mounted archers for his capital on top of having enough catapults to attack you, then you probably won't have it easy.

It's all about being one step ahead of your followers. 3 steps ahead is even better.. simple as that.
