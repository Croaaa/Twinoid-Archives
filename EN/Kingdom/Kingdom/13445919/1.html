This is very good information to log, but I think you'll need more than that.
Here's why:

Conditions conducive to famines are programmed by the system that churns out the wheat symbols.
Generally, since turns=more aging, more turns bought will likely decrease the numerical age of when you turn good, bad, or die.

I'm convinced that it's based on how you match the symbols.
I've felt a strong correlation between quick aging and replay turns, and a moderate correlation between quick aging and money symbols.

Combos, in which you match a 3 gold and then 3 food, is good to keep you at normal health.
But *epic* combos in which you match 3+ gold, 3+food, 3+wood etc, is much better if you want to live.
