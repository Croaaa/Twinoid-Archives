[8801828]

The enemy general must be stationary.

The province or village must be under your control (since you will be the defending party).

You have to have a general in the province or village where the enemy general is.

The enemy general may not be a general of your follower.

The 'provoke' button will show up once the conditions are met.

The general that provokes and the garrison of the province will commence battle; none of the other generals will join, unless you order them to.

If the provoked party wins the battle, it does NOT conquer the province or village.

You can get Marquis at 22 years to get level 4 constructions, it is no problem. You just conquer 20 territories in a rush without even putting any unit in it. The sum of negative trade must not exceed -250, else the general will refuse to take more territories and you may not be able to take your desired 20 territories. You have to make a plan first and draw a sketch which territories to conquer, and add up the -1, -4, -9, -16, etc in a calculation scheme to make sure you can stay within the -250 limit with your 20 territories. If you chose a capital near the border of the map, you wont be able to do that, as you have too few -4 and -9 territories and need to grab expensive -25, -36 territories instead. You wont be able to stay within the -250 limit with 20 territories then (with 10 to get Viscount, it will work). Barbarian capitals are good, as they do not cost. After you have 20 territories and got Marquis, you take turns, and as you cannot finance the territories, you will lose them one after the other, one per turn. So to collapse in an optimal way, you only take 19 turns and lose those 19 territories quickly. It is good to bring gold in the bank to zero by spending it on constructions, so you do not lose any gold at all while collapsing. Also, grabbing 20 territories is a race against time with only one general, because if you do not manage it after your turns are at 100 again and you used up the "wasted turns", the auto-turns will trigger every 30 minutes and because gold will be deducted every 30 minutes, you lose your territories one after the other in 30 minute intervals. As you usually need around 1.5 to 3 hours of travel time to get to a new territory plus also need additional travel time through your kingdom to get from one point to another, you should optimize your route before doing this, and probably need to wake up 3-4 times for two nights to take a territory and move on. Its also possible to wait until you have level 2 constructions and a second general, which you can also have around 22 years already if you play your turns well, and which will only take you half the time to get 20 territories. This technique to just grab 10 or 20 territories and collapse by intention in around 10 to 20 turns is actually my favourite way to start a game. The 10 to 20 turns that you lose do not really matter regarding building up trade, but the boost of having more available constructions right from the start and more citizens quickly pays off, because getting Marquis the standard way by building up trade in 20 territories usually takes you an estimated 8 to 10 years (or 400 to 500 turns), but you can achieve the same with just grabbing 20 territories and spending only 20 turns to get rid of them again. However, it will only work with enough free territories around you.
