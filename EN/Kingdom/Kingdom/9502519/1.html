No that's what I said there is no specific formula for gold vs swords, but it's not pure luck either. If you have a lot of gold you have more chances to get swords and that's it. But there is no formula like there is with food vs citizens.

Hard to tell you if you need to disable recruitment. You should decide, if you feel that your army is increasing faster than your trade can manage. Definitely use your troops to help you build trade, if your capital is not in danger of attack, then keep in it only those who are free; depending on what barracks level you have, you can have some free units in your capital.

Is there a village near your capital that you can take? Like max 2-3 squares away? Take it, put a good amount of troops in it; initially that may seem like a bad move but after a few turns your trade will be higher than it was before you took it. Many times, taking a new and not too distant village is a way to bail yourself out when maintenance cost (troops cost) is too high, but, only if you have plenty of troops to put in it so that it won't take a lot of turns until it pays off, and of course only if you still have some decent amount of gold and you're not yet in the threshold of bankruptcy.
