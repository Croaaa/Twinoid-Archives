Launcelot
If it's a distant village that's too much for your economy then it may not be worth the risk just for some resources. Best speak with the guy and ask him if he could share that with you, maybe if he's got plenty in his kingdom or something. Couldn't hurt asking.

If he won't answer, or his answer is negative, increase your traders carefully don't sacrifice crucial members of your peasants for this gotta preserve a good balance, strengthen your economy and then attack the village, hopefully by that time there will still be some decent resources in it. Another way to strengthen your economy besides traders, is create a large army, conquer a closer village, dump troops in and start to take turns - meantime deactivate recuitment if you have to - until your trade is enough to conquer the other distant village. So when you conquer that village, your deficit won't be nearly as much and your economy will survive without a risk.

Zanarkand
depends on how many troops he had stationed there and what kind they are.
if they are only soldiers a couple of catapults should do the trick, if he had ballistas, you will need more catapults.
only send catapults for the attack, though. you will lose a lot of soldiers if you attack with them, as they have a 50/50 chance on dying vs soldiers and a 100% chance of deing against everything else (except cats, if they are on defence)
Attacking with 10 cats vs only soldiers should be enough for a victory. (if he hasn't got too many soldiers there.)

Pay attention to his title...the higher his title is, the more soldiers he probably has stationed on his villages.
