How you word your messages could be crucial to how, or even if, other players respond to them. To a non-role-player, it may not be obvious that that's what you're doing, and talk of your "empire" or "mighty armies" or whatever may make you come across instead as someone who is taking the game too seriously and who is rather arrogant.

I'd suggest making it obvious that you are role-playing by forming your messages something like this:
A messenger from Viscount Plaihaven arrives in your capital with the following warning/offer of peace/proclamation*:
Your actual message goes here.*Delete as applicable.

If they're interested in role-play, they can reply in a similar fashion. If not, they should at least realise that you are just having a bit of fun.
