So, I have two enemies that are relatively close by, enemies by way of a plethera of backstabbing, and double teaming, and strategically backhanded play, both on my part, and their own. I'm the biggest person on my server currently (although only a duke, lol), and so eventually, even though both of them were double teaming me, i was able to overpower them and capture their capitals. Only problem is, even though i won the war, and captured both of their capitals, it gave them complete access to my lands (even though I have them both listed as enemies, with no rights of passage). This effectively gives them a massive strategic advantage against me in that there is a bridge between their lands now, allowing them to pool their resources, and, moreover, they can now just run units right into my capital. Currently, they're each sitting around 30+ units right at my capital, and are planning to rebel in their main cities. This puts me between a rock and a hard place, because if i send units to quell their rebellions, it'll give them easy access to my capital, but if i don't send units to quell their rebellions, then they'll be able to rebel, effectively starting the process all over again, only this time they'll have the advantage.

It seems to me that if you have an enemy with someone, and you conquer their capital you should still be able to keep them out of your fucking lands, if you want.
