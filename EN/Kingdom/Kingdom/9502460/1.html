Gambit: a chess move early in the game in which the player sacrifices minor pieces in order to obtain an advantageous position

I have discovered a cool strategy to attack far away lands without losing trade.

First you need a follower/master/team mate with a some troops and 2 generals, and you will need a big army divided between 2-3 generals of your own.

Ok here we go:

Sometimes only a big stretch of savage villages (without troops or some troops) divides you and another powerful player. But that player may have a bad defended capital in reach. To get there however, you will have to capture a few villages, far away from your capital, with huge negative trade. There is an easy solution for this however.

Take the villages and rush for the enemies capital. Leave no troops in those villages, they have huge negative trade ( I have a village with -121 trade in my current game). Let a friend/ally/follower take over those villages, then take your turns. Take the villages over again and let your friend take his turns. Both players won't suffer from the negative trade, because trade only occurs while taking turns on the grid, but you will be switching places.

Thank me later for this strategy But I bet some advanced players have discovered this already
