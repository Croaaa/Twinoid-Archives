In the beginning I would also recommend, as Paradoxien says, to have 50% peasants. You're more vulnerable to famine, so having 50% peasants could really save you in times of trouble. Whenever you're ready and have some soldiers at your disposal, start taking 2 villages near your capital if possible.
This allows you to get the rank of sir and it allows you to upgrade your palace to level 2 and extend your farm etc. to level 2. This makes you produce more with less citizens and you'll be able to store more resources. Now you will have a decent stock to save you whenever your grid messes up. Also keep working on your trade and send more soldiers to your conquered villages to increase trade.
And don't be discouraged if you find yourself in a difficult situation. Even the best players can have multiple famines whenever the grid messes up and doesn't want to cooperate at all. Just try to make as much combo's as possible and see your chances of survival grow.

There some thoughts on food production and the grid I would like to share. As most players now, the granary can be upgraded from level one to five. With each increase the amount of food (and citizens you can have) is increased. Additionally, the number of citizens you can comfortably support is roughly equal to the square root of the granary's maximum capacity. Please note the rough chart below to see what I mean.


Level 1: 250 Wood 16 Citizens
Level 2: 600 Wood 25 Citizens
Level 3: 1,500 Wood 40 Citizens
Level 4: 3,500 Wood 60 Citizens
Level 5: 9,999 Wood 100 Citizens*


*It is possible to have more than 100 if you are at 99 and have enough people icons to create two or more additional citizens.


The reason I posted that chart above is because the higher you go the more the margin of error increases. For For instance, at maximum capacity, and with 16 citizens, you will run out of food in 16 turns if you fail to produce any food during that period. At level two, it's 24 turns until you starve. By level five, that margin increases to 100 turns. As such, the importance of food production and having enough citizens to produce it is extremely important.


At the early levels, I go with half to two-fifths of my population as peasants. By level five, that number is 26. With that many, I almost never go below 9,000 food. I am currently at 94 citizens, and don't anticipate having additional citizens affecting that margin overly much.
