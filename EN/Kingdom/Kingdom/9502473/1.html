Do there need to be specific conditions in order to provoke a fight between you and another general? I am currently in my follower's capital and there is an enemy there also, but I do not have the option to provoke a battle.

When you provoke what you are doing is taunting the other player's general into attacking your territory, so you only have the option to provoke when your general is in a location that belongs to you. When you are in the other player's territory and want to fight, your only option is to attack. (The opposite is also true: you can not attack another player inside your own territory; you have to either wait for them to attack you or provoke them into doing so.)

Oops, I misread your question slightly.

I think the reason you can't provoke the enemy at your follower's capital is that, although it is part of your wider kingdom, it is still regarded as belonging to him and not you. In a similar way, you are unable to instruct your general to defend any of your follower's locations or place your own troops there to help him raise trade.
