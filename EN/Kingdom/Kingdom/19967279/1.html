you will almost always be behind the trade curve as far as income from a village is concerned.

you can drop all your soldiers into a village, build up trade, then remove all but Trade divided by 2. then move onto the next village. wash, rinse, repeat. and to offset the trade imbalance, create a bunch of Traders, and build as high a Market as you can. the Traders and highest level Market offset the loss in gold from your extra soldiers. But the Traders only make you money on each gold match in your turn grid. So occasionally you will spend more gold during a turn, but when you match up gold coins, you will make more than you spend during that turn.

Hope that makes sense.

As you take turns, trade will increase in your villages with troops left in them.


The more troops in the village, the faster the trade will increase. Trade is a way to generate extra income to fund your army. One good tactic is to have a general with alot of troops and leave them in one area to increase trade over a short period of time.


Say you have 100 troops and your goal is to establish an area with 20 trade. Leave all your troops in that area and take turns until the trade rises to 20. When you've reached your goal of 20 you'll want to leave 10 troops in that area always so your trade won't decline.


Then you move your troops onto the next area.


Remember, 1 troop can manage 2 trade.
(even 1 extra, though it will show you in the negative )


Theres an advanced way to work out the ideal numbers you should be aiming for, but I'll leave that for someone else to explain if your interested in it.


That tactic gets more difficult as time goes on, but as a rule of thumb, whenever you take turns you'll want as many of your troops as possible stationed in villages, so your always increasing trade.
