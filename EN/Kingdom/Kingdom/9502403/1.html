When I first started playing, I had the mistaken belief that building a Guard Tower allowed you to see how many troops other players had because I'd misunderstood the pop-up description of that building:The Guard Tower is valuable strategic viewpoint which because of its height, can be used to see enemy forces from miles away. This is also where soldiers are trained to become Paladins.
I was disappointed when I finally built a Guard Tower and discovered that it didn't reveal troops numbers after all, but I was also relieved because it meant that other players didn't have the ability to see how few troops I had either.

I like the idea of having spies, but I also like the fact that when the size of your army is unknown you can sometimes avert another player's attack on your territory by bluffing that you have a much larger army than you actually do. To a would-be attacker, a puny group of 10 soldiers marching to greet them looks no different from a much larger force of 99 men.
