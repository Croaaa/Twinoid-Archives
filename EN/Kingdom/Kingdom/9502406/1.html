You are correct. Every new village you conquer will start off with a trade deficit equal to the square of the distance from your capital. 5 squares away? -25. 3 squares away? -9.

The only way to increase this trade is to leave a large amount of soldiers in the village. It will take a while to null out that trade, but that's the way it works. Why is it like this? Easy. It's to make it more difficult to expand. As you rank up and expand your army, deficits like that won't matter as much. For instance, if you reach the rank of Marquis and have an enormous army, a -36 trade might only be about as difficult to overcome as a -9 was for you when you were just a Sir. After all, if it was just a -1 trade for every new village conquered, and I had an army with 400 troops, what would stop me from just plowing through the entire map over the course of a day or two?
