It's not. I have to think some of you must have noticed, that if you dump a number of troops in a village, take turns and raise its trade, then remove part of them to take them elsewhere, you can remove exactly just as many so that trade won't decline, which isn't x2-1, but rather x2.


Try this.. Say dump 50 troops, raise trade to +10, then remove 45 and leave the remaining 5 in there. Go to Home > kingdom tab and find your village. Guess what it says.. And it works actually, I've been testing it for a long while and trade doesn't move by -1. In our example it will remain stable at +10, with 5 troops in there.


Of course as you know, it's different if you place 5 troops and let it rise all by itself. In that case trade will eventually stabilize at +9 because it's the -1 in the equation that comes into play.


Now how can that be useful? It's not a big deal really, but there are some trade building techniques that would benefit from gaining 1 extra trade point out of each village. It's 1 free point if you come to think about it, you get 10 trade instead of 9 and you do that with 5 troops.
