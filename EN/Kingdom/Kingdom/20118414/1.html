Having figured that out, some advice to get you started..


Make sure to keep 50% of population reserved for peasants while assigning jobs. This will help keep granary full or near full. Doing this helps you bring more citizens and relatively faster. As a rule of thumb, take note that although citizen icons will keep coming no matter what, their appearance is almost 100% affected by the amount of food you have in stock opposed to the size of population. What you want to do is keep food stocks above the square of population, starting from now, through every level and throughout your entire reign. So with 20 population you want 20x20=400 food at the least. Of course at some point you won't be able to stay above their square due to granary limitations, and that's when you need to upgrade it by expanding your kingdom and advancing titles. As granary is upgraded, it allows for bigger stocks so you can keep it up. When you've reached level 3, you can start lowering your peasants proportion under 50% based on how you're doing, but making sure you make enough food to keep granary full or near full.. so don't neglect to assign other important jobs too, you need to experiment with that. More citizens is the recipe of power, so everything starts and ends with peasants. And having said that, it's a good thing to have farm go hand in hand with granary as you upgrade = moar food.
