The only connection is the one between food you have in stock and appearance of citizens. As a general rule, the more food you have in stock the more frequent their appearance becomes and on top of that, if you manage to maintain a full granary per given level, i.e. level 2 allows 600 max, they'll become even more frequent.


In specific, what you need to do is try to have more food than the square of population, i.e. if you have 20 citizens, square of 20 is 400. Let's assume your granary is level 2, so you still got another 200 to 600 max. If you stay in the area of 400 or less, your chances of getting more citizens reduces to nearly nothing, but if you exceed 400 which is exactly the square of your population and stay in the area of let's say 500, you'll notice a rush of citizens just give it 5-6 turns if they don't show right away, keep being above 400 and it'll happen.


Of course since level 2 only allows 600, that means once you build 25 population, you won't be able to stay above square anymore because square of 25 is 625.. and here is your limitation, so if you want to see another rush of citizens after that, you need level 3. HOWEVER.. you will still be able to get a few more citizens even at level 2, IF your granary remains full, in other words if you keep wasting food. It won't be a rush.. but it's better than nothing.


So key points:


1. granary above square of population per given level
2. when square of population isn't possible anymore, upgrade to next level and expect another rush when square is met again
3. full granary will always net you more citizens even if granary needs upgrade, won't be a rush but it will be something - don't avoid to waste a bit of food, you may think it's useless but it's not, wasting helps


PS: There is no connection whatsoever between gold and appearance of swords, if you ever read something about that (i.e. at help section), pretend you never read it, it's entirely false. You can be on the brink of bankruptcy with grid raining swords, just like you can be wasting gold and celebrate because you just got a sword after 15 turns. Entirely random stuff, just like wood and hammers, health and aging.
