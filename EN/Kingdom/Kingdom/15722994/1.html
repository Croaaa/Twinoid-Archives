nicus9

I don't think anyone can really tell you what you should do without seeing it for themselves. Your actions would depend on his army size, your army size, how quickly you are growing, your past history with him, how willing he is to listen to you, etc. So a lot more variables then we can possible comment on lol.


Depending on the difference in your army size your action should change. If he has a VASTLY larger army where you have no hope then you can try to negotiate your surrender (and possibly let him conquer you so you can save your troops).


If he is only slightly larger in army size then you could play defensively and maybe he will give up or waste him army enough that you could go on the offensive.


If you are the same size then you can do pretty much anything and anything goes depending on your tactics


If you don't have enough of an army to defend your territories then you may have to abandon them to defend your capital but watch your gold income. Or depending on the map you may be able to force him to fight in the territories and keep him away from your capital, as I said I don't know enough to really tell you for certain what you should do.


As for how many soldiers you should station in each territory, it doesn't really matter. Eventually your territory will give you 2x-1 gold (where x = the number of soldiers in that territory). Use this website to see Territory Gold Simulator
Commerce is the territory's current trade and garrison is the number of troops there.
So if you station 3 soldiers in a territory then that territory will max out at giving you 5 gold a turn. If you station 10 soldiers in a territory it will eventually max out at giving you 19 gold a turn.
