I've been seeing a lot of repeat questions and answers on this thread and I think, due to a lack of functioning English-based Kingdom Wikipedia, lots of new players are losing interest as soon as they get into the game.
-
To combat that, I propose we create a 'Frequently Asked Questions' sticky that has a compilation of questions that seem to get asked repeatedly.
-
To lessen the burden on any single player, I'd like to make this a volunteer-effort.
-
This thread will be the "rough draft" version of the Frequently Asked Questions thread that (hopefully) one of the Moderators will create and sticky for future reference.

If things work out well enough, it's even possible that the information provided here can go into creating an English Kingdom Wiki for the European and North American playerbase.
-
When enough questions have been answered, we'll make a final post with summarizes all the questions & answers given, then when it's been deemed satisfactory, we will close the thread and I will message the final draft to one of the three moderators of this forum and ask them to make a sticky out of it.
-
How can you contribute?
Easily - Just think of a question you've seen asked pretty often, or a question you know most new players will ask (such as who to contact in regards to players who violate rules or simple in-game questions like how to restore a positive trade to a barbaric village that's in the negatives). Once you have a question, provide an accurate answer to the question and post it here in this format:
-
Q: Question?
A: Answer.
-
RULES FOR POST
• Question with Answers only (Accurate Answers)
• Keep discussions to a minimum (to avoid clutter)
• Keep postings understandable - It doesn't help if we can't read your post
-
So, without further adieu, let the Questions and Answers begin!

Q: How do I increase my civilian population?
A: The more food you have in stock increases the rate of citizen icon spawn.
-
Q: How do I increase my soldier population?
A: The more gold you have in stock increases the rate of sword icon spawn.
-
Q: How do I increase the trade of a conquered territory?
A: By placing units on an area, it will gradually increase the trade value to positive, every few turns. The more units, the faster the positive influence.
-
Q: How to report players violating the Rules and Terms of Agreement?
A: Send the player's username and a brief description of the violation(s) to the current forum moderators (ie. npfan, coro or norby89).

Q: What is the use of the Cabin and the Farm?
A: The Cabin increases how much wood your Woodcutters produce, as well as increasing storage for Wood. The Farm increases how much food your peasants produce.
-
Q: How many soldiers do I need on a territory to earn gold instead of lose/balance out?
A: Using this simple formula, (Tx2)-1=Trade; T = Number of Troops, you can figure out what your Trade value will be, total, at the end of enough turns.
-
Q: I've moved my general to a Rare Resources village but I can't collect it - Why?
A: The Rare Resources is just like any other village. If already conquered by another Kingdom or it is Barbaric, you will need to conquer it first before you can collect resources.
-
Q: I've conquered a territory but the trade is extremely negative! What do I do?
A: First off, make sure you have all your conquered areas connected. It reduces the overall trade negatives if you have connecting territories. Only conquered Capital cities don't require a trade cost.

Q: Left for awhile, came back and food, gold, citizens troops were all gone.. what
A: What did you expect you can't just disappear you could be gone for a year.. don't let turns go past 100 when that happens the game will wait for you a while longer and if you still don't take any turns, your kingdom will start collapsing until it disappears from the map and eventually you'll die. Just above the grid where you take turns you see where it says your remaining turns? Hover over the number and you'll see your available wasted turns. These turns are the answer to the bold part above. This is how much longer the game will wait for you to come back and start taking turns. So if you got like 30 wasted turns available, that means 30 / 2 = 15 hours because wasted turns are automatically taken 1 every 30 minutes just like normal turns stack up. So you got yourself 15 hours before you start losing everything and countdown begins right after you've gone past 100 turns not taken. One last thing, you earn wasted turns the longer you play in the current session, meaning you got more the older you are.
