I was in your situation early in my current game. If the village you want is held by basic soldiers, you might be able to take the village is you're smart.

I checked out your map just now and noticed it is garrisoned by regular soldiers. I think with ten catapults, you should be able to take the village. The only real concern is both the trade cost of so many units and keeping yourself above water while you build up those catapults.

I would suggest transporting as many troops as you can to your other village to built its trade while making the catapults. Once you have ten catapults, I would suggest attacking the village. Catapults have a 200 % bonus when attacking any location. With that many catapults taking out all 27 soldiers shouldn't be a problem. You might lose some catapults, but you'll win in the end.

Launcelot
No, that happens only in 2 cases. One, your capital gets conquered and two, you go broke. In both cases this is a temporary state not permanent, you go back and reclaim both villages and troops. If you die your villages become savage and your entire army disappears, your capital becomes barbaric too.
On february 19, 2012
Launcelot
One last note, if you die while you haven't yet reclaimed a village and the troops in it, then this is the only case where that village remains savage with barbarian troops forever, nobody can reclaim it since the former owner (you) is dead. So naturally, whoever wants to conquer it they have to fight for it.
