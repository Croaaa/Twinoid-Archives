Sure. Just avoid conquering another follower of your master because chances are he/she is not gonna like it. By doing so you practically steal part of your master's prestige. Your master gains prestige by conquering followers who then expand and every village they claim, it counts to their prestige. But here's the thing, if you have a follower of your own, your master will not gain any prestige out of your follower, except 1 point coming from their capital, but no points when they expand. So just have that in mind, unless you've discussed it with your master and they approve. Other than that, sure you can have your own followers and tax them.

MagicDragon

You especially want to avoid pissing off you master if he is one of the more powerful ones around. If you have a master whose trade is around 3,000 or so, you definitely want to be on his good side. If he has a trade that high (along with high reputation as well), then his army is likely around 3,000 troops or more. Pissing off a master that strong will only bring you pain.
-
One thing to keep in mind with regard to a particular master is how well they seem to manage their followers. If you spawn within the territory of a powerful master the first thing you should do is check his or her kingdom profile and those of his or her followers. If he or she has a lot of his or her followers listed as friends (and those followers listing him or her as a friend), then you will want to tread carefully in your dealings with them. If they are listed as friends, than that master likely has cooperative and friendly followers. It would be folly to attack the master, his or her followers, or the territory of his or her followers.
-
There are also benefits to being a follower of a powerful master. In my last game, I reached the title of Emperor. I had a trade over 3,000 and for the most part my followers were left alone (I did have trouble from Brodilko attacking one of my followers, but the trouble I had with him was my fault to start with). I had trouble with another player attacking one of my followers as well. I ended up destroying his kingdom and making him my follower (he died shortly thereafter). After that, most other players steered clear of me or my followers.
-
A powerful master will be able to ward off other players simply by their presence alone. A smart player wanting to expand will be hesitant to attack the follower of a powerful emperor, for instance as attacking the follower could piss off his or her master. As strong master will have little problems squashing you if you piss them off.
