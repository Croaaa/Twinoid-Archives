Launcelot
MA against Soldier: MA wins and is left with about 1/3rd of his health. If he has 30 MA, bring 60 soldiers and you'll walk out of it with 20 alive soldiers.

MA against Catapult/Ballista: MA wins and left with 1/4 of health. If he's got 30 MA, bring 60 of them to walk out with about 30. So things look a little better here for your army.

MA against MT/Cavalier/Archer/Pikeman: MA wins with 1/5 of health left. If he has 30 MA, bring 60 to walk out with about 40 or something. Even better for you in this situation.

So far MA wins and you have to bring more of the others to survive the battle. But next case is different:

MA against Knight: Knight wins and is left with about 1/4th of health. If he has 30 MA, bring 30 Knights and you'll walk out with about 15. To make a more proper comparison, if you bring 60 like previous cases, quite possible to not lose a single Knight. And this is the only case where you actually have advantage.

Please allow for these numbers to change a bit because there is also a factor called luck, where you never know what units will be selected to come back for a 2nd duel and what not. But you get the idea. MA are very tough units, be prepared for a long fight if he's got a lot.

Now, what I would do if I didn't have Knights. Since I always concentrate on making it very easy to construct cats and bals, I would not choose to waste any other upgraded units, but just bring many machines. I know I could lose a lot to win against MA but I wouldn't mind because I would make them easy. Definitely better than having to consume more rare resources to remake my dead upgraded units. But that's just me and how I choose to play.
