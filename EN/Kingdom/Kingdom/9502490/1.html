Speaking of strategies...

Make sure you don't leave cats at bordering villages, because they will go down fast as hell. When a cat is defending, it is almost equal in strength with a soldier (in fact it's exactly only 1/5 or 20% more powerful than a soldier - this means you put them in a fight, the cat will always win the duel but will be left injured with 20% health), and the exact opposite goes with bals.

So you can use any unit you want for trade but avoid using cats at bordering villages. More advanced strategies would include pikemen, if your opponent has mounted troops, unless they are mounted archers, your pikemen will totally destroy mounted troops.

I have my pikemen always 1 village inside of borders. I keep 2-digit ballistas at borders together with 3-digit soldiers, they will significantly damage attackers, and if they lose the fight (supposedly the enemy uses mounted troops which usually last longer) pikemen are waiting inside to finish them off. Also the fight at the borders will last so long I will have gathered a ton more ballistas if I have to, so my pikemen may not be needed at all.

And I keep cats in the inner villages and in the capital so I can easily and quickly grab them to start a military campaign.
