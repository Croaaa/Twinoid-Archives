I've been attempting to work out how reputation is earned for a while now. In my last game, I managed my greatest reputation yet at 91 reputation points. One thing that bugs me is how it works.


I think I finally figured it out, though. I think it depends on how many units your troops kill over the number of free troops your general can have assigned to him. To make this statement clearer, I'll use my current battle I am fighting.


I attacked a barbarian controlled village with 77 units defending. The general I sent has one reputation point. He has only one free unit, as such, every unit he kills after the first should contribute to his reputation earnings. If I attacked with a ten reputation general, then he wouldn't earn any reputation until his eleventh kill. As such, you don't start having kills contribute to reputation earnings until your kills pass the number of free units your general can have.
