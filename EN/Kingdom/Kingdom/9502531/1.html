You need to know the bonus your soldiers have, thats easy, they all have a small explanation, and then know your adversary if you are attacking troops...
Ex: I'm in middle of a battle, the adversary only had normal soldiers and Balistas
I challenged him with 20 catapults that provide a 200% bonus damage 20 Mounted night that provide 100% bonus damage against normal soldiers and 15 soldiers a total of 55 troops against 30 and more 17 in reinforcements...

The opponent having a single balista and normal soldiers, my army has a massive advantage over his troops, the result so far is a ratio of 1/4 kills i have lost 5 units 4 soldiers and a mounted night, and destroyed 1 balista, killed 16 soldiers and 4 Cavaliers, note that cavaliers provide 100% bonus against archers, but i dont have any so they provide no advantage over normal soldiers, if the adversary had a battalion of pikemans, i would have to think twice about their 250% bonus against horses, my cavalry would be in danger of being annihilated, on the other hand, since he had no mounted units, it would be useless for me to recruit pikemans for the battle...

A good strategy is always plan the best units to counter the enemy, Catapults are very cheap, provide a great bonus and last longer than any unit in the field, this should be a priority in any attacking force, but they are useless to defend territories, thus the balistas provide a reverse bonus of 200% on deffence and counter the catapults in battle...

1.a. Mounted Archers just like Mounted Knights are harder to make than other units because they utilise 2 resources, so if you wanna do what you're saying, you're gonna need a whole lot of resources to build a strong army of MA's. And say MK's are a bit easier because at least metal you can create on your own, but MA's need cotton on top of horses. Also, Knights will beat Mounted Archers because they have more health, so you're still not invincible. You're never invincible no matter what unit type you prefer, they all lose to a certain type whether on attack or defence.

1.b. Ballistas may be a lot easier to make than MA's but again, you're not invicible here either. Ballistas will lose to MA's in every possible scenario, even in defence because MA's have more health and the Ballistas have no bonus against them. So like before, you're not invincible, but yes, Ballistas are a MUST when you need some good and solid defence.

Summing up a and b: Better make both Ballistas and MA's. You can't rely on just one of them, each has a handicap, good management means you can reduce that handicap by bringing the best units in each individual battle.

2. Bonuses apply to those units when they go up against a certain type of units. Archers will always have a bonus against Knights, Cavaliers will always have a bonus against Archers, etc. The situation between Cats and Bals is more complex in that their bonus is affected by whether they are on attack or defence. Cat on attack will always have a bonus against all units (except MA's - MA's beat Cats in every scenario) and Bal on defence will always have a bonus against all units (except MA's - MA's beat Bals in every scenario).

3. Yes and you are still a defender. Your general (who brought the reinforcements) will join the defence in a separate group, not in the same group as the troops stationed in the village. You will lose the battle only if both groups of your army are gone. If only your general's group is gone, your general will die, but you can still win the battle if the other group holds till the end. Then you will have to recreate your general, but his reputation points - if he has any - will be lost. On the other hand if you win that battle, your general will earn some reputation points.

4. Never, there is no time limit.

5. Different types of units do not form different groups, they join the same group. Unfortunately you have no control over the duels, the system decides what units to put in the duels and the decision is based solely on luck. It's something that we all wish it gets fixed in future versions of the game, but I expect it to take a long long time because it will require a very complex programming. For now all you can do is notice what units your opponent has, then decide on what units are best to match them. Some duels will not be in your favour, others will. Make sure the duels in your favour are more than the duels against you.

Generally I like attacking with Cats but I've made a whole lot of them. Why I prefer Cats because they have the largest bonus and they lose only to MA's. Here is what happens to a Cat after he wins a duel:

vs Soldier: health left 80% (4/5 of the bar remaining)
vs Archer, Pikeman, Cavalier, MK: health left 75% (3/4 of the bar remaining)
vs Knight: health left 60% (3/5 of the bar remaining)

No other unit can do this when attacking because no other unit has 200% bonus. That is why I prefer Cats.

Now if my Cat goes up against a MA, the MA will win with 10% left and that is the only duel my Cat will lose, but it will be close I mean 10% left on the MA he'll go down before he even starts his next duel.

Turn all that around and consider the same thing will happen with a Ballista on defence. So I like defending with Bals, but I also use a lot of soldiers because I need them to keep my trade high and also to buy me some time to bring reinforcements if I have to. Upgraded units I use for more specialised battles. For example if an enemy comes at me with a horde of MA's I'm not gonna defend only with Ballistas, in fact most of my defence will be Knights! Knight will beat MA and will go back to the group with 10% left. Similarly I wanna attack a village with a shit ton of MA's in it I'm not gonna go there only with Cats I'll use Knights too!

So yeah, Cats and Bals are units I really like having loads of, never failed me.
