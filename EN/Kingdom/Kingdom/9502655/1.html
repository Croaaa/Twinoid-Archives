They are not connected and that means you cannot collect trade generated in these villages. But trade itself will keep functioning like it use to regardless. Reminds me of what I saw happening between 2 rivals; there were 2 rival kingdoms having a war, 3-4 consecutive battles actually. The defending side won all of them, then went on a rampage against the other guy, took a village and pushed him back, then did something very clever. Instead of going directly for his capital (still a couple villages left before his capital), she decided to aim for another village first which was the only link to the rest of his kingdom. She took that too and once that happened, the guy was left with 3 villages disconnected from his capital, which led to a huge issue for his economy. That alone soften him up and then, she went for his capital. Of course the battle never ended as the guy died first, but it was a very effective strategy nonetheless.

Anyways.. why I'm telling you that story, because it's really important to keep your kingdom solid. Even having a follower sit between 2 of your villages is something that needs caution imo, personally I'm against it. Just keep playing and you will start losing villages. From what I've seen, you start from those villages with the lowest trade.
