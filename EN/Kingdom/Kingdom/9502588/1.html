MagicDragon
It has nothing to do with paying real cash. When making new citizens, you are given an option to choose a job for them. One of those jobs is recruiter. With each recruiter made you get an additional soldier you can recruit for each three swords aligned.

If a player has five recruiters, for instance, they'd be able to recruit six soldiers for each three swords they align.

Launcelot
Oh and what I mean by aiming for it? I mean for example, say one of your villages is going to be attacked soon. If you happen to be at your computer and you have a general somewhere close, send him there and when the enemy gets there after you, take all the troops from the village, put them all on your general and provoke the enemy.

By provoking the enemy with all troops on him, your general is engaged in the battle with no garrison present. What this means is all kills will count towards your general's reputation and I have made it 500% sure that reputation is affected by the number of kills (and time like I've said before - the faster you finish the better). If a garrison is there (garrison is the chief in command of those troops left in a village without a general for those who may not know), whatever units his group kills are wasted reputation, you don't earn anything of it. So, no garrison for rep!

Battle has already started and you were late? No problem, again if a general is handy send him there and join the defence. You will earn some points, better than no points.

Do it, I've done it a thousand times I've got a bloody master's in provoking. It really pays off. So again, aim for it. I'm not talking about attacking reputation because obviously when you attack, you do it with your general.
