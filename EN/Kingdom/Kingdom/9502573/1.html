You should be also looking at age. The title itself sure means something, it means he probably has high level buildings etc which means he can produce more resources, looking at his castle graphic you can tell approximately how many citizens in there, for example castle+bridge without houses means 50 citizens, so you know he probably can recruit a lot of soldiers, probably can build ballistas and catapults quickly, you can look at his kingdom see if he has any rare resources available which could make him stronger.. You know all these things can tell stuff about his power.

But, like I said in the beginning, you're forgetting one very important factor, age. The player may be a Duke so you reasonably think to yourself, so he's got level 4 buildings how's it possible he goes up against a Prince who has access to level 5. If the Duke is 100 years old and the Prince is like 60, it probably means the Duke had time to build an army strong enough to bring it. Means he knows very well what he's doing.

Please note I haven't looked at the case you're describing but I'm speaking from experience, don't forget to look at the player's age, it's a very important factor perhaps more important than title itself. Keep that advice for the future many players overlook age and start making plans.

I forgot another very important factor, trade. That usually goes with army size. In your calculations, add his reputation and his title. Level 4 title means 50 free units in the capital, plus reputation say 50, that's 100 free units in total. Suppose he has 1000 trade, this means he can have 1100 units without paying a single penny, look at his castle graphic see how upgraded it is, more than 50 citizens means he could take a -100 hit so that should put him to around 1200 troops.

Just gave you a basic idea on how to tell someone else powers. But like I said before, don't forget age.
