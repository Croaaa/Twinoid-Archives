basic rule your population is limited to the size of your granary and since you are only lvl 1 it is 250, so you are limited to 16 villagers...
To increase population you need to level up, by conquering at least 2 villages, and then upgrade your buildings to lvl 2, this way you can increase you population up to 25 and recruit a second general...

I forgot to say, population = square root of food...
Limits are
250 - 16
600 - 25
1500 - 40
3500 - 60
9999 - 100 (max game population)

Population can vary up to +5 villagers, but it will happen very slowly when you gt stuck at a certain level, thus it is possible to have a population of 20 on lvl 1 and be stronger than a lvl 2 player that only has 16...

If you are stuck on lvl 1 as you are with no options to expand, it is possible to rise you population up to 20, a carefully managed population can support a considerable army even with 0 trade, you will have to rely a lot on building the most catapults in the less possible time and trader to support you...
Access to early resources can help recruit stronger units to conquer a couple villages, if your master guests his arse busted, you might have a chance to reclaim some villages, but you need catapults to do that, 5 should be enough for starters...
