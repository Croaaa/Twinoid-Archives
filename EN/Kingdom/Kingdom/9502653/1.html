Launcelot
When you upgrade granary and go near full once again you will start seeing citizen icons a lot more often. More icons appear when your food value is above the square of population. After a while on each level your population has increased so much that the granary is unable to hold more food, but once you upgrade that enables you to break the square of population once again and that will bring more icons.

Nevertheless, there is a population limit in the game and that is 100, which is feasible at level 5, at level 4 I highly doubt it. Note that you don't have to always be above the square of population to see citizen icons; they will still appear anyway, just more rarely. You haven't seen an icon in a while but sooner or later you will. If you want to increase the chance to see one, try to preserve a full granary if you can, or wait until you can upgrade it, that will be even better. Upgrade farm too, together with peasants it helps to keep granary full. Good luck and remember:

upgrade palace => upgrade granary => upgrade farm => population increase => more of everything => more power

In by book, the strongest all around unit is the one with the least handicaps.

#1 mounted archer (1 handicap)
beats: everyone except knight (knight is left with 30% health)

#2 catapult/ballista (attack/defense) (1 handicap)
beats: everyone except mounted archer (mounted archer is left with 30% health), you just have to never confuse attack and defense because without their bonus they're full of handicaps

#3 knight (2 handicaps)
beats: everyone except catapult/ballista (attack/defense) both war machines are left with 60% health, and archer (archer is left with 20% health that is a tough win for the archer but a win is a win and it's guaranteed)

#4 archer (3 handicaps)
beats: everyone except mounted archer (mounted archer is left with 20% health, tough win), catapult/ballista (attack/defense) both war machines are left with 75% health, and cavalier (cavalier left with 50%)
equal to: mounted knight win or lose, doesn't count as handicap

#5 mounted knight (4 handicaps)
beats: everyone except mounted archer (mounted archer is left with 20% health, tough win), catapult/ballista (attack/defense) both war machines are left with 75% health, pikeman (I think about 70% health left) and knight (knight left with 40%)
equal to: archer and cavalier win or lose, doesn't count as handicap

#6 cavalier (4 handicaps)
beats: exactly the same like #5 with the only exception being that he beats archer and is equal to #5

At this point I'd like to say why I put mounted knight above cavalier. With a quick look they deserve the same rank, but mounted knight has his bonus against the most common unit, soldier, and that makes him more useful. On the other hand cavalier has his bonus against archer, a less common unit than soldier.

#7 pikeman (4 handicaps)
beats: everyone except mounted archer (mounted archer is left with 20% health), catapult/ballista (attack/defense) both war machines are left with 75% health, archer (archer left with 50%), and knight (knight left with 40%)

Another short comment here, as you notice he's got the same amount of handicaps like #5 and #6 but I put him last compared to them because there is no one he's equal to, obviously he's equal to himself but no one else, so that counts as another small handicap. But #5 and #6 apart from those who they beat, they also have at least 1 they're equal to.

#8 soldier (a million handicaps)

Launcelot
Yeah, war costs turns, gold and food. What you want is make it as cheap as you can, and that differs from time to time, from kingdom to kingdom and battle to battle. Here's an example:

Suppose your opponent has 20 ballistas. I'll present 2 alternatives:

1. Go with full catapults, say 30. What will happen is you'll lose 20 catapults, maybe 15 if you're very lucky. Suppose you are indeed very lucky and you lose 15. What does 15 mean to you? It means:

If you need 3 sets of hammers to make 1 catapult, that is 45 sets to make 15. With a bit more workers you'll need 2 sets of hammers, and that is 30 sets.

2. Go with 30 soldiers using 1 general, wait until 20 die and the general is left with 10. By that time the enemy will have almost all his 20 ballistas injured, not all probably because we have to assume that some ballistas will never engage into a duel. I suspect the number will be about 15-17 injured. Then, you throw your other general to the battle with these 30 catapults from alternative 1. What that means to you? It means:

If you have let's say a reasonable 4 recruiters, pretty reasonable number, you'll need 4 sets of swords to produce 20 soldiers, which is about how many you will lose (perhaps add a couple more, but the 30 catapults you'll reinforce with, will provide great protection and you won't lose more soldiers).. so 4 sets of swords for the soldiers. How many catapults will you lose against an almost fully injured army of ballistas? 3? 4? What does that mean to you? 8 to 10 sets of hammers. So we have 4 sets of swords, plus 10 sets of hammers = 14 sets of stuff, that will be the cost of your attack.

I dunno if you see the benefit here, but alternative 2 costs half of alternative 1. The idea is to injure as many ballistas as you can, without risking to have your first general killed. You lose more soldiers than catapults and that is the key to make the battle cheaper for you.

And to answer to someone that will possibly say, why don't you go with 120 soldiers to kill these 20 ballistas? You will likely lose something like 90 and with 4 recruiters you will need 18 sets of swords, pretty on par with your complicated alternative Launcelot. My answer is time.. you don't want the battle to last a very long time for the obvious reason, at some point you'll throw your catapults to be done with quickly and efficiently.

Lastly, I said earlier that it differs from kingdom to kingdom, battle to battle etc., because if you suck at recruiting but you own at building, perhaps you should use different strategies. Or if the enemy has other units. I just gave you an example which I think it represents a very common case. Just wanted to show you how you can make a battle cost as little as possible, because every turn you take, it brings you closer to your death.
