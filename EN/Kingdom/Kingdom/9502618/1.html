If the trade is really 0 then I think it makes no difference. But I have found that at higher trade I get better gains by working on 1 village at a time, slapping all troops in each, taking a few turns, then leaving some troops there to hold it, moving on to the next, etc.. For example at starting point 100 trade and with the amount of troops you have available, it's better 1 at a time than distributing. That's what I noticed when I was able to dump about 2k.

Anyway just study the graph in the calculator, whatever that says is pretty much the truth.

choice 1: distribute 100 troops in each of 10 villages, take 50 turns.. and you get gains of all villages combined.
choice 2: work on 1 village at a time with all 1000 troops, take 5 turns in each, until you're done with all. Just don't forget in the choice 2 when you're done with a village, place some troops in it before you move on in order to hold trade.

If you put all that on the graph and it says it's the same, then it's the same. But like I said for 0 trade starting point I think it makes no difference but for much higher starting points I've noticed choice 2 works better.
