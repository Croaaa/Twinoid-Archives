This is no cheating at all, only because he wiped the floor with you and taught you a lesson.


As a Viscount with level 3 constructions, you have 42 population in the end. So he likely had a distribution like this:


- 12 peasants for enough food for 42 citizens
- 6 lumberjacks for 52 wood each turn -> 3.2 alignments for 1 balista
- 4 workers to assemble a balista in one single turn
- 6 traders
- 14 recruiters


A single alignment of swords builds 15 soldiers. This guy probably only aligned sword symbols 6 times to create 90 soldiers. Nothing easier than that. In the production grid, you pile up the swords in the lower part of the grid with a bottom up strategy. And then towards the end when the grid is full of swords, you just do 6 consecutive turns to generate 90 soldiers in a row. it looks like this: http://up.picr.de/30936424xb.png


the costs are minimal this way. as you can store 20 units in your barracks for free, the costs for 6 turns and 90 soldiers are 0+10+ 25+40+55+70, so only 200 gold taken from your account to create the 90 soldiers. add a little bit of gold for the balistas, but during the many turns to prepare the swords, the <20 balistas does not cost any gold. it is easily affordable. so with a good grid performance and keeping gold above a certain threshold, it can easily be done and financed.


if a single alignment of swords creates 15 soldiers, it kills 4 catapults. and you only need one turn and one alignment of swords for that, whereas you need a minimum of 4 turns to build the equivalent of 4 catapults.


it is even easier with a fight at your capital. with the right strategies to delay battles (wall level 3 for 4 simultaneous battles, transforming nearly dead soldiers to pikemen which restores their full health, and of course using mounted archers in 1:1 fights which delay battle time by nearly 2 hours while you get 4 new turns in the meantime), you knock down nearly every attacking force as long as you are able to protect your trade income. it doesnt help there at all if you are an emperor. the only chance you have is to take the territories surrounding his capital to disconnect him from his income, so that he gets no revenue from his territories, but still has the costs of his units as a negative factor. this is what will break his neck, because then gold level is sinking faster than gold income from his recruiters, and as gold level is less than 1000, he gets no swords in the grid anymore and cannot keep up producing enough units. but this is what you have not done.


i know someone who took down an attack force of 330 catapults with this strategy just while being viscount! it is no cheat at all. if you cannot access his territories with income because they are located behind his capital, or you cannot take them without crushing your own economy, you´re done.
