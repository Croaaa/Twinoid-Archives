Here is mine. Took that screenshot about 50 turns before I died.

After my entire campaign I can say 30 peasants would have been enough, I didn't need 35, but at the time I didn't know 100 citizens was the max so I was left with 35% of my total population being peasants.

I also found that 20 lumberjacks was a very good number because I was able to create 4 metal with 1 turn of wood (combined with level 5 cauldron of course), which turned out to be more profitable than a mine itself. I don't need to speak about building war machines or make buildings, these were piece of cake.

10 workers were ok but I could have made a couple more at the expense of peasants. Still they weren't bad, combined with level 5 workshop.

25 traders was a good number because I was recruiting real fast, my grid was packed with swords most of the time.

And my 10 recruiters were a good match with the traders, but I could have made a couple more at the expense of peasants. My traders would be able to hold a little more aggressive recruiting.

So like the picture shows I had 35 - 20 - 10 - 25 - 10
I think the ideal would've been 30 - 20 - 13 - 25 - 12

That was a very general guideline, because the grid is not the same for every player, like I said I was packed full of swords most of the time. Partly because my gold was usually above 7k, that tends to affect appearance of swords. Different needs will require different management, I think overall I'm happy with mine, just a couple things I'd have done differently now that I know there is a max in population.

Hope this helps
