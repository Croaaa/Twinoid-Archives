byzanthia
That means the trade was higher than the # of soldiers. You should always leave enough soldiers behind to support the trade.

E.g. you took a new town, with 10 soldiers in your army. Two days later trade was at 12 (the max would be 19, if you waited a little longer). You then want to move those soldiers somewhere else. You would have to leave behind at least 7 to sustain the trade (the max for 8 soldiers would be 13). If you leave less, the trade will drop to the max for that # of soldiers, and you will see that red arrow.

On september 17, 2011
Scorpian
The peak trade is equal one less than double the number of soldiers in that village (2x[troops]-1). If you have 7 soldiers in a village, the trade will max out at 13.

However, trade increases slower as it approaches that maximum number. If you have a trade of 6 in a village with 7 soldiers, it will take 10 turns for it to increase to 7. It will take a further 90 turns (after the initial 10) to get it up to 13 (the maximum). This is why it's recommended that you leave as many soldiers as possible in a village after capturing it. If your target trade is 12, it will take 82 turns to get there with just 7 troops. However, if you left 20 troops there, it would only take 29.
