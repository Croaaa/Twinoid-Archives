Launcelot
No usually battle duration changes as they carry on. Also when mounted archers are involved and there are lots of them, the battles tend to last even longer because 1 there is no bonus against them so their injury carries on really slow and 2 they have a lot of health. Once all of them die, you may notice duration to drop somewhat but that will depend on what other units are still involved. Fastest battles are those which involve mostly catapults and ballistas because their bonus is huge and they will either injure other units fast, or get injured fast, so one way or the other the battles will last much less.

To give you an idea, even a battle of initial duration 20 hours, may turn out to last only 14 or even less. Imagine what 4 days can turn out to be. Not saying it will drop that much, depends on what units are involved and how the battle carries on. You cannot predict duration, it is what it is and what it will be. But yes, initial duration is not always reliable.

By the way, something you may find interesting. If the other player dies first before the battle ends, you still earn reputation which will be affected by the number of kills you make. So don't be surprised if you earn 40 points without having finished the battle. You also get back all your injured troops, just like if you had won.

Longest battle I have seen, 5 days. Good luck.

Definitely don't underestimate lumberjacks and not only for making Ballistas or buildings which require a lot of wood. They can be of tremendous help in making metal, allow me to explain with numbers I bet you'll find it very interesting.

Lumberjacks + Cabin + Cauldron: Your hidden ace

Suppose you have: 20 lumberjacks, lvl5 cabin, lvl5 cauldron. That means 200 wood which you throw into your kick ass cauldron and get 4 metal (50 wood = 1 metal). And that is with ONE single production of wood in ONE turn.

Let's make another assumption. Suppose you have 48 turns available, which is what you get per day (24 hours). How many wood turns will you be able to take you think? 10? At least.. right? 10 x 200 = 2000 / 50 = 40 metal. That's right, that's 40 metal per day. Remind me just how much metal do you get out of a mine per day? 48? You're making awfully close to as much metal as a mine makes, and that is with ONLY 10 wood turns out of 48, which means you can in fact easily beat a mine in metal production because realistically you WILL take more than 10 wood turns! And the best part, you never run out of it, constant flow of metal, forever.. constant training of Knights and Pikemen, forever..

Now I'm not saying it's easy to make lvl5 cauldron or lvl5 cabin, 20 lumberjacks are much easier. And to be able to make lvl5 buildings is not easy either cause you need to be a Prince, fair enough. I'm just showing you the potentials. For lower levels do your math and figure it out, just don't underestimate lumberjacks, they can do miracles with a few buildings combined, so don't miss your chance while you still have it.
