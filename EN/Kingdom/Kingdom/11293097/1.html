Peasants dying first in famines is a right pain in the behind. But thats just sod's law, the game doesnt seem to do it on purpose.


And yes, often a famine is a runaway, because the number of food icons on your grid is dictated by things like your food storage level. If you overgrow too rapidly due to some fortunate gridwork, maybe you get a lot of citizen icons on when you can really only support 1 more and you line em all up and assign em the wrong jobs... Well then, its going to crash hard.


The best advice for these situations is to examine the grid very carefully, look for the moves that seem like a boring 3-tile link of something like wood, but will cause chains due to dropping tiles, remember that hammers when not building anything (after construction site is up) will also generate food. Dont just spin the highlighted tile everytime, thats often a poor move (its a possible move, not a recommended one)

If all else fails i like to try and jam my grid in hopes of a respawn.
