Alright, those of us who had our capitals taken over should be very familiar with what happens: you lose all conquered villages and any troops stationed at them. According to the Help menu, if you attack one of these locations, your troops will recognize you and ask to rejoin you. That's a tad bit misleading though. If you attack, it adds an option to recruit "x" allies, where "x" is the number troops still remaining at the village. However, there is a bug preventing this option from working.

I decided to look into this a bit to see if it was something I could work around (I'm a programmer, after all). After about fifteen seconds of research, I found exactly what the problem is. It's this line (line #192 on Firefox Nightly):
if( confirm('The barbaric troops fighting for you have recognised your general's banner. '+n+' units would like to join your ranks. Do you accept?') )
See the problem? Yeah, that's breaking the script when it runs and preventing the button click from doing anything. However, this is very easy to work around. Here's what to do:

1) Make sure it's actually a village you used to own. You'll save yourself the embarrassment later by just checking now. :P

2) Attack the village and view the battle. You'll see an option to recruit some allies. If it works, ignore this thread. It has no meaning for you. If nothing happens, you're getting nailed by the bug. Read on.

3) Pop open the page source (varies per browser, but is usually accessible with ctrl+u) and do a find (ctrl+f) for onclick="allies (including the quotation mark). You should see a line that looks like this:
<div onclick="allies(XXXXXX,n)">
The string of x's is going to be a number, e.g. 147232 (it may or may not be six characters, but that's what it was for me), and the n is the number of allies at that site. Go ahead and ignore the n. It's the id (string of x's) that you want. Make a note of it. You'll need it in the next step.

4) In the page source, look five lines above the line with the allies() function. You'll see a line that looks like this:
document.location = '/battle/YYYYY/allies?g=ZZZZZZZ;id='+id;
This is the part that can get a bit confusing. Both of those strings of y's and z's will be different numbers, and will vary depending on the user and battle. Copy everything within the single quotes (i.e. /battle/XXXXX/allies?g=XXXXXX;id=). Now, look at the URL in the main window (the one with the ongoing battle). You'll see that the last part of the URL matches the beginning of this string.

WARNING: Be sure you copied the line with "allies" in it. There are scripts very close by for the other options in battle (e.g. flee and refresh). If you accidentally copy those lines, you'll end up performing an action you might not wanted to have done...

5) Delete everything, even the forward-slash, after after "muxxu.com" and paste that string. DO NOT HIT ENTER YET! Go back to the page source, and copy the ID represented by the string of x's from above, (i.e. the one within the allies() function call in step 3). Paste that at the end of the URL. It should now look something like this:
http://kingdom.en.muxxu.com/battle/YYYYY /allies?g=ZZZZZZZ;id=XXXXXX
(there shouldn't be a space in there. It looks like it's being automatically entered in there when I post it.)

6) Go! Just hit enter and submit that link. It should then appear to reload the battle window. The difference now will be that you won the battle, and all of the troops from the village are now with your general.

NOTE: Remember that this feature automatically adds the troops to your general's forces. The village will be undefended. You have to manually assign troops after taking your general off of defense.

This is basically just applying duct tape to a very simple problem. I was actually a little disappointed in the fact that it's such a simple problem. That should be easier to catch... It does work though. I've done it three times so far, and haven't had any problems yet. It's a bit of a pain, and does require some thought, but it should be easy enough to do.

I'm going to try contacting motion-twin about this problem. It should be ridiculously easy to fix. Keep your fingers crossed. With luck, this work-around won't be necessary for long.
