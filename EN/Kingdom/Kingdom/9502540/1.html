So I remember long time ago someone brought this up, it's not my idea, but I thought it over and I came up with a suggestion on how I think this could work very nicely. Any other ideas, please

Training building: hospital
Building requirements: gold, cotton and metal (for the equipment? lol)
Training requirements: cotton and metal (you know.. bandages, stitches, nails etc.)

Medical units have a very small health, say like 1/3 of that of a soldier and with no bonus. They are used in battles not to fight but to stay back in the group and heal injured units, except machines (catapults and ballistas). However as in any real war, casualties do exist so a medical unit could also be dragged into a duel. If this happens, since his health is only 1/3 of a soldier's he stands no chance (unless the other unit is already almost dead).

So you can train a soldier to become a medical unit and you can have as many as you can. You can also bring to a battle as many as you want. Each medical unit you bring to a battle can heal by 1% the health of all injured units that return from their duels. If you have 2 medical units in the battle, then injured units that return from their duels are healed by 2%, with 3 medical units 3%, with 5 of them 5% and so on.

However the more medical units you bring in a battle, the better the chance they are engaged in a duel and you don't want this. So there you have your restriction; you can't have too many medical units in a rather small army in a given battle, because you'd waste them. So for example if you bring 20 of those in a battle where your army is 40 units, that won't be too smart because you realise there is very good chance that they'll get dragged into a duel.

For their training requirements, we could make it very costly to train, say like 30 cotton 30 metal for level 1, then -5 for each next level, so one more reason to carefully choose how many you bring in a battle. Medical units cannot heal themselves, or others of their kind.

Thinking it through, I believe the "healing percentage based on number of medics" would be a bit overpowered. I think it'll result in armies where every wounded unit is fully healed, making it practically unbreakable unless chance starts putting medics into the fight. So I'd like to suggest a slight change to the proposed medic mechanics in a battle.

Instead of healing percentages depending on how many medics you bring, how about a seperate medic "duel" system? For example: after the first few duels are completed and the wounded units return to the army, your medics then "duel" with the wounded, one medic per wounded, starting with the most wounded. The wounded would then be healed at the same rate a soldier would cause damage. When the next wave of wounded comes in, the medics may move on to them even if the unit they're currently treating isn't at full health because they give priority to the most wounded.

This way, bringing many medics to a battle would be beneficial as more medics = more wounded units healed at once. But you'd have the same disadvantage before as more medics = better chance of a medic being drawn into a combat duel and they might also sit inactive if there aren't any wounded to heal. The balancer is it would also be just as possible that an army could end up with more wounded than the medics are able to heal and they could become overwhelmed.

Another suggestion might be that regardless of treatment, a soldier won't be able to be healed back up to full health. The logic behind it is if a knight survives a fight but, say, takes an arrow to the knee, proper treatment might get that knight back into the battle but he won't be at full capacity until the wound actually heals. This could be simulated by putting a cap of (again, pulling numbers out of the air) only 75% of damage inflicted on a unit can be healed.

Just a few ideas I had. Feel free to discuss.
