When you get to select which map you want to join, you are presented with some map options. On that screen you can also see how many slots are available. As long as the map continues to appear as a choice, then it will continue to take new players. The map ends when it stops appearing, which is what happens either after a certain amount of total joins (counting current and dead players) or after a period of time. What happens is MT withdraws that map from the options screen and nobody sees it anymore, so naturally players already in there will start to die one after the other, without anyone being able to join and eventually the map will be left without any players. Have a look at the pantheon of kings, just give it a try and click on a few maps in there, very possible you'll find quite enough of them that are completely empty.

I don't know whether this happens so much now. Part of the reason it happened in the past was to do with a bug. Inactive players weren't removed from the game as they are now, so the maps filled up more quickly and more maps had to be generated to accomodate everyone. When the bug was fixed, many of the maps died off, but I imagine a small number of those original maps are still in use. Maybe the others could get used again if there is a large enough increase in the number of people playing Kingdom. Conversely, if the number of players drops significantly, some of the current maps may fall out of use to keep the number of players per map high.
