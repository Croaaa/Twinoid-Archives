1) Don't conquer any more territory just yet as it will only make the problem worse.

2) Don't make any more moves on the production grid, unless they are free turns, until you've done step 3.

3) Increase the number of troops you have stationed in the villages directly adjacent to your capital. The increased trade here will offset what you are losing further afield.

For more info: http://muxxu.com/forum/thread/658796

Long answer:

If they're in your capital, and within the capacity of your barracks, you don't need to pay them each turn. But they won't be helping to improve trade.

If you leave them in the locations around your capital, they will be improving trade but the downside is they'll want to be paid each turn.

Depending on your barracks level, you should only keep the number of soldiers there that can be kept without paying (check your Kingdom tab to see how many are being paid). Move all of the rest to your other villages (not towns of other players that you may have mastered). After all of the soldiers are in place, try to get as much money as you can from your grid. It's also good to get more people and make them into traders. This increases the amount of money you get from lining up 3 in a row. If you happen to make more soldiers, move them out to those cities before taking more turns.

Chances are that you will have some soldiers desert you. If this happens, don't take any more turns until you make any necessary readjustments to the remaining soldier locations.

Yes, you can pass through your own wall.
You can't destroy someone else's wall.

If someone else has a wall and you want to go across their land, you have two options:
A) Ask for permission to go through
or
B) Conquer each village on your way through

A "Wall" is just another building like a farm or barracks. You cant see it on the map or destroy. The only thing a wall does is prevent people from trespassing on your land and forces them to fight instead of walking straight to your capital. (It also makes battles longer at higher levels)
