Launcelot
Commerce = Trade, Garrison = Troops

For trade, you type the current trade of that village. For troops, you type the current number of troops in it. Hit Valider (validate).

You use this calculator to have an idea of how your trade is going to change, depending on how many troops you leave in a certain village. The more troops, the faster trade is goes up, and the more trade goes up, the less your cost will be to pay your troops. What the calculator does not include, is distance of that village from your capital, which is another factor apart from quantity of troops in the village. The closer the village to your capital, the faster trade goes up. However you can still have a good idea of trade prediction.

Rule of thumb:

Conquer a village. Leave troops in it. The closer the village, the faster trade goes up. The more troops you leave in it, the faster trade goes up. Then use the calculator to have an idea of how soon that will happen. And by the word "soon" that is how many turns in the production grid that will take.

And, high trade means low cost to pay your troops, which in turn means you can recruit more troops, then conquer more villages, etc.

Launcelot
Best thing to begin with, is adding as many troops as you can in those villages that are closest to your capital. This will make your trade increase faster, and while that happens you will be paying LESS money to your troops, that means you will be able to recruit even MORE troops and use them for what else... conquer MORE villages.

Then you can add troops to these new villages, etc etc.. this is how you build trade and army, without going bankrupt (without losing all your money).

Many times it is a very wise decision to transfer troops from one village to another, to help increase trade in the new villages. So when you are done building trade in your closest villages (those villages that I mentioned in the beginning of my post), then you can take SOME of the troops from them, put them together with few more troops that you will recruit fresh and add them to the villages that you will conquer next.

Repeat this process everytime and more or less that is all you have to do to maintain a strong economy. And remember that EVERYTHING begins and ends with economy. Strong economy = Power. You'd rather have a small land with a strong economy rather than a huge land but with a sick economy. The small land has potential to get bigger thanks to the economy (trade). The huge land without trade is doomed to lose everything. So, take your time to expand, don't rush unless you have to (i.e. if you must get to resources first before your rivals.. and even then, gotta be careful with trade).

That is all.

Launcelot
Best I give you an example.

We have a village 3 squares away, so it's in -9. Next village is -16. If we have a strong trade and enough gold, we could take both villages simultaneously, so we will have -25 from both villages, we leave troops in both and wait until their trade rises. Obviously, the -9 will increase faster because it's closer (supposedly we leave the same amount of troops).

The other option is what I mentioned before. Suppose we don't have such a strong trade or so much gold, we can take our time and do it one village at a time. So we take the village -9, we leave 50 troops and start to take turns.

With 50 troops, in about 20 turns the -9 will become +10. We are already earning trade now and our total cost has dropped already by 10 gold than before we took that village. Time now to take the next village...

Next village is in -16. We take 40 troops from the previous village, which has 50. So previous village is left with 10 troops now. 10 troops and +10 trade will still generate trade, slower than before yes, but it will. In 20 turns from now, the +10 will become +11, another 10 turns, +12, another 10, +13 etc..

So 40 troops we took from the previous village, plus another 10 troops we will recruit fresh in the capital, we have again 50 troops to work with. We leave these 50 troops in the new village with -16...

Because the previous village keeps rising and is already above +10, our newly recruited 10 troops will cost nothing for us. In other words, the previous village will finance our next conquer...

So in the next village we have -16 with 50 troops. In about 40 turns, the -16 will become +10. Add the +13 from previous village, we get +23 trade from both. Now we can recruit 13 more troops for free...

In the meantime we keep aligning gold in our production grid, and our gold increases while we still recruit troops and conquer more villages, for free. We do that because we use our trade to finance our conquers, not our gold.

This is how I do it and right now my gold is 9999 and stays there all the time, I don't even need many traders. I use many lumberjacks and workers to construct a ton of machinery whenever I want to and a shitload of peasants to never worry about famine. Don't need many traders cause trade works for me.
