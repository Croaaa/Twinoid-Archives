Number of traders depends of trade and army size, but the more recruiters a player has, more traders might be needed to support the army, but not necessary equal to peasants...

I will give you some good numbers that i use:

lvl 0 10 population
Should be 5 peasants 2 jack 1 worker 2 trader 0 recruiter

Lvl 1 16 pop
8 - 3 - 3 - 2 - 0

lvl 2, 3 prestige required, 25 pop
10 - 5 - 6 - 4 - 1
Usually you can get up to 27 quickly as long you keep food maxed, actually i even have 29 population on lvl 2 thanks to being generous on peasant and my population figures, are as follows: 11 - 4 - 5 - 7 - 2
By having 2 recruiters my army is growing to quickly and to support i had to put more traders and less lumberjacks, i also could get by with 1 less workers because with workshop lvl 2 4 workers can do 6 construction, enough to get by and build some occasional machines...
One piece of advice, when you start growing your population after a granary upgrade the first 2-3 villagers should be assigned as peasants, this way you ensure a quick population growth and the possibility to go over the theoretic limit of 25...

lvl 3, 10 prestige required, 38-40 pop
13 - 7 - 7 - 10 - 3
With lvl 3 workshop you will only require 7 workers to do 12 construction, you can do ballistas in 1 turn or cats in 2, you also wont take ages upgrading buildings...
The number of traders and recruiters can vary, for example less lumberjacks and workers will allow you to put more traders and recruiters, but this way you will rely more on simple soldiers and less on machines...
Getting 12-13 peasants should be first in the line, that way, population will grow very fast...

lvl 4, 20 prestige, 60+ pop
20 - 12 - 13 - 11 - 4
Again, a workshop lvl 4 might be required with 13 workers to make catapults in a single turn, or 24 construction, but given the hight cost i usually sacrifice an extra worker and dont build it at all...
With this numbers you can make war, you can make ballistas and catapults at ease, but you can recruit lots of soldiers, but you must be careful with you trade because you dont have a lot of traders...
There is a different strategy you can use and is equally effective: Using the lumberjack and worker numbers of lvl 3 you can have more traders and recruiters and make some occasional warmachines, but remember warmachines are feed on soldiers and you enemy knows it well because 1 catapults\ballista is worth 4 soldiers and the cost is 1/4...

Lvl 5, prince 50 prestige, max 100 population
Haven't got there yet, even though in 3 games i had lvl 5 granary and farm, you need to last long enough to get to these numbers i would say 60-70 years and it will probably not be enough, unless you are an ace players and built lvl 5 at 40 yo...

Hope this guide serves as reference for everyone and its very flexible depending you strategy, just to go cheap on peasants, the more the better...

The only "rule" is about peasants, the rest will depend on your map and needs. Peasants should always start at 50% of your total population as it's already been said, and in my opinion you should maintain that percentage up to level 2 farm and granary. When you get these buildings to level 3, you can drop percentage to 40%, and 30% at level 4. If you ever get to level 5 you can experiment and see how much lower you can go without losing food.
_
As I said the rest will depend on each case. Like it's been said, if you pay taxes you may have to increase traders a bit more, which will have to sacrifice other important jobs but you may have to do it. You may also need to recruit with caution to keep your costs down, so any more recruiters may be a waste. Generally, if you require to deactivate recruitment often, something is wrong you've probably made more recruiters than you need, or less traders than you need. Do that couple times it's fine, sometimes the grid can rain swords when you least expect it, you can't help that. But do it often something's wrong. Also, are you in big need of workers? Got stuff to build? Are you aggressive and want a catapult per turn or something? Then make more workers. Otherwise, maybe make more lumberjacks to be able to make ballistas and never run out of wood.
_

I never go over 20 farmers.

After a lot of trial an error I think 20/15/12/32/21 is a good balance.

On my way up as i gain villagers I try and keep my food and traders equal. I'm guessing here but this is what I did this time

Baron 5/3/3/5/0
Viscount 10/7/7/10/5
Marquis 13/10/10/15/9
Prince 18/14/12/22/12
Currently 20/15/12/32/21

Until you hit prince its a balancing game. Early on its easy to run out of food. You do that one too many times and youve hamstrung your gains for several years. Around Viscount/Marquis Money becomes more important as you are now grabbing areas 4-5 spaces away and incurring quite a trade deficit.

After Marquis its just a balancing act of having enough funds available so you can grab territory further out, drop tons of troops on it, get the taxes from say -49 to 50, grab all but 30 or so troops and move to the next one.
