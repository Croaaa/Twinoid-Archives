Erem
Next time, be sure you select "deny right of passage" in the diplomacy screen. I think it's the default even.
The default is that the follower does have right of passage:A master automatically has right of passage and the authority to collect resources in his follower's kingdom. In return, followers can move freely throughout their master's kingdom (see the Diplomacy section).
You can deny your followers access to your rare resources but, as far as I'm aware, there is nothing you can do to prevent them travelling at will through your kingdom. That's just a perk of being a follower. Denying right of passage to a follower using the diplomacy options does not appear to have any effect. When you consider that your follower's capital is part of your kingdom, any movement they make from there is, of necessity, through your kingdom. If you were able to deny them right of passage, they wouldn't be able to move their generals at all.

On the other hand, a follower is unable to deny his master right of passage or access to resources, regardless of whether the diplomacy settings say otherwise. Right of passage is necessary between the master's capital and the follower's in order for the master to collect taxes. If the follower were to have control over this, they would be able to withold payment.

(I think what I've just said is correct, based on playing both as a master and as a follower under both good and bad masters, but I could be mistaken. Three games in and the various diplomacy options combined with Master/Follower/Friend relationships still leave me somewhat confused about exactly who can do what.)
