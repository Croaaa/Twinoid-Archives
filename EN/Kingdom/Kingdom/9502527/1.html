Launcelot
A very good tip to avoid famine is make sure for the first 2 levels your peasants are always at least half of your total population, for example 20 citizens in total, peasants at least 10. The next should be traders and then the rest of citizens, in our example say like 5 traders, 2 lumberjacks, 2 workers and 1 recruiter.

It's very unlikely to suffer from famine that way. From level 3 and above, you don't have to preserve the 50% ratio because the farm starts to play a significant role in food production. To give you an example right now I'm at level 4 with 88 citizens and my peasants aren't 44, but 35 instead and actually I could do the job even with 30. Of course having enough peasants gives you another advantage; since you never have to worry about food which is always full in your stocks, you don't have to use turns to search for it so you can just concentrate on finding other stuff while food just takes care of itself. You save turns I mean. Not to mention you can start building constructions which require food whenever you please, never have to worry about it.

Here's an example of the benefit of having enough peasants. Suppose you're at level 4 with 3500 max food. It's a whole lot different to have a food production of 300 or more, than 150. Because when your citizens consume 50 per turn, that means with 300 food production and in order to have a good balance between consume and production, you can take up to 6 turns without having to search for food at all, meaning you can use these turns to find other things, hammers, gold, swords, wood... wouldn't you save turns this way? Instead of having only 150 food production, which would require you to align food every 3 turns, leaving you only the rest 2 turns to find other stuff. Gold is important too, of course, but for that you also have trade to work for you, but food only comes from the grid which makes it really important to make it your first priority.

So from then on, you're set until the rest of your game. But the 50% ratio of peasants vs citizens in the beginning is very important and I would say you're pretty damn unlucky if you still have famine. Or I dunno, unless you avoid aligning food for some odd reason.
