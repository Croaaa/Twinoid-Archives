Launcelot
You will lose the villages that your follower owns and any access to resources -if any- located in those villages and you will lose prestige along with it. The only thing from your dead follower that you preserve is his capital, which turns into barbaric with no inhabitant.

Furthermore, in case there are villages of your follower as the only connection between 2 parts of your kingdom (the part with your capital and the other), since your follower dies, these villages will turn into savage and you will lose connection to the rest of your kingdom. That means you will lose trade as well. And that is why it's very important that you manage to keep your own land solid, no follower's land getting in between because that land is yours only as long as he's alive. Otherwise if your land isn't solid, when he dies you're gonna have to capture all these savage lands, and fast.
