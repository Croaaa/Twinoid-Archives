Since MagicDragon brought up my good Emperor game, I went back and checked my pantheon. Age is by far the best predictor of how well I do. I've finished 12 games so far:


Title and age at death:
Emperor - 74, 66, 61
Prince - 48, 48, 49
Duke - 52, 45, 39, 37
Marquis - 50
Viscount - 38


So I'd have to agree that a long life is by far the best predictor in how far I will go and that is why I still think health is the most broken aspect of the game. (The Marquis game was one where I went to bad health at 32 and just stopped trying thinking I would die soon and just kept on living)


As for how long did kings live, the longest I could find was Wilhelm IV of Henneberg-Schleusingen (Holy Roman Empire). He was born in 1477, pretty much the end of the medieval era, but lived to 82. Wile not exactly a king, Richard Cromwell lived to 85 in the same Era and That is a rarity, but finding kings who lived to their early 70s is not hard (including Charlemagne).


If anybody is reading this that actually codes this game, there are three things broken. First the health levels drop too unpredictably, second finally bad health just does not mean you will die soon - 20 - 30 years is not uncommon, and third lifespans are too long - a good player who reaches his mid-60s should almost always make Emperor.


My thought was you start the game at 25. Stay in excellent health for 15 years, 10 in good, 5 in poor. That gives an age of 55 which is a few more than the average middle-ages lifespan of a king of England or Scottland (of course that includes a lot of battle death and executions). Toss in a 25% random factor which makes natural lifespan 47.5 to 62.5. Then allow a 33% adjustment to loose health for things like low resources, famine, bankruptcy, capitol under attack, successful revolts (stress) and add for high resources, wasting resources, loyal followers, troops in the capitol (luxury & security). That would give a theoretical lifespan of 40 to 75, though the top 5 years or so would be next to impossible to reach. That seems about right. Only one English king from 770 to 1700 lived past 75, but a lot lived to their 60s in peaceful times.


That seems realistic, keeps most lifespans short enough that Emperor is a real achievement and is not just a reward for a long life, and lets players know that when they hit bad health it is time to start giving up on expanding and start taking care of their followers.
