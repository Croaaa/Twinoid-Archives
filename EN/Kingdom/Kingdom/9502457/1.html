The first thing to check is the difficulty icon to the left of the Join button for each of your choices. Easy difficulty maps are represented by a soldier, normal difficulty ones have an archer icon instead.

Make use of the Rankings and Map links too. The rankings will allow you to the opponents you would be facing and whether there is one dominant player on the map or several smaller kingdoms jostling for power. You may also recognise some of the players on the list from previous games of Kingdom you've played, and decide that you'd like to play alongside them again - or not, as the case may be. The map, if you switch to the strategic view, will give you an idea of where the powerful kingdoms are and also where the rare resources are located. When you choose a map to join, you will be allocated one of the unoccupied capitals, so if you can find these on the map, look to see what's around them and whether or not they are already part of another player's kingdom and how many, if any, savage villages are nearby - but bear in mind that you have no control over which of the unoccupied capitals will be yours.

In truth, apart from selecting a map with the appropriate difficulty icon, it's really just a case of hitting one of the Join buttons and hoping for the best. There's simply too much you don't know about the maps to make a fully informed choice, such as which village will be yours and how friendly or aggressive the players around you will be.
