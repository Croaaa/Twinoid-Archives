Launcelot

It's not about getting more difficulties, I mean that alone won't change anything. Actually I think it will make it even worse, because maps are already so sparsely populated as they are. More difficulties would mean all players that have qualified for the next level, would be put on a separate list just like what happens with players that have qualified for normal difficulty and aren't allowed back to beginner. So who would be left to play normal? Take a look at beginner maps, yep.. that many. On the other hand, players leaving normal to join hard wouldn't be happy either, because their new maps would be so sparsely populated they'd look like beginners. Of course the overall skill level on the map would be higher and that could potentially make it a little more difficult, but that alone wouldn't be enough to make it "hard".


There are just 2 ways you could really make the game more challenging. Either remove a few normal difficulty maps from the list, so that the remaining maps get a little crowded (50 or 60 taken spots out of 80 would be ideal, remember we are now at 30 or less) and there is no need to bring more difficulty levels, or somehow bring more players on the english servers. With more players, normal maps would be back to where they were 3 years ago (no more than 3 to 5 free spots left per map) and that would open the way to more difficulties, which would decongest normal maps and bring them back to 50-60/80 like I described on the first option, and at the same time offer those currently in normal difficulty a place to a higher skill level. But see, you can't do that without more players, otherwise hard maps will be so sparsely populated they'll be a joke. Take a look at the french server and you'll see why they have more difficulties.


I say for now remove a few normal difficulty maps, so the remaining maps are brought up to 50 or 60 out of 80 spots. In my opinion that would be ideal, the best compromise between challenge and playability. And if or when we do get more players, we can talk about more difficulties.
