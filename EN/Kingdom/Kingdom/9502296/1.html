Owyndaw
Your question is too hard to answer. It depends on what you are trying to do, which we cannot tell from here. You might not want to post that here as it will give it away to your competitors.

You need to make sure you have enough food. You can get more with another Peasant, but also with a Farm. A shortage of Food will cause a loss of a citizen (random, so it might be one you don't want to lose); it is also bad for your health.

You probably want more soldiers, so you might want a Recruiter. Put more soldiers on nearby villages and get more trade to earn enough to pay for the soldiers. Put soldiers in your Followers Capitals to stop them revolting. Have soldiers in your Capital to help defend your Kingdom. If you cannot pay for them, you need coins, so you need a Trader, but perhaps a Market instead; a coins shortage will cause soldiers to desert.

You might also want to build up your Capital. Choose the buildings that help you the most; they need Food, Coins or Wood. You might want Lumberjacks, Peasants or Traders to boost those resources, or Workers to build them faster.

All of these choices might be affected in the short term by what you have in your grid.

So, it is your choice. If you can plan to get another citizen, you can correct the balance then if conditions or your plans change.

Also note: more food in your store gives more people in the grid, more coins in your store means more swords.
