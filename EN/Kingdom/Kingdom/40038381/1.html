NERON

It's true that the food supply and economy have a sightly greater margin later in the game, it's not anywhere to the point where starvation is commonplace. I never had people starve at any point in of my games, ever.


Whether it's early or late game, if your civs are starving, there is something seriously wrong with your civ builds. At the level 1 farm, approximately half of all your civilians should be farmers, this ratio decreases as the farm and granary is leveled up. Don't be afraid of recruiting farmers to the point of constantly hitting your food cap, especially in the first two palace levels; it's a little known fact that the more food you have, the more frequently citizen tiles spawn on your grid. We want as many citizens as possible.


As for the grid, there's a certain degree of luck as you have no control over what tiles spawn [well, not control it in a way that can help you in this particular situation, anyhow] But, you can control the number of tiles spawning per turn... think of it this way : once a grain tile spawns on your grid, sooner or later, you're going to reap it. Why not ignore it and go for big combos so more tiles are cleared and spawned. The faster you can cycle through your tiles, the more of each type you get per turn, including grain. Admittedly, this tactic isn't going to help you now but it prevents grid depletion of a particular tile type from happening in the future.


For now, I recommend you assign all farmers until you can dig yourself out of your mess. Matching free turn tiles can help, as no food or money is consumed on the turn where you match three or more of them.

MagicDragon

The thing with mounted archers is that they are only good for cancelling all enemy bonuses. That is very good for defending a territory, but little else. Additionally, it isn't advisable to use them offensively as that would make for a prolonged battle.


For instance, ballistas and catapults counter each other but don't cancel each other out. What this means is that they retain their bonuses when used against each other correctly. This means that if you are confident you have a large enough force of catapults, you would be better served attacking a ballista defended village with catapults instead of mounted archers. You would lose a lot more units, but that's offset by the much accelerated speed of battle.


You could catch the defender off guard if he or she isn't online when you attack. If you use mounted archers in this scenario, there is a decent possibility that the battle will be prolonged enough that the defender would be able to reinforce his or her village.


Additionally, training mounted archers require the use of rare resources in cotton and horses. You only need turns on the production grid and wood to make ballistas and mounted archers. As such, I think the best use for mounted archers is for defending loyal follower capitals, your own, and strategically important villages.
