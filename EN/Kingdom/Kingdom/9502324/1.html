On july 25, 2011
Owyndaw
From what I can tell, you have a capital and 2 villages. You have Troops at the Capital, and Troops with the General. You do NOT have any Troops at either village. You would have started with -1 trade at the near village, and -4 at the second. You need to move the Troops into the villages. Troops attached to a General do not increase your Trade at a village; in fact a General defending a village will freeze Trade at that village.

Select your General and move all Troops but 1 to the village. The Trade in that village will go up. Move your General to the Capital, and get some Troops and move the General to the first village. Move the Troops from the General to the village (all but 1) and the Trade in that village will go up.

On july 25, 2011
Owyndaw
Leave as many Troops in a village as you like. It depends on:
* How quickly you are trying to raise the Trade in a village (use the Trade calculator, see the link above),
* How many Troops the village can 'support' based on the current Trade,
* How many Troops you need to defend a village against attack,
* How many Troops you have in total.

So, in other words, when you make Troops in your Capital, it is up to you where you put them
