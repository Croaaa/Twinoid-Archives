quattro

zardoz: I never saw anyone taking a potion during excellent health and i am not convinced it makes any sense. I think it´s also counter-productive, as you cannot restore a full health level but only a part of it.


There is no proof, but from my personal experience, I assume it is true that if you take potions at the right time, it allows you more lifetime for the same amount of money spent.


Let me try to explain it with an example of a simple scale: You start a game with 1.0 (which is excellent health), at some time it degrades to 2.0 (good), at some time to 3.0 (bad), and at 4.0 you are dead. during game play, the digits after the comma keep increasing with every turn in the grid, or if citizens die of starvation and such.


If you take a potion, you get into a better state of health, but i do not think that it simply subtracts 1 on the scale. If that would be the case, if you had a 2.9 before, you would be at excellent health at 1.9, and just get the next health decrease again right after a few turns after having spent money. People would be upset and the programmers knew that.


I assume, that it not only subtracts 1, but also truncates the digits after the comma. So, given the previous example, if you are at good health at 2.9, it subtracts 1 so you end up at 1.9, and then it truncates the digits after the comma so you have 1.0 health.


To me it seems, most people make the mistake that when health turns to bad, they immediately buy a potion to make sure they do not die. So from 3.0, they go back to 2.0, and they buy an 1.0 increase for their money effectively. But if they would buy the potion already at 2.9, they could get back to 1.0, and get twice the amount of life time for the same amount of money (an increase of 1.9 instead of 1.0 for the money). The problem simply is that you never know when you are at 2.9. So best practice is not to restore health right after it went to "good", but to wait until you had "good" for a long time, and then to buy a potion to restore to "excellent" just before it decreases to "bad".


P.S.: if you see my list on top of this thread, i restored health from "bad" to "excellent" directly by buying two potions after health decrease #14, and it gave me 15 years in return before going to "good". Before, i bought potions when health went to "bad", which was not good. Above 120+ age, there´s not much you can do, they come too quickly.


P.P.S.: I have the feeling, also the time of day when you take turns may influence health degrades. From my personal experience, they seem to occur much more often between midnight and 1 a.m. CET server time.
