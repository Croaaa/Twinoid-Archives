According to twinpedia when you go bankrupt you start losing troops from your most costly villages until you lose the villages too. To my understanding this means that villages with the highest trade will be the last to lose and maybe it's not only about trade but net profit of that village. Net profit would be trade minus troops. So if a village's trade is 30 and troops in it are 24, net profit would be +6 for that village. If all the other villages have a higher net profit, then that one would be the first to lose.

Again, I am just writing how I understand it, I never went bankrupt to know.
And here it is from twinpedia with Microsoft Translator:

Bankruptcy : If you have enough gold to pay your soldiers or maintain your territories, you will be in bankruptcy! In this case, your soldiers déserteront and you will lose little by little your territories too costly.
