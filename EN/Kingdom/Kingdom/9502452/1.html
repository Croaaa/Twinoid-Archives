Too bad that other thread is locked -- it was hil-LARRY-ous! And I just wanted to add my appreciation to the OP.

regarding generals:

Keep in mind, generals are the way you move troops around. No general, no 100 troops dropping down on your enemy capital. Also, your general has the option of retreating in a battle, at the cost of whatever troops are currently in duels.

Other than that -- nope. I don't see any benefit to having generals in a battle.

It WOULD be nice -- in a perfect world, where we could make suggested changes to Kingdom, and they'd actually get implemented -- if you had a General involved, that that General would be able to assign a number of troops to fight the first duels, equal to the number of Prestige he/she had.

That way, Prestige would have some added benefit, other than minimal gold savings.

And the Generals would do more in a battle than saying, "Well, sending five Soldiers up against his Catapults didn't work. Maybe we should send in... MORE SOLDIERS!"
