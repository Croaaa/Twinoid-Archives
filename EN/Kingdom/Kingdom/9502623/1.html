MagicDragon
I would suggest getting into the habit of raising trade anyway. You don't need all your generals to do that with. One general should suffice, really. The reason I suggest working on trade is because the normal maps are a lot tougher to expand on as the class of player is much better depending on what map you end up on.

A higher trade means you can field a larger army. The larger your army is, the more you can expand as well as defend yourself from other players. I also do believe a larger army would also have a net benefit to your final score as well.

In any case, I wouldn't worry about your final score. You'll score high no matter what, really. You earned the Emperor title before you turned 50, I think. I think at what age you earn a title has an affect on what you earn as well. Just look at Melian and Omen. He had little more than half the reputation Melian did, and he his score was nearly as high as hers. I think the reason he scored so closely is because he made Emperor much earlier in terms of age.

BearBoy you lose the villages and the troops assigned to them but they are not lost from the map. They just remain in these villages as barbarians and the villages become savage. Take your general and recapture them, you will not have to fight these troops. When you go back to a village you will have the option to attack and fight them in battle, or get them back to your camp and recapture the village without a fight. You will see a message saying that they acknowledge their master (that's you) and want you to accept them back. No harm is done. Just hurry before someone else fights them and since they are still savage your travel time will be a handicap.
