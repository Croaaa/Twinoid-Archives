The appearance of people on your grid is determined almost solely by how much food you have in stock. If you don't have at least enough food to equal the square of your citizens (e.g. if you have 10 citizens, you need at least 100 food), people will appear extremely rarely. So, in essence, yes there is a limit on how many citizens you can have. Once you have enough to where the square is more than the max number of food you can store, getting more is extremely difficult. Once you level up your granary though, you'll have a chance to expand more.

It's extremely difficult if you don't plan for it in advance. For example, when I start my new games, I always ensure that I have more peasants than anything else. I find that getting up to around 4 or 5 peasants before becoming a Sir tends to be sufficient. Then, get up to 10 before becoming a Viscount. From there, focus on getting more workers and you're pretty much set for the rest of the game. I used that strategy during my last game and never once even came close to facing a famine or a bankruptcy.
