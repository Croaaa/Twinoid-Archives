When available turns start accumulating and you still don't take any, they will eventually reach 100. Once they are 100, then every 30 minutes the game will be consuming your resources (food and gold) just like if you were taking your turns but without aligning any food or gold. What would happen? You would consume without producing any. This is what happens after 100 turns have been accumulated and you still don't take any. So eventually that would lead to famine and/or bankruptcy, you would lose your troops and eventually you would die.

And here's where "wasted turns available" come to play. These turns which you can see by rolling your mouse over, will actually protect your kingdom from consuming and eventually losing these resources. So every 30 minutes, you will be wasting 1 turn, of those you have available. Only when all of these turns run out, you will be losing your resources per 30 minutes.

So if your grid is full with 100 turns and you have 12 wasted turns available, you don't take turns, this means in 6 hours they will run out. So only after 6 hours you will start to consume resources every 30 minutes. You have a 6-hour safety net so to speak. And that's about it.

Usage:

You may not want to take turns because you're too old, you don't wanna take any more potions but you wish to take a few capitals, so you can extend your life with safety by using up your wasted turns, or you may be away for a while, you just do your calculations and see how much time you have to spare.

Launcelot
First of all you can't collect any resources if the village which has them is not connected to your kingdom, unless it belongs to someone else's kingdom and they have granted you right of passage and right to harvest on their kingdom. But even then, your kingdom has to be connected to theirs. In other words there must not be any savage village in between the resource and your kingdom or your friend's kingdom.

About crossing a savage village with troops, you can't, you are going to have to fight them first. Unless they were once your troops and you lost them because you went bankrupt or your capital was just conquered. If they were your troops, you can go back there and you will be given the option to fight them or accept them back to your camp, in which case you reclaim the village too, along with the troops.
