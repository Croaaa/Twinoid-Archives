I have come to the conclusion that reputation points that your general earns after winning a battle, is not affected entirely by the number of enemy troops that his army kills/destroys but also by the time it takes his army to win the battle. Some examples from old battles:

1. Killed 57 units in 8 hours 16 minutes. Was on defence, provoked the enemy general. Took that long because I had 100 soldiers and 20 ballistas. Only 14 ballistas engaged in the battle, rest was soldiers, so that didn't help to end it quicker. Enemy was mainly soldiers too. That victory gave my general 8 points.

2. Killed 20 units in 1 hour 15 minutes. Was on attack. All catapults against all soldiers. Won 2 points.
3. Killed 11 units in 54 minutes. Attack again. Almost all catapults against all soldiers. 3 points here.
See here, more points with less kills, but quicker.

4. Killed 10 units in 45 minutes. Defence, provoked the enemy general. Full ballistas against different types. 2 points.
Again, compare this with number 2. Same points with half kills, but quicker.

5. Killed 4 units in 24 minutes. Attack. All catapults against soldiers. 1 point.
6. Killed 9 units in 44 minutes. Attack. All catapults against mainly catapults. 2 points.

If you closely compare the results you'll understand that there is a certain equation in the game which accounts both the number of kills and the time to complete the battle. I suspect the equation makes some sort of division of the number of kills by time or something and comes up with your reputation points.

The way I see it, want more rep? Finish it quickly, which almost always means gotta love machinery (cats or bals).
