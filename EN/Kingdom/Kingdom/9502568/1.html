There are actually two savage villages between your kingdom and his: Barnaton and Kiluchover. Despite their proximity, there is no direct path between his meadow and Barnaton.

One of you will need to capture Kiluchover (the savage village next to his meadow) as there are barbarian troops there who will not let you pass.

It's a while since I played this game now, but as far as I recall, savage villages have a lot to do with it.
Help > Rare Resources:Each location where you find these resources must be connected to your kingdom in order for you to collect them.
Normally, having one or more savage villages between your kingdom and the resources you wish to collect would mean that you could not collect them as there would not be a safe route back to your capital for them to take. However, in your case, your two kingdoms are directly connected further along the border, between Berkechesey and Penirough, so the resources should be able to find their way back to your respective capitals via this alternate route. I'm not certain, but I don't think that either of you would need to grant the other right of passage for that to work, as it's only the resources that would be travelling the length of your kingdoms, not your generals. However, I think one or other of you will have to grant right of passage for the following reason...

You, or the other player, are going to have to capture the village with the barbarian troops in any case. If he takes it, you would have to take two steps inside his kingdom to reach his meadow and would therefore need to have passage rights. His general would be able to leave his kingdom, passing through the remaining savage village, to enter your kingdom and collect from your mine without the need for passage rights (as your mine would be just inside your border). If, on the other hand, you were to capture Kiluchover - in which case, it would make sense for you to capture the village closer to you as well, providing your finances can cope with the negative trade, as passing through savage villages slows down your generals - he would have to pass through at least one territory of yours in order to reach the iron mine and so would need you to grant him right of passage. He would not need to grant you right of passage though as his meadow would be adjacent to your newly-expanded kingdom, meaning you could enter his kingdom, collect the resources, then turn around and go back the way you came.

Another option would be for you each to capture the savage village nearest your respective capitals. This would move your kingdoms' border further away from both resources, meaning that you would both need passage rights in order to be able to collect.

I hope that all made sense!
