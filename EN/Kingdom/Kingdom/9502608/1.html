MAs might not be beaten by anything but kinghts, but that doesn't mean they are cost effective. Keep in mind that 1 MA costs the same as 1 archer and 1 jouster combined. Those 2 units together could easily kill an MA with 60 HP to spare on 1 unit, and the other dead. Ideally, you'd only want to use MAs on Ballistas and catapults, as killing them with ease is really what MAs are designed for, against anything else they are not cost-effective, unless the battle is short and they only fight 1 duel, which is their other purpose, winning battles against weaker opponents with little or no loses. This allows time for your enemy to come online and send reinforcements, which is bad. The only exception is when the enemy has a lot of ballistae, in which case there isn't much of a choice...

Launcelot
Fighting in a village has a completely different logic than fighting at a capital. In a village, while it's always in your best interest to finish the battle soon, it's not a top priority thing. Top priority is just to win. That said, you bring with you all those units that you know, based on what units the enemy has or you think he has, that you're going to win. Basically there are no invincible units as each has its own weakness against at least 1 of the others, but truth is that by far the most powerful units are just 4:

MA's.. because they lose only to Knights (easily I might add)
Knights.. because they lose only to Archers (although barely), catapults and ballistas (easily I'm afraid)
Catapults.. because they lose only to MA's when attacking (easily too)
Ballistas.. same thing when defending

These units have the least possible weaknesses and therefore they are the toughest. All the other units, can be a very good choice if you are not able to construct a good amount of catapults or ballistas. If you are though, then answer the following question and you will see where I'm coming from:

Using a catapult to attack or a ballista to defend, are you able to replace a Pikeman, a MK, a Cavalier or an Archer? What more do any of these units offer, that your catapult does not? Nothing.. in fact the catapult does at least twice a better job. Think about it you'll see that these units are all replaceable. In other words if you can build cats/bals at a very high rate then you don't need these units.

The only units totally irreplaceable are the ones I mentioned earlier, MA, Knights, Cats/Bals.

Now let's go back to where we started. Everything I've said so far will work great when the fight takes place in a village. But what if you're going to take a capital? That is where you need A LOT of catapults. Actually if the enemy has no MA's or he has very few of them, DON'T bring anything else just bring catapults, lots of them. The reason is simple: since the fight is at their capital, they can bring reinforcements by taking turns on the grid, as long as there are swords appearing or hammers. You DON'T want the battle to take long, because the longer it takes, the more reinforcements they can bring, which will constantly increase duration and eventually the battle may never finish, or it will finish and your general will be dead. If they have a lot of MA's in their capital, then that's another story, in that case either stay away, or be prepared the battle is probably going to last forever, in which case and if you still insist on doing it, go with Knights since only they can beat MA's. He has ballistas too, bring your cats along. But be prepared it WILL take a very long time.. not good. Keep in mind, when you wanna take a capital, your top priority is time, you NEED the battle to end asap. Catapults and catapults only.. just go all out with cats, lots of them. Unless they have a lot of MA's like I said.

Hope that helps. Sorry too for the long answer.
