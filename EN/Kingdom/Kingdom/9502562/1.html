Launcelot
When you move your general outside of your kingdom, if there are savage villages on your way you need to capture them to reach where you're going. The only way to travel through a savage village without having to capture it, is if it's undefended. If there is even 1 single soldier in it, you can't travel through without capturing it first and you need to do that all the way to the village where you wanna go.

If resources is what you're after, then I should let you know that you're never going to collect if there are savage villages on the way to it, you need to capture these villages as well. If they are undefended, you may be able to pass through without capturing them but you won't be able to collect resources.

And before you start capturing 15 villages in a row, you should also be aware that each village you capture gives you a negative trade, which translates into a certain cost. You need to leave troops in them and start taking turns to raise trade. Then the more distant the village is, the hardest it's gonna get for you to raise its trade since it starts from an increasingly negative value. What will happen if you're not careful, is you'll go bankrupt and lose everything you've worked for.
