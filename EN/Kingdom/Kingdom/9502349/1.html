Scorpian
It all depends on your strategy. If you focus on getting a lot of peasants at first, you'll end up shooting yourself in the foot. You need to be careful with what job you give your citizens. Keep in mind that each level has a ceiling. You'll need to upgrade your buildings to accommodate your population. If you have 20+ citizens and are still a Sir, you'll find that keeping enough food for them is ridiculously hard.

The best bet is to expand your kingdom alongside your capital. Don't just focus on the grid forever. Start conquering other villages. Expand. As you expand, you unlock new buildings. These new buildings give you more room to work with. As you expand your capital, you'll need it.

The best thing I can recommend is to always be near the max amount of food you can store if possible. That way, if you enter a famine (lack of food appearing on the grid), you can last for a while. If you find that the max amount doesn't last very long, that's a sign that you need to expand and upgrade. If you don't, you'll bump into the ceiling. That's what's killing you here, not the mechanics of the game.
