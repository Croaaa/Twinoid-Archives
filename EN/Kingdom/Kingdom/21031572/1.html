Swords are random. No matter if you're broke or you can spit on the floor and grow coin trees.. Citizens on the other hand are entirely related to the amount of food you have in stock. While they never stop coming even if you get 1 every 10 years, their appearance becomes much more frequent if you keep food stocks above square of population, so for 10 population you want more than 10*10=100 food in stock. This is in fact your limitation as well, since you'll eventually reach a point where granary won't be able to store any more food, so making the square won't be possible, until you upgrade. That is the reason why you should try to build farm and granary as fast as you can right after a palace upgrade. Farm gets you more food and granary stores it. Following the same logic make sure you have enough peasants to produce the necessary food. Remember citizens do everything, they are your strength.

At the beginning of the game it happens everytime because you have a very low number of citizens and granary is enough, even at level 1, for 250 food. Even without a granary you can store 100. And what is the sq. root of 100.. 10 citizens. That's why you get a rush of citizens.


The reason it happens after a famine is that, you end up with less population, which causes a higher chance of you making the square of their number in food, with your current granary.


Rule of thumb.. citizens will rain, when your food is above their population square. When this isn't possible, you need an upgrade.
