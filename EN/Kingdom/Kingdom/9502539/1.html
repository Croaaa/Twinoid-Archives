Launcelot
The way to go is every time you upgrade palace you need to think:

Are your peasants enough to waste food? If yes then you're on the right track, the very first building you have to construct is granary to increase max food stock, which in turn once above the square of population will literally make the grid show 100 times more citizens. I'm not exaggerating when I say you can have 10 citizen icons simultaneously in the entire grid. More citizens means more workers more lumberjacks more of everything. So everything starts from peasants.

If your peasants aren't enough to waste food, that means they are probably not capable of increasing stock, which means a larger granary won't mean much, so there you go in need of peasants again if you want more citizens. EVERYTHING starts from peasants, even gold starts from peasants because peasants will enable you to make traders and recruit troops, who in turn will generate trade.

And one more thing, enough peasants means you save turns to do what you want because when you are able to produce with 1 turn, the food consumption of 5 turns, that means you've got another 4 turns available to concentrate on other things, instead of looking for food every turn and you can do all that while having max food, which you know what it means.. more citizens when it's time for level up.

Peasants is the key, it's not everything, but everything STARTS from them.
