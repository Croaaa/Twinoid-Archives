Launcelot

Wasted turns are your safety net in case your remaining turns go past 100. If there are no wasted turns available to you, then every 30 minutes beyond 100 turns you will start losing a bit of food and gold and that will eventually kill your citizens and your troops will desert you. That means your kingdom will collapse. But with wasted turns available, you'll be granted some time until that starts to happen. How much time? 50 wasted turns equals 25 hours (on normal speed maps like public ones), so that means you'll get 25 hours on top of 100 remaining turns and only then you'll start collapsing. 100 wasted turns equals 50 hours... etc. That's all there is to it, they don't age you and basically you do nothing on your own with them, they are used automatically to protect you from collapsing.

Launcelot

No, you will not die earlier. You don't age while they're being used. It's like time pauses, yet you can still do things like fight wars, move troops, collect resources etc., you can do all that and you won't age in the process. You age only if you start taking turns on the grid. If your going to live for 2000 turns (equals roughly 40 years game time) your going to die when you're 60 whether you use wasted turns or not. The only thing you'll gain from using these turns is when you're let's say 59, you'll be able to pause time and live for another 3 or 4 days (which would equal 3 or 4 years game time otherwise), so in a way you trick death and can do things like those I mentioned above that perhaps you wouldn't be able to do otherwise cause you'd be dead.
