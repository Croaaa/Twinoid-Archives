MagicDragon

For masters (especially those close to promotion), loss off followers means loss of prestige. Additionally, if a master has multiple followers and one successfully revolts without response, it could cause other followers to do the same, which can be a huge headache to deal with.


For instance, let's say you have a prince at 74 prestige and about to make King when a viscount who is his follower revolts. If the follower has, say 11 prestige, his former master drops back down to 63, and now can't become a king. That can be very frustrating for the master attempting a higher title.


Also, when it's functioning right, the master / follower arrangement can work quite nicely for both parties. For instance, the master benefits in the form of taxation and access to territory he or she couldn't afford to acquire otherwise. With the ability to collect taxes from followers, the master can raise an army that would otherwise be unaffordable.


Followers also receive benefits as well. They can have a larger garrison protecting their Capital thanks the their master. If the master is generous, a follower would have access to resources he or she would otherwise have to acquire on his or her own. That can be problematic when you're a sir or master. You also get the protection of your master when starting out and therefore, you're not on your own.


For instance, if someone were to spawn in Dickenbutte on Tamomere, other players would be less likely to attack whoever spawns there since they'd be my follower. Generally, players tend to avoid taking actions that could potentially draw the ire of more powerful players. For instance, when starting out in a new game, I would be much more reluctant to attack someone who's the follower of a powerful player. It simply makes little sense to get on their bad side.


For my current game, I started out as 45424's follower and that largely insulated me from attacks by other players. Had I not been his follower, I would likely have been seen as easy pickings and attacked by someone else looking for an easy victory.

Tips for getting to Emperor:


1. Skills
Trust me, you won't be Emperor just by making alliance with others. You need skills on the grid, managing citizen ratio, maintaining a not-too-negative trade, and controlling your generals and troops both at wartime or peaceful times. These can all be found in the forums and your own experience.


NO-GO: Press the revolt button as soon as you spawn in the map. You'll be doomed.


2. Diplomacy
No one wants war (except lunatics and trolls). Negotiate with your enemy if you find them stronger than you, or fighting them really hurts your trade/army (they're the same thing really). They may just be attacking you because you're in their way. Why can't you just give them passage rights? You may even form alliance with that player if you join them to accomplish what they're doing (most likely to attack the player that's on the other side of your territory). And alliance with a stronger player is always good in this game.


NO-GO: Blocking your enemy from sending message to you. That means war and destruction.


3. Activity
Activity is the key to every RTS game. You simply won't make it if you just log in, use up your turns and move some generals once per day. You need constant attention to the map, nearby players and their generals, the emperors on your map (someone may just have died and you won't want to miss out the opportunity to take some barbarian villages) and any wars going on. Offer help to one side of a war before it starts can make a good relationship.


Forum activity is also necessary. If you don't find anything to post on any thread, just say hi in your map forums. Someone may notice you that way.


NO-GO: Flaming in the forums.


4. Luck
I shouldn't put this last
This game sometimes trolls players by giving them short lives. You can't do anything with it. (What? Potions? I'm talking about free play here) Just try again and wish for better luck next time.
