It depends. Zoom was my first follower in the game I made Emperor. He did OK under me, but was stuck in a corner and had limited room to grow. After he died, he resurrected well away from me (as the kingdom you are all complaining about) stuck under a new overlord. He played the game though, being a good vassal until his master died then exploding forth afterward.

Many of my followers in that game had I'm sure pretty poor games. A few did well - the loyal ones I helped to take over territory from the unloyal and a few special ones were allowed their own followers. Several made king after I died because we set them up to be ready to follow me. Many others though were just stuck biding time until I died and often died before me anyway. Not much they could do - short of a serious alliance, Emperors are hard to stop. I was just lucky to have a long life.

Becoming Emperor can not be your goal in every game - the rules just make it unobtainable. Your goal may be to stymie someone you dislike or to help a friend to Emperor status and maybe one game out of ten the stars will align to make Emperor doable. The other nine games are about making allies that will help that 10th game soar.

Either you enjoy the social aspect of the game or it is not worth playing.

Having said that, Emperors can be taken down if they are not caring for their followers. Get an alliance together of multiple large followers and all revolt simultaneously. Anybody at Emperor status is relying on taxes of their few biggest followers to survive and they only have five generals to act with. If I had lost my 3-4 biggest followers simultaneously, especially if I had units tied up in a battle at the time, my entire empire would have collapsed.
