Launcelot

3 factors that affect wether a revolt can take place:


=> time (once per 24h)
=> forces in your capital (how the force you have in your capital compares to your master's garrison)
=> total strength (how your entire army compares to your master's)


So don't be surprised if your master has left 1 foot soldier in your capital against your 20 and you still can't revolt, 1 day later, 3 days later or a week.. Chances are he's orders of magnitude stronger than you in total. So to compensate for that, your capital defense will need to be significantly beefier than the garrison.


Sadly, no one knows the exact formula to be able to say when revolt is possible, but this is how it works more or less. Use these factors as a guidance and experiment.
