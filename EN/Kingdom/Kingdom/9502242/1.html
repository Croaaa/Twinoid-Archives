@TheCreator

In theory it all sounds nice but if you think about it, it won't work that well. In order to make your enemy go bankrupt you'd have to damage their trade real bad. By conquering their villages you will only make your trade plummet down and you will barely scratch theirs. Heck if you take over the wrong village you might even help them gain more gold than before.

Best defense is a high level wall (at least 3) + enough workers to build Ballistas in 1 or 2 turns and/or 5-10 recruiters to hire more soldiers. Taking over as many neighboring villages also helps as your enemy has to capture each and one of them to get to your capital. Depending on the situation it might be better to save your soldiers and let them take over your villages because their trade will be so bad by the time they're close to your capital that their general will simply refuse to fight. This way you don't even have to attack them and you can use the soldiers you saved to take back your villages and get the trade back where it was.

Fighting has its benefits though, it's the only way to raise your generals reputation and also a good way to get rid of your extra soldiers you don't need (when you're close to bankruptcy).
