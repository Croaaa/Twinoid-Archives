Davout
SRNM is correct. Your kingdom will be reduced to simply your capital, but if you quickly go around and attack your previously owned villages, they'll "recognize" you and return to your control.

Ballistae will have 200% effectiveness against attacking troops. They also have a longer life line (you'll see this in the battle screen), so they'll last lost longer in combat. BTW: I just attacked someone with 18 troops, mostly archers, with the defender having 4 catapults (bonuses only on attacking), and 4 ballistae. I took out the cats right away, but the ballistae took out 10 of my archers and cavalry!

So yeah, they're very effective!

Scorpian
You raise trade fast by having an enormous army and putting it all in one village. Of course, the downside is that you still have an enormous army to pay.

Honestly, having a positive trade is probably a bad thing. As you get more powerful, you can afford to spend more gold per turn. Having a cost of 60 per turn is horrible when you can only generate 40 gold max in one turn. If you can generate 100 in one turn though, it's not really a problem.

The point? A large army is easier to fund than you think when you're smart about where you keep it. That's the trick that all of those people with 1,000+ trade you see every now and then used.
