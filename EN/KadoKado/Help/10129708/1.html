90 s are needed to produce 30 s. If the final head count is 89 or lower, tough luck -- nobody levels up.

Lately, VIP3-only games like Happy Pti Tank and Digestomax -- and to a lesser extent, 10000 shop games like Cosmo Crash and Razor -- have not been attracting enough newbie players to produce any rank-ups.

Bottlenecks have been occurring frequently in the metallic levels as well, with frequent player shortages in producing s (6 minimum), s (6 minimum) and s (3 minimum).

This is obviously a major problem, and fuels support for my suggestion that Motion-Twin lower the prices of the shop games or make them accessible via other ways, such as the challenges. Maybe replace the two VIP3-exclusive games with a different VIP3-only privilege, while they're at it.

You should report this issue to the admin team, as it's been happening for a while.

Lowering the minimum number of players will help the newbies, but those in // will remain stuck in level-up bottlenecks due to insufficient players.

The solution is to reduce the cost of unlocking highly-restricted games (VIP3, 10000, etc.), and to use a combination of good site exposure/promotion & incentives (challenges, etc.) to get players to play them.

Ranking the games by descending order of popularity on the games list is a horrendous idea, and only leads to a self-fulfilling cycle where high placement -> high visibility -> more players -> higher placement. Flushee, Iron Chouquette, Opalus, etc. don't need any more players: the maximum 500 advance from to every period, leading to advancement rates of well below the 7% target set by Motion-Twin (view this page in French), while other games barely get any player interest and suffer level-up bottlenecks across all ranks.
