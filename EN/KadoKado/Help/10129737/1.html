... tell everyone !

Type of proposition : ..... (game, rules, how to play, etc.)
Explanation : ..... (Clearly explain your idea)


Express yourself!

In my opinion, the single worst aspect of KK is the RANDOM element present in nearly every game.

Earning high scores on this site is far more about good luck than good skill.

For example, yesterday I got an unusually terrible Linea play: 4-5 minutes into the game, and there wasn't a single +12000 bonus item in sight. I scored 16.000 pts. Today, I got a "good" play with numerous +12000 bonus items and I scored 82.000 pts after only 2-3 minutes! Did I magically become so much better overnight? No! The absence of bonus items in yesterday's game was the sole reason for my poor score!

Another example is Hypercube, where the game often ends without new time-adding pieces (the ones marked by white-coloured dots.) Without them, I cannot earn a high score no matter how quickly I assemble squares. Some players get very rare parties with enough time-adding pieces to allow them to continue playing indefinitely, hence the 2 million+ pt. scores.

I believe that all games should be balanced so that each play is equally lucky (or unlucky!)

Proposition: Gameplay Change
Game: Ellon in the Dark (also Iron Choquette)

In both Ellon and IC, power-ups arrive at random times and what they are is also completely random. As such, once you've mastered the basics of a game, the real determining factor in your final score is how many and what kind of power-ups you get.

For Ellon it's especially frustrating because the various weapon power-ups don't stack and they don't level up when they're different types. Simply making each weapon power-up add a level in addition to changing the weapon type would fix most of the problem.

For both games, giving the player more control of which power-ups they get would make the games much more enjoyable because they could actively pursue different strategies. Have the weapon power-ups stay on screen longer and cycle from one type to another slowly. That way, if I want a particular type, I can just wait for it to change to that type before picking it up.
