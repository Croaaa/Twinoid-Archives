Even after the first step, it is impossible to find a definite way in two more steps of figuring out which weight is the "off" one if you don't know if it is lighter or heavier.

However, there is an answer if in the first step, the scale stays balanced.

For the sake of clarity, let's label the weights A-L.

Let's say weights A, B, C, and D on one side and weights E, F, G, and H on the other are balanced on the scale (step 1). You are then left with I, J, K, and L.

Step 2: Put I and J on one side and A and B on the other.

Step 3:
If I+Jâ A+B, put I on one side and K on the other.
If Iâ K, I is the odd weight.
If I=K, J is the odd weight.

If I+J=A+B, put I on one side and K on the other.
If Iâ K, K is the odd weight.
If I=K, L is the odd weight.

Of course my favourite and the ultimate in these types of puzzle is the Four Fours puzzle where you can use 4 4 4 4 putting in any operations trying to make exactly the right number, the fewer and the number and the greater the recognition of the operations used, the better the solution.

But as that can go on infinitely, and can use some very cheaty operations, I don't suggest a competition!

In the Four Fours you can use concatenation, i.e. using two 4s to become 44 is allowed, which it wasn't in the previous puzzles. You can use + - x / ^ ! and also square root (as the 2 is not traditionally written this does not require a number other than 2, however a 4th root would use a 4) and the gamma function which is (n-1)!, ie gamma4 = 3! = 6. The other very useful one is the decimal point, .4 = 2/5, which can of course be used with what is normally displayed as a dot above, to be .4444444, which in this syntax is best as .4~. A useful result from this is root(.4~) = 2/3.

As I say the numbers from 0 up to 100 are all possible and several more after that are too. All 4 4s must be used in any result e.g. 4 - 4 = 0 is not valid, but 44 - 44 = 0 is.

There is something wrong.

Taking each step (out of order) one at a time:
The number is a multiple of 3, 5, and 7.
For this to be true, the one's digit must be a 0 or 5.
The hundreds digit is the only digit that is higher than the ones digit.
That means the ones digit must be 5. (Even if you considered a number at this point with the rest of the digits being 0, i.e. not greater than the ones digit, none of the numbers would be a multiple of 3, 5, and 7.) So, we now have xxxxx5.
The hundreds digit is a cube.
The only cube number greater than 5 is 8, so the number at this point must be xxx8x5.
The hundred thousands digit is both a square and a cube.
The only digit that would make this true is 1. So, we have 1xx8x5.
The tens digit is a square number.
That means the tens digit must be 1 or 4 since this digit cannot be greater than 5.
each digit is used only once
That means the tens digit must be 4, since the hundred thousands digit must be 1. At this point, the number is 1xx845.
With all the clues together, excluding the multiple clue, the number must be 123845 or 132845. However, neither number is divisible by 3. This can be quickly checked by adding up the digits and seeing that the sum of the digits is not a multiple of 3.

ANS1
First, the waiter stuck the match into the lemon wedge, so that it would stand straight. Then he lit the match, and put it in the middle of the plate with the lemon. Then, he placed the glass upside-down over the match. As the flame used up the oxygen in the glass, it created a small vacuum, which sucked in the water through the space between the glass and the plate. Thus, the waiter got the water into the glass without touching or moving the plate.
