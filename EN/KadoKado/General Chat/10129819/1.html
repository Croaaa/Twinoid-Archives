I can't claim to know the actual reason why, but I'll throw out some possible reasons why:

1) To have games be as unobtrusive as possible. Some people like to have things be at a comfortable volume level, so not having sound prevents annoying those people

2) Sound design adds more time to game development. I have no clue how to make Flash games, but I imagine adding music/sound effects and event handlers (I think?) to manage all the sounds potentially brings up more bugs. And since it's related to sound, those bugs are much more detrimental to the user experience

3) Creating/acquiring sounds is fairly involved/expensive. Unless M-T has someone in their employ that's a musical whiz, getting music/sound effects isn't that easy! They would have to spend time making it in-house, or spend money commissioning an outside party
