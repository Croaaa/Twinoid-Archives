Synopsis

Chakré Buddha needs your help to reach nirvana!

Help him open his chakras as wide as possible before he is completely enlightened.

How To Play

Use your mouse to play this game.

Click on any place of the game screen when you see a white circle around the chakras (coloured balls).

The quicker you click, the more points you obtain.

If you don't click quick enough, the energy around Buddha dissipates. If it reaches the bottom, the game will end.

Tips

Don't click when you see a triangle.

By clicking faster you can to activate a bonus and increase the energy.

If you don't fail during a couple of turns, you'll get more points.

There is an incredible amount of variation in the RNG. The important variables are
(1) the frequency of circles, especially during "phase 2" of the game (see below for explanation)
(2) the movement of the chakrés (side-to-side movement is more favourable than circular rotations)
(3) the frequency of "Super Chakrés," which yield double points
(4) the appearance of triangles, which ensure your game's demise.

After you reach about 50.000 points, two things happen:
(1) the "energy level" goes into rapid descent mode
(2) the number of circles decreases dramatically (depending on your RNG)

I agree that this game needs improvement. The penalty for a single premature click is disproportionately large (your game is doomed), while you can sustain quite a few late clicks.

All of the "unbelievable" scores were attained thanks to "unbelievable" RNGs. (As stated above, good RNGs = lots of circles, minimal chakré movement and NO TRIANGLES.) The French player who scored 1.081.041 pts encountered no triangles until he had reached well over 900.000 pts.

I recall that my 157K play could have continued on for a good amount of time (judging by the energy level) had I not run into the dreaded triangle. Since then, I've scored a few 100K's but nothing closer...
