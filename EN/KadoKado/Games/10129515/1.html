I personally think this one is only 85% luck, unlike others that are 100% pure luck (please raise your hand Opalus)

Opalus is indeed 100% pure luck.

There are two main sources of randomness in Phago:

the starting placement of the red enemy cells. The game doesn't check to make sure that your cell's starting position is at a safe distance from all the enemy cells. Once, my cell got eaten alive with 0 pts in Phase 1. This happens a lot on the higher levels.

 the pattern and positions of the other cells.
"lucky" levels will place the cells so that similarly-sized ones are grouped together, so you can enlarge your cell without having to worry about accidentally bumping into giant ones, or having to travel long distances. I've game over'ed too many times from the stringent time limit, which I find quite annoying and unnecessary given how difficult the game already is.

What's so bad about Phago is that the randomness starts anew with each level, so I may be on a hot streak through Phase 5 yet game over right at the beginning of Phase 6, and I have no control over that.

Whereas in other games, a good RNG generally lasts throughout the whole play: for example, in Linea, if you get lots of 12000pt bonus items at the beginning, you'll probably see them often until the end of your play.

So that's where the 99.999% luck comes from.
