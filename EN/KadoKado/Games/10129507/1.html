Synopsis

Help Ellon the Apprentice Sorcerer fight the Absolute Dark forces.

Blast the hordes of monsters who are rushing into London.

We hope this adventure is only a figment of Ellon's imagination...

How To Play

Use the arrows on the keyboard to move in all directions.

The space bar will start up Ellon's broom-magic-gun.

Tips

If you move Ellon nearer to the ground, he will be able to aim at the monsters nearest to him.

Regarding the various weapon power-ups:

Yellow: This is considered an upgrade to your base weapon. It adds little random-ish extra bursts that increase the overall power and spread of the weapon. Probably the best all-around weapon there is. The charged up power up is especially effective with this weapon.

Purple: Adds a spread to your weapon but makes it fire more slowly. Good for taking out lots of little enemies. Charged up ability is just a bigger spread.

Blue: Turns your weapon into a beam that goes through enemies, doing slightly less damage per hit, but continuing on to hit enemies behind them too. Charged up ability is a weak lightning that automatically hits nearby enemies. This weapon is generally not as good as the base weapon until you get 2 stacks, and is only a real improvement at 3 or more.

Note when collecting power-ups that the new weapon comes in at the same strength as your old weapon. You will only improve your weapon's power by getting another copy of your current weapon. Example: At the start of the game you grab a blue power, then a purple, and finally a yellow. Your weapon is now exactly the same as it was at the start of the game.

(Dear KadoKado - that's a terrible mechanic and it's very frustrating. Given how randomly they are doled-out, power-ups should always be a power increase.)
