Synopsis

Maximize your points by controlling a line lost in a mystic electrical system!

How To Play

Use left and right arrow keys to move the line and avoid obstacles.

The X-Factor is a coeficient that multiplies the score.

Advance forward to the right to increase the X-Factor.

Each line on the background indicates the X-Factor level.

Tips

Press the right arrow key to advance and increase the X-Factor.

The more you advance to the right, the higher the risk but you'll get a higher score for each bonus you obtain.

I wrote some kind of a guide to Linea. I'am stucked on for a couple of periods. I have average scores about 80 000 points and my best is 136.978 points.

I hope it would come in handy to you.

Sorry for my bad English.

The main idea of this game is to test our reflex and ability to fast calculating everything what may appear on the screen.

The rules are simple:
1. You control the line that is on the screen.
2. Using the arrows you have to prevent your "Line" from hitting appearing squares.
3. The squares may stand still or be moving up and downwards.
4. The more (+) you collect, the more lines you are controlling. It means higher multiplier, but then again, it will be harder to dodge squares.
5. From time to time they may appear bonuses (4 small circles in certical line). You have to catch them. The more small circles you cross, the more points you have.
6. You can push your line to the right. Multiplier will increase but you will have to be much faster.
7. Game ends when you crash all of your lines.

Something about points:
1. You earn points for the whole time you play.
2. Every (+) you collect (maximum - 4 lines (three pluses)) will increase your points for time of playing multipier.
3. Bonuses - small circles - gives you extra 2 000 points for each of four circles. If you manage to collect all of small circles at once, you will be rewarded with extra 4 000 points.
4. You can see four sectors on the screen that are signed: [nothing], x2, x3, x8. Each sector increases your points for time of playing multiplier.

Difficulty:

The longer you are playing the harder game is.
Game is devided into five periods (I don't know if the sixth has been discovered yet):
1. 1/2 squares are moving and the screen is moving slow.
2. 1/2 squares are moving and the screen is moving faster.
3. 2/3 squares are moving and the screen is moving quite fast.
4. 3/4 squares are moving and the screen is moving very fast. In this level after a while you will see that the squares become faces. But I haven't noticed any influence on your playing.
5. Still the same number of squares is moving, but the screen is going extremely fast.

Tips:
1. Don't try to hang in the x8 sector. You will fail.
2. Getting the highest scores is all about collecting as many bonuses as you can. If you won't catch any of them you may play for a very long time and you wouldn't even have a half of high scores.
3. It is possible to catch one more small circle than the number of lines you have. To do it, you need to be almost in the centre of bonuses.
4. Don't try to manipulate just in front of the bonuses. It is very likely, that you will miss some of small circles.
5. The squares only changes direction of moving when they touch the edges of screen. You can in some way control when the squares will change their direction. Just move your line up or down in aim of closing edges to squares.
6. If you will touch the edges with more than one line - your lines will get much closer to each other. It is useful when you want to have good score basing on your time of playing (it is easier to dodge squares then), but if you base on bonuses (what I recommend) you will get maximum 4 000 points on each, instead of 12 000 points.
7. In first period of game try to hang on in x2 sector or even x3. But if you will catch some pluses - get back to x2 sector.
8. In second period, especcialy when you have four lines - stay in x2 section. You will have to get used to have less time to avoid being hit and to get onto good path in collecting bonuses.
9. In third period fall back into about 1/2 of x1 sector. You don't want to crash yourself into fast moving squares.
10. In fourth period - try to stay in 1/2 of x1 sector.
11. In fifth period - pray .
12. Why do I recommend staying in x2 sector or exactly 1/2 of x1 sector? If you collect a 'plus' the new line appears. If you stay in places mentioned by me - the new line will get to your lines much faster.
13. If you will crash when the new line is going to your lines - don't panic. Focus on controlling this new line because you will gain control on it.
14. Remember that you can collect bonuses using the new lines which you don't control.

I wish you a lot of high scores even if it would mean that I will have more opponents .
