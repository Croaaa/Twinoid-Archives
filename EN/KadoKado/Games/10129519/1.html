Synopsis

Alchemie returns in a brand new format! You don't know this game? Well put on your alchemist's robe and start manipulating the elements.
You never know, one day you might end up becoming a powerful alchemist capable of creating mythic gold nuggets!

How To Play

Put 3 identical elements together to make them disappear and create a new element. Try to create as many rare elements as possible in order to create a gold nugget!

Each element has 3 times the value of the element which preceeds it. Create as many rare elements as possible to increase your score.

Tips

Be careful of the rocks which can get in your way. Try to destroy them as quickly as possible by successfully aligning elements next to them.

Magic objects can appear at any moment, make sure to use them wisely.

This is a match-three puzzle game. You lose if you drop a piece that results in there still being something above the printed line on the screen, after everything has finished responding to your drop. It's OK if your drop initially leaves things above the line, though.

The lower right part of the screen shows you a list of items. The order from top to bottom matters, as this tells you how the pieces react when combined. Putting three or more of the same piece touching each other causes all of those pieces to vanish, and the leftmost piece on the lowest line (among the ones that vanished) will be replaced by the next piece in sequence.

The first four items on the list are the Green Potion, the Yellow Potion, the Orange Potion, and the Purple Potion. Dropping three or more Green potions together will cause them to vanish and be replaced by a single Yellow Potion. Three or more Yellow Potions get replaced by one Orange potion. And so on. The stone blocks don't react with anything, and are just obstacles. Occasionally a bug drops, and that will destroy the piece you drop it on top of along with any other pieces on the board that match it. It will not remove stone blocks, I've tried.

It is important to always remember that when you match pieces, the new piece appears on the LOWEST LINE you matched on, in the FARTHEST LEFT position that you matched on that line. All the other matched pieces vanish, causing the pieces above them to fall. Then the board is checked for matches again, and this process repeats until there are no matches left.

Matching things next to the stone blocks seems to cause them to gradually decay. Enough matches next to them will eliminate them.

Is NC2 difficult to understand, speaking no french?It depends on how much you want to get out of the game. If you simply want to play and complete quests (there are 177 of them), it's not strictly necessary. Most NPCs' dialogue trees are set up so that if you keep selecting their topmost choices, you'll be led to their respective quests. And of course, if you have any doubts, you can always use a machine translator...or PM me and I can help you.

If you want to understand what all the NPCs are saying (some quests can have quite a bit of text), then reading comprehension is obviously required. If you want to communicate on the forums or join a clan, you'll need to write in French (and I usually use a machine translator.)

I started NC2 not speaking a word of French, but after a year, I would say I've acquired a 2-month-old's vocabulary. And I'm still learning.
