Synopsis

Have you ever dreamt of managing a toy factory?

Well, in Toy Maniak, you are the boss!

Group identical toys on the same line and get rich!

How To Play

Move the toys with your mouse to make series of identical toys on the same line.

There is a counter on each line which tells you how many identical toys you have aligned.

Tips

The aim of the game is to have as many identical toys as possible aligned together.

For example, if you align 8 Safari cars you'll get 360 pts (10 pts for the first, 20 pts for the 2nd, 30 pts for the 3rd, etc.).

But be careful. If you "break" a line, the counter goes back to zero.

Pick up the bonus to increase speed or modify the points multipliers.

You need to concentrate on only 2 types of toy to build up the longest possible chain. If you get up to 199 then you'll get 2000 points per item.

For instance put all the snakes in the top conveyor belt, the car in the middle one and the other 2 in the bottom one as the junk belt.

For speed ups i put the 1st 2 in the bottom belt and any more i vary between the top 2 and bottom one.



For +20's put them in one of the top 2 belts asap, out them all in the same one to get the score as high as you can, though remember you can only go up to 199 max.

For x2's put save them in the bottom boxes until near the end of the time limit. But if you get 3 x2's and have a score of 25 in one of the belts then that would multiply it up to maximum of 199. The same with 50 and 2 x2's.

It's simply not possible to get the without a good number of +20's and x2's. This is entirely dependent on luck, so you may have to grind for a while before you get a favourable bonus distribution.

That said, I usually put the orange Flushee monster on the top belt, the Manda on the middle belt, and alternate between and Safari jeeps on the bottom belt. Statistically, the Flushee monsters and Mandas have tended to be the most plentiful. Since you're sorting four different types of items on three conveyor belts, one of them has to be used as a "trash" belt. Other than that, it's just a matter of making the highest combo you can without messing up.
