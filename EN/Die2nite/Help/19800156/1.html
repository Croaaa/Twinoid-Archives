After collecting a little data and doing a few tests with zooming in on the bar in MS Paint I counted the effective bar to be 148 pixels wide with the red line resting on the 60-pixel mark. Doing some math with those numbers I got the bar resting at something like 40.540540...% (or 40.5% rounded)

What I've noticed is hunger grows 37 pixels after the attack, eating a corpse drops hunger 15 pixels and eating a meaty bone drops hunger 22 pixels. I haven't eaten anything else (except other citizens, which clears the bar completely) so can't say first hand what other foods drop the meter by.

Theory I have is turning into a ghoul on day 1, eating a corpse in town on day 2 and nothing else (+37 -15 = 22), then on day 3 you'll be at 59 pixels and be able to survive the next day without eating any hunger-reducing foods (pretty much what Fortis said above). Haven't been able to test this yet, though, mostly because it's difficult to make someone die in town on command other than eating them, and few people will leave the body laying around until the next day.

For those who like percentiles, usable numbers are:
Hunger grows 62.5% daily
Corpse drops hunger -25%
Meaty bone drops hunger -37.5%
Red bar = 100%possible you don't die of hunger untill you exceed 100%
Still need to do some testing on this.

When/if I get a chance to eat a traveler's corpse and human flesh while a hungry ghoul I'll adjust the numbers further.
