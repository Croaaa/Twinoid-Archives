yes i agree with what mentioned above: the respawn will only be triggered only after the spread after arrgh , to be more specific:

  • first the attack happens,
  • then the spread,
  • then attrition (dying from grief),
  • then last if current number of zeds on the map is lower than the number needed to NOT trigger a respawn then you get one.

now how i found that out, it was funny but hell, that is how we discover new things.

in one of our distant zoo towns too many zeds were killed to the point that the formula (assuming the one i have been using is correct) gave a number which was right below the edge of triggering respawn , so it wasn´t realy clear if we were gonna trigger one or not, BUT the number of zones which were cleared of zeds were over 5 zones all with 20+ zeds in them. and that caused Alot less spread next day because most of the spread zeds died from attrition right after spreading and that did cause the number of daily 30+ zed spread to decrease to the point where a respawn has been triggered.

now that alone wasnt proof enough by then that the trigger happens after spread of next day.
but after that in 2 towns one in S8 and one in season 9 i calculated the formula numbers + zed numbers and even announced in the forum in both these towns that we went 5 and 6 below the respawn number on those 2 specific days. But on both next days a respawn wasn´t triggered, because imo clearly the spread of the new day evened out the few zeds we killed too many on previous day.

FYI the formula i have been using was T = 28 x Day.
could be 27 x Day + X but that is a bit too low imo

Maybe for distant map it is length of map x day, whereas small map it is length x 2 x day.

I looked back on a map we intentionally triggered respawn right at the end. We didn't have enough time to do it as slowly as we wanted and of course some zones with estimated zombies might have made the math slightly off but this is what we find if you assume our zed #s were accurate. The map was aa 27 x 27 map.

End of Day 39

1122 projected zombies at beginning of Day 40 (after spread/regrowth)
1122 / 40 = 28.05
No repop = Above 28 x Day is safe


End of Day 40

1130 projected zombies
1130 / 41 = 27.561
No repop = Above 27 x Day is safe


End of Day 41

1138 projected zombies
1138 / 42 = 27.095 zombies
No repop = Above 27 x Day, still safe


End of Day 42

1097 projected zombies (this is where we went faster than we wanted)
1097 / 43 = 25.512
REPOP on Day 43. Below both 27 x Day and 26 x Day were not safe.


So based on the above we never got the chance to test if above 26 x Day was enough. 26 and 27 being sort of weird #s and the map being 27 x 27 makes me think a distant map is Length x Day. If you want some insurance you can always set your repop limit slightly higher, so if you do it at 28 x Day like Dream suggests, then you have a little wiggle room.
