Hello everyone!

I got to witness a little discussion/argument over whether or not heroes should make a Siesta Time™. Note that they did this on Day 1. The argument against was that is better spent doing other stuff on Day 1 (such as going out to scav).

The argument for it was that, if a town lasts past Day 10, with Siesta Time™ level 2, which costs 9 you get:

10 x 66% * 2 = 13.2 = ~13

Well, so... you could get super-lucky and get 20 AP back from it. Another point was that if the town starts late and people aren't even able to log on until short before attack, they are spending their AP to make something that will give AP in return in later days.

My opinion is that it wouldn't be SO BAD for Suvivalists to build a Siesta Time™. But it's shaky at best. I am not sure about other classes.

Your thoughts and number crunching are welcome

Hacksaw
There is an adjusted cost for Siesta Time than just the standard it costs. It also costs a Twisted Plank to make level 2.

While Twisted Planks are a common material, they're also needed in a LOT of constructions, and the underlying cost to replace the Twisted Plank is between 2 and 5 (depending on Watchtower level, and presence or absence of Factory and Hacksaw).

Assuming Watchtower level 4, it costs only 1 to go out and dig for scraps and returning to town is free.
Cost to convert a rotten log into a Twisted Plank is between 1 and 3 depending on whether or not your town has a Hacksaw and/or Factory built.

Accepted adjusted cost to build is about 12 (including parts and labor) meaning you'd have to successfully use it at least 7 times to actually gain any over the cost.

With a 66% success rate (meaning a 33% failure rate), you could earn the back in as early as 7 days, or it could take you 20 days to get it all back. Depends on how fickle the RNG gods are being.
