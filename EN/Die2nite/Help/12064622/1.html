What is Standard Deviation?
On may 22, 2012
Hannelor

I've tried to make this as simple as possible but it can be a difficult concept...
It is much simpler than it first appears.

Standard deviation is the root of(Sx - (n times mean result))/(n-1))

Where Sx is the sum of of the squares of every set of data
and n is the number of sets of data.

It measures the average spread of data around the mean (average) result.

Standard deviation is a measure of the distribution of data. It tells you how far from the average most towns fall. So since the SD here is 2.5, 66% of all ruins tend to fall within 2.5 km of the average of 8 km (between 5.5 km and 10.5 km).
