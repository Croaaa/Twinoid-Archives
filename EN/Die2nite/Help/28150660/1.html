rumata

My experiment showed one DO to be worth at least 1.86 (if we accept every other number in the equation as is: e.g.: the flavour texts for zone status, one upgrade's worth of 5%, distance modifiers)

It isn't an important distinction though: you are safe to calculate with 2 upgrades/DO when upgrading, unless you wish to install at least 6 DOs in that zone in some consecutive fashion.

The experiment in brief: 7 DOs + 1 upgrade in a 1km zone, done in 2 consecutive days. The zone turned from really poor to poor with the last DO upgrade. That means (7*x+1-3)*5-25 >= 30%, where x is the DO's worth. Solving for x gives 13/7, which is roughly 1.86.

Hacksaw

My tests support a DO being worth a +9% bonus to camping chances, which reverse-engineers the DO's value to be worth 1.8 zone upgrades on the grounds that if 1 zone upgrade is worth a +5% bonus then 5% / 5 = 1, therefore if a DO is worth a +9% bonus, then 9% / 5 = 1.8.

I thought someone recently mentioned that they did a test that supports the value of 1.8 as well, but don't remember who or what post they mentioned it in.Edit: Oh, it was Rumata in the post Kiwi linked. Should have read that first.

9 upgrades + 1 DO brings you to 10.8 upgrades (+54% survival chances)

After the attack the zone degrades 3 upgrades (-15%) bringing you down to 7.8 upgrades (+39% survival chances)

Your options from here are A: Install 2 DOs, B: make 1 normal upgrade and install 1 DO, C: Make 2 normal upgrades and install 1 DO, or D: make 3 normal upgrades. End outcomes are as follows:
A: 11.4 zone upgrades (+57% survival chances)
B: 10.6 zone upgrades (+53% survival chances)
C: 11.6 zone upgrades (+58% survival chances)
D: 10.8 zone upgrades (+54% survival chances)
