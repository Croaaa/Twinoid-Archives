If a citizen dies in town and their body is not destroyed, their body rises during the nightly attack and either destroys some rations from the well or kills a random survivor (personal defenses are ignored completely).

If a citizen dies in town and their body is destroyed, this is the last you will ever hear about this citizen for the rest of the town.

If a citizen dies in the world beyond, however, they are "added" to the nightly attack for the rest of the town's life in the following capacity:
During the nightly attack, there is an extra line added to the Gazette registry saying X number of former residents were identified in the horde attack where X is the number of citizens to die in the world beyond over the town's life so far (if only one person has died in the world beyond during that attack, they'll be called out by name).
If the gates are left open (or destroyed and the town devastated), these zombies will be added to the attacking horde (for instance, if you know 12 people died in the worlcd beyond and the watchtower says between 275 and 325 zombies will attack, if the gates are left open then between 287 and 337 zombies will be seen in town when all are accounted for). As usual, the gazette will tell you the actual number of horde zombies actually attacked and the "former residents" will be extra zombies not included in that number.
If the gates are closed, all former residents will be blocked entry from the town and will not be included in the total number of zombies seen in town in the instance of a breach. For example, if the town has 250 defenses and the gazette reads that 300 zombies attacked with 12 former residents visible, the total attack size is 312 zombies. Gates open will accumulate to 312 zombies visible in town, but gates closed will accumulate only 50 zombies in town with 250 blocked by the town's defenses and the 12 former residents blocked by the closed gates.In fact, if you had 300 defense exactly and were attacked by 300 zombies + 12 former residents, you'd still have no breach because the 300 defense would block the horde and the former residents would still be blocked by the closed gates. Data suggests that former residents are included in the formula for attacking zombies. For example, if the gates are left open and 300 zombies attacked with 12 returning citizens and the formula allows for only 150 zombies to attack, the gazette will say 162 zombies were seen in town but didn't attack anyone making it seem like (300 - 162 = ) 138 zombies attacked if you forget to account for the 12 returning citizens.

This is what I've observed during season's 4 and 5. During season 3, however, I recall it looking like the former residents were added to the attacking zombies if the gates were left open. For example, during season 3 if there were 10 or less people in town, then 150 zombies would attack with any excess being "lazy", but if there were any former residents, they'd be added to the 150 if the gates were left open (so if there were 150+ zombies in the horde with 12 former residents, the gates were left open, and 10 or less citizens in town then there would be 162 attacking zombies with any further overflow being seen but not attacking anyone). IMO, this X-factor needs to be added back into the game to make end-game camping with open gates that much more dangerous. Edit: Now, if you're asking if the dead bodies of former residents can be seen on the map while you're out scavenging, no, they aren't, you only ever see buildings, zombies and living citizens.
