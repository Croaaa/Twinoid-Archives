fodgycakes
Follow me if you want to live!

Another option is to use an app to remotely connect to your home computer. TeamViewer and SplashTop both work well, TeamViewer seems to have fewer issues with connecting remotely from outside of your home network. You can use an iphone to do that if apart from wanting to play d2n you like the iphone better than an android phone. The bonus of doing it this way is that you will still be updating the external maps with any updater apps you use since you are just using your phone to connect to your computer.

I primarily use TeamViewer to do d2n things on my phone and have Puffin installed as a back-up in case my computer is down or have issues connecting for whatever reason. Then I can at least move on the map as needed.

Edit: And if you're just in town, you can use most phone stock browsers, including the iphone, to do things like eat/drink/build/etc.
