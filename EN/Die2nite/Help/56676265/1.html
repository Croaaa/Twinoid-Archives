Spike is just a conventional term to qualify an attack above the average on a given day. The average attack numbers are based ofc on statistics of previous towns on that season (since atk numbers may vary with sasonal updates).

The best way to check that is on map viewer stats.
Note that the numbers change between distant and Hardcore, so if you want HC atk numbers find a HC town on map viewer and check stats in there.

Attack estimation formula: Well, I don't know the excat formula but they usually works this way:

If 30% estimation is 500-1000, and 100% is 700-800, attack is around 750.
But if 30% estimation is 400-1000, 100% is 500-800, attack is around 600.
In first example both maximum and minimum attacks move same "speed" while in lower example max. attack lowers twice as fast as min. attack rises.

Hopefully you got it from my weird explanation.

Ah, the formula to estimate the exact attack - don't trust it completely as it may have changed over the seasons. This is from an old notepad I have:

Formula to estimate exactly attack number:

=D25 + (E25-D25) * (D25-D24) / ((E24-D24) - (E25-D25))

final estimate min + (final estimate max - final estimate min) * (final estimate min - first estimate min) / (first estimate max - first estimate min) - (final estimate max - final estimate min)
