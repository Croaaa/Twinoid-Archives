Naia

My advice would be, Don't do anything stupid to get yourself killed, and be a nice guy.

In a small town ( and also in many distant ones ) 75% of people get them self killed within the first few days. Either by forgetting to return to town, exploring to far from town, getting stuck and then not making a help post, dying of dehydration or just something as simple as not using FDTD and therefore have no clue of where they can safely go.
Basically most people get them self killed without you having to do anything.

The remaining 8 - 10 guys are the one you are competing against.


First off, let's remember that a town needs to survive at least 5 days, and the longer the better.
You can of course get the LMS award before that, but I'm assuming it's the free hero days you are more interested in.
So you need the other guys to work together and stay alive for as long as possible.

You can try playing hostile, but there are really not that many options to get rid of people.
And by playing hostile, you at the same time makes yourself into a treat, so it can backfire and make your own chances worse.
It's a small community, and there is a good chance you will run into the same people in the next town.

Poisoning food or water, or closing the gates early, might work on one or a few people, if you do it right.
But it's unlikely you will get rid of everyone that way. And people will hate you for it.

Upgrading your house and hoarding defensive items, might work, but it quite unpredictable who will get attacked and by how many zombies. Even if you have the best defence, you can still end up as the only one getting eaten. If there is enough people left, you will probably get shunned.
Quite often house upgrades turn into an arms race, that actually kill the whole town faster ( not really what you want to get those sweet hero days )


So be a nice guy. The longer the town runs, the more people will vote them self of the island, and the larger the reward will be for the winner. Put your Ap into town defence.

Does this ensure that you win ? no. But neither does playing hostile.

( and it's looks like you are off to a good start whit 3 out of 3 )
