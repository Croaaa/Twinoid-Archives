Warning! Wall of Text follows!

Some of you might remember that a while ago I had the idea of making a Die2Nite themed fan game.

I haven't forgotten about that idea! I'm definately wanting to get back on board with it.

I feel I can achieve my goals using RPG Maker VX Ace. Theres tons of free resources that can be used for game creation but alot of editing would be needed and thats where the community comes in.

Well what sort of game would this be? Think of an RPG set inside a Die2Nite town. Story focused, with a side quest system to grab those resources to help defend the town. You'll play as the "leader" of the town, making sure everything is running how it should be. I would also want to flesh out a nice social system, letting you forge relationships, make difficult choices. Hell I'll even add dating sim choices in there if theres a demand for it.

Quite frankly, its going to be a long project and I suck at creation! Music, art etc. My talents are with story and with tedious compiling, yay ocd
So my idea is to create the basic storyboard of how the game would work and I make requests of the community to help fill in the gaps! The sort of things I would request would be:

Tile editing - Handy with pixel art? Good because your very important! Theres tons of resources that I could use but would need to be converted into the die2nite format. It won't just be converting however. Newly created images can be used of course. Don't worry; I would supply the shopping list of whats needed.

Music - Maybe you know an awesome free track that would fit a scene perfectly or your willing to craft the "Horde attacking town!" theme. Sound effects and musical talents are also very much needed.

Story & Scenes - Creative writing helps alot! Maybe you want to create a scenario/event for the town to experience or you want to flesh out a characters personality.

Coding & Scripting - Any help on this when things just arn't working right would be awesome.

Enough Already!

I think you get the idea now. What would be awesome is to put actual Die2Nite players in this game. Maybe Workshop will want his character to play the role of town saboteur and it will be your job to catch him in the act. You might want to focus more of your characters time into trying to score a date with Deblyn before you die. It's open to alot of possibilities.

First order of business

Will be for me to get more familiar with the engine and tests the limits. I'll be wanting to create the basic shell of the town you walk around and make sure it's sufficiently Die2Nite themed. Once thats done then more tile creation and story elements can begin.

Here is a trick for Photoshop that allows you to increase the size of a pixel image as much as you want while maintaining edges, colours and sharpness.

Tutorial - Resizing Pixel Art

It has made going through the images of the ruins from the wiki SO much easier.

Basically:

Open picture in Photoshop
Go to Image > Image Size...
Change 'pixels' to 'percentage'
Entire how much you'd like to enlarge it by (keep it to multiples of 100)
Constrain Proportions
Change Bicubic to Nearest Neighbour

Poof! Enlargement without quality loss!

Poof! Enlarged Pixel art with no quality lost

In response to your advise Vanish...

This game will be heavily story driven. A well thought out story can be alot more memorable and epic then dealing with too many random features. But some randomness is important, like picking if a certain character will live or not thus impacting the rest of the story. We have enough story writers to make sure its done right


There will be quests and some may involve sending other citizens outside. But it won't be a feature, that would impact the story too much.


The shamans tent already is a bit different to the others Modifications to the graphics can be done anytime during development, so things can be changed last minute if it doesn't feel right.


In regards to having "different zombies" Not many know but this already will be a huge feature. Due to engine and gameplay elements along with story. During the day the plot is that the world beyond has your typical, slow moving zombie. Easy to deal with unless in numbers and also letting typical towns people head outside without too much hassle. However special zones will be fully explorable when uncovered and also have different zombies for each one thus keeping battles fresh with different enemys. It will make sense in the game with the feature I'm putting in, trust me on this

However at night all hell breaks loose. The burning sun during the day hindered the zombies yet at night, they become fast, they become smarter and it will really impact the game. This change means they come for the town in force and if they break in then you've got some TOUGH fights on your hands.
