Oh yes. Forgot to explain warriors abilities.
Now all the warriors collected some Fury and can use special abilities (or keep stacking it for the next turns to use more powerful ones, but remember that you can not have more than 3). Fury for warriors is like Mana for mages, but collects by a different way. You are earning fury for succesful attacks and losing blood yourself, look for information at the spreadsheet.
Abilities will add damage to your weapon's power, or add protection to your shield power, or add armor. For example, if you have steel sword (4 damage) and attacking Ruby with powerfull strike - it will deal 4+1=5 damage in a cost of 1 fury. If you will attack Ruby twice with powerfull strikes - it will deal (4+1)*2=10 damage in a cost of 2 fury.
Also there are some group actions that you may make teamed up with other warriors, like "Double attack". It requests 2 warriors and 1 fury from each one of them, will add +3 damage to their summ attack. So it is way better to make this actions, because you will actually deal +1,5 damage per 1 fury, which is better than powerfull strike (+1 damage per 1 fury).
Also the crowd favorite "Back to back" ability in the cost of 1 fury will give both warriors +1 armor for this turn and will not request any AP Wise planning on your fury using and gaining is the key to victory!
