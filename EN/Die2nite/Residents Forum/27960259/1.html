Annette

We didn’t want to make a big deal out of this but after being around for five seasons and seeing recent mentions of it here and there, we thought there should be some notice before the new season that we are retiring the MMJ.

The MMJ was created when the meta cap was brought in so that active players who favoured cooperative teamplay could play with others who were like minded. You didn’t have to be experienced or a hero, there were no stipulations at all. After the cap was lifted in season 4, the MMJ was continued as a fun jump to get to know other active players in the game and mingle with a variety of players and personalities.

As time passed, we saw less and less interest with each passing season. We’ve been able to find enough volunteers to jump into towns for people that couldn’t be online at the jump time, but even that hasn’t been without issues. There’s also the issue that there just aren’t as many people participating that have the long-time knowledge that are willing to play in an MMJ and help steer a town and/or pass on their knowledge.

Since the introduction of the MMJ there have been many other community jumps, and we feel that without the MMJ, there will still be plenty of opportunities for players to participate in events that mingle the player base. We’re sorry if anyone was already looking forward to the next MMJ, but with these community jumps now available to fill the role that the MMJ once had, its declining participation, and just overall lack of time from us (the organizers), we feel it is time to retire the MMJ and have these other jumps be the focus.

Thank you to everyone who championed and participated!!

- Spacekadt, Fodgycakes and Annette

It is fairly easy to organize... in theory. For the last three MMJs we've had to chase people down that were supposed to invite people to a coalition. Or find a new person to jump that coalition. That is where things get obnoxious and "difficult"... and truth be told, I'm not willing to herd cats anymore.

As for the name, it hasn't been accurate for some time. Mega Meta Jump. In season three, we had every meta in the game plus newbies coming together to make towns succeed. The last few seasons, it's been the "90% newbies and a few experienced folks." Hell, the last MMJ only had 85 people sign up for it.

MMJ has been a good place for people to get their feet wet with organized play. It's been a good place to learn about the game, to meet meta folks that may be recruiting, for metas to do recruiting. But for some time now, the metas are off doing their own things. These days, even with MMJ, you're lucky to get one or two people that know the basic strategies and are willing to step up to lead a town as far as it takes to be "successful".

Can we rightfully say you're not allowed to use the name? No, we can't. But if someone's determined enough to do it, I'd personally like to see them stand on their own two feet and not take something that others made successful. Do a community jump if you want. Use our templates as a starting point... I'll even send them to you. But it's not a "mega meta jump" and you know it.

All opinions in this post are mine and do not speak for anyone else.

idare, our intention isn't to "take away" the event; even though we started the event, it is very much the community's event too. That's why we gave notice, because we figured that if there were not any other community events currently planned (BoTS and CotT were two potential events coming up), that someone might want to take the lead on organizing a jump similar to MMJ.

The reason we presented it as a "retiring" of the MMJ is that when the jump first started, it was a way for all the different metas to be able to jump together since they couldn't jump as full metas anymore. Over time it has evolved to no longer be meta-centric. Many newer players don't even know what MMJ means and think the name means you need to be in a meta to participate. So someone planning an all inclusive jump with the same format, by all means use our old threads as a resource and guideline on how to organize it, we just suggest coming up with a new name.

For those willing to take it on, here's a google doc with the thread posting. Feel free to use it as a guideline, but after five seasons, we encourage anyone organizing a new jump to think if a new format would be better. For example, the mention of doing it HC is a pretty good idea that would concentrate more participants into the event towns rather than spreading them out. If people are willing to do HC at least!

Since it looks like there are people willing to take on organizing a jump, for some advice, most if it is pretty straightforward. It's not some overbearing workload, it's not a complicated task, but it does take some time and it's just not for us anymore. No need to be insulting and saying "but it's so easy!" We just always tried to be as inclusive as possible and that's the main part that requires more effort. You will have people who can't make the jump at the time specified or have fewer than 100 SP and if you want to include them, it'll take a little more time beyond just setting the time of the jump. The way we did it was to make a google form where people could sign up and say they were either jumping but not able to drag anyone else, were jumping and could drag someone else, or if they would need to be dragged. Then use that list to assign coalitions. Be prepared for flakiness such as having jumpers who say they can jump but then don't show up or never send out invites. Again, it's not some overwhelming task, but just be prepared to keep tabs on that.

Again, thank you for everyone's support of the MMJ over the seasons. Given the interest expressed in this thread about continuing it, hopefully something new and better can take its place.
