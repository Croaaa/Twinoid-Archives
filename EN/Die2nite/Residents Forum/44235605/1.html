I also blame metas even though I am the boss-thing of one.

Metas were created to avoid those random-lame-griefer players and to push the limits of this game. In order to do that they had to be very selective with their members.

Meta towns last 20+ days. That is enough time for a rather averageish random player to have 3-4 other existences in random towns. Most of them being with griefers and early death people.

That random player will soon be bored of the game and, if he continues, he will either become a selfish/griefer player or attempt to join a meta. Not everyone is selected/stick with the game after this, though.

As soon as people started to gather on these metas, Motion Twin realized it would be bad to the game and they came up with the meta-cap idea. This was made to force people to organize MMJ-like events in order to get to good towns if they wanted. This engaged more random players that stickied to the game after participating on such towns.

Meta-cap is a healthy thing for this game. This is so true that you can see it in place on Hordes, the original french version, today.

Our early Heroes fought against this handicap in order to be able to play with their friends and the same 39 dudes they were used to. Meta-cap was bad with prior-jump plans, and this was not good at all if you wanted to aim the Leaderboards. They made it and the meta-cap was lifted.

After the meta-cap fall more metas appeared, but not that much. The game flourished for a couple years, but that was more because MT still had their focus on updates than because of the meta-cap being removed.

Now the game is on decline and it will only get worst as MT already claimed they will no longer produce things for us.

The only people playing as dedicated Heroes now are inside leaderboards-aiming metas or in a distinction-whore quest. Other heroes are just Ghoul-wannabes and LMS people.

It is funny to watch the recruitment thread. Most recent posts are of people that somehow got a few hero days and want to spend them on a good town rather than the lame towns they got the days.

It is required a great strength of Will to endure the early stages of this game and become an established meta player to, then, start having fun with the game. I can count with a single hand the amount of good players that appeared and sticked with the game since last year.

Even if we remove coalitions and add meta-cap back it is too late for this game. We are breathing with the help of devices and with our death timed.

Limbo awaits us and we will be there forever
