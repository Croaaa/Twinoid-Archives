You're cliched, Colette. In all seriousness, though, many of the common things you see in zombie stories make lots of sense. "There's a group of survivors." Yes, because wide-angle camera shots of zombies stumbling around or burning cars would be incredibly boring, and 95%+ of stories have groups of people in them. "One gets bitten". One person being bitten makes sense, because at that point the group they are with most likely is not taking the appropriate steps to avoid bites. The terrible, terrible consequences of their bitten compatriot kind of clue them in that are bites are a VERY BAD THING. "One becomes annoying". Yes, some people are annoying. What do you expect of a group that contains people? "by fighting with the group and making bad decisions" Intragroup Conflict. It's a thing which happens. Thus, it is a thing which happens in fiction. "Eventually gets eaten" Bad things happening to antagonists? In fiction? Lordy lord, what a cliche! I will now only view stories that bad things don't happen to antagonists. "one that is miraculously immune to the virus" This one only happens in a small amount of zombie stories, actually, and it often provides the crux of the plot. Even when it isn't the focus of the plot, it was allows the plot to occur. Imagine Left 4 Dead if the Survivors weren't immune to the zombie infection. Oh wait. You can't. Because they would have died nearly instantly.

Too Lazy, Can't Read Version: Don't focus on what makes stories the same, look for what makes them stand apart from others (and hopefully makes them good).

On-topic: Haven't read any zombie books, though I guess the Death Gate Cycle did have zombies in it, sorta.

I, for one, am not a fan of that one.

I'm not a fan of this cliché either. About bad things that happen to antagonists, that's an entirely different thing. In zombie stories the antagonists are usually the zombies themselves.
