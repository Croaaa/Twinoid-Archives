Gosh Miudo, maybe some proper noun-verb plural agreement is in order? I think you mean "has yet to confirm her death". Also, your second sentence is only grammatically valid if we consider her confirmation of a death as a recurring action. Since we are referring to a single incident here, it would be better to say "when that happens she will get the SP". Finally, you forgot a comma after Devastation, which should be capitalized considering it's a formal event, forgot a period at the end of that sentence, and didn't put an apostrophe in the word "its", which is needed if you want it to act as a conjunction, rather than merely a possessive pronoun.
