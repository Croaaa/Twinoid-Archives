Zelgadis

I know it's a photo contest. But! "Today will be a story of a (failed) attempt of making one ([very] short version. Kudos to Joanne for making it, lol)". Or so I thought. Scrap that, I'll just write a random story just for the hell of it, it's boring to sit without any left.
Always wanted to write something worthy to become a RP text, so bear with me please and let me practice a lil' bit. This might become another crappy story or, maybe, a good one (I hope it will be the latter). Here goes:


It was a cold evening as any other. Autumn was always harsh in this country, but Zel was grateful for finally managing to get away from that godforsaken Desert populated with walking dead, ghouls, madmen, cannibals and plain douchebags. He had to abandon his friends, but who could judge him? He was the one who tried to convince them to leave that cursed wreck of a Town. It was obvious from the beginning that staying in there was a bad idea - they were basically living in a tin can that zeds were hell-bent to open and feast on. But they never listened, they continued to fortify the Town with every scrap of iron they could lay their hands on, as if they were possessed. And so, he packed his belongings into his rugged backpack, waited till early morning and secretly fled from town, hoping to reach as far as possible before the midnight Horde started crawling towards their prey...

Two days passed. That patch of green land he believed to be the end of the Desert was a lie. Just a small parch of land covered with damp grass, that a treacherous mirage made look like a vast, infinite field of greenery. He was at his wit's end, almost about to snap: sleep deprivation, lack of food and water, exhaustion and need to hide from every single walker and now THIS. It looked like the Desert was mocking him, giving a false hope just to shatter it a minute later. In desperation, Zel started to dig with his bare hands, he needed the water, and grass just couldn't have grown there without it. A slim chance, but that's far better than nothing.

Night arrived suddenly and so did the zeds. Hundreds, maybe thousands of them, even. Groaning eerily, they came from the direction of the Town. Apparently, it has ran out of zombie food... Sweating profusely, Zel stood up and ran. It seemed like a hopeless try, but capricious Lady Luck doesn't like it when others break her toys, she does that herself. And once again, like many times before, she decided to prolong poor bastard's life. After running for what felt like hours, he stumbled upon a Strange Circular Device. The thing looked really out-of-place and had a strange-looking glass cockpit on top, it might have been some sort of high-tech combine harvester for all he cared. There wasn't any time to waste; if he could start the thing's engine (or whatever that thing had), it might become his ticket out of here. After a quick search of the wreckage he found an old cellphone from times long ago, the ones that exploded one day and killed millions of people while they were at it. He threw it at the cockpit, and a loud explosion roared across the Desert. Cockpit's glass didn't stand a chance.

Groans became louder by the minute. Zel scrambled up the sleek surface of the machine and sat at the "driver's seat". After taking a few quick kicks on the control panel, thing started humming in low tone and all was flooded with bright light. World seemed to drown in this radiant flash. And then, there were darkness...

<...>

A man quietly opened his green eyes. He couldn't remember a thing. He couldn't think clearly. He was on a bed in a white room, two people were standing above him, having a conversation. One looked like a stereotypical scientist - in his sixties, bald, thin, pale, wearing huge glasses. The other man looked like a businessman of sorts from the old times before the Pandemic - a man in his forties, wearing suit and tie, his hair cut short and a case in his hand.

Scientist: ...it is incredible, mr. #####! The device really worked! We could have spent half our lives to think of a way to find appropriate energy source if you hadn't been for that yellow crystal you gave on the last time! We used it a a catalyst and the cross-dimensional teleporter brought this man here! Although...
Mr. #####: Yes?
Scientist: It seems like his memory was completely wiped clean, he doesn't remember anything. He's in state of deep ataraxia and doesn't respond to anything. Hmm, that must be the consequences of cross-dimensional travel. What should we do from now on?
Mr. #####: This subject... I have a great interest in him. Use the latest brainwashing techniques to give him false memories and cram his head with basic knowledge of this world. Also, he needs something else. Something... Important.
Scientist: Which is?...
The man grinned: "A name. Something appropriate of the situation, like... Freeman. A man who craves freedom, but will always remain the slave of fate. Ironical, yet quite amusing. Sweet dreams, mr.Freeman. Enjoy the peace while it lasts, because I'll need your..."cooperation"...soon enough."

With that, Mr.##### and the bald man left the room. All was quiet again. Zel fell asleep for the last time. By the time
he opens his eyes, he'll become another man, literally.

Another man.
Another time.
Another story.

The Town finally lost it's last resident.
Town is dead, long live the Town!
