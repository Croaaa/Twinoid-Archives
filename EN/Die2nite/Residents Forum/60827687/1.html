1. It`s a simple case like in your first example.

2. Tortured souls add to each attack (everyday), until they are purified. On day 5 it`s 130 instead of usual 100 with 2 red souls present (doesn`t matter if outside or already collected - but they are not purified yet), on day 6 it`s 260 instead of usual 200 and so on.

3. Attack estimation changes (includes) the number of souls not purified - it changes instantly after each soul is purified. It allowed the whole testing and number crunching in the first place. On day 6 estimation shows 200-260 with 2 red souls, after purifying them you`ll see 155-200 for instance.

Correct matsuuu, it indeed got changed to 7,5% per red soul, sorry for the mistake.


goob you don`t need to add anything from previous days, since attack on given day doesn`t have any influence on the next days` attacks. The daily attacks have a possible range, just in my example the day 5 attack (best estimation and actual attack - it would be shown as estimation between lower and larger numbers in your town) was 100 and day 6 attack was 200, they are both increased by 2 x 15% (should be 2 x 7,5% to be correct).

Leaving one or several red tortured souls outside or inside in bank, chests or rucksacks will increase the size of the attack by 7,5% for each red tortured soul left not purified over night. It will be visible by an increased change of all attack estimation numbers from the 30% to 100% and it will increase the size of the actual attack.
