Jean-Ralphio

Zombies are a uniquely American Monster. They were first envisioned in Haitian voodoo but they were more solitary slaves of a witch doctor then hordes of hungry undead.

The modern zombie was shaped in old movies starting with White Zombie (1932) but that movie still followed a more traditional voodoo explanation. The concept really took off with Night of the Living Dead(1968 ).

In my opinion though, Zombies really found their perfect medium in video games. Slow easy targets are fun to pick off.

So I like zombies because they are a relatively new monster, and while born in other cultures have really matured in American cinema. I'm a big movie buff (you can follow me on twitter if you like @movielesson if your into that sort of thing) so it's cool that movies have so recently created such a classic monster.

Zombies as an idea are interesting because they are less about the monster and more about societies response to a lack of control. If you really think about it: who is the actual enemy in Die2nite? is it the horde at midnight? is it the zombies in the waste land? No those are the obstacles in die2nite the real enemy is your neighbor, the guy who doesn't put the saw back in the bank, that newb who builds the small cafe EVERY SINGLE DAY!
And honestly the arch nemesis in Die2Nite is the people who don't read the forums.

Zombies are about the inability to organize and communicate. Because in all zombie media if the living could simply organize and communicate right away the undead wouldn't stand a chance.

So thats my really long essay on why zombies are so lovable
