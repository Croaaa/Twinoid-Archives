Lerajie

Unfortunately Workshop, the more quality you want the more programs you'll have to use.

If totally new to editing then stick to something simple like Windows Movie Maker, very easy to pick up. If you have the time then you can learn more advanced programs like Sony Vegas and get better results.

For audio editing as the others have said, Audacity is very handy.

I use Cam Studio to record my screen (fraps for actual game engines) but Cam Studio can be annoying to set up for first time use.

Other programs may also be needed like gimp for image editing or video converters to get the right formats after capturing.

My advice would be to start small and get a feel for making videos. Use Windows Movie Maker. It's very simple but with limitations. I've also been wanting to produce a video for the new season, just not getting around to doing it >_<
