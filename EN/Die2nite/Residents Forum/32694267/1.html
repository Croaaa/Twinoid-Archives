Hacksaw

Griefers or not, selfish players or not, random towns are always going to be short lived and disorganized messes. You gotta change your game up when you're in a random town.

When I join a meta town, I know for the most part that people are going to be on the same page and shit that needs be doing will be done. It still doesn't stop me from scavenging the bank and/or a few close zones for a decoration or two those last few days before camping phase, but for the most part most distinction-giving and life saving items are given to the town for either raffle or for those who need them to use on the complex missions that are often run.

In meta towns, animals are brought to the town to wait for the butcher, hen house and any other building that needs them. In random towns, those buildings usually won't be found, let alone built, so I usually chuck animals at the first zombie I see.

In meta towns, if I find a manbag, I'll drop it in the bank, sometimes to never see again, but I know it's gone for a good cause. In random towns, I'll keep the first manbag I find and never mention I have it.

Boosters are in short supply in random towns. You can bet if I find one, I'm most likely going to hold onto it. I'm also just as happy to camp the shady bar for a few bottles to chug over the next few days as well.

In random towns, I'm not interested in wrecking my brain trying to survive in town. But even if I do have distinctions in mind, and am just as happy to be dead by weeks end than see day 8, I still keep what's good for the town in perspective. I might bring in a decoration item one day, but it'll be among other necessary resources for the town (and almost never in the first few days, since I'm good at living longer than that anyways ). Usually I won't take any resources form the bank (including drugs, booze and other boosters) unless it's to help clear and improve a campsite in range, as the blueprint will certainly help the town (not likely, but they still try anyways ) and, as a survivalist, I have good odds at surviving.

I find the biggest difference between a selfish player/distinction hunter and a griefer is a griefer never has the town in mind. Even the most selfish of distinction hunters has to know a little slow and steady does win this race. After all, you gotta survive the day (before day 5) to keep your distinctions from that day. And usually, by day 5, you have a pretty good idea on how well your random town is (or isn't) going to do. But you gotta chance your game up. You're odds of surviving the long haul are low. Find other goals outside of just surviving. And for god's sake, get out there and scavenge. And log in more than once a day.
