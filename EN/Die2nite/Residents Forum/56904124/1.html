There are a bunch of thoughts about Colosseum, divided into 3 parts: Past, Present and Future of the game. I am mostly writing it to structure my own thoughts, because I abandoned the game for 1,5 years and forgot what am I doing and why. You don’t even have to read this. Though if you are interested in the development of this game – it will really help if you will read this and maybe help come with ideas or something else. Also there will be a lot of surprises for you So lets start.

Past of the Colosseum

I never thought that Chaos Colosseum will become a big thing, and still doubting it. First it was developed 6 years ago back in 2010, and was made just as a fun mode for Mafia to play just one time.

Back there I was playing forum Mafia a lot. You probably don’t know what is this game, so I will explain in a couple of words. Mafia is a “Party game” for 10 players. Each of them secretly receives one card with a role. 3 players become “Mafia”, organized criminal group, 1 player will be “Detective” and 6 others are just “Innocents” or “Citizens”. Game is divided into 2 phases: “Nightime” and “Daytime”. During “Daytime” everyone is discussing and voting on whom they think is mafia, and the player with most votes gets killed and removed from the game. During “Nightime” innocents fell asleep, mafia wakes up and together silently decides whom to kill. “Detective” wakes up after then and can “check” one of the players if he is mafia. The game goes until mafia kills all the innocents or innocents get rid of the mafia.

This is what called “Classic” Mafia. But there are thousands of different modes in this game. It can be played with more or less than 10 players, there can be another roles like doctor, maniac, there can be 2 groups of “Mafia” at the same time… I’ve heard about mafia for 50 players and mafia for just 2 players.

So I’ve played forum version of this game for years. There was a lot of game roles, but still the majority of the “town” was innocents without any special ability. And some players didn’t like to be innocents, so someone proposed “What if we make one game where everyone will have a role?”

And well, I entered a strange mood and in a couple of weeks designed such a game. Besides of it just making everyone murder and innocent at the same time it also solved most of the problems forum mafia always had, like you can’t prove your role, you can’t speak with other players outside of game topic and else. In my game all of this wasn’t an issue. And also it became a fantasy roleplay game with a good plot.

And it was pretty much the same game you can see right now, and it had even more stuff. There were 3 classes: warrior, mage and archer. Mages could summon creatures, curse, poison, launch fireballs. They had even more spells than they have right now. Turning mages onto elementals was also planned from the very beginning. Warriors had their badass shields, armor and elemental swords. Archer had 3 AP, could buy elemental and powerful arrows, but his attacks wouldn’t work if he would get attacked by his target.
Archer was a stupid class and was picked only once or twice, so I deleted it later.
The game didn’t even have a name, but moderator said me, that’s the thing I created is not even a Mafia anymore, so moved me to another board of the forum) And I made 3 or 4 games there. And it was total shit.

First of all, it was unbalanced as fuck. Protection was lower than attack, so people only attacked each other and games ended in 3-4 days. I couldn’t explain rules normally and just made an MS Word document with 20 pages of text that no one read. The game was so bulky, so I had to spend 3-4 hours to calculate the results of one day, and still made a lot of mistakes. People were not much interested and kept forgetting they are playing, so I had to text everyone to send me their actions. It was only a deathmatch games, so one group of players just made a huge team of 6 or 7 and dominated every time. But well, there was also a fun moment when in game 2 a zombie with 1 HP won the game =)

During these first games I made several contests for in-game gold, just asking people for a new ideas for the game. This is how the name “Chaos Colosseum” and Death Totalizator appeared. And after this 3-4 games people didn’t sign up anymore.

Some time later this forum died and I moved to another. Year later this new forum died too. I kept wondering through Russian forums playing mafia and made attempts to organize Chaos Colosseum again. I managed to make one or two more games. I remember the last one.

Somewhere in 2012 I decided to make game more simple for both players and myself and deleted mages and archers from the game, leaving only warriors. Also I made a spreadsheet to calculate almost every result. Only 4 players joined, but I still made it a game. This went bad and so I abandoned the game for years.

I will not tell a story how I came to die2nite and found myself in Oozing Inn meta. I’ve played it something near a year, and we always played some forum games in our towns to take the time, while zombies were sieging our walls. Accidentally I remembered about this game and proposed people to play. This happened back in 2015.

So I translated it into English. This time I was more experienced, so I made the game simple with warriors as the only class. I made a good MS Excel spreadsheet calculating everything. Used google spreadsheet: previously when I made old games there were no such a thing. Purposefully deleted long explanations of the rules, leaving only the basics: its better to explain contentious issues on their appearance than freak people with the huge walls of texts. And also used my skills to make roleplays.

And it worked. The game went good, someone even gave me MT passes to award the winner (back there I couldn’t afford em myself). So in next town I made a new game, adding mages, improved my Excel spreadsheet to calculate mages stuff. And it went amazing! Game 2 was really cool with gameplay and people roleplaying. So I’ve decided to keep it going and moved on resident’s forums to make game 3 for bigger audience. And well, it went through big troubles. There was a huge conflict between players, but somehow I finished the game and went for new ones.

During this three games Daxio proposed me to make a board game or PC game from it. First I thought that none if this is possible, but she insisted, and after several days I found a way how to move all the bulky calculations into the board version. So I’ve made plans, decided what else I need to add into the game to make it complete and every next game made with new updates and rebalancing, taking a baby-steps to my goal. So I made a “Cooperate teams”, which I realized is important during the first games. Added a lot of team actions. Made a roleplay world, that made a game into a continuous story, not only a bunch of names and numbers. And kept increasing my Excel spreadsheet.

No, seriously, I’ve created a monster from this spreadsheet) It have near a thousand columns making calculations for me. I just have to put information about people’s actions inside, which takes not more than 10 minutes, and I am receiving all the results – the amount of damage, number of health, received gold and fury, everything you can see in Google spreadsheet: I only have to copy it there. But it’s still not any near to be finished and working well. For example, it still can’t calculate the damage transfer from creature to it’s owner when creature dies. Elementals and team actions are not added. After a long break I can’t so easily understand everything there, and the game surely needs some better program than MS Excel. But for now I will keep working on it and improving. Here how it looks like.

Day 1: End of Monday

the one thing i can say is that elementals are too strong the way they are now, but there are many ways to counter that. i'm sure you'll have a few ideas. i like the one about elemental form not being permanent, but permanent transformation is obviously easier to track.

some ideas:
don't give fixed hp, give the mage a bonus to his current hp. going form 2 hp to 17 is massive, simply gaining 7? means you are still in the fight (and gain new abilities). it would still be nice to have, but not a gamechanger in itself. maybe tie the hp of the elemental to the mana the mage has left somehow? i think that would be too complicated for such a game.

more spells needed to turn. 3 elemental rounds in a row should go, it's way too quickly and easy to accomplish. (on the other hand, it "should" cause everyone to attack any mage that could turn, so maybe the mechanic is sound and some of the players... didn't realise how strong elementals were?) more testing might be needed before anything is changed.

you already mentioned that the way elemental attacks work on other elements should be retained once in elemental form. fire > light > darkness > fire

also, they should retain their "personality". giving the light elemental the best attack seemed weird. i'd give them all 8 points to divide.
light at3 def5, darkness, at4 def4, fire at5 def3

for a third class i'd recommend a thief. the game already has a priest of sorts (a light mage), to get the basic 4 you'd need a thief of some kind. a thief's special mechanic could be traps. take curses and poison away from mages (darkness should go full necromancy - healthdrain, zombies, fear) and make thiefs a potential debuff class with stuff like caltrops, smokebombs or stuff like that. give them weak attacks that ignore armor and let them steal money (or even items?!).

but beware! adding a third class means you need... *counts fingers, gives up* a huge number of new team abilities!

sorry, can't help you with illustrations. but i'm sure you'll find plenty of talented people.

2 person teams might look great on paper (and might be necessary for a boardgame-version, but the (ahem) chaotic nature of the game (especially the first turns) would make that seem unviable. teams would be down to 1 player after the first turn very often. i don't see how that would not turn into "everyone uses their team ability on turn 1, then we see what happens". some might enjoy that. i wouldn't.

add a game mode where one player controls all the members of their team, then the number of teams is irrelevant. removes the cooperative part of the game, but makes the game playable by as few as 3 people.

don't spend too much time thinking about a board game version or an online version at this point. you've got a solid core. improve what you have, experiment a bit. teach others to be gamemasters so you can get more games run for more feedback. what the game needs to to become ready to think about publishing is a bit more time.

try to find as much information about small publishers or self-publishing. there's a lot out there, i'm sure. i might be able to get into contact with a few game designers and i''m sure they can point you in to right direction. figure out what you want to do exactly and then start wracking your brain.
