Enfilade

There is an unlockable skill list for each character (including a mush-only list) that requires experience to unlock.

  • Gold mode earns XP faster.
  • Gold mode players can use to four skills, wheras silver (anyone who payed once) gets two, and bronze (free) gets one.

Each character gets three inventory slots, two of which are the radio (which gives access to the shipwide chat) and the tracker (map and person locator).

  • Gold mode combines the two items into a single-slot item.

Edit: Oh, and gold players also get +2 max HP and morale.

Rather than simply allowing ghouls to eat other citizens, perhaps add the option of nibbling another citizen (uses 1AP, and can only be done once each day by any ghoul, and only once each day to any human). After a few nibbles (4 in distant towns, 3 in hardcore), a human citizen becomes a ghoul. If there is anyone besides the ghoul and human victim, it will appear in the zone log, or if it happens in town at all, it will appear the the Great Register (subject to tampering like any other entry). Every ghoul can see who all the other ghouls are, but the humans are oblivious. (Poor, oblivious humans.)

Also, the dynamic surrounding punching a ghoul would have to be changed in order to prevent the humans from randomly punching everyone on Day 1 to kill the ghouls. Instead of instant death to ghouls, every punch should guarantee a wound to both the victim and assailant (eye/head wound to victim, and an arm wound to the assailant), but a ghoul's max AP would not be affected by the wound. That and the relative rarity of bandages should be enough of a deterrent to prevent random attacks to kill the ghouls early.

"But how would a human kill a ghoul?"
The same way you would kill a zombie - any weapon could be used against any citizen at an AP cost (1AP for water weapons or the cell phone, 2AP for blades of any sort, 3AP for any heavy item).

There could be ghoul-specific honors/distinctions/titles, such as for preventing a town from reaching Day 10, or the number of humans converted to ghouls. Just like a game of Mush ends if the only crew left alive are Mush, if the only citizens left alive are ghouls, then the town ends.

This could go places, but we must also keep in mind that the MT crew are responsible for a large number of games (and mini-games) in several different languages, so any drastic changes like this will likely take several seasons to properly code and test.

To get to eden, you need to find pieces of star map. They're most commonly found in Cristalite zones and occasionally from Intelligent Life.

Sure, there's AP required to do the research and presumably some small amount to calculate the location (maybe someone who's played the French version can confirm... no one's found the third piece in our beta yet), but it's not out of reach for any active crew that's even somewhat working together.
