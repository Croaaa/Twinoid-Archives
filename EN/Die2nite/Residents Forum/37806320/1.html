rumataI don't see the night-time bonus. Is that only applied in UFC?

Since it isn't normally a part of Die2Nite I didn't feel the need to add it in here. Those in the UFC will just have to be mindful to add +10% to their chances when camping at night, or can offset the value by adding +2 camping items to the calculator.

RulesyCool. Was using Tamle's one, what are the differences?

Two most notable differences are a change in the layout and a few more fields added in to more finely tune an individual's chance of survival.

A major differences I'm sure many will notice is some of the modifiers used are different than what tamle's calculator uses, for instance, most of the distance modifiers are 5% higher.

In a brief conversation I had with him maybe a year or so ago he told me he forced all his modifiers to be expressed in terms of normal upgrades (meaning plus or minus 5%). Using the power of the survivalist's 100% chance to camp and meticulous data acquisition to find the exact percent bonus or penalty to each given modifier by first recording the zone information and then tweaking the possible modifier (by as little as 1% if need be) until it held true over several data sets.

My two assumptions in data collecting is that all modifiers are integer values and the in-game help manual is actually correct when it stated "decent" chances cap at 90%. That and several data sets proving it takes a minimum of 18 normal upgrades to go from "hee haw" to "optimal" all the values used in my calculator were derived.

And for anyone who might ask how the Zone Upgrade Calculator can produce a decimal value, it's my belief that the game simply adds +5 to an upgrade variable for each normal upgrade made, or +9 in the case of DO's (and probably plywood, but haven't had a chance to test plywood yet), and checks to make sure this variable is less than 50 before letting you make the upgrade (and kicking back the message about being unable to upgrade the zone if that value is greater than or equal to 50).

The Zone Upgrade Calculator and it's derived value is used as an intermediate step designed to serve as an aid in counting how much AP will be needed on making normal upgrades and installing DO's into the zone (the final output in the calculator proper will still end up as an integer value). Initially I had them as their own fields inside the calculator itself, but decided to tinker with expanding to a derived value to be able to more easily take into account upgrades made the previous day and the degrade made after every attack.

I expect the degradation and residual modifiers won't be well utilized by most players, but for meta's especially during end game, when the same sights are camped several days in a row, it might be useful to more accurately account for residual upgrades still on the zone to keep an easy count on how many normal upgrades and/or DO's will need to be installed on the following day to maximize the worst camper's chances.
