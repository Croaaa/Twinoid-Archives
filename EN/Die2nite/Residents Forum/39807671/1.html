This is a Guide for all players who are interested in Playing Organized distant towns (not for HC) in a team working atmosphere and wish to lead their towns to a higher ranking spot than they usually reach in the seasonal leaderboard along with breaking their personal record of surviving days, but they lack the experience and knowledge of all Strategies used to optimize their team´s performance.

All informations in this thread are of course for the entire community BUT the main Goal of posting this is not to open a pointless Discussion about how to play the game whether in a meta or any other way known by anyone reading this! there are probably 100 ways to play the game and this one example of Organized play is simply one of them from my personal point of view and based on the main goal of surviving for as long as possible and rank as high as possible. so in more specific words this thread is more meant for all who are unfamiliar with meta play or they do have a meta but they have no clue how to play organized or how to optimize their own organized Play.

This Guide will provide 3 deiffrent Sources of Informations of explained strategies and Tips which will help you start with a team of about 30 to 40 players who all share the same interest of playing Organized and have the goal to rank a town high enough to stay in the leaderboard ranking by the end of the season.

    • Holding specific BPs Strategies:
      first of all Common BPs are the only type of BPs which must be opened immediately after bringing them home.
      now about other types of BPs:
      this is one of the most important Strategy for a successful Town. so you have to make sure it gets done correctly:
      you must hold all BPs you find from Camping , in Worn Leather Bag, Architect´s Chest or Sealed Architect´s Chest , (short WLB, AC and SAC) untill you will find the Explorable Ruin, search it, deplete it and open Most BPs you find inside it. (here not all explorable Ruin BPs should be opened immediately, there are special cases mentioned below)
      now you do this because the BPs from the Explorable Ruin can be Duplicates to the already opened BPs from Camping, WLB, AC or SAC and therefore they will be useless by not unlocking any projects and cost you one less project if you open them after opening the BPs you find from camping , WLB, AC or SAC.
      the normal BPs (from Camping, WLB, AC or SAC) on the other hand can not be duplicate for other BPs and will always unlock a new Projct in the Construction Site till you unlock them all along with all "Parent Projects" which have "Child Projects". and that is why you should hold the Normal BPs in the bank and never open them till you open most BPs from the Explorable Ruin.
      now to the 2 special cases:
      • 1 - if you wont unlock Vegi Plot (common BP) early which is the "Parent Project" for both "child Projects" Ferilizers (rare BP) and Grapeboom (uncommon BP) then you should hold about 2 normal rare BPs and 2 uncommon for as long as needed till you unlock Vegi Plot and then open those 4 BPs because you can not unluck any Child Project if you didn´t unlock their Parent Project first.
      • 2 - depending on the type of explorable ruin you will find on the map you may also need to hold uncommon BPs from ruins after you get all from its pool unlocked except the child projects under the "parent project" Guard Tower (Uncommon BP).
        i will give a small example in here: if you have a Hotel Explorable Ruin then the list of all Uncommon BPs you can get from it are:
        Animal Dump , Food Dump , Frat House , Wood Dump , Metal Dump , Guerilla Traps , Swedish Workshop.
        now the first 6 Projects are all not child projects of Guardtower, so once you find the first 6 uncommon BPs from the explorable ruin and open them all you will 100% unlock those 6 projects. BUT the last 7th project (Swedish Workshop) is a "child Project" of the "Parent Project "GuardTower" (also Uncommon BP) . so when you reach this point after unlocking the first 6 uncommon BPs from the explorable ruin and then find a 7th uncommon BP from the ruin you have to first HOLD this 7th uncommon BP from the explorable ruin and open all normal uncommon BPs you have from "Camping and Worn Leather Bags" till you unlock "Guard Tower" and only after that the 7th uncommon BP from the ruin you have been holding will unlock the "Swedish Workshop".
        of course the same should be done with all 3 types of Explorable ruins and you can find a full list of which projects you can/will get from each ruin in here: Explorable Ruins BP List
      • there is one last special case for Small cemetary (common BP) being a parent project for the child project "Coffin Catapult" which is a (very rare BP) but there is no smart way to deal with it because it involves Holding common BPs to avoid unlocking Small cemetary to avoid discovering Coffin Catapult and instead get a useful Very rare BP like Builder´s Marchant... if your town did manage to unlock "Vegi Plot" along with "central cafeteria" as early as possible then you could hold all common BPs till you have opened all very rare BPs from the explorable ruin and normal BPs.

      also if the town has Radius Radar Beacons then all scouts should take a manbag or a trolley to be able to carry the radius and use it when they sense a building they can´t walk into or simply use it at the farthest zone they will reach to make the Survivalist Expoeditions easier.

    • Survivalists Plans (Day 2 to 5)
      each Organized town should have between 7 and 9 survivalists.
      here is the main plan which has 3 goals:
      • open the entire map to find all 5 Architect Chests + 2 Sealed Architect Chests.
      • camp all 20 buildings but first give priority to only those in 10 km range or farther at first to get all rare BPs first which can be game changing if opened early shortly after the explorable ruin gets depleted.
      • make the Zoo (if your team chooses to try to make one) by clearing all the zed in the entire map except one Corner which will be decided later on day 4 when the watchtower lvl 3 vote uncover the entire 10 km range. (but as always each map has its location and circumstances)
    • now here is a detailed Guide of what this class is supposed to do to accomplish all these 3 Goals:
      • each team of 2 Survivalist must wait for the 3 to 4 Scout routes and once they are finished 3 Survivalists teams can take all the Supplies they need to walk to their assigned campsites and camp them along with some zed clearing if weapons and enough ammo is available.
      • only 3 teams of 2 survs in each team will/should go camp on day 2 and the last team will keep scavenging inside the Diamond as usual and wait till a decent weapon, like mower, machete , Laser Pointer or MK2 will show up and be found. once you will find one of those weapons the 4th survivalists team or just one signle survivalist will go help one of the 3 Survivalists teams which needs help the most.
      • each team of 2 partners will take:
        • trolley or the extra manbags if you have enough (after the scouts take the needed manbags) (here try to sort the selfish play instinct by beginners to learn to never hold containors and always Share them)
        • pretty much all the weapons you have in the bank, each team should have one loaded Water Pistol, 1 EWB (Exploding Water Bomb) and back up water to use for ammo. then fill the few empty spots left with other weapons like Fatcats, hurling sticks, serrated knifes
        • if you manage to find bandages one team should take one bandage + as many furious kittens available and can be carried.
        • and last of course take your food (6 AP food unless you need that extra AP for clearing debris) and if your trip to first campsite is far or headed to a DEEP buried building take one drug and one alcohol.
      • this may seem like alot to carry but i am listing those items in general and 2 partners will be able to carry 12 to 14 items easily...
      • when you head to campsite both survs in each team must make sure to walk diffrent but parallel routes next to each other to open new unvisited zones on their way to campsite and help each other if someone gets trapped and run out of ammo or run into an unexpected building.
      • few hours before arrgh after one of the 2 survs in each team can make sure optimal camping chance is garanteed then him/her or the partner MUST try to clear all zeds around the campsite with the rest of ammo still available if any is left. (here be smart and dont get greedy with breakable weapons But still don´t skip the clearing if you still have ammo left and 3 camera flashes)
      • next day all surv teams will get supply drops from town at specific zones and give BPs + goodies they found to the dropper and then carry on doing the same till the entire map is explored, all 7 architect chests are found and the zoo is made.

    • Dividing Daily Tasks from day 2 till End Game:
      generaly you need to keep scavenging with big numbers in the first 5 days because the Survivalists Expeditions and making the zoo depends on it.
      • Day 2: you will have 4 scouts and 6 survivalists outside and that leaves 30. and 27 to 28 of those 30 should all continue scavenging inside the 9 AP Diamond and get the max out of all replenished zones early.
        the 2 to 3 who will stay inside can build if guitar hasn´t been built yet Or wait till last 30 minutes before the attack to Rescue people.
      • Day 3: you will have 6 survivalists camping outside and then send about 3 to 4 Supply Drops using 3 to 4 heroes with Heroic Returns. and then about 3 to 5 Heroes or BiA heroes on clearing zeds missions possibly using Heroic Returns as well using any decent weapon the town has found and built. then when all this is finished you send the last team of 2 survivalist to join the expeditions with that decent weapon. then from the 24 rest of citizens about 20 should continue scavenging inside the diamond OR working on the explorable Ruin if located, and 4 builders stay inside to repair Repair-kits and weqapons + build what is needed.
      • Day 4: you will have 8 Survivalists camping outside and then send 3 to 4 Supply Drops for them, about 4 to 5 more will go on clearing zeds missions possibly using Heroic Returns. the rest of 18 will keep scavenging and working on the ruin and 6 builders can repair RKs and weapons + build.
      • Day 5: you will have 8 Survivalists who will either Heroic Return home or Camp one last time, only 1 or 2 supply drop will be needed if at all. that leaves about 4 Tamers on clearing zeds missions, 18 ascavengers and 6 builders.
      • Day 6 to End Game: from now on the numbers of how to divide tasks among the citizens is going to be heavily dependend on when to unlock and build Searchtower and how the daily Build Plans will look like. But in general you must keep scavenging with the highest numbers you can afford. and here keep in mind that you should never start rationing water and keep drinking daily for all scavengers and builders and to do so you have to build all water projects you have unlocked or if badly needed vote few Pump Votes. same with eating food daily , try to build Apple Tree, Hennhouse and Vegi Plot + Fertilizers and once you do that you will be able to let everyone eat everyday no matter what.

  • End Game: (Part 2)
  • About classes:
    • Tamers: are the best classes to collect souls so those should camp in 4 diffrent locations to collect as many souls as possible before they die.
    • Guardians: have extra 1 home defence in town so they should stay inside (not a must but prefered).
    • Survivalists: all should have camped 3 to 5 times so they wont have more than 3 to 4 days camping with optimal chances to survive 100% (mainly depends on having Pro-camper unlocked). so it is best for Survs to camp for as long as they get Optimal chances then get back inside when needed to fill out the 10 inside spots or just keep camping till they die if the town gets devastated before they were needed inside.
    • Residents: those who have been BiAed as Scouts/tamers will be needed to camp outside to collect souls if you will still have enough Heroes to BiA a hero day. all other Residents also should camp first because they can not upgrade their homes with Fences, Reinforcments and more storage unless they get a BiA hero day but when the time comes for filling the 10 spots inside they should return back home regardless.
    • Scouts: the one full time scout must camp in the nearest campsite to the ZOO if one has been built , otherwise if there is not zoo then its best to let the scout camp in the nuclear bunker one of the best campsites in the map to garantee getting souls from zones heavily infested with zeds.
    • Back Up Shaman: each Town should have a main shaman who will stay inside during End game and the back up shaman will go camp outside for as long as possible to have a back up Plan in case very bad Luck happens and the main shaman inside dies from unlucky RNG . if all goes to plan the back Up shaman should keep camping till the time comes for him/her to suicide so the main shaman can collect his/her soul which should be the 39th soul.
  • About Home Defence:
    • all 10 inside must take all DOs from the bank and put them in their chests BUT only after the shaman gets as many DOs as he/she can fit in his/her chest. so whenever the shaman gets more storage upgraded one of those inside must send him/her a DO.
      keep in mind it is not important for ranking if other classes survive one or 2 more days but it will be a disaster if the shaman dies and you don´t collect all remaining souls.
    • All 10 inside must build reinforcment lvl 1 and install a fence for 4 home points using (chain + metal Support)
    • All Makeshift Barricades, Iron Chests, BOxes and rest of Wire Mesh not used in Constructions Must be collected from the desert wherever they are and only the main shaman must use them all to get the highest home defence which can be between 75 and 105 depending on how well the town worked for this Goal.
