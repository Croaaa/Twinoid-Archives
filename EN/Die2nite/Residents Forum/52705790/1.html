here is how Spread works:
https://gyazo.com/474cb624117ac31b279...


  • so basicly if you have a zone that is zed free and completely surrounded with 8 adjacent zones full of zeds then theoreticly there could be 8 zeds there over night, (but only thoereticly) if there are only 4 adjacent zones to it then next day you could get 4 zeds.
    from my experience there is however a limit to the spread to a completely zed-free zone, and that is 4 or 5 max.

    and also keep in mind it is still RNG so it is totaly random if all or some or even none of the adjacent zones will cause a spread.

    it is easy to test for confirmation when you go open a path to a campsite and kill down to zero and in 2 days you go back on the same path (2 days because on the first day most of the zeds which will spread will die from grief, but the second day that zone will be surrounded with at least 7 adjacent zones full of zeds. and the adds of finding less than 3 zeds there is very low)
  • about zeds dying from grief : for each 2 zeds you kill in each zone one zed left in that zone will die from grief. BUT Spread happens before Grief , SO that zed could spread to adjacent zones then die from grief.
  • if you kill more than 2 zeds in a zone and next day zeds spread from adjacent zones to it they do successfuly spread BUT then they die from grief because last day 2 zeds were killed in that zone and they will cause any zeds present in that zone before or after the new spread to die from grief.
  • a zone that has at least one zed in it and completely surrounded with 8 other adjacent zones which all are not zed-free can only get 1 additional zed over nighti hope this could help
