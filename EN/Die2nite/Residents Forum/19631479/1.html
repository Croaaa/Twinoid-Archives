There is safety in numbers, whether it be a meta or a good coalition.

Band together in groups of 8 to 10 while you are new to the game. That way you can defend against griefers with a quick shun from your group.

Always assume there is someone from a Meta in your random town looking for promising players. It's how most meta's recruit. Play your best game and stay active in forums, eve ntually someone will notice you. My group picked up 2 new players in our last random town. Both worked out beautifully. One was invited into an allied meta and the other is part of our double coalition.

Remember, you never know when someone is looking and you'll only get chances every so often. Always play your best game if you want to move up to meta play.

Luceid: there is difference between meta and meta play.
Playstyle might change with towns as well, if your meta has a different goal set.

For example, we never ration water in the Oozing Inn. Never tell the players what class to play, or where to go step-by-step. Sure, it won't be as effective as if we did, but it keeps the fun aspect of the game for every member, and requires everyone to develop good d2n skills for themselves.
This way we have 40 thinking individuals who like to play together under a loose coordination. It is working pretty well for us I'd say. We just have to set our goals accordingly. Currently holding the 1st spot in distant towns, but each of us knows, that this wasn't nearly a perfect town, so we don't keep our hopes up too much.

And you can easily avoid boredom in any organized town if you participate in the organizing.
