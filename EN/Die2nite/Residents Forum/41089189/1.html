[ ] Build 1 Battery Launcher

[ ] Score 250 decoration points
[ ] Pillage 150 items from dead citizens
[ ] Excavate 100 piles of dirt and debris
[ ] Product test 50 unlabled drugs
[ ] Collect 20 souls
[ ] Log 15 total extreme expeditions
[ ] Survive 10 days zen camping
[ ] Build 7 total wonders
[ ] Find Hacksaw (special rules)

Lightning Round (10 points per task)

[ ] Consume 10 Alcoholic beverages
[ ] Butcher 10 animals
[ ] Perform 10 acts of Cannibalism
[ ] Camp 10 times
[ ] Log 10 total Distant Discoveries
[ ] Construct 10 Extreme Projects
[ ] Perform 10 acts of Masochism
[ ] Earn 10 total Mystic Points
[ ] Explore 10 total Ruins
[ ] Survive 10 nights as a Watchman

Special bonus
Earn 10 points for every 10 days survived in a single town

Finding Hacksaw special rulesEdit: On second thought, this was a convoluted idea, as I'm going to want to coordinate with people to get into wonderhunt towns myself.

Usual rules for hunting people.

To sign up post so in this thread, and if you would be so kind as to provide a screen shot of your soul page at the start of season 10 that would help greatly. I must be able to clearly read all applicable distinction numbers in the screen shot (or clearly see that they are absent for those you don't have any points in); If need be, you can supply two screen shots or use some constructive photoshopping.

Event will begin upon launch of season 10 and will end at attack time New Year's Eve or when season 10 ends, whichever comes first. If you are in the middle of a town, you will be granted no more than 7 days to finish said town to receive credit for distinctions earned within that town.

Winner will be the one who scores the most points.
In case of a tie, those who score more primary goals will take priority slot.
In case of a tie there those who score more Bonus goals will take priority.
In case of a tie THERE, I'll write your names on a sheet of paper, stick it in a hat, and whoever's name I pull out will be the winner.

I'm counting a total of 33 projects under Foundations, and I know at least one of them is a temporary building that can be built fairly easily day after day. True many of the projects need blueprints to unlock and many of them usually won't be built in most towns, but there's enough projects of varying degrees of quality and ease of building that only the worst of towns won't be able to figure out how to get a good chunk out of.

Much like the masochism and cannibal distinctions you'd have to have both a bit of luck and maybe a few people dedicated to farming them out to gain that headway, but it's still possible if a large enough group gets together. Same with the Mystic points, since a good wave of newbie deaths means you could get upwards 30 ticks for 10 people inside of a week.

Also, if I remember correctly, Butcher counts every time an action you perform results in the death of an animal critter, to include using animals to kill zombies, so can be easy to rack up a ton of ticks in a couple days.

Anyways, Alcohol and Cannibalism I'm definitely keeping because 1: I want to encourage drinking and eating human flesh, and 2: part of the idea is to have some goals promote selfish play while there are other goals (especially the big point items) that encourage working together. Also tried for a good mix to support going out and scavenging while also giving a fair chance to those that stay inside and build.

Overall, going back to the original 1 point per tick on the Lighting Round items is probably best. IF a good farming of a couple of items does give a bunch of points, it should still be fairly overshadowed with the big ticket items considering those who get them should also still manage to rack up a good chunk of bonus points to balance out. At least, in theory, I've been wrong before.

Also added a special goal of awarding 10 points for every 10 total days survived in a single town (so if you survive 20 days in one town you'll get 20 total points) to promote survival over massive distinction hunting.

I also reduced the number of Extreme Expedition ticks needed for it's bonus points.

While I'm not a huge fan of hunting other people, I won't deny the right mix of people would promote further community involvement.
