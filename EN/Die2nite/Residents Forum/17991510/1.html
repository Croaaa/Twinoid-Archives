It's time for another MMJ!

Jump planned for: Tuesday, November 20th at 23:45 game time!

This may be Wednesday in some time zones. To see the time in your time zone, click here.

What is MMJ?

MMJ stands for "Mega Meta Jump" and is a mass jump, where many players jump together at the same time. The goal is to create towns with a large number of active citizens who are interested in working together to do the best they can. MMJ towns are typically a mix of experienced players as well as newer players who are eager to learn.

Why participate in MMJ?

MMJs are a great way to meet new players and make friends! If you are new to the game, MMJs are a nice place to connect with other active players and can be an introduction to a more competitive Die2nite experience. Now that there is no Meta Cap, many experienced players will be jumping with Metas throughout the season but MMJs will still continue and we hope that the experienced players will still participate in an MMJ town or two each season in order to play with those outside their Metas as well as to guide newer players.

Who can participate?

MMJs are open to all players who are interested. The jump is just as successful as the participants make it!

Will my MMJ town make the rankings?

There is no guarantee of making the rankings by participating in MMJ, but your town is what you and the others who land in your town make of it! Many MMJ towns do end up organizing toward the goal of ranking on the leaderboard, some with good success!

Please also keep in mind that due to the nature of the jump, there is also no guarantee of ending up in a town full of other MMJ participants, depending on how many players take part. When the jump occurs there are usually some towns that are already partially full so you might end up in one of those towns, or you may end up jumping towards the end of the jump and ending up in a town that does not completely fill up. This doesnâ€™t mean your town cannot end up doing well or being a fun time either, but this is just a heads up to know what to expect!

How do I participate?

If you are available to jump: You do not need to sign up but you are encouraged to so we can later have a better idea of how many participants are in each town. If you are willing to drag off-line participants with you in a coalition, then please do sign up and include your email so we can contact you as a jumper. Then on the jump day just look for the jump announcements and jump into a random town!

You are also free to jump with your own coalition/friends!

If you are not available to jump: If you cannot be online to jump for yourself or have fewer than 100 soul points, then you will need to be in a coalition. If you have a friend who is participating, feel free to coalition up and have your friend jump for you. If you do not have someone who can jump for you, you will need to sign up so that we can assign you to a coalition. Please leave any coalition you are in right away so that you can receive an invitation.

Sign-ups: http://tinyurl.com/s6mmjform


Contact information and more

After signing up, please be patient for coalition postings. An announcement in this thread will be made with coalitions a few days before the jump so that people have time to invite and accept. Again, please make sure you leave current coalitions if you will need an invitation.

If you have questions or problems, stop by IRC, #d2n on Quakenet. Webchat can be found here: http://webchat.quakenet.org/?channels...

You can also message Annette, spacekadt, and myself on Twinoid. Please copy all three of us on any Twinoid messages since we are busy in varying degrees at different times so sending a message to all three will help make sure your question gets answered more quickly.

What follows is an account of what happened in the MMJ town of Greenish Desert.

The town began on an auspicious note. We had two deaths the first night, and remained unable to find a jaerpen table through 16 days.

To kick the nightmare off, a user (Who we will call C) invests a bunch of AP and resources into personal upgrades for his house. This is noticed and pointed out in the Complaints thread by a vigilant citizen. C has a hissy fit, and runs off with cyanide. Killing himself, he leaves the message "Seriously, this town sucked. My last random town was better, sorry, I really just couldn't be arsed."

These last words proved to be prophetic. Two days later, we saw the beginning of a dedicated griefing campaign initiated by two users (Who we will call A and B). It is important to note that we had no Jaerpen tables throughout the entire lifespan of the town. A realized that he could cause a lot of chaos by building useless buildings to sabotage the town's construction efforts by consuming resources.

It started with what could be viewed as an innocent mistake: Scarecrows.

This irritated the citizens who cared enough about the town to realize what had happened, but people moved on quickly.

Shortly afterwards, B begins his campaign. He sends a private message to our shaman, ranting and raving about how he is the only one who knows how to run the town and demanding that our shaman convert the souls immediately. Our shaman tells the rest of the town, but B does not realize this because he has not set foot in the IRC or posted in the forums since the town's inception. After an unfortunate accident by our shaman that resulted in his death, B would go on to create a thread where he called the rest of the town idiots for not listening to his ridiculous demands. This thread was rightfully met by scorn.

A few days later, A struck again. The town had just completed The Big Rebuild, and has totally covered the incoming zombie attack. A realizes he has the chance to build the Bonfire, consuming 5 planks that we need for our next project. This move incensed the town, as people realized that this was not a noobish mistake, but the act of an organized opposition of unknown size.

The war on planks continued. B decides to brazenly go to the workshop and openly convert them into patchwork beams, despite a town board posting telling him not to, and several threads in the forums, which he would later admit to having seen and blatantly ignored. Noticing this, I raised the issue in the IRC, and decided to file a single complaint. B, upon seeing that he had a complaint, decided to send an accusatory and insulting private message in my direction. This message reveals his belief that he expects me to be eternally obsequious to him because he was available to be online at a certain server time to make the jump.

I copy and paste this message into the IRC, and we laugh about it for a minute or two, and I decide to ignore the insults. However, another concerned citizen (who makes an appearance as Y later in the Town of Noobs image) decides to file another complaint against B, sending him an accompanying private message. We believed that the whole issue had blown over after 2 hours or so.

We were wrong. B carried an intense hatred of me with him.

Then, we unblock the Shower at the construction yard. Recognizing that the terrorists would attempt to build a worthless building, several citizens began hoarding the copper pipes to prevent its construction. We were also facing a tight water shortage at that time. A's greatest dream is now possible - waste valuable resources, and exacerbate the thirst that we were all feeling. A takes drugs and alcohol, goes into the world beyond to recover a pipe, and blitzes the construction of the shower.

An angry mob quickly materializes in the IRC. B is not aware of this, as he still hasn't found the channel. He wastes no time at all, creating a thread titled "Town of Noobs" just minutes after A finished construction of the Shower. This "coincidence" did not go unnoticed, nor did the question of where the final AP to complete the shower came from.

The Town of Noobs thread is full-on B rant. Every post is laced with profanity and personal attacks, as he blames everyone else in the town for its failure. This time, things were different. The honest, hardworking citizens of Greenish Desert had reached their breaking point. The mob descended on A and summarily banished him from the town.

B met the same fate not long after.

The shunnings complete, the town was safe from further griefing.

A at least had the good sense to do the honourable thing and kill himself. Using a capsule of cyanide he had been holding for an unknown period of time, he commits suicide. Everyone is happy.

B did not have the human decency to do the same. He continued to use his Town of Noobs thread to berate the rest of the Town, insulting them and making numerous fallacious arguments. B claimed that the town had not given him and A a chance to show that they were playing for the good of the town, followed by a number of equally hilarious and incorrect statements. The heavily redacted highlights of the seven page thread are included below.

Not content to allow his idiocy to be confined to a single thread, B decided to visit the broadcasting station of Trynt, who had been putting together a video series. This series is available on the Resident's forum for public viewing. B attacked Trynt, posting a number of unprovoked hateful and abusive messages.

B's posts in these two threads lead many, myself included, to come to the conclusion that he was either a troll or autistic. It is also evident that he only cared about hearing from those that supported him, as he openly declares that everyone but Z is overreacting.

Also of note is the part where he makes an effort to convince the town that he was drunk. It is clearly shown in the shunnings screenshot that he had removed the Vodka Marinostov from the bank.

I will offer an alternate title: How C Unbelievably Became the Third Most Hated Person in the town of Greenish Desert

So there you have it.
