Colette

Bah, that's not a wall of text. Too small. And I never mentioned that each town could have one and only one safehouse per map...

Anyway, the point of having special safehouses instead of buildings is to leave buildings untouched. You don't want to make them useless or cause interference with existing features. That's for several reasons. For example, I wouldn't want to mess up with the buildings' functionality. Buildings are just that, abandoned buildings. Most of them are different to each other, they contain supplies, provide cover for camping and have a blueprint to get if you do. Safehouses, on the other hand, are constructions made with protection in mind, like fallout shelters or something like that. They contain the supplies you expect to find in such a place, like a few cans of food and water. You can't camp in there, so you get no blueprints. It's basically an alternative to camping, not a replacement for camping.

Now, turning all buildings into fortresses sounds good at the start, but it isn't so good if you think about it. Let's take your example, the Old Hydraulic Pump. How in the world would you fortify an Hydraulic Pump? Even if you could, a good fortified building would need to be as inconspicuous as possible and an Hydraulic pump surrounded by bits of barbed wire would be like a lighthouse to guide zombies to it. Even making those nitpicky details aside, I wouldn't think it's a good idea to make multiple places available for people to stay outside.

About the attacks, I believe it should be a little different from just making them safer as distance increases. For example, we could have a camouflage option for the building's upgrade options, which increases the chances of being killed. Crowded safehouses should be easier to spot than lone ones, and staying there multiple times in a row should reduce your chances too. And when the town is destroyed, the hungry zombies that attacked the town will lurk around the desert looking for food, so survival chances are greatly reduced.

As I've said before, the purpose for this feature would be to provide a safer place to spend the night outside, not a replacement or alternative to the existing town. Just my opinion.
