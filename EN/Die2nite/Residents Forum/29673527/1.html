Die2Nite Haiku Off!

What a haiku is:
Haiku 俳句 is a very short form of Japanese poetry. The essence of haiku is "cutting" (kiru). This is often represented by the juxtaposition of two images or ideas and a kireji ("cutting word") between them, a kind of verbal punctuation mark which signals the moment of separation and colours the manner in which the juxtaposed elements are related. Traditional haiku consist of 17 on (also known as morae), in three phrases of 5, 7 and 5 on respectively. (from wikipedia)

"On" refers to phonetic sounds, or timing, but for our haiku we'll use syllables.


Rules:
Must be Die2Nite related.
Must consist of 17 syllables, in three phrases of 5, 7 and 5.
Must have your entries in before the deadline.
Each person can enter 6 times a week but not all in one post. Only one haiku per post and only re-post after someone else has, so never in a row.

Please, mark your entries with 1-6, this will really help the judges! If you've made a correction, use the same number and state that it's been corrected.
