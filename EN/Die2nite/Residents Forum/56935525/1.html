if you mean impact the way you play the game then almost all rare distinctions impact how you play.

  • if you aim for ranked town distinctions you will have to play organized in a team play mode. this mode requires the most effort and high activity and coordination between all town members.
  • if you aim for wonder project distinctions or atomic death distinctions you need to play less organized BUT organized enough to get the town to build a 300 AP project after you try hard enough to unlock it which involved finding very rare BPs which you can find from hidden sealed architect chests in the desert or from explorable ruins or from worn leather bags. this mode is mostly fun since it doesnt matter if some are not super active. just average effort can pay off.
  • if you aim for personal distinctions like what you do daily like what you eat, how many zeds you kill, what you repair or butcher, repair or build, join watch.... have decoration in home when you die, theft building guns that give rare distinctions and many many more, for all those you dont have to change your play mode much, just get each one when the chance present itself when you play each town no matter which goal it has as long it doesnt bother others and doesnt interfere with plans and resources needed for the town...
  • there are few more distinctions you can hunt specifically like entering the explorable ruin or searching a building far away from town outside 18+ km range... those just happen depending on experience since consequences of hunting them most of the time have risks of death or injury , so if you hunt those you need to know what you are doing and have back up plans to survive if things go wrong...
