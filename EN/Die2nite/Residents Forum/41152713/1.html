However, I realized that it's not just a courtesy. Honestly, it's the only thing that would make any sense for MT.
The german server will be shut down in a while, let's not kid ourselves about this. There can be no other outcome. There have already been threats of lawsuits simply because MT has been neglecting the server and without any kind of support, those lawsuits would have actual grounds in case the games are not playable anymore. So the only thing they could do once this happens, is to shut down the server to avoid these lawsuits. So far, these threats have been more in the style of a "meh, i could have done it better"-bitching and moaning that is bound to happen once a group of people are pissed off - especially if Germans are involved - so nothing will come of this. But I doubt MT is stupid enough to have actual lawsuits on their hands, so they'll shut it down for convenience's sake if not to simply be able to work on "more important things".

After all, the German server is a strain on their time and resources. If it would actually make enough money, we'd have had an admin long ago because they wouldn't be able to ignore it, but the way it is right now, the German server is not important enough for them to care about. So why keep it running?

As Muido said, MT has already lost a lot of customers with their disregard for the German server. A lot more will follow. Sometimes, they don't seem to realize that their games are just bait to get customers to get here, it needs to be good enough to attract people to come . The thing that is keeping most of the players, however, is the community. Let's be honest there's not that much variety in any of MTs games. Sure, the ruins pop up at different places, the hunters try to shoot down your ships at different cycles and the RNG is there to piss us off in every other game as well. But the thing that really changes, are the people. The 39, 15 or hundreds of others you play with, are what make these games enjoyable.

Honestly, the best course of action would have been an honest and upfront
"Hey, we know this sucks, but we have to shut down the German servers in 6 months. You are welcome to join the other servers and we will help you by reimbursing you for any hero days (or whatever) left by giving you MT passes and will reward your loyalty by integrating your account into the corresponding game version of your choice."

The outcome of this would have been simple:
People would be bitching and moaning about it. But they would soon realize that this was not just a way of appeasing the community till they can shank them some more, but an honest and efficient way of actually working with them.
On MTs side, on the other hand, they wouldn't be losing any money. The players already paid for their hero days, so giving out MT passes would really just be a way to not make them pay twice - once for the days already lost by shutting down the server and the second time for getting new hero days on other versions (which, arguably, is a way of thinking you have to get into, if you want your company to survive).
But on top of that, they would lose far fewer customers than they are doing with their current course of action. Sure, some people will not be able to take this opportunity, whether they simply don't want to or because the language barrier is too much of a hindrance is not really not really important here. There will be some, that will not enjoy this. But, as I said before, the German server will be shut down anyway and they would lose them regardless of what they do. This way, they might actually get to keep some of these, because, as stated above, the community is what manages to keep people here. So far, we have already seen D2N community and crows alike to actively welcome us here. If there were additional DV players to welcome the others and encourage the transition, I'm fairly certain even some of the nay-Sayers will continue to play here.
