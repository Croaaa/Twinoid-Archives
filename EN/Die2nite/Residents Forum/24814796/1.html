I think that a big part of the problem getting new players to stay, however, is more the nature of the game itself and the way it is played. When people think of a zombie-apocalypse game, many of them will think of shooting at hordes of zombies instead of hiding from them, and an action game instead of a strategic, social game. In this case, it's the quality of the players rather of the quantity that matters.

Unfortunately, what the game really needs is tweaking the requirements for towns so the less enthusiastic and participative players are barred from entering distant and HC towns, while still letting the better players in. It's unfortunate that many bad players can leech off the efforts of others and get the same benefits, and in the end they ruin the game experience for everyone else.
