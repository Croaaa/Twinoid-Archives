Dances with zombies

Just for fun!

The room was dark and filled with shadows. In the deepest shadow in the furtherest corner, behind the Christmas tree, 2 children sat huddled.

The little boy whispered to the girl “I don’t think Santa is coming this year”
His sister whispered back fiercely “Of course he is!!!! He comes every year!”

They sat there quietly for a few more minutes.

The boy whispered again “What if we have been too bad? You know mama said if we were too bad Santa wasn’t going to come”
His 4 y/o sister reached for his 6 y/o hand and squeezed lightly. “We’ve been good most of the time. That counts” she said with an emphatic nod of her head.

The candles on the mantle flickered and the 2 children huddled closer.

This wasn’t the first year they had tried to wait up for Santa. But in past years it was in warm beds. With heavy eyes. When they had begged to be allowed to stay up, their parents had said, “Of course you can! As long as you stay in bed with your eyes and mouths closed you can wait up all night.” Of course lying in a warm bed unable to speak and keeping your eyes closed led to quick slumber.

This year was different. They were waiting for Santa in the living room partially hidden behind the tree.

Their tummies growled at almost the same time. They looked at each other and giggled. They had not eaten since dinner and it was now almost midnight.

All of a sudden they heard a noise on the roof above their heads. The looked up with excitement. They heard what could only be the stamping of reindeer hooves and the huffing of air that the deer were exhaling.
They looked at each other with shining eyes. SANTA!

They tracked the footsteps on the roof…..their eyes following the sound to the chimney.
They heard Santa grunt as he stepped over the lip of the bricked chimney and with a whoosh they heard him in the fireplace itself.

The excitement was almost too much and the lil girl almost squealed in anticipation.
Her brother slapped his hand over her mouth JUST in time.

By peeking through the branches of the tree they caught glimpses of his red suit. He was just as plump as all the books said he was! He was humming and seemed rather jolly. He had a huge bag with him. He bent over and started riffling through it.

The 2 children used that opportunity….while he was practically upside down in his bag to sneak from behind the tree…moving slowly they snuck behind him and grabbed the fireplace poker.
While the boy rapped Santa on the head as hard as he could. His little sister couldn’t wait even a minute longer. She sunk her teeth into Santa’s red clad thigh, tearing a chunk of meat off that was too big to even swallow. Santa squealed and thrashed around in the bag. Trying to straighten and pull the bag off of his head and shoulders.

The reindeer on the rooftop made restless shuffling sounds. Soft sounds of distress?

The boy thumped the bag about 5 more times. Santa fell quiet.

With a few more thumps on Santa’s head for good measure…the boy wrapped the large sack around Santa’s head and tied the bag’s rope around Santa’s neck. They tugged the fat man over and his belly….well, jiggled like a bowlful of jelly.

Over the body of Santa the 2 children smiled happily at each other.

It had been 9 days since their parents had died. With their last coherent thoughts they had tried to protect their children. Before the final death took them they had boarded the house up from the inside. Making a prison out of their once happy home. Sentencing their children to death with them. To a slow painful death. Being young zombies…they had outlived their parents expectations. The adults had thought the children would die with them. The children were the ones that decided to live off the bounty the parents had left them. The children had portioned the bodies of their parents out as long as they could.

And when food became scarce….they waited for Santa.
Just like every year, they knew he would visit. And bring them a gift.

They begin to eat.

Edit: Most children believe in Santa. Don't they?

The town Shaman told me to relieve my stress by writing down my thoughts at the end of each day. I don't feel particularly calm yet. Maybe it takes time?

Day 17
I showed what I had wrote yesterday to the Shaman and he told me it was a good start, but I should write more how I feel and any actions I have done throughout the day. I worked today on the wall by using some spare wood to plug up any of the holes. There was a loud argument in the town's square too. They yelled about how they had found a dead body and spoke of a ghoul. I do hope we don't really have a ghoul running amok...

Day 19
I couldn't find time to write yesterday. We found out we had a ghoul and who it was, but he had fled into the desert. I was sent with a search party to find him. We did eventually find him, but when we got there he was face-down in the sand, dead, with his liver removed. No one could figure what had happened to him. The Shaman, however, said it had been The Crow.

Day 20
Things are getting more and more hectic. The walls waned and swayed purely from the might of the horde last night. The attacks are growing evermore fierce. The town leader is scrambling people to be watchmen for the next few days. Hopefully it will be over soon.

Day 21
I got drafted for watchmen today. The attack has also spiked to bone-crackling numbers. After a quick run through of our weapons, we're low on weapons and defenses. I fear tonight is my last night. The moon sure looks pretty tonight though.

Day 22 - December 25th
It is still the 24th, but that shall change in a mere matter of minutes. I was just greeted by a child. A small little boy whom I yelled at for being in such a dangerous place at the more horrific of times. His eyes watered, but then from behind his back he showed me his teddy bear. It was sand-worn and torn from much play, but I stopped yelling the instant I saw it's beauty.
He told me, "Merry Christmas, sir."
I grabbed him and hugged him tightly. Suddenly, loud howls and groans came riding through the wind, ruffling the sand. The hour has struck midnight and I must finish this entry quickly for they are only several meters from the wall.

Alright, here goes Christmas NightLucia looked out the window sadly, then calmly boarded it up. The hammer she used was too big for her small eleven year old body. Her younger brother Timmy silently walked over. "What are you doing, Lucy?" he asked.

"Shhh..." Lucia replied. She looked out into the deserted town. All the adults were on the wall, preparing for the attack that night. Lucia would go out to help, but she had to take care of Timmy, as her mother had patiently explained. Lucia suspected that she just wanted her out of the way.
Lucia sighed and continued to board up the window. The sounds of the hammer echoed throughout the house. Bang, bang, bang.
The windows finished, Lucia walked to the door and started boarding that up.

Today was the first day the zombies would get through their extensive defenses. Everyone was called up to the wall; carrying torches, knives, anything. Everyone, except her.
After finishing her work, Lucia settled down to wait. Timmy walked over and looked at her. "When is Mom and Dad coming home?"

Lucia stayed silent.
Timmy's voice rose high in panic. "Mom and Dad ARE coming home, right? Right? They are, right?"
Lucia lifted her head up sharply, then her face softened. "It's okay, Timmy. Everything is going to be okay."
The clock chimed midnight. A heavy silence fell onto the town like the first snow of the year.

Then, the moans. Impossibly loud. Lucia thought of the townspeople: Uncle Ralph who would strum his out-of-tune ukulele; Miss Mary who sneaked candy to them; her parents who really just wanted them to grow up right. They all were watching, waiting on the wall. Courageously defending the town from the advancing horde.

Lucia shut her eyes tight. Timmy, concerned, laid a hand on his sister's shoulder.

Then the screams began. Lucia jumped up and ran to the window. But it was covered with hastily hammered plywood, and impossible to see through. She sank onto the ground. "Please... please..." she whispered. "Let Mom and Dad be okay. I'll listen to them from now on... I'll stay home... I'll do anything..."
Now it was Timmy's turn to comfort his sister. "Everything is going to be okay. I'll protect you."

Lucia looked into her brother's face. She could tell that he was trying to be brave. She smiled and hugged her brother, hard. "Yes," she said, her voice cracking. "Everything is going to be okay."
The screams continued, getting louder and louder. Lucia looked at Timmy's frightened face, and it nearly broke her heart. What kind of world are we in, she thought, to force a five year old child to be this scared?

The screams stopped. Now there was nothing but the moans. Lucia shivered. The moans grew closer. Lucia knew that her parents were probably dead. She had yelled and raged at them only this morning, screaming that she was old enough to go on the wall. She never told them how much she loved them...
The moans now sounded like they were right next to the house. The door started pounding, urgently. Timmy's face lit up.
"Is it Mom and Dad?" he asked hopefully. Lucia tried to smile, but her eyes teared up inside. "M..m..maybe," she choked out.
Timmy burst out into tears. Lucia hugged her brother tightly, watching and waiting as the door bulged in more... and more. The nails popped out one by one, and the wood creaked and cracked.
And the moans... The moans...

Then, with one final crack, the door burst open. Lucia squeezed her eyes shut, waiting for the undead to attack her and eat her piece by piece. She thought of her life... The time she saw her first zombie, safe behind the gates with her parents... Helping to gather twigs, then logs, for the defenses... Sitting with Timmy, watching him grow up... The days passing and passing by. Day by day, year by year. She was relatively safe, she was relatively happy... Surrounded by people who cared about her.

But that was all going to end very, very soon. Zombie teeth sinking into her arms, her legs. Ripping her apart. Perhaps Mom, Dad, or Uncle Ralph would be among them...
To her surprise, no teeth, no hands grabbing, no undead came to eat them both. Lucia opened her eyes cautiously. She gasped in surprise.

A fat old man was standing in the doorway, blocking the zombies' path. He had a tattered old red suit on, and a worn red hat. He had a fluffy white beard with bits of leaf and dirt stuck inside.

It was Santa.

"Ho ho ho," he said, rather strained. "Merry Christmas... Oof!" A zombie knocked into him from the left. Curiously, they didn't try to bite and eat him. Instead, it was as if he was just another obstacle, another barrier between them and the tasty children.
Santa looked back at the wide eyed children. "This wouldn't work," he said. "I can't keep this up forever... Time for Plan B!"

Santa turned around to face the advancing horde. He grabbed the black bag slung over his shoulder and started taking things out and flinging them at the zombies. Toy cars, planes, and stuffed animals flew out of the bag. The zombies seemed to flinch away.

"Take this, you monsters!" Santa growled. "Childhood... Love... Innocence! Don't you dare steal from MY children!"
Santa chucked all sorts of toys at the zombies. The zombies fell, one by one, until there were only a few left. Santa dug deep into the bag and pulled out a machete. Lucia stared. It was the kind she'd always wanted.
Without hesitation, Santa swung the machete at the zombies, cutting a wide swath through them.

Then, Santa reached into his big black bag for the last item inside: a large, fluffy teddy bear.
"Ha! Ha! Ho! Ho!" Santa cried as he flung the teddy bear at the last zombies. Unexpectedly, the teddy bear burst into flames in mid air, burning the remaining zombies into a crisp.
Timmy walked up to Santa and tugged on his arm slowly. "Are you... Real?" he asked shyly.

"Oh of course I am," Santa chuckled. Then his face turned serious. "Well, your gifts got destroyed when I fought the zombies... I hope you don't mind."
Lucia stood up, emotions swirling around her. She was alive, and so was Timmy! Hanging from death by a thread, then being rescued by Santa. Santa, of all people!

Lucia blinked, dazed. "That's... alright," she told Santa. "You've given us the best gift there is. I have my brother with me, and I have my life. But most of all, you've given us hope. Thank you."
Santa's eyes crinkled at the corners as he smiled. "You're welcome," he said. "That's what I'm here for."

He winked, then walked outside and was gone. Lucia ran to the door, but didn't see anything except the silhouette of reindeer and a sleigh, outlined against the moon.
Timmy joined his sister. "You know," he said, looking up at her. "I always thought Santa wasn't real."
"Well, he is," Lucia answered. "He is, and he is a creator of miracles. Merry Christmas, Timmy. I'm so glad you're here."

December 22, 3 days for Christmas
North Pole Base, Gift-Wrapping War Room

-What's the status report on the preparations?-the Boss asked. He is an old man in a red and white suit with a long white beard. He is always comfortable with this meetings, but that one year, it was all different...

With C-day nearby, the situation is always tense but, with the new circunstances, things were really chaotic. Many, and i include myself, didn't think we'd be able to make it that year. Even the Boss had his doubts, as he told me, some years after the fact. But now, back to the meeting:

-Well, sir-answered the First Elf, leader of our small workforce-The situation is a mess all around. The new housing that now covers the target zones is awfully inadecuate for our landing needs, let alone entering. Also, due to the impersonators, many people would attack us on sight if they caught us on the ground. We barely secured the main workshop area after the attacks of the 16th, and it's only working at 70%. But the worst damage sustained was in the takeoff zone and reindeer stables. We lost almost the entire pack after the last incident. Honestly... I don't think we can make our goals this year.

The Boss raised from his chair and started walking around, he was obviously nervous. He asked:
-What about reinforcements? Is there anyone we can call?

-No one sir-the Second Elf, the communication manager, responded-They all seem to have lost their antenna relays. We don't even know if there is anyone left...

-What am i going to do know?-the Boss asked- There is no way we can make our deliveries, even if we got a new transport running... There is no time.

The resignation was an unthinkable alternative, and yet, it appeared to be the only one. After so many years of flawless work, to think that we would fail... But what could we do? We had nothing left.

-No!-cried out the Third Elf, tool master, and in this days, leader of the defense operations-We can't go down without fighting! There has to be a place where we can get supplies, something to we can use to fight back!

-There is a place-I answered. As the Sixth Elf, and mapping expert, it was my job to know such things, but i never thought we'd go to that particular place again.

I displayed a map of Russia on the table and pointed to a place in Siberia, as i said:

-There was an old Soviet weapons bunker, right here. I remember because we always dropped a little coal there back in the days. As far as we know from our last run, no one knew it was there. We should find it full with old weapons we can take.

-Are you suggesting we deliver weapons?-the Forth Elf, gift specialist, asked me with a tone of disapproval-You know we have a strong anti weapons policy. We aren't arms dealers!

Then a large discussion started between us, as some where with me, while others disapproved of the idea of a weapons delivery. In the middle of the screaming, the Boss hitted the table with his fist, and we all stopped arguing and turned towards him as he spoke:

-We can't deliver weapons-he began, in a tone that allowed no reply- but we can deliver safety, even if only for a while. We will take them, and use them to do all the good we can. Prepare vehicules, strong and fast,, we have global operation to do, and only 3 days left!

Some thought about replying, i will admit even i had my objections to such a plan. But the Boss was completely confident, and he surely knows how to inspire you.

We all got down to work. There was lots to be done. But we were elves. If this was even possible, then we would do it.

SnilueyOnce upon a time in a lonely town, there was a shaman, and 28 other people. They were thirsty for water, but they had only 10 rations left, and hadn`t found any blueprints for water. Shaman thought, that they should lighten up the mood a little by making "a sandman" from the sand. It was christmas soon afterall.

The plan was taken into action, and the sandman was ready in the evening. After making it, they went to sleep and the shaman closed the gates.

Suddenly, in the middle of the night, shaman heard a crow. He went outside and saw the crow sitting on the sandman. But it wasn`t a sandman anymore... It had somehow turned into a Snowman, a talking one no less!

Shaman decided to show the snowman places, the workshop, the gates, his own and other peoples houses. At the end of the course, he took the snowman to the well.

Snowman looked deep into the well, wanting to know what was in there. Shaman smiled, took a shovel, and SMACKED the snowman into the well. With a loud SMASH, the snowman had gone to pieces in the bottom of the well.

The next day, people noticed, that the well had 50 more water rations for them to drink, and they started celebrating. The shaman looked at the smiling crow atop the gates, and smiled back.

the end

The man smiled, as this was all a man could ask for in the end of the world.

His wife sat across from him in their decorated hovel, smiling back at him. He gazed into her unique hazel eyes, and she stared back.

The man switched gazes to his son, where the boy played with an old rusty truck on the hovel floor. Blurting out distorted truck sounds and imagining the truck was going 100 miles per hour, the man couldn't help but laugh. His son was perfect. This life, also perfect.

The small brown shrub in the corner of their abode shined bright with neatly lit candles on its strongest of limbs. A few crudely crafted decorations portraying stars and spheres hung from more limbs. Though this was not like Christmas once was, it was as perfect as ever.

The man heard a large beeping sound, and his gaze was broken with his wife. He looked over at a small stove and saw that the steak his friend had stumbled upon in the wastes was cooked to perfection. He gestured to his wife that he would be back in a moment.

The man got up, and served the meal as nicely as he could. Christmas day was upon them! He picked his son off of the floor and spun him in the air, the boy now making sounds that resembled that of an air plane.
