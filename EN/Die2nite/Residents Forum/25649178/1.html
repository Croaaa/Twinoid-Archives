The watchman system is relatively new, so I'm not sure about the differences. Watchmen, as far as I know, need to equip weapons and items to improve their attack power. The higher their attack power compared to the zombies' number, the higher their survival odds. I also think only Guardians can achieve 100% odds of survival while others get a limit of 95%.

Surviving in your house is simpler. It depends on the number of zombies that manage to bypass the town's defenses and the watchmen. They seem to attack citizens randomly, so some citizens can get a lot of zombies on their door while others are left unharmed. If your home defense is higher than the number of zombies attacking you, you survive, but with a high chance of being terrorised. If the defense is lower, you get eaten in a very unpleasant way.

As far as I know this is wrong. If you die as a consequence of the attack in town (hence watchman or in your bed) you'll get the LMS distinction.

As for the attack spread, it considers the maximum number of zombies that will attack the citizens. If you manage to stop some of them (with the town defense or with watchmen) then the survival chances provided by the attack spread are an underestimate of the chances you really have.
Example: day 14, citizens in town 2. The maximum number of attacking zeds is 150, no matter how many will make it into town. If the attack is 2000 and the town defense (considering the watchmen) is 1900, then only 100 zombies will make it to town and all of them will attack the citizens. However, if the town defense is only 1500, then 500 will make it into town. Of these 500, only 150 will attack the citizens.
If the watchmen die the thing is more complicated, because then it depends if the watchman bug will happen or not.
