Ok, time to chuck my two cents into the ring.

In a completely random town, or indeed the game in general, there's generally a multitude of goals, some selfish, some selfless. People may wish to try and survive as long as possible together, with ranking being a goal, or indeed getting the alarm clock and the bragging rights that come with #1 on that 'board, others may wish to go for a decorative soul and hunt distinctions, either at no expense to other members of the town, or at an expense to them, or possibly with other members of the town (i.e. a wonder hunt), depending on quite what they're hoping to achieve.

So it follows that there's always going to be (or the majority of the time, at the least) conflicting interests, or just a conflict of personalities, and this leads to disagreements, and potentially one group of people trying to take control of the town by eliminating other members of the community in an attempt to achieve what that group want to achieve, e.g. give themselves are better shot at LMS, and in a game of survival of the fittest (in whatever context that may be) this makes the sturdy gallows the obvious choice IMO.

This is because it allows a group of team who are wishing to remove another player, or players, from the game the capability to do so if they're organised without a disproportionate amount of resources, it also creates fun dynamics within a town and the tables can be turned oh so easily if another group disapproves of the first groups actions and wishes to take a stand, whereas temporary gallows is just a bit too large a drain on resources and doesn't allow for those fun dynamics to occur.

You can obviously still prevent the first group from building the gallows in the first place and shun them for their sins if you react fast enough, and if you don't then you can still try hang 'em first, otherwise your milkshake will get drank.

Of course the argument for 'griefing' exists, but it's just a bit bleh if I'm honest, ok, sure, from time to time you will run into coalitions hanging people with no motive other than to grief, but it's really not that common in my experiences and would most likely happen with temporary gallows/in other ways regardless, and if you can't get a group of people out of 32 to deal with these players, with all the avenues available (twinoid, Forums, messaging houses etc.) then your town probably wouldn't survive that long anyway.
