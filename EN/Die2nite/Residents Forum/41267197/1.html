On the reverse side of rumpled notepad

It may sound cliche, but I can not find better words to start... The fact that you are reading this means that I'm already dead. And you found my notebook, which I normally used for work records before everything collapsed in hell. No, don't think bad, I'm not a pessimist. Like others I hope for the best, I believe that it is necessary just to survive another day, another week, maybe a month, and we will be certainly rescued by someone. National Guards will arrive with helicopters and machine guns, they will clear the way and bring all the survivors to safety. Or at least give us a vaccine against the virus. Or, in extreme cases, just explain what the hell is going on, because no one still even got news from the outside world.
I am a realist. And, unlike others, can understand perfectly well the difference between faith and facts, dreams and forecasts. And the facts are: we are very deep in the ass. And even if anyone will be able to survive until better times - it just will not be me.
I decided that technical information from my notebook may be useful to someone. Especially now, when there is no internet and mobile communication, even the book is extremely difficult to obtain, when weird creatures wandering everywhere... And I will do my best, so even in a case of my death notebook will not be lost. Perhaps, information from it will save many lives.
I'm a technician. Expert on nuclear physics, to be precise. In this notebook you can find information about the construction of reactors, routing of electrical networks, some details from the course of the “resistance of materials”, classical physics and mechanics. And numbers Pi and e up to fifty decimal places. However, there is one problem - many notes made in my native language. In Russian. Shit happens, and at the beginning of the apocalypse I was in a foreign country, in the middle of nowhere. Who ever could know, that this business trip will finish like that... Just turned out that in our department I was the only one who knows English at a sufficient level for the "exchange of experience".
And now, after a crazy day with a lot of events and new acquaintances, a couple of free hours stood out and I decided to keep a diary. I just required to maintain a kind of "Chronicle". I will write the names and events without pins and without morality. After all, those who find this notebook should learn something about me and the people around me, that provided all kinds of support in this difficult time. Or maybe you will use the paper with my scribbles and invaluable knowledge to build a fire or wipe your ass. This is the thing I can not forecast.
Well. At this point I will probably finish opening remarks and move on to the point. I apologize in advance for any mistakes or inaccuracies in the text. You know, I am writing in a hurry, with a disgusting light, and the language is not my native.

Day 1. Welcome to Oozing Inn

I'd like to start the story from the very beginning, from the day of X, as we call it. However, a lot of time has passed since then and many things happened, so I am not even able to sort all the events in a chronological order. Perhaps I will tell you this story on the following pages. Now I will just say that it has been about two months since zombies staged a general panic in the supermarket and I miraculously got out of town and went to wander in the deserts. On the one hand it was the right decision: chance to meet the monster here is much less than in metropolitan areas with high population density and, as a consequence, a huge number of zombies. On the other hand it became very difficult to find food and water, and I met new dangers: wild animals, snakes and scorching heat. Anyway, after a whole series of events, walks and changes of shelters, this morning I came across this place.
To be honest, I was purposefully going here for the last two days. Many kilometers before I saw signs of "intelligent life": footprints in the sand, dead zombies, fireplaces and the temporary lodgings. And all these traces went into one direction. And yesterday I saw absolutely definite sign. It was a long board in the sand with the inscription:

«Everyone who needs – come Inn!
10 kilometers NE»

And this morning I saw ... A huge camp. In the circle of grubby barricades a whole tent city was located, in which were bustling a couple of dozen people. To my surprise, I even heard the barking of dogs. Also on one side of the barricades was some semblance of the gate leaves, and above them stood a huge billboard with two words written with a red ink: «Oozing Inn». Apparently, it was somehow dragged from the roadside. And finally, I belatedly realized that until I was staring at all of this I have been noticed: two people in camouflage were looking right at me and talked about something. I had to throw away all doubts and move towards them. It was too late to change the decision and try to escape: if it is bad people, I would be hunted down and caught. And on the way here I drained the last reserves and anyway could not last for a long time without help. How lucky I was...
Only after coming closer I saw that one of these people ... Was a girl! It is a pity that I do not understand the units and ranks of the American army, and still have not bothered to ask them. I have no idea what is the meaning of letter "G" on the uniforms and still conditionally call them Guardians. The guy was taller than me for half a head, tanned and with short black hair, but not very broad in the shoulders. The girl much shorter in stature, but obviously older than both of us. At the first sight I would say she is around thirty. Smile made her face with regular features pretty cute, but some harshness in eyes, black hair, neatly tucked into a bundle at the nape and confident strut were talking about serious combat exposure.
Only when I went up to a distance of ten steps they stopped to exchange words and drew all the attention on me. The guy friendly waved:
- Welcome to the Inn, stranger! – He had pretty cheerful and sonorous voice and the letter R sounded especially expressive. - Make yourself at home, we are very happy to all newcomers!
- Uhm... Hello, - was the only thing I could utter.
- Is everything OK with you? - spoke the girl. - These friends did not bother you too much? - She pointed to the zombie corpse, lying next to the barricades.
- I am fine, thanks. - Their unprecedented friendliness made me discouraged.
- It's pleasure to hear! - she said with a big smile on face. - Rico, will take a duty here? I'll bring him to the main, and the guys need to be instructed.
- Affirmative, Lieutenant! - blurted man and saluted.
- Come with me. - Really? This girl have a higher rank? Somehow I did not dare to protest and followed her into the camp.
Some mess reigned around. People were mostly too busy and did not even looked at us: someone hammered pegs into the ground, trying to tie the tent, another was shoving rags in a backpack. A small queue lined up around the table at which sat pretty girl. When I passed through she gave another uniformed guy one of the flasks and a paper bag from the table and began to write something down in a notebook, holding a pen with her long-nailed fingers, covered by a blue polish.
Company of a several of guys and girls laughed, looking like a guy depicts a zombie and talks animatedly:
- And then she is like – wooo, rrr, wooo! - among other things, his "roar" sounded really similar to a dead man. - Then I told her : “I'm sorry, honey, but my heart is already taken”. And brandishing...
- Hey, syndesis! - Interrupted him off my companion. – Stop veer tales! We are moving out in a hour.
- But maaam! - Replied the guy like a cartoon character. That called the new batch of laughter, but the people get up and began to diverge.
On the other side of the camp I saw a small "paddock", inside which half a dozen fluffy dogs were playing, all completely white in color and resemble each other like two drops of water. I'll have to find out what kind of breed it is. Near the paddock girl explained something a group of people, but I did not hear the words.
- We are here, - said my companion.
I stopped twirling head around and stared at the destination point. It was a real shack - small in size, but with a roof made of logs, solid door and a small glass window. It was located right in the center of the camp, and was easy to guess that this house belongs to the "Main", as opposed to all those grubby tents, beds and sleeping bags, randomly scattered around.
- You may be confused a little bit, - warned lieutenant. - This guy is a real shaman. But don’t worry, he is very nice! Let's go! - and she opened the door.
