Let's get a quick run down of how it is pay to win, and how your statements weren't even relevant.

1) Experienced heroes instantly come with resources, and even blueprints.

2) Heroes have increased inventory (both house, and rucksack) which makes both endgame mechanics, and scavenging better.

3) They have much higher CP, especially if they have the clean status. A guardian can have SIX CP by himself. SIX! Others can have 4. That is 2-3 times a resident's CP. This is incredibly significant for expeditions, requiring smaller groups to do expeditions

4) Heroes have incredibly powerful abilities. For example a shaman can mean an easy 300 points added to the town just from soul collecting, not including his other powers.

5) Heroes take a lot to die in the world beyond because of heroic return, second wind, and many other abilities.

6) Heroes are essential for meta play. Without a good base of heroes, it is impossible to play meta.

7) Most meta mechanics are dependent on heroes. For example scouting the world beyond can only be done by scouts, soul collecting can only be done with any impact by shamans, most clearing methods you need a tamer for, and camping is best done by survivalist.


So, with all of these things which only heroes can do which are crucial to the town's survival, and competitiveness you can see that it is very pay to win. We haven't even gotten to the selfishness play style yet, or ghoul.

With selfishness (or LMS hunting), you can do stuff without leaving entries on the register, you can hold much more defensive objects in your house, you can have things added upon your house such as increased inventory or defense.

For ghoul, same deal. You can delete screw ups, while a resident ghoul has only one chance. (you have no idea how many times I have died from not being a hero while being a ghoul) You can also use second wind in emergencies that you would have needed a water for, and heroes are in general more trusted by newbs.


As for your statement about hardcore towns, and pay to win: He never mentioned anything about that, nor were there any mentions of hardcore towns being "better". In fact, you were the first person to mention hardcore towns. As for your comment about team work, yes teamwork is important.
