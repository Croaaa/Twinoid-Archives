Hello everyone, I open this subject because in the MyHordes project, we try to be as faithfully as possible of the 4 versions of the game Hordes / Die2Nite / Die Verdammten and Zombinoia. For those who do not know what MyHordes is, I refer you to this thread (in english).

Among the various functions of the game, there is the Night mode. I remind you how the Night mode works, which only exists in Hordes.fr and (apparenlty) in Zombinoia.com :

Allows theft of objects in the Bank (33% chance of not being spotted). It doesn't gives a "Theft" pictogram.
Increase the chances of survival when digging your grave during this interval (equivalent to 2 camp improvements)
Provides a bonus for scout camouflage (lack of accurate data)
Reduces the chances of finding an object from 19:00 to 7:00 game time by 20-33%, but penalty eliminated if a bedside lamp / torch or Novelty Torch is placed on the ground

There is a disagreement regarding the implementation of night mode in the community. For the French, it's obvious (french poll result here).
An overwhelming majority of the french community consider that night mode is compulsory in MyHordes; and for good reason, it is part of the habits of the server.
But some players from other communities do not want it added to MyHordes, for several reasons, in particular related to the time difference of communities spread across the world and players who are restricted in their game during French night hours, being at a permanent disadvantage. At the level of the English organized towns, it is also an addition which, according to them, would allow the French to keep all their strategies when they should review them from scratch to take it into account, and therefore an advantage for the French server.
The reasons are multiple, some players simply do not like this mechanism, and to try not to lose any player it is necessary to find a compromise solution.

Here are the 3 solutions that have been thought of so far; do not hesitate to discuss it on the subject:


Possible solution #1 : We add the night mode as is, but we may lose some of the other players who are really against it. This solution is the simplest, but goes against the idea of losing as few players as possible.



Possible solution #2 : We are adding the night mode as is but we are also adding a new construction which depends on the Lighthouse and which would be unlockable with a common plan, Public lighting or Novelty street lamp (name to discuss). It would be upgradable like this:
- lvl0 : the night penalty is removed 2km from the city
- lvl1 : the night penalty is removed 6km from the city + consumes one battery per day (like the Water Turrents) and does not work if no battery in the bank
- lvl2 : the night penalty is removed 10km from the city + consumes one battery per day (same)
- lvl3 : the night penalty is removed on the whole map + consumes 2 batteries per day (same)
- lvl4 : there is a bonus to night searches of 20% + consumes 2 batteries per day (same)

This solution allows to keep the night mode, and the usual functions to get rid of it (bedside lamp / torch / Novelty Torch) while providing a new alternative which can even lead to discussions and new strategies for organized towns; without impacting the random game:

- as it depends on the Lighthouse and the opening of a plan, it does not impact random cities by adding a new site that is useless for them
- for organized towns it adds a voting option, and therefore a strategy option (where to place it? is it profitable compared to sending Novelty Torch with the expeditions? battery consumption? ...)
- 4 votes to arrive at a search bonus is 4 votes lost for other things; defense, water, PA in the workshop, ...
- allows to leave the penalty at night for all, but gives an option to remove it without the use of objects; less profitable among the English because of their individual experiences with zoo
- still consumes batteries like the daily use of a lamp; new evolution going in the direction of Water Turrents



Possible solution #3 : Another solution would be to activate the night mode only for French cities (because on MH, cities have their language). That would suit everyone: the French will have night mode and the English players will not. The problem with this solution is that we intend to make a classification of all the cities in common, and therefore to do that it is necessary that all the cities have the same rules, otherwise it would unbalance the classification. And then if we do that, it's also simple for a French player who doesn't like the night mode to go to an English city, and that's not the basic intention because we have only one server and not 4. Even Brainbox (the main developer of MyHordes) does not like this solution.

In any case, we will lose players. "The trick" is to try to lose the least

Consequently, I let you indicate in the commentary of this topic which solution seems to you the most interesting; and if you have other ideas do not hesitate to let us know
