ChrisLeeNealI'm currently engaged in a discussion in my town where many people (new ish to the game) are convinced that Factory is the much better option of the two. I'm disagreeing with them and saying the Architect Study is much more useful and great in the long run.

Yeah, this is an interesting balance. Ideally you'd have both pretty soon.

I used to think you needed the study pretty early, but I've changed my tune at this point. An ideal town (that doesn't have 40 Architects) would have both by day 10.


The payoff for building the factory is immediate, and you start saving AP each time.
The other factor is that by saving an AP here and there, you not only can make your AP last longer, but you can fit in conversions when it's appropriate.
Before you have the factory you need 2AP for each conversion, 3 with the hacksaw. That means that conversions pretty much only happen with people that stay inside and build.


After the factory + hacksaw you can start using guitar AP to finish conversions.

I still think that the Common blueprints are pretty critical, but my opinion is that towns that will make it to day 20 will end up getting the second Jarpen table for the study (or they'll find the blueprints some other way) and towns that don't make it that far probably won't have a chance to build those extra buildings anyway.

It depends a bit on how long you think the town will last,

A ten day town - factory, as you won't last long enough to run out of things to build and the extra AP saved will possibly help survive an extra day or two.

Upto 20 days - Architects Study, the extra BPs will really come in handy with trying to cover last few days.

More than 20 days - Factory, a town of this length will probably find most BP anyway and the AP saved on conversions will be huge.
