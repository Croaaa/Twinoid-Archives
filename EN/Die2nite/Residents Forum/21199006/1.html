I came to this game a couple years ago from a link on a comedy website.

At first, I thought it was a FPS with 40 other people, I quickly learned otherwise, and as -100 soul, I felt very alone, and confused.

What made you fall in love with the game?

Simple....the people.

After a few towns, struggling on my own, one man named BerZerg (Yeah, you may know him as the creator of From Dusk till Dawn Map App)

Was in a town with me. He saw potential in teaming up with other players to make survival easier.

I looked at the people in that town, way back when, and selected the most active, to form a COA, and then teamed with BerZerg, and his newly founded COA, to form a quarter meta.

Some of the names I selected may be familiar to you this day....

Jadzy , JamesHenry , Aivaras.


Between BerZerg and I, we became the Highlanders. And later, we reformed into the 7 Sins.

Yeah, we were noobs at the time, but I am proud that the core of that original group still plays together to this day, as among the nearly highest soul point members of this community.



What made that love multiply the most?


Easy, the experience.

We all have been through so much. We stick together. There has been unbelievable drama. enemies to deal with.

In the last two years, I have had more fun with my friends, no matter how we rank, no matter how many soul points we earn. it is all worth it.

Yeah, we wanna rank. But if you take the cumulative of the laughs we have had....

We already rank number one.


I love my meta.

I love my friends I have made here.


If D2N success was rated on fun, laughs and good times, then I would know I have WON this game.


What made that love multiply the most?



My dear friends.

DaryleDixon

Great thread idea rover420. (I also liked your posts in 'the other thread'- cool as a f'in cucumber)

I thought I'd share my most memorable story of D2N. It's a bit long winded so I'll warn you now that if you don't have time for reading- what are you doing in the forums section?

The story starts at the beginning of season 3. The meta cap had just been introduced and players were trying to find away to still play competitive organised towns. Sense, the leader of Wonderful Failure, came up with the WJ. I decided to partake and ended up in a town called 'The Strange Sinkhole of Fire.' No ordinary town was this. With lots of great players in the same town, leadership conflicts were inevitable. Too much happened in this town for me to explain it properly but the whole story can be found on the first page of the stickied thread 'Art and other futilities'. But basically war broke out, two camps founded and the rest of the towns days would be filled with deceit and ultimately blood. As a relatively new recruit to Wonderful Failure, I sided with them. We killed anyone who opposed us including a well respected player named axiin, who plays a part in the story to come.
After that town and a few others, I found myself with less time irl and left the game, deleting my account in the process. But a few weeks/months later I was back. I created this new account and gave myself a mission to form a coalition with the new players I would come across. I found some great players to play with. Beauchateau (which I'm still proud to say can spell without checking), Piorunes, dhaos and shikki. We all had the same goal to find more players and have fun in the process. Our next small town would not be what we expected. The town was called 'Regions of days without end.' Accustomed to small maps we were expecting a hopeless town that would hopefully have a few players we could cherry pick for our coalition. Not the case. Axiin was here. He had decided to join a small map to help new players come to terms with the game. He took up leadership and lead the town to victory. We were able to almost clear the map of zeds and set up a little zombie zoo to contain them. It really was a great town with all the thanks due to Axiin. But to him I was just a new player. He didn't know who I was. He didn't know I'd killed him. When the town was all but dead I joined the town chat to be met with the heading: 'Just your average run of the mill town.'
'I hope you don't mean that' I said, deeming Axiin responsible.
The heading changed to something like ' Awesome townies town' or something like that.
'That better?' Axiin said
'Much' I replied.
When I died in the 'Strange Sinkhole of Fire' on my old profile, I left my last words: 'Just your average run of the mill town.'
Is this just a coincidence? Or does he know?
Enshrouded in paranoia I confided in Beauchateau. Who compared my situation to a detective series that I had never heard of. I agreed with him for some reason.

But I never found out if Axiin knew. Probably never will.

Thanks for reading if you did. Love the game and love almost every player I meet. Peace.

What made you fall in love with the game?

When I was originally invited to the game I didn't play because I didn't find it attractive (Yes, silly me ) It wasn't until a good friend dragged me here that I loved the game. Why? At first because it had a form of in-game justice. In other games I was playing at that moment the griefers ran rampant and there was no way of stopping them. What made that love multiply the most?

Several things. First, my awesome coalition. I dragged old friends and met new ones. Then, the liberty that the game offers. You can be helpful and nice, or be a selfish jerk. One of the most fun parts of the game for me is finding the correct balance between a bit of selfishness and helpfulness.

Griffonn

What made you fall in love with the game?
When my brother first discovered this game, and I began to play shortly after, I remember the foreign feel to this game. I remember my first town, and being wounded before the first attack, and thinking it was a HUGE deal . I remember posting on the forums my goodbyes and goodlucks, and then, after the attack, I was dead!

The exploration that this game offers is beyond me. Its literally a world beyond me, in a world beyond. (See what I did there )

I loved the great feeling of entering a new town, and just having a table of food set before you, and having the option of taking any food that you want! SO many options and so much freedom, allow for so many different people to be brought to one game!

What made that love multiply the most?

The community. The community is the greatest thing that happened to me with this game. I used to never use the world forums, and only come and go through towns. Although, after discovering and using the world forums, the game totally changed. Instead of a huge unknown community of random players, I was introduced to a closely knit array of personalities, and people with similar interests as me.

There are RPers, there are comedians, there are great friends!

I remember, after one of my bounce-backs, and being gone for over a year, I entered a town, and met someone called DrJamez. We stuck together, and he looked out for me. We faced a meta called "the Family" and we were driven out of town, and eventually died! But we became great friends after that.

The forums allowed me to be known (to a degree) in this game, and make new friends. This community gives me a place to go where I can talk to friends, and converse with people who share common interests.

So along with giving the community of this game credit for me being here, I want to thank everyone and let you all know that this game is great because of you!

A) What made you fall in love with the game?
Originally, I didn't really like the game. It was just something to do while waiting for my next moves in DinoRPG and the original MyBrute. Then I jumped into a town with some players who were willing to teach the newbies (not noobs) how to play the game, and I got hooked. That was in Old Towns.

A few weeks later, I created my first coalition, but the others were always off doing their own thing instead of waiting for everyone to die. I happened to get into a town with Soundwave and ScumbagLawyer, and they invited me to join their coa. Scum is no longer with us, but Soundwave and I are still in the same coa two years later.


B) What made that love multiply the most?
Metas, contests, Antique ignorance of brutes, Apocalympics (#1 town!), continuing to find more experienced players willing to teach us relative newbies, and at least attempting to pass my knowledge to others less experienced than I. The unpredictability of the game is what makes it so fun - I actually have to think a little bit.

The addition of new elements is always something I look forward to, although the Season 3 Metacap is one of the worst things I think MT has ever done to this game. At least they realized their mistake and undid it for Season 4.
