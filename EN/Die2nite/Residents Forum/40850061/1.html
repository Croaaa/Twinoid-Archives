Just wanted to say that I think many of you underestimate how many players DV still has.

It's true that hardcore towns fill very slowly on DV (partly because no one wants to join them BECAUSE they fill so slowly which leads to many deaths at day 1). That's not the case for distant towns though.

There are less players then on D2N, but we DO have 100+ paying players actively playing the game, there are several active metas on DV and we managed to fill all 3 UFC towns with volunteers (120 ppl) although many players didn't want to take part in the event for several reasons (e.g. not believing for it to work out without issues).

That's enough money to justify the hosting costs and the costs for ranking resets. The point is that MT is run by game developers, there is no such a thing as a dedicated admin who just does administration or community stuff.

Every hour they use to maintain a game is an hour they can't use to develop a new game. As game devs usually love developing new games, and new games bring more money than old games, that's why they don't want to spend the time to maintain the server.

At some point, Die2Nite will suffer the same fate imho.

As for having no german admins: In the past the few news posts we had were translated by community members and all game content was also translated by community members free of charge. All requests to the admins were done in English.

The language barrier was no issue.

Still, allowing people to pay for further hero days and not even writing an official news post that there is NO further support (no tech support, no customer support, no bugfixes, nothing) is not very nice imho.
