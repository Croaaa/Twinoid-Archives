I'll post the guide here then, so anyone interested to use on random towns just quote this msg and take it with html codes and all:

Welcome to the town!

I know that die2nite can be a little overwhelming at first, so here is a quick list of helpful information to think about on your first day!

Forums

- If you're reading this, it means that you are already on the right track! Check the forums everyday to see what is needed, and participate in the discussion. The town forums are where you and the 39 other citizens in town will plan your defensive strategies for the town. Be a part of the conversation! Don't be afraid to ask questions!

Scavenge

- Especially in the early days. Scavenging for resources in the world beyond will never be as easy as it is today! Just head out into the world beyond, pick the farther zone your AP can take you (counting the returning steps too, of course), and start auto-searching! Just remember to make it back before you end up on the wrong end of the food chain!
Autosearching on a zone near town is a wasteful destination because that zone will deplete regardless of your autosearching. This is because people will walk through and search the zone on their way to autosearch a more distant zone. Not only that, it's an inefficient use of AP because you get fewer searches per action point.

External Map - VERY IMPORTANT!!!!!!!

From Dusk Till Dawn (D2D) is an external application that offers a huge amount of utility over the in-game map.

The in-game map only lets you flag a zone as having "items" or "important items", D2D tells you exactly what is in a zone.
The in-game map only lets you tell other how many zeds are there within ranges, D2D lets you say exactly how many zeds are there.
How do you use such a wonderful tool? It's easy! In the upper left hand of your page is a small square. Hover over that and you'll see that it is called the Directory. Click on the From Dusk Till Dawn link and follow the prompts. It'll open in a new tab. Then every time you move you click on the Update My Zone button, and it handles all the updates for you automatically!

Remember,the external map use is no less than crucial for the town to work, interact, plan missions, gather resources, etc. So always use it when playing d2n!

Got stuck?
- Don't panic!
If you are in trouble in the world beyond, stay calm!
- Post a call for help in the town forums. If you're trapped by zombies, or out of AP, you can request help. Maybe a passing resident can lend you enough control points, or take out enough zombies to get you home! Fleeing from zombies is a LAST RESORT, as it will ALWAYS leave you injured! Which leads to other nasty things...

Don't waste AP trying to punch them, it's pointless, the chance of killing is ridiculous and you'll ran out of AP to get back to town and/or to complete your mission.

In short: got stuck? ask for help and wait patiently.

Have fun!

-> feel free to improve this guide if you feel there's other important basic guidances

Iced Guardian

here's a second stuff with some more tips to avoid frequent random town mess-ups:

Construction sites

DON'T ever build anything before reading the forums.
Explanation: Every spent on construction counts and can be the difference between a smooth night and a catastrophic carnage. Thus, always make sure you're allocating your into the proper buildings. Not to mention, that if you build the wrong structure, it will eat up resources needed for the right structure.

Town Upgrades

Daily Upgrade Project

DON'T vote before consulting the forums.
Explanation: Try to make a concerted effort with the other residents to vote the optimal structure every day. If you don't know, what is the optimal structure, consult the forums.

DO hold off your vote till the last possible moment.
Explanation: Basically, vote only if it's your last log-in before the attack. The reason being, that the later it is, the more information and options are available to you, thus making the correct upgrade decision easier.

The Workshop

DON'T open cans, chests or other containers in the Workshop.
Explanation: This action costs if performed in the workshop. You can do it for 0 with a proper tool in your house.

DON'T convert anything without consulting the forums.
Explanation: Before you make any conversions, use the forums to find out which building your town wants to build next. Then, go to the construction sites and check the required materials for said building. If it's missing materials that could be procured in the workshop, then go and convert those. Don't ever convert things "just because". It could be a waste of :.

DO use a Hacksaw while working in the Workshop.
Explanation: If your town has a working hacksaw, make sure you have it in your rucksack while converting materials in the workshop. It will reduce the cost of the conversion by 1. Also, do remember to put the hacksaw back in the bank as soon as you're done, so others may use it.

The Bank

DON'T mix Pharmaceutical Products.
Explanation: First of all - the Vegetable Plot needs one to be built and believe me - you really want that building. Second, if you mix the pharms, you'll probably get some crappy water purifying tablets or unlabelled drugs. Instead, let a Hero with a laboratory do the mixing. Contrary to you, they actually have a chance to come up with some useful drugs.

DON'T put Batteries into stuff.
Explanation: I've seen it one too many times. Some guy is eager to do something, but out of , takes the one pocket vibrator in the bank and a battery and a couple of seconds later, he returns a charged pocket vibrator to the bank. So, believe it or not - Die2Nite is a game. Games are not like real life and in D2N you actually can't take the batteries out of an object. So, don't put batteries into stuff, unless you need to use the stuff at this particular moment. Also, try to save them for later, when you get some superior battery powered weapon, like the chainsaw.

DON'T read blueprints on sight. Only common (green) ones. Explorable ruins have a pre-set of blueprints that shall we read before reading regular blueprints. This way you avoid getting a duplicated blueprint when reading the ruin ones. So do not read regular blueprints in bank unless you're told to. Asking before acting is the golden rule here!

DON'T repair stuff.
Explanation: Sometimes there are broken items in the bank. Sometimes there is a repair kit or a kwik-fix in the bank. I know it seems like a match made in heaven for the untrained, droopy eye, but in reality - it's not. Don't ever fix something just because it's broken. These repair kits are scarce and should only be used on key items and only when the need arises. Also, fixing things costs 1.

DON'T assemble things without consulting the forums.
Explanation: Some items come in an "incomplete" or "broken" state. They usually require some duct tape, iron and a couple of other resources to assemble. Because these resources are very important, it's crucial that they don't be used without consulting the forums first. Especially, since a large part of the incomplete items is useful only in very specific situations. The notable exception is the first hacksaw in town. This item is so important, that it's assembly can take precedence over most buildings. But still, do consult the forums before actually assembling it.

DON'T light up torches.
Explanation: Torches are fun! They can be used to kill zombies or as defensive items. There is one problem - they cost both matches and rotten logs to make and they only last for one attack. So don't build them just because there are some matches and logs in the bank. It's just a waste of resources.

DO open crates, cans and containers.
Explanation: If you have the proper tools (usually a can opener, but many others will do), you can open certain containers in the bank. Things like cans, toolboxes and metal chests could contain useful stuff, so there's no need for them to lay around sealed. Plus, if you open them with tools, you might prevent a droopy-eyed, armless child from opening one of them in the workshop and losing in the process. Be wary of the safe, though. It costs to try to open it and it has a low chance of success.

General tips

DO / DON'T build a Tent.
Explanation: This is actually grey territory. Some people advocate building tents and some don't. So it's basically up to you. If you don't have a better way to spend those 2, then go for it.

DON'T build a Hovel.
Explanation: Building a hovel - contrary to the tent - not only costs : but also resources. One twisted plank to be exact. Early in the game, these planks are worth their weight in gold, so if you use one to build yourself a better housing - you're in for a quick :ban: shunning.

DON'T hoard furniture.
Explanation: First of all, it doesn't count for anything until the last day of your existence in the town and it makes you look like a hoarding, selfish player. Then, if you're not a Hero yourself, you need to realise that that Carcinogenic Oven or Beer Fridge has a multitude of real-life applications - killing zombies is one of them. Being a part of a Hero's laboratory or kitchen is another. The bottom line - don't hoard furniture. Even chairs can double as a weapon.

DON'T put defensive items in your chest Unless you are in a Hardcore town. DO put them in the bank.
Explanation: Simple math - a defensive item in your home's chest is worth 1:gard:. The same defensive item is worth 2—8:gard: in the bank (depending on the level of Defensive Focus). If you keep defensive items in your chest, you're on your way to a quick :ban: shunning.

The Well

DO drain the well (if the town has a Pump).
Explanation: After your town builds a Pump, you have the possibility of taking an extra water ration every day. Take it and then deposit it in the bank. This is because the rations themselves are required to build certain structures (like Vegetable Plot and Moat). Also, there are water based defensive structures that actually use the water in the well as ammunition. Suppose your town has one of these structures, but the zed numbers are so low tonight, that you actually don't need it to fire. Well, you can't stop it. It will fire at them and it will waste your precious water. Unless, of course, it has nothing to fire with. That's why all the water from the well should be kept in the bank. Plus, in the event of a large zed spike, there's actually no problem to refill the well, so that the water based defences can fire at the zombie menace.

Watchmen

DON'T join the watchmen unless the town dosen't have enough defenses. You have a chance to die and if you take weapons with you they may break, resulting in a waste.

Thanks for reading, and please, if you play this game often and want to make it better, copy this list in a notepad, save it and paste it in your next town
