Well, on one hand it could be a pay-once and you've got it forever thing. But then earning hero days for heroes who paid becomes pointless, which ruins the incentive to screw over your fellow man.

On the topic of balance, there's really no way to do heroes except to make sure they aren't completely gamebreakingly OP. A lot of the mechanics of the game are derived under the assumption that you are a bad person who would kill, steal, destroy, and above all else betray your fellow man in order to 'win', which is to say, lose the least.

The unfortunate fact is that this means heroes are going to have a much easier time surviving. However, that's the only way to do this- if Heroes couldn't survive better, becoming a hero wouldn't be worth sacrificing the distinction of possibly becoming the greatest town in the season.

However, this is not a justification for the ludicrous levels heroes can get to- the ability to risk-free extreme camp is both absurd and an abhorrent reward which just encourages recursive selfish play, which quickly becomes detrimental to towns and players instead of encouraging a fun competition.

As it stands heroes have strong potential to become basically invulnerable and that frankly just shouldn't be possible.
