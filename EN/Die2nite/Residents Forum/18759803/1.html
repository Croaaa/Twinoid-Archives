I'm sure many veterans of this game have been there before, but this type of gameplay isn't all that fun when idiots and griefers abound. However, let me offer a Divergent view from this point forward:

- The game could use a better tutorial, perhaps a tutorial town that you only play once as a resident for only a set number of days. I'm really hopeful the world doesn't have this high of an idiocy rate.

- without disclosing spoilers, explain in more detail the strategies behind using your AP inside the town vs wandering outside.

- show what happens when you run into a mob of zombies and how to get out of it in various scenarios, perhaps once w/ a water bomb, and another running away to be injured.

- explain the injury/infection process in the tutorial.

- explain the use and addiction of drugs.

I'm basically saying - let's "stupid proof" the game from the onset so that the only ones left after a mandatory tutorial are those who at least mastered those basics, and then the post-tutorial generated towns would only have those who at least have half a brain.

Here's what I think about the "idling" zombies and midnight horde...

The zombies aren't aggressive until midnight, which is why campers risk death then. Every midnight a horde will arrive and attempt to wreck the town, but they get killed (most constructions are lethal). Tomorrow a larger horde will arrive, attracted by the noise from town defenses. As every day more buildings and noisier defenses are set up, a larger and larger horde gets attracted, requiring more and more loud defense (have you ever stopped and notice how much sound you've made when you hammered in an extra layer of armor plating!?). This vicious cycle eventually kills the town with an overwhelming horde.

Meanwhile, the zombies in the desert zones simply idle, and over night may join in on the the horde to attack the town (death by attire/loneliness), or attract several zombies from the horde to idle with them in nearby zones (zombie spread). The overall change to the horde isn't noticeable (since it's add/minus a handful of them at most), but the zombies appear to spread/disappear.
