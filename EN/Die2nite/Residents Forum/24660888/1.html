Iced Guardian

"I once met a man,
Who came from lands afar:
He brought to me the secrets of life
Concealed inside a jar.

"Hello there, young man," he spelled out loud−
A sound of curious shade−
And mocked me with a sinister smile
While with the jar he played.

"Would you like to see what I have here,
Here brought to your delight?"
"You take nothing from a stranger," said I
"My mother taught me right".

"No stranger am I," he cried; "in truth
Your kin, and dear, dear friend".
He held out the jar to me in vain:
You should wait for the hordes to descend

"Just answer, and from you I will take
The jar: just who are you?"
He grinned, then I knew; those ruddy eyes
Their objects they were two.

"My name, my friend, is Zombor;
I have come from lands afar
And bring the secrets of life and death
To reveal from out this jar."

"Your name, my friend, is, I know, Shamaniac;
To me you are no friend,
Go away, down to the lands afar:
Your venom do not spend."

Still he grinned a broad and rotten grin,
He raised his arm, and spang
on the ground the jar threw down, and thus
The jar, it burst with a bang.

From the shards came sounds of varied sorts,
A spate of sounds pell-mell
That stemmed from sundry strata of earth
From heaven through to hell.

The shards then started to melt and thaw
And I, all while he laughed
And laughed, got one of the shatters up
And hid it very daft.

He laughed, and then was gone in a flash.
I saw myself so clearer
In the piece of jar remaining here:
Inside there was a mirror!

I once met a man named Zombor
Who came in mists of steam
He tried to fool me, but I fooled him−
He came to me in a dream.

Yet he did change my life, for the shard
He left me was a gem, so bright:
For in that little shiny fragment
I felt I wasn't going to die2nite!"

Out-of-contest piece of parody on Lewis Carroll's "Haddock's Eyes", some part towards the end remained completely unchanged:

devoted to Ayono and MadMax

"Zombie's Eyes" aka White Tamer's Song

I'll tell thee everything I can:
There's nothing else to add.
I saw an aged aged man,
A-sitting in the chat.
"Who are you, aged man?" I said,
"And whats your function here?"
His answer trickled through my head,
as bubble through a beer.


He said "I'm waiting for the horde
To sweep their nightly rush:
I'll hunt them down with bombs and sword
Whilst batteries will squash.
My dog will send all goodies back
To resupply my friends,
And then I'll find one wooden plank
While day rolls to an end."


But I was thinking of a plan
To catch and trap few Zombs,
And try to cook them on the pan
With carrots and melons.
So, having no reply to give
To words said by old man,
I cried "Come, tell me how you live!"
And gave him opened can.


He sounded milder as he spoke:
He said "I use my skills
To talk to girls, I trick and joke
And charm them to the thrills;
But when it's time to leave our hold
For camp at chilling breeze,
Instead of warmth for bones my old
I get their air-kiss."


But I was thinking of a way
To make 4 zoos at corners,
Thus giving job on every day
For tamers and their mowers.
I shook him well from side to side,
Until his eyes went bright:
"Come, tell me how you live," I cried,
"And what's your name in fact?"

He said "I hunt for Zombie's eyes
Throughout the sandy dunes,
And then collect them in Neclace
That gives off smelly fumes.
Shamans will sell their souls for such
Amusing amulet;
But still refuse to fill the cratch
To better feed my pet."


"As for my name, its many ways -
Sometimes I'm called LeRoy;
They nick me Grumpy other days,
It makes them laugh in joy!
My real name (he gave a wink)
Is hidden in the cracks,
For all my friends with whom i drink
I'll ever be MadMax!"

I heard him then, for I had just
Worked out explicit way
To breed our pets so strong and fast
To make 3 runs each day!
I thanked him much for giving out
His story and his name,
And chiefly for the spirit that
He shown throughout the game.

And now, if e'er by chance I break
My mower at first kill,
Or cannot find in bank an egg
I was supposed to wield,
Or if camp with very low'
Odds to have my guts -
I weep, for it reminds me so
Of that old man I used to know-
Whose look was mild, whose speech was slow
Whose hair was whiter than the snow,
Whose face was very like a crow,
With eyes, like cinders, all aglow,
Who seemed distracted with his woe,
Who rocked his body to and fro,
And muttered mumblingly and low,
As if his mouth were full of dough,
Who snorted like a buffalo-
That summer evening long ago,
A-sitting in the chat
