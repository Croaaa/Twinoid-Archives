Hacksaw
DannyI never heard of event towns. What's that?

An event town is where a group of 40 people (or multiple groups of 40 to pit town versus town) get together to play in a town under an agreed upon set of rules that's outside the normal rules of Die2Nite.

Some examples:
A few seasons ago we had the Clash of the Titans (CotT) (complete with distinction and the just used forum icon). What we did was we got 240 heroes to run in 6 different towns, each townhousing only a single hero class . Winner was the last town to succumb to devastation.

Last season we had an Amazing Race type town. We had 19 teams of two players eacy (38 players in total) with two Guardians to stay in town to prevent devastation. Rules were everyone except the Guardians had to leave town Day 1 and each team could not return to town until it had touched all 4 corners of the map. The winner of the race was the first team to touch all 4 corners and then return to town. For this, both teammates had to touch each corner and then return to town, but if one teammate died, the other could continue on regardless and, if he was the first to return to town, would win it for himself and his teammate. I believe this is how the first "team" one, so there was also honorable mention for the first "full team" (of 2) to finish the race as well.

At current we have an event called the Ultimate Fighting Championship (UFC) going, which pits 3 towns from each of the 4 versions of Hordes in a cross-server competition (that's 12 towns and 480 people in total). Winner of this competition is scheduled to be the one town who amasses the most points (as scored same as on the season leaderboard) except that any points from collecting souls don't count (since not all versions have them). Some things have been evened out accross the servers, such as we're suffering the nighttiime penalty to scavanging (and nighttime bonus to camping) that don't normlly apply in Die2Nite, but other things, such as items (hurling sticks, for instance) are still unique to it's respective server on account that these can't be changed without really screwing up the game's code.

These are only examples of three events I've personally been a part of. There have been more I haven't taken part in that I'm sure others have more information on.

Addendum:
There's also Wondertowns, where up to 40 people jump a town with the intent on hunting down very rare blueprints with the hopes of unlocking and then building one or more wonders and/or building and subsequently blowing up the reactor.

And there's the MMJ (Multi Meta Jump, I think it is, been a while), usually run at the beginning of a season as both a sort of test run of any new features and as a competition between each town to see who can score the most town points (and since this is strictly the Die2Nite server soul points do count). The idea of the jump is to mix up the teams by jumping randomly into distant towns, meaning those who play in metas should hopefully be mixed up pretty well amongs each other as well as with members of the general public. But whether a meta player or a lone wolf all players are usually serious players looking for a legit competition and are agreeing to play seriously and (mostly) cooperatively.

DannyNot sure if i want to join a hardcore town.
It was mentioned before, there seems to be more griefers in hardcore.

If you form a coalition, griefer is much less of an issue.
There are tools you can deal with bad citizens.
You can shun them if they behave badly. It takes 8 votes.
If you can get the gallows built, you can even hang the offender.

A coalition is a group where you can invite people to join in. If all are set ready, one jumps to the town and the rest will follow. The cap is 5 people.

If you manage one more coalition to jump together, you can guarantee a shun when you need it.
Even not, it is still easy to get 3 more votes from other random citizens.

If the offender hit you (5AP) and manage to get you wounded (they can only wound you but not kill you), you will need to find a bandage to cure you. But if you have a group of friends, they will help you to find one and send the bandage to you.

The key is to group together and play with people who share similar minds. So you won't feel hopeless when you are a bad situation. The game will be more enjoyable.

My first game is the most frustrated. I feel better and better when I gather friends to play with me.
