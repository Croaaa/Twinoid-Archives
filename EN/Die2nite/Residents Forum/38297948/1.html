Actually, they aren't. I've been watching it for a while.

Returning residents (those that died in the world beyond for any reason) are not included in the watchtower estimates, or in the attacking horde size as displayed in the gazette, but are added to the attacking horde size.

It's difficult to tell this when the gates are closed, and that's because closed gates will always stop your returning residents from entering the town, no matter how much defense your town has or is lacking. Returning residents seem to always go for the gates, just like they did when they were alive, and are always stopped by closed gates, just like when they were alive.

It's when the gates are left open that you can tell the numbers are acting funny. For example, lets say you have 10000 zombies attack on day 25 with your 10 citizens in town to hold the fort while the rest of the town camps. By the calculator you will have 310 non-lazy zeds attack the 10 residents in town. You leave the gates open, because defense doesn't matter at this point, and you get your 10000 zombies plus 7 returning residents. By the formula, you should have 9690 lazy zeds, but if you read the gazette, it'll tell you you had 9697 lazy zeds. Rest assured, you did have your 310 attacking zeds, but it was out of the 10000 horde size PLUS the 7 returning residents you left the gates open for, thus 10007 zeds in total - 310 attacking = 9697 lazy.

In my opinion, leaving the gates open should add the returning residents to the attacking zed size, meaning leaving the gates open on day 25 with 7 returning residents should result in 317 attacking zeds with 9690 lazy zeds (using the above example of a 10000 zed horde size as would be displayed by the gazette).

Edit: At least, this is how it worked from season 4 through season 8. Haven't had a chance to observe such attacks in season 9 yet.
