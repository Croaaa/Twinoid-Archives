In fiction, you have two kinds of logic: A) Could it happen in the real world? B) Could it happen in this fictional world, given the apparent rules it has?

If you break the world's internal logic, you damage the suspension of disbelief. For example, if your zombies die of a splash of water, they must consistently do so â€“ otherwise the audience/player can get jaded as basically anything can happen without any rhyme or reason.

If the world is close to realism, but has some magic in it, the audience generally expects the realistic parts to work quite realistically, while the magical part can take larger freedoms. Your immersion in the world depends in part on your willingness to accept different kinds of unrealistic things as parts of the fictional setting.
