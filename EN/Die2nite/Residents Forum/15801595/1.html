It has been years since the apocalypse. A team of investigators, reclaiming the sandy wastes, find the wreckage of an old, destroyed town. What resources remain are almost unusable— but, one investigator makes a lucky discovery.

A journal is found, with a strange, Olympics-esque symbol on the cover. Inside, a resident kept a diary all through the lifetime of his town.
This is the story of that town.

This is the story of Laziness of cannibals.

(This is a town journal that I wrote when I was taking part in the Apocalympics. It is being reposted here in parts for your viewing pleasure.)

~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~~

Day 1

We're saved! This morning, MayorGriffonn led me and the other 39 survivors to a small ruin of a town, a beacon in this dense desert. It's not in good shape. There's walls, but they're patchwork things made of metal, and in some places, cardboard.
The town does have a pair of strong gates. No one else seems to be present. There is also a well, full of fresh water. It's been decided: the town will become our new home, as we try to hold out for as long as we can. If the zombies were to attack here, a few might die— from tetanus.

Our first order of business is finding supplies. In our trek from the ruins of our old town, we have eaten every scrap of food, and drank every drop of water we had. When the food ran out, those who collapsed along the way were eaten. 100 survivors became 80, and then 65. By the time our new town appeared in our sight, only 40 of us remained.
We've stepped out of the gates to scavenge supplies for our new home. I am no exception— with a canteen of the well's water, and a tasty-looking melon I found hidden under my new house's mattress, I have trekked to a spot around 5 km from town. It is time to see if the desert will yield anything useful.

Success! After meeting goodnames, one of my fellow survivors, I searched, and have found a roll of duct tape, poking out from beneath the sands. I’ll keep looking.

It’s been over ten hours now. In addition to the tape, I also managed to unearth an old mattress, a half-empty bottle of pills labeled ‘Paracetoid antibiotic’, several twisted old planks, and a chicken! A live chicken! I will head back to town soon, and deliver my findings.

I had to leave the animal behind, as intriguing as it was to see a living being in the desert. The flapping thing took up too much space in my pack, which was already jammed. I found myself having to leave other items behind as I came upon more bounty upon my return trip— a bag full of assorted nuts and bolts. More tape. A strange gun that appeared to use batteries as weapons.
I left a trail of items as I moved towards town, and eventually took back the antibiotic, the bolts, the mattress, and one of the planks. Everything else would have to wait.

I returned to town to discover some of my companions had made improvements. A crude watchtower rose at one corner of the walls (helpfully blocking one of the most cardboard-y sections), and a large building, a barn, had been refurbished as a sort of workshop. Another large building had been designated the ‘bank’, and here we placed the results of our searching.

We had made progress. I placed my bolts on top of the pile (I’d dragged the mattress over to a hole in the outer wall and left it there), and dropped the antibiotic in one corner and the plank in the other.
A pile of dusty papers near where I had put the plank caught my eye. It was a stack of blueprints. One detailed plans for an enhanced lock for the gates. Another, the tower that had already been built. A third explained a means of reinforcing the walls.
This explained how the others had known what to do to improve the town— they had architectural help.

As I examined the piles, mamenity came in with a new blueprint, putting it on the pile. I looked at it. It was some sort of automatic lock for the front gate.
The other scavengers had found useful items as well. FirebugZane had found a busted old coffee machine somewhere, while MayorGriffonn had found several pints of beer, hardly even dusty.

A shout from the tower broke me out of my overview. ishimu and KittyKatz were perched at the top of it, and they had disquieting news.
Some of the zombies from our old town had followed us. From the sound of it, about 20 to 50 were slowly shuffling towards the gates. They’d be here by midnight.
I climbed the ladder and took a look myself. There were less than 50, that much was certain. KittyKatz revised the estimate down a bit. “OK, 20 to 40, then,” he said.

We need to be all inside the town before midnight. Four of the survivors had gotten trapped by zombies in the desert, and had to be recovered. (We’ve found one person can fend off about two zombies on their own. More than that, you’re in trouble.)
As I write this, the horde has come closer; they will hit us in around an hour. 7 people are still outside. May they make it home safely.


The horde is almost upon us. I have spent my last ounce of strength working on a pump for the town well. ReplaY021 has closed the gates. All I can do now is hide in my tent.

May we survive. May those poor three left outside the walls live through the night.

Day 2

Last night, I dreamed of a kingdom.
A great country, where one could grow, and hold territory. The dream changed to a grid of changing symbols, gold, people, food, wondrous things... and death, inevitable as time passed. War and diplomacy, ruin and creation. The cycle of life continued.
The clanging of the watchtower bell woke me.

We had survived our first night. 24 zombies had approached the town, but the walls had held firm. Less happily, the three caught outside— goodnames, funi, and Vinin— didn’t survive their night in the desert. May they rest in peace (though I suspect they won’t.)
MayorGriffonn has insisted from now on that if we scavenge far, we must bring a partner with us.

My partner for today is ReplaY021. I have met with him 4km east of town and brought him some food— along the way, I gave a steroid pill I’d found to AnachroNia. He’ll see it home safely.
We have journeyed even farther east, making a loop. There are a number of scavenged resources here, left behind from yesterday. I found a large, heavy toolbox of some sort, as well as more bolts. ReplaY has found an old copper pipe, and a battery gun like the one I found the day before. We’ll now start heading back, and scavenge along the way.

We’ve now been scavenging for several hours. I found a knife, and pried open the toolbox. Inside were more nuts and bolts. It’s a relief not to have to carry that heavy thing around. We’ve agreed to split up, and search separate (but adjacent) areas of the desert.

~

It’s been several hours, and I can hear it over the radio. Arzanian has snapped! He was trapped in the desert by some zombies, and now he keeps ranting about wanting to kill KittyKatt! We’re trying to find out why...
Oh my god. Arzanian’s said he killed his... last town? Not quite sure what he’s talking about. He says he’ll go back to helping, but... if my tent could be locked, I’d lock it. He says everything’ll be better after he kills KittyKatt. What is going on here?!

Oh. After we tried our best to talk Arzinian down, he suddenly burst out laughing. He says he was kinda serious about it at first, but we kept carrying on about it so much that he decided to string us along a bit. Then he pointed out that it would be extremely hard for him to kill KittyKatz.
I feel kinda stupid for falling for that.
Going to move a bit towards town. I’ve dug so many holes that this stretch of desert looks like it has measles.

ReplaY and I met up a few hours later. Our journey home took us through a region with two zombies, both of which ReplaY obliterated with his battery-firing gun. We danced with joy when the walls of the town finally came into view.

~

The town looked even better now that I’ve returned. Many citizens have already set up crude tents. Should some lucky zombie breach these walls, perhaps the thick canvas will dissuade them. We’re also building a ‘study’, for the drafting of blueprints— one of the prints in the bank had a scrawled note that if you bring vodka and steroids enough together, people can get suddenly creative.
I don’t understand how that works.

Also, DrJamez found an old telescope, sitting at the back of an old cabinet. KittyKatz has installed it in the watchtower. Not only can we see the zones around us, we can even get a glimpse of far distant lands.
We are not alone. There are 6 other towns in the wastes with us. They are far away— too far to travel in a day, or two, or even three. But we can see them.
The other towns appear to be doing well. All are bustling, and each sport their own watchtower, as well as wall upgrades similar to our own. One has a pump; two others have the same study we’ve built, and a third has it half-done.

We can see hordes of zombies approaching the other towns, too. Each town has a name; after KillSkill joked that ‘we were too lazy to become zombies’, we’ve called ourselves the town of Lazy Cannibals. Linktt painted up a big sign above the town gate.
We can’t really read the other town’s signs through the telescope, so we’ve taken turns making up names instead. One town, the Crazy Centre of Lost Souls, has an extra-big horde coming for it— 50 or 60 zombies! I hope they can survive intact. They have reinforced their fences with coils of thick barbed wire.

We have only a few zombies coming for us, and the reinforced walls will easily stop them.

~

Is it wrong to do a bad thing for the greater good? vasarhai was keeping a blueprint he’d found in his house, ‘for later’, he said. I couldn’t think why he’d want to save a possible key to our survival for later— it’s not like food, where you eat it and it’s gone.
So: I stole it.
The print turned out to detail a powerful form of armor plate to reinforce the walls. It’s not cheap— the amount of wood and iron required to build it is quite impressive— but it’s worth it.

I am worn out by exploring. I will rest now. When I wake up, the attack will be over and a new day will be beginning.

Day 3

Today dawned pleasantly. Everyone made it in safely last night. Three people— Dath, mamenity and AmanRawr— decided to camp out in some of the abandoned buildings within the desert. They returned with a valuable blueprint each. Our ‘study’ has generated a print of its own. As well, other citizens have scrounged up architectural diagrams.
The new blueprints correspond to the following items:
• Some sort of mechanical upgrade for the pump. It appears it will require a solid foundation to work, though.
• A faucet, to fill water weapons from the well. It’ll also need a foundation.
• A drill for the pump, once we get one.
• Cannons that fire water, to fend off the zombie horde!
• Some sort of lighthouse... I’m not sure what it’s for, but it looks useful.
• A gadget that fits around the watchtower telescope and counts the number of zombies in any location in the desert.
• And, a sketch of... something involving a big mound of food? I don’t know.

It turns out that the 20 zombies that attacked us yesterday were just the small fish swimming in front of the shark. The watchtower reports a good horde of 100 zombies coming at us today! When I woke up, people had already taken action. The ‘wall’ blueprint I took yesterday was now a real thing. Another group of people had dug a large pit around the town, and some others had fashioned barbed wire above the pit. Still, from the looks of it, we could end up with 10 or 15 zombies inside the walls unless someone builds something more.

I have gone out scavenging one more time. A quick check of the desert map we keep in town showed a bag of cement, some ways southeast of the town. I managed to trek out there, then poured my water ration onto the bag to save space in my pack; I have found a pen-knife, which should hopefully protect me from the zombie horde. After a ‘lunch’ of old chewing gum, I went as far south as I dared. I wasn’t alone— two zombies warily circled me. I stabbed one with my knife.
Nothing happened. I blinked, pulled the knife out, and stabbed the zombie again. This time, the penknife blade shattered.
Cursing, I got as far away as I could before digging my first hole to search.

It’s been a few hours. The area’s almost certainly picked clean now. I’ve found some usable iron, a plank, and a large 12V battery. Time to head back to town.

Things are much the same now that I’ve returned. The town has a pump for the well, now, and we’re readying construction of some piles of supplies outside the walls, where we can grab them for emergencies. They should close the gap and protect us from the zombie horde tonight. I’ve helped fill one of the piles.

Argh! What is SexToys doing building a foundation in the center of town?! If people keep up like this, someone is going to die tonight.
I have taken some of the iron from the construction site to my tent, and am now sitting on it (literally). There is enough to accumulate the emergency supplies, but not enough to reinforce the foundation. This should set the building on track.

I took a quick nap, as I was tired out from all the work I’d done. When I woke, there was an angry note on my door, ranting about blocking construction sites.
I don’t know who it was from, but I looked around and saw the supply piles were finished. I dragged the metal back to the bank.
The foundation in the center of town was also finished— I’m happy this town is so productive. We have just enough defense to turn away the entire zombie horde tonight.

Up on the watchtower, vasarhai has been doing some time with the telescope. Crazy Centre lost two more people tonight, and don’t have the blueprint study we built. I hope they can hold out for a while. Another town, Roads of Sand, has what looks like half the mattresses in the world propped up against the front gates!! They don’t have many buildings near town to search, though, and they don’t have a study either.

The third town out there, Insanitary Bay of Cyclops, is facing a huge horde! Huger than ours, that is. They’ll be able to keep out only half the zombies, from the looks of it. I fear for their survival...
They have a lovely vegetable patch, according to vasarhai. I’m starting to get hungry.
The other three towns seem to have everything very shipshape.

One more thing before I go back to sleep: Linktt ate an old, meaty bone that someone found after the attack on the first day. I don’t know what was in it, but he came down with a high fever... so he used the paracetoid antibiotic I brought in. I’m not sure why he ate something so foul-smelling, but hopefully we can use the bone for something.

Closing the gates now. I wonder how things’ll be tomorrow?

Day 4

Last night, I dreamed of a snake.
A fruit-eating snake, slithering around on a playing-field made of cards, lusting after fruit as it grew longer and longer. Could it find a way out, or was it doomed to crash and die?
The question occupied me long after I woke up.

Today, me and some others went on a scavenging trip to a buried ruin southeast of town. I was told to bring food and water, as well as a water pistol to fight off the zombies.
However, the bank was completely out of food! I grabbed a bottle of vodka instead. Myself, Dath, MayorGriffonn, and Taylork2 set out from town in a group.

Our trip south was uneventful. When we started walking east, we ran into zombies. Dath hacked some down with his machete, and Taylork2 got three with his water pistol. I cleared out zombies around the ruin itself with mine.
As we were approaching the ruin, we heard a human voice hailing us. It was fdamned26! He tossed us some steroids and stayed to help us clear the area.
Clearing buildings is tiring work. I need a pick-me-up.
It’s time to drink an entire bottle of vodka.

~

[what follows is written in a shaky, and nearly illegible, hand]

I fell in a hole!

This building is— is— what is it? No, it’s not a building yet. It’s a site of constructions. Like the constructions in our town. Was this a town? What was this?

There’s twice as many zombies here as there should be! Good thing everybody’s split into two to finish them off.

I fell in another hole!

There’s two many too many whole holes here. I’m going to load up our old stuff and search back over backwards.

I think I tripped into a hole! Wheee

Who keeps digging these holes, anyway? Is it me?

I found a can! And metal? Like that metal I tripped over in the building!

You know what I think? I think this town is... I think I am.... I think I gotta...

[The rest of the page is splattered with something thick and putrid. At the bottom of the page, someone has added an annotation in thick black ink:]

Note to self: Do not explore while drunk. Finding things is almost impossible, and you will fall on your ass.

[A further annotation has been added in the margin:]

Repeatedly.

~

Sorry about that. After drinking myself into a stupor, I decided to head back, packed up the depleted water pistols we’d used into a manbag and started retracing my steps. I also grabbed a large metal support that was lying around the building (it turned out to be an abandoned construction site). Dath and MayorGriffinn are camping overnight at the site, in hopes of finding something useful.
On my way home, I found some useful iron and an unopened can, lying where people had found them. I packed those into the bag as well.
The only thing I was able to dig up was a few rotting logs. I left them where they were. We can pick them up if we really need them.

Once I got home, I discovered something very worrying.
The well is dry.
It’s not as bad as it sounds. There’s a big pile of water rations stacked up in the bank, and we have blueprints for projects that’ll dig deeper and find more water. The town will survive today’s outbreak easily— thanks to the study, today’s blueprint (plywood wall reinforcement) has already been added to our ever-thickening walls. There’s a project underway to build a drilling rig and refill the well. That should take some of the pressure off.
We also found a sketch of a crude henhouse. If we can find chickens to put in it, the townspeople will enjoy fresh eggs!

Good news from the watchtower: Insanitary Bay was able to raise its defenses in time. Crazy Centre of Lost Souls, however, lost a lot of people— most of whom were locked out when the citizens had to close the gates. The horde approaching them today appears to be relatively low, however.
Taciturn Lakes of Thirst is having problems. They have 30 or 40 more zombies coming than their walls are prepared to handle! I hope they can find the defenses to repel them...

I will take a break from scavenging tomorrow. I had a nap when I got back, but I still feel horrible. Don’t drink and scavenge, kids.
~Goodnight~

Day 5
Ah, my head! It feels like it’s been split open. I’m not doing that again.
We lost four more citizens today. Arzanian, Koola, SexToys, and SirTakis were all caught outside when GabitzBoy closed the gates. I’ll hope they rest peacefully, in perhaps a pond or lake. The three who died before assaulted us the next night as zombies.
It would have been more, but FirebugZane was saved by mamenity.

The zombie horde has swelled. I have been on construction duty today, and we have built the drilling rig. The well is no longer dry; with it active, we have constructed a misting device that should stop a large number of zombies. We are also constructing cannon emplacements, and, if we can gather the materials, a flock of crude scarecrows that should confuse the zombies.
It may not be enough. We expect up to 30 zombies inside the walls tonight. Perhaps we can deepen the pit to make up the total.

We are not the only ones facing problems. All of the other towns except The Hospital of Bees have more zombies charging at them than they are prepared to handle. May they survive! From the crows flying over the other towns, it is easy to see that some have had more losses than others. Crazy Centre of Lost Souls was particularly hard-hit.

Upon hearing the news about the zombies, ReplaY021 got a sullen look, and started talking about committing suicide. About half-a-dozen other citizens have talked him out of it— we found cyanide, and now MayorGriffonn has taken it for safekeeping so he does not try to use it.
Town morale is uncertain.

We don’t have enough time to make the scarecrows. We’re going to set up some spikes instead and hope the zombies wander into it. IceKunni has gone out too far, and is at the Construction Site. He is going to camp there— it’s that or die on the sands.
Here in town, we will deepen the pit, but I don’t think we have time to even build the spikes. If we’re lucky, that’ll be enough. If not, at most 5 people will die. I think.

I’ve closed the gates, and I’m going to hide under my mattress.

Day 6
Last night, I couldn’t sleep. I sat rigid in my tent and listened to the town walls bowing in under the pressure of 237 zombies.
However, the walls held. No zombies made it in with us. IceKunni, trapped at the construction site, also survived.


This morning, there was a big argument about what to do. fdamned26 insisted he wanted to visit an abandoned bunker, over 9 km away. We talked him into saving IceKunni instead, and he opened the gates and charged out as fast as he could.

Meanwhile, there was the question of how to hold off the horde. We are reinforcing the walls, and have a blueprint for some large cannon that will fire water at the zombies. However, it needs time to get up, and I’m not sure the well can handle it.
We’ve decided to work on that and the mechanical pump upgrade, but you should have heard the arguments before we decided!

~

Just heard a call for help over the radio. For some strange reason, fdamned26 dropped his water pistol in the desert and is now trapped by zombies. I’ve got rescue duty— have loaded up with steroids, battery launcher, and water for me and am heading there at all speed.

Have killed 2 zombies where fdamned26 is. The zone, amazingly, hasn’t been picked clean! I’m going to stay and search a little while.

~

This piece of ground has been amazingly productive. IceKunni joined me, and we just finished digging through the last of the area. We found a battery gun, a strange green costume, and some food and drinks! Also, raw materials and iron (which the town sorely needs for building.) I am taking back the steroids I came in with (didn’t need), some food, a screwdriver fdamned26 took to open chests, and some iron back to town. IceKunni says he’ll follow fdamned26 to safety.
Rescue achieved!

In town now. Everybody is packed into the workshop, turning metal into support beams for the mechanical pump and water turrets. We will just about be able to make the zombie defense today, either by deepening the pit, or by erecting some flimsy wooden spikes by the front gates (we almost finished them yesterday, but never actually put them out there.) We’ll make it, today and tomorrow!

The telescope shows several of the other towns might be in trouble. Crazy Centre of Lost Souls and The Outdated Port have many zombies approaching them.
Also, something strange: Three people have fallen around the well in Taciturn Lakes of Thirst, hands reaching desperately towards it. Are they living up to their name?

The spikes are in place. Omnifire put the finishing touches on them, though I don’t quite understand the purpose of the fuzzy dice hanging from the largest one. The rest of the town is working on augmenting the pump a bit. We’re getting a little more water; it won’t be like the yield when the mechanical pump is completed, but it’ll tide us over until then.
Morale is high. We have a decent chance of survival.

We can do this!

Day 7

The sound of hammering and sawing woke me today. 6 zombies got through the walls last night, and Prolapse was scared stiff! No one was eaten, though, and Prolapse calmed down after smoking a cigarette.

While I was out in the desert yesterday, everyone in town was working on readying the mechanical pump. It’s now finished. The well is once again full, and a number of people have breathed sighs of relief. People are filling canteens and putting them into the bank, so that the water turrets can finally be finished.

I’m out to scavenge raw materials from the depleted areas around town. On my way out, though, I’ve seen the turrets— they’re mounted on the mounds we constructed earlier, and look like nothing so much as giant lawn sprinklers. The zombie horde tonight is in for a surprise.

~

Yipe! I walked a little ways east of town, and suddenly I was trapped by four zombies! I didn’t know they’d come right up near the town walls. There were two logs on the ground. I rolled the logs into my pack, then signaled to the watchtower.

Three days ago, someone came up with a bright idea: Keep a coil of rope and a clever pulley attachment in the tower. Then, if a citizen is close enough and wants to come in, they load up the rope and toss one end to the scavenger, who ties the rope to anything really heavy, and holds on tight themselves. I waved, and they tossed the rope.
Once I was fully hanging on, GabitzBoy, on tower duty, grabbed on to the other end of the rope and jumped off the tower, his weight pulling me over the walls (I had to push off with my feet to avoid the barbed wire) and into the tower myself.

After my fright, I decided to scavenge somewhere to the west of town, instead. I found one more log there; then, I walked back to the town gates and dumped my bounty of logs into the Bank.

~

The turrets are now fully operational. A quick test-firing has blotted a wet arc in the desert. It won’t quite be enough to stem off the horde coming at us today, though. Over 400 zombies! We are gathering materials to build some crude scarecrows around the pit. Last night’s spikes served us well, skewering 15 zombies— but, they broke. We are rebuilding them as well.

AmanRawr has set off into the desert to camp in the small house, northeast of the town. vasarhai has gone to help him improve the camp site. However, AmanRawr has returned, instead! It seems they changed their minds about who’s staying the night.

Meanwhile, some very strange news from the telescope crew. Mabachi said he saw a strange building in front of Crazy Centre of Lost Souls. Sort of a cage, with a chair in it. If someone sat in it (or was locked in it!) it’d certainly attract zombies, but... I’m not sure that person would survive the night. Is the town so desperate to sacrifice its own?
Outdated Port might be having defense trouble again— we counted 6 zombie-munched corpses there early this morning. We’ll have to see what happens.

We have been low on food for some time. I have had chances to eat, sometimes... I think the last time was two days ago. Most food gets given to people who are scavenging in the desert— sometimes they find old takeout bags, as well.
The exception is the meaty bones stacked in a corner of the bank. After what happened to Linktt, no one wants to touch them. We think they’re human, too.

fdamned26 came into town just now, dragging a big crate of furniture parts behind him. When assembled, it turned out to be a sturdy wooden table. This’ll be the perfect addition to the workshop! Now it won’t be quite SO tiring to saw up the desert’s logs and smelt its scrap metal.
The spikes are done, and we’ll probably upgrade the shop tomorrow. The gates are closed, too.
Let’s survive with the best of them.

Day 8

Last night, I dreamed of an arena.
A great, eternal arena, where men and women in bright costumes (it’s been so long since we had any nice clothing) took up a variety of weapons, fighting against one another, in elaborate tournaments and rankings. At the end of the fight, one person kneels and one person raises an arm in victory, and every day, contestants return to full vigor.

I miss the pre-zombie world.


This morning, the sound of angry shouting was my alarm clock. Apparently, in addition to the furniture, fdamned26 brought home a large plank, which he used to fix up his house. I’m not entirely sure who complained about it, but now the bulk of the townsfolk have banished him. All his stuff has been put into the bank, and a good half the town will chase him away from construction sites now.
Me and MayorGriffonn both think it was pointless.

More bad news: vasarhai hasn’t returned from his ‘desert vacation’. LLIBED saw his mangled corpse by the small house in the desert.
Because fdamned has been banished, about the only things he can do now are scavenge outside, or camp. We’ve decided he could try and camp where vasarhai died.
I’ve cleared his way a bit, and left him water and a water gun so he can get there.

The study produced a blueprint for a large gun that fires metal plates at the zombies. There’s a nifty rewind mechanism that pulls the plates back in for later use, too. We have built it. This cannon should be able to handle the current horde tonight.
What I’d like to know is, where is the horde coming from? We’ve only lost 9 people, but we’ve got over 450 zombies coming at us tonight. Whenever we dig in the desert, we never find corpses. Would a horde of zombies really trek across the desert for 31 human survivors?

New trouble: mamenity was trapped by zombies while exploring a zone that we hadn’t dug to shreds yet. I took a water pistol and went to bail him out. Once 3 of the 5 zombies trapping him were reduced to stinking puddles, I started digging alongside him.

~

Today, I got up early, just as the last of the water was being emptied from the well. I kind of freaked out. We needed that water for the turrets, after all.

~

OK, we finally have a plan for the day. We have to build 5 different items! There’s the Water Catcher, the Water Purifier (using our last bolts!), the Spikes, some more Armor Plating (we got the blueprint this morning), and we’ll reload that metal-firing cannon.
With the Purifier up, we’ll be able to filter out all our old jerrycans of water (we have 5 or 6 more).

Day 12

Last night, I dreamed of a happier place. A place full of games, all sorts of different amusements. Then, competition! People banding together, completing game-missions, solving puzzles, trying to do better than every other band.
There were strangely colored gems and stars in there too. It sounded like a nice place.

Some people are camping. Others (including me) are reinforcing their shacks into proper houses, and piling anything heavy they can find against the front door. My best guess is, staying in town gives chances of 50/50— or less. The bank is quickly emptying.
I have taken one of the gnawed arm bones, to give me a boost in an emergency. Will I really need such a gross thing? I hope not, but...

There is an attitude of despair in town. We were fortunate last night— 44 zombies got through the walls, but only one person— lemonpassion— was eaten. 7 people were scared out of their wits, though.
Our fellow towns are in dire straits as well. Insanitary Bay of Cyclops and Roads of Sand have only 8 citizens left. They’ll both be dead soon. The Hospital of Bees can only hang on for a bit longer before the dehydration deaths start. They and Taciturn Lakes are facing hordes only slightly smaller than ours. The Outdated Port’s, by comparison, is the biggest wave of zombies I have ever seen.
I fear for all of them. If I am alive tomorrow morning, I will update this with the other towns’ situations.

Mamenity has opened the gates, and several people have taken their belongings and headed out. How did it come to this? We thought we were saved when we first saw the town. Even the knowledge that 24 zombies had followed us was not enough to dampen our high spirits. But now...
24 turned into 1240...
Why does it have to end this way? Has anyone in the entire world survived without this threat? Is there some remote mountain where the zombies could not reach?

The Bank is almost empty now. The number of available materials is slowly depleting. The energy-boosting drugs have been snapped up. So has the alcohol. There is no water, though those that remain will upgrade the pump, in hopes of hanging on for a few more hours. Someone even took our one working radio.

DrJames and omnifire have left town. fdamned has decided to stand outside the gates tonight, arms spread wide, to end it all. I am hiding in my house. I have passed a torch to FoxMcCloud, to help scare one more zombie away. The sounds of zombies are audible even now, an hour before the attack.

This is the end. I leave the rest up to you, now.
