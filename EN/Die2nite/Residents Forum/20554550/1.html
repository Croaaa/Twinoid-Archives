To The Future of Mankind if There is One,

If you're reading this, I hope you're reading this in a time of piece. I, lived in a time of darkness and despair. You could say that it was the darkest days of mankind. I lived in a town with four other friends, among fourty people, always struggling to survive against "them". But one day, the unthinkable happened. One night, when fending off against the attack, "they" made it over our walls. To put it in the simplest way I can, it was a complete massacre. A lot of people died that day; I don't know how many, but me and four other people escaped the carnage together. As the night wore on, we struggled to find a place we could hide at. We ran, and kept running, because it was night time and we knew those things wouldn't be far away from us. A lot of times, I almost gave up hope. But looking at the four people that were with me, that were supporting me, that I was supporting, I knew I needed to go on.
It was early morning when they finally stopped. We spent a little while to catch our breath from that horrific night. As far as we knew, everyone from our town was gone, and there was nothing to go back to. But looking up, Dirk had spotted something we could go to. The town that he had found was quite abandoned and empty, but unlike our old town, it was still standing. Looking around, we found that there were about 4 people who had been there before us. They had told us a similar story about how they had run away from the carnage and destruction of their town. Slowly throughout the day, people came to the town seeking refuge. A few people stayed behind to work on building important construction projects, while a lot of us went out to find materials and food. We were lucky, because the town seemed like it had water for several days, unlike my group's previous town that had already had a quickly dwindling water supply.

After some discussion in town, it was quickly agreed upon creating a text form of communication that could be used even if one was outside of town. We called this method of communication the town forum. I quickly found that there were a variety of uses for the town forum. For example, in my exploration, I found myself trapped by a group of eight of "them". They were quite docile, but I knew from experience, that as soon as I tried anything, that they would attack me. I put a message on the town forum asking for help, and very quickly, a small group came out of town to assist me. What I couldn't handle alone, we were able to hold off together.
After the situation was under control, we realized that we were standing upon the grounds of an old abandoned construction site. A place like this would likely be a treasure trove of building materials. At the least, much more supple than trying to scour the barren wasteland. So we all searched around a number of hours before we decided it would be best to head back. I can't exactly say our town will never need anything again, but we came back with a full load from our trip.
Back in town, I found that a number of things were going on for those who had worked hard and were resting for the day. There were training seminars going on in teaching other residents how to efficiently search for materials out in the wasteland, and seminars about what to do if they ever missed gate closing time. Along with that, there were also different games of cards going on, such as poker, Black Jack, and a coin guessing game. It seemed like everyone was working well together and having fun together. At that time, I was actually pretty bushed myself, so I dumped my find into the town bank, and went to join the others in some black jack and poker before the night ended.
While walking back from the poker table, I notticed an ad on the town forum for people to join the watchmen. There was no payment offered really, unless you counted security. I was pretty tired, but I didn't want this town to end up like my old one. So I signed up.
The attack that night was quite brutal. One after another, they came at us, piling over each other to try to get over the walls of our town. Unfortunately, that ended up working. A small number of them got over our town walls, and it was now time for the watchmen to keep them away from the rest of the town; this included me. None of us really had a weapon, but we fought hard to fight for everyone's lives. It felt like forever, but finally morning came, and they mysteriously just left.
Checking the town newspaper, which was made by a couple citizens that weren't on watch, I checked the survivor count. Everyone in town had survived an attack; that is only people who were in town survived. Unfortunately, there were 3 people who had been outside town when the gate had been closed who hadn't made it through the night. It was sad, really, that we lost 3 people all in one night. But, for the 37 people that were still in town, we had to keep on going.This is the story of our dull gray lives in t he Arcane Junctions.

I honestly can't imagine why anyone would be reading this. After all, I'd rather leave this all behind forever myself. But in the end, this is still where I am. This second day for the most part was rather uneventful. There are only a few small points to bring up today. Everyone seems to be cooperating more smoothly than the first day. With everyone working together in groups, no one seemed to get into a dangerous situation.

However, near the end of the day there was a little bit of trouble. There were four different people outside, and the gates were going to be closed in less than twenty minutes. The worst part is, one of them was a member of my coalition. Vyooloo had just been speaking to everyone over the town forum a few minutes before, but suddenly, she wasn't responding to me when I called her to come back. At that point, I became worried. But, a hero by the name of Steef understood what had happened, and performed a truly heroic rescue, bringing her back to town.

After that, there were only three people outside. I continued calling out to those people, telling them that it was getting really late, but only one of them responded. His full name is LostAlien, but most people just call him lost. Lost is another one of those spectacular heroes, but probably also one of those crazy ones. I mean, he was insane enough to actually decide he wanted to stay out over night. He wasn't giving up, he truly thought he stood a chance being out over night. He said he had found a building that he thought there would be a helpful blueprint is. I don't know if that's true, but he's supposedly a trained survivalist, so I just decided to hope that he'd be alright.

In the end, Lost ended up being amazing, rather than crazy. Looking at the daily morning newspaper, I looked to see who had made it through the night. With our defenses, "they" didn't even make it past our walls. As for those outside, Lost did in fact make it through the night, and even crazier, ended up finding that blueprint he was looking for. Too bad I couldn't say the same for the others who spent the night out doors. According to the Gazette, neither of them made it through the night. I don't know exactly what happened, but I do know that they won't be coming back anytime soon. I guess it's time to work on getting Lost out of the world beyond before he gets himself killed. It's a bit of a pain to try to figure this all out sometimes, but I guess that's just a part of my dull gray life.

To The Future of Mankind, if There is One,

Today started out as what seemed like would be a rather inconspicuous one. Things started out business as usual as I left town to begin my search in the world beyond our little town. It felt like a very satisfying day until it began to end.

Heading back into the town, I performed my daily ritual of checking to see if anyone was still outside of town. There was only about an hour left, so it was dangerous for anyone to stay outside of the town. On the map, I saw that there were about 4 people outside of town still which worried me. One was Lost, who had already been camping out the night before. He was planning on a second night in a row because he suspected another set of blue prints. I decided to myself that if he made it again, I'd take a drink with him when he got back. Anyone that hard core deserves a little something for t he risks they take. Looking at the other three names that weren't checked into town yet, my face paled as I saw one particular name.

A member of my coalition, Vyooloo was once again outside of town. There was no message from her, so I was sure that there was something wrong. People wanted to help her and the others that were stuck inside, but we just weren't able to get to them. To top it off, another member of my coalition, Dirk, found a mysterious letter from a person he was sure was his uncle, with a message that his uncle was in danger. He knew the dangers of leaving the town, but he decided that he had to go to his uncle no matter what. So with the decision to leave our coalition, likely for good, Dirk set out for the sake of family.

Really, the end result of the night was quite horrible. Even though I managed to save a person by the name of Greggy, there was still a large amount of victims in the end. A small number of people in town had lost hope, and simply hadn't drank any water in two days, and they died of dehydration. Another 3 people, including my friend Vyooloo disappeared in the middle of the night. Vyoo probably isn't dead, I kept telling myself that. She was pretty good at hiding, she was probably out there some where. But with "them" closing in towards our town, she probably doesn't have a way to get back to us. Wherever she is, I hope she's okay. We lost 10 people tonight, and things seem like they're only going to get worse from now on.

To the Future of Mankind if There is One,

Today was another day of mixed blessings and sadness. Today seemed to be going rather well. Everyone, at least who talked on the town forums seemed to be busy, all doing their parts. Everyone was working hard to find supplies, or convert supplies we already had in order to make the Evolutive Wall. Quite a few people have tried to warn everyone that at this point, we need to ration our water even further than we previously had before, or risk dying of thirst. But it seemed like everyone was ignoring us, as when I looked at our water supplies in the well, we only had three rations of water left. We started with around 25 rations of water today, and now we only have three. I convinced everyone to vote in the daily town meeting for the town effort of upgrading our pump, but with only 20 extra rations of water coming out of that, we can only last so long, as long as everyone keeps drinking all the water like this.

If that wasn't bad enough, we lost two people today. The first was a resident named Methodeck. I didn't really know much about the guy. He didn't work in the work shop, he never went out to search for supplies....really, he was a pretty quiet guy. I would assume he helped in the construction projects, but spending most of my time in the world beyond, I honestly have no idea if he even did that.

While Methodeck was an enigma that I, honestly have to say I won't miss much, we did lose someone very important to the town today. After taking all those risks thus far, it seems like Lost finally ran out of luck. He'd come back from another expedition, when he saw that there was a call for help in the world beyond. He immediately ran out there in order to help the person that had been stuck outside. The person had quite literally ran out of energy, becoming so tired, hungry and thirsty, that his body just wouldn't respond. Lost ran out there to help him, despite how tired he already was, and tried to use his survival book to find some water for the man. Unfortunately, he realized all too late that the tips for finding supplies out in the world beyond didn't apply so close to civilization, and he knew only one of them would be making it back to town that night. He selflessly gave the citizen his last ration of water, and bid everyone else ado. We must continue to survive, but Lost will be missed.

To the future of mankind... but I don't think there'll even be one now,

Last night attack was one of the longest that'd happened to our town. What usually took them seven minutes now took them nine, or maybe more, I wasn't sure. Obviously, some struggling had taken place. I shared the same fate as eight others this morning; finding myself in a terrorized state. Voices echoed within the skull of my head, images of their dark set of teeth and their rough-edged fingernails flashed before my eyes as they seemed to reach out for me and my limbs simply couldn't stop shaking. It was a close encounter and nothing like how they were in the daytime.

I am Lyzan, an acquaintance to ManInGray. I happened to come across his journal when I visited his room, right after I'd gotten a grip of myself. He was gone by the time I reached there. Checking the town's gazette, I learned that there's thirteen of us left and also saw the names of those whose body was taken outside. Gray, was on that list. Climbing up the watch-tower with sadness on my expression, I scanned the surroundings. Approximately over six hundred of them had gathered.

Our chance now lies beyond the safety of our walls, I thought. Staying here any longer would be suicidal. So I told them to conserve their strength, to take some rations and some defensive objects with them for their campsite. I headed south myself with the intention to have those terrorized follow my trail as I take the zombies on. I cleared the first two zones with ease but I ran into trouble on the third. I ran out of water-bombs and had broke the hurling stick. Things weren't looking good for me. An abandoned construction site was just ahead. That's where I was headed to, thinking that the structure could at least hide us from tonight's attack. Time was running out; for me at least, and I couldn't afford risking myself to be stranded and rest without setting up a camp.

So I did what I wouldn't quite encourage others to do, I fled the zone. It was a desperate move. In my attempt to escape, I was scratched on a wrist pretty deep. Reaching the targeted site, I started working on a particular hiding spot. I warned the others about the overwhelming numbers before my current location then so they wouldn't be trapped. But I was a tad too late, CprlKirk was right on my trail along with one other. A few more of our people were scattered to the west while a handful decided to remain in the town. Wishing the best to everyone, I went into hiding way before it'd gotten dark. With the faintest of light, I wrote and continued this journal to honor those who'd struggled with us along the way. Without them, we couldn't have made it this far. I may not be able to write again tomorrow though. With my wound and the lingering threats that were moaning outside, I saw no hope.

No hope at all.

~Lyzan

Oh lookie here, I didn't expect to find my journal out here. For a bout two our three days now, I've been hiding out in a ruins somewhere away from town. The other watchmen would be here soon to help the few of us here, but I knew by then that it would be far too late for me. So I did the only thing a coward like me could do; I ran. Dropping my weapon, I ran as hard and fast as I could to stay out of the reach of those things I knew were there.

My plan was that I'd use the system that we used to pull citizens into town, to get out. Hurriedly, I climbed my way up the watch tower, almost losing my footing a couple times in my panic. But I managed to make it to the top of the tower where the system was. I looked down and paled as I saw them climbing on top of each other to get up the tower. It would probably take a while for them to get to me because so few were actually after me.

But I knew they still would, and that on one's own, it could take a very long time to pull the carriage across the rope to the other side.
