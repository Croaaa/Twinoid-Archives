Ghost

Well, He had done it again.
As he entered the town of the gates, the 12 remaining citizens of his current shanty-town, smiled at him, and then at the old blue construction plansheet in his hands.

"He has returned with a BluePrint" Some Yell.
"Ah Ghost! The squatter does it again!" Others exclaimed.

Out of his many Nicknames:
"Ghost" (The name he had adopted when the World went straight to hell)

"The Chef" (Based on all the wonderous meals he has created. He takes a liking to cooking it seems. Well, as far as cooking goes when you use moldy bread and sand as main ingredients.)

"Cattle Thief" (He never seemed to hesitate taking items from the dead.)

And then his most recent Nickname, Squatter... He was not fond of it, but he did enjoy camping. And he was good at it to.

In any means, it was time to crack open this new Blueprint, and see what he had discovered!
After hours of trying to figure out what it meant, there was a message found on the back of the Blueprint. It read:

PROPERTY OF VILE DUNE.
WHAT THE WASTES HAVE YIELDED US SHALL PROSPER IN OUR HANDS.
WE WILL NOT FALL.

Vile Dune?
That sounded... Familiar. A rush of feelings. A short lived take. Knowledge.

Snap out of it! Ghost thought to himself, ripping off the small corner of the map with the side note, and burning it in the candle next to him.

After a day or so of deep thought, the plans had been deciphered! They were:

A... nuclear Reactor?

The residents decided to go for it. The power would no doubt bring them success in living a prosperous and long life within the wastes.
A week went on. Makeshift capacitors were duck-taped to supermagnets, which were set into place with stones, while the reactor, made up of everyday supplies, was all suspended with wood beams and more duct tape. The whole thing was just a mess. But it would work. It had to.
But that is just when things started to get bad. Very bad.

As one of the construction workers carried a heavy haul of wrought Iron, he dropped it. Hitting into the not-yet-working reactor, the iron was vaporized. Not to mention his arm.
As the mutilated man squirmed and screamed on the ground, the reactor tipped over, and chaos began.

Settling down, it was decided that a new Blue Print was needed to fix this mess. And only one person here could do just that...

As Ghost looked back, he saw his fellow residents waving at him. Something told him that he should not return. That a danger awaited him...



That night, Ghost camped out at an old Silo. And when he woke up, something strange sat next to him. A crow.

The red Beady eyes glanced at him, then in the direction towards town.

The ground shook, and what happened next was foggy and unclear.

All that Ghost knows, is that when he next woke up, he was sitting in a tipped Silo, and the land all around him had been replaced with a green foggish setting.



His memory became clear. he was not sure from where, or what, but he knew what had happened. The reactor Blew.

Now Ghost was left to wander the wastes. Only he knew what power that blueprint had held. And he intended to use it.
