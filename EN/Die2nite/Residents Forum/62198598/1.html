Just go play Small Maps.

They don't take ages to fill and, after a handful of them, you'll gather quite a bunch of fellows to bring to serious maps.

Sure there'll be quite a lot of Dehy deaths on all towns, but that's normal. Even if half the town dies from that, it is still possible to have a nice time in a quite different way of playing (if you were doing Distant/HC ever since you became a big boy/girl).

Get yourself a coalition or 2; no need to bring more than that. The real challenge on Small Maps is to engage the residentlings into becoming everyday-players and to teach some basics first (not complicated stuff yet).

For real, don't flood them with information, even if there's 1 or 2 lads there that absorb anything. Go slowly so you'll get more long-term players for the d2n cause.

Again; this is meant for people that play d2n in a way that they want to engage with other people and not just "go online to spend AP and don't have time to see what the others are doing". Seriously, those residentlings WILL be derpy. Like fumbling every time, even if you explain nice and slow and with drawings.

Having a decent Small Map town is 1000x more challenging than having a regular Meta town. Are you up to that?
