A new app called DOMINI NOTABILI can be accessed through the site directory.

The project started with iDare (D2N) asking me for help on creating a tool to compare the towns participating in the UFC event. That is, all towns from all four servers. As I am the developer of the Oval Office, I already had some ready-to-use code snippets at hand and created the app about six weeks ago. In the meantime, I added bits here and there and it now offers some functions. Alongside the set events you can even compare any towns from any server (as long as data is present). You'll find the comparing options right in the first part of the website (below your ID card). And this actually the part where I need your help. The towns' data is not automatically transferred to my server, you need to trigger it for your town. All you need to do is to find one reliable person who logs into the app through the site directory once a day (preferably just before the nightly attack). That way, the app is able to get a data series for your town.

The app currently offers the following comparisons:
A table view showing all the main parameters of a town such as citizens (and jobs), resources, defense and constructions.
Simple maps for each town to compare position, exploration, zombie spread and ruins at a glance.
A whole bunch of diagrams showing the daily development of all towns regarding several parameters.
List of players involved in the towns.

This list also holds a cool feature. If you hover an avatar you'll see a box with the player's name, his job and his last known location. Additionally, if that player has an avatar there will also be the Twinoid icon that links to that player's Twinoid profile. And if that player ever logged in through the site directory, you will also see a blue star appearing next to the Twinoid icon. This triggers an additional comparison in the app. This time, it's not about comparing towns but a single player's performance on all servers. However, this only works, if the player has logged into the app from each server. You can test that by just looking for myself and click the blue star, here's also a screenshot of that function.

The app is multi-lingual, you can choose your interface language in the menu bar at top of the page. The data that comes straight out of the XML are server-specific and thus show static strings (e.g. building names).

The app is also providing five (more or less) different layouts which you can choose from by going to the end of the page or clicking the "settings" menu item. (I am still accepting additional designs if are still working one some.)

I'd like to hear your criticism, experienced errors or even feature requests.

Enjoy!
CC

Thanks to hottest (for pushing me and coming up with new ideas and improvements on a regular basis) DO.NOT now includes three map modes.

Default (single) is what you all know: one single map per town which shows the current state.
Second option (multi) offers one map per town and day. You can move from day to day using a slider (works best in webkit browser, e.g. Google Chrome), some already might have seen that during the past days.
Last option (snapshots) even takes it to another level. Starting at noon last Monday, the app now takes hourly snapshots (if at least one of your town mates calls the app in a given hour). Thus, you can have up to 24 snapshots per day. All these snapshots in a row make a nice animation of your town's map development. And yes, there is a play button.

Map mode and animation speed for snapshots is now in the settings.
