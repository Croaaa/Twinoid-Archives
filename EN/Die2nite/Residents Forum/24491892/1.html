Anyway, in regards of the original post, I have always believed that most players' justice system is far too lenient. People react too slow and are reluctant to give punishment to those who deserve it. Complaints, however, should never be used as a form of punishment. Complaints should be reserved, in my opinion, only to deliver the punishment itself.

For me, the best system to use is to create a thread immediately after noticing that a player is acting strangely. The thread should have the name of the player on it. Since any respectable player should look at the forums at least once a day, it should be hard for them to miss the thread if it has their name on it. The player should be given a chance to stop their behavior and explain the reason behind it. If the player does not stop and ignores the thread, a hanging should follow.

However, there's no way of standardizing the justice system in the game. You may try to convince other citizens in your town, or your coalition/meta to follow a system, but you can't force anyone to follow it.
