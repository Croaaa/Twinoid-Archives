On computer, you have something called "hsl" which is the easy way to look at color. It's an acronym for Hue, Saturation, Light.
The hue form a circle, starting from full red and going orange, yellow, green, blue, pink then red again*.
Saturation is "how much of the color there is". Full saturation is the plain color while no saturation is gray.
The light make the color vary between white and black.

this tool can help to experiment with that.
If one use color as a synonym for hue, then white, gray and black aren't color. But if color is used to describe how we see stuff, then we definitely see white and black "color".

For color spectrum, it's related to how we see color, wave length and what not. As far as I understand that, it's related to hue in hsl.

* The color name used to describe the hue is typical to western culture. Some other culture only have two words for color, one for hot color, everything between pink and yellow, and one for cold color, everything between green and purple.
