"Free speech" generally applies to the right to criticize your government with impunity. It does not apply to private enterprises like websites, who usually have a set of guidelines they wish their members to follow. When you sign up on a site, you agree to follow this set of guidelines and if you don't, your messages could be deleted or you could be banned along with other actions, like you could get your liver pecked and a stern talking to. Welcome to Interneting 101.

As for what gets moderated here on the Town Forums, generally bigoted (racist, sexist, homophobic) comments are deleted and a warning given. It is common sense in a community or society where you strive for an environment in which you want to communicate with some semblance of success, that you don't tolerate hateful communications. "Big Rubber Dick" and a homophobic or similarly hateful comment are not the same thing.

The moderators follow the guidelines set out here by the company we work for, Motion Twin. If you would rather people ignore personal attacks and other disruptiveness, take it directly to Motion Twin. As for now, this is the system we play and work with.
