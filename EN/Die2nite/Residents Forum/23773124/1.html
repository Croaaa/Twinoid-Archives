The door blew open with an explosive element, sending wooden shrapnel inward, toward the cowering old man. The old man hid under his bed, tears protruded through his large blue eyes. The tears dribbled down through the chin of the clay mask he wore, the first of anything to leak out of the mask since the old masked man put it on. Screams erupted through the now open doorway, horrible inhumane pleas. He looked up from under the bed he was concealed under, and with faint confusion peered at the black boots that had splintered his door. Questions zipped through his head, which was completely engulfed in survival instincts, which he had accumulated over the endless tortures. A familiar voice sent snakes up his spine.
“What, is this not what you expected to see?”
The invader kicked the mattress, revealing the old masked man. The old masked man looked up, his confused expression hidden behind his clay mask. Memories slammed his mind like a wave, threats of revenge by the sneering monstrosity that stood above him.
“I want to see behind that disguise, feel your soul slip from your shrivelled husk, witness it attempt to escape and see the fear in your eyes as I whisk it away, just as I watched you take his”.
The old masked man opened his mouth to protest but a large hand wrapped around his throat. He felt claws tear into his cheek as the clay mask was ripped from his face. The monster sneered triumphantly. It reached into it’s pocket and brought out a tool of destruction so terrifying the old man soiled himself. He struggled with all his might, wriggling around, trying to slip his way out his restraint. This pleased the monster, it giggled in delight. The cold iron of its weapon pressed up against the old man’s forehead. The old man screamed, his legs kicking, his nose erupted, sliming the hand that gripped him in place. The monster was unmoved. It pulled back a lever with it’s thumb, and pulled the bottom trigger with it’s index finger. With a shattering explosion the steel projectile slammed into the old man, who immediately collapsed. The monster kicked the corpse. It’s sneer turned into an unsatisfied snarl. It levelled the weapon and fired several more times, then spat into the hole where the old man’s face once was. Then it turned and left, back into the dark turmoil that raged outside. A raven overhead circled the house a few times, then followed the monster in black boots. Their work was far from finished.

Max and Glen had split up. Max went east and Glen went west. The ground Max tread on was perfectly flat, but between the sand trying to suck him into its depths, and the heat leeching him of his strength, the walk was challenging. But he had walked these lands before, many times, and he had become accustomed to their struggles. He had also become perceptive in the search. He stopped walking at a sense he had picked up from the sands, a tingle he felt in between his toes. He bent down and began digging his hands into the pale flesh of the earth. The sand did not like being upturned as it was, and kept piling in on every scoop Max took. It was a slow and tiresome process, but little by little he dug deeper into the earth. One foot, two feet, three feet… until around four feet down his fingernails struck a solid. Long ago this would of shattered his fingernails, but after an eternity of physical torment his hands had become as strong as iron, his palms covered in permanent callus’, his fingernails like steel. With a great heave he wretched the object up out of the earth, and claimed his prize. A single plank of wood, twisted about by the sand that had been digesting it. He smiled in pride, and peered up at the sun. He still had a long time before sunset to keep searching, so he kept pacing east. Three more times he dug, and three more times he uncovered a new object. He looked down at his collection, a plank of wood, an old broken rocking chair, and two pieces of junk metal. Peering up at the cruel sun, he decided it was time to go back. He piled up his loot in the rocking chair, took a sip from his ration, and turned to head back to town. He could just make it out, it was smaller then his thumb from his distance, the only bastion remotely safe to survive the night. A cry for help stopped him from his goal, a distant shout coming from the north. He followed the shout with his eyes, searching for its creator. He caught sight of a blot in the distance; it seemed to be a building sprout out from the ground. Further staring revealed a silhouette running around on the roof, bellowing the pleas for assistance. Max sighed. He fingered the scar across his palm, focusing his attention away from the sun, away from the sand, away from himself. He indulged his focus into the scar, he felt the groove along either side of its peak, felt the hardened cast that had formed after the claws incision, thought of the transition he and Glen had made. He called out to Glen, through his scar, through his blood, the blood he and Glen shared. “I need some help buddy”. He felt his hearts’ beat quicken, their shared blood rushed through his system, sending Glen’s answer from his heart to his head.
“What’s wrong? Are you ok, did they get you?”

“A fork? It’s a little too barren out here for ‘forks in the path’.”
“Some guys screaming for help out north of me. He’s standing on something tall, so it’s probably a building. Sun’s going to start setting soon, and I don’t know how many of them there are”
“Listen, the only way we are going to escape this hell is if we work together…”
“There is no escape man, how many…”
“Every man is worth saving. I thought you of all people would understand that by now”
“But what if I get trapped there too”
“Me and you both know that they aren’t the problem”
“What if…”
“Why did you even ask me if you want to question my answer”. Max cringed; the scar across his forearm grew hot, reminding him of his deeds. He was a shallow worm, constantly outcasted for his erratic behaviour. He hoarded, stole, murdered, all out of self-preservation for his own hollow husk. And then he met Glen. Max sighed, clearing his mind of the dark clouds that had been forming while he revaluated his past.
“I’ll do it, but I might not make it back in time”
“Don’t worry, I’ll hold those gates until you get back. And you WILL get back. Now hurry up, you’re giving me a heartache”. Max looked from the town to the building, then to the sun, then with a deep breath headed toward the banshees plea.

Max knew the problem as soon as he heard the cries, but a solution he had to mull over on his way. The sun was sinking fast, and it would take awhile to help the poor sod on the roof. The man had seen Max coming and had ceased his pleas. Max could just see the upper part of his body; he seemed to be sitting cross-legged on the roof watching him. Max was close enough to make out the structure. It was a fairly large building, half of it obliterated and swallowed by the sand, but the left side was intact. Dirty and badly weathered, but intact. “GLEN! GLEN!”
“WHAT!?”
“THE DOOR, MANS STUCK, NETHERBEINGS!”
“WHAT? WHAT’S GOING ON?”
“THE MAN, HES STUCK IN BETWEEN THE DOOR, DOESN’T FIT THROUGH, SAND PUSHING AGAINST DOOR!”
“DIG”. Glen’s order shot from Max’s heart, through his blood it flew, straight to his brain. Max jumped down to his hands and knees and slashed at the sand, ripping chunks of it away from the door. The door budged outward bit by bit until the man flew out from between his constraint. The netherbeings attempted to follow but Max slammed the door shut. The sand immediately began filling in, burying both the door and Max’s ebony black boots.
