dragonxcore

      I'd say we kind of ARE in the end game of RL. For me the most important things are knowledge, especially how to grow food. If small communities can grow their own food, we could potentially avoid a social collapse by restructuring our communities.

      So both avoiding the social collapse of the world, and what to do in case of it, have the same answer for me. Of course, stuff like defending yourself with a weapon becomes a lot more important after said collapse. But depending on how isolated your geography is, I think it is possible to put into place enough collapse response mechanisms, such as post-apoc VICTORY GARDENS that some communities may be able to transition over with minimal or at least reduced violence.

      So of course I garden the heck out of every piece of land I can get a hold of, including helping friends and neighbors who want to get started. There are a bunch of agriculture methods waaay better than petrol chemical monocropping (dumping pesticides and fertilizers on 1000s of acres of the exact same species of crop) which is being used for 90+% of what is being consumed in supermarkets. Natural farming, permaculture, aquaponics, agroecology, etc, etc. but this isn't a rant about all that

      As the 1st post describes, disease and antibiotics would play a big role in who lives and dies, so some knowledge of microbiology and safe food handling / sanitation would go a long way. Those antibiotics work on both fish and humans for a reason - they target a group of bacteria. If you understand how that works, you have a better shot at using the right antibiotic (so long it's not an Unlabeleled Drug ). Hope this answer isn't too far from what you were looking for.

      What would I do in an end-of-the-world-as-we-know-it situation? I would start preparing now - ♫ Strengthen your mind, we're living in serious times♫♪
On may 31, 2015
dragonxcore

      P.S. If you don't think antibiotic resistant diseases are the result of a social collapse, just google: livestock antibiotic resistance. Doctors and microbiologists understand well that when using antibiotics 1) you only use it when needed, 2) use a specific antibiotic for the bacteria you want to kill, and 3) use the right dosage, for the right amount of time to kill all the bacteria.

      If you don't do this, then a bunch of bacteria are getting exposed to the antibiotic, but not enough to kill them all completely. So what survives, evolves resistance.

      However, factory farms don't give a shun about wiping out the world with antibiotic resistant diseases, because they get more profit using low-dosage, long-duration, broad-spectrum antibiotics on all their livestock. In other words, break all 3 rules above.

      That's a CEO / Board of business people somewhere deciding to let this happen. A Social collapse.
