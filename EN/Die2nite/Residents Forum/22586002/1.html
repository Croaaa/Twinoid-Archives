You can't settle scores on the World Forums. Everything that follows is true and accurate but names have been omitted when it was needed.


How much I love this game!

I have loved this game from the start, partly because of the post-apocalyptic atmosphere and the strategies involved but mostly because of the freedom to play any way you want and the scope for manipulation and deceit.

I have fond memories of the first contests such as the Caption contest or the Fashion Parade (and of course the original Clash!). I loved conducting interviews of cool players.

Of course, it's been frustrating (infuriating) at times. It takes a calmer person to lead a meta or accept life's hiccups and take it all on the chin.

But yeah, like everyone says, you make great friends along the way and rewards abound even if you can lose your soul along the way.

Now my favourite, favourite bits!

I confess I adore screwed up situations, whether things go my way or not. I remember this town a few seasons back with a meta (multis? I still have my doubts and I sure reported them) had me on my toes and finally hanged me only hours before completing a wonder. Well played (I suppose )...

Seeing a player whine and give us the standard emotional blackmail is always a highlight. I never tire of it.

Now let me tell you about three awesome towns I got to be part of. All random ones, too!

I joined a small map town - as I was farming LMS awards and Spiritual Guide distinctions - and perhaps maybe wanted to help newcomers appreciate and enjoy the game.
Bad luck, an old player from way back was returning to the game and still had vivid memories of how I got him kicked out of my meta because. He was all over me on the town forums, in a bad way, warning everyone not to trust me, that I was selfish and devious. (Me!!!).

Oh my... He had it coming

I fabricated a fake private message of him letting me know how he followed me to this town in order to kill me. I made sure he sounded like a rabid lunatic, like the kind of player you want to ban and hang from your town asap. It was wonderful. While at first, people were confused and thought he might have a point, as soon as I made this "private message" public everyone sided with me and the fucker got hanged. Yeah, I got LMS, too.

In another town, this time a Hard Core one, A FAMOUS META LEADER followed me (a coincidence, he told me) and decided he was on a mission to kill me. He almost succeeded, too!
He punched me while I was ASing 6AP from town, injuring me in the process. Luckily I happened to find a bandage while ASing! Unfortunately I didn't notice I was injured, and drank my water BEFORE using the bandage. As a result I only had 5AP left and Aaaarrgh was approaching. I took a gamble and went camping at a nearby building, using 3AP and a DO to improve my campsite. Phew, I survived - only to see that A FAMOUS META LEADER had come and dropped a 7AP food, a booze and a water ration for me! Sweet! I used the food and the booze to go dig out another building (I am not impartial to distinctions) and, of course, dropped the water ration at my earliest convenience, using heroic find to summon a non-poisoned one
Cutting a long story short, A FAMOUS META LEADER died a few days later and despite being away from my computer and from the Internet for the last two days of town, I got the Hard Core LMS. Ooooh that was good

Finally, I recently got to be a ghoul!!! It was a distant map town with a young and enthusiastic meta I'd never heard of. I just happened to stumble across them - didn't plan a jump with them or anything.
They (tried to) play the meta game, even held contests for battery launchers. I won one of them and got to assemble an MKII totally legit! The next day, a human meat turned me into a ghoul and I spent the following two eating fellow citizens. They organised a huge ghoul hunt, making lists of suspects. I was in none of these lists!!!!
In the end they demanded everyone post a screenshot to prove you're not a ghoul. I might not be the best at photoshop, but obviously my job was good enough and everyone who had not submitted a screenie got punched that day.
I had to go die in the desert as I was jumping the following day with a lovely bunch of friends but being a ghoul and having to fear for your life while killing others on a daily basis was such a buzz!

AnnualRainfallbecause of the freedom to play any way you want

That sentence is the core of my love for this game. When I see folks complain along the lines of 'this game is boring you just log in for 1 minute a day to hit some buttons and come back the next day' I marvel the amazing void of creativity and imagination.

Part of that Radical Freedom love is that you can log on for one minute or spend the whole bloody day here if you're crazy enough.

In the mechanics and atmosphere departments I love the care and attention to detail (the 2 hour mark AS comments, the "subliminal" map messages, the possibilities that a certain arrary of buildings, blueprints, and items on the ground open [or close] for you).

I love the roominess and claustrophobia you can get from being with 40 other people to make the town go (or go down).

So here's a run down of one memorable town per season (and I love the fact that in a number of these cases I had struggle to make the pick):

Season One

I am inclined toward crazy in everything I do. If I'm going to do something (be that work, school, or fun) chances are I'm going to do it obsessively (I had my performance review at work last week & one of the few concerns is that my boss doesn't want me to burn out 'cause I've been running at such high speed the year I've worked there, to help illustrate what I mean by that).

This means that when I decide to play a game like D2N I don't just start playing it. Instead, I read everything there is to read about it as I play my first town. I pay attention to rookie mode, I read every word of help, I read everything in the Forums, I google it and read articles, I google deeper and read the public external Forums of many other groups who've just found the game as a group and are now plotting on how to start playing it meta-wise. I was easily able to do this 'cause when I first found D2N I was unemployed and desperate for things to fill a void and oh what a void D2N could fill...

But what this all means is that I knew from day one what my goals are: get to 100 soul points and get into a meta and since, as an unemployed bum, I can't do it as a hero I'll have to do with through Worth Proving.

That's what makes The Crimson Sinkhole of Sadness such a memorable town for me because, in that town, even though I am the careful, methodical one who knows what to do to not get injured I wind up injured due to being forced, along with one other guy, to flee on an expedition gone wrong when a third party went totally the wrong way! I called it my Most Bitter Moment in D2N because not only did we take a chance and get nothing from the expo, not only did I wind up with a injury mark and infection death on my soul having read so much of the game and how to not end up that way, but when I died of that infection I died 1 soul point short of 100 and the goal I'd been working my ass off toward since town one!

(The Crow was merciful though because my first distant town I got my only LMS to date, which allowed me to post in the then Heroes Only WFs with my 1 Day to say OMG SAVE ME TAKE ME INTO THE ARMS OF META AND LET ME REACH MY GOAL!).

So how ironic a statement is this from someone who claims the thing that they love the most about the game is that you can play it any way?

Season 2

Yup. By the end of Season 1 I had found a meta and we had a grand ole time. We could go a month and have a tonne of fun doing it. I was core organizing crew along with shatter and Moolie (some of you may remember his Beaker avatar). We had wacky fun with Bhayne4444 and kenster92 (I bet some of you still remember his fatass batman av) and a mix of both with players like juliusz and Alexscorpion. This season was also the season we found a plucky young player by the name of Adzman. Most of those players never linked to Twinoid and all of them no longer play, which meant that deep down inside they just didn't have the obessiveness I was looking for. I needed a greater level of crazy and I was pretty sure I could get it from the group most of the D2N world called The Rabbits.

After a bumpy start (which wound up lucky for the Rabbits as they picked up Crowley the random resident in the fail first try who is still playing with chunks of that crew as a hero to this day) there was The Antique Ignorance of Brutes, which gave me all I wanted in the both the obsessive and the drama departments because some of the New Recruits didn't mesh so well with some of the well established crew. Not me, though, I was the resident fly on the wall watching the techniques and fixing the weapons daily: an organizing crazy on a one-town vacation as a willing foot solider for Efrafa.

Season 3

Oh, season three. I hate you season 3. For someone who was all meta and all about learning and working on techniques to better hone said meta a meta cap is about the last thing on this earth I could want especially because it was so clearly what the community did not want. It was a restriction on freedom to play you want and because of that it shook my love at its foundations.

But! There is goodness in all things and the goodness of the MMJs and meta cap was the fact that started to get a chance to mix it up with All The D2N Characters of the world, which is something you can totally see when you check out who I was working away with in The Downs of the Lost.

I think folks thing the MMJs capture D2N at the best of its intent: loads of active players... many who happen to have minds of their own and DotL had that in spades. Community minded folks like me who Just Want The Town To Run Well and sneaky bastards doing their all to sneak one last town damaging distinction for themselves in. There's fun, there's infuriating, and in DotL there was fun in meting out justice to the most infuriating...

And, I'll also touch on this a bit by the conclusion, but this is also where I remember #d2n on quakenet IRC really coming into its own. This is how I knew there were more people in the big wide world of D2N I wanted to play with.

And, speaking of the people, it was at the end of S3 when I got my first taste of hero mode because I was kindly gifted with days 'cause I sang my first ever parody song (that sure opened one hell of a box of worms). He didn't know me, I didn't know him (but of course now I know his sunshiny goodness very well) but he rewarded my good D2N community behaviour with his good community behaviour.

Season 4

Going to have to go with my only hardcore town to date and my only #1 in the Hot Tombs of Criminals but not because of either of those reason. Yup, I see you go , because of the people.

I'll flag 3 of those to illustrate what I mean by that in that town. First, I was back with Adz for the first time post-meta cap (and there was always the lingering guilt of abandoning him for Rabbits not know that I couldn't just come back next season due to Surprise Meta Cap and appreciating how much he'd grown as a player since our crew regularly threatened to "joke" hang him. Second, having known her from other places online pre-D2N I was finally playing with spacey, and third I got to meet Camping Calculator tam' and bring the joy of hockey teasing to life since we're both Leafs "fans" (tam' being much more "straight" with me as the self-hating, all-hating but still weirdly loyal one... which makes for some primo teasing possibilities.).

But, speaking of Camping Calculator, this town was also memorable for seeing in action the true glory of the spreadsheet (amazing that after 4 seasons you can still find more to be done with a game that looks so simple on the surface). Oh, you don't know the imaginative possibilities this also opened up for me! So many spreadsheet ideas started to swirl in my head... oh so perfect for the ordered and obsessive.

Season 5

Finding Adz again was dangerous. He'd asked if I consider reviving ye olde meta and my guilt and fondness meant I couldn't say no so S5 it was back to seeing if I could order in this brave new world. When not ordering I spend my time in wacky towns wonder-hunting away (mostly fail but still fun [well, other than the giant fail that prompted my vendetta]).

Any yet the most memorable town of S5 was either of those kinds, instead, it was BotS 1 (Crevice of Shrunken Heads). The town I should never have been part of.

Why not? Well, mostly 'cause few things on this earth piss me off more than gender bullshit. So why do it? Well, pretty much 'cause the organizers worked their asses off to make it happen so I can at least support them in that work and just hold my nose and bite my tongue (even though I often felt like I was going to bite it in half).

And yet! BotS wasn't memorable for the gender BS it was memorable for being totally D2N enraging. We had dehydrations (and! one of those was the nearly main organizer... I made a little vid of them going to the brink of doom multiple times), infections, and, worse of all fantastic ruin water drinking "derps", and what looked like no way to get folks doing the "10 inside" tactic. By endgame a crew who couldn't take it anymore went on a voyage to a far corner of the map to camp and never return on the way to D2N utopia. And, somehow, that's when the town started turning around. The derping slowed and those remaining near the town rallied to start nabbing souls left and right. In the end the town would have won (something I never thought possible a week in) had MT not been derping themselves at the start of this season with soul bonus points missing from all the town scores.

In the end I'd credit this town with helping to loosen my play expectations up a bit more. A town doesn't need to be perfect if you can find the joy in it somewhere.

BOTS also heralded another change in the way I play D2N.
