ColetteSleep hadnt come easy that night for her. Twisting and turning in the bed like a twinoid addicted research monkey she had burned enough calories to work off the previous nights meal of chicken and canned corn a thousand times. Defeated in her futile attempts at rest she rose. Still dark outside. Still quiet. Still.

Rising from her cobbled together makeshift bed, pushing aside the few remaining "luxuries" she had accumulated since The Dawn had struck, (an unconventional sized cat sent sprawling, the tattered old teddy she had transformed into a pillow, "Eduardo" her toy) she ventured through the zip of her tent to greet the morning with gusto. This same thought passed through her head at this same time every morning, the thought of better days spent in the warmth of a family breakfast table, the smell of strong coffee drifting past her nostrils....mmmmm.

Her ritual daydream broken for a second by an unusual thought. Somethings not right, somethings missing...off. But she couldnt quite put her finger on it, last nights vodka-fuelled killing spree hadnt helped her sleep but had certainly left her a little worse for wear this morning. "Its the hangover" she thought to herself, still unconvinced. That feeling in her stomach could very well be the booze but this knot felt more akin to the fear induced when a womans instinct is kicking in. Her spider sense as it were. She wished for some Paracetoid or some Alka-Seltzer, anything to settle her stomach and shake this eeriness away. She knew there was little chance that the small band of survivors she had found yesterday would have any stored away though.

Though she had only met them recently she felt she could trust them, one of them a real gent that seemed handy with tools and slightly overly concerned with precipitation levels (bordering on OCD) had even saved her life when, starving and dehydrated out There, being circled by Crows the size of buzzards and unable to take another step He had heard her plea for help and escorted her to this new haven. What a hero.

ColetteThat niggling feeling still buried deep in the Great Pit of her stomach, she stumbled over scraps of metal and an empty Pi-Keya furniture packaging and made her way to the Wall to see what the night had brought this merry band. She knew herself to be an early riser and could understand that not everyone had the same vigilance when it came to morning productivity but the pureness of the mornings quiet was somewhat disturbing. The dread was creeping, she recognised it as being the same dread that seemed to follow her everywhere she went, from Hovel to Stronghold, town to town and she knew before she saw it that fate had played its cruel card once more.

Terror, deep and true and honest now held her close as her morning fog lifted to reveal her greatest fear once more. Its hinges creaking in the morning sun, its latch no longer buried in its hole as she had left it not more than 8 hours before, its heart on its sleeve telling her that everything she had worked so hard to combat had happened anyway. She could only hope the horde had been small enough for her town-mates to beat away with torches and mattresses and whatever the Watch had to hand. But the town devoid of activity was beginning to show the signs of an attack that would leave no survivors but her. Why did this always happen?

AnnualRainfallHe hadnt seen this coming. THIS was new to him and he thought hed seen it all. She was new to the town and so with all newcomers he had kept a watchful eye on her. She was a survivor of that there was no doubt, proving herself useful in the public debates, handy with a pistol and productive in the workshop. Shed even volunteered to close the gates that night. Hed watched her bolt it down, satisfaction on her face. Watched her say goodnight, the bottle of vodka slipping from her hand as she zipped her tent, seemingly unfazed by the gathering horde outside.

He had watched EVERYTHING because thats what he does. Watched her listlessly shifting in the tent, battling her demons in her own way. Watched her silhouette slowly rise as the horde was building to a peak. Watched her leave her tent and make her way across the town square to the storage room. When he saw her reaching for the festering flesh of one of the towns past residents he felt ill, but when he saw her cover herself from head to toe with it he vomited pure bile that burnt his throat and made him wish for a downpour of lifegiving aqua.

His body wracked and shaking he could do nothing to stop her as she unbolted the gates and ushered Them in to feed. This display of pure insanity had him rattled and he was just able to hide himself beneath a groundsheet for the evening. From his vantage point he could just see her face as she watched the madness ensue. She looked like a different person now and not just because of the lumps of what used to be SaucyemmettC dangling from her cheeks. She was enjoying this.

His gaze captured it all: the town fell and the hordes appetite lead them away and only she was left and the moon starting to fall as her body shifted back to her tent. Then a time passing and her tent full of movement again. Her mighty yawn and stretch as she exited, tripping past debris with a morning head, the innocence in her eyes......

A different person. She doesnt know. He saw the fear growing in her as she first picked out the gates arc in the morning wind and then saw the Wells contents and he saw the tears rolling down her cheeks as she picked the dead clean and cried aloud about "Why they hadnt kept the gates closed" and he saw his hand rise high in the golden light of that awful morning, its rays glinting off the sharpened screwdriver as he drove it straight through the back of her skull nevermore to sabotage the unsuspecting townfolk again.

The worst case of split personality disorder hed ever imagined
