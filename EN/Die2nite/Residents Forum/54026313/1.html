Is there any way to make it better? Yes I'm probably beating a horse that's been dead for a very long time, but the experience for most first-time players must be incredibly aggravating. How many of you try out any game for the first time, find it to be very frustrating day after day, and continue playing it?

It's hard to get enough slightly active players in a small town to keep the town going. I'm not even referring to people reading the forum, but just people going out and bringing stuff in and building. I know it's probably always been that way, but now there sometimes isn't even enough defense for the first night. So a small town that has 7 or 8 people going out and searching, with realistically half of that returning before the attack, might have more deaths during the first night. How is that supposed to make a player feel like playing this game is worth one's time?

For example, the hammam requirement, which apparently was added in season 11, makes things worse for small towns. How many small towns find nuts and bolts and are able to get the hammam built before zeds start getting into the town? Why make things more difficult for new players?

Most games I've played in the past will make the starting experience pleasant enough that a new player will want to keep coming back. I know D2N is sort of in a league of its own in terms of gameplay, but that doesn't mean the game has to be frustrating for someone who is trying it out for the first time. Is there anything that can be changed in the game to make new players more likely to stick around? In a game that seems to have a population on the decline, it doesn't seem advantageous to push people away when all they want to do is see what the game is like.
