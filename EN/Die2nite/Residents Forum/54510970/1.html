So I was sitting here a week or two ago and started thinking about how long a really big group of good, experienced, possibly-meta players would last in a small town. I'm betting probably 40 days or more. I mean, the map alone is small enough to be fully mapped by the end of day one and I'm betting by day two or three, we could kill every single zombie on the map.




But then I thought: Yeah, but who would WANT to play in a small town for that long? Probably nobody. Nobody would want to waste their hero days, if they're a hero, or spend 30-40 days in a small town when it wouldn't even wager into the rankings. I think I'd have a hard time finding 30 people willing to give it a try, even if just for fun.





But hey, people sometimes make their own towns (via Mayor) for special fun events. So...meh. An idea started to form.
It may never pan out and it will probably never take off...but if not, that's okay. This is just a "What if...?" sort of idea. What if we did try to do something like this, just for fun?

And furthermore, to stoke peoples' interest in it, I was thinking about how maybe I'd implement PRIZES being given out. Prizes like...
...
....maybe 100 dollars to the person who lasted the longest...
...and maybe 30 hero days to whoever got last man standing...
...and maybe other random prizes, like the whole thing being a raffle and, if you take part in it, you're automatically enter in for a chance to win another 100 or 200 dollars.



Me, I'm kinda sorta rich. I definitely have a lot of money to waste. I constantly buy myself hero status in this game and I ALWAYS will be a paying member of it, because I think this game is fun enough to be worth spending a lot of money on. I also enjoy spending money to buy MTPasses and giving them out to friends and people who I think deserve them....and in the next three or four months, I'm going to be coming into even more extra money, so... I don't know, I'll have to think about this some more. I might be willing to give out some 100 dollar prizes for people who participate or who would last long...
..but again, all of this is just conjecture right now. Just the FAINT HINT of a (probably bad) idea.

If there is any interest, even the slightest, maybe it will grow a bit bigger....but I figured I'd make this topic first and put it out there. I predict (like I said above) most won't like the idea of wasting their hero days in just a small town... but *shrugs* maybe if I made it worth the while with a few really good money prizes, people might give it a shot? I don't know.

You underestimate a few points that might turn this attempt of making a 40 day town on a small map very hard.

  • There are only 7 ruins on a small map. All of them inside the 10km range (so all give Uncommon BPs only). There are no E.Ruins and their type is basic (just ruins that usually spawn inside 6-7km from town, so no advanced ruins that drop belts, engines, etc...
  • With few ruins and no E.Ruins means that you won't get all Blueprints a advanced meta town needs. Architect Study sure helps, and so does the Worn leather bags, but you will not get all water blueprints and this means some players will eventually die from dehydration for the LACK of water. THis is something that never happens on big maps (more jerrycans, all water bps available, etc..)
  • There is no point killing all zombies in the map as it will trigger a mass zombie respawn. This actually won't be a problem for a meta, maybe it will just turn things interesting, however they will waste more weapons on zombies than saving them for Watch.
  • With so few zones to search the town might have some issues finding rare resources, even when they maximize the searchtower. If they get badluck finding a hacksaw or ECs/N&Bs this can become a serious issue. It only takes a full escorting party to deplete all the zones refreshed by a storm. What would the other 35 people do? Punch each other with their APs?
  • I still need to test this, but it seems the attack curve/size on small maps is the same than the one found on Distant towns. This is a problem for later days when there wont be many Watchman weapons around and the attack gets bigger and bigger.
  • When its time to camp there won't be much places to go. Distance bonus on camping is limited to 50% and penalties for having a lot of people camping at the same spot are bound to happen more frequently (trying to fit 20 ish camping people on 7 ruins is the same as trying to make an elephant enter a car).

With those in mind, I think that a meta will actually live LESS compared to a Distant Town jump.

Why not adapt this a bit and instead of using a full meta go for a coalition or two? This way you can add some prizes for the town that brings the most under-100-SP alive past day 10-15, or just compare the distinction related to small towns.

Event ideas are always appreciated Dont let my criticism deter you.
