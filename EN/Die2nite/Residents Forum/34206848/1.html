'Twas the night before Christmas, and all through the town
Not a creature was stirring, the gates locked tightly down.
The guardians were posted on the walls high
In hopes that the zombies couldn't and wouldn't get by.

The shamans were nestled all snug in their beds,
While visions of aimless souls danced in their heads;
And the drunk in his 'kerchief, and the addict in his cap,
Had just settled down for an unrestful night's nap,

When out in the desert there arose such a clatter,
The tech sprang from his bed to see what was the matter.
Away to the gates he flew in a haste,
Grabbing a repaired machete, you know, just in case.

The moon on the breast of the desolate wasteland
Gave the lustre of mid-day to objects at hand
When, what to our weary eyes should appear,
But an old sleigh, with undead massed in the rear,

With a little old driver, so gaunt and so sick,
We knew in a moment it must be Zombie St. Nick.
With a groan and a shamble his minions they came,
And he whistled, and moaned and called them by name;

"Now, Biter! now, Gnawer, Chomper and Slasher!
On, Moaner! on Groaner! on, Shambler and Gnasher!
To the top of the town, to the top of the wall!
Now slash away! slash away! we've got to kill them all!"

To the gates the horde approached with not much wit,
a couple dozen dispatched by an upgraded great pit.
Armour plating, screaming saws and an old-school trap
Sliced through a crowd of undead in a snap.

The remaining zombies scrambled a top of the wood.
To where our drunk guardian watchmen stood
With pistols, makeshift guitars and the sort
They'll even throw and an old washing machine from our fort.

Through all the screaming and yelling, the blood and the gore
At last, just one last zombie was heard at our shaman's door.
Where mistaking it for another lost soul being found,
Opened the door and Zombie St. Nicholas came in with a bound.

He was dressed in fur that was ripped and tattered,
And his clothes covered with blood that had splattered;
A bundle of corpses he had flung on his back,
And he looked like a ghoulish scavenger with a full rucksack.

His eyes -- how empty and sunken! his dimples how withdrawn!
His cheeks were like ash, his nose completely gone;
His droll little mouth missing teeth like a sieve,
And the oozing wound on his neck was quite massive;

He spoke "You need not worry this festive night
Just this once, I bring you gifts to help you fight.
A hacksaw, jarpen tables, a machete or two
Twinoids and booze for the survivalist crew.

A ruin near town that's easily run
Multiple rare blueprints let the techs have some fun.
Palaces for the shaman and materials by the oodles,
Plus plenty of treats for the tamer's labradoodles.

For all, an easy path to collect each and every soul.
Except those without escort on, sorry, they get stuck with some coal.
"I've come loaded with gifts and toys for each citizen
Just this once, though, for tomorrow I will again try to blitz in."

And with that he finished and out of the gates he retreated,
And off back into the desert on his sleigh he was seated.
But I heard him exclaim, ere he rode out of sight,
"Happy Christmas to all, and to all, don't die tonight."
