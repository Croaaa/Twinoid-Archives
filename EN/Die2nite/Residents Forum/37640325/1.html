My advice would be get ready to get punched day 1 by some random dude.

But here are the mechanical differences in HC towns as well:

1)-70% camping penalty

2)Chance of dieing of infection is 75%

3)There's 2 explorable ruins

4)You have less starting water in the well

5)Constucted buildings take damage every night which needs to be repaired before the next attack. If they aren't then they get destroyed and you have to rebuild them (there's a lot of formulas that can go along with this but that's just the basic).

6)Any defensive objects left in the bank during the attack have a chance of being destroyed.

7)You can't use external map auto updates

8 )LMS gives more hero days

9)Despite what the help section says they DO NOT start with 1 random ghoul at the beginning of town

I'm afraid it is not the case.

Hardcore town is also known as griefer town too, where some people injure and eliminate others for LMS (blame this reward system!).

You may also try to form a coalition yourself. In Die2Nite, everyone can form a coalition and invite up to 5 people. Then when anyone in the coalition jumps into a town, the rest will follow. When you go to each town, you may meet some people who like cooperative play too. Then invite them to your coalition.

You may ask to be invited in a coalition. This is where cooperative play shines. Here is the request and recruit thread.

Official Recruitment Thread (resident)

What you really want is a multi-coalition or meta. What is it? It isn't a official feature but it is what the community develops to tackle the problems of inactives, selfish players and griefers.

A town has 40 players. So 8 coalitions join together and play in the same town (which is called meta). The idea sounds nice but there are a few things you need to tackle. First there must be at least 1 hero in each coalition, because only hero can select specifically which town to jump. The person who is in charge of the jump is called jumper. (The exception is hardcore town because there is only one hardcore town available at one time. You don't need to "choose" which town to jump.)

8 jumpers have to meet together at the same time to do the jump. They usually use free real-time chatrooms like mibbit to do such coordination.

It is no problem if you cannot get full 8 coalitions. It can be 3, 4 or 6 coalitions or any number. The idea is you gather more people who like cooperative plays and play together in the same town.

There are existing coalitions or metas who keeps doing this. You don't need to do everything yourself.

Good luck!
