Final_Girl

Here's mine.

6 - Scout needs some serious buffs. It promises you'll be good at just that, scouting, and you are, but that's about it. You can sort of tell how many zombies are in one small part of the map, but there's a chance that you'll totally whiff with it. You can move through zones with lots of zombies an get behind enemy lines, but what's the point? No one can follow you, and you can't search buildings or do anything important without getting trapped out of camo. Some of these jobs, I'd say I'd have liked a different job more. For this one, though, I'd rather you just play a technician and upgrade the watchtower. It can do the work of multiple scouts, without taking the risk or AP item use.
5 - Survivalists are encouraged to play 100% selfishly and live far away from town. You'll get a stockade of blueprints, sure, but it's not like you can bring them back to town with you sense you'll be on the other end of the map by day 2. Overall, good class, not a very good teammate.
4 - Technician isnt awful, it's just... Pretty ok. 6 AP for construction or workshop is nice. Being able to take required materials off of a construction project once a day or something would have made it really good, but instead, the second utility power is being able to make keys for ruins. It's cool, but the AP to find the key, bring it back to town and have the techie work on it is not worth the haul you get. I never really understood the ruins hype anyway, auto scavenging will get you more stuff, and is less dangerous/takes less effort. Speaking of which
5 - Tamer and Survivalist are both useful depending on the situation. I like them best in a team, the tamer able to take the items the survivalist finds back to town so the survivalist can find more stuff. That's three inventories full of useful loot!
6 - Guardian gives the town defense, and gives more control points. While scavengers find items that give defense points or weapons, a guardian doesn't need them. While technicians can build defense projects, a guardian is a defense project. Scout can sneak past enemy lines while a guardian can lead her teammates through them. Overall, just a really solid class, and the #1 on the tier list - but it's good to have every job in a town if you can manage.

VertigoProcess

It's largely situational.

For meta towns I'd say this:

1. Survivalist/Guard: only way to get BPs with 100% survival chances which is needed for ranking high even 1 camping death can screw your score. A large number of guards are needed for defending the town in late game from those massive attacks.

2. Tamer: Needed for clearing out zeds with the best efficiency. A town clear of zeds is a town that can scav non stop.

3. Scout: Scouts are needed for the first few days of town to find those distant buildings. However outside of Hardcore towns survivalist groups can do distant camping without them pretty easily. The scout class jobs can be completed with a few BIAed residents too.

4. Tech: Techs are really only needed for 1 day to unlock the ruins so you can just BIA a resident to do the job after that they're useless. You can say they're one of the most important because they're the only one that can unlock the E ruin but I ranked them low since they're only needed for 1 day.

5. Scavenger: 100% useless and they know it.

Random towns:

1. Guard: 1 guard can defend a random town for a number of days just by themselves. So even in the most inactive town a guard can keep them alive for at least a little bit. Plus that CP bonus is good for not getting stuck since you most likely won't get help.

2. Scout: Just for the fact that they can move through zed zones and not get stuck since help will most likely not come. Also since little zed clearing will be done so the map will be over run pretty quickly.

3. Survivalist No need for either food or water and can do a lot of the camping for BPs for the town. But since it's a random town camping deaths aren't as big of a deal so other classes can do this job as well.

4. Tamer Can send twice as many items in which is always a good thing.

5. Tech Most random towns don't bother with the E ruin unless you're super lucky and get one very close to town, so their primary job won't be needed in most random towns. So they'll mostly be used for their extra construction points which really isn't a great use of a hero.

6. Scav: Still useless just give up.

The random town list is mostly opinion, people play random towns differently and have different preferences. So I can't really say for sure which is better then which in that case.

Also I just realized you're using a scoring system. I ranked them from best to worst instead.

Iced Guardian

If you're talking about casual play, there's no way to rank, you just have to pick whatever you like to do and have fun with it. Each has unique features and can provide fun depending on your playstyle.

Now speaking about meta gaming, as VP mentioned, you need more number of certain heroes than others, but doesn't make others less important. You need only 1 tech working on a single day but without him you won't explore most of the ruins and that will have a huge impact on your town. You can also camp all buildings with no survivalists but risking to lose lives for no reason. you can defend town with no guardians but you'll have more watch deaths and fewer defense and so on.

So there's not point on making a rank as it makes many wonder that the more of a top tier profession a town has, the best it will develop, and that's blatantly wrong. Strategy games as this one are about balance and cooperation, it's imprecise to call one role more important than other.
