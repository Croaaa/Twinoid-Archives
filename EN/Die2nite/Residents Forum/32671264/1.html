Hello residents,

as you may or may not have noticed, a new app has been added to the D2N directory yesterday.

It's called ZombVival and it's an addictive zombie survival game developed by some random German guy (me) and based around the Die2Nite universe. Try surviving as long as possible (alone or with 4 other players) in a world filled with flesh-eating zombies and poorly translated text. Try various game modes and professions, collect achievements and fight your way to the top of the ranking!

ZombVival has been around for more than a year, but was only available for players of the German version of D2N. As I've got a lot of loyal players over there who really enjoy the game, I thought I'd try localizing and publishing it on D2N, too. The game has its own forum and wiki, is actively developed and has a great community (mostly comprised of German players until now). Of course it is absolutely free - no ads, no paid content, no hacking your computer to get access to your webcam and watch you in your sleep).

If this all sounds interesting to you, just try the game out via the D2N directory. If you have questions, technical problems, ideas for future game content or any kind of positive / negative feedback, you can contact me using the game forum or just send a Twinoid message.

Brainbox

The machete requires energy - if you don't have enough, you can't use it, even if you set it as favourite weapon.

Also, all of the weapons have ranges; you can't use a battery launcher for zombies that have already reached you, and you can't use a machete on zombies that are far away.

The favourite weapon setting only takes effect, when you can use more than one weapon in your current situation.

If you want to avoid using a specific weapon altogether, just put it on the ground; you won't be able to use it in combat then.

On june 22, 2015
Brainbox

Yes, that idea does come up from time to time.

However, there are two main reasons why I don't plan to implement that:

Firstly, I want time to be a factor in the game. Having a "next tick" button would make many actions in the game meaningless (for instance, sleeping would just turn into some sort of "instant energy regeneration"-action, since you can just skip ahead until your energy is full).

The second reason is more of a technical one - processing a tick takes quite a bit of processing power. Having 1 tick every 15 seconds for each game isn't a problem, but having multiple ticks a second from people spamming the "Next tick" button would probably be too much for the server to handle.
