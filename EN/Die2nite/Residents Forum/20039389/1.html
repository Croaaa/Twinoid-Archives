rumata

I shared my own experiments as well, it must be floating around somewhere on a sheet.
The 1.8<x<2 is easy to find, you can get the limits in one day, by installing 4 and 5 DOs.

After installing 4, you could still upgrade the zone 3 times, which means a DO can't be equal or more than 2 normal upgrades.

The 1.8 part comes similar. I installed 5 DOs, and then I could only do 1 upgrade. If it was lower than 1.8 then the 5 DOs would add up to less than 9, and I'd be able to upgrade more than once.

These numbers can be refined by testing a zone in consecutive days.
Here is an example, with the zone status at the start of the day in brackets:
Day 1 [0]:

  • Install 5 DOs (9<x<10)

Day 2 [6<x<7}:

  • Install 2 DOs (9.6<x<10.6]
  • try to upgrade:
    • if you can, that means a DO is less than ~1.856
    • if you can't, then higher than 1.856

With even more DOs you can further refine it, but this is really only interesting for crazy people like tamle, Ghost, kiwi or me.
