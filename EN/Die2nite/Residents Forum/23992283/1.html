MadDonut

So there were 20 of us left in The Land Of Ever Morning and that night the reactor was predicted to blow. We wanted this. We built it to watch it fall. We were eager for our own self-destruction.

Suddenly, there was a clash. The Reactor steamed and glowed with heat, but it was not the Reactor that collapsed, it was the gates. A foul fool had made repairs to the Reactor and we cursed as the horde let out a thunderous growl. Buildings shook and the dust flew as their footsteps pounded into the town. Some panicked, fleeing into their homes. Others ran outside, trying to lead the horde towards the Reactor. I ran out to join them. I waved my spoon high and made noise with a crowd of others to attract the zombies into coming to the Reactor. Multiple people were dead already.

By the time we reached the insides of the Reactor, only two others were at my side. We yelled and smacked on the Reactor's heavy steel pipes, attempting to tempt the undead into attacking the Reactor. The plan failed and they steadily marched towards us. Realizing that they had cornered us in the maze of maintenance halls and that they would kill us before the Reactor blew, we defended ourselves. The night was a blur of fists and flesh, not something I remember too well.

Eventually, the sun rose and the last of the zombies had stumbled off. I was the only one remaining at the Reactor so I ventured through the halls, staring at the blood that painted the walls. I reached the entrance, moving aside a sheet that served as the door and covered myself with my hand from the glare of the sun. I noticed only three others had survived like me: Ghost, Persephone, and FenderTremolo. I slumped to the ground, exhausted. The others made a few glances at me, but no longer minded. We had survived. We were chosen for something greater. We were blessed to be upon an atomic path. The others might of not talked or thought about it, but I sure noticed. The rest of the day was eerily quiet as I spent all my time just outside the Reactor thinking deeply. The other survivors ignored me and tossed out a few corpses but then stopped when they felt like it wouldn't be worth it. I crawled back into the entrance of the Reactor and fell asleep.

I awoke, hearing a growl that reminded me of the night before, but it was twice as loud. I stood up, also noticing the horrid buzzing and grinding noises of the Reactor. I calmly walked out of the Reactor and stood in the center of town, eying the other survivors as they stood around, waiting and uncaring of the impending doom. The horde came like a tremendous wave, stampeding over the broken gates and rushing right where I stood; their teeth were bared and their souls dark. The corpses that hadn't been discarded rose from their death and came for us aswell. The ground shook and the horde came closer. I watched as they came, desperately trying to think of something witty to say. I figured silence would be the best thing to be said but then the Reactor yelled in an amazing uproar, speaking the words I could not. It gave a shout louder than the horde as it glowed white as the sun.
Then everything became white.

I awoke to dirty, grungy green air. Green vapors misted off of me as I got up. I wasn't in the town anymore. I wasn't sure if I had been thrown from the town or if the entire town had been thrown from me. My doughy flesh boiled and sizzled with intense heat. I felt powerful, like a god.

I raised my doughy hand and examined it, smiling with a satisfactory glee.

Then it crumbled to ash and blew away on the wind.
I began to laugh.

As I laughed, more of my figure faded into grey dust.

Suddenly, a large black streak came from the sky, penetrating the beautiful stillness of the air. Large, ghastly talons came at me and I hated its approach.

"I want to stay! I don't want to leave! Let me go! Leave me be!" I did not say nor think this for I was no longer of physical form, but that is what I felt as the claws captured me. My blue flame grew larger with aggression, upsetting the black figure as we soared out from the greenness. We flew higher and higher and I got angrier and angrier. By the time we reached the land of limbo, the black figure's leg was mildly scorched. The figure, turning out to be a large black bird, pecked at my flame in anger, causing me harm even in my spiritual form. It flew off and I tried to find my way back to that beautiful green land but limbo is endless.

I've gone from town to town, hoping and praying that perhaps I shall build another great Reactor and I shall again be basked in its radioactive glow. I know, however, that blasted bird will interrupt my moment of ecstasy again. This is why I shall do it repeatedly, each time savoring it more and more. The flames beckon and I march forth like a moth.

To victory I march.
To radioactivity I fall.
All hail The Almighty Pastry.
