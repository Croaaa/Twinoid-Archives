Its actually pretty cool! To watch brain waves and know where people are at in their head.

1st stage of sleep. Theta waves. Most people will tell you they are not asleep even if you hear them snoring.

2nd stage. Beta waves: Slow eye movement.
(They are asleep but can respond to questions if asked...this is the stage most people go into under hypnosis)

3rd stage. Alpha waves. Dreaming but can wake with clear memories of the dreams. This stage is where you dream of falling and jerk awake...or think you hear some one calling you. Also when you have the sometimes whole body jerk. People that have sleep problems never go past this stage. They have frequent (100s) of apnea, where they stop breathing for up to a few minutes each time. The body goes into survival mode and wakes itself up. They can "sleep" 10 hours and wake up exhausted.

4th stage. Delta waves. Growing, hormonal sleep, therapeutic. Rejuvenating.
(teenage boys in particular will sleep a lot during this stage. This is when they will do all their growing spurts. IF they don't get enough sleep that could affect height, testosterone levels etc....)

5th stage
REM sleep, deep paralytic sleep. rapid eye movement.

And the typical cycle of 5 stages only last 90 minutes and you have 4-5 of the 90 minute episodes in a good night's sleep.

I watch 8-12 hours of recorded sleep sent to me. Scoring what stage they are in every 30 seconds. Which is called an Epoch. I specialize in children that need ventilator adjustments or need to be assessed for critical recovery to make sure they will survive serious surgeries.

I give reports on kids from Madrid, France, India, Barcelona....all over the world. I am taking a hiatus from my real career and only doing this for a bit.
