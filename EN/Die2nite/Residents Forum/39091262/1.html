Well we had this one game, my elementary was built during the cold war, so there was a grand total of 3 windows in the entire school, we figured out that you could turn lights off through these random holes in the wall if you have a knife (one of those table knives) so at recess during rainy/snowy days we'd go turn off the lights and the school would become pitch black except for those 3 windows, there was around 300 students total, we made 10 students "ghosts" and the rest were the living these teams were normally decided months in advance. the only places ghosts could not enter was the light (with 3 windows there wasn't alot) but with 45 minute lunch breaks and 15 minute recesses the goal of the humans was to make it into their classes (which had seperate lights you could turn on) and the teachers and janitors would slowly begin turning on the lights again (there were dozens of these little slots to turn on the lights and only 1 master key for them)creating more and more safe zones for the living, the ghosts had to be fast and quiet if they actually wanted to win, and if a ghost caught you you'd become one. fun game all in all
