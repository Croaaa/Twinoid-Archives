On october 3, 2012
coro

It's because of MT's general stance on the issue

Allowing people to make multiple accounts lessens their ability to make money. Let's talk about a theoretical situation

There are 4 residents and 1 hero. The one hero can control all 4 residents if the residents leave themselves in escort mode. Whether these accounts are run by separate people or not, it's exactly the same end result (ignoring the potential for one of the residents to exit escort mode)

If 5 different people control these accounts, it's more likely that one of the 4 residents may get an urge to buy hero days. One person is highly unlikely to pay for hero days on multiple accounts


If you think it's because MT wants to make sure that all towns are fair, then you aren't thinking like a businessperson
On october 3, 2012
Hacksaw

It's a blanket rule for all MT games since, in some games, the ability to have more than one account can grossly imbalance the game. I used to hear about it a bit in Kingdoms where one person would run multiple accounts to gain greater control over a map, both by blocking towns from other players and by using those multiple accounts to amass a larger army.

In other games (Caverns of Hammerfest and Natural Chemist, for example), you have to pay to play more than once a day (well, Natural Chemist had an in-game way to eventually play a bunch for free), so multiple accounts means you didn't have to pay for more turns.
