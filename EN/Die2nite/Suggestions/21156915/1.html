Perhaps some sort of automated rating system?

Each time a player dies of dehydration due to inactivity, dies outside without camping, don't spend any AP or don't use the forums at least once a day, would receive some quantity of points.

Say it operates on a rolling fortnight, so after 14 days that/those point(s) disappear. If you have more than a certain number of points, you can't join Distant or Hardcore towns, just Local or 'Purgatory' towns - 'Purgatory' towns being the same as Distant towns, but exclusively for people not meeting the average criteria for an active player - you wouldn't shed points while not in a town, but you wouldn't gain any either.

This way, if people decide eventually to sort out their issues, their points would disappear over time and they would regain access to standard towns with other active players, and if they lapse and stop doing those things, they would gain points again and drop back into those limited size towns.

Thus, keeping main towns active and shoving people who don't do the essentials into special towns until they demonstrate for an extended period that they can actually be active.

As for 500SP towns, I think 100SP is enough. Keep the towns at that level, it forces experienced players to play with noobs like me so I learn stuff.
