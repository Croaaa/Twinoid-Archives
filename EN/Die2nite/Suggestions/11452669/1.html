Adzman

That's an interesting idea, R3D.

If you look at it from an AP point of view, then there is a benefit for voting Workshop, for arguments sake you build 1600AP on buildings over the first 8 days, each distributed evenly per day, so 200AP per day (Just for simplicities sake, I know it's not distributed like that, ever.)

First day you would save 10AP
Second Day 20, 3rd 30, 4th 40, 5th 50

So over the first five days you save 150 AP, then another 150 AP over the next 3 days, then you take into account how that AP is distributed per day, you're probably looking at an AP saving of around 250AP for the 8 days.

Now, in those same 8 days, let's assume you vote Watchtower, you will get no AP benefit for the first 4 days, but if you have say 20 long range scavengers (By long range I mean non scrap searching) on each of the next 5 days, with 10 scrap searches at 1AP, you will achieve an AP benefit of 50ap on Day 5, and 70ap on day 6 through 8, so a total saving of 260AP over the 8 days. And those scavving numbers are probably slightly exagerrated, as you're less likely to have 20 long range scavs on day 8, so lets say 200AP saving in total.

Ok, so why not vote Workshop?

The AP maths fails to take into account a couple of quite significant factors which Watchtower has as a benefit over the Workshop, as from a purely AP perspective, you will almost always save more in the long run AP wise. (With probably a couple of exceptions), but Watchtower gives you the ability to get into searching at 10 and 11ap zones without any additional need for boosters, which is probably 40 to 50 undepleted zones which were previously inaccessable in the same way as before, meaning that a hell of a lot more stuff can be found, reducing the need for the searcthower to be voted and boosters to be untilised for 'normal' scavenging, tie that in with the ability to see zombies for 10km around, which is invaluable in effective zombie killing in more serious towns, meaning that you can actually scavenge without having to worry about zombies (sure, you still have to do the clearing, but it makes it a hell of a lot easier), is why, in my opinion Watchtower always trumps Workshop.


Disclaimer: I pulled all of those numbers straight out of the air, and were purely for demonstration purposes.
