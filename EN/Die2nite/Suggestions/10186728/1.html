Just food for thought... how about, instead of some suggested multiple towns per map, there's "mini-towns" such as 10 people in a hideout, scattered amongst the map, maybe 5-10 spaces apart.

Think of it as small towns within ruins. One has the WPP, another an Armoury, another a store, another a something-random. They have to coordinate to find what they need, deliver items to each other, and if cooperation fails, one can try to cut off the other so they dehydrate or are eaten and the citizens split.

Nightly attacks would have to be divided some by how many are staying and where, and alittle more emphasis on residential buildings being interchangable (so no more than 15 or 20 people are in a single town at a time, but one's kitchen can be used by a visitor in his place).

Alot to work out, but I think this is something that would build on strong coalitions and not metas.
