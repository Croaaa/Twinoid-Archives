Leapfrog

We all know Survivalists rule roost when it comes to camping, and that's the way it should be. The lack of a cap on their camping skills is part of what makes them the coveted class that they are.

But have you ever thought that it might be nice to have some bonuses with the other heroes to shorten the considerable camping gap? I'm not talking about increasing the cap for any other class; they all should stay capped at 90%.

I don't actually know if this is a good idea in terms of game balance, etc - if not, just treat it as a creative exercise:

Tamer: If the Tamer's dog isn't exhausted, the Tamer can command it to help guard the campsite. This grants a moderate boost (worth 2.5 defence objects?) to the camping chance, but the next day the dog will be exhausted.

Guardian: The Guardian is able to ignore an amount of zombies equal to their control points for the purpose of determining their camping chance. They also gain a slight boost (.75 of a defence object?) to their chances.

Scavenger: The Scavenger becomes very familiar with the zones in which he/she spends time digging up items (and the shovel comes in handy too). For every autosearch they have performed in that zone on that day, they gain a +2% to their camping chance.

Scout: While they already have an advantage when camouflaged, the scout could gain a bonus to their chances according to how well-explored (by other scouts) the zone is.

Technician: The Technician is able to use their construction points to upgrade a campsite or install defense objects, but not to look for a tomb.

Shaman: By expending a weak soul, a Shaman is able to create mystic wards that provide a significant boost (30%) to their camping chance. If they happen to have on hand a powerful soul, they can use that for an even greater increase (50%).
