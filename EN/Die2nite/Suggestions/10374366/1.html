Hi Motion-Twin.

First of all, thanks for continuing to support Die2Nite with new updates. This one was a little bumpy, but things are coming together now. I'm really looking forward to trying out the new Necromancer Shaman class and collecting peoples' souls.

But enough of that - to business! My suggestion is, please, give more notice for scheduled, irregular downtime, such as these updates, and let us know what time they will begin, not just what date.

You may or may not have already seen the thread 'Maintenence Stopped me from logging on' in the Help forum. Players have been killed because the extended and unexpected downtime during the Season 5 update prevented them from logging in and getting into town before midnight. I'm sure there are many more than just w00d and Comedian who experienced this problem in that thread, and I myself would be dead too if I were outside at the time - the game didn't come back up until around 1-2 AM in Australia, and I wasn't able to prepare in advance because we weren't told when the downtime was going to begin. I'm just lucky I'm a builder.

Now, sometimes things go wrong, and updates taken longer to implement than expected. There's not much that can be done about that, and that's fine. But please start notifying players about exactly when the downtime will begin so they can plan accordingly.

To do this, I suggest the following:
1) In the news feed, please mention the time that the update will begin, instead of just the date. A timezone should also be specified, but the international GMT or UTC is possibly more ideal than local French time, given Die2Nite's international player base. In any case, please don't forget the time zone so that we can figure out when it will happen locally.
2) Since the news feed is a little out of sight and out of mind, please also list any upcoming scheduled downtime in the main Die2Nite page after logging in (perhaps above the Daily Tasks), or on the sidebar, above/under the Hero Days and Chaos mode messages.
3) Optionally, perhaps consider sending an email to players a few days, or even a week, in advance. Not everybody likes receiving game emails though, so there should be an option to opt in/out.

In conclusion, please start notifying us about not only the date, but the time when these updates are planned so that we can plan accordingly.

Keep up the nice work.

Sincerely,

Faraday
