Private towns are currently not eligible for official ranking. I think there should be a leaderboard for private towns. I do not believe the original reasons for exclusion are relevant to competitive gameplay anymore.

Consider the following:

Majority of top-scoring towns have well-organized teams
Teams "reroll" when starting conditions are unsatisfactory
A "jump" can be unbearably delayed by slow-filling towns

The implementation of this suggestion would alleviate my pain-point as a metacoalition leader by simplifying recruitment, coalition organization, and jump planning which can be quite significant for a meta that has an average player turnover/rotation of approximately fifteen players per town.

I suspect these benefits are appealing to many other organized players as well. Additionally, I think the majority of non-organized players who enjoy the game and aspire to play and learn more will benefit from the decreased barrier to metacoalition gameplay. Metacoalition play, to me, seems like a natural progression in a player's Die2Nite experience. It allows for the exploration of game mechanics that are otherwise inaccessible.

While there are risks and complications involved, I think many of them are predictable and could be solved with a few simple modifications to Private Town Creation interface.

I bring forth this suggestion now because I have recently observed just how slowly distant towns fill from 0 to 40 nowadays. It took more than 5 hours for a town with 18 players to fill after the attack. The subsequent town elapsed approximately 20 hours to fill from 0.

Thank you for reading,
Sense
