Interrobang

If you're just looking for a co-op board game of sorts, Pandemic is a pretty simple version of that. Only four people, though. Definitely not on the scale of Die2nites 40 person town.

Arkham Horror is in a similar "co-op against the board" situation, but the similarities sort of end there, outside of the fact that it also has a horror theme. Much more complex than Die2nite in my opinion.

I haven't played Zombicide myself, but if you're really just looking for a co-op board game with zombies that sounds like the safe bet. (Outside of the Walking Dead boardgame. Neither of which have I actually played, so I couldn't tell you for sure if the similarities end there.)

The problem is, die2nite isn't strictly co-op though. And to me it seems kind of hard to put the "cooperate, but also play selfishly" deal into a boardgame. At least not in the way it's presented here.

That Dead of Winter game looks extremely promising though. And probably hits the mark better than anything I've listed. I'm definitely going to look into that game.

Daxy

I was massively disappointed in Dead of Winter, to be honest ... maybe because I preordered from Plaid Hat a year before it shipped and was expecting too much.

As for the "feel" of D2N - working together, but also having a selfish element (random towns) I'd suggest trying Shadow Hunters or the Battlestar Galactica Board Game.

Neither is zombie themed, but both require coop play without knowing who may betray you. Both games are very fun!

With Zombiecide there is usually one person who essentially ends up "running" the rest of the people at the table, either because they have acquired the good stuff in the game, have played more often or are just plain bossy by nature. This is a common feature of most coop board games, unfortunately.

Edit: If you want a non-coop Renaissance themed game, please give mine a gander:

Renaissance Wars
