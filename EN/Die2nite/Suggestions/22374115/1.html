Perhaps there could be a twisty thing going on with the tree:
As you unlock powers, they become less potent but they also cost less.

At first, you have trees for Scavenging, Survival and Co-operation.
As you proceed up those trees, the old abilities become less potent, but cheaper...
...like this:

Before unlocks in the tree:
Constructions - get +6CP as well as your AP. (12pts)
Locked
Locked

After 1 unlock in the tree:
Constructions - get +3CP as well as your AP. (6pts)
Conversions - every Mechanism or BED has a 20% chance to yield 2 objects instead of 1. (10pts)
Locked

After 2 unlocks in the tree:
Constructions - get +2CP as well as your AP. (4pts)
Conversions - every Mechanism or BED has a +10% chance to yield 2 objects instead of 1. (5pts)
Productions - Every 5AP used building (in one go), gives an extra 1AP progress. (12pts)

This way, after 2 unlocks in the tree, you can opt to spend only 4 Class points on getting a 2CP pool.
OR you can choose to get it three times, and spend the original 12 Class points to get the full 6CP pool, or get it twice for the midway and get a 4CP cap.

This way, you'd always have the same amount of points - new players will get powers of equal strength to the experienced heroes, or the experienced heroes can have a plethora of weaker skills that aren't as useful individually, but make them a 'jack-of-all-trades'.
