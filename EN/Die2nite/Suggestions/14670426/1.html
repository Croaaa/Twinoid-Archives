So my suggestion is simple-whenever a hero dies he leaves his professional tool behind and it can be picked up by any resident. The resident will automatically adapt this tool as professional and will not be able to toss it or get another one.
The catch
1. they get NO special hero powers or bonuses besides the ones the tool provides.
2. All tools will be in a bad condition and will only function at half efficiency. Moreover such inheritors do not get the passive bonuses of the class.
Namely
Scavenger- bent shovel- -15 minutes +10% chance to search. No additional benefit.
Scout - torn camo vest - half the chance to succesfully sneak past zombies. Does not allow the bearer to use crow's nest, does not increase the zone's exploration count(but can make use of it)
Defender - dented riot shield- 1 cp does not provide town defence does not allow the bearer to use guard tower
Tamer- sad labradoodle - can only carry 1 object per day cannot be drugged.
Survivalist- tattered survival guide - 50% chance to find nothing when searching for food/water. DOES NOT provide any bonus to camping.
Technician- worn-out toolbelt - 3 cp per day, can only take impressions for 1 ap 5 oxy and no more than 3 per day.
Shaman- chipped voodoo mask - any healing done only succeeds half as much. Cannot call rain or transform souls, does not count as a real shaman for temporary defences.


The purpose it to give the residents a chance to see how heroes get to lead an easier life reliably, but without notably empowering them. Of course seeing how scouts can skip zombies or how survivalists can easily find water the person might just decide to beome a hero~ Basically a way to trial hero status without getting the ever-elusive LMS.
The chance of tool dropping can be reduced to 50% just to make griefing heroes less attractive.
