I really like the Excavate idea, but only if it were a guaranteed pull of a random item (normal odds applied). I could see the code on this being adjusted if the zone is undepleted or depleted, as you could argue that a depleted zone should have an increased cost (2 AP?) although admittedly we'd all like that same 1 AP cost even in a depleted zone.

In response to the original question, "Why are scavengers unpopular?" I offer the following:
First, a discussion on what we know Scavengers are given (based on the description at the Help page):

- 30 minutes less for each search. This in itself is not advantageous if the player has a pattern of playing the game in long log-off increments to where the zone would be depleted hours later, whether the searches lasted 1.5 hours or 2 hours. For example, if a player logs on immediately after the attack, moves quickly through a few zones to a spot 6 AP from town to an undepleted zone and then starts searching, then logs off the computer for 12+ hours, the player's inventory could be filled and the zone depleted regardless if a Scavenger. That same player may log in before the attack, move quickly from that depleted zone back in town and then see no benefit from the Scavenger skill. However, a counterpoint is that if this same player logs into game late in day after the attack, the time required to deplete the zone is lessened. So in summary - players' pattern of play can make this more or less effective.

- Scavengers have a higher chance of finding an item. That's what it says at the Help page, so let's not confuse that important and simple point. This does not mean you get a better chance of finding rare items, but just a better chance to pull an item. Combined with my prior discussion - this is also more or less effective a skill depending on the players' pattern for logging in and playing the game. And as result, it could be totally irrelevant and ineffective.

- Scavengers can see if a building is in the next zone, or if items are there. This skill is near useless for veteran players who initially know what to look for in new towns. Smaller zombie spawns on day 1 can be indicators of a spawn point (i.e. site/ruin), and combined with high percentage of participation of the town (especially a meta) makes this skill near useless. The skill to see if items are in a square over is also superseded by the plug-ins like FDTD which provide more data and specificity than a Scavenger can provide.

- 50% more oxygen in a ruin. This only matters if you are a subscribing Hero. For a player given only a short time to be a Hero (like after LMS), by the time you discover/clear/explore a ruin you have nearly run out of Hero time so this bonus won't do you much good. For a subscribing Hero, given the arguments I have already laid out, this bonus could be the single and only advantage you have. So - is it worth it then? To most, No.

I know I'm simplifying this a bit and for veterans - not intending to affront you but the Scavenger class is lacking at the moment.
