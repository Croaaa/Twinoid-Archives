Not sure if any of you are aware of this, but it is common practice with business' dealing in of shore currency to make short term sales more attractive to their OS customers when a particular currency is losing confidence in the world market.

As noted a few posts above me, the AUD is currently stronger than the USD (and has been for some time ), yet onshore US customers seem to be getting a bargain basement deal as compared to onshore Australian's (36% better).

Now I don't suspect Motion Twin to have an International Currency expert on their books, but it makes perfect sense to get as much revenue now from the US, as market trends are clearly showing a lack of confidence in the USD, which generally leads to a marked reduction in the value of that currency.

Why take 10 monetary units in your own strong currency now, when the same service you provide, and over the same time frame, can net you 20 units in just a few months time?
