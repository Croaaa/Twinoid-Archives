Soon as I die in this town I'm going to collect some season 6 camping data.

For starters, just want to point out that you are a scout, not a survivalist. You do not have 100% optimal survival chances, you have 90% screw you when you least expect it decent chances.

Here's what I found out season 5:
Base chances for distance:

  • 4 KM = 45%
  • 5 - 11 KM = 55%
  • 12 KM = 60%
  • 13 KM = unknown, but greater tahn 60% and less than 65%
  • 14 KM = 65%
  • 15 KM = 70%
  • 16+ KM = 75%

Pro camper degrade scale:

  • 1st night = no modifier
  • 2nd night = -10%
  • 3rd night = -20%
  • 4th night = -40%
  • 5th night = -50%

Miscellaneous modifiers:

  • normal zone upgrade = +5%
  • camping item = +5*
    (at the very least holding 1 groundsheet and 1 festering flesh = +10%)
  • 5 zombies = -35%
    (supports season 4 data that 1 zombie = -7%)
  • Installing a DO or digging a tomb = less than 10%
  • Buried buildings, despite being a level 2 topology, provide a +40% bonus, errant to the standard bonus based on topology
  • each night zones degrade -15% (or 3 full upgrades)

I have reason to believe the bonus for DO's and digging a tomb is +9% (or was in season 4 and 5), but haven't been able to fully test their values yet.

70% + 20% to negate camping penalty = 90%
90% - 35% to negate the level 4 topology provided by the Scottish Smith's Superstore = 55%
With no other modifiers in play except for distance, 9 KM must be 55%.

Also, testing in season 4 and 5 points to the survival scale loking as follows:

  • hee haw = 10% or less
  • really poor = 11 - 30%
  • poor = 31 - 50%
  • limited = 51 - 65%
  • largely satisfactory = 66 - 80%
  • decent = 81 - 90%
  • good = 91 - 99%
  • optimal = 100% or more

bold numbers at the low end of the scale are the important ones to look at. Upon reaching optimal I fill in percentages in reverse, removing each upgrade until I get to the starting point to determine values. based on a +5% bonus per normal upgrade and final two upgrades following the pattern
decent good optimal
