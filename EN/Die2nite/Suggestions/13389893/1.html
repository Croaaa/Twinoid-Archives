Remember when everyone was excited at the beginning of S5 that Shaman might be able to equalize unavoidable stupid early deaths?
I for one was a bit disappointed that it did no such thing, but rather spice up the endgame a bit, which is good imo.
The problems remains though: Have early deaths and you have no chance at the leaderboards.

My idea is simple:

Let souls in the World Beyond "age". For example, for every 2 days a soul has been in the WB you gain an additional point when converting it into town points.
However, to make things a bit more challenging, let souls wander towards the rim of the map after 2-3 days so that you really have to think if you want to let it age or if you want to get it now.

In the end, it's not so much about rebalancing the Shaman, but about rebalancing early deaths
It's just an idea and a very rough draft.

I play in semi-organised/semi-random hardcore towns and we have found souls very helpful.

We have a dedicated shaman and we use him to help with water and with Pluie Bénite.

Pluie Bénite is by far the most economical and productive temp from souls, and since we have so many randoms it has helped us a few times gain that extra day because getting the dumps built with randoms is very very hard.

Our shaman has his own thread and tells us where the souls are (and healing requests) and it's usually the tamers who go out and get them. Our shaman is the cook too and he starts his kitchen from day 1.

In our healing experiments so far it looks like curing hungover or drunk is the best use because it can help your experienced long range scavengers do it again tomorrow and maintain their drug free CP for a bit longer, and with no risk to the shaman.

We experimented with the making it rain and decided that making it rain in town was by far the best use. But it does have a failure rate (unconfirmed estimate 10-20%).

We balance up the confirmed souls in the desert and try to make sure we have 5 in the bank by day 10, and all the rest are used to try for water (each shaman can only do it once a day). The water gained has usually helped us to avoid one pump upgrade.

I like the aging of the souls idea, and the progressive moving away from town, but it would make more sense imo if they were to age in the negative.

That would give your shaman more use earlier on imo because the souls have maximum point value the first day after death, and each subsequent day they are uncollected they are not only worth less, but there is the risk they will move in to untravelled zones and be lost in the shaman sights.

I don't see what the negative fuss about the shaman is anyway. If you're after to make the leaderboards then the shaman/tamer combo is OP.
