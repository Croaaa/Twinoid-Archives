Hacksaw
I like the idea of spending Soul Points (or equivalent) for things, but I don't think it should be permanent powers; I think the shop should be on a town by town basis.

First thing I'd say is actual Soul Points wouldn't be spent, even though I'm not going for the SP leaderboard, I still like my high number. ^.^ But I do think your XP/MT-points should be earned the same way (say, if you survive 5 days, you get 15SP and 15 MT-Points).

3 ways to spend MT-Points:

When starting a town and you don't have any hero days (or just ran out during a town), you can select a Hero class and pay 1000 MT-Points to get 15 Hero days.

Otherwise, points would be spent in a new tab on your soul page. You could spend X amount of points for a serrated knife, or even more for a chainsaw (all weapons would come completed, so no good for getting rare distinctions). Could also by some food or water if you want or almost anything you might need.

Could even have a community contribution in the town bank where everyone could pitch in to get a hacksaw or jarpen table (as opposed to spending all your points yourself; points would deduct when enough contribution is made to get the item).

Con to this kind of shop is it'll favor those with obscene amounts of SP already when going for the leaderboards.

Another way could be that you earn MT-points based on days and citizens alive.

Lets say you die on day 10 and 40 citizens alive you'll get:
10+(40/5)= 18 MT-points to spend in your next town.

You spend your 18 MT-points in this town and you die on day 5 and 19 citizens alive:
5+(19/5)= 8 (rounded down) to spend in your next town.

You only get the points from the previous town to spend in your next town.
