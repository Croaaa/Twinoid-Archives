Right, so I was thinking of adding a customization option for residents who reached a certain amount of soul points, let's say 5000 soul points. As residents, they would unlock a perma-item that they can carry from town to town that's similar to a hero profession, but noticeably weaker. This is to give residents a slight taste of what it's like to be a hero and give them a goal for playing this game and surviving. It's basically an item that let's the resident have a much weaker version of a hero profession.

For example, let's say I was going to use the tamer class. When a resident reaches 5000 soul points, he has the option of picking "hobbies," a slight hobby the resident might have had before the zombie apocalypse. So the weaker tamer version for residents would be like a pet mouse, which gives 10 guard points when volunteering for watchman and can carry only ONE item back to town from anywhere in the wasteland.

Adding a slight option of customization and individualization would give residents something to look forward to as they play the game and an actual sense of commitment in their towns to do well and get their soul points to 5000, instead of hoarding items and getting shunned. The ability to chose a hobby, a much weaker version of a hero, would make them feel more important to the town and capable of doing more than complete noobs who just joined. It would also make the game less pay to win like and probably boost player count.
