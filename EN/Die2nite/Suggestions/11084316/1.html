floyduk

I'd like to air my thoughts on something that I'm sure has been much discussed but I'm not a forum regular and I just want to get this thought out of my head.

The risk/reward balance for camping is way out of whack. Camping is really *really* important for the town but it's so risky that those people willing to take the risk are punished by often dying and therefore pick up much reduced points scores for many of their towns.

The problem here is with the way points are allocated. The simple points for days alive equation rewards players who take less chances and therefore contribute less to the success of the town. Engineers will, therefore, pick up more points on average than survivalists or scouts. Even non-heros probably earn more points on average than your typical scout or survivalist.

I like to play classes that spend time outside the town. I want to be useful and contribute to the town so I often try camping for blueprints but I die a lot more than my non-hero coalition mates. I pick up way less points than they do and I spend less time alive in towns with them.

Spending lots of days dead while I wait for them to finish a town isn't *fun*. And *I'm* the one spending real world money on hero days.

Something is wrong with this picture.
