spacekadt

Ignoring that 5% is what screwed you. Terror and wounding doesn't matter on the day that everyone is on watch.

We've found that planning for two deaths is optimal. You'll likely see one, but hopefully not see any.

The best way to figure the excess defense is to figure out what your average watchman defense is and plan for two of those people to die - which means two other people won't be counted (though the dead ones will). So if your average defense is 300 per watchman, you want to be 600 over the number you're aiming for so you can still cover after two watchmen don't count.

grieferNo , I mean he agrees that even a watchmen gets killed upon watch, his defense still counts during that watch.

Let's make a simple example. Say that after counting the defense of the buildings in town you realize you need 300 points to cover the max estimate. Therefore you add 4 people on watch:

1) ghoul at 100%, giving a defense of 200
2) normal resident at 95%, giving a defense of 50
3) drunk resident at 99%, giving a defense of 100
4) hooked resident at 85%, giving a defense of 30

Adding their defense together (380) you would cover the max attack. You think: "Cool, the chances that one will die are low, and even if one dies, it'll be probably the hooked resident that provides only 30 defensive points. So if he dies, we're still left with 350 points, that are enough to cover the attack".

The attack comes and say that you're unlucky and it really is equal to the maximum estimate (for the sake of making an example that is simple).
Now say that you were right to a certain degree: the hooked resident really dies on the wall. And here comes the bug: his defense is still counted (30). However, the defense of another random person on the wall is not. Say that the RNG really hates you today, so unluckily the unprocessed watchmen happens to be the ghoul. You make the math now: the effective extra defense you got from the watchmen is: 100+50+30=180. That's a bad news, because it means you have an overflow of 120 zombies entering the town. So you end up with an ex-hooked corpse + some other deaths in town from the overflow, while wondering why must life be so hard.

so when person a died, person b's defense didn't count

if person b had a large chunk of defense, that loss is taken into account and you may lose additional watchmen if the defense doesn't bring you to the 95% survival rate

how to survive:

spread your defense as evenly as possible. if your average defense is 200 per person and you have a large number of non-guardians on watch, plan to lose 2 people. this means 400 defense won't count. so overshoot your target defense by 400. that way when you lose that 400 defense, you're still at the 95% mark (and covering attack) and your watchmen and citizens will come out of it just fine.
