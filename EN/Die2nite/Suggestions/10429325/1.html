On Topic:
The cost of a Twisted Plank to build Siesta Time 2 can almost be ignored. Assuming your town has the hacksaw or has the factory built, then the first two AP earned after building Siesta Time 2 can be used to convert a rotten log into the expended Twisted Plank. Sure, it still doesn't make up for the fact you spent 3 AP on Siesta Time 2, but since Siesta Time can be attempted daily, it's the potential for 2 AP extra each day per person who has it built, and together it could be pretty powerful.

Early days have low zed attacks, so even if you do miss a build or two on day 1, with a few siesta times, it could be made up by the end of day 4, and then it can start giving the town extra AP to get an edge in workshopping and building as the days progress. If it takes about 10 days to pay for itself AP-wise and most organized metas don't abandon the town until day 15 or later, then it's arguable you'll gain more out of a few Siesta's than you'll lose in the beginning.

Depending on how the attack curve's been adjusted, it's possible for towns to survive even logner than the 15 day mark, at least, I'm hoping the attack numbers have been made lower, it's been WAY boring after about day 10 with being forced to stay inside and blow all AP at 23:30 on defenses just to make the defenses for the rediculously inflated attack numbers.

No matter what, though, it's still a gamble, and if Siesta Time 2 isn't up by day 3, then it isn't worth the risk at all, IMO, and certainly won't be worth the risk in a random, unorganized town that isn't as likely to reach day 10 let alone surpass it.
