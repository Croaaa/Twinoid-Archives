First off, i just want to apologize if this has already been done in alternate versions of the game. Having only played this version and having never been to any Die2Nite wikis, I like being surprised just like everyone else

So, possible spoilers ahead, but completely unintentional..


My idea is a simple one, and it involves putting 2 or more towns on the same map. This map would be larger than usual, and the reason why I said 2 or more towns is because one town would not be able to see any other town until one of its citizens discovers it.

Citizens of town A could stumble across citizens from town B (pr C, D.. etc depending on the number of "other" towns) but citizen A would not know which town this citizen was from.

The point of this is making Die2Nite more of an apocalyptic simulation game, when the issue of coming across other survivors arises. Do you help them or shun them? Try to sneak into their town (possible new hero class, "spy?") or peacefully trade resources, and even invite them into your own town, if their own town is hopelessly failing?

I think it would be best if the two towns were spaced far apart, far enough that it is not guaranteed that you will not run into another town, and once again, it is impossible to know what other towns (if any) are out there. This would help create a feeling of hope mixed with isolation and the despair of being one of the last citizens alive, without any water, roaming the desert for help...

What do you guys think?

Some other suggestions.. (sorry btw, I should've titled this as a Suggestion thread, noob mistake )

"Versus" maps sound cool, and would definitely make events like Clash of the Titans much more awesome

I do not think that "versus" towns should be the only form of "multi-towned" map, because imo it would be interesting to be put in a map with no idea what other town or towns are out there. The map would be the same size regardless, and big enough so that towns wouldn't be running into each other all the time. This wouldn't happen every game.

There could be towns that want to ban together and help each other out, but there could be other towns who wanted to stay isolated (in order to save resources such as water and food).

LIke Nathan said, the BP could be used as currency, and should definitely not be allowed to be used more than once.

An issue could be that as one town nears another, they would start to stumble across depleted zones and items such as meaty bones, which would be dead giveaways (pun INTENDED). Perhaps scavengers could gain a buff that would allow them to make zones appear undepleted to citizens not in their own town. There could also of course be the option to never search a zone until it is depleted, and to be sure to retrieve meaty bones and bring them back into town (perhaps the Tamer's dog could do this without taking up a bag space?) and this would require a huge amount of coordination within the town.

Instead of a brand new "spy class," a buff could be given to the Scout which would make him either invisible to members of other towns, or appear as a Zombie, but could be attacked, injured, and forced to come out of hiding. The Watchtower could be buffed to reveal all disguised scouts within x number of km from the town.

Maybe one town could kidnap a citizen from another town if they have more control points them said citizen, and they could hold hostage until other members from the citizen's town come to rescue.

There would have to be a separate chat for town to town communication, and this chat would only be unlocked if both towns run into each other in the world beyond, because, once again, there would be no way of knowing who else is out there until you either find them, or they choose to be found.

Overall, I think this would be best if designed for towns to be AGAINST one another, as oppose to multi-town games to always simply turning into 80+ player towns.

dDShockTroop
Sorry for the long-winded response, but I always like to have detailed suggestions to convey my idea...

How about they add one huge world with a map size so large it would be impractical to completely transverse (128x128?), with the minimap only showing a portion of the large map (27x27 centred on your current location, however the co-ords are still based off your starting town, perhaps even with edge wrapping between N-S & E-W, to make it even more confusing). Then have towns of 40 players start springing up in random locations on the map, provided they are not too close to an existing town. Even while someone else's town is still running, a new town can still spring up at any time if people are joining the world. Also, coalitions will stick together in the one town, as always.

Attack sizes will be independent of each town (as with chaos mode), and a new town springing up will reduce the number of zombies in the area to a reasonable amount. A player from one town may enter another town, however, they will not be permitted access to the well, bank, construction sites, workshops, opening/closing gates, citizen online status & locations in the world beyond, and of course, they won't have a house there. So even if a town had been devastated long ago, its citizens may be travelling the world and hiding in towns that have left their gate open. They may also steal objects from other players' houses when in a different town, with the chaos stealing rule only applying if the town they steal from is in chaos.

A player in another town that does not belong to them may search refuse like a shunned player of that town, even if they are not shunned. If 8 complaints are registered against a player not belonging to the town they are in, they are thrown out of the town gates and may never re-enter, if a gallows has been built, they will be hanged, likewise if a meat locker has been built they become temporary defence. Complaints can only be registered against "guests" if they are in the town.

When a town becomes devastated, it will completely disappear when either 7 days have passed, or all of the original inhabitants are dead, whichever comes first. Also, from the moment the town becomes devastated, the nightly storms will begin to replenish depleted zones surrounding the town.

Structures and ruins will "reset" after 5 or so days of inactivity by caving in and becoming inaccessible, and requiring clearing to enter again. This will replenish the items found in the structure. (though they won't necessarily be the same ones) Also, each town may get 1 structure camping blueprint per co-ord, regardless of how many towns have slept in that structure or whether or not it has been reset.

The only issues I can see apart from general programming changes & data storage issues is the nightly storms would require a completely new system in order to work with multiple towns, along with the problem of scaling LMS awards & soul points to match the new multi-town system. I hope epoc considers this idea, though with my experience with browser game suggestions, I doubt it will stick.
