jollijim

Thanks Greenwing. What I'm attempting to achieve with this thread is sound out some ideas and suggestions of what players feel would be a good handicapping system. Either ideas on a whole system, or maybe even just a simple idea of an aspect that should be considered.

There's really little to discuss regarding a formula at the moment because it's not written yet (at least not in full). I'm asking for the input of people like yourself to help write it.

The current goal is to draw participating towns close together with regard to final town scores. Ideally we'd like all towns to finish within 100 points of each other to create a close competition where there is no clear cut favourite either before or during the competition.

Obviously there would be towns that last much longer than others. If you look at the current leaderboard you can see nearly a 3 week longevity gap between town #1 and town #15.

niggle has discussed with me a staggered start system, where the town leaders give an honest projection of how long they expect their town to last. Towns would start on staggered dates in an attempt to have them all finishing within a couple of days of each other. Ideally we'd like them to all finish on the same day... Unlikely, but we can try to make it close.

Stay with the current leaderboard.

It uses a reasonably simple points scoring system to rank towns. What we are working towards are some tweaks to that scoring system which would bring the town scores closer together.

957 points is a significant gap between #1 and #15, so we are currently focusing on statistical details that show definitive patterns as to why there is such a large scoring gap between these towns. We're not forgetting the towns in between.

niggle has highlighted to me one area that shows consistent data, and has devised a formula to attach to that area of gameplay only. Applying the formula to those two towns would bring Soulful Graves final town score down to 1451, and Tombs of Losers down to 618. A 13% shift.

I'm currently working on applying the formula to the other 13 towns to check for consistency, whilst niggle is working on a new formula that he estimates to have a further approx 15% shift.

Is there a statistically quantifiable area of game play that you think we should look at?

Check the Wikipedia entry for 'Polo handicapping'. We are working with a distinctively similar method to this 120 year old handicapping application.

Thanks for your input to date, and I hope I have answered all your questions and addressed all your concerns.
