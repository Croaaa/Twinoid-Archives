It has happened too many times before:

You've beaten all the odds,
No one else is left, they've all died...
All but you, You're the Last Man Standing

Now you're alone...
In an empty town shouting into the radio,
To no response, nothing left to do but wait.
The horde will come tonight and there is no way to stop them.
Nothing left to do but stare at the wastes and wait for death.

No! You've learned too much!
You've come too far to die in such a wasteful way!



My suggestion would be for hardcore maps only, and on that rare occurrence when the Last Man Standing is literally the only man still alive in the town.

Instead of sitting there waiting all day for the attack, and doing nothing the player gets the Last Man Leaving option.

This option would have a small chance of success, but the player forfeits a guaranteed LMS and basically dies. However in terms of the game his character goes out into the wastes in search of another town, because there is no reason to stay in the husk of the town alone.

Most likely your character dies, uselessly in the endless wastes, but there is the small chance they find there way to a new city.

If your character manages this then:

  • You recieve the Last Man Leaving Distinction and 3-4 times the Hero Days to make up for the LMS and Hardcore LMS days you lost.
  • You are only able to enter a Hardcore town that is just starting.
  • That town receives certain bonus for having the LML Citizen join (Inheriting blueprints or an extra constructed building something maybe a multiplier to the towns overall defense)
  • The LML receives a random selection of rare items from his trip through the waste.
  • For this life the LML gets certain extra bonuses on watch, ap, house defense and scavenging (Maybe just one or two of these)

The downside being that once this new town reaches the day that the LML's town died on, then horde that "followed him" arrives from one side or corner of the map. The zombie generation in this area ramps up and each day spreads more across the map further each day.

The number of Zed's in the nightly attacks skyrockets as well from that day on.



Achieving the distinction
Certain things add or subtract to your slim chances of survival in your search for new town.

Bringing food and water, weapons, Shelter (such as the groundsheet), certain heroes have better odds and always more then a civilian, as well as being a ghoul (though water would be a negative if you were). These add to your chances.

Carrying resources, heavy objects(Some might have a greater positive), human flesh of any kinda(unless your a ghoul), Items with little use. These would all be negatives.

Certian combinations would give even higher chances of survival but even the perfect combination never give you greater then 50% odds of survival.

You will always die when you play DIE2NITE:
The greatest achievement you will ever brag about,
Is how long you can survive.
