What you have to consider here is the overflow of zombies with and without your own watchman defense taken into account.

The overflow has an upper limit you can see here.

If the overflow is higher than that even with you on watch, then the answer is no, you shouldn't be on watch.
If the overflow is smaller than that, then all you can do is calculate your survival chances with and without your watch defense.

For example:
Day 10, 15 people inside, your home defense is 12, your watchman defense is 80, and the overflow is 100 (with your watchman def included).

If you sign up for watch, you have about 80% watchman survival odds (completely made up number, you have to check it on the watch/overview page - didn't have the patience to calculate it), and 90% when your home is attacked. That means 80%*90%=72% on your survival.

If you don't sign up for watch, then the overflow will be 165 (would be 180, but it is topped at 165 that day). That gives you a 60% home survival chance.

In this example you are better off not being on watch, but you have to consider things like people dying on watch, that won't count when the homes are attacked. This can be a good thing, if you have much more people inside than 10. If you have less than 10 people, it is detrimental to your odds.
It is pretty complex, and there is no simple answer (except when the overflow is much higher than what you can cover with watchman).
