spacekadt

Erg, so much misinformation.

Devastated town means everyone camping has an automatic 50% penalty. Yes, lighthouse helps, but that's a 25% bonus. You still have a net loss of 25% when camping. Considering the whole point of camping is to live as long as possible to get more points for the town, that's not the best way to achieve the goal.

10 people in town - you will still have lazy zombies with less than 10 people... or more than 10 people. But MT uses a formula starting around day 5 to figure out the number of attacking zombies. Or the maximum number, anyway. I just woke up so I might be slightly off, but I believe the formula is: # of citizens * (day # +x) where x is a modifier that increases with time and I forget where the modifier starts and where it increases. The "problem" is that there's a floor to that formula. Regardless of how few people you have in town, the minimum is always going to be 10 people in town for purposes of the formula. So on day 20, you have approximately 250 attacking zombies... would you rather spread those zombies among 10 people (which is the actual number being used in the formula) or would you rather spread them among 5? The fewer people you have in town, the higher the individual attacks and the more chance of more people dying before they need to.

Hacksaw

There was a post made by tamle in season 4 (or the information was reposted with credit going to tamle, I don't remember which) that went something like this:
When we have an overflow, the number of zeds attacking the citizen inside depends on how many people stayed inside town. The rest of the overflow zeds become lazy zeds--they wander and don't attack anyone. So if you understand how attack zeds are calculated, then just subtract that from the overflow zeds to find out the lazy zeds.

The attack zeds follow this simpe formula that reflects the number of citizen inside and the day of overflow:

attack zed = (day + x) * y

where,

day = day of overflow

x = 1 for day 6 to day 14
x = 4 for day 15 to day 18
x = 5 for day 19 to 23
x = 6 for day 24 or higher

y = 10 if citizen # in town is 10 or less
y = citizen # if citizen # in town is 11 to 25.

The formula does not work if citizen # is greater than 25 and if day is 5 or less.

If the overflow zed is greater than the predicted attack zed, then the difference becomes the lazy zed seen for that day. If the overflow zed is less than the predicted attack zed, then there will be no lazy zed, ie., all overflow zed will attack.
In summary, if you have more than 10 citizens in town, more zeds will attack and increase the chance that someone will die. Also, if you have less than 10 citizens in town, then each citizen present will be attacked by more zeds, increasing the chance more people will die, possibly resulting in everyone dying and the town getting devastated.
