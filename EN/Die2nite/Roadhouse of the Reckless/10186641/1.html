thomas5251212
I can think of a couple of possible approaches. The big thing would be not making it so reliable it swung too far the other way.

Setting Guards. This would be an AP consuming process (probably 6 AP each day) That would make it so someone would have to get a certain number of "votes" to finish up a Construction project or take material from the bank. The big problem is logistical (an issue that anyone who's ever had to get the Gate closed understands); since everyone isn't on all the time, it could get hard to make it possible for people to get things out you did want them to. I suspect the only way to make it work would be to authorize some people to hand things out or authorize project starts, and make sure they were around a lot. Just for that reason, you'd want to have the whole thing require a town vote in the first place.

A more conservative approach would be to have some standard, low cost buildings (the "Bank" and the "Construction Yard", say) that required them to be "opened" and "closed" by vote and needed a minimum number to do it (probably eight just like shunning until Panic sets in). This has the advantage that it doesn't tie up ongoing AP, and if a town is completely unconcerned, they don't have to bother to build it.

Both of these should have a chance to "sneak in"; maybe it'd require an AP to try. In the case of the first idea (the "guards") if the guards control access you could always try to bribe or otherwise suborn them.

Just some off-the-top-of-my-head ideas. There's probably other problems with both of them I haven't seen.
