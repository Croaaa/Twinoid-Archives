Here is what I usually post in my beginner towns. I've got a few others saved that I sometimes bring out depending on the experience level of the other players, including help on water rationing, scavenging, camping, and ZKing.


Welcome to the town! I know that die2nite can be a little overwhelming at first, so here is a quick list of helpful information to think about on your first day!

Forums!

- If you're reading this, it means that you are already on the right track! Check the forums everyday to see what is needed, and participate in the discussion. The town forums are where you and the 39 other citizens in town will plan your defensive strategies for the town. Be a part of the conversation! Don't be afraid to ask questions!

Scavenge!

- Especially in the early days. Scavenging for resources in the world beyond will never be as easy as it is today! Just head out into the world beyond, pick a zone, and start autosearching! Just remember to make it back before you end up on the wrong end of the food chain.

Ration!

- You don't need to drink water every single day to survive! It's enough to just drink every other day, ESPECIALLY if you are inside building. Many towns have been brought down not by hordes of zombies at the gates, but from excessive water consumption. The general rule (there are exceptions) is that you should only be drinking on days when you have the thirsty status (displayed in your status bar up top.)

External Maps!

- There are currently a number of options for detailed maps. These maps allow citizens to keep track of zombies, items, replenished zones, and more. MUCH more information than can be found on the ingame map. There are currently 4 external maps, Atlas, External Map, From Dusk til Dawn, and the Oval Office. Firefox users, you can update all 4 maps at once by downloading this add-on: https://addons.mozilla.org/en-US/firefox /addon/die2nite-agent/

Unfortunately there is currently no equivalent for other browsers. If you're not using firefox, you'll need to update each individually, or find another way of working with your town.

These applications, and others, can be accessed by clicking the little book icon at the top left of the die2nite screen.

Don't Panic!

- If you are in trouble in the world beyond, stay calm! Post a call for help in the town forums. If you're trapped by zombies, or out of AP, you can request help. Maybe a passing resident can lend you enough control points, or take out enough zombies to get you home! Fleeing from zombies is a LAST RESORT, as it will ALWAYS leave you injured! Which leads to other nasty things...

Have fun!

- And don't take things too seriously


I'm trying to hit a balance between letting new players know what they should do to have a good experience, while avoiding the urge to just tell them what to do. I think this intro manages to fit that quite well.

The following text is brought to you by, Banshee85 Who sadly can not post on the forums anymore.

TL;DR Version:

Go out as far as you can! Always! Plan ahead!

If there is a zone more than 9 AP out, take those drugs or alcohol! Don't feel bad--you have a good reason!

Drop non-essentials on the ground when you stop to autosearch for a while!

Don't take risks without backup! Stop and be patient! You will survive!

USE AN EXTERNAL MAP! Look at the upper lefthand icon. Pick one of these: Atlas, External Map, Dusk2Dawn, Oval Office. Update every zone!


Non TL;DR Version:

1) Always scavenge as far out as you can!

The best zones are the ones that have been replenished by the Search Tower multiple times, after several days. Even without the Search Tower, zones will still replenish, you just won't know the exact direction. Choose a target area, and always take what you need to get there for AP. Know your limits--don't go on side-missions if it will leave you short of AP! If you can rescue someone, but will need a Heroic Rescue for instance, coordinate it ahead of time! After you get it set up, trust your fellow citizen to follow through.

2) Don't be afraid to use the town's resources if you think the return will be great enough!

Is there a zone more than 9 AP away that you know is undepleted? Feel like you need to ask the town's permission to take those Steroids or that Vodka in the bank? Well--take a look at the forums! If there isn't a reason to leave them there, go right ahead and take those drugs to scavenge. That's what they're there for, and why people put them in the Bank in the first place! Use them wisely.

3) Always drop all of your non-essentials on the ground when you stop to autosearch for multiple cycles (i.e. overnight)!

Why? Because there might be someone in a different timezone who could use any of the items you're carrying--not just what you dig up that you can't hold onto. Drop everything that you don't need to get home! Someone might log on right after you go to bed, get stuck nearby, and the person who could rescue them might have just be lacking the one item you're holding on to! Better to be safe than sorry.

4) Be patient!

If you get stuck, post a call for help on the forums. Don't simply flee, or fight hand to hand. Communicate with your fellow citizens! Odds are, someone will be able to come assist you. If you will need to log off, feel free to do so. Better yet, let the town know in your Call for Help thread. And if you know, let them know when you'll be back online. Try to include any information you think anyone who might be coming to rescue you will need to know--how much Control Points (CP) are present in the zone, how many zombies there are, what weapons you have, how much AP you have available including food, water and drugs--believe it or not, this is ALL helpful information! Help others, help yourself!

5) ALWAYS use an external map application when scavenging!

If you are going more than 1 AP out from town, take a look at the upper left icon. Mouse over it, and pick from one of the many options: Atlas, External Map, Dusk2Dawn or Oval Office. If one seems too confusing at first glance, try another one! The idea is to let everyone else in town know what is where. Information is key! Try and get into the habit of "updating" every zone you pass through. It's okay if you miss one once in a while--everyone does. But in more organized towns, having this information available is absolutely critical.

These apps have been developed for free by D2N players for your benefit! There are even custom-built extensions for your browser that have been created to make it as easy as possible to update these maps with as little effort as possible!

The Basics of Die2nite!

Never played before? Want to know how to get better at die2nite?

Well, here's a good starting point!

Forums

DO use the Forums!

If you're reading this then you're already on the right track, the forums are the place where everything goes down, even if you don't want to post in them, then read the various threads and find out what the town wants built , where people who might need help are and to generally talk to people in town! You can always throw your own voice in and tell the town your ideas!


DON'T spam the important threads.

It's best to leave the threads clear, so, if people are in a rush, and don't have time to read through the various construction discussions and the like, then they can just jump to these threads and know what to build, so you don't get people building or voting for all the wrong things!

My House

DO/DON'T Build a Tent.

There's much conversation about whether this should or shouldn't be done, especially early on in towns, some people support building tents early, some don't. Discussing it with your town first on when to build them is probably the best idea, but you will more than likely end up building them.

DON'T Build a Hovel.

Contrary to building a Tent, Never build a Hovel until your town has decided to allow them, as it's a great waste of 6 early doors and a surefire way to get yourself a shun on your soul, or possibly end up with worse! Those can be put to much better use scavenging/building vital defences for the town.

DON'T Hoard Furniture.

It's another surefire way to get yourself a (not so) nice shun on your soul, As they don't count for any distinctions until the day you die, and most of them (with a few exceptions) all have uses outside of looking pretty, many of them can be used for killing the zombies that are outside the walls of your town, and some, like the carcinogenic oven and beer fridge, can be used as part of a Hero's Laboratory or Kitchen, which can make really useful drugs and 7 food.

DON'T put Defensive Objects in your chest, DO put them in the Bank!

There are really two reasons for this, 1) It will yield more defense in the bank, as it doesn't yield any town defense while in your chest, but will yield one defense if in the bank, and 2) Is that it's indicative, like the Hovel/Furniture, of selfish play, and will more often than not get you a shun .

The Well

DO drain the well of water (If you have built a Pump!).

After your town builds a Pump, you have the possibility of taking an extra water ration every day. Take it and then deposit it in the bank. This is because the rations themselves are required to build certain structures (like Vegetable Plot and Moat). Also, there are water based defensive structures that actually use the water in the well as ammunition. Suppose your town has one of these structures, but the zed numbers are so low tonight, that you actually don't need it to fire. Well, you can't stop it. It will fire at them and it will waste your precious water. Unless, of course, it has nothing to fire with. That's why all the water from the well should be kept in the bank. Plus, in the event of a large zed spike, there's actually no problem to refill the well, so that the water based defences can fire at the zombie menace.

The Bank

DON'T Mix the Pharmaceutical Products!

Again, there's two reasons, one is that they are used in a couple of constructions, like the Vegetable Plot, which yields food everyday, so you want to get that built. The second reason is that mixing them by hand can get you any drug, whereas if you leave them for a Hero with a laboratory to mix them, they have a much higher chance of getting a twinoid from them, which is an extremely useful drug which gives 8 , you want lots of them!

DON'T use Water Purifying Tablets unless it's Needed.

Ok, It's not quite the sin it used to be (A few seasons ago it was one of the worst things you could do with a jerrycan), but you should still avoid purifying them until you absolutely need the water or your town is going to die of dehydration, as if you're lucky enough to find the Water Filter BP , which increases the yield of the Water Purifier, which currently yields between 1 and 3 rations, like the tablets, to between 4 and 9 rations, so hanging onto them for a while, while you don't need them is best.

DON'T put batteries in stuff.

Putting batteries into stuff, unless you're planning to use said item, is not a good idea, as you can't remove them from the items if you find a construction which needs them, or you find a better weapon which you now don't have the batteries to use it as you put them all into the lamps and pocket vibrators.


DON'T assemble items before consulting the forums.

There are some items in this game which come back as incomplete, like a devastator, which require various materials to make, like duct tape and nuts and bolts, and since these are valuable items, you don't want to build something which gives you a nice distinction, but then find you can't survive the night now since you can't finish the small fence.

The Notable exception is the first hacksaw, as the hacksaw is a very valuable item which reduces theconversion cost by an , so it often takes precedence over other buildings, but you should always double check first.

DON'T light up torches.

But Torches are fun!

They can be used to kill zombies (although they're bad weapons) or as a defensive object, even though they only yield a single defence, and since they cost both matches and rotten logs to make and they only last for one attack, they are not a good build, unless you're planning on dumping them at the dump.

DO Open cans and containers.

So much stuff you shouldn't Do! but there are things which are helpful; If you have the proper tools (usually a can opener, but many others will do), you can open certain containers in the bank. Things like cans, toolboxes and metal chests could contain useful stuff, so there's no need for them to lay around sealed. Plus, if you open them with tools, you might prevent someone from opening one of them in the workshop and losing in the process. Be wary of the safe, though. It costs to try to open it and it has a low chance of success, nor can it be opened by an item.

Citizens

DON'T attack people.

It's a lose - lose situation, you waste by attacking people, and if you injure them (despite the low chance to) they will need a bandage to heal themselves, which is a rare item which shouldn't be wasted and saved for emergencies. You'll also likely get shunned for your trouble, even if he was the one in the wrong, if you have got a problem with someone, sort it with them in a private message on twinoid or in town, and if you feel that they deserve shunning, then raise it with the town and everyone else can form their own judgements.

DON'T complain against people for NO reason.

It's just not a good idea, even if it's just for a joke it's likely to cause trouble, if you have a genuine problem with someone, then talk to them about it in a private message first, then, if that doesn't work, talk about it on the forums before deciding whether to shun them or not

Construction Site

DON'T Build anything before checking the forums

Every spent on construction counts and can be the difference between a smooth night and a catastrophic carnage. Thus, always make sure you're allocating your into the proper buildings. Not to mention, that if you build the wrong structure, it will eat up resources needed for the right structure.

Town Gates

DON'T Go outside without proper planning.

Plan your route and know what supplies you're gonna need for your trip before you go out, there's a bit more on scavenging later on

DON'T lock the gates before consulting the forums.

It may seem like a simple task, but locking the gates requires a lot of organisation. If you lock the gates too soon, you could lock some people out (especially if your town has a portal lock). Thus, always make sure your town has a designated gatekeeper for the night and try not to get in their way. Use the forums to organise a proper gatekeeping schedule. But whatever you do, remember to get them shut!


Watchtower

DO start your day here.

This should be a habit, just like brushing teeth or taking the morning piss. Every day, on your first log-in after the attack, go to the watchtower and help calculate the zombie attack for the next day. The sooner a town knows the approximate zed numbers, the easier it is to plan what should be built that day.


Workshop

DON'T open cans, chests or containers at the Workshop

These actions cost at the Workshop, yet can be opened at no cost using things like can openers.

DON'T convert before consulting the forums.

You need to check what things are needed, if anything is needed, to be made at the workshop for the current construction project, you don't want to waste converting wrong things

DO use a Hacksaw while converting.

If your town has a Hacksaw, make sure it's in your inventory when converting, as it saves an per conversion. Just remember to put it back afterwards!

Daily Upgrade Project

DON'T vote before consulting the forums.

Try to make a concerted effort with the other residents to vote the optimal structure every day. If you don't know what the optimal structure is then consult the forums.

DO wait as long as possible before voting.

The reason for this is that after more things have been built for the day and more info surrounding the attack and things like that is known, the easier the decision to which to vote is, and the vote may change over the course of a day.

Basic Scavenging

DO stay outside in one zone for the day and "autosearch"

Bascially, after your initial searching of a zone, every 2 hours you will automatically search a zone, without using any or doing anything whatsoever, so you can log off and then log on again 16 hours later and see what haul you collected overnight.

DON'T autosearch in depleted zones.

Unless you're 1 from town searching, as these zones only yield rotting logs and scrap metal, so wasting your day's for an inventory for them isn't the best idea in the world.

DO Update the external map applications.

The map apps, available from the Directory , are ways you can track what items are lying around where in the desert, and track which zones are depleted/undepleted, so if you keep a map up to date, which one agreed by the town, you can know where to scavenge most effectively!


DON'T drag items further from town.

Dragging items further from town is a waste of , because someone else can always pick up that item on their way home if they don't find anything better, but if you have dragged it further from town just to drop it, they can't.

That guide will get you started off with the basics, but it's nowhere near enough to let you succeed, read any other guides you can get your hands on, if you don't know something, check the wiki or ask someone more experienced in your towns.

You can also ask questions in the help section of die2nite.net, a free forum which is open to all players, alternatively you can jump into the official die2nite IRC channel and ask people in there for help, along with meeting some of the 'big names' from the World Forums.

But here is what the Camping Guide looks like:


How to camp effectively!

Don't camp on open ground!

Never camp on open ground out in the desert unless you absolutely have to! (I.e. you couldn't log on 'til 22:58 and you're 10 from town and can't make it back in time/locked out) There's no reward for camping on open ground for the town, you have worse chances camping on open ground than on a building and you're just risking your life for no reason, the scavenging bonus from camping, learned camper, is not a big bonus at all and has very little effect on your haul the next day.

Upgrade your zone!

Upgrading your zone is the best way to ensure survival, you can upgrade your zone a maximum of 10 times (each upgrade is worth 5% camping chance) and is simply done by hitting the "improve the campsite" button on the drop down camping menu, accessible by hitting the "inspect your zone" button, just above the "Search the Zone" Button.

You should aim to get your chances reading " You reckon your chances of surviving here are decent : you just have to hope for the best!", this means your chances are between 80 and 90%, and, once you get this message, you only need to tomb to ensure 90% camping chances (A tomb is worth 10%), Why not keep upgrading further? Because your chances are capped at 90% chance of survival unless...

...you're a survivalist , you can then get up to 100% chances! If you get 100% chances, then your zone message will read "You reckon your chances of surviving here are optimal. Nobody would see you, even if they were looking straight at you.", so a further 2 upgrades are necessary to reach these chances.

Always remember to tomb!

I’ve already briefly touched on this, but it’s worth repeating, always save an when camping so you can tomb! This gives you a camping bonus of 10%, which could make the difference between being zombie chow and getting that your town desperately needs!

Only camp on Buildings! (and make sure they’re clear of !)

The Main purpose of camping while the town isn't being over-run by zombies is to get Blueprints , and these can be obtained by camping on a building which has no debris on them. All you have to do to obtain the is to camp there overnight and survive, if you do, it will be lying on the floor for you in the morning! then you can pick it up and go celebrate finding that blueprint which has let your town survive an extra day!

Don't Camp a Building which has already been camped!

Don't camp buildings which have already been camped while the town isn't devastated, as each camped building only yields one , so repeated camping on it while the town isn't dead is pointless!

This also means don't camp multiple people to a building per night, as you'll only get one , so are risking yourself unnecessarily.


Don't camp with zombies!

Don't camp with zombies! As they give a camping penalty of roughly 6% each! (Reduced if you're a though), so you should ALWAYS try to clear your building of zombies with weapons before camping there!

Try to avoid camping repeatedly before town devastation

You want to try to avoid doing this as each night you camp means you have a camping penalty! For each night camped you get the following penalty from your original chances (If you hadn't camped at all):

-20% for 1 night camped [previously]
-30% for 2 nights camped
-40% for 3 nights camped
-50% for 4 nights camped

And no this penalty doesn't go away if you camp one night, then not the the next, but the day after again, it is with you for the rest of the town!

Try not to camp close to town!

If you have a building close to town, try to avoid camping on it, as it has a penalty for being close to town, the further the better!, so if you have a building one KM out of town, then try to avoid camping on it unless you have lots of camping items (Festering Flesh, Groundsheet etc.) which will add an extra 5% to your camping chances.

The percentage modifiers for the following distances from town are below:

-25% 1km
0% 2km
25% 3km
40% 4km
50% 5km
55% 12km
60% 13km
65% 15km
70% 16km

Try to take camping items!

I referenced these in the point above, but I'll mention them here again, in brief: Try to take a camping item or 2 when camping, as each one adds an additional 5% to your camping chances! So they're well worth taking out with you.

And... Finally:

When the zombie attacks get too big, go outside and camp!


When the attacks get too big for you to stay in town, the best thing to do is to go out and camp beyond the towns life, you will be able to rack up extra soul points for yourself, which means you can get to the distant regions even faster!

Although camping post-town devastation does come with a 70% camping penalty, you can still survive for a couple of extra nights out of town despite this penalty and rack up some nice soul points, and, if your camping post devastation beyond Day 10 you get a Rare Distinction called Zen Camper for each night post devastation you survive, which is a very desirable distinction!

So, there ya have it, a (not so) quick guide to camping!

How to scavenge effectively in the world beyond!

Go Out and Find a non depleted zone!

Scavenging in non-depleted zones is the best way to find resources! Searching in depleted zones only yields low quality resources, or, in other words, rotting logs and scrap metal. Generally searching 9 outside is the best place to start, maybe not on these smaller maps, but once you get to the distant regions, then it's the only way to scavenge!

Auto Search!

The Best way to scavenge is actually the way which doesn't actually use !, Every 2 hours, you automatically search a zone, without having to do anything! So you can go off straight after the attack, start searching, go off and do your everyday things and then come back online, as you're preparing to return to town and see what goodies you have found! Of course, if you can get on in the intervening time, and you see your zone has depleted, then you should move on!

Update the External Maps!

Please Remember to update the external maps in every zone you visit, you can do this in a few different ways, go to the map application of your towns choice in the Directory and update it from that site itself (The map apps are Map Viewer, Dusk Till Dawn, External Map, Cartographer and Atlas), or, if you're using Chrome or Firefox, then you can download an addon to update all 5 for you! The links to them are also in the directory , they're called Die2nite Agent CH and FF respectively.

Leave Stuff in the zone you found it in on the way out!

There's no point dragging stuff further from town, so if you find something as you walk to the spot you're planning to Auto search in for the day (updating the map apps on the way, naturally), you can then let someone else pick it up on the way in from their trip, or indeed your trip, if you find nothing better! Everyone will be able to see where everything as you've all updated the map app, right?

Check your Thirst Status!

In this game, you have a thirst counter, and you have to drink to live (much like real life), and for every 11 steps in the world beyond, you will decrease a thirst level, and if you have not drank that day, the next day you will wake up a thirst level down, so you have to plan your drinking and eating around your scavenging, otherwise you’ll end up dehydrated at a bad time, and will be stuck out in the world beyond, as to get off of dehydrated you have to drink a ration, but you don’t regain any from it!.

So what do you do? Eating followed by Drinking is the general rule, but if you're going on a long range expo make sure to check out the Zombie Survival Almanac and it's Dehydration Guides created by Annette and Sense, available by going to the or by clicking this link.

Don’t Scavenge in zones with lots of zombies!

You’ll get trapped by the zombies in a zone if there are more than 2 zombies in a zone, so don’t go and wander into a zone with multiple zombies in! If you however, get to a stage in the game where there are too many zombies around the town to go out solo scavenging, then team up! Your control points will increase by 2 for each citizen in the zone with you, so when you have enough control points you will be able to search! You can then coordinate getting back to town by either setting a time you’ll leave together in, or, if one of you is lucky enough to be a hero, by winning hero or purchasing it, you can leave your character on escort and they can be escorted home for you, so you don’t even have to log in again that day! However, it’s worth remembering that if you lose control of the zone at some point, your search will be interrupted, meaning you can’t search or auto search that zone again for that day.

There are things that do increase your zone control points, and being a Guardian or unlocking certain perks can also allow you to search in even more heavily infested areas, too!

Don't Die!

This should be pretty obvious, but don't forget to come back to town!

If you don't think you will be able to log on again to get back home from your scavenging trip, then DO NOT go outside and search, a dead person is no use to the town! You can just throw your into the appropriate construction project of the day

So, that's it, Scavenging 101, Hope you've found it helpful!
