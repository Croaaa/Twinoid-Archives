Did your love of zombies draw you to this game or is it just another game you found to play?

I love the zombie genre, but no one gets it right, not in movies or TV or games. The survival aspect is what's fascinating, not the gore and violence. How do people meet their basic needs and protect against the zombies at the same time? More importantly, how to people protect against each other? What rings true-to-life and makes the zombie genre truly interesting, is that the survivors ultimately prove more a threat to each other than the zombies were. (This is a huge part of what makes Romero's early zombie films, Night and Dawn, so gripping. It's also why 28 Days Later is pretty good.)

This is played out beautifully in D2N. Cooperation is the best way to ensure the longest survival for the most people. But invariably, sooner or later, the desire to survive produces selfishness. That's why I love random towns; the organization and selflessness in a meta doesn't reflect and develop the the game's heart, which is a psycho-social dynamic similar to the tragedy of the commons or the prisoner's dilemma.
