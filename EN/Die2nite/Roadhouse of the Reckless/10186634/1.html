Gomenolium was stuck 4 kilometers from town you see, three rotskins he said were ''marching with sniffing hands'' as they lead a army of undead towards town. I quickly grabbed my Battery Launcher.

I slide in a cylinder packed with gunpowder, the battery charged, pulsing electricity. I finished consuming my little meal, a bruised vegetable.

With food in my stomach, I hurriedly set out, communicating through walkie-talkes with Gomenolium. As I went there, I picked up a wrought iron along the way, seeing how heavy it was. Muncho must be pretty tough, I thought.

Idiotically, I went straight up to the zone, just waving at Gomenolium as he was crouched behind a lump of rock.

Without thinking, one of the rotters came up from behind me, awkwardly falling on my chest as I shot in the air. The battery exploded near us both, some shards glistening off my cheek, but most of the damage was absorbed by the rotter.

Throwing off the body, I threw out the shell casing, reloading a new one and then slamming down the hammer on the back.

I could tell the other two were near, but the sandstorm raging in the area was enough to make my eyes tear up.

I reached for the collar of my shirt, covering up the layers of skin from my nose to my chin. I brought down my hair, but it was no use. My hair is too short, the kind that looks like it gets grown out and cut ever few weeks.

The pistol in my hands, I felt unstoppable, but I was no match for what I faced next.

A freaking clown leaning over a gutted pig.

The pig was already dead, its organs being fished out by two rotters, a gray-looking female taxpayer wearing a totally wrecked suit, sliced open at the back and sides to show her raw intestines wrapped around her rib cage.

I was shocked as the clown surprisingly stopped its meal, leaving the taxpayer tearing into the heart of the juicy, pink pig.

I raised the gun. Shot. And everything was silent.

I could hear my ears ringing as my legs fell under me, something trying to grab at my face. I knew I hit something but really wasn't sure.

I struggled, my vision filled with foggy air and sand. Soon, the weight was lifted off my chest, but the pressure remained a still.

The next thing I noticed was I was back at the town, walking to my tent. And acting like nothing happened. The good thing was, Gomenolium was safe, but the bad.... the clown... he's still out there... I can hear'em.
