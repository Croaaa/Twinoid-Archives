What follows is nothing but my opinion of the moment based on my observations.

It's very difficult to get a successful group of players together, reasonably on the same page and ready and committed enough to challenge for #1 spot.

So far I have noticed three patterns (I won't name names):


A Three metas get rid off anyone who's not top notch and merge, with cohesion, discipline and dedication in mind.


B You meet people in game, recruit in towns and grow as a powerful meta because you're able to guide newcomers and they want to fit.


C You 'aggressively' poach a large number of players from less successful groups in a very assertive manner, promising excellent results.



Of course, nothing's all black and white and every Meta probably did a bit of A, B and C here and there.

Personally, I really like A and B and would recommend more dedicated players follow these ideas. Not that I can necessarily talk, but hey, I love talking.

FortisIs a high score the only measure of meta success?

Not even remotely. Each meta will define their own success... whether it's living til day 10, building a wonder (or 4), getting a ranked town, topping the leaderboard or something else entirely.

Regardless of your goal, you need a group of people working toward the same goal in order to achieve it. This shouldn't be a foreign concept to anyone that's been around for a while.


FortisI was recently assimilated by a highly successful collective who shall remain nameless.

First thought here - everyone has their play styles and some just don't enjoy certain other styles... and that's ok.

Then after more thinking, I realized that this is really rather wrong. Machetes didn't "quietly appear" and they were oft celebrated. Each large chest was bragged about and congratulated with much fanfare. Good scav days were noticed and commented on... but it was 99% in IRC and I remember you checking in here and there, but I don't really remember you being around all that much so your experience and, let's say PearShaped's, experience (as another person who played with us for the first time) were wildly different. And it wasn't just because of play styles and what you like and don't like. It was the interactivity among all of us and the friendly banter back and forth. The derps... the drama... not interpersonal drama but the fun (I think it's fun anyway) of fixing the last minute derps so someone wouldn't die.

I'm also going on record and saying your characterization of everyone having scripts down to the last AP throughout the town isn't exactly accurate. Unless it was a zombie killing mission or camp time, the bulk of the plans were "go scav 8/9/10 ap away", "build your house today", "sorry spacey, you can't scav today because we need build AP".

Maybe it was because the camp plans were the most recent in memory or maybe they just irked you enough that that's what you remember... or maybe you felt that way through the whole town. But if basic planning for the best way to use AP in the more general sense is too restrictive for you, "topping the leaderboard" probably shouldn't be something you strive for. Our crew has never made a secret of how we play and what our goals are.
