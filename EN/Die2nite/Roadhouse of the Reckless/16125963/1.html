I like to introduce a slight bit of maths, something like rolling a d4 and then multiplying it with a d20, or something like that.
An idea of Canyon I fondly remember went like this:
You can either roll 3 d20 or one d100 and one rps. Highest roll wins, but if you choose d100 and roll scissors you get 0.

There is also good old Blackjack, though that isn't much suited for large scale raffles...

And a personal favourite of mine, Boccia!
Roll a d100.
The other players then start with a d20 and can add as many times d20 as they want. Whoever comes closest wins. But: Get over the target and you lose, no matter how close.

Createx made mincemeat out of my RNG olympic contests. Most of the games were Olympic Events with a theme change.

My favorite was called running with scissors which was a foot race (d4) for the minutes (d100) for the seconds /over 60 added a minute/ and we threw in four RPS's and each scissors was worth bonus points. (The time was scaled for base points) By the end of the town there was over 15 events. I had lots of help with ideas and ubdking did an amazing job once 10 zombies bested my 9 home defense.

We tried not to get overly complex but the goal was to get some forum participation and it seems to have been a success. When I died on day 10 the thread had about 250 posts.

For non complex ones - my favorite is actually get close to my d100 with out going over.

UndeadBurgerKng

The key I find is to combine using some of the more obscure RNG elements along with some humor. One challenge I presented was to perform a seance with those players in town who had died in order to pass a message to them in the afterlife. So I made use of the letter generator and let people decide how many letters their attempted word would be, and if they were successful, got points based on how long the word was.

UMR

Even a simple d100 raffle can be made more interesting if you provide a story around it and allow for some roleplay in rolling the results.
