This is just a heads up, to get your camping gears prepared, and give comments on this.

So, what is this idea you say?
This is a short (8-10 days tops) competition between teams of 5.
It will happen sometime in Season 5.

The goal is to keep as many people alive in your team as you can, only by camping.

Anyone can sign up for this, but for obvious reasons, heroes, especially Survivalists and Scouts will have an advantage here. Also you will need a hero in your coalition, to jump.

The number of towns participating will be decided based on the number of signed up coalitions.


Rules:
any team that's town isn't devastated after the attack on Day 1, will be disqualified from the competition

attacking others is allowed, even encouraged

ninjaing other teams' campsites is allowed

NO touching the gates EVER (after it is initially opened)

proper use of map applications is discouraged, but feel free to help your opponents with zombie numbers, supply locations and your own location (after day 1's devastation you won't see others in the World Beyond)

Scoring:
teams get the sum of the soul points they earned in the town, PLUS:
. . . 3 points for every Distant Discovery they earned in that town
. . . 5 points for every Expert Expedtion they earned in that town

the competition goes on until the last camper is dead

Bansh33
It's commonly misunderstood that Survivalists have some sort of bonus at camping. It's possible this is just shorthand for referring to the class perk, but I've seen it too many places to think that everyone uses it the same way. In actuality, Survivalists don't have a bonus at camping. They are no better or worse at camping than the other classes. Their perk only allows them to reach better than the 90% limit on camping survival chances of the other classes. This isn't quite the same thing as a bonus, but in some ways its more powerful. However, it does require some kind of cooperative assistance from other players to really maximize its usefulness in most cases (bringing DOs/camping items to your campsite, using AP to improve in addition to the Survivalists', etc.).

The Scout really has more of a 'bonus' to camping, but again, it's restricted by only applying to camping with zombies in the zone. No zombies present (or in small enough numbers to be ignored by anyone) = no better than any other hero.

I hope this helps clear up any confusion someone might be having. If it's completely unnecessary then no harm done!
