Unless they've also built the lock, or have the Padlock And Chains item in their chest (or any other item/effect that makes stealing impossible), but most of this was covered in the two posts following the quoted post.

In my opinion, the game advertises and promotes selfish play, which curtains certainly fall under, but unfortunately, the community consensus implies selfish play is bad. True it's 4 AP that doesn't help the town, but I've wasted more AP in less conspicuous ways before, as well as dragged in decoration items, and many other things that fall under the "selfish" banner while in random towns.

Random towns are already at a disadvantage, AP-wise, since between inexperienced players, distinction hunters, griefers, other selfish players and a long list of other factors, IMO, you see someone with a curtain, send them some messages (In-game and/or via Twinoid Messenger) and ask them that's up? If they respond, but refuse to tell you why, let them know they'll be watched (not that paranoia isn't a part of the game and some people are watching everyone else like a hawk already). If they don't respond at all, send a follow-up telling them, "Okay, but don't be surprised if people assume the worst."

And if they respond and tell you exactly why they built the curtain (like I'd love to build one, and a lock, to keep a wide array of Chuck Norris action figures hidden and safe), well, a curtain is a curtain and you should still expect to be watched like a hawk. And be prepared for people to automatically assume the worst, because the community consensus appears to be that selfish play is bad, even though it's an advertised part of the game (though even those advertisements like to make it sound negative ).
