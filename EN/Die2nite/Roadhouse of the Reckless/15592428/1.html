Oh, that reminds me, did a quick test the other day and zombies all provide a penalty to scouts, but it's a reduced penalty.

With two zombies in the zone and cloak on, I had "limited" chances to survive, I killed one zombie and my chances improved to "largely satisfactory" with one zombie in the zone.

Based on data I've been collecting over the past two seasons, the penalty per zombie as a cloaked scout is 3-4%; I'm leaning towards 4% myself, will need to do further testing to verify.

In this effect, it takes about two zombies to equal the penalty for one zombie for a non-scout or non-cloaked scout (7% penalty per zombie by my research), which gives the illusion of "ignoring" some zombies when there's many in the zone.

Based on data I've been collecting there is no discreet penalty for that message, I think it's supposed to be a hint for newer players that their chances are being reduced by the presence of zombies, and since the design of the game is to try and kill you it makes sense they don't hint at it when there's only a few zombies in the zone; it probably triggers when the total penalty for all zombies exceeds a certain threshold, and since the penalty per zombie is less for a cloaked scout it takes more zombies to reach that threshold ergo more zombies are needed to trigger the message.

I'll certainly need to keep an eye out for it and do a conclusive test or two to make sure, but I'm pretty sure 11 zombies for a non-scout should have carried that message, and in the data set I used to discover the penalty, a 6% penalty per zombie was too little and an 8% penalty was too much, but a 7% penalty was just right (I cleared that zone down to 3 zombies with 8 normal upgrades for decent chances in the end, tomb for good).

Cross-checking over other camping locations and data sets, 7% per zombie has been used to successfully predict the final message for survival chances and, where I'm able to kill one zombie at a time, to successfully predict when the message for survival chances would change every time.
