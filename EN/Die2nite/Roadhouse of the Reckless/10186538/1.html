On march 8, 2012
Hacksaw
It isn't really a band-aid solution as much as it is how things work.

When you update a zone, what you're doing is your sending the app the XML feed. The XML feed contains information, such as item ID codes, which tells the app to add those items to the zone you are occupying.

For example. You drop a Crate Lid on the ground, the map app reads the XML feed and says, "Hey, there's a crate lid here, let me add that." You then pick the crate lid back up and the map app reads the XML feed and says, "Hey, the crate lid is gone, let me remove that now."

What happens in a hardcore town is the zone data goes from being public to being private, so when the map app reads the XML feed, it's never getting any item tags, so it keeps thinking the zone is empty and wiping everything out.

Disabling the auto-updater is the only way to stop the map app from constantly reading an empty XML feed.
