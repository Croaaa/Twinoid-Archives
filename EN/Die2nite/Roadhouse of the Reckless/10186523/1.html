Now I am going to repost a point I tried to make.
Don't jump the gun and start wrongly accusing me of encouraging selfish play...


FACT: A significant amount of players give up the game because they get frustrated and disappointed to see selfish players, betrayals and because many players don't put the town first.


My idea was that in order to help prevent this phenomenon, it's easier and more enjoyable to understand early on that it's the way things are.
It's what Die2Nite is and it's how it goes. It's not Carebear2Nite and it's not a sandpit building game where all we need to do is fight the zombies.

Now I am not intending on saying this in a welcoming message in a way that would say "it's ok to play selfish go ahead do what you want no-one cares".

I am not intending on making things more complicated than they need to be.

But at the same time, should the welcoming messages be aimed at being moulds to produce nice little robots that will fit in the way "we" want to play the game? Maybe. I'd like to word them in a way that is not too pushy and that is not a ridiculously restrictive view of the game and how it's played.


Selfish play when you're doing your best for your town is infuriating. Sure is. I don't like seeing it in my towns when I want the town to last for as long as possible.
But if you've got reasonable expectations and a "wholesome" appreciation of what Die2Nite is then you're not going to ragequit because the game is full of meanies.

Just like EnglishNinja said, I think it's one of the things that makes this game so enjoyable! I'm just willing to spread the enthusiasm.


Now I am going to paste CursedHermit's suggestion here (I am taking the liberty of rewording it slightly):

--------
"Why not shortly evoke the background of die2nite and remind the new players of its danger ?

Zombies of course, but the most dangerous enemies are within the town.
No need to mention any vocabulary about "non-oriented cooperation citizens" but point out that the neighbourhood can be tricky and it's up to one to trust or not.

Human interaction is a big part of this game - avoiding them to consider die2nite as a mere point-click browser game.

So to sum up : Prompt them to add few RP elements for deeper immersion and a small warning regarding this world they are all creating for the better or the worst"
--------


It is not directed "against" anyone and it isn't putting anyone or any way of playing the game down, quite the contrary. It's a more encompassing approach, welcoming new players and willing to show the game for what it really is while saying that it's a great game and it's fun to play it as it is.
And yes playing it as it is may well mean doing the right thing, playing for the town and encouraging others to not be selfish.
