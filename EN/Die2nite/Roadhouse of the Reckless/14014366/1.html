On july 6, 2012
Leapfrog

The Guardian town began with boundless enthusiasm, though we were soon somewhat demoralised by a rash of early deaths. Most were unavoidable - internet or power outages or 10% camping deaths.

Our spirits were rekindled when NyxOwl took the bull by the horns and came up with the stellar plan that our town had previously been missing. Everybody was friendly and knew their role, Poopsicles was having great success with his long-range "Guardianing" thing (and saw fit to bequeath his hard won Devastator assembly rights, which I re-won with a stirring poem) and everything seemed great.

Unfortunately, however, we were not able to avoid those 90% camping deaths. and I among others fell. I stayed up with the town through Twinoid messages and Sirnh1's invaluable videos, but from then on all my information was second-hand.

'Twas a sad day for me, because I had enjoyed the Guardian town immensely. There was boundless competence, and enthusiasm rather than ego, friendship rather than infighting.

Unfortunately, that cameraderie was tested when tempers flared over the revelation that a certain someone with plenty of Survivalist experience and no Guardian experience was wasting vast quantities of water to go out and kill zombies. Not only that, but they had refused to stop when implored by several people via PM. When the topic was raised publicly (and the perpetrator shunned), it was met with egotistical claims by the perpetrator that he knew the game better than everyone complaining. Then there were excuses (water weapons are more reliable, life troubles, etc) then finally a grudging apology, and so for the sake of the town the matter was dropped.

I can't attest as to the mood of the town in those final, grim days of camping. Certainly as long as I had been there, and for a time after, it was characterised by good will and good play. As to those final days of despair, when the zombies overran and last place was cemented, I count myself both fortunate and unfortunate that I am unable to attest. I like to think the Guardian spirit held strong throughout, that the last of us died with our shields and our chins held high.
