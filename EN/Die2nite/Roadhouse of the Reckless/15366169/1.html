What follows is nothing but my opinion of the moment based on my observations.

Early deaths are still very frustrating and will deprive your town of points, resource and manpower but they don't mean everything the way they used to.

Mind you, you're always better off not having a single early death and I certainly hate when they happen if I want my town to do well.

Collecting souls and turning them into points for the town is totally the way to go. I know, I know, it's obvious. But it's taken a while for quite a few groups to realise just how crucial it is, hasn't it?

To collect as many souls as possible,

you probably should have more than one shaman. A shaman can carry as many souls as they want, thus making the logistic heaps easier, especially when the souls are all over the map. That's why 3 shamans is certainly not excessive.

You must explore the whole map. Souls only appear on the shaman's map if the zone has been previously explored.

It's more important than ever to kill lots and lots of zombies all over the map and as early as possible and prevent a situation where their number gets out of control. It's hard to collect a soul when you need boosters to get there and tons of weapons as well.

A lot of the town planning should be about nothing but collecting souls and how it's going to happen. Having drop zones for weapons and boosters might even be an idea?

It's amazing how many town points you can get with souls. It means everything these days.


And remember that every death means a new soul out there to collect and use, no matter where or how you die. So far from what spacekadt and kiwiluke told me, only cyanide deaths don't produce souls.
