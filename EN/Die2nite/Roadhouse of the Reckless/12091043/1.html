'In my own way' meaning poorly thought through ideas. Anyway here it goes.

Lunch Out!

Teams of four compete for the ultimate prize. Nothing.
To win this fabulous prize they must obtain (and retain) as many Armageddon lunch-boxes and doggy bags as possible over the course of a blood-filled week.

The set-up will be simple. Teams of four will be clearly allocated before the start.
They will join a hardcore town and try to hold on to as many Armageddon Lunch boxes and doggy bags as possible.

At day 7 the boxes will be tallied.
Whichever team has the most (and it must be proven that they have them!) wins.

One more thing in case it was getting too comfortable.
In addition to the initial team, one player (from another team) must be chosen to win alongside them.
This is done after the winning team has been decided.
This encourages people to work together, but not to lose hope if the others die/are stupid.

The rules will be as simple as possible to allow as much leeway as possible for sneaky tactics.

One more thing... no gallows. Seeing the curtains town burn was an experience I would rather not see happen again.

And that is it!

Any criticism, advise or expressions of interest will be appreciated.
If, by some miracle, 40 people are deluded enough to think this sounds fun, a date can and will be arranged!
Some explanations for the method to my madness;

Why armageddon lunch-boxes and doggy bags?
Everyone starts with one. This allows you to start stealin' and plottin' immediately.
Furthermore scavenging is encouraged as doggy bags can be found.

Why teams of four?
Balance between too large teams (too many victors!) and too small teams (not enough options)

What will we do about the gates?
There's four of you. Work something out.

Other people have boxes and I want them! How do I get them.
Murder. Theft. Summon Cthulu. There's four of you. Work something out.

Luby's A-Z of planning Community Events;

A: Awareness - You need to give time for your event to build up awareness in the community. The morte people that are aware of the event, the more interest you can build up for it.

A: Availabilty - Not all players that are interested are going to be able to join in at the drop of a hat. Players need time to plan their jumps, as often they will already have coalition/meta/community jumps planned. So 4-6 weeks notice of an event allows for good planning.
