Wrath of the Titans

Before I continue, I'd like to make it clear that this is going to be an event for the true d2n titans: experienced players who have already proven their reliability and ability to become a cog in a 40-man machine. It is going to be "unfair" to new players, probably going to be considered "elitist" by many, and no doubt a "sense of entitlement" will be involved. Sorry about that. The reasons behind the creation of this event are irrelevant. Constructive feedback and suggestions are welcome from everyone.

OK, so... I've had some emails and PMs from people interested in a more serious version of COTT. The idea is the same, 7 towns, one for each hero class. Winning town is the one that accumulates the most points. The only real difference is that the sign ups will be much more restricted.

What I'd like to do is discuss the best way of weeding out the mere mortals, and gauge interest to see if this is something worth pursuing.

BerceaMondialu has an idea for COTT3, which is an SP limit and an invitation-only system. His idea also involves people only playing the class they have the most experience of. I'd like to break that down for use in this event:

Invitation-only: not sure how this can be handled effectively and fairly. In an ideal world, I'd like to get everyone signed up who wants to play. Each person would indicate which town(s) they'd like to play in, and if they want to be a captain. We'd hold votes to determine the captains, and then each captain would take turns selecting players and backups from their town's signup list. Remember, you can sign up to more than one if you want, so you'd end up playing in the town of whichever captain picked you first. If you only picked one town, and the captain doesn't select you, tough titties. I can code a system for this to make things simple.

SP limit: although my personal experience with newer players has generally not been a good one, there have been exceptions along the way so I think any limit should be low. 200SP perhaps? Bear in mind captains will ultimately be choosing their team.

Most experienced class only: I don't think this will work, especially for the newer classes, but again that's up to the captains.

So, what it boils down to is that everyone will decide who is going to be best at deciding who is best at being the best. Or something.

Thoughts? Suggestions? Interested?

The history of the Clash of the Titans in a brief summary.

Chatter began on the world forums about the power levels of each class and how they were unbalanced; some of them overpowered (survivalists), others underpowered (guardians). Workshop then comes up with the "brilliant" idea to take 40 people, put them in a town, make them all one class, and see how they do. Do this with 6 towns, one for each class, compare them side by side and see how well each one does when you only have the powers of those like you. This is the Clash of the Titans.

Initially, there was no MT support because the event didn't exist before then. Most of those who were involved in the first clash, most of those who were on the initial sign-up sheet, were those who were active on the forums at the time. So it stands to reason there were more top-tier players in the first Clash.

Second Clash as hosted by MT was advertised to all via Facebook and Twinoid bulletin. With knowledge that the first Clash gave distinctions, and a bulletin letting every player know about the event, it isn't surprising we got nearly every player under the sun wanting to get in, and with no organization from MT's side, and little time granted to us, it's little wonder this event has become more an exercise in extreme patience than a clash of the titans.

To reproduce the conditions that made the first Clash an outstanding event borders on the impossible. It's unlikely MT will sponsor this event (if they didn't have tome for CoTT, what makes you think they'll have time for WotT?), but on the upside, I wouldn't expect any special distinctions for a "copy-cat" event even if they did help out, which itself may be enough to dissuade some of the distinction "enthusiasts" or lesser skilled players from joining. Of course, without MT support, we'd either have to deal with trying to jump 40 people into a public town or need 7 people capable of making private towns, and I've only heard of 2 people with that skill unlocked.

MT support (or lack thereof) aside, the fact that the Clash is known and more eyes are likely to catch this advertisement means there's the potential for more entries from both skilled and unskilled players alike, making the selection process that much more of a nightmare, which, in my own opinion, seems to be what happened with CotT2.

That said, I am interested in an better organized competition, hopefully not too soon after this Clash to give everyone some time to cool off.

As far as a weeding-out process goes, I'm willing to overlook a shun/hang or two, since anyone can become victim to a griefer, but when you get into the 5+ range, chances are you have a troublemaker and other means of reputation would need to be used.

I can't count SP as the end all be all of skill since not everyone can be in a season 1, 40+ day town. I will say if you don't have around 500+ it's likely you haven't been in any or many organized meta towns, and even if most of the strategies are becoming cookie-cutter, you would do well to learn them.

I also wouldn't consider having a Ranked Towns a requirement per se, but since the leaderboard is pretty much dominated by meta towns, it's safe to say every Ranked Town you have is probably a meta town, so it can help gauge experience, notably for those who's SP falls short of 500.
