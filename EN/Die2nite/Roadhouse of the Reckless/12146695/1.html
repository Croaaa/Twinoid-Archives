Living Citizen Kennel

Why would you ostracize or hang good manpower? Sure, you could enslave your shunned citizens for labour but does that really help with the zombie problem? No, says a prominent (but controversial) forward thinker, we should find another use for them!

The kennels requires quite a bit of wrought iron and several guard dogs, but once it's built we can just throw our criminals (or just our least popular) in there and treat them exactly like the animals they are.

The idea is that when the attack happens, each shunned citizen will now exhibit such behaviour that they add +20 defense to the town. Whether they do it by scaring the zombies away with their lunatic barking and territory marking, or just rip their throats out... it's better left to the imagination. Suffice it to say, it works, somehow. But these new "guard dogs" are unfortunately useless for anything else, and their constant baying (as well as the fear of meeting the same fate) has a small chance of terrorizing any citizen who lives through the attack.

Mrs. Forward-Thinker says this is a small price to pay for security. I think I'm beginning to hate her...

Mechanical Gates Garage

You know those roller doors on garages? Those are pretty good for keeping zombies out. We decided some of the abandoned garages around town would make good, strong distractions against the zombies, if only we could get some of them interested in splitting off from the horde.

Unfortunately if the zombies do get in the things are death traps, so people are understandably reluctant to volunteer to be the bait.

So we decided to rig them to open and close mechanically. For each garage we have a citizen remotely opening and closing the doors with gleeful abandon, joyful of the fact that they can finally do it without looking stupid. It makes the zombies think there's someone in there, so they split off to investigate, and if they happen to get inside while it's open, they can just be shut in. Be careful opening it in the morning, though...


Adds 60 defense.
Requires electronic component, wrought iron, nuts and bolts.
