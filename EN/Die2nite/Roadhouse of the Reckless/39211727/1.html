In a few small towns I have joined recently, as a measure to combat nonexistent/nonoptimal scavenging or inability to move on map due to it being overrun by zombies, I have organised expeditions where I've asked for a few volounteers with whom I would hold hands and scavenge, maybe visit some buildings or collect some important item.
I must say it has worked wonderfully, as it turns out whenever I've asked for it there were a few new and eager people who would be up for it... at least the first night. The following night, where the similar circumstances called for another expedition, interest would soon drop and sometimes even perish.

So, therein comes my struggle: how to make my expeditions more fun? Technically they've been very satisfactory, no problems ever occured and everyone came home with full rucksack and with no trauma experienced. I even select the best items for others to carry, so they get satisfaction of finding good things. I try to be communicative and friendly through the expeditions.
Do other people have problem with diminishing turnout for expeditions? Do new people expect to kill dragons and save princesses on the expeditions, and find themselves disappointed to return only with a full rucksack? Is the good turnout for the first expedition only a result of its novelty? Or is there a secret recipe for making expeditions a lasting fun?

From my personal experience it depends a lot Grothe.
I mean, there are always a couple of helpful/interested players in small maps that I play (like I was one of those back then, remember?). I really like to scout/train new talents and drag ppl into meta playing. But it's quite rare to find a significant numbers of new players willing to get ready and active enough to perform them every day.

Some players got really involved in the very first days then it gets harder and harder to plan big things, even making cheerful forum thread of even PM msgs. Most of them simply doesn't develop enough interest to keep it for many days, but you'll eventually find some from time to time.

That being told I don't think you should blame yourself for the lack of interest/activity to your expos, that's just how it is. Lots of them play, a very few stick around to develop real taste to the thing.
