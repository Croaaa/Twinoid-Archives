It would be great if there were a way to join a town by yourself without subjection to deadbeats. It would be really awesome to join a town with 39 active noobs. Try to disagree with that, I dare you.

I think the 100SP barrier accomplished this for a couple weeks when the first players reached it. Unfortunately, it is now an irrelevant benchmark. I think that's why raising that limit is a go-to suggestion for recreating the deadbeat-free atmosphere while preserving random factor that keeps the game dynamic and interesting.

There are two sides to achieving this goal. On the one hand, MT needs to discourage players from deadbeating and encouraging them to be active. For this they've implemented a 100SP limit which is now ineffectual... but they've also integrated with Twinoid which I think has hurt a lot.

On the other hand, MT needs to discourage over-reliance on meta-play. I think they had this goal in mind when they enforced the horrible coalition limit in Season 3. They correctly identified the problem, but tried to work the solution from the wrong end.

So far the community has stepped up with things like Clash of the Titans, Mega Meta Jumps, Battle of the Sexies, and other events.

Despite these great and commendable efforts, I agree with the OP. The community-created solution is insufficient.

While it has become accepted and understood by most players that joining meta towns is a natural course of events for a player's "career," I believe more is possible. I believe it's the responsibility of the developers to facilitate the gameplay atmosphere I desire: joining a town with random players who actually want to play this forum and text-based game, regardless of whether they're selfish or selfless players -- just as we could in Season 1.

We've all learned to adapt to the injustices and shortcomings of Motion Twin's mechanics. I reject this complacency and impel all of you to do the same. The game can be so much better.
