As explained in section 3 of the in-game help, it is possible to build "Dolmens" or "Portals" from Black Kubes. They help you to map out the surrounding area so that it becomes permanently visible on your Map. Each portal uncovers a 5x5 area, like this:






In order to make the best use of these portals, I suggest the following:

Build your portals only at coordinates that are multiples of 5.

In other words, portals can be built in the following zones: [0,0], [0,5], [5,0], [0,-5], [-5, 0], ... [125, 35], [500, 500]... but should not be built in other zones that aren't multiples of 5, such as [1,2], [3,4] and so on. Because building them elsewhere would create some overlap between the portals and in the end you would not cover the map as efficiently.

Once they are built, the portals cannot be moved and cannot be destroyed. They will stay there forever. The world of Kube will evolve around them: islands and bridges will appear and disappear, zones will be rented and then abandoned, things will be destroyed and rebuilt, but the portals will not move. Therefore, building them in the right places is very important because the Black Kubes are rare and you do not get a second chance once you have transformed them into a portal.

Building portals only at multiples of 5 brings several advantages:
This is the easiest way to ensure that there will be no overlaps: each portal covers a 5x5 area.
It is very easy to verify that the coordinates of a zone are multiples of 5.
If you are exploring the world, it is easy to focus on coordinates that are multiples of 5 and check if somebody else has already built a portal there, without having to waste time on the other zones. If you find a portal, then you can touch it and extend your map easily. If not, then you know that you can build one in that zone.

Of course this is only a suggestion. You can build your portal elsewhere if you want to. But think about it... If you want to map out the world efficiently, using coordinates that are multiples of 5 is the best way to go. Thanks!
