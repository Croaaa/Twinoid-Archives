Hm...sorry, but I disagree with some of those points.

As far as the lab points are concerned, where you should put them depends on your strategy and how you plan on playing this game. Obviously, I would put some of those upgrades as low priority, but I wouldn't necessarily avoid them.

Wait until you've established all of your key facilities ( Lusto Pump, Laundromat, Chowrightnow, Shambrewery ) before you start placing money facilities such as ( Astro-Gastro, Disco, Cremate oven etc )
You could do that, but my belief is that if you're going to built money facilities, you should build them as early as possible, provided you have already built the "key facilities" you can build at the time. Otherwise, you might as well not built them at all if you wait until you cannot profit off of them.

Try avoiding making rooms that can only be effected by 1 room ( having dead ends ) those are never good for placing clients with 2 likes.
While you should never place guests with two likes in a dead end room, that doesn't mean you should avoid making dead end rooms. However, you should definitely avoid building narrow rows or columns, unless you just don't care.

If you just want to have fun, have fun. If you want a great hotel as soon as possible, treat this like an accounting game and consider the costs and benefits of each move you make.

In case you only have a 2-star-hotel (or less) and you want to bequeath as soon as possible, don't invest in money facilities (Astro-Gastro, etc.) at all. Having extra guest rooms instead will provide more income, even if you have to buy ad campaigns in order to keep your guest rooms occupied.

In case you don't want to rush but instead want to have a bigger hotel than needed for bequeathing, go ahead and build all the fancy rooms you like.
