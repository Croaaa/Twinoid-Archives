You get 3 guests per day if you don't use the employees on the welcome desk. With 1 employee you get 4, 2 employees you get 5, 3 employees you get 6, and 4 employees you get 9. At higher levels, some of those guests stay more than 1 night

If you only get guests at satisfaction 7, you will only get 27 at midnight and 27 when leaving. If the guest has satisfaction 10, that goes up to 50. If you improve your rooms, that is even higher. At 54 income per guest, the advertising is not economic, at 100 per guest it is and gets better the more you can get out of each guest

The challenge to managing your Hotel is to maximise the income from each guest. If you don't want to spend real money, I don't think you need to

seeing as how 4 clients costs about 300 you know you dont get your worth because 1 client will pay up a max of 50 to 60 if you are lucky which wont add up to 300. You might have a poor set of advertising campaigns. They vary, both from player to player and from bequeath to bequeath.

But even at 4 clients for 300, ad campaigns are worth it. You just need to know how to maximize your returns. Mix the campaign clients in amongst your regular daily clients and you can boost the return from both sets of clients. Swap a guest with a smart friend and you'll have several clients at 10 satisfaction.

Tip: Don't throw clients into rooms early in the day. If you can, wait until it is closer to midnight and you'll have a better set of likes and generated effects to help you maximize satisfaction. Sometimes I'll have a guest or two who I don't even try to maintain their satisfaction. Pyros are good for this since they will destroy the room anyway, and suspicious packages are also good for this since they don't pay. These guests are used for their generated effect to boost their neighbors' satisfaction, since a Blob with a 10 satisfaction is far better than a Blob with an 8 satisfaction and a Suspicious Package with an 8 satisfaction.
