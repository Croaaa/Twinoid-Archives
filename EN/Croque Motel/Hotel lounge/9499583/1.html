QuisUtDeus
It's dependent on what you did earlier in the day. A couple times I haven't been able to put an employee at the front desk in the morning and it moves all the clients arrival times back. As far as I can tell every client that arrives while the employee is missing moves the time back by that many hours.

ex.
For 2 employees at the front desk, a client arrives at 6 no matter what. If you leave both employees at the desk the next clients arrive at 10, 14, 18, and 22 meaning you get 5 clients.

For 2 employees and neither at the front desk, a client arrives at 6 no matter what. There are no employees at the front desk so the next client arrives at 14. If you put both employees at the desk after that, the next clients arrive at 18 and 22 meaning you get 4 clients.

For 2 employees and only one at the front desk, a client arrives at 6 no matter what. If there is 1 employee at the front desk the next client arrives at 11. If you put the other employee at the desk after that, the next clients arrive at 15, 19, and 23 meaning you get 5 clients.

From the info you provided I can only guess that you left one employee at the front desk for 3 clients meaning guests arrive at 6, 11, 16, and 20.

Owyndaw
The guests have an expected arrival time, set by the wait time for number of employees on the desk at the time of arrival, and calculated from the arrival time of the last guest. If an employee returns to the desk, the arrival time is set to match the the number of employees at that time. If the time has passed, the guest arrives straight away, but can be considered late and the time the last guest arrived is now set to that expected time, not the actual time. The first guest arrives at 06:00. With 0 employees on the desk, the wait time is 8 hours, with 1 it is 5 hours and 2 is 4 hours (and so on)

If your guests are all away from the desk at 06:00, and you move them to the desk at 06:10, the next arrival time goes from 14:00 to 11:00 to 10:00; you are still waiting

If you move them at 10:30, the time still goes from 14:00 to 11:00 to 10:00, but now the expected time has passed so the guest arrives straight away. The last arrival time is 10:00 (look at the log)

If you move them at 11:30, the time goes from 14:00 to 11:00; now the expected time has passed, the guest arrives straight away, and moving the second employee is too late. The last arrival time is 11:00 (look at the log). You have 'lost' 1 hour in the arrival time sequence
