Thank you, Ziorac! I was starting to think I was the only one not rushing to bequeath.
Bequeathing your hotel allows you to:

■ Earn a new star next to your hotel's name,
■ Earn + 150 ,
■ Increase the maximum number of research points which can be used in the Lab.
Star next to hotel's name - So what? Prestige is a better indicator if how well your hotel's doing.
150 prestige stars - You get almost as many by paying the consortium tax.
Increase the maximum number of research points - This is the only reason I might consider bequeathing but on it's own it's not much of a selling point. And do I really need to increase my maximum research points anyway? Some of the items in the Lab aren't, in my opinion, worth investing points in. Crystal Ball and Mastery: Pyromaniacs spring to mind.

Bequeathing seems like a step backwards - several steps, in fact. You start over with a single employee and - correct me if I'm wrong - just the three basic monsters (Capitalist Pigs, Pyros and Witches) and you lose all your decorations.

I see bequeathing as something to do when you've built all the special rooms you can (or want to) and the game is starting to become repetitive and boring, rather than an objective in itself.
