Bequeathing to a 4 star hotel, your new goal to bequeath to a 5 star hotel becomes:
Bequeath your hotel

You can choose to bequeath your hotel to an employee so that they can run it and take over its development. You will start again with a new establishment (at level 2), but you will retain the following possessions:

Your money,
1 employee,
All un-used objects (except decorative objects, whether installed or not).

Bequeathing your hotel allows you to:

Earn a new star next to your hotel's name,
Earn + 150 Pr ,
Increase the maximum number of research points which can be used in the Lab.

To bequeath your hotel, you must:

Have reached level 8 or higher,

Kompera
So is it worth it to shell out a few hundred for a couple points of fame?There's something being missed here though. It's not just a few hundred for the 5 .

Let's say that the quest is to (amongst other things) earn 2 . To earn this, you've got to toss 10 guests into the Institute. Sure, this could be less if you can use Abominations, but once at 6th or 7th level they are diluted by being a part of a much larger pool of random guest types. Let's say 8 guests, on average.

That's 8 chances to reduce the satisfaction of every other guest in your hotel, perhaps as much as 4 or 5 satisfaction lost on a few unlucky guests. And some of those guests would have left with a 10 satisfaction, and would have given you 1 . You may break even, or you may be at a loss in earned. Plus there is the additional cost for every guest whose satisfaction was reduced.

No, I think that completing a quest which includes research can only be justified in terms of allowing you to advance to another quest which does not include earning research as a part of its conditions. An investment of sorts. But I don't think it's really worthwhile, in the long run.
