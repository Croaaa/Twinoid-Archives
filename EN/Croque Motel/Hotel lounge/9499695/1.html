With 6 dogsbodies you get another guest every 45 minutes, that is 23 guests per day. With a little luck with your ad campaigns you get another 15 guests per day in your office. During my pretty long lvl5 period I counted 218 guests who stayed for a total of 331 days, thats 1.5 nights per guest.

That makes 57 rooms occupied per day. Let's say you need 10% extra vacancies for guests who are picky about neighbours (although I noticed placing guests gets easier with bigger hotels) you need 63 guest rooms and that's it.

If it comes to C€-making rooms, the math is: 2 out of three guests have at least 1 "like", the other one has two "dislikes" and is not interestet in visiting wet rooms etc. That makes 42 C€-room requests per day, each visit plus a short idle time add up to about 3 hours, that again makes 8 visits per C€-room per day. That would mean 6 rooms would be enough for all the guests, but it's wrong.. as soon as I have placed 20 guests in my today's hotel, all my 5 special rooms are occupied all the time. For our 63 guest, let's say we need 20 special rooms, 4 of each kind.

That makes 87 rooms out of 150 rooms (!!!) that you need for your 7th star. I'd say that's a LOT of laundromatrixes in our 6-star hotels! Better throw in a few useless lvl1-rooms, they are cheaper...

Yeah I know I'm a bit nerdy... I just like playing with numbers... a lot... sorry that you had to read all this!
