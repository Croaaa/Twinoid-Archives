Multiple room service requests


It is possible to get two room service requests from the same guest in the same day. This is highly dependent on the time of day you place the guest in a room. I place most of my guests around 14:00, with the late guests placed later. Out of 254 guests, there were 22 double room service requests in the same day, and 15 of them (68 %) were multi-day guests on their second or third day. These guests are the majority because they are 'placed' earliest of all guests, and can generate their first room service request as early as 4 AM (as far as i can tell, this is the cutoff for departing guests to make a room service request before checkout)


The type of request, and the number of requests DOES NOT depend on what type of room service requests you can complete based on your level. For instance, It is possible to get two laundry requests from the same client in the same day, even if you can handle other request types. The type of room service requested seems to be completely random, and subject to 'clumping' of the same type of request, just like random guest types can clump.


I will test the rate of double requests at level 7, when i have all the room service rooms. For now, it appears that you are actually worse off having the different types unlocked, versus just having the first one unlocked, and having lots of the same room service room built. (less variety means all your room service rooms can run at the same time, all the time there is demand, without a 'clump' of one type of request overloading that particular room service room(s)).


Of course, you would normally want to be level 4 for the chance to get Hypno-cats (Laundry + Shoes), but if the rate of room service requests does not change, it might be worth it to stay at level 4 as you build up.

Item usage strategy, from statistics :


Avoid using two permanent items at once. You double the chances of losing an item, because you will always lose them both if one is destroyed, also, its harder to use a room with two effect items, because the odds of a second guest liking 2 specific effects is very low.


Move multi-day guests one day before checkout. Besides the random events/explosions, items are only destroyed at checkout. This includes the Pyro effect, and 'one use' items like Diamond Bed and Isolation Chamber. This is easily exploited with 3 day guests, and I have several rooms with Matelalala/Diamond Bed, which my 3 day guests spend the first 2 nights in. The Matelalala bonus means that they are happier every day, even on the last day after being moved.


Tech : Toughened Equipment


Estimated effect :
None : 50% item destruction
Level 1 : 40% item destruction
Level 2 : 30% item destruction


Even with the tech at full, you won't usually get more than 2-3 uses out of a normal item. The normal rules of the game do not apply when a guest checks out with 0 or less satisfaction. I tested this by reverse-placing all my guests for a few days, so they were super pissed off at checkout (away from their likes, next to their dislikes), and I have determined that guests checking out at 0 satisfaction always do 5 damage to the room (the max), and they never forget items in the room. This effect overrides all techs related to these things as well.


Pyromaniac mastery does not activate for this


Armor plating never reduces this type of damage. This makes the description of this tech misleading, since it clearly does not 'reduce the chance of a bedroom being destroyed by a client', since the effect only ever activates to prevent the 1 damage that a satisfied client sometimes does at checkout, when the term 'destroyed' clearly implies it would work when the room is actually being completely destroyed by the 0 satisfaction client at checkout.


Capitalist special ability does not activate


Racket tech does not activate


No normal chance of an item being lost


These last three also do not make real-world logical sense. By never forgetting anything when checking out with 0 satisfaction, the game implies that the items forgotten by guests are, in fact, presents to the hotel for a job well done, instead of accidental events. Logically, if they stayed at least one night, and spent all the time to trash the room before not paying, there would still be a chance of losing something. Probably even a greater than normal chance, if they made a huge mess in the room, which could cover something up.


There is not a lot of strategy to this effect, since 0 satisfaction is an unusual event. It just means that even more care needs to be taken with Hystericals (and to a lesser extent Bombs, which can cascade explode, and upset a neighboring hysterical) if you will be offline for a long time, and its always a good idea to check before checkout that an event has not activated the hysterical's 'special' ability. Room service requests :


Once you have the ability to build your first room service room, you will start to get room service requests in the form of various items in front of your guests door that you can fulfill to get +1 satisfaction. This is much better than having your employees give tokens of appreciation, because the room service rooms always succeed, and take no time to get the +1, although each room service room cannot do another request for 30 minutes. The type of requests that you can fulfill has no effect on the rate of requests you can receive, the only thing that having more types of room service rooms does is makes it more likely that you do not have enough of the right type of room, because the request types are random, and you will get clumps of one type of request every so often


The requests last 6 hours, and the sooner you meet the request, the sooner the next request will be made, if there is enough time for a next request. If you do not meet a request, it disappears, and the timer restarts on the next request. The timer for the next request is different if you meet a request or miss a request.


First Request :


The first request after a guest is placed always comes randomly 1 to 12 hours after being placed, in even units of exactly one hour, so there are twelve possible times the request can come. Average time of the first request : 6.5 hours


This is the reason that you can be overwhelmed if you place a large number of guests before going offline. If 6 hours pass, you will have around half your guests making requests, and the rest will also make new requests as you work on the first half.


The last time a request can be made on the day of checkout is 4 AM. This appears to be so you have the full 6 hours to fulfill the request before 10 AM checkout. What this means for one day guests is that if they are placed in a room before 16:00, they will all make a first request. If you place one day guests in the last hour before midnight, you will have only a 1 in 3 chance of getting a first request.



Second Request :


The second and any further requests always come in exactly 12 to 24 hours after you MEET the last request of that guest, in even units of 1 hour. The average time of a second request is 18 hours.


This is why you hit a lull in requests after 4 AM, only your two and three day guests can request room service, and their timers are much longer than the one day guests.



Multiple Requests :


Its possible to receive more than one request a day, or more than one per day of a multi-day guest's stay. This is highly luck based (getting the shorter request times), and also depends on how soon after making a request you fulfill it, which is needed to restart the timer for the next request.

What is the net effect and savings of the Armour Plating tech ?


It has no effect on the Pyro room trashing effect, or pyro guests at all, or a 0 satisfaction guest trashing the room. The sole benefit is the reduction in the chance of a normal non-pyro guest doing 1 level of damage to the room.


Odds of a non-pyro guest doing one damage at checkout without Armor Plating tech : 1064 guests, 590 damaged rooms


53.1 % chance of room damage


Odds of a non-pyro guest doing one damage at checkout with Armor Plating tech at level 3 : 4911 guests, 1529 damaged rooms


31.13 % chance of room damage



The net reduction is about the same as the Toughened Equipment tech at level 2, or 50% to 30%. The value of this tech is related to the odds of getting a non-pyro guest, because Pyro's only do 100% damage, and this does not affect their damage. Even if you have Pyro mastery, they still never do only a singe damage. If they do any damage, its the full 5 levels. Assuming you have all level 3 rooms and all 3 levels of this tech


At Level 4, 87.5% chance of non-pyro guest.


Net savings per guest : Odds of damage reduced * odds of an affected guest * cost of room repair


0.2 * 0.875 * 12 = 2.1 per guest, or 0.7 per point of tech


At level 7 + 91.67 % chance of a non-pyro guest


0.2 * 0.9167 * 12 = 2.2 per guest, or 0.733 per point of tech



The relative value of Pyro mastery tech at level 4 is


Odds of damage reduced * odds of a pyro * cost of 5 levels of room repair


0.4 * 0.125 * 60 = 3 per guest, or 1.5 per point of tech.


At level 7+ : 0.4 * 0.083 * 60 = 1.992 per guest, or 0.996 per point of tech.



The relative value of three levels of DIY guys tech at level 4 is :


(Base odds of 1 damage * odds of a non-pyro * cost reduction of room repair) + (odds of a pyro * cost reduction of full room repair) + number of random damage events * 6 * level of damage)


(0.5 * 0.875 * 6) + (0.125 * 30) = 2.625 + 3.75 = 6.375 , or 2.125 per point of tech.


At level 7+


(0.5 * 0.9167 * 6) + (0.083 * 30) = 2.7501 + 2.49 = 5.2401 , or 1.7467 per point of tech.
