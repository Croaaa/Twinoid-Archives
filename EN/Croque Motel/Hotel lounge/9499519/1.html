To play the devil's advocate:
To get back to your initial question though, where is the proof there are six year olds playing Monster Motel?There needs to be no other proof offered than the fact that this game is online and available without any check for age. Your statement is the equivalent of a porn site saying "Where is the proof that a minor has accessed the pornographic images my site offers?" Good luck in court with that defense.
Most little kids don't search the web for out-of-the-way French game websites.You may find that Muxxu is "out-of-the-way", but you managed to find it. A defense based upon obscurity is no defense at all, when there is no effort being taken to ensure that minors cannot access the content in question.

The real question are not "Where's the proof a minor accessed the site?" nor "Could a minor even possibly find such an obscure site?" Neither of those questions has any merit. The site is internationally available to anyone who finds it, and no age limit is set for access by the content owners.

The question is as simply put as the OP stated it: Can exposure to the site content be construed as being harmful to minors?

And that question is far better addressed by the Muxxu legal council than by the forumites. They are the ones who need to assess the potential risk vs. the potential value of the content. Unless there are lawyers and/or international law lawyers who are willing to contribute to the discussion?
