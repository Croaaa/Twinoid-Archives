Is it worth it to upgrade rooms ? This depends on how they are used, and how long you will use them. If you put normal one-day guests in level 3 rooms, the rooms are full every night, and your average satisfaction level is 8 (met both needs and 1 token, this is pretty normal for me), you need about 18 days of +60% payouts to cover the initial 850 upgrade cost, which comes to about 2.5 weeks.


With only the mastery payout and blob guests in the level 3 rooms, your payoff would be closer to 1.5 weeks, which is not long in this game, so I would recommend 3-4 upgraded rooms for one star and above hotels, as long as your next bequeath is longer out than 2 weeks.


Every hotel should probably have one fully upgraded room, just for VIP's, even if you do not do their campaigns, you will get them every so often at random, and you are unlikely to be able to get any payout from them without such a room.


At three stars and above, it would make sense to have all the normally used rooms fully upgraded, since you will for sure need more than three weeks of guest stays to be ready to bequeath, but having enough rooms for all your normal guests should take priority over this, because a non-campaign guest left without a room is just throwing away free Croqueuros. With the average guest staying 1.5 days at level 4, a free account's 9 daily guests would need about 18 guest rooms, assuming a few extras for dislike guests and hypno-cat bonus guests. I like to use a campaign every day so i can have enough early guests to place the first batch before i leave for work, so with another 5 guests on top of the free 9 guests, i find i can usually keep 25 guest rooms almost full, so that is a good baseline before starting to upgrade the rooms. Room upgrades can also be used to avoid paying your taxes, as you can easily get rid of almost all your money below the cost of the next hotel segment on tax day by combining the 250 and 600 cost upgrades carefully.
