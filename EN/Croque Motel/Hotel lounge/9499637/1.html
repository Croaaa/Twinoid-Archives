Erem
even if they get 10, just move them from room and they should drop to 9..
If their Satisfaction goes up to 10 again (because of a random event or lack of concentration on your part), moving rooms a second time won't solve the problem and you will have to find another way of lower their Satisfaction, e.g. moving a neighbour who is giving them a beneficial effect, or more drastic measures like destroying beneficial equipment in their room or sacrificing another guest (which could lower several guests' Satisfaction if you haven't invested many Research Points in Padded Cell , and may not even affect the Bomb if you have).

Bombs aren't too bad once you get used to them - a bit like Pyros - but the hardest thing at first is remembering not to raise their Satisfaction to its maximum until just before they are about to leave. You get several hours' warning (about 5, I think?) and the information panel tells you when a Bomb is going to detonate (NB: server time, not local). There is usually plenty of time for you to put things right before it's too late.

The trickiest Bombs to deal with are those who are reliant upon neighbours for their Satisfaction and are staying a second night whilst their neighbours aren't. If school, work, or any other reason means you can't get to your hotel after 10am and before the Bomb is due to explode, you will need to ensure that her Satisfaction is high enough to still be above zero when her neighbours depart but not so high that there is the risk of a random event raising it to 10.

Screaming Hystericals can be a nightmare too, causing your Bomb's, Satisfaction to plummet - especially if you have more than one Hysterical staying and a chain reaction starts! If you find yourself in this situation, concentrate on pleasing the Hysterical(s), not the Bomb. If you don't, and raise the Bomb's Satisfaction instead, you can find yourself in more difficulties when the Hysterical(s) eventually stop wailing and your Bomb's Satisfaction is then too high.

One final point, the information the game gives you when you examine guests, about when they are going to leave, is not always correct. So far I've had one Bomb stay on in the hotel despite having been told she would be leaving that morning, and another who departed after I'd been told first that she would and then that she wouldn't. This, obviously, makes the decision whether or not to raise their Satisfaction to 10 a difficult one. All I can suggest, until the developers look into this, is to keep track yourself of how many nights they are going to stay. As far as I'm aware, the information you are given about this when they are waiting to be shown to their room is reliable.
