We call them "random spawns" but what they actually are is procedurally generated. Then the world is made, it calls upon specific code to to determine what goes where. If it were random, there would be no order to where anything id, and blocks would hang in mid air in a random scattering. But instead we have very orderly mountains, forests, and the like. In order for the ships to generate, the game has to call upon a specific script. The question is, what causes it to call upon that script? There is obviously a percentage variable (probably below 1%) but there's still some sort of spawn conditions. Nothing, at least in procedural generation, is truly random.

A good example of this is the villages in Minecraft. They spawn exclusively in large open spaces with lots of flat land. (which is why they pop up in deserts so often) Once you know this, finding them is much simpler, although the percent chance of spawn is still low.

G55 is obviously a different game, and honestly uses a much smoother procedural generation (much cleaner than Minecraft's actually) But all procedural generation uses some form of patterning at it's core which is why blocks spawn in groups of similar blocks, and form recognizable features.

Now, despite all the nay-saying, we actually have learned something. Franz24 has discovered that the ships CAN spawn on guest planets. That wasn't necessarily assured before, and means that Gorok and DD321123 may produce relevant data. Or at least grant us some idea of what the spawn percentage is. When more is found, our understanding will get better. I just wish I could sort through the French boards and find out more about those spawns.
