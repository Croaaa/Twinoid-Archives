This is a discussion that's on going in other threads, but I feel this is able to stand on its own.

Taken at face value, with no consideration for similar games, Galaxy 55 is cool. It may feel a little limited, but that's to be expected of a pre-alpha game. When you compare it with minecraft, 55 seems wholly inaquedate; much smaller world hardly anything to do. Again though, it is pre-alpha.

The developer seems to have a well laid out plan for the release of features, though it comes as no surprise to anyone that games take time. The more time spent as a "minecraft clone", the higher the risk of being written off by many people. Were I at the helm, I'd focus wholly of making differences to minecraft as soon as possible.

I am very optimistic for this game, though I am slightly worried that if taken in the wrong direction, it could end up being unable to claw back into its niche.

On a side note, the website design leaves a little to be desired. It gives off an air of something a child would set up on geocities. People don't just judge products on the product, they judge it on its competitors, and peripherals.

In short, this has great potential, but one should tread carefully. Well thought out releases and features are your friend.

Thanks for reading my inane ramblings.
