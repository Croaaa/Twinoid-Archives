It works the same, although you may want to start building dams and clearing out the water if you want to do some serious mining. This is especially true on acid worlds, where all the good stuff is in the acid water.

If you're going to try clearing it out, I highly recommend getting a desert planet and doing it with glass blocks.

ElBob

Glass, from the sand on a desert planet.

It's a lot easier to come by than nitrogen (for big jobs like clearing out a substantial amount of water), and the transparency of the glass kubes lets you see when the water is directly on the other side.

That means less accidental flooding.

Plus, it looks much, much cooler when you have a huge vertical wall of glass...

ElBob
hkmalyI though that nitrogen will freeze bigger chunk of water ...

Nitrogen isn't reusable. You'll freeze a large chunk once, yes, but then that's it. Glass kubes are reusable, so you can keep pushing back a wall of water with the same supply of blocks.

I don't think the sources for nitrogen respawn, so there's a finite amount of water you can actually freeze. No such limitations with glass.
