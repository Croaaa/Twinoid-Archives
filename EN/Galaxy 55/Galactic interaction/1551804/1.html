But now that it has been mentioned, I feel like I should elaborate for those who don't know (and I'll keep it simple).

EVE has 5000 star systems ("maps", in other MMO's). They are classified/categorized in three groups: High-sec[urity], Low-sec and no/null sec.

High sec security rating ranges from 1 to 0,5. On paper, there aren't many risks beside scamming and getting killed by NPC's. If you are attacked in here, NPC police will warp in and kill the aggressor (time needed depends on system security rating).

Low sec security rating ranges from 0,4 to 0,1. On paper, it's medium-risk, as there is no police protection. In practice, it's a popular place for pvp-roams, pirates and as staging places for invasions (but rarely and only few systems have that kind of connections to other systems). Any aggression/attack in lowsec ends up in player's security rating to go do down (and below a certain point, that player is unable to enter high sec due to police interventions).

Null sec has security rating 0 (to -1, for gameplay mechanics). On paper, it's maximum risk. No police, no protection whatsoever beside your wits and skill. In practice, if you're in one of the megaalliances, it's the safest place to be in. Otherwise... actually, i wouldn't know, as i never experienced it first-hand. It's more-or-less a lawless space, only with player-made rules.
