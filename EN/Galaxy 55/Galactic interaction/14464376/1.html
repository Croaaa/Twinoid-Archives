As Shiroten said, the Pyrite (or aluminium mineral) spawns near the HardRoq (or red rock). HardRoq is easy to find in Red Earth planets, as it can be found on the surface (without going underground).

In other planets is harder to find HardRoq on the surface. I've only found ONE HardRoq deposit in a Barren Sand Planet.

In a Snow Peak Planet, I've found some Pyrite and HardRoq beds in mountains.

Mining like you do in Minecraft is pretty pointless in G55. I tried it mostly out of habit when I first started and it got me nothing but a pile of rocks. Just look for deposits of hardroq (the red and purple stuff) and start mining. I find a solid strategy to make your crystals last is to excavate around the deposit first as much as possible, as some of the ore will likely be exposed and accessible without having to dig into the hardroq itself.

Worthy of note however: aluminum is WAY more rare since the last patch. Seems you'll mostly find iron (fer) which is far less useful.

Also, barren waste worlds have been very good to me. The surface usually has little to no deposits, but if you climb down into a ravine or crater, you'll inevitably hit a cave system chuck full of the stuff!
