And while I agree that this does feel like a facebook game in the "begging for friends" respect, there are some key differences in the atmosphere.

For one, Facebook's main function isn't a hub for games. Sure it's become a massive part of it, but it's a social network at its core. Twinoid, meanwhile, IS more or less a game hub. And it's been around for a while, but I can't say there's been much "social" benefit to it on my end, as most of the games that use it so far have strict limits on things you can send to other people (for example, you can only send so many students to people in Teacher Story, so once you find a circle of 5 people to play the game with, there's no further need to socialize until one of them stops sending you students.)
