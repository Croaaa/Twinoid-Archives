The cheapest way is probably the 2nd layout from the first picture. Either build a row or a column of rooms so you can take advantage of column/floor monsters. You can only get the bonus satisfaction from a maximum of 4 of those types, there are only 2 rooms for them in the pic so it'd be a good idea to add another 2. Additionally you'll want to deal with guests that like light as well, building a room that isn't neighbored by anything but gets the bonus from phantom/floor monsters can easily take care of them. You can also place aliens there and drop a Creepy Crescent on them to instantly max them out.

The only problem with this design is that you commit to either column or floor monsters so if you get column monsters when you have a horizontal layout or vice versa they become useless. It also takes some time/luck to get 4 of them before you can easily max out monsters. Personally I built a mix that, it takes advantage of either column or floor monsters which makes it significantly easier to set up since you don't have to rely on luck but it costs more to build.

Anyway, no matter what layout you pick the general idea is the same. You want 5 main rooms, one for each type, 4 of them surrounded with other rooms where you'll place Plants or Neighborly Nightmares and an isolated one for monsters that like light. Then you'll want some extra rooms for column/floor monsters to boost your main rooms. How you choose to do that effectively is up to you.
