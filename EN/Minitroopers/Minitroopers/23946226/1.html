It's been suggested before that inactive players be removed from the continuous championship, with a few different ways to manage it being proposed. I never really liked any of the ideas, though.

I'm going to suggest it again myself though, and see what people think.

The problem:
Powerful armies get stuck in the rankings when they are no longer actively taking part. This causes a blockade at lower levels and prevents less powerful armies from rising up and enjoying competition against a variety of enemies, instead they have to keep plugging away at the blocking army until they get lucky, or their power goes up, or they give up and stop playing too.

Why we SHOULDN'T cut inactive armies:
People have earned their place in the ranking. There was no "you will be cut if you are inactive" clause in the rules when people signed up. A player may be inactive for a period of time against their will; if they come back to find they've lost all their work then they would be rightly upset.

Problems with cutting inactive armies:
Determining who is inactive is difficult. A player may not move in the thread because they cannot beat the army above them, rather than because they are not trying. Keeping track of activity or otherwise is likely to be an administrative nightmare. Building a complicated system would irritate people (and make mistakes more likely than a simple system).

My proposal:
I suggest a one time cull, each year.
Culled armies would not be removed, but would be marked as "inactive" and able to be skipped over.

Proposed method:
On August 1st, someone will go to each army link in the championship.
If that army has not been attacked at all since May 1st then it is reasonable to say that the army immediately below is not actively taking part (i.e. 3 months with no attempts to beat the army which is being checked)
The army immediately below would be marked as "Inactive 2013" for the 2013 cull, or 2014 for next year, etc.
An inactive army may remove their inactive tag at any time.

Anticipated problems with proposed method:
An inactive army may not be detected if the checked army is being attacked by lots of other people. So not all inactive armies may be dealt with.
There is a very small chance that someone may be marked inactive if they take exactly three months to climb into position ahead of an inactive army on the exact day of the check - however they can then just remove the inactive tag.

Discussion:
Please discuss below. If the general consensus is that this would be worthwhile then I'd suggest we start this up this August, and add something in to the rules in the first post of each thread to state "inactive armies will be checked in August each year" or something along those lines, so that it's not a shock for people.
