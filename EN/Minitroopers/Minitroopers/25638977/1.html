Alternatively, there is the "soak it up" candidate. His goal is to deflect or absorb enemy damage whilst dealing as much damage as he can:

The Tank
Soldier (Health!);
Assault Rifle (I have to choose one default weapon, right?);
Knife (Preferred weapon, and doesn't require Munitions since a Knife never runs out of ammo);
Frenetic (Moar initiative!);
(Equip) Heavy Armor;
(Equip) Full Metal Balaclava;
(Equip) Bullet Proof Vest;
Hard Boiled (Damage reduction;
Commando (Health!);
Huge Calves (Health + Speed!);
Camouflage (Damage avoidance);
Tuck 'n' Roll (Damage avoidance);


This L11 Trooper has 2 Armor and 1 damage reduction, basically making any hit of 3 or less a zero damage hit. That's 75% of Rat hits since they do 1-4 damage. Tuck 'n' Roll and Camouflage can eliminate a lot of hits that do manage to connect. Hard Boiled prevents the Rats from scoring double damage against already injured body parts. And any damage that does get through is soaked off by his high Health of 28. Since Rats do only 1-4 damage, this should be a fairly effective means of limiting their damage.

Both Troopers would target the head for the free double damage if they manage to land a head shot. Rats don't have a helmet to deflect the first head shot, so targeting the head is a sound strategy. This also makes Heart Breaker a rather poor anti-Rat skill, since a double damage shot to the head is likely to kill via damage alone, and make Heart Breaker + Hydroshock Shells just about equal for kill shots vs. targeting the head + Hydroshock Shells. All Heart Breaker does is cost a skill (and a level!)to achieve about the same effect that targeting the head accomplishes.

Initiative is speed.
Initiative is being faster.

If I take a Twinoid, I get 500 initiative of actions where you don't act at all.
If I have Frenetic and get a kill, I get 200 initiative of actions where you don't act at all.

I know that this isn't the exact same as having a fast weapon or skills like Cold Blooded, it's actually much, much better.

If I'm using a Knife I act every 40 initiative. That means that the enemy can act on initiative 50 with their Beretta before I get a second attack. But if I get a Frenetic kill with my first attack, I get ~5 unanswered attacks before the first Beretta shot goes off.

This is what makes Guy With a Knife so deadly. He has On Point, Twinoid, and Restless. If he had Frenetic he'd be like unto a god.

Even if I'm using a slow weapon, getting an initiative boost will often allow me a couple unanswered shots before your side can act again.
