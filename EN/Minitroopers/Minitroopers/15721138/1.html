Hi guys!

I'd like to create a test system. I will use three to six-man squads, and I'm gonna try to determine the following:

1. Is there a metagame? Or is it just the circumstances that create a "certain" trend in the game, at least among the forum readers?

2. Which is stronger? Centralized? Decentralized? Is there room for both in a single army?

3. Does a balanced build work out? Does a blue-coded/red-coded/yellow-coded (based) build work? And against whom? For what reasons?

4. Everyone knows how Guy With A Knife rips whole armies apart, and everyone knows that the less the # of troops, the less chances of being ripped by OM armies as this one. Question: When does the tactical side disappear?

5. Orthodox vs. Unorthodox: Everyone knows that common builds gives less risk, high dividends, and strong shows; however, if one were to pursue a diff. strat, would the "more chances=more risks=more unpredictability" model be correct? Or will it fall down short?

FINALLY:

A scoring system for replays (based on strategy). The more common and imba the strat used, the lower the points; the more rare and novel the strat, the higher.

In addition, there will be two more categories: Difficulty (everyone knows what it means) and Execution (clean style, fun style, crazy moves, fails, etc.) Gonna be useful for future commentators!

So, any ideas?

Gonna start:

Average replays go 5, with Twinoid, Frenetic, and vehicle-based battles getting that score. Scores would be different for the other two categories, obviously.

By the way, this will take a long time. But I hope that everyone can contribute, whether it be a link to a three to six-man army, some words, criticisms, and so on!

I hope you have a good day ahead!
