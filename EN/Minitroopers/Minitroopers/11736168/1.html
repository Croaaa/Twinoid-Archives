This is a new competition to test your decision making skills. There is one prize for the winner.

Format:
Random draw single elimination knockout tournament.

Entrants:
Each player may enter ONE army only. The army must be a new army created on the first day of the competition.
On day one I will post a link to the prize army. All competing armies must be recruited under that army.

Time frame:
Sign ups are open until 21st May, which is day one of the competition. Fixtures will be drawn and posted on this date, so each player will know who they will face as the competition progresses.
Players MUST post a link to their competition army by day two of the competition or they will be eliminated.
From day one there will be a four day training period before fixtures begin. Players may improve their army in any way they see fit. After day four the fixtures will be played out.
Each round will be one day.

Challenge rules:
Either player may challenge. Only the first challenge counts. Only challenges on the fixture date may be counted. If both players fail to report a challenge then both players will be eliminated.

Prize:
The winner will be given the password to the prize army.

Sign up below by posting to register your interest.

Jay, yes that is correct.

KofK, monitoring would not be "easy" even if you were active. The various suggestions for limitations on income are unworkable.
The only way to ensure a level playing field would be to limit expenditure in a competing army. That could be calculated exactly based on the number and levels of troopers in the army without having to monitor the types of missions undertaken or whether raids take place.

The simplest thing would be to say that no competing army is allowed to have more than X troopers.
This is controllable by the participant (assuming they remember to set a password) and very easy to check.

If we set X at 4 then that limits the expenditure to 8+24+60 = 92 coins for troopers.
Other expenditure will be limited by the one-level-per-day mechanic of the game.
Getting two recruits (150 coins) on day one would allow you purchase your starting troopers and begin to level them.
By the start of the competition upgrades for all four troopers would total a maximum of 4(1+5+15+32)=4(53)=212

Perhaps a start on day three would be better, giving 4(1+5+15)=84.
What happens after that, even if people are "powergaming" and making absurd amounts of recruits to be able to afford four upgrades every day, should still be competitive due to the way smaller armies interact.

pickupsticks

I'm going to stick with the existing format for this one, as it's straight-forward for me to administer (and I am very short of time at the moment, with IRL commitments that are getting in the way)
These are some interesting ideas though, and could certainly be worth explore for future events.

Knowing who your opponents will be adds something to the strategy of building your army. Of course, building a "general army" suited to all comers is also a good mental exercise, but I think that for this one having prior knowledge of who you will face will increase the anticipation of the matches. So, I'm not sure that losing that is the best idea.

I'm hoping that having a SHORT sign-up window will reduce the number of deadbeat signups that go AWOL. We've managed OK with the seeded tournaments though, and those have had a couple of weeks' run in. Things didn't work out too good for the Experimental Six Week League back in the day, there were lots of drop-outs. I guess it just depends on who signs up, more than the format.

If there were going to be bys then I'd do a random draw at the beginning. From a practical standpoint this would not confer any more or less advantage to any player than making the draw on a round by round basis. With low power armies and limited competition rounds though, a by is a big advantage. Much more so than in any of the other competitions I have run. So I am keen to avoid it.

Thanks for the input though, you and KofK, and anyone else that wants to speak up. I know I sound over-critical of suggestions and criticisms, but that's mainly because I've already budgeted my time for the admin when I set things in motion. Ideas and discussion are always a good thing, even if they are not acted upon until the next event begins.
