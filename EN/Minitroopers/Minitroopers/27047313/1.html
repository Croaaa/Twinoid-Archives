On july 14, 2013
Pause

Hi all, I know it's been a long time and thanks for your patience.
I'm sorry to delay this even further but I feel that I must make rule changes public before they come binding.
Please feel free to object to changes or to suggest alternatives.

KOFKOREA has help me to see some of the loophole in the original set of rules.
I feel that closing these loophole is totally necessary.
However, doing this will drastically change the framework of the tournament.

New rules.
- - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - -
Change 1
Only Attacking Scores Points.

Point scoring is only when attacking.
Defending earns no points.
It is intended that each player will attack their opponent the maximum number of times each day.
The attacker is responsible for posting up their replays.
Each replay showing the attacker as victorious will earn them 1 point.

Change 2
Safety Time.
No player may attack their opponent during the safety time.
Safety time is the 8 hours of time before the server resets ever day.
It is intended that players will be upgrading their armies and getting ready for the next day after all of the battles of the current day, however, your opponent may become inactive.
Because of this we have safety time, during this time any army may be upgraded for the next day.

Change 3
The CK-Magellan.
The staring trooper of each army may not have the CK-Magellan at any time.
Players may clone the staring trooper to see their future skills, it is recommended that you do.
- - - - - - - - - - - - - - - - -

Sound good?

Point scoring is battle by battle, it is irrelevant who wins the over all match or if the match would have been a draw.

The English Server Cup is a round robin.
Here is an old example of a round robin ... The English Server Cup # 3

For each day we have a round. For each round the players are paired, 1v1.
Each pair of players is called a match. There will be many matches in each round (see example).

The winner of the tournament is the player who has the most points at the end of the tournament.
This is not necessarily the same player who has won the most matchs.


With the new rules above we may see match scores of "3 points to each player" or "0 points to each player" but it's not likely.
