Are you sure about that?
My take on camouflage was that it was a straight up 20% chance for any shot fired at the camouflaged trooper to miss him. This would mean that a shot from a aim 100 weapon would miss 20% of the time, and a shot from a aim 100 weapon with laser sights (total aim of 180) would have the same 20% chance to miss.

The same would apply if camouflage was just an addition to the overall dodge chance.

The wiki says "It grants a 20% change to Dodge every shot fired at the Trooper", but the use of the term 'dodge' by the wiki could be entirely misleading if camo just makes one shot in 5 display as 'miss.' The skill text itself makes no reference to dodge.

And how to even try to measure the "less likely to be targeted" effect, well, it seems like an impossible task.
