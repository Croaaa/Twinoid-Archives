SilentPinoy

My personal opinion of best army composition.

Minimum requirements
3 Spies (Maximum Spy Deployment)
2 Comms. (All Comms. Commands at a reasonable level)
2 Doctors (1 per each wave)
2 Munitions (1 per each wave)
1 Saboteur
Once these have all been met...

2 Mixedbag(Soldiers > Comms. > Doctors > Munitions > Saboteur <> Spies > Pilots, Scouts I personally dislike but many people swear by them so I'm not including due to extreme bias) In terms of best for an army.

There are of course advantages to having certain units over others and the list I made isn't without drawbacks.

Soldiers I put as the top unit for an extra because of the neutrality it has compared to all the rest of the classes. It sometimes feels like having too many of a certain class is a waste i.e. too many Munitions or Saboteurs is redundant with no added bonuses for your team wave, and because the soldier's upgrade is self serving his is the only one without that drawback. Strategically though his is the not the strongest(certainly not the weakest) as if your main supply of healing or munition does die in a wave you are locked out of that resource bringing the counter argument that having more than the minimum can be a good thing also. I just simply take soldier over the rest once the minimum is taken to optimize, in my opinion, the most powerful in terms of formation.

Comms. is probably the most valuable class in terms of specialties because you always want to vie for tactical advantages in this game. Not to mention how overpowered the commands are. Having 2-3 Comms. is always a healthy amount and eliminating opposing Comms. is always a big win.

Doctor is a class I have an extreme soft spot for because they have strengths similar to being the best all around unit in the game. They don't have as much health as a Soldier, but are immune to poison and paralyzing, they don't have as much buff power as a Comms. but they heal and can get access to a revive skill.

If anything you want these 3 in excess if you aren't lucky.
Everything else is a toss up until we reach my personal bottom 3, feel free to mix up Munitions and Saboteurs if you have no other choice.

However...

Having more than 3 spies is not very useful... barring you want lots of initiative which is why I switch them with having additional Saboteurs. My theory seems to confirm your spy deployment ability works based on your opponent and deploys on a ratio of 1:4, having more than 3 just lets em chill in main base.

Pilots I have no idea what they do at all... My army I have 3 Light Tanks, 2 on spies and 1 on a munitions. I seem to deploy at least 1 tank per day, just look at my fights. Very much a waste.

Scouts... For me I've made a few side armies with scouts and I just have to say that I am so glad they are in my side armies. Scouts serve as a gamble. A huge gamble. They either make it that you win through sheer madness in numbers or add 1 body to the wave 1 death count, I can contest to this because having scouts also increases the odds of a dangerous spy to deploy against you in the first wave. The difference between Scouts and Spies is in terms of tactics, both allow numbers to be changed into your favor but the Spy gives you additional initiative and is close ranged deployed and takes up your opponent's deployment count. Sure they add to the possibility of getting that all mighty Heavy Tank, but let's be real, we've seen that ish in raids before and that ugly block of 50HP metal doesn't even budge till at least 300 initiative and is as strong as its Light Tank counterpart.

For me I hate to sound condescending but I have everything save the Saboteur in my army composition that I may have the perfect army class composition in this game.

3 Comms.
3 Spies
2 Munitions
2 Doctors
1 Soldier
