A long time ago I started talking about the relationship between scouts and vehicles, there was never any conclusion but now I think I know the answer.

We say that light tanks have a deployment cost of 2 points, but we have never seen 2 tanks on the field with 4 deployment points, why is that?
The theory is that... "all vehicles must be accompanied by at least one trooper"
...and this is why a scout is needed to get 2 tanks onto the field.

Scouts: 0
Deployment = 4, Max L.Tanks = 1, remaining = 2
Scouts: 1
Deployment = 5, Max L.Tanks = 2, remaining = 1

Going by this theory their must always be some remaining number of deployment points for troops and that is why we can not have 2 tanks at 4 deployment points.
This old theory would explain why the Heavy Tank could not deploy with 6 deployment points but it does not explain why the HT still could not deploy at 7 DP.
The HT needs 8 DP, being accompanied by 2 trooper.

Heavy Tank
Dpl. Cost = 6 (1 tank)
Dpl. Required = 8 (4 scouts)
Remaining = 2 (troops)

...but in this next link we see the HT with no extra troops.
Image: Link
The above is not a good example, it is not a player owned army, but I believe this would be possible for a player.


I disagree with the old theory that 'vehicles must be accompanied by troops'. I say that troops are not needed and that an all vehicle wave is possible.
I believe that a player could have an all vehicle wave with only 2 scouts and 3 light tanks.

3 Light Tanks
Dpl. Cost = 6
Dpl. Required = 6 (2 scouts)
Remaining = 0 (no troops, no spies)

Here is my theory to explain why I think that a player could have 3 tanks deployed with 6 DP but not 2 tanks with 4 DP.

More or less, a 4 trooper deployment looks like image 1 here... Link to images 1, 2 & 3.
If a light tank deploys it always takes the two red deployment positions ( A and D in image 1).
If a second tank was also going to deploy then where would it go?
Tanks are always horizontal, the only two remaining deployment positions are vertical.

In image 2 I have added a scout. The scout adds position E in front of position B and now there is a horizontal link between the two positions, 1 scout = 2 tanks.
Continuing with this thought a second scout should add another position horizontally linking with C, positions C and F linking in image 3.

Here is an image of horizontal linking with 1 scout.

This 'horizontal linking theory' would explain everything. What I don't have is any evidence.

I'm looking for a replay of 3 light tanks (or helicopters) deploying on the first wave of a player owned army without any troopers.
If anyone sees a replay like this please let me know.
