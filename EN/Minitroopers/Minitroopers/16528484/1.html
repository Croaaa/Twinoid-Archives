WORLD DOMINATION

This is a long-term competition for up to 42 armies.
It takes place on a map of the world that is remarkably similar to that seen in the boardgame Risk.
(details of which may be found on Wikipedia if you are interested)
When I get the chance I'll make up a nice map... although anyone else who wishes to do so is more than welcome.

Each territory may be controlled by one army only. An army may attack any territory that is adjacent to a controlled territory. A successful attack requires defeating the defending army twice (only "Select an army base to infiltrate:" fights may be used, BAT and MISS missions are not permitted) then posting the two replays in this thread. If an attack succeeds then control of the target territory is exchanged. If an attack fails no change takes place.

The game ends when one player controls the whole world.
If a player loses control of all their territory, or a new player wishes to join the competition, then they may start a local uprising in any territory of their choice by defeating the controlling army THREE times and assuming control.
If you control any territory you are not permitted to cause local uprisings, you may only attack from the territory you control.

A player taking control of a new territory must post their replays in this thread, and also copy/paste the updated complete list of controlled territories. The same care taken when playing in the Continuous Championship must be applied here, to avoid mistakes. In the event of disagreement the earliest correct post will be binding.

The rules of which territory can attack which other territory follow the convention of Risk as seen in the image here: http://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia...

To begin with all territories are owned by the friendly global mega-corporation The Rats. Army link here: http://the-rats.minitroopers.com/hq Four troopers, power level is 41.
This army is not taking part in the competition. It will not attack, it cannot win. To claim a territory, a new player must defeat The Rats one time to cause a local uprising.
(NOTE: this is a special case for The Rats. Any other local uprisings require THREE victories)

Each player may only have ONE army in the competition at any one time.
