In my opinion it's a little early to rate your army, I just feel you should wait until you have a 5th unit or your troopers have a class. I feel where we have the most control in comparing our armies to one another is in our choices of classes whether we have a wide variety of classes, or omit and/or amass specific classes...

-Having effective Spy units
*Distraction + Assault
-Amassing Scout units
*For all-or-nothing strategy
*Or to have a vehicle or scout equipment advantage
-Maintaining Soldier and higher HP units
*Outlasts many troopers
*No real consequence in losing if your army doesn't rely on him to provide a buff for the team
-Having or omitting
Saboteurs(Weapon Disabling)
Medics(Healing)
Comms.(400 Commands)
Munitions(Resupplying)

Many people determine an armies effectiveness based on the choices of these, they determine your army as a whole. I can only give you an effectiveness of your individual units because not only do you only maintain 1 wave(bearing no real strategy other than run on the field as a cluster and shoot with your guns), but they lack any real substance to differentiate them from one another.

Murder many long range options offsetting Shotgun and Faceboot. But I'm assuming that was so he had close range options just in case.

Suicide has the makings of a good assault unit. Too bad he has that Bazooka M25 which doesn't sync with Vicious or Sprinter.

I don't know why Death has Biped with short range, higher accuracy, zoning weapons.

Kill could use more focus, Twinoid already shows promise on this unit.

Overall I think there may have been lack of knowledge and planning in what your units are going for in terms of synergy. I lack knowledge of your other options so I can't determine if you choose many of these skills by mistake or by taking the less worse skill.

I hope you are doing this with your team

Murder and Suicide will provide long range coverage while your closer ranged units move in. Specifically Death will take a Twinoid early to get to mid range and start firing. Murder should start Mos-Teck over his Bazooka so that he can immediately start firing. Suicide on the other hand should start with his Bazooka instead of running in immediately because your other units lack options, covering more options will lead to less death for yourself. I also specify aiming to non-closest targets for your longer ranged units because you'll waste time having to re-aim to new targets, especially if your sniper kills the closest target, wasting range, your other units time and resources, and leaving your opponents free to return fire unchallenged.

I noticed that you seem new to the scene. Read around, check the wiki, there's tons of information on effective strategy and planning for this game.
