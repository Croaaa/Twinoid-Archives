pickupsticksMaybe smart should be the starting skill, to decrease the chances of only having two no-good-for-rats skills to pick from.

I'm a huge proponent of Smart, but I'd skip it for an Exterminate army. Other than the 3 skills offered on level-up (which is still nice) the entire rest of the Smart advantages are useless against rats.

As a preferred starting skill I'd favor an army with something like Frenetic, Battle Ready, or Paralyzing Shells.

Drawing from the lessons learned from the old thread "Exterminate, what?", the issues with beating the rats are thus:
Running out of ammunition
Fratricide (Waiter, I ordered Ratricide!)
Melee

Battle Ready and Munitions Officers would help against the issue of ammunition. As would the Knife.

Fratricide can be eliminated by only taking rifles and pistols (and the Knife). Managing to ensure that you have no launchers/assault rifles/shotguns/machine guns would be a matter of luck. Friendly Fire and Suspicious can also help here.

My attempts to delay melee by setting preferred target to the leg didn't seem to have a lot of effect in getting me further along in waves. Paralyzing Shells would help there, and as mentioned above it's a shell with no limit on use. (No posted limit, at least. How does "Freeze, FBI!" fare with shell availability, Sticks?)

Savior and Retreat might be helpful for refreshing ammo, skill use and health. Survivor might have some synergy here.


But nothing trumps speculation like experience, right? The army with the record posted on the wiki is ryuuji. Made up of 10 L12 Troopers, this army does not avoid launchers and machine guns, having 5 troopers out of 10 with those as a primary weapon. The remainder are equipped with 3 CK Magellan, a Spy armed with a MOS-TECK (he has a Revolver!) and 1 Shotgun (He has a DBS!). The replay shows that he does not adjust his starting weapons for Exterminate missions. He appears to be set to some combination of "The closest" and "The biggest threat", as his sniper armed troopers were excellent at killing the rats which had closed to melee with the machine gun armed troopers. And they didn't appear to disrupt each others aiming very often, either.

His officer breakdown is:
3 Saboteurs (a useless Officer choice against rats)
2 Comms
2 Munitions
1 Soldier
1 Spy

His success can be described like this:

The initial Trooper deployment of 3 CK armed troopers offered a large number of kill shots. His army doesn't seem to be completely optimized, but he has some skill combinations which serve him well, such as one of the CK units also having Frenetic, Nimble Fingers on 2 of the CK units, and Cold Blooded on one of them. The Gatling Gun trooper has EotT, while one of the launcher armed units quickly switched to a Minigun, and the reinforcements called machine gun users had a HMG user with Laser Sights and a Comanche Auto, so all of his machine guns are high accuracy. There was also a nice use of Grenade with KoB.

Having units set to Defender helped him avoid fratricide and rat melee. I watched a shotgun armed unit (primary a CK) more than once back out of melee as well as avoid some fratricide potential. Some dumb luck also helped, the Gat trooper once fired an entire blast at a rat in melee with another unit and hit the rat with every round! This "machine gun into melee" luck was repeated several times. But with any "best of" you have to expect to see some good luck involved. The guys who have bad luck don't make the record performances, after all.

Even when he had some bad luck, such as a friendly fire round stripping the Comanch user of his machine gun, it was turned around. The guy pulled out the basic Sniper and scored killshot after killshot even with no skills to boost those odds, and was again kept topped off by the Munitions Officers.

It was the combination of fast and deadly sniper rifles and accurate machine guns bursts which secured this victory.

They could not have done it without help, of course. The Munitions Officers were key, as they kept topping off ammo supplies which kept 2 of the 3 CK armed units on the primary weapon for a very long time. The Comms Officers were also key, as Solo Mission and Commando boosted initiative often. Reinforcements also brought in needed, er, reinforcements after two of the initial deployment had died. Locked boosted kill rates, but it was of minor importance overall.

The Spy was useless, deploying with the rats armed with a MOS-TECK, and was dragged down without doing anything other than providing a distraction. The second Comms Officer who deployed last also had little benefit once on the field. He swapped his preferred Shotgun for the Revolver quickly (perhaps prompted by range) and may have scored a few hits. Then swapped to a launcher and almost toasted the last remaining machine gun operator. Both of those units could perhaps be improved by changing their preferred weapon. But I suppose of the Spy deployed on his own line that the MOS-TECK would have been handy. In any event, neither of these units did much, indicating that an 8 person army might be able to have similar success.
