I think On Point operates on a different system to regular initiative. Multiple troopers on the field with On Point results in a metaphorical diceroll to determine who goes first.

For clarity on initiative in general, think of it as your AP for VATS in the fallout games. The game is basically turn based; how many initiative points they have determines how many actions they can take before it is someone else's turn (this is represented by the blue bar at the top, every action such as reloading etc. causes the bar to shrink).

Spies get a bonus amount of initiative every level up, so basically the higher level the spy, the more time they have to whoop ass once they get their turn (which is why spies often get wiped by troopers with On Point). A spy with on point that gets to go first will use On point's initiative to go first, and then use their bonus initiative to carry out actions like firing, moving and reloading.

This is also true for solo missions, they get a sudden burst of initiative (remember the little blue bar kiddies) in which no one else can move, and they're free to wreak havoc.

The only part I don't understand (assuming I understand anything about this game) is how some troopers manage to 'overwrite' other's initiative (for example, a trooper taking Twinoid during a spies reign of terror, thus cutting it short).

As for speed, that's just how quickly they use up their initiative. A trooper with sprinter that gets a solo mission can make it to the other side of the map and still have plenty initiative to make an attack, whereas a trooper with heavy armour that gets a solo mission will make it about 5 metres before their initiative runs out.
