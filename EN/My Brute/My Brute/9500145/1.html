I don't think the shuriken is unfair at all. Think about it logically. Although there is seemingly no end to how much damage the shuriken deals, in actual fact, it takes about the same damage as the sword, before the opponent has an opportunity to strike again. It works like any other one strike powerful weapon, only that it is divided into a billion throws.

When you're up against a shuriken, you're basically up against a weapon as powerful as the sword.

Although it has the potential of dealing damage equal to what the sword could deal, the fact that it is thrown is a disadvantage because it gives the opponent numerous opportunities to dodge or block, thus minimizing its overall damage for that set of throws.

The more weapons a brute has, the less effective the shuriken is also, because the user tends to switch before finishing finishing the set of throws

I think we are simply looking at the shuriken as a weak weapon that is spammed and does not give us the opportunity to stop it. However, like I said, it is in actual fact a weapon that is designed to deal about as much damage as the sword. So dodging or blocking those one or two hits is better than we originally thought.

I hope I've made sense
