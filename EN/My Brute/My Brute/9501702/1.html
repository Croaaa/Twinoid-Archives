While I don't question MyBrute's motives, and I certainly understand the frustrations of its spokesmen, its claim that we should avoid personal responsibility is not only an attack on the concept of objectivity but an assault on the human mind. If MyBrute thinks that it can make me hang myself by the neck until dead then it's barking up the wrong tree.

We've tolerated MyBrute's domineering refrains long enough. It's time to lose our patience and chill our kindness. It's time to condemn MyBrute's criminal ineptitude. It's time to shout to the world that it uses language that would make a sailor blush. (Yes, it apparently wants to use us to fulfill its simple-minded mission, but that's a different story.)

When MyBrute was first found rubbing salt into our wounds, I was scared. I was scared not only for my personal safety; I was scared for the people I love. And now that MyBrute is planning to exploit other cultures for self-entertainment, I'm downright terrified. Some people apparently believe that if we don't bother MyBrute, MyBrute won't bother us. If MyBrute succeeds in its attempt to give lunatics control of the asylum, it'll have to be over my dead body.

I should expect to find—this is a guess that I currently lack sufficient knowledge to verify—that life is a search for the true, the good, and the beautiful. The simple, regrettable truth is that MyBrute's rantings are eerily similar to those promoted by madmen such as Pol Pot. I'll probably devote a separate letter to that topic alone, but for now I'll simply summarize by stating that mandarinism is dangerous. MyBrute's cruel version of it is doubly so.

Are you still with me? Do we not, as rational men and women, owe it to both our heritage and our posterity to cast a gimlet eye on MyBrute's précis? I think we do. The spectrum of views between pharisaism and cannibalism is not a line but a circle at which crafty incubi and credentialism-prone storytellers meet. To properly place MyBrute somewhere in that spectrum one needs to realize that one does not have to take us all back to the Stone Age in order to bear the flambeau of freedom. It is an ophidian person who believes otherwise.

MyBrute's goal is to do exactly the things it accuses malodorous, censorious kleptomaniacs of doing. This is abject ageism! MyBrute takes things out of context, twists them around, and then neglects to provide decent referencing so the reader can check up on it. It also ignores all of the evidence that doesn't support (or in many cases directly contradicts) its position. Those of you who thought that MyBrute was finally going to leave us alone are in for a big surprise because MyBrute recently announced its plans to project a stream of hypersensitive images of death, sex, disaster, material goods, celebrities, and other fixtures in a mock-Olympian firmament. It's quixotic for MyBrute to enslave us, suppress our freedom, regiment our lives, confiscate our property, and dictate our values. Or perhaps I should say, it's addlepated. People sometimes ask me why I seem incapable of saying anything nice about MyBrute. I'd like to—really, I would. The problem is, I can't think of anything nice to say. I guess that's not surprising when you consider that if you are not smart enough to realize this, then you become the victim of your own ignorance. Okay, I've written enough for one letter, so let me just finish by saying that we may never learn the answers to some of the more vexing questions surrounding MyBrute's motives.
