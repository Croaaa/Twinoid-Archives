MadMooney
Well, to break this down:
The first problem are new users unwilling or too lazy to use the search. They produce threads.
The second problem arises from the first, because the search results go down, quality-wise, which produces even more threads.

There a 4 ways of dealing with a question of those threads:
- just answer, as if nothing's wrong. The polite way, tiring, sending the wrong signal.
- answer but comment on the question. Might be rude, tiring nonetheless, see above.
- just bash. Rude, fills the forum and produces bad search results in the process.
- ignore the question, don't answer at all.

Our aim is
- a polite forum, no rudeness
- an inviting forum with a dozen or more threads on the first page that don't deal with repetitive questions
- Users should use / shall learn to use the FAQ and the search
- the search function should produce good results if needed

Bashing a user because of his lazyness is rude, no question - but answering every thread is equivalent to teaching users not to use the FAQ but to just ask away.
Sadly, "Here's the answer but next time please use the faq/search", from my experience, doesn't produce the desired result, either.

What bugs me most is that by aiming to answer every question that was asked a hundred times before, responsibilities and work load shift from the lazy non-searcher to those who are eager to help - and soon those will be not so eager anymore.

If you ask me, ignoring those threads would be best, reply count would be zero, and a halfway intelligent search function user wouldn't even click such a search result. The thread will wander away from the front page of the forum, unless the poster bumps it up again. One could intervene then, by just providing a link to a better thread, with no comments about the FAQ or the search function, but one: "please use this (link) thread for further questions around this topic".

I declare this post RFC20110617-1 as in "Request for Comment", so...
Whatcha think?
