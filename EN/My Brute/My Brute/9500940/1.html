Hello!

The purpose of this thread is to figure out various my brute strategies that can go anywhere from the best way to train your brute or even what specialties/weapons/pets works best against what. (NOTE: I DO NOT WANT PEOPLE POSTING RANDOM GREAT COMBINATION SKILLS, YOU MUST SAY WHAT YOUR COMBINATION OF SKILLS ARE GOOD AGAINST). Feel free to disagree, but please say why!

Here is one to start things off,
High hp (200+) high armor brutes vs weapon throwing brutes
I would immediately suggest the noodle bowl against these tank brutes, however it is very rare, so shurikans and piou piouz work very well too. These brutes can do almost nothing against an onslaught of thrown weapons, and will almost always just sit and take all the damage for the entire match. If you have a brute who specializes in these thrown weapons, your target should be these high hp high armor brutes! Other great skills against these brutes include Flash Flood + Hammer combo (very very rare combo, does not work well separately against tank brutes, you need both), weapon master + knife combo or any high damage weapon (like flail, axe etc)
Now if you're the tank brute and you want a few tips on how to deal with these weapon throwing brutes, I highly highly highly suggest shock above all other things. You have to remove the poison, which is what thrown weapons basically are. They slowly but surely take consistent chunks away from your health. However if you are not so lucky to have shock in your tree, then other great skills are untouchable + shield combo, sabotage + first strike combo or 20+ agility as thrown weapons are easily dodged.

There's always a combination of skills that works very well against some other combination. To list them all would be futile not just from the sheer amount of combinations, but also because it would make no sense to aim for a specific combo just to defeat another few specific combos, even if you already have part of it.
Since there really is no catch-all combo in this game that you can really aim for, the only thing that is worth discussing is a specific choice that complements an existing skill set - and those have to be made individually, case by case, and even there is - more often than not - no right or wrong answer.
Even discussing some existing or imagined level x brute where a specific next-level-choice goes towards a specific combination more or less suited against various other combos, the above practical limitations still apply.
This thread will then mutate into a "I have this brute, what choice should I make and why?" where there's still no right or wrong answer. Sorry for painting that picture, just my 2ct.

I'd concentrate on training questions like:
"How to 'read' my opponents roster without spy?" or
"How do I find out who to train against with the best results measured in wins vs losses, for maximum training effect?"
Answers could look like:
If you have a brute with great disarming skills like with trident/sai/shock, go for brutes with low to medium health and speed, they either have many weapons and/or pets, pushing the stats down. Anti-weapon weapons usually work pretty well against pets, too, but not so much the skills. Low opponent health then also makes for a fast kill when disarmed. If you have opponents lined up with an average health of 100, with the lowest 2 are in the mid 60s and one's in the 70s, those last 3 probably have pets. The medium health brutes usually come without pets, the 200hp brute has some special or other, stay away.
If you have a brute with high-impact weapons like the stone hammer, long sword etc., go for opponents with higher-than-average health and low agi. They usually don't have pets, and it shouldn't matter if you win after 2 or 3 hits.
If you have good anti-pet skills, go for brutes with ultra-low health.
If you don't know what brutes to go after, train on day against brutes with high health, one against lower health, one day against ultra-low health with pets. Fight against high agi one day, low agi the other. 4 to 5 days max, and you should know what to do.
The thing is: if you have that experience, and have a brute in Avenger or Soul already, why tell your secrets? To create more of those strong newcomers who will later beat you up as a way of saying thank you? I think I've already said too much....
