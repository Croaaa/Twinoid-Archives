Hey there!
In the old thread http://muxxu.com/forum/thread/1123744 people told about occasional freezes of their browser, in fights, Tournaments and especially when returning to cell.
I had that problem too for a while, getting more ugly over time.
Found the solution.

It seems that when you don't tell Firefox to delete your history every time you close it, it holds it for months, which seems kinda stupid. Open your history via Firefox Menu / History / Show all history.
Select a month, like December. Select the first entry in the list.
Scroll down, click and hold shift, click the last entry.
I had more than 6000 entries there, a whole bunch of those were visits in my main brute's cell.
To get rid of those entries, select them in chunks of 1000 or so, and hit DEL. If you select more, it takes exponentially longer and can lead to freezes. Should take you 2 minutes to clean your history that way.

Close your browser, wait a few seconds, open it again, try your brute's cell.
Faster? Thought so.

Optional step to clean the Database and defrag (advanced users):
Download from www.sqlite.org/download.html the sqlite3 tool, for Windows that's the precompiled binary
sqlite-shell-win32-x86-3071000.zip (251.64 KiB)
and unpack sqlite3.exe into your Firefox profile directory (that's where the file places.sqlite is)
Again, assuming you have Windows, open a Command Prompt in that directory and type
sqlite3 places.sqlite VACUUM (Don't forget to close Firefox first!)
and hit Enter
Wait a few seconds, and done.
Check the size of your places database.
Mine shrunk from 31MB to under 4MB, and my Firefox is FLYING - it opens faster, the address bar is faster, and my brute's cell opens in like half a second.
