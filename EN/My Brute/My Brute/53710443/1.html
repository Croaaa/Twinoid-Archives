This is where destinies come in. When you win a tournament, you can pick either of the 2 things. NB: you don't have to do this but you won't be able to do any more tournaments otherwise. If you got a good build, you can continue it and can even tinker it to cut out certain skills, which is known as forking. If you think you got a bad build, you can pick new destiny to start all over, which is where the purpose of tournaments comes in.

Here's an example:

You have a brute with martial arts at level 2 and herculean strength at level 3. Those are core skills which you can hold onto and arguably, you'd be crazy to pick new destiny.

Each choice contains a path, so say you have an option to pick knife at level 4 or +3 speed.

On picking +3 speed, the next 2 levels give you relentless and +3 strength (+4.5 because of Herculean), and then the next few levels give you fists of fury and fierce brute and then the rest of the options are just ordinary so you pick stats from there. You win the tournament and pick same destiny. You're back to level 1.

OR

On picking knife, about 4 levels later, you get the option to get a Bear pet, along with shock, first strike and sabotage.

Suddenly, the leveling up using old destiny is not as random anymore and you can have some ability to tailor the skills you want.


The return to level 1 through destinies gives you those options and the prerequisite to use reset option is to win a tournament.

When you reach the top rank (feel free to correct me on this brutal legend players), you're no longer able to reset your brute but every time you win a tournament, you get an ear tally and you gain a bundle of XP. At that stage, you can aim for the high level brutes you see from time to time in bruteTV or the links some people post in these forums.
