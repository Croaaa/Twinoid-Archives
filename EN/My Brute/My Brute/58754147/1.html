Hi there,
I am a player of MyBrute for 8 years now. As many other players I think there is an unreasonable point in tournament system of the Muxxu version. The winners of the tournament won't be able to join in another tournament unless they reset to level 1 and start all over again. This is unfair, making their game boring and their training slow down! On the other hand, if they can join in another tournament with the weaker brutes then the weak brutes will find it more difficult to rank up or to choose a new destiny.
So I have a solution for this issue: The tournament winners will have an option to stay at their rank without losing their XP, on the contrary receive XP from their wins. From now on, they can join in a tournament as usual but all members of that tournament must be the one who ever win the tournament of this rank. This is for assurance of justice for the weaker brutes who haven't win a tournament of this rank. In conclusion, winners with winners, losers with losers.
Thank you for reading my long post. Looking forward to your reply

This is actually a really good idea, you took into account the argument against staying at a rank after winning a tournament. Alot of people just say "We should be able to leave the tournament, stay in the same rank and enter a new one" without realizing that this will pretty much make it so brutes new in the rank will be stuck there for eternity since it will be impossible to compete with brutes that have won various tournaments already and just farm exp.

Something people have mentioned in the past has been tournaments categorized by levels instead of ranks. Like a tournament brutes level 50-60 can enter, regardless of rank. Maybe make it so brutes in lower ranks have to fight an additional bracket, just so brutes in higher ranks get the advantage they deserve since they had to climb up the ranks already.

There's alot of ideas that can be implemented into the stale tournament system that this game has, but it's almost impossible that after 6 years they'll do anything about it. Still, it's fun to think about.
