Kagerou

At very low levels brutes with high (but not insanely high) stats tend to be weaker because more stats means no weapons. Afterwards very low HP means bear/wolf so you gotta avoid those until you have no problems with pets. You probably don't want to fight high agility brutes with your heavy weapon guys. At the same time, insane speed usually means Recon, so if you have high dps, you can just get a (nearly) flawless victory.
For the most part, it just comes down to fighting a brute once or twice, see if yours can defeat it and farm that guy if the answer is positive. You should have a text file for your destiny anyway, might as well write a list of "easy opponents" there. Stats just don't give enough information, every brute can turn out to have ridiculous weapons/supers/specialties.

ZyngHow should you pick which opponent to fight? Highest stats? Lowest stats? Just pick one space to click on and hope it averages out? What do you guys do and is there any method anyone recommends?

Do you have a brute with spy skill? If not, if you manage to get a brute with spy skill. Do not change their destiny. It is a massive help to have it.

Spy aside, as previously stated, it's hard to figure out which brute to fight, but it will depend on what your brute is good at. You only have stats to look at, so here are the following factors:
- high stats will mean few skills and/or few weapons. Vice versa may also occur, but the brute might have a bunch of stat boosters early on.
- Lots of speed on a brute likely means it's a recon brute. They start with less initiative.
- Lots of HP will likely mean they have vitality, but if they have tons of HP, it's an immortal brute.
- Low HP brutes may mean that brute has a pet.
- other than that, memory helps.


Tips for early-mid levels:
- If your brute specializes in strength, consider going after squishy enemies.
- If your brute is tanky, consider attacking low strength enemies.
- If your brute is agile, consider attacking low agile enemies and avoid tanks. Having high agility brutes early on is very hard to level up quickly. Agility difference means almost nothing in fist fights.
- If your brute has speed, fight against a low speed enemy. Over time, your brute will get very weak unless you find some other attribute to rely on.
- If your brute has a pet (and again no spy skill), develop keen memory to figure out which brutes have net, cry of damned or hypnosis.


The entire game is trial and error and RNG, so success without spy depends on memorizing your opponents.
