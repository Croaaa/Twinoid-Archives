If you're one of those people that have a hard time seeing all your brutes get pummeled into the ground ( especially in tournaments ) here's a few tips I use that helps coping with bad brutes lol !

If you're one of those people who have brute(s) that very seldom fight more than 4 or 5 matches, here's a tip with trying to get more matches in (exp).

1. You have 24 hours to do all 10 of your matches, and joining a tournament. When you first start battling you'll notice during your exhibitions people will be fighting your brute ( it starts as soon as you fight your first match ) Fight 1 exhibition ( win or lose doesn't matter ) and then wait for 20 minutes or half an hour, if successful, you'll notice a few people have challenged your brute. You can review these matches to be able to get a glimpse of what skills/weapons the brute who challenged your brute have thus being able to avoid accidently fighting brutes that are too tough for yours. ( it works for me )


2. Try to read how many levels the possible challengable brutes were used on stats ( minimizing chances of them having a weapon/skills/pets) Personally I go for brutes with low agility and health.

3. If you have two brutes of the same level, you should be able to use the stronger brute to fight first, that should change the 8 brutes that the weaker brute can be able to fight ( in case they all appear strong to you ) just fight some of the weaker brutes in the selection with the stronger brute to lower the chances of the weaker brute losing.

For tournaments, it happens that even if you have a lot of brutes, they call can lose several times over in tournaments. Usually one would think "at least one of my brutes should win" but that's not the case, I know this personally XD

4. It helps to watch your matches every hour as opposed to waiting 5+ hours so that all your tournaments are finished already. It can be quite frustrating to have to log in hoping for the best, only to see every brute you have 1 by 1 get pwned by stronger brutes ( less stress on you and less raging ) Sometimes when I'm watching TV my brother hits the table in frustration seeing all his brutes lose 1 by 1 stupidly lmao !

Hope this helps someone

Another thing that can help is if you enter a tournament before you do your training when you need more than 10 exp to level up. That way if you win your tournament you will have all your training remaining for the next rank up. And if you lose your tournament you might gain extra exp to get closer to your level up anyway. However, you should always train before you enter a tournament if you're within 10 exp from your next level up, to maximize your winning chances.
