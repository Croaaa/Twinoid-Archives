There are people who keep pounding that "pets are useless in higher levels"
First of all, in this context, what is to be considered a "higher" level? 4? 40?

Wolf:
Consider this scenario: If you get the choice between, say, Flail (a weapon notoriously called OP), and a Wolf at, say, level 12, I'd take the beast any day. While it usually does not deal the same amount of damage as Flail, it often runs more than once, often dealing 40-70 damage within just a few seconds. And the brute next to the beast attacks, too, so that adds to the overall damage. And every damage dealt is good damage.
Determination aside, If the opponent dodges an attack with a heavy weapon, that's usually it.
The way a wolf dishes out his damage, some some of it is bound to get through.
One other thing often overlooked: With a handful of weapons in an arsenal, you never know when you will draw the heavy weapon you like. The wolf is already there.
Now for the anti-pet-weapons league: I see disarming abilities and -weapons way more often than cry of the damned and hypnosis. I talk about the Net in a minute, but let me say right now it's overrated.
One very important aspect of small pets: Yes, my wolf takes some of my Health points when chosen. It then takes triple that in damages and still lives. And more importantly: If I got 20 health left, and a wolf next to me, chances are 50/50 that my wolf takes that 87-89-86 flail attack.
"Thank you very much", is what you write on that beast's tombstone, not "You're useless!"

Dog:
Thank you for catching that net that was meant for me, I couldn't have survived that potential 120 damage heavy weapon attack, but I was able to dodge it.
Thank you for taking that 50/50 chance of catching the net, my Wolf will share his meat with you later! For those who wonder, a few brutes in the top 50 do have that combo, and I had to learn to respect it - the hard way.
Thank you for taking on that Fierce brute with a Mammoth Bone on steroids, I would not have been able to survive him, but now I'll bury him with you so you'll have some brute meat for your afterlife!

Bear:
Ah well, sorry for waking you up from your winter hibernation, I can see that you're still half asleep from the way you don't attack.
But thank you, too, for catching that net that was meant to me.
Thank you for catching that fierce brute and that wolf and that flail and that wolf (again) - I could not possibly have survived all that.

All of those pets are susceptible to anti-pet weapons and abilities, but other than that little Health investment, they usually don't hinder at all. The only moment my wolf hindered me in a fight was when I went Fierce, threw my Net, took my Bone, and - that beast ran, attacked, dealt 30 damage, and came back, destroying the net while at it - and my attack was dodged. Ah well.
In the grand scheme of things, getting a pet might set you back a day (think about that for a minute, willya?), but keeping it boosts you through the rest of your stay in your current rank.

Those many brutes with pets in the upper ranks did not suddenly cheat by hacking the server or bribing a muxxu dev to get this pet, they are up there because they got lucky with a pet early in the game and decided to run with it. They did not listen to those Naysayers claiming a pet is no good in higher ranks so you better stay away from them.
You, too, can decide if you stay away from pets - or from those Naysayers.

And even in higher ranks, damage my pet intercepted is damage I didn't die from, so...

Thank you!
