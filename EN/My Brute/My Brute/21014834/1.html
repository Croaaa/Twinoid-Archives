I see a lot of brutes that suck.... as did every brute I made in the first 8 months when I was playing mybrute. Now I understand what it takes to make a good brute, so I would like to help new players avoid wasting their time making brutes that suck.


In order to make a brute that is effective a higher levels, it needs to meet these criteria:


1. Martial Arts.


At higher levels, you can't afford to go a few rounds without doing effective damage. Since most brutes will have one or more ways to disarm you (see below), you cannot rely on weapons alone. You must have martial arts. Branch your destiny until you get it.


2. Good Stat Boosters.


Herculean Strength: +3 STR, +50% boost to all previous and future STR bonuses
Feline Agility: +3 AGI, +50% boost to all previous and future AGI bonuses
Vitality: +3 END, +50% boost to all previous and future VIT bonuses
Lightning Bolt: +3SPD, +50% boost to all previous and future SPD bonuses


Most brutes will get 2 stat boosters. A few will even get a third, but it is rare. For those 2 boosters, aim for herculean strength and feline agility as they are the top two. Vitality is in third place - not a bad pick, if you are unable to get the top two. Lightning bolt is a distant fourth, because speed is just not as important as strength and agility.


Then there are two "special" stat boosters: reconnaissance and immortality.


Reconnaisance: +5SPD, +150% boost to all previous and future SPD bonuses.
Immortality: +250% boost to all previous and future END bonuses. -25% to all other stats.


Reconnaissance is so bad it actually makes your brute worse, so it should never be taken IMO. Immortality is good if you have the weapons and talents to deal damage; it gives your brute much more health, but your AGI and STR will suffer. If you can't deal enough damage, the extra health means nothing.


Note reconnaissance and lightning bolt stack, as do immortality and vitality.


3. A Small Set of Good Weapons


Don't take every weapon that comes along! You only need a handful of weapons: anywhere from 1 to 3 is enough. Do not take bad or average weapons, ever!


The only weapons worth taking are:


Sai
Flail
Trident
Whip
Frying Pan
Trombone


Note this is a "general" list... there are some special combinations which make other weapons good enough to take (e.g. counter-attack, shield and broadsword, or first strike + sabotage + leek, mug and martial arts), but that's beyond the scope of this guide.


Try not to mix and match weapons that don't belong together. If you have sword, knife and weapons master, you really don't need whip. If you have sai and knife, you really don't need axe.


4. Shield.


You simpy must have this. It blocks half of all blows coming in, effectively doubling your health and your ability to do damage to your opponent.


5. A Way to Disarm.


If you have no way to disarm your opponent, you are at a disadvantage. These are the ways to disarm:


Sabotage
Shock
Iron Head
Shock via a weapon (sai, baton, whip and some others)


You should have at least one of the first three. Note shock can also disarm your opponent's shield, which gives it an extra edge.


6. Good Other Skills and Supers.


You should aim for at least some skills and supers from this list, which will greatly improve your brute.


First Strike
Counter-Attack (assuming you have a shield, see point 4)
Untouchable
Fierce Brute
Ballet Shoes
Fists of Fury
Sixth Sense
Hostility
Hammer
Determination
Relentless
Resistant


There are other skills, talents and supers, which are not listed, but they are generally not that great. The ones above are what you should try to obtain.


If you follow these guidelines, your brute will not suck. If you choose to take bumps, baton, lightning bolt, net, spy, broadsword and halberd, then I hope you don't mind that at level 20+, you will consistently lose 3 fights a day. Learn from my mistakes. Enjoy!
