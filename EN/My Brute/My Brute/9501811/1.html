I don't have this all worked out yet, but I thought I would put it out there; another way to see the fights develop. I began to think about this when Muck made the comment that he "never wastes a level taking something that won't help in a tourny" i.e., any of the 24 hour/1 life a day "skills"; spy , chef, etc.

Anyway. Do you ever watch a fight, especially in a tourny where the levels are mixed, and think about the gaps in the brute's levels becoming greater or smaller? E.g.: I am a 14 taking on a 20. He strikes first and I block. Our levels remain as they started. I strike back, knocking away his shield and doing 18 damage. That -2 level choices: Now we're effectively a 14 vs. an 18 (I took 3+end, and the shield: each is one level). I have iron head, and he hits me with his dagger 2x for 8 each time and it bounces off my head the 2nd time, he took about one level from me, I took one level (his knife choice) away from him, now we're a 13 vs. a 15.

Anyway, you get the idea. So the point is this: is there a way of approaching a brute build in such a way that you eliminate others' levels most effectively and maximize your own?

So, i.e.: MA is a much better choice than many weapons because it is a "permanent level". Also good is Bear over a Panther, because a Panther is easier to eliminate. But, it's not that simple either. Is MA a better choice than flail? Sometimes, but not usually- even though according to "fight math" it is a weapon and therefore an potential "temporary Level".

Well, there it is. What if builds were approached in terms of the best way to preserve your own "levels" as well as the fastest way to take "levels" from others?
