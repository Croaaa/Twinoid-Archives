I've lost interest in this game complately because of the imbalance.

The choices you make when selecting a destiny should determine how you'd like to build up your brute. Do you prefer better offense? Or do you want to focus more on defense? A certain stat? Do you want to build your brute into a certain direction to make better use of a weapon? Etc.

To achieve that kind of balance requires most skills and weapons to be on fairly the same level. They can be different, yes, but one should not clearly totally dominate everything else.

Yet what do we have?

Most brutest get bumps, knife, mammoth bone, baton, etc. The usual weapons. And the same for the usual skills.

Then there are some who are lucky enough to get skills and weapons that are COMPLETELY and TOTALLY imbalanced, such as long sword, flail, immortal and the like. If you are lucky enough to get one of those, your brute becomes insanely more powerful than those brutes who get the usual stuff.

This makes the game total fail. You either get one of those and have an edge over everyone else, or you are the "everyone else" that gets sh1t on when you face someone with one of those skills and/or weapons.

I don't know what the "designers" were thinking putting items in the game that are 10x as powerful as everything else.

The imbalance is what makes the game interesting. What bugs me more is that you don't really have "choices" to "build" your brute. At levelup, you either get an ability (which you will almost always take) or a weapon (which you pretty much take if it's not one of the sucky ones, or if it synergizes with one of your skills). The other alternative is always stats. Stats aren't useless, but it's usually a pretty straightforward decision.

Choices should be more balanced. E.g., choose between bear and wolf. Flail or Whip. Immortal or Sabotage. +3 Agi or +3 Speed. That would make building your brute much, much more interesting
