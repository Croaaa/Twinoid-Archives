determination goes into effect when you miss a strike, it doesnt effect how many hits you do.
So your brute misses a hit, determination comes into effect. It has a 70% chance to make your brute strike again. It can miss or land a hit. If it lands, that strike ends. If it doesnt land, you have another 70% to strike again etc.

Determination only activates when your opponent dodges. Determination stops working after successfully hitting your opponent. Determination will never boost repeating strikes like fists of fury does.

Examples
Fist of Fury
Strike your opponent twice in a row

Determination
Opponent dodges your first attack so your strike again and hit opponent.

Determination deactivates on a successful attack. It will not return your misses as extra attacks for later. One successful attack. That's all it can do. Nothing else, zip, nada, nyet. Fist of Fury just creates a 20% chance of hitting your opponent again (i.e. combo'ing). This 20% is calculated on each attack. It does not store any bonus attacks that rolled in the 20% margin from determination in a bank for later use.

Example
You attack enemy, attack misses (determination activated successful). You attack again. Attack lands (20% chance combo activated successful) You attack enemy. Attack lands. Your turn ends.

This is not guarunteed to happen every time. It is just an example of how the two specials can work together.
