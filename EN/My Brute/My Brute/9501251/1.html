So when you level up, you get two options. One of the options is always stats, while the other can be a weapon, a skill or stats. Now I wonder how the latter option is determined. I can think of two models:

Model A: A random weapon/skill is chosen (weighted somehow, as some skills are more common than others). If the brute has this weapon/skill already, stats are offered for the second option as well

Model B: All weapons/skills the brute does not have yet, as well as the stat options, are collected in a list. A random element is chosen from that list, weighted somehow as some skills/weapons are more common than others.

If model B were true, it would be advantageous to pick any weapon you get offered even if you don't really want it, because it increases your chances to get the other weapons and skills (as picked weapons are not offered again). But if model A were true, picking unwanted but common weapons would not help at all, as it would just increase your chances of getting offered more stats. Also, one side effect of model A would be that you would never get two stats options on level 2.

Both variants come down to what we know already: You don't get offered a weapon or skill that you already have.
While it is mathematically true that with every weapon you get, the list of weapons on offer gets shorter,
it doesn't help you amassing weapons specifically for the purpose of shortening the list to finally get the weapon you want, because
with n being the number of weapons & skills you have
and N being the number of weapons and skills in existence
during the process of amassing weapons, the probability of pulling a specific weapon or using a skill in a fight shrinks much faster than the probability of getting a specific weapon grows (1/N for variant A, constant, or 1/(N-n) for variant B) during the same amount of time, so this thought exercise is kinda self-defeating, even if B is true.
This only gets interesting for levels where n nearly is N, but with >20 weapons and >40 skills and supers, and since we don't know the base probabilities of those weapons and skills, this stays a thought experiment for the time being.
In other words and to bring an example:
If you want, say, Flail, and use it, it doesn't make sense to bury it between a knife, a keyboard, a broadsword, noodle bowls, shuriken, and half a dozen more, before you even have it.

You are certainly right for weapons. However, skills and pets are always "used" (in the sense that they're not blocked by having other skills) and they can have a much larger impact on the fight than the type of weapon you have.

Let's say you go up to ~level 20 before ranking up and get the chance to turn down, say, 6 weapons on your way. That's around 10% of the available pool.

Now if model B were true, accepting those 6 weapons would have increased your chance to get a good skill in the next level-up by around 10% - at the cost of those 6 weapons you didn't really want. Seeing how it affects every following levelup, that's actually a big increase.

If model A were true, you'd get only the cost of carrying less-than-optimal weapons and not the benefit of increased likelihood to get whatever you consider a "good skill" (unless you count the offered stats bonus as "good" )
