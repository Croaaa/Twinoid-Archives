WonderWoman
It's not so much that a brute's agility in itself would determine the brute's accuracy. It's more like the agility of both of the brutes is what determines whether an attack hits. The accuracy of an attack would always remain the same. It is the brute's agility that makes them dodge it. There are some weapons like the bumps and the halbard that are low in accuracy, but even never change regardless of how high a level it's wielder becomes.

The effectiveness of speed and agility stats is determined by the brute's own speed and agility.

It is similar to how a brute with a shuriken which is known to throw multiple times, has it's effect minimized when up against a brute extremely high in speed. The shuriken throws multiple times because it is a fast weapon. However, another fast opponent would basically negate it's effect, as would an opposing shuriken.

Do you get what I mean? It depends on both of the brutes that determines whether an attack is going to hit or not. That is where my example of the level 9999 brute vs the level 100 brute comes in. Although the level 100 is up against the bumps which is notoriously low in accuracy and has an agility of 51 which would be really effective against lower level brutes, the sheer amount of agility points it's level 9999 opponent has would basically cancel it out and make land every hit with the bumps.
