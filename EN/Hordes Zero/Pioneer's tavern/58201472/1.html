A quick news to let you know that we’re now taking applications for Beta testers. With the Early Access right around the corner we’re looking to get some experienced players on board to help take the game to the next level.

Therefore we’re trusting you to keep your experience with this early version of Dead Cells for yourself (no streaming, no recording). If you’re still interested despite all of this, you can apply through this google form. We will need your Steam ID so we can check your experience as a player.


If you don’t hear from us then you haven’t been selected. If you do, you’ll receive a Steam key via the email address provided to us. Please note we’ll be taking a limited number of testers each week (probably only 20 at a time).


We can’t wait to see your feedback.

Sounds like you aren't very familiar with beta testing but I'll try to explain it to you. It is usually done with a handful of people, depending on the size and scope of the test, you do not need a whole lot of testers (5-10, maybe up to 100). The point is to iron out last minute bugs that might have escaped the devs and to see how the game handles under different environments. More players than that are only good for stress testing the servers in case of online/multiplayer games.

Furthermore, testers are asked to sign an NDA that doesn't allow them to post screenshots or speak about the game until it's released. You seem to be under the false impression that receiving a beta key is somehow a reward for being a "good community" member or whatever. That is simply not true. Just because you helped report bugs in the past, that doesn't entitle you to receive a key. You might get bonus points for that but it certainly isn't a guarantee.

Now, onto streamers. Handing out free keys to random personalities is nothing more than a PR move. It helps spread the word about the game and it doesn't cost the devs anything. Win-win situation for everybody. It has nothing to do with beta testing or with this site and saying streamers don't deserve "free" keys more than people who answered the google form makes absolutely no sense whatsoever. Unlike streamers, they don't get to play the game just for fun, they have a job and that is to report any bugs they may encounter.
