My playstyle is really similar to Kompera.

Warning: lots of text incoming!

When I pick missions, I try to pick a mission that is not a minimum score mission. With extra class level 2, this is generally pretty easy, although they have been times when all 4 have been minimum score missions. I value non-minimum score missions significantly more highly because I have a constant stream of friend pupils coming (<3 you all who send me) and they help me complete those missions easier. I feel that, in general, for most non-minimum score mission (abbreviated as set score mission from now on), the game will give you x-1 students that are really close to the set score (where x is the number of students you need to hit) and the last one will be really low, which will need you to use more classes. Friend pupils help to provide a student with a score much higher than what the last student is, thus giving you a chance to complete the mission with less classes
(i.e. BX3 mission, 5 classes. Top 3 scores are 13, 10 and 6. A friend pupil could come with a goal of 18/20, but a score of 9).

This is what I generally use friend pupils for, and they help me complete missions quickly. 90% of the time I don't hit the side missions. I consider it worth it to burn 1 more class to complete 2 side missions that you would not have otherwise completed, but only if the class is not troublesome (does not contain more than 2 of the following: lovestruck, wizard)

Minimum score missions, though, tend to have friend pupils that can come in with a really low score, thus increasing the amount of missions you need to do. IMO, if you're going for minimum score, I would avoid all friend pupils entirely.

I would value a Ax2, 6 classes, 60XP above a 11/20 minimum score, 5 classes, 100XP. This is because I can probably quickly rush through the Ax2 mission and move on, while the minimum score mission might cause me to take a lot longer than required. Furthermore, I consider SC very important. If I pick the Ax2 mission, I likely just have to kill 2 students/class, and thus can spend the rest of my moves on Life transfer, gaining SC. However, if I coose the minimum score mission, I likely have to kill all the students to be able to up them all and keep them above the mininmum score, and cannot play as relaxed and may have to skip some Life transfer (Life transfer is a great skill btw, gives you 6SC effectively as long as you keep the double desks open.) Furthermore, with the Ax2 mission I can choose to focus down the annoying pupils at first (lovestruck, wizard, joker, clown, cruel are the main ones) to reduce my SC damage.

Not sure entiely how the score equation Coro made works, but was doing some thinking.
Leaderboard Points will be refered to as "Points".

  • You earn 3 points per student per class no matter what. You get those points even if a pupil is excluded from class (via the Teacher's Pet ability). That last bit was an important one for me to test since you still get perfect class bonuses when you exclude a pupil
  • You get 1 XP for every pupil you defeat
  • You get 1 XP and 3 points for every turn remaining for a perfect class
  • Peggy may give you 2 or more bonus XP for defeating pupils (because you need to defeat at least two pupils at once to trigger her effect)
  • You may gain 1 bonus XP when using the Synergy pet ability
  • You can gain 4 or more XP when using the Share The Experience after-class ability
  • You may gain bonus XP from completing friend pupil side objectives
    * You gain variable bonus XP for completing missions

For the most part, if you score a perfect clas, you will gain X and 3X points. To figure out your baseline XP, could even set in the equation where Raw XP = Estimated XP - (points/3)

In my case, Estimated XP for my class/level is 13662 and my current points are 15087
Raw XP = 13662 - (15087/3) = 13662 - 5029 = 8633 XP earned beyond that earned from defeating students and completing perfect classes.

Raw XP is X% of total XP

IS over OF = % over 100

8633 over 13662 = X over 100
(8633 * 100) / 13662 = X
863300/13662 = 63.some long decimal value

Since the game considers 20/20 to be a perfect score, or 100%, then if 100/5 = 20 then 63.n/5 = 12.63(and a long running string). Rounding (down?) to the nearest half is 12.5. Downside of this is since you cannot get away without earning Points is there's no way to get a 20/20 using this formula as is.

Using Hyoga at G9L5 with 75246 points from Coro's last example here I'm getting 17.35n with X/5, 21.69n with X/4, 20.41n with x/4.25 and 20.66n with X/4.2

So final score formula could be ((totalXP - (points / 3)) * 100) / totalXP) / 4.2 rounded down to the nearest half grade (cap of 20.5)

For me: ((13662 - (15087 / 3)) *100) / 13662) / 4.2 = 15 (rounded down to the nearest half grade)
