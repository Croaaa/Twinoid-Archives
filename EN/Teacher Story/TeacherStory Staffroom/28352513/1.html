Colette

That's your grade. When you reach your level limit you can start the next grade, and you are returned to level 1. For example, if you're Grade 1 (Intern, blue pencil icon) and reach level 12, you can start Grade 2 (Teacher, yellow pen icon), but you're returned to level 1. You can stay at Grade 1, but you can't keep leveling up.

The advantage of advancing in Grades is that you get to keep one skill per Grade advancement.

So, assuming you're at Grade 1 and reach level 12. You then get to advance to Grade 2 and will be reset to level 1. But you'll get to choose any skill you like to keep with you, and this skill will always follow you as you advance in Grades.

Check out the Top Profs to see how this works over time. (Ranking -> Top Profs)

That's right.

Choosing to advance in grades also means that you'll get a better start each grade:

Grade 1: start with no additional skill (only basic skills)
Grade 2: start with one additional skill
Grade 3: start with two additional skills

And since your permanent skills don't change, you'll always have something to rely on (eg when the choice you're given when leveling up sucks).
