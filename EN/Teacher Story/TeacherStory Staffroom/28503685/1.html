Colette
45424Btw, calculating average cooldown is irrelevant and inconsistent, since we can have 2 to 4 skill slots and different amount of skills in the pool.

I think you didn't get it. The calculation isn't about the average number of turns it takes for players to get a chance of using the same skill again. It's about calculating the theoretical minimal number of turns that have to pass before the skill becomes randomly selectable. For example, let's say that Player A and Player B use the skill Educational Flick. Player A can use the skill again after 3 turns while Player B can use it after 5 turns. In this case the cooldown is 3 turns, as the skill became randomly selectable for Player A after 3 turns, even if Player B was unlucky and didn't get a chance to select it. This can't change unless Player C can prove that Educational Flick can be used after less turns (lowering the skill's known cooldown), or until Player D can prove that all skills are completely randomly selected every turn, proving the cooldown theory wrong. All this requires much more testing.
