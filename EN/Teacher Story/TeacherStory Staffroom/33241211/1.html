Post your discovered interactions in this thread.

===

A Harmless pupil can't hurt you, as all damage from them is reduced to zero. In practice, you will get a "Resist" notice instead of seeing the -3 (or whatever) for the damage the pupil would normally have done to you.

But a Distracted pupil who is also Harmless will still do damage to you if they take damage.

===

Applying Individual Work removes a lot of negative status. And some which aren't removed simply do not operate.

But a Distracted pupil under Individual Work will deal you -1 SC every turn until they are defeated, or until Individual Work is removed for another reason. I've not seen Distracted expire in this situation, but then I've only tried it once. The pupil had a large amount of and never lost the Distracted status, but that's still just one data point.

===

45424What if you defeat the 2 pupils that notice the items? Can you take both of them?

I would assume so. I think that the usual restrictions would apply, you would have to spend a turn for each item in order to pick up both. And you'd need to be able to defeat both pupils on the turn the items were thrown in order to prevent a 'Yoink' of one of the items.

It's a situation which will only come up very rarely, since it requires:
1) At least one of the pupils in the class has Clone;
2) Uses that ability (I've had Clones who never cloned anyone, just as I've had Wizards who never used their invisibility cloak);
3) Clones a pupil who then throws an object;
4) You have an attack available to you which defeats both pupils on your very next turn. Which basically means that the items need to be tossed to pupils at the same table, or that both pupils are in the same row or column and you have the appropriate row/column attack available to you. Or possibly that you have an ongoing effect such as Exercise which can help you defeat both pupils in a single turn. And that the pupils are both low on .

As you can see from my screen cap, both pupils with items had 4 and were not along the same row or column. So there was no way for me to defeat both in one turn.

It's quite a rare set of circumstances required. Perhaps the players who have advanced far ahead of me might have seen this, or through luck a player who is behind me in advancement might have seen this.

In my case, I was able to use an attack to beat one of the pupils, which gave me the option to pick up the item at my leisure for the rest of the class, but the other pupil did as expected and 'Yoinked' the item before I had a chance to pick it up.
