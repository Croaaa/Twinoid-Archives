StarsickleIf anyone has any tips for me for surviving past level 10, let me know. I could use a better game plan.

Don't get to used to things, you're still in Grade 1. Your cap will be level 15: you will have to go on vacation (think CoD's prestige) to go on, so you only have to worry about them hitting hard for four more levels or so.

If you want tips, here:
1. Save up for Farrier. Only skip when you can afford to, I.E when it's the cheapest/unable to affect your ability to get Farrier. I'm pretty sure any player in this game for the long run saves up for ten packs always, you will get much more mileage out of that than a 3 pack.
2. Target trouble students. Some of the ones 45424 point are definitely worth the extra attention. My personal targets are Wimps (Slow Auras are HORRIBLE, they can cut your chances of finishing the class proper in half or worse) Less-than-Gifted (Pretty much Wimps without Slow Aura, but they CAN make others Slow.) Shy people (Dizziness knocks them out, inflicts either Laughing Uncontrollably or Distracted, both of which drain SC), and Manipulators (REALLY good TP skill if you see them, otherwise prioritize them as they can have Voodoo Dolls, and frequently use Demotivator, which makes them harder to beat off the bat and makes others harder to beat over time)
3. This might sound crazy, but always go for higher XP payout classes. It's hard on you, but the faster you level up, the faster you go on vacation, the sooner you can keep a skill you've gotten and enjoy the breeze of the first ten levels again. And it really does only get easier as you come back over and over again. Once you start playing with a Mid-game mentality it's incredibly easy to be able to save up for Farrier even without maxed out Experienced Buyer.
4. Hold out. You will get better skills as you level up, and yes, skills that increase effectiveness of Farrier (2, one definitely better than the other), effectiveness of Resting, stuff like ending Laughing Uncontrollably/Distracted faster, even more time in a class/more skill rolls per turn are there. It's just a matter of time and hoping RNG favors you. If you get Rigged Clock 1 (i.e the one that gives you an extra turn in class) or Good Memory 1 (i.e increases your skill choices by one) I HIGHLY recommend taking one of those two as your first permanent skill.

While I agree you level up as fast as you can, it's not the case the best option is to take a highest XP payout mission. You need to consider not only how much XP you will be rewarded but also how many classes you expect to finish the mission. For example, while a 120 XP mission sounds tempting, if it's going to take you four classes to finish that compared to a 100 XP mission that you can finish in three classes, it's better to take the 100 XP mission. Sometimes it might be worth it to take a mission that does not give you the most XP per class if you can save on resources. For example, given a choice between a 110 XP mission I can finish in three classes provided I have to use 1 Farrier per class and use sponges and twinoids like crazy and a 100 XP mission I can finish in three classes with maybe at most 2 Farriers used, I might go for the 100 XP mission. You can get a sense of how many resources you will need based on difficulty, student types, and starting scores. Of course, without Psych Record, you'll only have difficulty freely available, so you'll just have to make judgment calls.
