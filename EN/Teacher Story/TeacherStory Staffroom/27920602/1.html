I suppose the real question is "What happens when you reach Grade 9?" Right now (and this could change in the future), it does not appear grades beyond 9 have ever been programmed into the game. So at least for now, Grade 9 cannot be completed.

As we know from certain "elite" French players (ahem) who have spent 10000 years (or is it Euros?) on this game, James traces his heritage back to 65 million years ago, and hangs up portraits for 7 of his ancestors, who represent the following traditional values:

• Authority, represented by the sheriff John Norray (1864-1901)
• Discovery, represented by the captain Jack McNorroy (1702-1740)
• Duty, represented by the Knight Templar Jean Nauret (1410-1482)
• Respect, represented by the general Julius Caius Norius (52 BCE - 0)
• Knowledge, represented by the architect Noris (c. 2000 BCE)
• Loyalty, represented by the priest Naruhatl (c. 12000 BCE)
• Sharing, represented by the hunter Noru'k (c. 65000000 BCE)

So what happens when you reach Grade 9? It's logical to infer that the "world's oldest profession" or the last universal ancestor could make an appearance in the final portrait.



But I think what will actually happen is far less exciting, though not necessarily out of place.

"I'm not worthy of that yet, though. You have to earn these things."

James' ancestors have clearly accomplished a lot. But after teaching some 5000-7000 classes and earning at least 137 500 , don't you think James has proven himself worthy of his heritage, despite an occupation that's arguably less exotic than "Knight Templar" or "Roman General?"
