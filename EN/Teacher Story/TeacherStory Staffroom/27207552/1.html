This thread is for skills that have an unexpected effect, either good or bad, that is not obvious in the description. Feel free to add any skill that you have used that have hidden or strange effects you noticed.


Skill: Individual Work - The student loses one boredom/stupidity each turn until the end of class and cannot be targeted.


This seems pretty good as a one-hit kill, if you have the turns, but there is more to it. The effected student is completely immune to "splash damage" from attacks, cannot be hit with a pet ability, and cannot be moved ( even by the tornado ! ), so they need to be in the right seating for raising (or not) their score before you activate it. The student loses one boredom/stupidity less than the number of turns on the hourglass when you hit them with it, so make sure there are still enough turns left. Because of that, this skill becomes almost worthless on the last turns Any effect that increases the number of turns also increases the power of this skill, that includes Rigged Clock and bonus students sent by friends. The real unmentioned plus of this skill is it stops any negative effect that that student is creating, so its actually like a free Call to Order attack on top of taking them out.


A passive game effect related to this skill to look out for is that the game forces at least one student in the class to be 'up' each turn (able to attack you), so if your last student(s) are self studying, one will be kicked out of it each turn and you will have to deal with them conventionally. (I suppose it would not be very interesting to skip turns until your last students took themselves out ) You can use this mechanic against certain events or negative statuses as well. Taking out the rest of the class will un-KO a student, or force them back from a bathroom break immediately
