I'd have to say it's worded correctly, at least as far as American-English goes, since I've been asked "do you mind" questions as well as having asked "do you mind if I go to the bathroom first?" myself (to which the reply was "yes" as in "yes you can go").

The problem with most "do you mind" questions (and the question "do you mind?" itself) is that it's usually used in slang, and they don't really care if you mind or not. Most of the time, "do you mind" should be replaced with "can I" or "may I" and answered accordingly (and the question "do you mind?" usually means "will you stop that!")

Edit: So, yeah, to answer your question, probably your bad english, especially if it isn't your first language thus making it that much harder to identify slang.

Edit 2: Will also add that being fuzzy logic trick questions that they are, there's really 4 different answers that can be given to a "do you mind" question, and since computers aren't capable of fuzzy logic and you only had two choices, even if you understood the question enough to know the slang, it's still a 50/50 shot on how it should be interpreted. But considering how all the other may I go to the bathroom "questions" are "asked", should have known that "yes" is the positive answer and "no" is the negative answer regardless of what the pupil actually said/asked.

Not quite. It isn't a rhetorical question to ask a teacher "Do you mind if I go to the restroom?", it is an explicit request for permission from the one with authority to grant or deny the request. The proper response would be "No, I do not mind. You may go", or simply "No, I do not mind", or even just "No". But leading with 'no' makes it seem like a denial of the request, and so many people will respond with 'yes' even though logically they have just denied the request.
