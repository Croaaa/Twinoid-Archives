TradeFederation

Grading up gets you a skill of your choice permanently, you also get a large number of twinoid points in the game. Resetting your level also gains you several more subtle advantages.


You get to ditch any skills that sucked. The descriptions of some skills are erroneous, sometimes your level-up choices are terribad, and some skills are just not that useful. Its especially important to ditch active skills that you don't use, because they can cause you to lose turns by not having an effective attack. My playstyle has also changed during my internship, so I was happy to ditch otherwise ok SC restoring attacks that did no damage. Now that I better know the game, I'd much rather beat the class faster for more XP and chug a Ferrier, that waste turns using non-damaging healing attacks like Life Transfer or Language assistant, because I earn enough budget to cover all the incoming damage with Farriers alone, and the closer I get to the end of the class, the more likely a critical status attack could cause me to not be able to complete the class at all.


Another thing you gain is several more fast chances to get a skill you like as you re-level up. I was already able to get several skills I was never offered on my intern year, and I'm looking forward to testing certain skills I've never had, but that others have raved about, like Exercise and Educational Flick.
