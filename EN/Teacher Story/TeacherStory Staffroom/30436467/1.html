Coro, if you'll recall a few months ago, I did mention I'd rather have the tokens to play my three daily classes a day at my leisure, but I also said that when the cost to skip the timer was a static 50. Under that model, given tokens to play the three games and then letting people pay 50 for each additional game the same day would have been a better model. Now that the cost to skip the timer can be reduced, it is possible to gain more games for virtually no cost, and using in-game money, with a little bit of skill, luck and patience, though I will concede that this model does reward skilled players more.

Way I see it, if you're wanting tokens, it's because you aren't willing to buy off the timer. If you aren't willing to buy off the timer you probably aren't willing to purchase budget. If you aren't willing to purchase budget then you aren't making MT money. From a business standpoint, tokens aren't profitable.

Way I see it, if you're skilled enough to get the cost of the timer down, then you'll be more willing to buy it off. If you're more willing to buy off the timer then you're more willing to purchase budget to do so, because you have some control on just how quickly your budget is spent. And since you're more willing to purchase budget, you're helping to make MT money. From a business standpoint, the timer is profitable.

Would it be possible to have both tokens AND purchasable classes? To use tokens when the budget cost is high (and you have tokens available) and use budget when the budget cost is low? Might work if the only thing reducing the cost is how quickly you beat a class, but how often do even skilled players get the class beaten with 8 or more turns remaining (thus getting maximum reduction at class end)? I think even the most skilled players are still going to end up needing to wait a few hours for the cost to come down to the cheapest 10 to skip. But if we have tokens to skip the class, what purpose would the timer serve other than to reduce the cost to skip it? Probably better to just remove the timer entirely, meaning it'll be less likely even skilled players would purchase the next class, meaning less likely budget will be purchased to make MT money. Kind of makes having both a lousier business model than just sticking with the timer and timer buy off.
