Hi everyone !

I'm gonna present you my brand new clan : Vanaheimr's Legacy
(totally unpronounceable , I know, so, you can get rid of the "r" and say "Vanaheim's Legacy"...or you can simply say VL, I won't mind )

I created this clan essentially for writers.
I could also accept players who are usually independent, if they need some help, for instance to gather dinoz at Mr Bao Bob's House.

Vanaheim's legacy is for players who like writing (stories, poems...). Wars, championships, and rankings are not our priority. We could take part in these events on members' request, but no one will be forced to participate.

My main worry will be to make sure all members have fun. For the writing part, funny and crazy writings will be as much appreciated as serious ones.
(And when I say "crazy writings", be sure I know what I'm talking about )

Anyway... I'm recruiting ! (hoping I'm not the only writer on this server )
The conditions are the following :

a correct written English (sms writing won't be accepted).

the Sidereal Feather and/or the Dinoland Tour trophy would be a plus.

No condition on gathering skills, donations or ranking.

A regular presence on the forum (at least once a week).

Send me a message with your motives. Any candidacy not accompanied by such a message will be automatically rejected.

I'm a French player, so I may have made some mistakes, but I hope you'll excuse me.

Here's the continuation of my story The Black Fountain (chapter 1) :

Ten days ago, she had received a simple envelope with a very strange seal, representing a facing black eagle, all wings spread, with a red rose on its chest. When she had opened it, a tiny key had slipped into her hand. It was made of black wrought iron, with a viper for the ring and a heron for the stem. There was a tiny ruby for each eye of the viper, and a sapphire for each eye of the heron. Meg had then read the short letter included in the envelope:

Wear that key on a gold-chain and come to the eastern edge of Nurheim Woods tonight. We need you to be part of an all-important quest. If you need a motive to come, I can assure you the mission will be well-paid.

The message was far too mysterious and she didn’t really trust the sender, but it had roused her curiosity. Plus, as a good mercenary, she had been particularly charmed by the last sentence.
It had been a long time since she had bought a new blade. Her sword was beginning to fade, with its steel darkened by the blood and the slice chipped by the blows. Her bow too, needed a makeover, especially the string she had already changed five times this year. She really needed better materials, but they were very expensive. A generous reward would be more than welcome these days.

At nightfall, Meg was waiting for her mysterious employer at the Eastern edge of Nurheim Woods, with a blade of grass flippantly pinched between her lips, but the hilt of her sword carefully kept within reach. After a few minutes, a frail black-clad woman appeared. Her face was very pale, and her golden eye glittered in the falling darkness. Meg didn’t know whether she had to fear her or laugh at her. The woman seemed so incongruous, almost comical, however, her only presence made the mercenary feel awkward.
While the newcomer was walking up to her, Meg started scrutinizing her. The strange woman looked older as she was getting closer. At first, the mercenary would have said she was in her twenties, but now she could make out light bags under her eyes, some wrinkles on her forehead, and the droopy corner of her mouth. The dimness of her skin brought out all those imperfection so that Meg considered she was in her mid-forties.
- Good evening, Meg. My name is Elvira. Her voice had risen up, soft and surprisingly clear from a so fragile body.
- You-you’re the Witch of Nurheim?
- I’d prefer “enchantress”, but let’s say Witch, I’m used to it…So yes, I’m the one people call “the Witch of Nurheim”. They fear me because ten years ago, I created a giant wild boar of 1.2 tons, which wrecked about one square mile of cereal fields…I managed to kill him with a spell, but the farmers were furious…She smiled at the thought.
