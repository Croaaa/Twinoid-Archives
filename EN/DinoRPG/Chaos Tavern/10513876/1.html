Prologue

The meeting hall of Dinoland Court was crowded and full of life. That is to say, an immense and heated discussion was taking place. Political parties were arguing with each other and debating about the issue of the following war. Yes, DWW3!! Though the Conquerors had lost the second war, they were not defeated...
“How long can we keep this up?!” one senator argued, “How long can we bribe the conquerors to prevent another major outbreak?!”
“Sooner or later, our economy will plummet!” argued another senator, “And once it does, they will attack!”
“Exactly what I said!” the first one agreed.
“I say we keep bribing them with our resources!” a third one disputed, “There’s no telling of the majesty of their power, and we do not want to face it!”
“But what if they’re bluffing?” a fourth one came into the discussion, “What if they only claim to have a majestic military force, but lack it in reality?”
“There’s no point in taking the risk!” the fifth one stated in a loud voice. He was an aged man with a plump stature, and, as many of you call him, he was Grandfather Joe! He had been a senator before, but then retired. However, after the second war, he was reelected as a Neutralist*, since no one could find a more trustworthy politician than him. He continued, “And it is, at the same token, pointless to bribe them with exhaustible resources.”
“So what is your solution?” queried the third senator, whose name was Arronway, a hard, Pacifist* of the Dinoland government who favored the bribing of the Conquerors.
“My solution is simpler than simplicity itself, but it will take substantial effort to put this solution to practice.”
“Are you saying we must summon our national heroes onto a dangerous quest?”
“If that’s what it takes to save a nation. Why mustn’t we call upon the skilled who are trained in this field?”
There were mutters in the air of Polemists* — those who agreed to the idea — and the Pacifists who oppose the idea. However, the brief debate concluded with the decision that the heroes had to be summoned unto the task.
“Then that is our solution to save Dinoland,” said Arronway with a hint of hesitation in his voice, “The mission of these following heroes I will now state is to find the weakness of the Conqueror demons and expose it. The demons are the only reason why the Conquerors are strong. And so they must be nullified.”
He read the list through quickly, but clearly. Welceon and Fangstriker were also included in the list. However, Grandfather Joe raised his hand in objection.
“Objection!”
“I thought this was your solution,” said Arronway.
“It is. But I object because you are missing one vital hero: T and his trusty wanwan, Thrust.”
“Thrust’s father was a Conqueror demon. And since he is a born-wild dino himself, there is no telling how he will react later on. He might turn against us any moment.”
“On the contrary, studies have shown that once a born-wild dino is attached to its master, it will only separate from him once at most. And if they reunite, which is true in the case of those two, then the dino will remain loyal to its master forever. Who here is in favor including T and Thrust unto the said mission?
More than half raised their hands.
“Then it is done. Let us now give our regards to these entrusted lads and lasses and their dinoz. And let us wish the best of luck to them...and the same goes — to Thrust...”

Chapter 1: The Reunion (Special thanks to Fangstriker)

I was a regular University student again, and I was glad about it! Before the war, I couldn’t wait to graduate and get a life of my own. But after the war, it taught me to appreciate the importance of studying, and I began to like it.
Just yesterday, we had a big test. The day before that, I was studying hard and reviewing everything that I had learned so that I wouldn’t forget them. And now, we were waiting for our results.
Professor Eugene passed through rows of students, handing down their worksheets with the results. I saw him give Fangstriker’s worksheet with an A+ on it. I was sure proud of my new friend! And after a few more rows, Eugene handed down my worksheet. A+! Score! Well, I obtained all that knowledge from experience in the adventure. So, in that sense, I was glad I got to travel the world.
“Hey, Fang!” I called out once the lesson was over, “Good job, man!”
“You too!” he replied.
I returned to my dorm and was greeted by Thrust and Azelle with warm meows and cuddles. I wasted no time after that and got into my homework, despite the following day was the beginning of a spring vacation. After a few hours of studying, I had gotten that homework out of the way.
Now, I thought to myself, What should I do next? Oh yea! I still have to write a letter to the korgons!
So I took a sheet of paper, grabbed a pen in my hand, and started writing:

Dear Amigo, Amigos, and Bob,
How are things? Sorry I haven’t written anything for a long time. I’ve been doing a lot of studying recently, but I guess that’s not much of an excuse, is it? What about Dian Korgsey? Here? I’m studying. What else can I say? And Thrust? He’s become a little lazy ever since DWW2* ended. In fact, he’s all cuddled up on my bed right now. I guess that’s a wanwan’s nature. I know if Dinoland gets threatened again, he will leap into action. I think you could say that’s one diehard nature of a wanwan. You can’t get them out of bed and you can’t stop them from acting, if you know what I mean.
Take care, man! All the best at the camp, there!
T
P.S. I got an A+ on my test today!

“Hey, girl. Could you send this to the Korgon Camp?”
She nodded and took off with the letter. Then I sat down at my desk, thinking of something to do this vacation when all of a sudden a stone smashed my window! It made me and the wanwans jump! Somebody had thrown the stone from outside, and he was going to pay for that window!!
But then I noticed that a letter was wrapped around the stone. It was from Amigo! Man, that was quick...

Dear senor,
Amigo here. Good job on that test! Don’t worry, Miss Korgsey has been treating us fine.
I know it’s a little too late to talk about Christmas, but Thrust hasn’t been so lazy, you know. I mean we’ve been on a quest on Christmas Eve — a demanding quest — and that should prove that Thrust still has it. He got a new skill for a present: Leafy dawn. And we korgons also got what we wanted. Miss Korgsey got a pteroz for Christmas. Boy, is she lucky. But at least she allowed us to borrow it when we need a ride. So that proves to you, senor, that she has been treating us the way we should be (Amigos hit on the head on this line. He says I’m ‘being a too proud son of a gun’...whatever that means).
See you really soon, senor,
Amigo, Amigos, y Bob

Really soon indeed. I looked out the window...and there they were with Skora!
“Amigo!”
“Senor! How nice to see you!”
I could see a bump on Amigo’s head form Amigos’ blow. But in spite of that, all three of them were happy.
“How did you get here?” I asked.
“You wouldn’t guess even if I gave you a million years.”
“Did you fly by Dian’s pteroz she got for Christmas?”
“O_o Man, this guy is a shaman!”
“You said it in your letter.”
“Oh...so I did... Si, if I had a purpose in life, it would be flying by pteroz. Hey, catcha later, babeh!”
A pteroz behind them shrieked before taking to the sky and heading off for the Korgon Camp. Amigo turned back to me and said, “You know, it’s your spring vacation! Why don’t we, you, the wanwans, Fang, Welceon, and everybody else go out for some spicy merguez pizza, no?”
I couldn’t resist, “Sure! After you fix my window!”

Chapter 2: Called to Duty (Special thanks to Killer60 and Fangstriker)

We had a super time at Angelucci’s Pizza Haven! It was me, Thrust, Azelle, the korgons, Welceon, Leafblaze, Fangstriker, Fricaya, and Skora. We ordered four large goblin merguez pizzas and ate until our stomachs could burst, and there were still some slices to spare! Angelucci’s also had a bowling alley, so we decided to play a round altogether. The winner would take the leftover pizza home while the loser would pay for everything. It was a pretty even match, but Leafblaze, with his muscular arms, had the upper hand. But I was more surprised to see the korgons rolling the ball as good as either me or Fang. They sure had some touch!
But suddenly, my new cell phone rang in the middle of the game. At the same time, Fang’s Dinopedia said, “You have voicemail,” and a glidewings flew in from the window with a letter in its mouth for Welceon.
“Hello,” I spoke into the cell.
“Dear contactee,” the voicemail played the recording, “you have been appointed as Defense Ambassador of Dinoland by the Dinoland Senate. Your job: Find and expose the weakness of the demons. Reason: Our experts predict that the Conquerors will strike back in the near future. As you know, the Dinoland government has been bribing the conquerors with exhaustible resources in order to delay another war, and now these resources are depleting. The only way to break the conquerors, we must break the demons. You have two weeks to complete your mission.”
That was it. Fangstriker’s voicemail and Welceon’s letter said the exact same thing. There was stillness in the air that followed. The only thing I knew was the thought running in my mind, the thought of the Conquerors. It seemed like an hour, although it was only a few minutes, when I stood up and told everyone that I would pay for the dinner and game. And after I took out my wallet and gave the cashier the money, we parted ways.
Although I and Fang were heading the same way, we did not say anything to each other. We kept our heads and spirits low all the way. Then we entered our dorms and shut the doors.
I sat down at my desk and tensely put my head in my hands. Thrust rubbed his head on mine to try to comfort me, but it didn’t help much.
“Thrust,” I breathed while stroking his fur, “we’re gonna have to repeat something we never wanted to repeat.”
Thrust gave me a glance into my eyes as the due response.
“Come on, how hard should that be? We did it once, so it should be easier this time,” Amigo said.
“Amigo, don’t push your luck. You’ve already eaten like 3 slices of pizza, you little korgon,” Amigos stepped up.
“Why should I care? I’m the ‘cook’s most wanted’. Angelucci’s even had korgon pizza, for Pizza’s — I mean Pete’s sake!”
“Quiet, everyone!” I growled, almost losing my temper. After a few deep breaths, I continued, “We should just all have a good night’s sleep now, and we’ll start our quest first thing in the morning.”
At that moment, I received another call. This time, it was from the wise Grandfather Joe. Or should I say...Senator Joe...maybe?
“Hello?”
“Hello, this is Joe.”
“Hey, Joe...Senator Joe, I guess?”
“Just call me as you always do. But for now I have something important to tell you. Have you received your voicemail?”
“Yea...”
“Good. Now listen carefully: do you know Senator Arronway?”
“Yea, I see him in the news every day.”
“Then you know that he’s a hardcore Pacifist senator and he was acting quite strangely today at the meeting. I’m guessing he may try to stop you. Believe me, I’ve lived long enough to see everything, and I can see through his cunning. Be carefully around him and don’t let him get in your way.”
“Yes, sir.”
And I hung up.That’s how the day ended. I changed into my pajamas and lied down on my bed with Thrust and Azelle right beside me (the korgons slept on my desk). I closed my eyes and slept the night away...despite that Amigo hadn’t yet fixed my window yet!... >.<

Chapter 3: Igor’s Dream

Day 1

My alarm clock rang at exactly 5 o’clock am. I wasted no time; I jumped out of my bed and donned my equipment — the same equipment I used our last adventure: the enduring rucksack, the leather gloves on my hands and rope around my shoulder, the buoy in the rucksack, the lighter, some spare pepper grenades, and finally, my trusty knife. I put the collar with the built-in walkie-talkie on Thrust’s neck and then tested it (my end of the receiver was my cell phone).
Now we were ready. So as not to wake up the other students of the University, we tiptoed past the dormitories and then past the professors’ dormitories. But then, we heard a disturbing noise in Igor’s dorm. It was supposed to be empty (as Igor was fired for joining forces with the Conquerors). But I was wrong...
We crept up to the door, opened it silently, and saw him. He was scavenging through the mess of papers all over his dorm. It only took a moment for him to notice us, but we advanced on him and shoved him forcefully to the wall. I took out my knife and drove it close to his neck!
“What did you come back for?!” I spoke aggressively in a low voice, but in such a way that I nearly shouted.
“Ia-ia...”
“Huh?!”
But he couldn’t answer properly.
“What’re you...looking for something for the Conquerors?! Or maybe you’re scheming to manipulate the government?”
But he pointed at his wall of pictures that all had a sort of landscape in them. I gave him some time to speak, but did not retrieve my knife.
“Totem Island!” he finally mouthed out, “Eet’s Totem Island!”
“What the heck is this fairytale?”
“Eet contains a secret...”
“What secret?”
He handed me a drawing of the strange dino. It stood on two legs and its fur the most unusual thing I had ever seen. Its face was all black and had two glowing eyes that had no pupils* or irises**.
“What is this?”
“Te deenoz I am looking for. Te kabuki.”
“Kabuki? A kabuki is a Japanese drama, you liar!”
“Niet, niet! It’s a real deeno! I swear to Bogh***!”
It was most likely that he was lying. But because of my adventurous nature, I decided to give him one more chance.
“You’re going there?” I asked.
“Da.”
“Then take me with you, or else...” I displayed my knife.
“Horosho! I’ll take you wit me!”
“Pinky swear?”
“Da! Da! Pinky swear!”
“Oh not with me — with Thrust.”
Thrust showed his pinky, which emanated lightning sparks.
“Fully grown wanwans can whip up one million volts. And that’s far more than enough to kill...”
Igor gulped. He nodded tremblingly and gave his pinky to Thrust. That done, he said, “I first need some ingredients.”
“Which?”
“Five lujidanee groupers, five peleenae leaves, five fur toofts, two basalt fleents, and one air and vater energies. You see, to get to Totem Island, you need a special aura that I can make with deese ingredients”
“We’ll find them, but what will you give us in return?”
“A kabuki egg. You’ll be te most powerful deeno master een all of Deenoland!”
“The most powerful... Wait! What’s this smooth-talking of yours?”
“Trust me, eet’s wort it!”
I gave Igor a skeptical glance, but decided to believe in him. We tied him in my rope and tugged him along for the adventure...

Day 3

“Is it ready?” I asked the professor Igor.

“Look, if you want to get to Totem Island, you have to use zees ting. Hold eet or wear eet to make your way to te island.”
