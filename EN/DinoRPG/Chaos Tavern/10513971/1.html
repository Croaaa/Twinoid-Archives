In a mystical and probably imaginary place far far away lives a Mommy. She has gathered orphans from all over the globe. She has taken them in to her own magical world where babies can live and play with each other and all different kinds of Mommy's creations. They sure have a wonderful time.

Mommy is caring and she is planning special events time to time for the babies. As events are mostly competitive so have the babies become. The whole human experience is not only about exploring the wonderful life in this universe but also about gaining and maintaining a social rank between the peers. Even if there were no Mommy to judge them, they would still notice themselves that some of them develop faster, stronger and are in fact better that way than the others. Every baby, young or old wants to feel special, loved and cared for no matter what. Or in the most simple way just to be recognized, heard or acknowledged.

Babies understand that the Mommy in fact is not perfect, she is not all powerful and there is actually a Daddy somewhere out there and an Uncle or two who maintain this whole place running. Even thou babies never meet the man in charge they have Mommy in which to trust. Mommy is the key piece in this whole mechanism. Without her, babies get confused and scared as when things go wrong there is no one other to calm them down. The whole world seems to be getting worse every passing minute without the Mommy.

Thou babies are just babies, they have a common sense of things and they are reasonable. They understand when Mommy needs some time alone away from the babies and that the babies in fact might not be the most important thing in Mommy's life. They also understand that Mommy might be new to some of the worlds different things also and she can mess up sometimes herself. And sometimes things might be just crazy out of balance for no good reason at all. There is not always someone to blame. The most important thing is to see and learn from your mistakes.

I maintain all the copyrights to this short story and every cross-references you might get involving some real events that might have happened is just your silly imagination.
