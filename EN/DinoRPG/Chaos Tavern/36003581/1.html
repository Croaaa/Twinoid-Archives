It's been a while. This post is a bit unusual, as it will be longer than my usual posts due to some explanations. It's also intended more for the new players as veterans will already know about all this stuff.

Did you know...?

What the difference is between the global new turn button and the new turn button on a dinos page?


Of course there is one obvious answer: the first affects ALL dinos (often saving you a lot of clicking) and the second only one dino (and the ones following it). But there is more. Let's take a look at it in detail:


The global new turn button gives a turn to every dino that has a pending hour glass and is active (meaning not resting) as long as it has either fought/moved or gathered or both. While giving a new turn without having fought/moved and just gathered is often suprising/annoying to new players ("Hey, I lost my turn!") it might be useful in certain rare situations. If you have gatherers in an area that is normally too dangerous for them (like Caushemesh or Nimbao) and you need ingredients from the grids there you can gather without having to fight. While the new turn button on a dinos page only appears if you have moved/fought the global new turn button can give you your next turn if you just gathered.


Now we take a look at the properties of the new turn button on a dinos page. For this button to be available a dino must be active (not resting), have a pending hourglass and have used up its last turn by moving/fighting. It does not matter if the dino has gathered or not.
Clicking on the button will give a new turn to that dino, and additionally to all dinos following it, if they also used their last turn and have an hour glass available. HOWEVER, the following dinos do NOT have to be active to get their turn! They can get it while resting! This makes the new turn button on a dinos page preferable to the global new turn button in some cases.


So how can we capitalize on this? Imagine the following scenario: close to reset you moved a group of 3 that had this and another turn saved up and got into an unexpectedly hard fight. 2 of the dinos are almost down to 0 HP and one has miraculously like 35-40% of its health remaining. You know that the 24 hours of the next day (plus the 1 or 2 hours of the current day) will not be enough time to bring back the badly hurt dinos to 50% by resting. So normally you would lose your turn on the next day, because your dinos are recovering. But if you make the dino that has much more HP left the leader of the group (with the leftover turn you have saved) he can rest up to 50% on the next day, stop resting and get a turn for itself and the other 2 dinos without needing them to stop resting. So on the day after that they will normally reach 50% health and have the current and a saved turn available.

This way you can often save an irma or two.
