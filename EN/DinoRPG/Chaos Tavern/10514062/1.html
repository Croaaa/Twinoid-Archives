HugoMVP
I used to love monopoly (probably still do, I just haven't played in years). I agree with bace, is not only luck. You can really develop negotiating skills while playing, it's not only landing on some properties and buy them, you can trade them with players, use them as leverage to pay a debt, just to try to hold on a while longer and try a comeback, etc. My favorite monopoly was stock exchange. The negotiating and strategy in that game really went to another level. It was fun to feel like a owner of part of a property that wasn't yours, or instead of fully buying a property, you could buy just half of it an it could be enough for it to be yours. It was awesome. But I think monopoly only is great if you are playing with people (preferably friends) that enjoy the game as well. Playing with people that don't really want to, or even worst, against a computer, takes the magic in monopoly away, which I believe to be interaction. Playing against a machine, that yes is pure probability. Against a person, anything can happen. I still remember the thrill of closing a deal that I don't think would be accepted when I offered it, because it favored me too much.

But... I was younger back then, I think if it was today, I would prefer a game of chess.
