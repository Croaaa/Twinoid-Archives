I just thought we could talk about SOPA in this thread. SOPA, if you haven't heard, is the "Stop Online Piracy Act." Even though it is a US proposal, if passed, it will censor the internet worldwide.

Here is the wikipedia article. http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stop_Online _Piracy_Act
(Ironically, despite wikipedia's open disapproval of the SOPA, they are being hosted by a company that supports SOPA.)

There is a push to get people to boycott the companies supporting this act. The American people really have no say in their government right now*, so the most effective thing that can be done to stop this would be to boycott the companies listed here: companies list

What effect would this have on DinoRPG/Twinoid/etc?
It seems like that under SOPA the entire website could be shut down if a user uploads a copyrighted image. Or links to a copyrighted video that someone didn't have permission to upload on youtube.
If that sounds unlikely...
Even without the law, people like Richard O'Dwyer (a British student who ran a website called TVShack- it linked copyrighted videos and did not host them), faces extradition to the US and possible jail time.


*In case you're wondering why I feel this way, another law was recently passed that violates our Bill of Rights. Something as minor as protesting can now result in indefinite detention without a trial.

Since we're not really discussing anything so far I'll play devil's advocate and try to get the ball rolling.
If people use a right for the purpose of committing an immoral and unlawful act is it always wrong to suppress this right? I don't think so. It's illegal to slander someone in the newspaper (I think) and you get penalised for doing so. Journalists don't want to get caught so they don't slander people. Thus slander(ism...ings) are indirectly censored in a public forum. Subliminal messages in advertising and porn on many TV stations are also illegal. There are no public protests about this kind of thing; no noticeable negative feedback from the public at all. Why then shouldn't the Internet (a bigger beastie of uncontrollable information than newspapers) be censored with sharper laws? Isn't it more important to give creators of great ideas credit for their inventive thoughts (so they have incentive to have other great ideas) than to just 'let boys be boys' and allow people steal things that don't belong to them?

I have no legitimate opinion on this issue but people should (at least try to) think about it.

As an artist/content creator, and someone who consumes lots of content, I'm not exactly sure where I stand on the issue.

I know for sure that the penalty does not fit the crime when we are talking about a FELONY for hosting a site that aggregates links to streaming content (ie. you are not streaming the content yourself). Should it be illegal if I stand on the corner and hand out flyers to people telling people where they can buy stolen merchandise? Even if I have no affiliation with the thiefs and have never stolen anything myself?

As a matter of clarification, people posting their own original content/songs on youtube won't have a problem with this. And people doing covers on youtube will not have to deal with labels for the most part, but song publishing companies since record labels rarely own the rights to the written song, but rather own the sound recordings. Of course this is all moot if youtube must shutdown because it cannot cull all the unauthorized uploads. And of course, supposedly, youtube doesn't have to worry because the "intent" of the site is not to host copyrighted content. Which is ridiculous! What 3rd party gets to determine the "intent" of the website.

On the plus side, this will create a ton of jobs as companies try to comply with the new statutes contained in this bill (search engines aren't allowed to list links to sites that share and service providers are not allowed let your browser reach those sites).

Actually, the more I read about this bill, it is hard to say too much about it because it seems so broad and undefined that it is hard to say exactly what is allowed and what is not allowed.
