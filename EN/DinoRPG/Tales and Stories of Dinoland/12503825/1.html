After the Great Cataclysm, Jazz Island split up, and one place was called the Underwater Dome. It is, of course, underwater. However, diving in the sea to get to the entrance could be fatal. Creatures called Scorpscubas could break in through the walls. 500 years ago, a man called the Archsage rode one inside. He cast a spell to make the walls repair themselves when broken. But he was also casting a curse. He would never leave again.
His young apprentice found him when just 600 years old. He defended the Underwater Dome from Scorpscuba attacks and was known as the Atlantean Guard. He sent a letter to his friend, Joveboze, telling him to make Scorpscuba Callers – to call tame Scorpscubas who could take young Masters to the Underwater Dome. Joveboze set up in Frish Port and learnt the language of the Frishers. However, he found he needed Scorpscuba bone to make a caller. He told Masters to fight Scorpscubas, and made them callers.

Day Two

We arrived at Mutant Falls the next day. We saw an old man and one that looked like a fish. We talked to the old man first.

‘Please... get me...’ he said.

‘A pad... of waterlilly...’ he continued.

‘From where? I asked him.

‘We seek the Underwater Dome.’ I said. ‘We have the Scorpscuba Caller.’

‘You must blow in it,’ said the guard. ‘That will attract the Scorpscuba.’

I thanked him and took out the Caller (even though I had no hands ) and raised it to my mouth. I blew a note into it. Nothing happened. I blew again. Still nothing happened. I blew as hard as I could. My face went red with the effort – a useless one.

‘Um, why isn’t this working?’ I asked the guard.

‘Because you must blow it underwater. The sounds attract the creature.’

‘OK, thanks!’

I dipped the small end into the water and blew. I heard a low sound grow larger as a Scorpscuba rushed towards us. The horn was making a sound like a foghorn and I struggled to keep it still in the water. If it lost the sound for just one second, it would go away.

It was only a couple of minutes, but it seemed like it was taking forever for the creature to come. It was soon here, though, and I climbed onto its back.

‘No,’ laughed the guard. ‘You must ride inside its mouth.’

I dived into the sea and my ears were immediately clogged with water. It rushed up my nose; it got into my eyes; it repelled me away from the mouth. I struggled to reach the mouth and the water pushed me down into the water. I hit the seabed and was so shocked I took in a breath of water. It got into my lungs and I lay down. While all this was happening, my family and the guard were sitting in the mouth, waiting for me to appear, completely oblivious to the fact I was drowning underwater...
