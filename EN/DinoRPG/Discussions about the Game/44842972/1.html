Recently, It was announced that multi-hits will be nerfed by a maximum amount of hits cap: "Multi-hits: We will put a maximum on consecutive hits, something around 8-10. If it's a too big nerf, we will rollback on that decision." Source

I will try to explain why I think this is a bad nerf, that will disballance the game more than one might think.

There are multiple ways to get a chance of multihits on your dinoz. The skills are Double hit (20%), Overload (15%) and Burning Fever (30%). For my comparison I will use three common, relevent combinations:
Multihit dino: 1.2*1.15*1.3 = 1.794. Thus 79.4% chance on multihits.
Fire dino: 1.2*1.3 = 1.56. 56% chance.
Any dino: 20% chance.

For starters I would like to mention that your perspective might have been biased. By this I mean that a high number of multihits will not go unnoticed, whereas a multihit dino hitting 1 assault will get noones attention. A 'miltihit dino' will have a 20,6% chance of not getting an additional hit, thus roughly said 1 in 5 moves, the multihit dino does only 1 hit! However, these events are overshadowed by the times it does 10+ hits.

Thats why it is important to know the Average amount of hits the Multihit, Fire and Any dinoz will do.
If multihits are uncapped this is easy to calculate since it boils down to the following formula:
A = 1 / (1 - C). In which A is the average amount of hits and C is the chance on multihits.

Multihit dino: A = 1 / (1 - 0.794) = 4.854_ average amount of hits.
Fire dino: A = 1 / (1 - 0.56) = 2.273_
Any dino: A = 1 / (1 - 0.2) = 1.25

Personally I dont think 4.854 hits on average is as much that it should be nerfed, getting all three multihit skills on your dino is not one of the most easy things. Besides that this is an assault, and will thus damage 1 opponent dino only. If you manage to get Force Control on your dino aswell (please note this is once again a different element and will thus increase the difficulty of getting all these skills on one dino) you will do 48.5 damage on average. This is not bad, but not nearly as good as certain group skills, for example KSB which potentially can do 5*30 = 150 damage. Besides that, multihits are vulnerable to passive damage.

Most importantly, setting a cap on multihits will have a different effect on the average amount of hits for these three different multihit-combination dinoz. A cap will favour Fire dinoz since the effect on their average hits is very small. Dinoz that went trough the trouble getting all three multihit skills will be affected alot harder.

ZIMBO: Although the end result is not far off, I think your calculations are wrong.
First we must make a distinction in calculating the chance on getting exactly 10 multihits, and the chance on getting 10-or more hits.

The latter number is more interesting but I will start with the chance on exacty 10 hits:
p = 1 * 0.794^(n-1) * 0.206
p = 1 * 0.794^(10-1) * 0.206 = 0.0258_ = 2.58%
p stands for chance, n for the mount of hits (sorry if I offended real mathematicians for not using the correct symbols:p)
Explanation: The chance on the first hit is guaranteed, thus 1. The chance on the 2nd to 10th hit occuring is 0.794 each time, thus 0.794^9. And finally the chance of the hitting spree stopping is 0.206.

The chance on 10-or more hits is the chance on 10 hits plus the chances on 11 hits - infinite hits. I did this in excel for up to 400 hits, but theres an easyer way:
p = 1 * 0.794^(n-1)
p = 1 * 0.794^(10-1) = 0.1254_ = 12.54%
Explanation: The cap of the multihits stopping after 10 hits is removed, so no more * 0.206.

Chances for multihit dinoz on:
10+ hits: 12.54%
20+ hits: 1.25%
22+ hits: 0.787%
40+ hits: 0.012%

Amount of times we should see 10+ hits: 1/0.1254 = Once in 7.97_ hits.
Amount of times we should see 20+ hits: Once in 80.06_ hits.
Amount of times we should see 22+ hits: Once in 127_ hits.
Amount of times we should see 40+ hits: Once in 8072.36_ hits.

It could indeed very well be that MT is working with different formulas for multihits than we know from the skill descriptions. So to answer Kayins question: MT should go back in time, and code the formulas for multihits as they described in the skill descriptions; that way a nerf is probably not needed at all!
