The issue (as I see it - so no attacks for me "being wrong" this is just an opinion)

This has nothing to do with a big player leaving a little clan to join a clan of peers. Its an argument on the shift in power and the continual lock down in the top positions.

A player will move, be it coaxed, a choice in growth, or the attempt to find that "fit". Leadership can play a part, yes. The clan as a whole and its attitude can play a part, yes. An individual player wanting can play a part, yes. People have free will and will leave. And I am sure that they all do so with heavy hearts, because you leave friends behind when you do it.

Top clans will attract top masters - thats how it is. And it makes sense. Back in Assasin's I spent ALOT of money on the first war. During the second war with DC, I spent less because I had attracted players who were of similar level - so it got cheaper all around. And during this last war, all of us did not have to spend to much (most of it went to irma's ) Playing with peers, and like minded players makes this game more affordable and enjoyable, as you are not the sole workhorse.

Does this affect competition? No not really... DC had some great battles with Dinotopia and BBC this war, (and we are looking forward to tearing apart C/D) as well as the last day defense against EC. Those were all highlights. However, even throughout the war there was interclan competitions on whose dinos could clear out defense fastest, the most flashy way, etc. We made a game, more into a game.

Having all the top ranked players in a handful of clans, it is not a bad thing. Regrettably it does in sort lock the top positions, However it does make all the lower tiers much more even and competitive.

In short - people move on, as a society we want to be with liked minded and similarly strengthen individuals. This game is no different. Players do what players will do...
