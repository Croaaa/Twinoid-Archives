Piemaster
The game seems fairly slow in the beginning, but picks up pace once you get your second dino. Once that happens, not only do you have twice as much to do each turn, but also you will be accumulating gold twice as fast so subsequent dinos will be affordable far quicker. So the thing you can do to make the game faster is to not spend any of your gold and save as fast as you can for your second dino, even though it's tempting to buy Irma potions.

People on the forum like to overwhelm newbies with advice about making collectors and Leaders and stuff early, and this is *sort of* good advice, but really I think with your first couple of dinos you should just build them for fighting (or however you like) and just get used to the game. If there's a fast-track route to quitting, it's probably having your 'best' dino being repeatedly trashed by monsters because they are fighting solo, have no useful combat skills, and you don't yet know enough about the game to know which fights to avoid with collectors and Leaders.
