Every network card has its own MAC address, and that MAC address is transparent in the network communications. So even if there's only 1 public IP per family, each computer in the LAN can essentially be differentiated by the MAC address.

Internet routing doesn't work that way. MAC addresses are not routable and are not visible in the normal course of internet traffic outside of the local network.

Of course there is nothing to stop the programmers from capturing the info with a flash program and sending it back (after all, even Microsoft saves the MAC address every time you edit an office document, which has nothing to do with networking). But it is not available in normal traffic.

You need Flash and cookies to play the game.

You can have 3 accounts per computer.

They can't be tied to IP addresses, as some people have dynamic addresses, and there could be hundreds of people who use the same address at some point.

You won't be allowed, by the software, to have a 4th account on a computer.

So, just try to make an account for everyone who wants to play, and always play on the computer you set it up on. Some people will have to use different computers if there are more than 3 people.
