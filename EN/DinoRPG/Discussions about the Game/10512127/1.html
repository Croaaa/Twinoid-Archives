Piemaster
I have recently been looking at demons to work out what kind of starting build I would look for if I wanted to buy one. It seems like all the starting configurations you could reasonably get are flawed in some way making demons start at a substantial disadvantage compred with their non-demonic cousins.

The problem, it seems, is that the AI always picks all level 1 skills before unlocking level 2. In the case of most elements this is suboptimal and leads to you having wasted skills. So the best you cn really hope for is getting most level-ups in your main element in which you probably wanted (or at least don't mind) all the level 1 skills anyway, then hope to get very lucky with the remainder. However this presents problems in itself. If you get 7 or 8 level-ups in your main element by level 10 you are probably looking to a dino who will have too much of one element come level 40. Softpigs and Gorilloz especially get forced into taking crappy skills if they get too much and respectively. Of course, you might get lucky and roll well after that, but demons are too expensive to start having to repeatedly trash them if they don't roll well.

So, for the players who have experience in such things, what kind of starting build do you look for when buying a demon? What would you consider to be a 'perfect' starting build?

Loppermann
all element distribution on every starting demon is the same (for me anyway... who knows, I might just have a freak account), so you will always start with the same number of skills in each element:
Wanwan: 1 , 2 , 1 , 3 , 1
Winks: 1 , 3 , 3 , 1

I don't have a softpig in my shop today so I don't know that one off by heart, and no one's really interested in Gorilloz so I won't bother with those

so basically, a "perfect" Wanwan would have all 3 base skills, Wrath, Agility, Mutation and the two wood paths you want to go down (if you want to choose 3, then the starting wood skills don't matter)
a perfect Winks would be all 3 and level 1 skills, Carapace and Mistral

of course, there are variations, these just seem to be the most popular choices

so really, for a Wanwan, there is no disadvantage to non-demons as there is nothing that you wouldn't pick in the build if you levelled it up yourself, and Winks only really have Intelligence that isn't that great, and that's not too bad as you can get adrenaline with it
