Wooloomooloo, I do not have fatal hit, so my experience is based on tricky hits, but I will do my best to add to the prior info.

Both tricky and fatal hit are A skills, so they are a random occurrence.

When the skill activates, the attack your dinoz makes may, unless defended against by the enemy (see prior post from Veren), cause some additional damage, from 0 to (for tricky) half the enemy's remaining or (for fatal) all the enemy's remaining . You will briefly see this additional damage show on the fight screen along with a skull image.

So:
. it may or may not activate
. when it activates it may or may not be defended against, and it may or may not work
. when it works, the amount of damage will depend on the remaining in the enemy that has been hit

I have seen up to 38 additional HP lost in a tricky hit against a green giant in Dinoland.
I think it is a good skill against the enemies but it is unpredictable.
It is a random result, like rolling dice.

good luck

In terms of pvp damage from fatal and tricky hits:

Fatal hits sometimes did up to 15 or more damage. (Dinoz jumps over victim and hits him in the back)
Tricky hits sometimes did up to 10 damage. (dinoz just hits the victim with speed)

A skull will appear showing that additional damage has been done from the skill.

I don't have the skill, but I've seen some videos of its use. Also, you can still do multi-hits whilst using the skill. So basically, those A skills are just damage buffers on your normal attack. Think of them as critical hits, but toned down.

In terms of whether to use it or not, perhaps its best to disable it to make way for more useful A skills. Keep it on for pve.
