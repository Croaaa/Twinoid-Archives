It depends on the situation and the dino. On heavy dinoz, focus is probably etter in most cases. But as I understand the skills, focus adds a flat amount of damage (depending on your level), while wrath inceases the dmg by 25%. That means wrath is only as strong as the attack that follows. So for example if a 1 dmg attack follows, it will still do 1 dmg with wrath, but it might do 5 dmg with focus. On the other hand, if a 40 dmg attack follows, with wrath it couldbe 50 dmg, with focus only 45.
I dont knowhow correct that is and how the defenses of the enemys play along with it, but i dont really think that is important, because i would never take those skills because i want them, as most level 1 skills are only chosen because of the path they unlock. They are nice to have, but for level 1 skills you have to see the path behind them rather then the skills alone.
Also i wouldn't deactivate them, as they don't block stronger E skills and it's not that much of a diffrence ...

Focus, add's 1 lightning element to the attack. Not awful but it's not "amazing"

Wrath add's +25% to the next attack, useful on attacks with a good amount of elements, not on attacks with say 0 element's,

As all the others have said, which is better from a strict damage point of view depends heavily on the dinoz.

That said, I would take Wrath on any dinoz that rolls , regardless on whether it's a main or secondary element because the path Wrath -> Warm Blood -> Burning Heart is a must on a Fire dinoz, and probably the only really good path on a dinoz with Fire as a secondary.

I personally wouldn't even consider taking Focus on any dinoz other than a Glidwings, Rocky or Wanwan (note that some may disagree with me on this), unless my dinoz had a rather comical imbalance, or if I had a very set plan for that dinoz (e.g. getting Kaos Path for Sprint on a Fire dinoz, or Gaia Path for Shock on a Gorilloz or Castivorous - both of which have a relatively low chance of success)
