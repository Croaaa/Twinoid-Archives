I have 21.

Currently rank 110 (as far as that matters ).

I don't think slow and steady "wins the race", haha, but it's certainly rewarding in its own way. Planning becomes all the more essential (not that it isn't otherwise, but with a longer time period, there's less room for error). You can't rush a build just to see if it works; you have to know for sure it will work from the start. On the bright side, you get to learn from those who went before you (e.g. which paths to take for Double Skills, what builds are (currently) best for Clan Wars). And if you decide to eventually buy gold, you're ahead of the game in terms of planning and efficiency (although you're still behind in terms of total gold earned). Still, spending the money to figure out new builds/strategies for yourself first can't be beaten in terms of competitiveness.

You absolutely cannot waste gold on potions/food, no matter how tempting, no matter how desperately you want to get that next accomplishment or level. Patience is crucial. At 17/20 Dinoz, this becomes more lax, but I'd still conserve all gold till I get a Dinoz with Merchant. At max Dinoz, all gold spent now is the same as all gold spent later; there are no further investments (e.g. additional Dinoz), just further improvements. Still, it's better to spend later with the benefits of Merchant and Cooker.

Use Irmas to heal instead of food: 900 to heal 45 (over two days) as opposed to 2000 to heal 30.

You lose out on half the content for most of your gaming experience (I'm still barely into the Forest and I started the second day of the server ), but... just pretend it's not there.

Until you discover it.

You likely won't get into any top clans, since high-level Dinoz are so essential at that level. I doubt there are more than a handful non-coding players with Dinoz above level 20. Collecting skills are still always important, though, so developing a good team of collectors is one option. If Clan Wars aren't your thing anyway, this isn't a big deal.

The French server apparently had Championships with three leagues, one being a "starter" league. I have no idea how well this worked or how it was implemented, but maybe something similar could work here.

Art design and writing talent is one way to get in, but you're one of the few that actually have one of those talents. That said, I'm sure a top clan looks at personality and participation, too. Clan wars are like mob wars; family is important.

Getting mainly collecting Dinoz--especially ones with Gather--is a great strategy regardless of what you intend to do in this game. You can get through most content with only three to five fighters. Having the rest gather throughout the year is a huge source of income.

Also, it's useful to rush a Gorilloz for Engineer and Leader early. Funnel all your excess Irmas into him and aim only for those two skills. Then freeze him once you have them and and reach the 21 Dinoz limit. You can do the same for other Universal skills.

It's good to do all the missions as quickly as possible when you're getting to learn the game and all, but for the three to five Dinoz you wish to form into your main fighting force, it's helpful to save mission for later when experience from monsters declines. This is a really long-term strategy, though, and probably best if you do buy gold. Unless you have the patience of a rock.
