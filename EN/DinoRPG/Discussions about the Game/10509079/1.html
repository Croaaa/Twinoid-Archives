yes you would only decrease 5%.

Skills you should avoid are attack skills that aren't from one of your dinoz' strongest elements. with the exception of flight, of course.

There are two ways to level up. one way is to choose one path and choose to unlock skills at every opportunity. this will make your dinoz stronger more quickly because he will have high level skills, however you may regret this later bdecause you won't bed able to haved as many skills as you would with option 2.

Option 2 is to choose several skills before choosing the unlock option, so that you unlock dseveral skills at a time. As you can imagine, unlocking six skills at one time instead of 2 is essentially leaving room for 2 more skills that you wouldn't be able to get otherwise.

What I do is use option 2 for my dinoz' main elements and option 1 for the elements my dinoz gets less often. A gorilloz will land on so many times that you could probably end up giving him every skill if you use option 2. A gorillozz will never learn every skill though, for example. So, it just makes more sense to only choose one path from the tree. For my gorilloz, I'm having him learn the path to double hit. Though looking back on it, I should have gone through the focus path to learn leafy dawn eventually. Too late now, though.

There's a 1/4 to 1/5 chance to get 2 or more lightning for a gorilloz level 10. By this point, if you don't have any lightning you should probably dismiss the chance of getting leafy dawn (still 1/3 chance with 1, but only 1/5 with 0).

If you are one of the 'lucky ones' with 2, you have a decent chance of getting 7 or more total (58%) and if you have 3 lightning your chances go up to over 75%. Most gorilloz won't get leafy dawn, but if you have one that could, it might be worth investing the early lightning skillpoints in that direction anyway.

The way I worked out the probabilities is with binomial expansion, I'm a bit rusty but I'm fairly sure they're right.
