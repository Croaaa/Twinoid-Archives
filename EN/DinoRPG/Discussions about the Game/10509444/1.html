Perception is a double-edged sword. Even with strategy skill (probably won't be a problem with analyze) your dinoz will do such things like attacking the immaterial target if there are two targets up and the second is normal.

The problem with that is that you only hit for 1 hp when you get a target out of immaterial form. That's actually the right course of action when you have a grp of dinoz, assuming the others do not have perception. However, in solo situations its rather retarded how you are risking wasting your stronger attacks just to get a monster out of immaterial form, so that he can possibly go immaterial next turn.

That being said, it is still a useful skill, you just have to be aware of its shortcomings, which doesn't make it a "must have" skill like mutation for non-water dinos.

In practice that usually means only +15 to the mutant dinoz, since you can only get to 50% by resting. ???Since it's pertinent to the topical point of choosing between Mutation or Perception:

Say your dinoz has a maximum 100 now, the starting amount and what most low level ones are accustomed to. You won't be at that HP level for long, since the normal wear of the game takes its toll on your pet's health. After a while, you'll be below 50%. At that point, the usual cap for most dinoz becomes 50% of your maximum, since that is the most you can get from resting. You'll usually only burn healing items for special fights, since they represent a drain on your inventory. The starting dinoz can recover to a level of 50 by resting; the one with Mutation, who has a maximum of 130, can recover to a level of 65. QED
