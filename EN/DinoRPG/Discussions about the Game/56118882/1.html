T-H-F+1, as a pokemon fans, I'm pretty disappointed with Pokemon Go. I live in a place where there's nothing to catch

Lol, as I just mentioned in our internal forum, it indeed grows boring quickly Finding and catching the pokemon is the only addicting part to it. Further than that, there's not much gameplay... The gym battles hardly have any strategy (only selecting the pokemon), compared to the normal games.

But ehm, as for DRPG, 2 reasons I haven't quit yet:
1) I still have a goal that I didn't reach. From the start I wanted to make a raptor team (which has now shifted to 6 Sirains) and since some years I wanted to make a dragon (huge Moueffe).
Getting quite close to those goals now actually After I reach them Ill see how long it's still fun, gotta give them a test drive of course.
Also gotta say I've been quite lucky with those goals. I might've quit as well if my dragon suddenly failed his build above lvl 50 and all my work was for nothing, as has happened to some other players.

2) Unlike many other mmo's, this one doesn't require much time. I've tried some RPG's, but in most of them you really have to log on every day, do the same missions over and over every day, and if you don't do that you have no chance of getting good weapons and armor and such.
I'm too busy for such games, feels like wasted time I could've put into my career instead and feels more like a chore to do every day than having fun/relaxing...
With this game, you can spend as much time as you want. If I feel like playing, I make plans, do some tests in the dojo, start a war etc.
If I don't feel like playing I just spend 5 minutes doing my turns and that's it. No pressure.

As for gaming in general, as I guess that's what you meant... Always loved gaming. Only do it a lot less nowadays because I need to spend more time on important stuff. But every once in a while it's nice to just start up oldschool Doom on a weekend and relieve some stress

10 years ago when I went to work in Japan for several years, I wanted to find a casual web browser game. I loved D&D and later MUDs and MMOs but I wouldn't have anything but my work laptop and probably not much time. I found dinoparc to be appealing because in the beginning it didn't take much time or require frequent logins. Dinorpg was a big upgrade and even though I'm back in the US, I still love having something that you can spend little time in but make progress and have a community to share things with. I guess in the end I like having my dino pixels that I've invested in over the years every bit as much as I liked hanging onto my old D&D characters that I shared many adventures with.
