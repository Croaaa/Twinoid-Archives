The new COTC was unfair because sometimes the pairings were awful at best. For example PL vs C&D (in its former glory, not currently). However, it was quite balanced. For example ROOT vs UL, a fair fight.

With the old COTC, well, we only had 3 leagues. The problem with just 3 leagues was that only the toughest clans were in the top league, while only 16 clans were in the middle league and the rest (even clans like Voodoo who were and perhaps are still stronger than UL) entered into the little league. Sure, the leagues contained level limits, which provides some consistency and fairness, but this poses 2 problems:
Internal factors such as high clan variability in strengths mean that either the strongest must participate with their undeveloped dinoz or the weaker members can't play.
inter-clan factors, concerning the mixture of strong and weak clans in the bottom league, meant that it was quite likely for say, Voodoo, to play against a clan so weak that it barely fields 15 dinoz. Does this sound like a fair system to you?

Other problems with the old system involve waiting 1 hour instead for 30 minutes for a battle turn and the chance of a bye. Worse still, the old system was rather cruel with the locations of the battles (e.g. BAH and the islands), whilst the new system only kept the battle locations within the dinotown kingdom.

In conclusion, arguing which COTC system is better is running along the lines of Winston Churchill's quote: "it has been said that democracy is the worst form of government, except the others that have been tried" (something along those lines). Basically, both systems have pretty bad elements. So I reckon a combination of the new COTC's battle structure (30 minutes per battle move; battle locations within dinotown kingdom) with the old COTC's league system. Venar made a good point earlier that the rewards in the top league must be very decent. The only problem is: how decent?
