Hi all.
First of all, explaining the title: I know you can sell, freeze and sacrifice the dinoz. I'm not asking about these options. I instead would like to discuss the possibility to add a "dismiss dinoz" button to just let the dino run free: I'd like to have some feedback to know if bothering Phantom with it is worth the time or if I'm the only one wanting this change.

Briefly, here is why I think this could be a welcome addition and why it wouldn't make other options less important:

- freeze: a frozen dino will still grant U skills to other dinoz, and you can recover frozen dinoz for different purposes; one could give all the U skills to to-be-frozen dinoz and avoid wasting level ups on his "battle" dinoz, or just freeze a bunch of dinoz who will be used later on; on the other hand, freezing a dino needs a bit of work, and isn't an easily reachable goal for "beginner" masters (for instance, I play since June 10th and still I can't freeze my dinoz);

- sell: selling a dinoz grants some income, but obviously one must be interested in that particular dino to buy it so it's not always certain that the seller will get rid of the dino he doesn't want anymore;

- sacrifice: it needs tons of time in order to have the required items on the dino that must be sacrified, so isn't an easily reachable goal, similarly to freeze;

- the hypothetical dismiss button: the dismissed dino won't grant U skills, the master won't gain money, tickets or demon tickets so the other options would still be more appealing; on the other hand, new masters will be able to get rid of "screwed up" dinoz faster, thus allowing them to have more fun with new dinoz, and "old" masters won't be forced to use Irmas or a lot of turns to freeze/sacrifice the "screwed up" dinoz.

For example, my level 4 Sirain got 1, 1 and 1 level ups. He's still at a low level, so he could also turn out good, but if he continues on that road it will be literally a pain for me to get rid of him: I guess nobody would buy him, and freezing/sacrificing him would mean I'd be stuck with him for a long time, while I could use his slot for another, new dinoz (since, by the time he will be at an enough high level to decide if he's screwed up or not, I guess I'll have 18 dinoz).

Apart from my example, I go back to my initial question: would an option like "dismiss" be useless for most of you, or could taking this to the admins' attention be worth the time?

Thanks in advance and sorry for the long post.
