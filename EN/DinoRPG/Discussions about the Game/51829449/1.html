I had doubts, but I think this format is far more superior to the previous long matches. Its true, the time of the fights are rather inconvinent sometimes (if you are in Europe, and the match isn't on the weekend then you either miss work or sleep for work if you want to participate), but you can't find perfect time for this either way in an adult life. It has many upsides though:
+a match is 8 hours. Period. You don't need to stay alert for 24-40 hours
+you can most likely around computer / be online for 8 hours. With longer matches there's that annoying feeling when you finally sleep / go out somewhere and your team would need you, the enemy attacks, etc.
+speaking of witch, its much more easier to find the necessary players for a movement. In the previous championships it was common that players were online randomly at separate times. Since its much faster paced, players tend to be online more.
+Its at least four times harder to coordinate. I may be a masochist, but because the matches are shorter its still less wearing for the leaders. The stress is the same, you need to focus more but its also much shorter.
+The faster AP and points gain gives much more possibilities in movements.
+Because the lower summa AP that can be gathered in a match is much lower, tactics plays more important parts, and in 2-3 hours the first blood is guaranteed.

I had a few more in mind, but I think these are more than enough reason to give some love to the new system. Many upsides, and the downsides could be solved by simply making it a quarter-year long event where all matches are at the weekends. The two sides could have 5 days to negotiate which start time would be the best for them, if there would be no consensus then a blind vote and the average would be the start time.

Just my thoughts. Sorry for the terrible grammar.
