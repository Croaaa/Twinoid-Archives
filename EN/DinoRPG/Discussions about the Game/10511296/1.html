Here's my rating:

Pros:
Nice variety of skills
Nice variety of dinoz.
Group fights.
Nice variety of enemies. Some challenging which is good.
Bosses (might need more)
Lots of ways to progress in this game.
Clan communities
Clan wars and the fierce competition.
Lots of fun generally, of course.
Turn system prevents game addiction, unless you spend real money.
Strategies
Make some good friends.

Cons:
might need to be more rewarding, especially in clan defence. Averagely, after level 10, you only gain 10 a day, unless you group your dinoz.
It takes a month to level up after level 20 without irmas on the side and including missions. Imagine up to level 30 how long it will take!
Even though parts of the randomness is controlled, the game is still very random, such as a record number of multi-hits or spamming skills such as vines activates 3 times in a row.
Having to develop a patience in this game (not really a con, but it is if you aren't patient).
Game is more in favour of real money players. Sure, they support Dinorpg by doing this, by to players like me, we feel looked down upon.
Real money players become easily addicted: "why stop now? You already spent $50 in coins. Keep going!"
Game can be slightly addictive to non-real money players too.
Some special dinoz can only be accessed to real money players.
Weaker clans have to tolerate the firm fists of big clans. Divisions are needed.
Scale!!! (this con can be ignored once I see the solution to this...ridiculous thing )
General glitches such as the summons lag (e.g. call a korgon, wait about 15 seconds for the battle to continue)

Overall, the game deserves an 8.5 out of 10. If I was a real money player, I would give it a 9.5.

I would say about 4/10, though I haven't found any better long term online games so meh
12agnar0kian pretty much shares my feelings about this game. It's just a typical pay-to-play online game. It's well made, I give you that and pleasing enought for me to do my turn every day.

Personally I would not pay for an all around bugged system with serious balance issues and a grind scale worse than in Final Fantasy Online but I, in no way, try to blame the ones that do pay for this.

Maybe it's just the nature of this game, everything is seemingly open ended: the wars, the superdom fights, anyone can (or must) challenge anyone. That in a way is the charm of this game for me, but like in any "free"-MMO that puts gives the upperhand to those who pay, a lot (in the case of DinoRPG).

That is basically my main gripe with this game, in a lot of the free internet MMOs like Silkroad, Shiva or Shin Megami Tensei Online the people whom decide to pay have an advantage, that is reasonable and I have no gripe with that but in DinoRPG the gap between the ones who pay and the ones who don't is unbelievable.

It might just be that this game, like a lot of Motion Twins products, exploits the primal human need to be better than his/her rivals. Because in reality this game does not require any real thought process if you are willing to pay, for 200 euros spent each month you'll be in a top clan.

You all might just skip my reply as I'm not a memeber of a Top 30 clan. Hell, I don't even have a Rocky trophy but I still think my point remains valid.
