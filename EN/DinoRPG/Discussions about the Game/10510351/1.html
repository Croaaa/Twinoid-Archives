I wish to add to that and clarify a few things...

In English, if a number ends with a 1, then the suffix would be, "st"... 251st.

In the Actions menu, after having taken a turn, is Madam Irma's Potion.

Your Dinoz has already its turn for today....

Should be any of:
"Your Dinoz already had its turn"
"Your Dinoz has already had its turn"
"Your Dinoz has had its turn"
"Your Dinoz has had its turn already"
Other ways of phrasing it, but mainly "had" as the past tense of "has" as the turn is gone.
"Has had" as the present perfect tense indicates, more clearly, that the turn is gone now.
"Had had" is past perfect, you don't want that.

The last choice, that ended with already, requires dropping "for today" (or putting "already" after that). Dropping it would make things less confusing for new players, with the new quick-start rules. There's a countdown timer right there anyway to tell players when the next turn is.
