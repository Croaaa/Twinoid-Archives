I've been wondering that myself for a while. I do not believe that stronger (higher tier) skills take precedence. But I also don't think that weaker skills replace stronger ones.

I believe that it looks more like this. A game makess a roll each turn (say 1-100 for example). Now at level one when you have no E skills no matter what you roll the result is nothing. Now say you start getting skills and you have focus and wrath. Let's assume that wrath has a 20% proc rate and focus has 40% proc rate (the numbers are made up, but are somewhat realistic). It will assign focus to numbers 1-40 and wrath to numbers 40-60. if it rolls between 61 and 100 nothing happens.

Also, this has got to be more dynamic most likely, as in the numbers aren't static, cause if you accumulate enough skills you will run out of numbers on a 1-100 scale. So I wouldn't be surprized if the game added all probability numbers together, including the probability for no event and made 1-150, 1-200 rolls for when you have too many skills to guarantee at least one of them triggering every turn on a 1-100 scale.

Translated French Server Avis Praeda clan page

This one clan page has responses from the developer about both the questions i have posted about today.

The bit that's relevant to this thread:

And attack each event has a trigger and a priority (due to its power).

To determine which attack is made, we take each attack in order of decreasing priority and there is a draw from its trigger percentage.

If it does not trigger, we go to the next. If you reach the end of the list of attacks available, DINOZE then performs a normal assault.

This means in particular that a weak attack does not decrease the probability of occurrence of a strong attack, because it can be triggered only if the powerful attack did not activate earlier.

Ditto for the events.

Warp

So there you have it...assuming your ideas of "weak" and "powerful" are the same as the developer's, you aren't penalized by having weaker E or A skills supplant powerful ones.

It does make me wonder how healing vs. summoning vs. attack buffs are prioritized, though.
