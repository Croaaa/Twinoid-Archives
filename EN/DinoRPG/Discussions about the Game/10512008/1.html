Confirmed: A Dinoz with both Concentration and Without Mercy will switch targets if a Korgon or Clone enters combat.

Confirmed: Such a Dinoz will go after the lowest Dinoz, not the one with the least percentage of health.

Test1: 230 + 130 + 230
Dinoz always targeted 130

Test2: 130 + 130 + 130
Dinoz randomly targeted 130

Test3: 170 + 100 + 130
Dinoz targeted 100
Other allied Dinoz damaged 170 to a lower % than 130
Dinoz killed 100 and switched to 130 not 170
: http://img689.imageshack.us/img689/8329/ wmtest3.jpg

Notes: Without Mercy is a Rank 3 skill while Concentration is a Rank 2 skill. I believe Without Mercy simply overrides Concentration much the same way higher-ranked A and E skills override lower-ranked ones.

Source: Dojo; several 3x3 battles; a Dinoz with both skills

Sanjar
Glad to help, DarkShogun.

Soag, yes, after the 100 Dinoz was killed the 130 Dinoz had less remaining than the 170 Dinoz. The 130 Dinoz was targeted despite the 170 Dinoz having a lower percentage of health.

And yes, the Dinoz will constantly monitor for the lowest Dinoz and go after it, regardless of whatever Dinoz, Clone or Korg it has targeted before. Basic rules still apply, such as no targeting of Flying or Immaterial Dinoz (even if they have the lowest ). Gorilloz Spirits were never immediately targeted in any of the tests, but the Dinoz did go after them once Immaterial was knocked off. I assume this restriction would be overridden if the Dinoz has Jump or Perception (or is currently on an Air Assault) but I didn't test this for WM+Concentration since that's standard targeting for all Dinoz.

WM+C targeting is exactly the same as WM targeting by itself.

For a Winks or Cloudoz, it's possible to skip Concentration and gain the important benefits of that skill using Without Mercy alone. Lightning Dance would still unload entirely on one enemy, for example.

The only problem are the Korgs/Clones (for which there is an elegant solution ).
