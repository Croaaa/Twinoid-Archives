Having just bought a second dinoz, I think I worked out the optimum dinoz strategy.

First few dinoz - focus on dinoz with concentrated skills, especially the ones with concentrated levelling up skills which you can find out here.
http://en.dinorpg.com/clan/2?page=257
The reason for this is that you want your first attack to be super potent to try to knock out the enemy first round to minimise damage to yourself. That way you won't have to spend too much time resting or waste on food, and can save it for buying more dinoz sooner.

I would say the optimum starting dinoz are:
Gorilloz, Softpig, Sirain, Glidwings and Moueffe.

I messed up myself on selecting a Cloudoz as my first dinoz, but selected a Moueffe as a second. My level 2 Moueffe actually has a higher first attack than my level 7 Cloudoz, and can knock out smashrooms in one hit, which my Cloudoz was not able to do until he was at a much higher level.

When selecting your second and subsequent dinoz (probably up to around the fifth), have a look at what the predominant skills of your current dinoz are, and select new dinoz that are focused on skills that you do not have. This means that when you group them later, they will be a much stronger fighting unit and will not have weak elements.

After getting three to five dinoz of varying skills, you can now use them as your main fighting force, and buy other mixed dinoz to train in the way you want - ie, distributed skills. By this time, hopefully should not be an issue and you can buy Irma potions or food to keep your new dinoz healthy.
