POTAATO
I actually like the prize structure as it is.

How do you make a solo contest in a money spending game, so that it remains attractive to the masses, and doesn't end up always being the one who is Willing to spend the most Money and has the Time to do it winning the top spot? By throwing curve ball like this

The Monster Hunt is a chance encounter game, and the only way to increase your chances is to increase the number of times you play, translating to buying more Irma than your opponents. There is no strategy, just a simple "who has the biggest wallet and willing to spend it all".

With a curve ball in the prize structure like this, it solves three problems:
1) Top spenders won't be bored of contests like this and will continue spending, and will need to second guess each other on when and how much to spend in order to end up in their prized target bracket, adding a bit of a challenge.

2) Moderate to low spenders now have a chance to either fight for the #1 spot, or play for the Top spenders' prized bracket, adding both challenge to the Top spenders, as well as giving them more value for their money.

3) While MT might or might not see a decrease in Top spenders' $$ in this contest, this will certainly help increase the number of new spenders, which is the target of every company. From their POV, they can make it up to the Top spenders who are dissatisfied in some other contests/events.
