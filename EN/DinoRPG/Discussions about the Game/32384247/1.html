The root of this issue, as several people here said, is the quick fix to a problem raised by a quick fix to another problem...

Been here for a long time, and I've seen (just from the top of my head):

My bubble builds rendered useless - I adapted by sacrificing those and building new dinoz.

My clone/heal based teams rendered useless - I adapted and even bought beer and the likes.

My level 50's builds (most of which were pre-60 rumours), so close to what I had aspired after countless, painful tries, rendered out of date - I again adapted, building from scratch for level 60 all those that I couldn't, in the least sufferable way, still deem acceptable

My level 60's builds... you guessed it, out of date with level 70 appearance (let alone not being able to go past 60 with a few brave dinoz as I explained in another thread).

The Monster Hunt become a Napo Hunt (and then open hunt for the players money), instead of an event for it's own right.

My beer/TM based dinoz... as things stand with these new items, useless...

Do not get me wrong, evolution, new magic and items, new skills... all good and welcome. Adaptation to the game's new circumstances is a part of it all as well. However, there has to be some sense of continuity. Players endure one such change here and there, but when those changes are constant it becomes depressing, like trying to run and not leaving the same place, no matter how hard you try to go forward. Imho, changes that throw away all the hard work (and/or money) to build competitive dinoz shouldn't happen. I, for one, got used to the constant planning mistakes and have little hope left that it will ever change for the better.

Don't agree that MT doesn't listen, they have listened many times in the past, acting accordingly (even if later than we, players, would have liked), but maybe they should listen (at least) to the more experienced players before hastily introducing changes, if they don't experience the game themselves.

Also, I am under the idea that the developers don't have long term plans and create things as they go along (I apologize if I'm wrong in my assumption). Sometimes, just sometimes, I find it better to stop and draft a plan, if not for the long run, at least for more than the next day/event/coding work. By structuring a plan with the next few novelties in mind, they would be able (if they wanted, obviously) to gather opinions from a group of experienced players from all servers, and have at least some preview of what the changes would introduce to the game.

My opinion and experience, as that of many other players, I am sure, is at MT's disposal for any help wanted. Would not like to keep on this descendent trajectory of motivation to return every day to the game I have been playing for years, which will end in the day I will not come back at all, in the same way it happened already to a lot of players I've known here.

Guess this has become sort of an open letter instead of a post, so it seems proper to end it with...
