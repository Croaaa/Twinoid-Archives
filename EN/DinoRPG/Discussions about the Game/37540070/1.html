So now that the 5th Cotc of the English server has ended and prizes have been given out I would like to say some things about the event. (I hope everyone got the correct things now, at least for my own clan it looks like that).

All in all I found it enjoyable, with some minor flaws. Prizes were late, but that is settled now. Compared with the other servers we got away ok regarding the bug with auto-wins in final rounds.

However there are 2 things I would suggest to be changed in future Cotcs. First of all the pairings during the group phase. Like several people already stated a round robin system would be very desirable for us, looking like the fairest deal for all. Pairings can be generated for any number of teams in a group with it and there are hundreds of examples for pairing tables all over the internet (like here: printyourbrackets.com/roundrobin).

Lets look at the 3 leagues we had:

Pro league had 8 teams in one pool with 7 rounds of matches, best 4 advancing to ko round. The same could have easily been done as round robin, 7 matches against different opponents for everyone.

Amateur league had one pool with 14 teams, 8 rounds, best 8 teams in ko round. Could have been 2 pools of 7 teams, round robin resulting in 7 rounds (6 matches, 1 bye) and best 4 of each pool advancing. That would have been 1 round and 1 or 2 matches less than we had.

Starter League had two pools with 10/11 teams, 10 rounds, best 4 of each pool advance. For round robin that could have been either 4 pools of 5/5/5/6 teams, best 2 advancing (which might be a little hard), or better 2 pools with 10/11 like it was, but with round robin getting 9 rounds/matches for the pool of 10 teams (1 less than we had) and 11 rounds and 10 matches (due to 1 bye for everyone) for the bigger pool with 11 teams. Again best 4 of each pool advance.

For each league with more than 1 pool the minimum size of pools should be 6 (with 4 teams advancing) and thereby the maximum size of pools would be 12. Except for just a single pool only multiples of 2 should be allowed as number of pools (2,4,8...) to not further complicate the structure of the ko round. Three pools for example would either lead to byes during the ko round (very undesirable) or to comparing the points of teams from all pools, which could lead to problems with fairness when having different pool sizes.

I know from a programmers point of view a mechanism that choses pairings simply by using 3 or 4 values (strength level, points, bonus points, number of repeated matches) is far more simple and probably less prone to bugs than implanting round robin tables or an own algorithm for creating them. Still I think it would be a great improvement for the event and all players. If needed the admins could just close registration for the leagues at a certain point, put in the pairings via tables manually and start the matches like 2 or 3 days later.

As I saw how long my previous post already was I decided to put the second thing I'd like to suggest in another post. It is really simple to do and was done for events before:

Suspend the joining of clans that participate in Cotc from beginning (first match) to end (handing out prizes)!

I have no problem with players going to other clans as mercenaries. There can be a lot of reasons to do it. Maybe your clan is inactive at the moment and not planning to take part. Maybe your clan registered for a league your dinos are either too strong (level wise) or to weak to participate in. Whatever it is, as long as a player stays with the clan for the whole event that is fine with me. This time changing clans was allowed during group phase and if I'm not wrong even possible during final phase/ko rounds.

Here are 3 reasons why this should not be possible in the future:

1. Trophy hunting / internal friction

2. Unblocking / teleportation

3. Pool rank manipulation


Trophy hunting / internal friction:

We all like prizes. I do as much as everyone else. Having the option to jump from a clan that did not make it into the final rounds into one that did (or maybe even into another league) can be tempting. True, this is more an ethical than a technical problem. Some people will be ok with that, others will dislike it. Just think what it could do to your clan community. Imagine a bigger, stronger clan has 3 spots open and offers you to join for better prizes (and ofc more fun and action ). How will your clan mates that are left out of the deal feel? To go one step further, what if that offer comes at the start of the event for the final round just in case you should not make it with your own clan? Will your clan mates think you gave it your best in the group phase with that kind of safety net? And if not your clan mates, maybe your opponents that have beaten you in the group phase will curse you (and maybe even secretly envy you) now that they get beaten up by the new buddies you have for that fight in the final round.
In the end every player or clan community will have to decide themselves how to handle those things, but if that option was not given by MT from the start everyone would have to decide with which clan to participate for the whole event.


Unblocking / teleportation

In the past it was a last resort to kick a player from the clan in case he moved to a blocking position and then went AWOL, because it would mean no prizes for him. If rejoining the clan is allowed - like in last Cotc - it can become a tactical resource. Not only for unblocking, but for teleportation. Imagine a dino in a cave position that just survived the last attack from an opponent with 5 HP and has too few AP to flee the next attack. Just quit the clan, rejoin, heal and a few minutes later the dino that was a sure victim appears again fully healed on the base line of the battle board.


Pool rank manipulation

Especially in larger groups it can happen that the fight among the middle ranks of a pool for a position in the final round is hard. Sometimes close to the end the next 2 matches are predifined as wins or losses (more likely losses as you are in the middle) because you know what opponents you will get. In that position it can happen that it depends on the outcome of other clans' matches if you will make the final round or not, by one of them tieing with you or overtaking you in points/bonus points. If you see that one clan of those needs to win so the other one can not endanger your position, what should stop you from joining that clan for that match and help out to get the desired result?

Or just go there for fun because you like one of the clans and hate the other one.
