Reflections:
6 weeks is too long. I think one month is a perfect time. Of course it can be as long as you want to make it, I guess, but I know there will be many clans that don't join until there is one month left. If we want everyone to start mostly at the same time, I would suggest one month.
(One month is perfect because it allows enough time for everything to settle out and ample time for clans to do wars against almost everyone they want. At the same time it is not so long that it gets too tedious).

Prizes... I think we need to work on the prize structure. First off, given the amount of clans we have here.. it is too easy to land in top 50. I saw clans with over 40 members just sitting there doing nothing... maybe 6 wars won and comfortably sitting in top 50. My suggestion is to limit prizes to top 30. Make clans fight for prizes. Also, another prize tier at 15 would be nice. There was a lot of sitting on hands amongst ranks 11-20 during the last week of war because there was no real incentive to try and go higher. If a clan conceded they would not be able to reach 10th place (or thought that the amount of effort/expenditure would be too high vs the incremental reward) there was no real incentive to fight. If there was a prize tier at 15, it is likely we would have seen more fighting amongst clans 11-20 trying to be top 15. Also, I think there should be a prize tier at 25 so there would be competition around that spot as well... rank 21 or 34... big difference in points but the same prize. Once again there is no incentive to push yourself as a clan, especially if you concede that you cannot make it to top 20.

Also as has been said before by many people, I also want to say that I think that registration will be a good idea. No new clans can be created and join in the middle of the war (don't care if they are created, but shouldn't be able to participate). If fakey clans register too, at least all the cards are on the table and we know what we are dealing with, and can try to plan accordingly.

Bring back spells (all of them) and heroics. So far I've only heard one person complain about them.

On time shield: They are too expensive to last forever. So unless it is the last day of the war, you know they will come down. All you have to do is wait for it to come down and then you can attack still. It's not that hard.

Well, the formula for reputation is 100 * ((500 + PWIN) / (500 + PLOST)) ** 0.8. If ROOT had PWIN = 4500 and PLOST = 1560 before, their reputation would've been 203. After getting 300 more PWIN points (for the victory against LOOT), the new reputation can be calculated at 212. 1500 PLOST is quite a lot though, is the equivalent of losing against 15 clans having the same points or against 5-10 clans with no (or few) points. And other such combinations.

Since this thread is about reflections though, I'm surprised nobody said anything about how bad the current scoring system is. For example (among the many flaws with the current scoring), if ROOT had fewer victories and loses, and still a reputation of 203, their victory against LOOT would've brought them a much bigger increase in reputation.
