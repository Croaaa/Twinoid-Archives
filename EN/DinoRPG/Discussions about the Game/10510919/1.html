Sanjar
Just luck and probability, of course. But if you don't want too much of a certain element in your ideal build, getting that element early on puts you at a disadvantage in terms of achieving your ideal final distribution.

For example:
Say you have a Gorilloz and you don't want it to gain more than 3 Air (for Dodge). Gorilloz have 5% chance of getting Air (or one box). If it somehow gains 2 or 3 Air in its first five levels, then there's a higher chance of going over 3 Air by the time its fully leveled. Each subsequent levelup has another 5% chance for Air, and even if that's a low chance, you might still end up with a few. Those later Air levelups would be wasted since you already have all the Air you wanted.


On the other hand, getting low chance elements early on can be a stroke of luck. Sumo is a tier 3 skill that requires at least 5 Water. It gives a substantial increase to health which is great for all dinoz. Wanwans only have 5% chance for Water. If they hit Water 3 times before level 10, they have a better chance of reaching Sumo by the time they're level 35 or so.

Its all about %'s, each time you level up thiers a % chance of getting all of it.
Im not sure if this is done simply upon clicking level up, but one would assume so.
If you got three water level up's with a dino say a petroz that only has a 5% chance of getting water, those being the first 3 levels, it doesn't mean a thing, you could get another 10 before level 40, or you could get none at all, but it is probable in the next 36 level up's that 1-2 levels will be water and that should be accounted for.

After a dino has leveled up, you may find it useful to keep a record of the percentages of getting perticular elements and the probable number of them you will get before your aim'd level. Everyone should have a rough level they expect to get their dino's up too, and a plan for what elements to take reaching it... everyone planning on making a dino for a specific purpous anyhow. Keeping track of the changes of elements can enable you to modify your build bespoke to the oddities of your elemental progression. Say in the earlier example getting those 3 water level's early, depending on what path you chose new options may well open. A petroz in 40 levels should via probable nature get 2 water elements. But you have had the fortune/misfortune of getting 3 so early. You still have the probability to get 2 more. So now a petroz can obtain Sumo if you took the mutation path or unlock bubble if you took the water cannon path so now you would in this case make sure you took the needed air levels to unlock bubble.

A good dino master will use the random misfortunes or "bad leveling" and turn them in fortutious opitunities.
