calorifere
While they're definitely not the best at PVP, they are definitely a big help at leveling PVPers. Once you start getting Kaktuz in the steppes, a poison-dealing Santaz will be a huge help to avoid damage on the other dinoz in your group.

A good (albeit strange) leveling strategy is to 'slow down' your progression so that you don't take the slow down skills for a long time. For example, when I leveled my santaz, I rushed the Black Hole, Specialist and Life Breath paths, but did not touch at all the Master Levitator or Fetid Breath paths until I had gotten everything in the other paths (note, you may want to choose different paths, I probably will for my other Santaz). I'm now working on the Fedit Breath path, then I will take the Master Levitator. So I have to take a lot of 'unnecessary' unlocks, and as a result, I now have a level 35 Santaz that will not have to take any slow down skills before level 44 (and that, only if he gets nothing but air until then).

SoulCollectr
I'll share my opinion after having leveled 5 to 40+.

They are my number 1 candidate for harvesting plants, especially in the forest.
They make the best leveling companions for your main PvP'ers in the Steppes.
They work pretty well as leaders for a 5 dino team. Sure a Demon Wanwan would make the greatest leader but I would personally choose a Santaz, if getting a Demon Wanwan is not an option.

I agree on most of what has been posted so far. Having a nice chunk of to go with your is definitely ideal. Having Sumo is obviously preferred but know sometimes Santaz can be stubborn. I have 1 level 41 that I sacrificed because he only got 2 water level ups.

With Vivacious Wind, Acrobat, and Celerity they get a pretty decent speed boost. Sure they wont be able to get the first attack in PvP but they will be able to get in some good attacks after the start, especially if grouped with 1 or 2 dinoz with Electrolysis.

I would say Earth Tremor is a must, truly a great skill. It also has a pretty decent trigger rate.

As it was mentioned, you need to plan ahead and "waste" some unlocks so you're not forced down the Awakening path. It actually only takes 27 level ups to get all the skills until going down Awakening, well 29 if you use to unlock and take Earth Tremor, my preferred method. Two of my Santaz already have 27 but I still have some skills to take just in case. I've saved Stealth, Attack Plunge, unlock then Master Levitation for last. Depending on what you want to do with it, you can rush to Sharp Eye/Fetid Breath, then proceed as normal. Well saving the skills I mentioned before for last. That stretches out to 31 level ups, 33 with Earth Tremor. You can adjust and use more unlocks if it looks like you're getting too much early on.

Wow, that was a long post. Hope it helps out someone.
