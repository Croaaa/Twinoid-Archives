I know that not all dinos hit the average exactly. But using a binomial distribution you can work out the exact probability of getting each number of each specific element.

For example, a level 21 Casti will have got, on average, 5 level ups. However, the exact probabilities of getting x level ups are as follows:

0 - 0.32%
1 - 2.1%
2 - 6.7%
3 - 13%
4 - 19%
5 - 20%
6 - 17%
7 - 11%
8 - 6.1%
9 - 2.7%
10 - 1.0%
>10 - 0.4%

Surely this is far more useful than a bunch of anecdotal evidence, especially as the data would be biased.

That's okay. It takes a while to work out binomial distributions with a calculator, but fortunately in this day and age, we have websites to do it for us

http://stattrek.com/Tables/Binomial.aspx

The top box 'probability of success in a single trial' in this instance means the odds of getting a certain element on one level up. The second box is how many level-ups you want to measure over and the third is the number of that element you want to measure the probability of.

That's quite confusing so let's use an example. You have a Casti and are interested in how many it will get by level 30. Casti's have a 40% chance of getting each update, so put 0.4 in the top box. Level 30 comes after 29 level-ups so put 29 in the second box. Now, by plugging various numbers in the 3rd box we can see the probability of getting exactly, more, or less than a certain number of level ups in that time. For example, put in 10 and hit calculate and you will see that at level 30 a casti has:

- A 21.5% chance of getting less than 10
- A 65.7% chance of getting more than 10
- A 12.8% chance of getting exactly 10
