Sanjar
I agree with Druid and Rex. Balance is hard to achieve in this game.

Scale can be used strategically in many ways besides the lone-10-suicide dinoz. It can be used very intelligently and effectively. However, due to the nature of the item it becomes disagreeable.

For the most part, DinoRPG is about controlling the randomness of the game through planning, enduring and pouring as much resources into ones dinoz as one can afford. The nature of Scale goes counter to this rule. And perhaps this is why it feels so wrong.

Scale might have also been intended as a minor counter to the paying vs. non-paying gap. Some players simply prefer not to buy gold, or would rather be conservative in how much they spend on a game. If they buy 30k every month or so, someone else is buying 100k. Players can try to "tip the scale" in their favor by getting an item like Scale, but a single dead dinoz won't matter in the long run. So the item becomes more of an annoyance than a real gameplay mechanic for smaller clans to use.

I think the real danger in this game is the runaway spending. If a clan spends a certain amount, a rival clan has to spend the same amount to keep up. But the problem is, they'll spend more to out-pace their rival. This happens back and forth enough times and you have a huge gap between those that play that game and those that don't. And it's not a happy thing for the winners in the end, either, as they've poured so much of their resources that they might not have otherwise had to. What's worse, those players are forced to keep playing, even when elements of the game become unbearable. How many threads do we see with complaints from the "paying customers"? In for a penny in for a pound, as the saying goes.

I'd like to think it doesn't have to be this way for all parties involved. Instead of a ban on Scale, perhaps a ban on the abusive structure of the game is what should be called for. (I'm only half-serious here, of course).
