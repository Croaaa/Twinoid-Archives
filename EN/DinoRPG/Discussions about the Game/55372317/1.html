Sure:
- In each fight you will earn XP with the surviving dino or dinos. Dinos that die during the fight won't get XP

- You will get a certain number of base XP for each dino depending on the kind of monster or monsters you meet and the level of your dinos

- The base XP each dino gets can be modified (increased) by certain skills and items

- The total of all your dinos modified XP is given in the fight report as a single number together with found gold and lost health points.

- In the beginning you will meet weak monsters that can appear when you reach a certain minimum level (smashroom level 1, wolf level 2, gluon level 4 and so on). The base XP those monsters give will go down if you attain higher levels with your dinos, so the highest amount given will be at their minimum level to appear

- Later on there will be areas with monsters that have no minimum level requirement. These areas can be dangerous to low level dinos for that reason. These monsters will give the most base XP at a cetrain level of your dinos and less XP the farther you are away from this optimum level, no matter if higher or lower, often much like a Gaussian bell curve. The reason you get less XP even at lower levels is that it avoids dragging low level dinos around with higher level dinos winning against monsters that they could never beat alone and thereby getting absurd amounts of XP.

- Each level you get will need more XP than the last one. The formula for this is:

X = 100 * 1,075^(CL-1)

with
X = XP for next level
CL = current level of the dino

at the end a fraction is always rounded down

- The XP modifiers that exist so far are:

Intelligence (a skill that gives the dino that has it 5% more XP from fights)

Professor (a skill that gives ALL your dinos 5% more XP from fights)

Encyclopedia (a magic item that gives 15% more XP from fights to the dino that has it equipped)

- The XP modifiers are applied by multiplication, so if you have Intelligence and Professor you will not get 10% but in fact 10,25% extra XP (rounded down).

- The base XP for certain combinations of your level and the opponents group composition follow a not predictable pattern. At least I never heard anything about anyone finding this pattern. So far all we could do is note down for example how much base XP a group of 2 wolves gives to a group of 3 level 4 dinos.

- If you use groups of dinos with different levels usually those with the higher level will gain a bit more XP than if they were grouped with dinos of their own level. By the same token the lower level dinos will get a bit less than if they were grouped with dinos of their own level. Again this seems to be a mechanism against power levelling lower level dinos together with higher level ones
