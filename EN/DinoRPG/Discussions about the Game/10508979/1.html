You don't loose the action when you click Follow, but when the leader moves or fights, all dinoz in the group will lose their action. If one dinoz in the group is resting or doesn't have an action available, that dinoz will be automatically detached from the group IF the leader moves or fights.

I suposse you're talking about the leader skill () which rises your number of active dinoz by 3 (18 is the normal, 21 with 'leader')

If you press Follow on Dino A and choose Dino B as the leader, then obviously Dino B is the leader...

If you make a lvl 3 dinoz follow a lvl 30 dinoz, your lvl 3 will be dead in the first battle. This is because the game set monsters regarding of the level, so the game will match the strongest monsters in the area that will make some sort of challenge to the lvl 30... but for the lvl 3 it will be WAY too much.

I experimented a bit with grouping Dinos of various levels:
A group of lvl7 + lvl5 Dinos get attacked by similar enemies like a group of lvl8 + lvl3 Dinos. (usually Gluon + Wolf, or Gluon +1-2 Smashrooms)

Result: The second group got a bit more , but MUCH more damage until they killed it

So really, the closer the Dinos are in levels, the safer it is
