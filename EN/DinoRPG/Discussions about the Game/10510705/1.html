There is no all-encompassing skills-guide..the one's you see are designed as guidelines only. How you build your Dinoz will depend on personal preference, intended end-game & a lot on luck, as in the luck of your rolls at each level-up. If you don't have Career Plan skill & you don't get the rolls you were hoping for, you obviously have to adjust your plan and adapt.

The best way to guarantee an at-least decent build is to decide what Skills you want to end up with at high levels, and design a plan of paths to follow in order to get there, as well as alternate paths in case you don't get the rolls you were expecting. In order to decide which skills you want to end up with, you first decide, for each particular Dinoz, exactly what the intention is.

If you want it to be a Gatherer, you must sacrifice some attack, defense and status skills. If you want it to be an Attacker, you sacrifice some Gathering, & Defense skills & focus on Area-Attacks like Meteors & Status attacks like Poison Claws. If you want it to be a Defender, you sacrifice Attack & Gathering skills and focus on skills and Status-Canceling skills.
Many players also design Dinoz specifically for Unique skills such as Leader, Merchant, Shopkeeper, Priest, Engineer & Cooker, and then freeze the Dinoz with those skills. That way you free up a spot for an attacker, gatherer or defender while still enjoying the benefits of the Unique skills, like being able to have 3 extra Dinoz from Leader or paying less for all items with Merchant.

I think Trainz meant which particular Dinoz is best for gathering. That of course would depend on what type of gathering your going for. If your looking for fish, then a Sirain or Winks are good choices. For Energies, a Glidewings, Wanwan or Rocky.For Sharp Eye, which is a must if you wanna join a good clan, Cloudoz are prolly best, although you still only get a %50 chance. Check the Skill Trees for each Element to see which Gathering Skills are possible for an element, then choose a Dinoz who's primary element matches the gathering Path. And of course, as XRobertX said, pick the fastest path that leads to the gathering skills you want , on each level-up.

Par Example:
You want to Gather Fish, you buy a Sirain or Winks, on first level-up you SELECT Perception, on 2nd level-up you UNLOCK Tricky Hits & Apprentice Fisher, on third level-up you SELECT Apprentice Fisher , on 4th level-up you UNLOCK Skilled Fisher and Marsh, on 5th level-up you SELECT Skilled Fisher, on 6th level-up you UNLOCK Master Fisher and finally on 7th level-up you SELECT Master Fisher

As you can see, it will take 7 level-up rolls that land on to get all the Fishing Skills, finally ending up with Master Fisher which allows you to search for very rare fish. All of these selections will be in the Perception path, and obviously your Dinoz will be very weak on attack and defense. So, it is a commitment, to a pure gatherer, at the expense of attack and defense, but worth it in the end depending on your particular Monetary and Ingredient needs.

Some gathering skill paths require less total level-up selections because they have less total gathering skills,but may be more difficult to attain due to lower %chance of landing on the element, like Sharp-Eye= Strategy--Gathering--Sharp eye(with 2 UNLOCK levels needed between Strat/Gather & Gather/Sharp Eye) for a total of 6 level-ups.

Happy Hunting
