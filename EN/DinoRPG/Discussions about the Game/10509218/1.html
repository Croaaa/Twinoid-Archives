say you group the 2, and go take a mission, how will it work, do u get the reward for both dinos?
Each individual dino gets their own mission. So if you want both to do fresh fish for instance, you will have to talk to grandpa joe with both of the dinoz and get that mission... then when doing the steps both dinoz will also need to individually do the step if it's an action.

can u even take a mission while in a group?
Of course, each dino gets their own set of missions and accepts them and completes them as an individual whether they are in a group or not.

or do u have to take a mission with each separate dino, then make a group out of them, and do the mission together and then validate it separately again?
You can group them all the way through, group them part way through their missions or group them just before your about to validate... each dino has it's own mission to accomplish.

the tricky mission is say wolf hunting, do you meat double wolves, or is that mission better played alone, couse it would take more time in a group(both would need to get their share of wolves, or do the kills count for both dinos)?
With wolf hunting (and other missions that require you to kill x number of monsters you have to kill that x number for each dino if they are in the group... so with wolf hunting if you had a group of 2 dinoz you would have to kill a total of 4 wolves at each location (which can be checked by viewing each dino and seeing their mission status on what it says to do next).

IMO your better off grouping your dinoz after the first few levels as you will lose less health usually and gain more gold usually, all for a trade off of slightly less xp (kinda break even if you run into multiples of the strongest monster your dinoz can fight). It may force a dino to stay in one location while the others finish their part of their mission but they are still gaining exp and making you have to heal far less (heck I stand around at the fountain of youth for days just to abuse the pearl).

It has been answered, but this would be a walkthrough:

1. Two Dinoz are at Dinotown. They form a group. Dinoz "Babe" is following Dinoz "Abe".

2. Abe moves to University. Babe follows.

5. Abe moves back to University. Babe follows.

6. Abe moves to Precipitous Hills. Babe follows.

7. Abe talks to person there. You need to switch to Babe, and have Babe talk to the person, too.

8. Abe moves back to University. Babe follows.

9. Abe moves to Dinotown. Babe follows.

10. Abe moves to Fountain. Babe follows.

11. Abe moves to Frish Port. Babe follows.

13. Abe moves to Fountain. Babe follows.

14. Abe moves to Dinotown. Babe follows.

15. Abe moves to University. Babe follows.

16. Abe moves to Precipitous Hills. Babe follows.

A few points to ponder:

If your group of Dinoz is required to kill XX number of beasts, the second Dinoz in your group will not start getting credit for killing monsters until the first Dinoz completes his quota. (i.e. *you've killed 29/30 monsters, *you fight 6 monsters, *you only get credit for 1 monster and 5 remainder don't carry over to your second Dinoz).

HOWEVER, if you fight more monsters than are required for the entire quota (you fight 6 monsters, but only need to kill 4), the remainder WILL carry over to the second Dinoz in your group.

If you are traveling in a group of 3 or more, I recommend fighting in groups of two (if you are using Irma's Potions). Group Dinoz A & B, then have the A follow C, then have C follow B. This will maximize your experience and lower the number of turns you must take to complete the required task, while still minimizing loss.

Don't forget to have each individual accept their missions, and complete each task at each location. There is nothing more frustrating than getting half-way to a distant mission location and realzing someone in your group isn't ready to move on!

If you are traveling in a group of 3 or more, I recommend fighting in groups of two (if you are using Irma's Potions). Group Dinoz A & B, then have the A follow C, then have C follow B. This will maximize your experience and lower the number of turns you must take to complete the required task, while still minimizing loss.
To clarify...

You have 3 Dinoz, and they are tasked with killing 20 Green Giants each.

Dinoz A & Dinoz B should fight together as a group and leave Dinoz C chillin' doing nothing. They will do this 10 times (2 Green Giants per skirmish). Then, Dinoz A, who has completed his quota will *follow* Dinoz C, while they fight together 10 times. Then, Dinoz C who has finished his quota will follow Dinoz B and fight 10 more times.

In total, you end up fighting 60 Green Giants, but you only used 60 Irma's Potions. If you left your people grouped together as 3, you would have used 90 Irma's Potions to kill the same number of Green Giants.

If you let each Dinoz fight on their own, it would have still taken 60 Irma's potions to kill 60 Giants, but (1) you would have lost health faster, and (2) you would have gained half as much experience.

You see, even though you gain less experience per monster when you fight as a group, you end up fighting more monsters in each skirmish, so you end up gaining MORE experience in the same number of turns, and exposed to the same number of monsters (Dinoz to monster ratio) as you would have as an individual. This is most true for groups of 2. The advantage of a group of 3, while it generally won't increase the number of monsters that you face, thereby slightly diluting the experience gain per skirmish, you will lose significantly less health, thereby saving you valuable in the long run.
