HugoMVP

I also leave my dinoz at 1hp, if you have a trio of dinoz that can hold the Dark Dinoz, even if with a few deaths every now and then, then it's a good spot for grinding.

I'd recommend having at least 2 out of 3 dinoz with high ini (hazard and reflex are fine) and 1 or 2 strong elements. Strong elements are needed because an element with 15 points does nothing to them, 20 does ok, but you need 25 or more to really hurt them.

As for dinoz without this characteristics, you have to pair them with other 2 dinoz who do or else find another grinding spot. If you have a leader (sympathetic and/or charisma), you can make bigger groups which allow you to train more dinoz without this characteristics, though obviously it's more expensive and from some of my tries, I'd say it gives less experience per dino, but on the other hand with 4/5 dinoz you are almost assured to not die.

[765481]

Braverockys are mostly the exception. (maybe other brave 1-elemtals like sirains aswell), since they have high ini (naturally) and pack quite a punch. And if the first one doesnt kill the enemy, the next lightning-skill will do fine. So if you are planning to level a braverock:just go to the extraction complex.
Other braves suffer either from their multi elemental nature (wanwans with almost "3" "main elements due to brave(which just meant to show that you got at least 9 elements of fire, that didnt went to wood/lightning to increase your elements there)) or the fact that they are slow (softpigs, moueffes, pteroz).
In the first case, like b1onic said, you need endurance to make sure your strong skills keep coming and you dont start doing 1-damage hits until your endurance or initiative runs out.
In the latter case, it all comes down to your ini. Softpigs with hazard and reflex might do well there, i also expect quetzus to be very capable of defeating the dark dinoz. But in most cases, especially if you dont have hazard and/or reflex, your firebased braves are better off grinding at the interior/rock.
