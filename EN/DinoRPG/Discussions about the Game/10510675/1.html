The strongest clans all have smart enough players to make good leveling decisions.

I think a clan's strength comes from (1) how much money their players spend on the game, and (2) how smart their leaders are at strategy... Keep in mind, my guides don't account for "gathering" Dinoz, and those are essential to a clan's success. I'm not giving away the shop... I'm just giving everyone something to think about.

I'm still holding off on Bubble a bit until we are 100% sure of how it works. It doesn't seem to work as advertised... I think its probably pretty awesome though... And yes, the Winks will probably substitute it in there somewhere.

Good point on Colossus. I haven't really considered it too deeply. Keep in mind, a typical Gorilloz will unlock every single skill on the skill tree. Other than that, I only use it on Hippoclamps and Castivorous, and also very late in the build...

This guide also assumes that you won't be doing any PvP or anything special until about level 30 or so. That's a huge assumption. Just something to keep in mind.

matej ~ You can have more than one Double Skill. Once you receive Double Skill, you'll be able to see all double skills that you have paths to.

I figured that Giant/ Colossus would be awesome on a Hippoclamp because they level up so low in all the elements... So notice the skill paths I chose for them... I have not tested this out, but really Hippoclamps are pretty worthless in battle until about level 40 anyway.

One final note:

Level ups are R-A-N-D-O-M as hell. If you follow this guide, always check in the D tab to see how you're doing compared to the probability (you can change the level number for the first chart and it tells you how many hits for each element). If you notice you're getting "ahead of schedule" on one or more elements, then play with it. Use your own creativity. I had a Dinoz with a 5% chance of hitting get it three times int he first 5 level ups. Weird, right? It never hit again though, so I kinda regret assuming it would. Maybe it will again. We'll see...

No leveling guide is perfect. What is perfect is knowing the skills inside and out, and knowing how to adjust your plans according to the strategy. That is what makes a good Dino Master - not following a chart.

Bishop X's Skillz Guide v4.0

I've updated the skillz guide documents. Now they include a tool to predict how many hits your Dinoz is likely to achieve for each element based on how level-ups it already has in each element (done by editing the "Actual" column). You can change the "target" level (in grey) and use it to modify your strategy when the distribution comes out unexpected.

There is a single sheet "P" with the predictor - you can select any race of Dinoz by using the drop down box. OR, as a bonus, I added the predictor to each skillz-guide page, already customized for that Dinoz. That way you can just copy the spreadsheet, rename it for an individual Dinoz, and use it as an individual Dinoz tracker.

[1165856]
Ardaluc is correct. I designed some of the paths a bit "funny" so that you can adjust your strategy mid-game if the Dinoz levels up unexpectedly. I was not considering the benefits of particular skills during the first 15-20 levels. Perhaps that is unfair to Dinoz masters who spend an enormous amount of time just to get to those levels and can't afford healing potions as much... but these leveling guids are teleological by design. I prioritize the *best* skills first, but only when it fits into the bigger "plan."wil

This is why I created the predictor tool. Future versions will have the predictor tool on each leveling sheet, as well as a special column for Career Plan enabled Dinoz. You can put in scenarios of a Dinoz with certain levels in each element, and the predictor will tell you (1) the expected max for each element after the adjustment, and (2) the expected max for a particular element if you use Career Plan every time you don't hit that element.
