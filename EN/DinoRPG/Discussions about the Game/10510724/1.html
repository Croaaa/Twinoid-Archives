This is a pet peeve of mine on several forums and I've noticed it starting to creep into DinoRPG too.

No, it's not people who don't use the search feature before posting a thread, annoying as this may be sometimes, it's people who are too quick to chastise people for not using the search feature, in situations where it isn't neccessarily appropriate, just because they asked the answer to something that most of us consider 'simple'.

Remember that the search feature is not a precision instrument. Just because a thread has been posted on a topic before, even fairly recently, doesn't mean that it's easy to find with search. Sometimes the words you search for will be too vague to give reliable results or sometimes the title of the old thread will just not be very revealing. Or maybe the player (especially if it's a newbie) just doesn't know enough about the game yet to know the right search terms.

We were all newbies once remember. Maybe some of us were introduced by friends who told us all we needed to know, but for the rest of us we had to muddle our way through as best we could, maybe asking questions on the forum to help us along. First impressions are vital and for many newbies, their first impression of this forum is being told off for not using the search function. On at least a few occasions recently this has been when the answer to their question hasn't been particularly easy to find with search for a newbie. A lot of these people are not going to come back to the forums, or even to the game, because it will seem like a very unwelcoming community.

I know it's annoying to read the forum and see half the threads are just newbies asking (what you consider) dumb questions. But try to show a bit of empathy eh? At least until the devs are kind enough to give us a separate newbie forum.
