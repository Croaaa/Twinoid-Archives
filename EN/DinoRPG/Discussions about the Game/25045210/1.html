we had our ideas about that:

1) no mana for hitting the wall.

Does nothing if the clans just put in small defenders. Also no mana on walls would make it possible to deny clans much mana just by removing defenders. Not so relevant at the top positions, but probably viable in the mid-low positions. Sure, they still get the 5% bounty, but nothing besides from that.

How to determine if a clan really defended or just let them farm by tweaking their def in an intelligent way? It is just not really possible from an objective standpoint. There are no parameters that really prove it 100% that I can think of.

So in the end the only solution I can think of:
make trading wins non-profit. So to say you win as much in the first win as you lose in the second. This only is the case if you don't get additional mana besides the stolen mana at the end of the war.

So a system where trading wins is not viable could look for example like this:
- something around 10% of total mana stolen
- mana stolen is split between clans that damaged the castle and proportional to the amount of castle hp you removed (remove 400 hp of 400 and you get 5%, remove 200 hp of 400 and you get 2,5% while the other involved clan gets 2,5% as well)


This could also be modified as well by:
- small %-gain of clans mana per hour as long as the clan is up (0,1% or something like that; maybe even make it dependant on the level sum of the top 40 defenders)
This way we would achieve a larger separation between bigger and smaller clans as well and make it more worthwhile for the bigger clans to actually attack other big clans (maybe even as alliance), as they will have really high mana by being up all times.

Think about it.

edit: in this system ofc trading could be used to help a smaller clan as bigger clan and after that the bigger clan regains the lost points elsewhere more easily. I guess this could be a small problem as well, but not as big as the old trading, as the big clan at least has to give away some mana without getting much back. So trading would only lead to equalizing 2 clans, but not boosting them.

My Opinions:

1. Any initiative based on an 'honour system' of not farming between the top x clans is doomed to failure. Firstly because it is very difficult to draw the line on what farming actually is, secondly because it just forces the practice underground and makes it more elaborate, thirdly because clans x+1 and x+2 could farm and end up finishing far higher than they should have and maybe costing clans x and x-1 a better prize.

2. I would be strongly against any MT imposed rules against farming that are unclear or involve 'judgement' in any way. I don't want Epoq to be forced to make an arbitrary ruling on whether a clan should be disqualified or not in a borderline case. 3. I'm sure we can all agree that the best solution is simply for the rules to be changed to make farming not worthwhile any more. Will this happen? Um... maybe, but don't count on it.

4. If the rules stay the same as last war, or change but don't really solve the farming problem, then in reality I think the choices are play and farm or just don't participate. Either would be a reasonable position I think.
