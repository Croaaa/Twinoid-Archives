I play a game, where at one point buying gold cost 1 euro/2 gold, and you could only buy 20 gold this way. If you wanted to buy more, it coasted more. A free player made around 0,5-1 gold a day with this free stuff.

Then they started changing thins: for 100 euro you got 300 gold, when promotions were around even 400 gold. The result was that the economy crashed, free players made maybe 0,1 gold a day.

The gap between free players and paying players got so huge, that free players, who weren't tied with RL meeting and such to the community (dinorpg is missing this totally) simply quit.

Keeping the balance is a very delicate thing, you must keep in mind:
- how many players you have who will buy for any price
- how many players do you have who will buy if they get more advantages
- how many players do you have who will not buy gold anyhow
If the middle class is not the majority of you players, you need a system like dinorpg has now.
