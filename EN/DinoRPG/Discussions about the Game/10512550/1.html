Having played a lot (and I do mean a lot, probably 25+) MMORPGs and browser games down the years I have come to the conclusion that in the long term the players are never happy. There will always be complaints about bugs, or the slow pace of new content, or that the players aren't listened to, or game imbalances, or cheaters etc no matter what. If the devs improve in one area, they will get praise in the short term, before players move onto the next thing on the list. Some games tend to have more vocal and demanding players, while others have more docile and accepting ones, but this seems to have little to do with the actual quality of the game.

I have no idea if any of you play Runescape, but that is probably the most stable, bug-lite, massive and progressive game I have ever seen yet the community is one of the most bitter out there. On the other hand I have played games (that I won't name) that are fundamentally flawed, but every hint of a complaint on the forum is immidiately shouted down by a chorus of fanboys.

In the long term, if players are truly happy then they will keep playing, if they are not happy then they will stop playing. After all, this is something we all do for fun. The minute I stop enjoying the time I spend on here my activity will start to drop dramatically and soon I would likely stop playing altogether. Players that rant and rave about everything under the sun, but a year later are still playing are just kidding themselves to be honest.

For the record Runescape has over 10 million active players after 10 years whereas most of the unnamed bad games are usually dying or completely dead if I check back to them several months after quitting. In the end, if the devs want to see how good a job they are doing, that's the best measure they can use.
