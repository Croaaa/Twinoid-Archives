So some things about Specialist and Overload:

Specialist:
Speed difference is 1,8% with adrenaline, 1,5% with lightning attack and 3% with both. Even if wanwans needed 0 turn time with their lightning attacks, the speed bonus would only be 6,666%.

Assault difference is ok. You can calculate it for every dino by (A B C D E are the assault damages taken from the enemy from strongest to weakest assault):
(A+B+C+D-4*D)/20 = mean damage dealt bonus / assault

So for example if the Wanwan would deal 16 damage with first element, 14 with second, 5 with third, 3 with fourth and 2 with fifth element, the mean damage bonus with Specialist would be 1,5. But ofc that is not the real bonus, as not every turn results in an assault, but ofc the Wanwans also use attacks. Thats why the perceived bonus is smaller (but that is also the case with Overload).

Overload:
Let's compare overloads damage bonus. It is 28,8% (together with double hit, compared to double hit alone). This does not include counter attacks and bonus for clones (which increase the damage bonus you practically gain from Overload). In the example from above that would mean: (16+14+5+3+2)*0,288/5 = 2,3 damage bonus per normal assault.
But it is not all good, you also take more damage from Acid Blood, Torch, Accupuncture and worst thing is: you can't counter those (except Torch, if no TM in play). So Overload should only be obtained on dinos with a large enough health pool, and it would be good if they had very strong assaults or skills that scale with assaults (counter attacking and clones).


So overall I'd say it is a matter of preference. Overload provides more damage, Specialist is a bit safer (mostly because the ratio between A-Skills and assaults will be higher with it, which leads to less Acid Blood, Torch and Accupuncture damage). Other skills have a bigger influence on the dinos performance, so I'd say both skills aren't really a must have on wanwans, but only a bonus at the end.

Actually I would say that Torch/Acid Blood etc do weaken Overload. What is important here is the ratio of Assaults to range Attack skills. If you are facing a torch dino you would much rather use your range attacks more and your basic assaults less, but Overload effectively swings the balance in the other direction by giving you more double, triple, quadruple (and so on) assaults.

On the other side of the coin, if you are facing a Bubble dino, Overload becomes better because you want the ratio to be more in favour of basic assaults.
