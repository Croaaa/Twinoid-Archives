calorifere
I'm not sure I like this idea of having it 5 on 5.

Right now, the IDT being 3 on 3 gives it a bit of a unique cachet since clan wars and superdome already tends to the 5 on 5 'market'.

Also, 3 on 3 events give a chance to 'middle spenders', since they only have to build 3 strong dinoz. Building 5 strong dinoz is much more expensive, especially since the optimal build for a team of 5 for IDT would likely be very different from the optimal build for a team of 5 for clan wars.

Finally, having teams of 5 gives a disproportionate advantage to people to have lots of napodinoz. Teams of 3 limit the number of magical items to 3, something that one might achieve in a little over a year with a decent clan. But getting 5 magic items can take much longer, especially given the price of 'higher-end' items like the friendly whistle.

I wouldn't be opposed to the occasional 5 on 5 IDT - or possibly even some level of alternance between 5 on 5 IDT and 3 on 3 IDT, but a permanent move to 5 on 5 doesn't sound too good to me.

I also do not support having 5v5 IDT fights. Here are the reasons:
Longer fights. If you think watching a 3v3 fight is long, wait until you see the 5v5 fights. They'll be as long as the Superdome fights.
Unfair advantage to stronger contestants, disadvantage to weaker contestants. Most players aren't able to field 5 strong dinoz compared to the stronger members like Andy and Rjun.
Crowded fights. Think about it: if you have a team of about 5 dinoz with any summoning skill, chances are that that team will be assisted by another 5 monsters/clones. Then combine that with the possibility of another side with the same capabilities as the former team. This will make fights longer, and worse, the fights will lag too.

My solutions:
Have 2 separate IDT competitions. One with 5v5, the other with 3v3. Prizes are higher with 5v5 or the current prizes for 3v3 IDT's are halved. This might help in separating the strongest players from the competition to some extent. This was originally Loamoat's idea.
Battle duration. If we are going for a 5v5 IDT fights, we may as well have a battle duration. It would last for about 50 battle turns, or something that makes the game go no longer than 5-6 minutes. At the end of the fight, all dinoz' will be counted from each sides. The team with the highest score wins.

Although I haven't been in an IDT tournament before, I'm planning to do so in several months time.
