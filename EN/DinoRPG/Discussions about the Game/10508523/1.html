Its easy to say "if you don't like it then don't play it." But that's kind of a weak response.

Obviously, he won't play it if he doesnt like it. However, most people will just quit quietly instead of telling you why they quit. In this way he's doing us a favor, telling us why there aren't more people playing.

If the game managers want to expand the game, this is valuable feedback on why they are losing customers.

It's not always good to please everyone, but just "writing off" feedback from players who quit is unhelpful to the game in the long term.
