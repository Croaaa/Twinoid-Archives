Not sure where to put this, but this seems to be the thread closest to a discussion thread for the current Cotc.

Lately I was a bit surprised about the battle mechanics in place right now. I have not followed it closely, but I know in the past there has been a change about attacking dinos on the base line. I guess the current situation is that it should not be possible to attack dinos on the base line. They can be drawn into a fight if dinos adjacent to them that are not on the baseline are attacked and that is perfectly ok in my book.

But it seems at the moment the dinos on the base line CAN be attacked, but they just don't fight. You might wonder where the difference is between not being attacked, and being attacked but not fighting. It is that other dinos close - that are not standing on the base line themselves - can be drawn into the initiated fight, while the dino it was initiated on is not part of that battle.

An example of that can be seen here. The Quetzu on T10 gets attacked, but does not fight. Instead the Sirain on S9 is drawn into the initiated battle and has to fight alone.

I would prefer a solution where it is impossible to attack dinos on the base line instead of just keeping them out of the battle. I think the original intention was to avoid a situation where an attacker could clobber down any new appearing reinforcements. So a clan would have a kind of recovery and comeback chance by placing dinos and then moving to next line to attack.
Just my 2 cents on that, but avoiding the initiation of a battle by an attack on a base line dino would do better than just keeping that dino out.
