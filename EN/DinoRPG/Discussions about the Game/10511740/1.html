Totoru
The only Unique skill a beginner takes advantage from is the Priest. The Merchant, the Cooker and Leader can wait, since they are only takes effect after you have 18 dinoz. You shouldn't spend your money on irmas until you have at least 18 dinoz. Engineer and Storekeeper are only important in the wars. Professor can wait until you have enough dinoz, and you have to be lucky to get it early. You also get some skills which will weaken your dinoz until you get it.

The best beginner should be a Glidwings: celerity, focus, unlock, regeneration, double hit, unlock, career plan, then go for the priest. Regeneration is extremely effetive when you have only a few dinoz.
Your next dinoz should be picked randomly. They won't be strong, but you will learn a lot about the game with them.
Your 7th-10th dinoz should be gorilloz, sirain, another glidwings and a cloudoz to get the other U skills.
From 11 to 18/21 you should train fighters and gatherers. Clean out the catacombs, then sacrifice your "learning dinoz", and get new ones.

I chose softpig first because I thought that skills were cool and I thought that the charge would be needed. It may have been a mistake, but at least I keep going rather than reset (or maybe that was because I had thought back then that you couldn't reset your account).

In terms of good starting dinoz:
moueffes/softpigs are handy for one-hit kills as soon as you get burning claws and wrath. They later become useful for counter-attacks, self control, 40+, meteors and high assault damage.
Gorilloz are good for groups, as leaders, but are very useful for defensive purposes such as carapace and endurance. Korgons and vines will help a lot as korgons will take the heat off you in damage taken and vines will immobilise your opponent for 3 turns. They're also have the chance to have the highest base speed of +33% (acrobat and celerity).
Sirains are very good dinoz if you want a mixture of attack and defence on a dinoz, but the defensive skills aren't available until you reach the level 3 skills such as sumo.
glidwings are useful dinoz if you prefer speed, multi-hits and initiative, that is, doing more attacks on an enemy before it attacks you, but the initiative boost doesn't come until you get hazard. Basically, if you want a ninja-type dinoz, a glidwings is a good choice and get flight if you wish to increase survivability of your dinoz. Flight has definitely worked for my glidwings when he had fought gluons on his own.
