LS_and_berli
First, I support having 4 leagues.

And I agree that we want to have meaningful leagues with a reasonable challenge and opportunity for all clans and members that choose to participate in this fun event.

The goal should be to have leagues that have a reasonably equal chance of having the same number of participating clans.

So I propose this, which will mean some work by Epoq and his support team behind the scenes (MT would need to use mass data analysis):
Have 4 leagues with the boundaries defined by the number of clans that have at least 25 members who each have at least 4 dinoz that will fit in those boundaries. I know that only 45 dinoz can be on the game board at a time, with a maximum of 3 per member, but this makes allowance for replacement dinoz (when some die) and for the fact that not all members will want to play.

As not all clans will participate, but participation % is unpredictable, this will at least avoid having a league with, for example, only 5 clans while another has 100 clans.

While this calculation will not be skewed by clans with less than 25 members, they will of course be able to play in whatever league they want. Odds are, of course, that they will be in the lowest league.

And as we all need a starting point, and all clans will grow and develop, there needs to be a cutoff date for the calculation. As we are now less than 2 weeks to the championship, and need time to plan and regroup, I suggest the sooner the better.

We will never get a perfect solution (I don't think there even is one) but this will increase the chance that the leagues are representative of players / clans on this server.

Stinkymeat
@Its4u:

That is the point. Once on a grid, there is really no guarantee that your 3 dinoz are going to be fighting together anyway. As dinoz die and move around, there will be holes. I actually think that the method of introduction of dinoz slowly would actually heighten the strategy needed to win. This is a championship of clans. This is not a 3 vs. 3 tournament (which could be an interesting separate event).

I think it would be great to actually have members of clans talk more openly about their dinoz builds in order to build a more quality team of complimentary players. Level 30 dinoz can vary vastly in quality depending upon build. Having a balanced effort of offense, healing, defense, and crowd control (stupid gorillaz spirits and clones) would make this tactically a lot more interesting.

More on point to the discussion:

We clearly see that the top clans would have no competition outside of themselves. In clanwars others can band together to opportunely take them down. That is not the case here. Plib by himself could march through most of even the higher level clans. A combination of having overlapping level ranges and the maximum value of dinoz on the board makes selection of dinoz a lot more critical.

Is 5 level 35s better than 6 level 29s? What about 7 level 25s vs. the 5 level 35s? If you had a set point limit of 175 (small example) this dilemma becomes important. It will again make good performing clans have to optimize team selections. Mass high-level dinoz (characteristic of many paying players) will not necessarily trump strategic selections.

I think another good way to consider level ranges is to evaluate skills. Generally, the good double skills (bubble, electrolysis, etc.) start becoming reasonably possible in a balanced dinoz in the mid-to-late 20s. Combined double skills and higher level power skills (blazing twilight, meteors, sher-kaar, etc.) doesn't start being consistent until the 30s. Sphere skills are becoming more fully developed at that time, too. Much past 35, and most of the dinoz have all the good skills of 1 or 2 skill trees. The jump off from there with max spheres, huge hps, etc. become very obvious and unbalanced vs. lower level. There are a lot of "mediocre" builds in the mid 20s to 30s range... there are almost none after that point because they have been sacrificed or frozen as the pain of leveling becomes too high to press on with bad dinoz.

As a result, I think that we should have a range not far off from what Jeno proposed via french dinorpg:

- Starter : levels 1 to 20 and a maximum sum of 675 on the playground
- Medium : levels 15 to 30 and a maximum sum of 1125
- Pro : levels 25 to 40 (35) and a maximum sum of 1575
- Elite : levels 30 to 50 and a maximum sum of 2025

That would be:
-45 level 15 dinoz in the Starter
-45 level 25 dinoz in the Medium
-45 level 35 dinoz in the Pro
-45 level 45 dinoz in the Elite.

Our ranges because of fewer high level dinoz, should probably scale downward a bit with 1800 max points for Elite (45 level 40 dinoz for max) and 1440 for Pro (45 level 32 dinoz) with the upper limit set to 35 instead of 40.

/end wall of text
