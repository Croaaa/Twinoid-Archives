I think we can distinguish between ingredients that are "fixed" for a certain grid and ingredients that only have a certain chance to appear in a grid. We can call them rare or whatever.
Forges for example always has 28 Leaves, 11 Mushrooms and 3 Orchids. So while Orchids need maximum gathering skill I would not call them rare, because there is always a fixed amount of 3.
Also if an ingredient is rare or not depends not only on the ingredient itself, but on the location, too. Fire Energy for example is fixed with an amount of 1 at Basalt Slopes. But at Nirvana Falls it only has a chance of appearence.

I see 2 possible ways how the game handles these "rare" ingredients:

1. Each square that does not contain a "fixed" ingredient is checked for separately. That would mean it is possible to find the same "rare" ingredient more than once in the same grid.

2. The whole grid is checked for rare ingredients with a certain chance for each kind. That means that more than one rare ingredient might appear, but not two of the same kind (like 2 Old Rings).

I deem the second possibility as being more probable than the first one. But as the chances of finding even one rare ingredient are usually low, finding 2 identical rare ingedients could be close to impossible.

But everyone is invited to prove me wrong.
