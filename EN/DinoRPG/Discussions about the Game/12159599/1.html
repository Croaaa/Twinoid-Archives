Masters and Mistresses!

Following much consultation and deliberation, the imminent Championship of the Clans will have 2 leagues:

The Amateur league which will allow Dinoz up to a maximum of Lvl 30.
The Pro league which will allow Dinoz of any level.

Both leagues will split into pools of 8, with 6 battles per clan. Battles will last for 48 hours. The survey vote pointed towards one action point every 30 minutes, but considering there were an overwhelming number (85%+) voting for 30, 45 and 60 minutes, we have decided on 45 minutes as not to penalize a large number of masters who are a little less available. Dinoz will be able to store a maximum of 15AP.

Prizes (hold on tight):

We have been in discussions with the King of Dinoland, and are starting to question his judgement... he appears to be becoming increasingly generous with age... as you'll see for yourselves!

PRO League:

Winner: Epic Reward + 3 Napoz + 200 notes + 150 demon tickets
Runner-Up: Epic Reward + 3 Napoz + 100 notes + 100 demon tickets
Semi-Final: Epic Reward + 3 Napoz + 50 notes
Quarter-Final: Epic Reward + 2 Napoz + 50 notes
Last 16: Epic Reward + 1 Napoz + 50 notes

AMATEUR League:

Winner: Epic Reward + 2 Napoz + 100 notes + 100 demon ticekts
Runner-Up: Epic Reward + 1 Napoz + 100 notes + 100 demon tickets
Semi-Final: Epic Reward + 1 Napoz + 100 notes
Quarter Final: Epic Reward + 1 Napoz + 50 notes
Last 16: Epic Reward + 150.000 coins

*Prizes could be altered depending on league participation figures.

If the pairings are done correctly, according to every working Swiss system I have seen, it should work as follows...

1. 1v2, 3v4, 5v6, 7v8, 9 gets a bye.

2. If at any point going through these lists you come across a repeat pairing, first clan in repeat pairing is paired against the next clan down they haven't yet fought (e.g. if 5v6 is a repeat, then 5v7) and then continue the pairings from there.

3. If you get a repeat pairing and one clan has no more opponents they can legally face, go back to the previous match, pair the next clan 1 down and repair from there. If still no legal match keep repairing the previous match until a legal arrangement is possible, if none possible go back one match further and repeat.

4. If any clan gets a bye for the second time, follow the same process as above to obtain a legal arrangement.

I have run Swiss tournaments (in the days before we had phones/tablets/wireless internet that could do this all for you) and have never once had a problem using the above system. Sure, if you have a lot of rounds and few participants, then you can have some odd-looking sets of pairings in the later rounds, but that is the nature of the beast.
