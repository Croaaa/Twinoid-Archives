Alright guys, let's talk about combat "lockdown".
The goal here is to discuss everything about it, and share ideas.
Do you enjoy lockdown situations? Do you think something should be done? Is the game mechanics just fine, or does it need balance? What are the causes? Any ideas how to correct it?


First, let's define what i mean by a combat lockdown.
LOCKDOWN happens when one side of a battle just stops fighting, and finds himself unable to battle for the rest of the fight. So basically, instead of a "battle", when lockdown happens you just see one side attacking over and over while the other side doesn't even get turns or assault anymore, and just wait to die.

How can this happen? Well usually, it's a mix of speed buffs and initiative debuffs. But it's mostly about imparing the victim, with vines or floods or marshes, etc...

How the phenomenon feeds and maintain:
Well, basically, 2 things generate this. First, there is no immunity, or diminishing return, or temporarly resistance. This is kind of strange, in most video games, you can't "chain stun" an opponent because he will develop resistance to it, and your stun will be less and less effective.
Second, in a team, the problem becomes exponential, because you get many attacks per turn. So, let's say all your 3 Dinoz have Flood, Vines, and Liquid Stump. When ONE of them cast Flood, you suddenly get an extra full round of attack or 2, meaning all your Dinoz get an extra chance to cast Flood, Vine or Stump (or petrify or hypno, or Mass Frenzy or Detonate, etc). If during the extra turn you do cast another "time-shifter", then you get more turn to cast more, which gives you more chance to cast more, etc...

This leads to Lockdown, a situation where it is impossible for the victim to "wake up" before the attacker casts another downer.

Is this a problem?

Me, i think it is. Lockdown fights are boring and frustrating. It's not even exciting for the winner. And yet, it happens almost every fight.

Solution? Let's discuss! For me, i'd either use diminishing return feedback, OR a recharge timer on these abilities (for exemple, if you cast vines you can't recast it for 3 rounds). I also don't think there should be initiative changers that affect multiple dinoz (flood, frenzy, fujin).

Ok, i wrote enough for now. Let's hear your thoughts.

I think initiative is fine by itself, since it's up to a master to get the best initiative and speed he can. Look at the final IDT 17 fight: Rjun attacked for 2 rounds before Pilaf moved, and not because of debuffs like Flood. He went first because he built faster Dinoz.

This would be fine with me: a player could decide to skip on initiative or speed and choose another strenght for his dino, like big attacks, counters, bubble, etc...

The issue is that when you add debuffing skill, then initiative become stronger. In this case, a team like Rjun not only gets 2 rounds, but during these rounds he can cast Vines and Stumps and Flood, and thus gain additional rounds and impose Lockdown. Hey, i use that strategy myself!

I think a solution would be to code in a diminishing return. When you are hit with a debuff, you have a "shield" for 3 rounds: 100% protection for 1 round, 50% for 1 round, and 25% for 1 round.

Exemple: You cast Vines. That dinoz is 100% protected next round.
Next round you hit another dinoz with Stump.
Next round you cast Flood.
The vined Dinoz is hit for 50% of Flood effect (lose 3.5 initiative)
The Stumped Dinoz is hit for 0% of Flood, no additional loss
The other Dinoz is hit for 100% and losses 7 initiative.
All shields are back to 100%.
