ZexyZap
Here we go again with the discussion regarding the thought that some skills are overpowered and need to balanced out or controlled in some way.

The last time this happened was way back in the days when all poison attacks was considered to be way to powerful and that there was to few options to prevent being poisoned. There was back then and now many solid suggestions on how to solve the power of poison that came from the community.

But as we all know MT decided to take a completely different road and released the Poisonite Injection that for a very cheap price negated all poison attacks for one round.

At the same time the Poisonite Injection was presented into the game so was the item Monochromatic and this items became very popular in clan war, cotc and dojo and soon the community demanded that a change was needed since the Monochromatic was to powerful. Once again there was a lot of good solutions presented from the community but once again MT decided that their way is always the best way. So they released the magic item Antichromatic that negates the effect of Monochromatic.

When it comes to balance things that are to powerful in any games its all about finding the middle way. What kind of balance can we do that wont make a certain skill/item useless or redundant, how can we change things so that the skill/items is still good but not overpowered. That is what game balance is about.

To throw in random solutions that totally negates a skill/item is not the way to balance the game. This is the way to ruin the game and the possibility these skills/items present
There are a couple of other changes that has been made that MT calls balance change when its not.

But this thread is about the mana war and the strengths and flaws in the system that was used and not actually about the balance of the game. Perhaps we need to create a new thread and discuss game changes that can improve the game.

Cheers all,
Rob
