zony
I agree with piemaster, selective perception makes us remember those extraordinary events more intensively than those "normal" events. If someone actually would count all doublehits and multihits he sees from one of his dinos, he would probably see 80% of his multihits are double hits. Ofc only if MT coded their rng in a good way.
But it doesn't really help to argue about it if it is that way or not - since we won't come to a conclusion until we have some numbers from fights for a empirical determination of the matter.
I know that trigger-percentages are handled different in some games. For example in Warcraft III pseudo random distribution is used: on your first attack you have a very small chance of triggering your critical (for example) but the chance gets bigger every hit, until it triggers. After that it is reset to the small chance again. This way after a specific amount of attacks the critical hit will trigger with 100% probability, preventing some very unlucky cases where it would be theoretically possible to have no criticals all game. But it also lowers the chance of to 2 or more consecutive criticals immensely. I am sure this system is not applied to DinoRPG, but I just wanted to mention, that I also know, that there is the possibility that MT coded the multihit system different as well.

Piemaster
Okay, I just did a small test of double hit. I recorded the results of a series of fights containing three different dinos (one Cloudoz, one Glidwings, one Pteroz). All of them are level 27, all have Double Hit, but none have Overload. The results of 70 normal attacks were as follows:

Singles - 48
Doubles - 18
Triples - 3
Quadrupels - 1
5 or more - 0

Obviously we can't read too much into this as it's a small sample size. The second hit seemed to land more often than it should on average (31% as opposed to 20%) but given the small sample size this doesn't really give enough evidence to say the % is wrong. There is certainly no evidence that once the second hit has landed, the 3rd and 4th follow more than they should.
