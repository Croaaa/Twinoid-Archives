Piemaster
I think you always have to be careful when discussing ways to retain new players more. Every browser game has a near perpetual discussion of how to hook more new players and make them stick around longer, but the fact is that a very low level of newbie retention is systemic of the industry. If someone finds or is sent a link for a free game they might be quick to give it a try, but even if they like the game that doesn't mean they'll stick around for long. Most people just don't have huge attention spans or are enough 'into games' to play an MMORPG.

It's *probably* true that if you made the game faster paced for newbies you would reduce the number of players that quit after the first 15 minutes. But these extra players that stick around, are they the type of players who you really *want* to retain that badly? They might still be here on Tuesday, but would they be here in a year's time, even a month's time? Are they likely to ever spend money on the game? Will they help build a community? Probably not.

I think that long-term success for a game of this type isn't about keeping the two hour player here for two weeks, it's about finding the types of players who will still be here in 6 months time and getting them to play your game and not a different one. Players of this type don't usually quit because "OMG I've been playing 2 hours and theres nothing to do!!!!" they quit because another game seems to offer more in terms of long-term fun, or their friends play a different game, or they find certain game mechanics frustrating, or there is no community etc.

Just food for thought.
