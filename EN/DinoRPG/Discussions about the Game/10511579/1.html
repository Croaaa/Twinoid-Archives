Stinkymeat
I posted this in Epoq's intro thread, but perhaps it fits better here. I want to go the opposite of "vets get all the goodies and make the barrier of entry to the top clans impossible to overcome" theme that some of these suggestions went with.

This is a general suggestion perhaps, but one that also could fit perfectly into the dynamic of encouraging new players to want to play more and perhaps consider buying gold and leveling up to provide more competition.

My suggestion is for events (like the monster hunt) that weight the advantage to newer players with lower level dinoz.

In the case of monster hunt, it would look like this:

Reward is for killing Gluons, wolves, giants, chickens.

Each animal has 3 (or more, if wanted) "parts" that would be harvested randomly after each fight. Each part (like on a wolf: the tail, teeth, pelt) would be given a point value. The most common part on a wolf (being lowest level mob in our hunt) would be worth 4 points, with each other part worth 1-2 more points than that each. Odds of getting common parts would be something like 60%, uncommon 30%, rare 10%. You would go to the market to trade in parts for contest tickets.

Each successively harder mob would have the base point for each part drop by 1, making the harder mobs give less points per kill. This is made this way knowing that people with the higher level dinoz are more likely either able to kill these mobs with much greater ease and/or are paying players with more money to attempt to overcome the penalty disparity.
Luck plays a part in all this, as it should, but the inherent advantage would be with players able to kill wolves and gluons instead of chickens or giants.

Skewing events like this could have a lot of benefits:

1) Those rewards are a lot more powerful to a lower level player and would tempt them pretty strongly to buy coins knowing that their advantage in fighting lots of wolves and having a lot of their quests happening in dinotown would give them a running start and a competitive advantage.

2) This could encourage higher level players to buy coins in order to either a) out-grind the newer players or b) freeze some dinoz, buy some more, and try and compete that way.

Either way, this kind of balance, where the rewards are conceivably possible for a newer player to attain, it could spread some of the rewards out and make the concentration at the top of the clans less than it is and encourage newer players to keep playing and consider supporting the game.

Wait, am I coming up with new ways to try and suck people into giving real money for this game? Say it ain't so...
