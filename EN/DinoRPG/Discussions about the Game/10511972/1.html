- Stange Prowler's Missions

Here's a short guide about having all useful status items:

- Rush doing Fresh Fish, Hills Smashrooms, Wolf Hunting and then the Strange Monster from Grandpa Joe's missions list. Your dinoz should be level 6 or so. You can go for the buoy while chasing the gluon (for less moves).

- Then go search for the shovel, dig at the swamp, talk to the guard at the falls, go back repair the shovel, then go to Fountain of Youth. After digging, go repair and then go on University again for climbing gear and go on Basalt Slopes to dig out.

- Return to Frish Port, talking to Jove Boze for the caller, you should be level 9 at that time and then got 100 from the Scorpscuba.

- Then go to the Dome, Earth Elemental give 100 at level 10. Then you encounter only korgons at those levels. So go get the lantern. In the meanwhile, by going on this circuit or returning of it, you can go repair the Shovel.

- Thereafter go all the way back up to Korgon Camp for the Korgon fins, at Ashpouk ruins you dig the old stone. After completing and own the fins, stop by the Soft Shaman to do the two first missions for the strategy book. Your dinoz should be level 12 at that time.

- Now you can grind your levels without doing any missions till you want to do them. Some aim for fast way up to level 30, and some people for a long way up to level 50.

HugoMVP
I remember reading some advice about spending irmas on a dino at the beginning, till somewhere aroun level 15, and then start doing missions, because this way it's easier to achieve very high levels, cause exp given is more on the low levels than on the high levels. Rushing a dino through missions can mean that around level 35-37 there is nothing else to do, so in order to level up you have to spend irmas. A dino that starts later the missions, may be able to finish everything at a much higher level.

So, it's up to choose, fast evolution with a low level at the end, or slow/risk evolution with high level at the end. I say slow/risk, because you either only use normal turns, or you waste a lot of Irmas, and the result may not be too good. Investing in a level 35 may give less xp but is safer, because you already know if the dino evolved well or not.

Personally, I adjust the dino to the situation. For example, my Kabuki only started missions around level 10. But that's really how high I have gone before starting missions. My Santaz/Softpig are now level 3 and I started the missions with them in this level.
