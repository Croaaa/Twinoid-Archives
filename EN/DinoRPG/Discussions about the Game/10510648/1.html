Siky
I don't think you can quantify how harmful allowing players to reset their skills are. The fact of the matter is that later on, people start building more cookie cutter builds anyways and this is something to address the problem of new players feeling like their first maybe 10-18 Dinoz are a waste since they probably experimented a lot with the different level ups not knowing how to efficiently use their level up points and such, but you have to realize that if it were so easy to change up your skills, people wouldn't get new Dinoz really at all. They would just stick to their 18-21 Dinoz and then spend the rest of the money on leveling those 18-21. We get rid of Dinoz now and choose to sacrifice because we have a choice of Dinoz to sacrifice, takes a lot away from the value of a Dino especially in the market. Having a good looking Dino with good skills potentially boost the value of the Dino by a great amount.

Also you can't just think of this as for newbies, you have to think of all the problems that come with having this ability like what if you got the U skill Leader and Engineer, you got 21 Dinoz and had the extra slot granted from Engineer full. What should happen if you reset that Dino? Would you bug out your account? Maybe lose your last 3 Dinoz and the last item in the combat slot that disappears? How about the strongest and richest clans just changing all of their Dinoz into gatherers in between War times just to pump up tons of ingredients? Yeah the lower leveled clans can do that too, but the stronger players will have more money and so they can get more ingredients by default.

I do not think it's worth it to have this option at all. There are plenty of options and design choices in the game to compensate for badly leveled Dinoz, why not make use of them and getting a new Dino to replace them? It's not like ever Dino looks the same either, they have randomly generated features which makes it feel different to have a new Dino rather than it just being you trying over and over to get the perfect build on the same Dino.

Initially, I did like the idea of this - it would make it easier to fix mistakes, but there are so many ways in which it could be abused, I don't think we need it.

Choosing the wrong skills is one mistake (your fault), but if you plan for a Dinoz to get lots of one element and the rolls go different to what you expect, you still get a badly levelled Dinoz (not your fault).

As said above, there are several ways to deal with these things already. I also think there is plenty of levelling advice in the forums and clan pages for those that bother to read them.

The danger is that this idea doesn't really work for new starters, but does for those that have been around for longer. Making it cost a lot is not a problem for top players, but is for new starters who should be spending money on new Dinoz, not fixing their existing ones - more Dinoz however bad means more money.
