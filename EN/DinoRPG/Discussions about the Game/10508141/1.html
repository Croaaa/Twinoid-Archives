I think turns more frequently (like 1 turn/hour) is a bad idea because most new players want to play longer once a day, not more times each day.

It would be better to give each Dinoz a turn bank, such that daily turns can accumulate, so a player can log in like once a week and play 7 turns, instead of logging in once a day and play one turn.

After a player gets a dozen or so Dinoz, the game takes a long time, and more frequent turns would just make the game nuts.

Would increasing the amount of gold players start out with be a workable solution?

Good:
-no direct changes to game mechanics
-speeds up the process either by:
--more money to save towards 2nd/3rd dino
--more money for other actions
-doesn't affect late game
-relatively easy to balance on the other servers (give all the players the adjusted amount of gold)

Bad:
-too much gold could confuse beginners
-players could still hit a slowing of game pace once they spend all their gold
-may not encourage players to save gold
-unfair to existing accounts
-discourages use of Bank
-takes away the "hard work" of early gameplay that adds value to overall gameplay experience


I like Husaurus's idea with a "turn bank"; however, I believe the developers want to encourage people to log on at least once a day. Perhaps a limit on "banked" turns could work. It still ends up making the game more complicated, though. And it is an unfair "fix" for older players who are fine with the established system.
