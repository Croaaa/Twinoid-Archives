1. If you group the older dino with the younger, it won't reset the mission for the older dino but you can use the older dino to certain missions again, like some steppes missions and the sage missions (very advanced). Depending on the mission type, it won't gain any benefit from helping the younger dino do the missions, except certain missions that offer big during fights.

2. In terms of assisting them, yes, but you won't get the same mission rewards again. There are some missions that involve fights, especially ones that give awesome and you can get a dino to assist another in those missions constantly.

Here's an example:

There is a request office mission where you have to hunt down, say, cervelah the poisoner. The mission itself contains specific fights where your group has to have an active turn together in order to fight together and not alone. the dino that is working on that mission has to lead. You can get some dinoz that have already done that mission to do these kinds of fights innumerable times by following a dino that is working on that mission, but you won't get any mission reward in the end. Only the dino(s) that hasn't done the mission will get the reward.

Overall, I'd recommend getting a group of 3 with very close levels (only about 2 level difference, preferably all 3 on same level, and even same level if possible) to do missions together, rather than have a group of dinoz on different levels.
