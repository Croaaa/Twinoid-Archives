phantom - Sunday 22 March at 03:09
A little explanation on the system and how it works.

It's easy, for each action you perform with one or many Dinoz using the "New Action" button, an action counter increases by 1 per action.

More this counter is high, higher will be the time you have to wait to play again.

So for the first one, it will be 1 hour and after a bit more each time.

The huge difference is when you have many dinoz.
For example if you use the new action for 10 Dinoz, it will be increased by 10.
So you will not see your time increasing slowly but directly by a bigger amount.

At the end it will block around one move for all Dinoz every 24 hours. Once you have passed the beginning (that should be faster this way), you will have the same amount of moves as before.

The two main advantages of this system:

- You can play more at the beginning and so enjoy this time.
- You will not be limited by the GMT+1 midnight for a new turn (that can be a bit confusing for other timezone players), but by your usual playtime. As it will be based on the moment you have used this action.

Of course, actions made by using an Irma's potion are not taken in consideration by this counter ^^.

Please remember, this new system is an experimentation and can be changed if there are problems.

And you'll have to ask Phantom what the original intent of this thread, and the other one about game future, were as well, since I think this thread was to discuss the old turn system vs. the current one, and the game future thread was to discuss currently released and soon to be released game content. There is no single official thread that is for players suggestions/feedback, where most of the discussions here would probably fit better in such a thread.

For new players, as much as you like/dislike about the current turn system, besides voicing your concerns there's really nothing much you can do, besides deciding if you want to stay or not. The Admins and Mods are not the developers and they can only forward our comments to Motion Twin (I believe Phantom mentioned this in another thread), so you can't blame them or pressure them to make any changes.

I myself do not like the current turn system, but have decided to stay with the game. I've read up on the old system as well, and indeed it seems to have a better advantage in the long run for players. As others have mentioned, the current system will eventually be maxed at 23hrs per turn, essentially making it like the old system 1 turn per day, the game developers seemed to have missed on the timing logic, in that you'll have to play at essentially the same time every day in order to have that 1 turn per day. If for whatever reason things in real life delays you from playing, for say half a day, you'll have to miss a whole turn to "retrain" the timer back to your normal playing time. This in effect puts you, the player, at a disadvantage because you are being penalized for having real life events happening. The old system, as I understand it, at least allows you to take your 1 turn per day at any time during the day, and you'll only miss your turn if you miss playing for that whole day.

As a new player, the current time system may seem to be allowing you to play faster than the old system, but eventually we all find out that the timer will increase to 23hrs. But the main disadvantage of the current turn system is that it encourages us to dislike the "waiting" part of the game. I was one such player, still am. There is absolutely nothing at the beginning of the game that explains the turn system, and you'll only find out after you've had all your turns, and start wondering what to do. There's no info in Game Help, and nothing in Q&A, a natural place to look for answers first. It is until we go to the forum and read this thread, then we start to realized that this turn system has in store for us.

As many others have mentioned, a stack-able turn system would be best. Whether you want to increase the turn per day or not I'll leave for other to debate, but for this purpose, let us say we keep it at 1 turn per day. With a stack-able system at 1 turn per day, no only will you be able to play your turn of the day any time you want, but if you have to miss a day because of any real life events, your turn is saved, and you can take it with your turn the next day. This brings a balanced playing field for everybody. You are no longer penalized with missed turns, and the business model remains unchanged because people can still pay real money if they want more turns.
