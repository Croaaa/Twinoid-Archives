It's quite funny reading this thread. In the four months since the CotC, everyone seems to have comepletely idealised, even romanticised it in their minds that it was a nice fair system where all clans could compete against other clans at their own level and the best strategy would win the day.

CotC was simply not like this at all

Pro league was down to who had the most super-high dinos, preferably demons and reincanated with spheres. Not saying there weren't some good fights, but even with only 4 clans, there was a very clear heirarchy there and the result wasn't really in doubt.

Intermediate league was mostly down to who could field the most level 28-30 dinos. Those who only had a handful, or had a lot but narrowly focussed within a few masters, let alone those that tried to compete with level 25s, were crushed.

On paper, Little League should have been pretty fair as a lot of clans could field a large number of level 18-20, or even build them from scratch. Yet some clans still has significantly more resources at their disposal than others and those clans ultimately took the top places. I think in the 12(?) matches we played only 3 clans (those we met in the QF, SF and Final) had any real shot of beating us no matter what they did. This made for a decent competition once you got to the last 8 (out of the 150 or so who enterred) but the matches before that were 90% bloodbaths.

The CotC also had the additional problem that it created adverse selection issues. Clans were often faced with the rather annoying choice of competing in a higher tier and getting crushed or competing in a lower tier and being accused of bottom-feeding. Bravery and stepping up to a challenge was not rewarded it was ultimately punished.

So in short (sorry, this post ended up a lot longer than I expected) I don't think the way CotC worked was either fair, a step forward, or something that we should seek to emulate with the Clan Wars.
