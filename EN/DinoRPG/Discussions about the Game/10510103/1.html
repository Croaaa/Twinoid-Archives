I think the current system, which allows clans sneak into someone else´s war and claim victory with 1 hit, is clearly unfair.

However, it is how things are at the moment. Responsible clan leaders should be expected to formulate and execute strategies, which allow their clans to achieve maximum possible success and benefit, knowing strength and weaknesses of their own clans as well as the enemy clans. It is a plain fact that under the current war system, smaller and/or weaker clans can wage very successful wars with sneaky tactic, requiring comprehensive intelligence gathering about relative strengths of various clans, their war effords, locations of their castles and precise timing, rather than brute force. A clan leader should not be debating whether his strategy is fair to other clans, he should only consider whether it benefits his own clan, because thats where his responsibility lies. Its no "abuse" of the system, because the system is clearly attuned to allow such tactic. There is no point blaming smaller clans for doing what the war system clearly encourages them, or at least the smarter ones of them, to do.

I think those controversies could be easily evaded in next clan wars by a simple measure: make the WAR page of each clan viewable only by clan members. If the information now viewable by anyone (whos being attacked by particular clan, how much time left, how many attacks have been made and what clans have been conquered and when) will be made private, it will turn the controversial strategies mentioned above impossible, because the vital intel will become unacessible. Each clan can then decide to give this sensitive information to their own allies.
