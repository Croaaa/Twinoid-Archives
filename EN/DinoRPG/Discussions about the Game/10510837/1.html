The short answer is yes, it does decrease the chance of using Water Canon.

The long answer is yes it does, but not in the way you think. Each A skill has a % chance of being used on any given attack. Let's say with Water Canon it's 40%. So if the only A skill you have is Water Canon, you will use it on 40% of your attacks and just do a plain vanilla attack on the other 60%.

If you have more than one A skill, then it checks them all until one of them 'passes' it's roll. The order it checks them is highest level skills first, going down to lower level skills if they fail. So, to continue your example, you get the Gel skill which we'll pretend has a 20% chance to trigger. So now when you attack, you will have a 20% chance of using Gel (because it's a higher level skill than Water Canon). If you don't use Gel, you will then have a 40% chance of using Water Canon. The probabilities multiply out as follows:

Use Gel - 20%
Use Water Canon - 80% x 40% = 32%
Use a basic attack - 48%

So yes, Gel has decreased your chance of using Water Canon from 40% to 32%, because some of the times you would have used Water Canon, you use Gel instead. However, your chance of using some kind of attack skill has risen from 40% to 52%.

This is my understanding of how it works, although I'm sure if I am wrong about any details somebody will correct me.

He actually got that, and said that the chance might be equal -> 50/50. We actually dont know the order which skill is checked first, we guess that the order is randomly chosen every time.

So his advice makes sense to not chose non primary Element A/E-Skills whose dmg is based on the Element lvl up.

It may not only block a Skill of the same Tier for 50% when they actually would trigger both, also it blocks every other (often better) lower tier Skills of your Primary Element, like Water Cannon in this example.

Explained on Piemasters exmaple it looks like this.

For example
Use Burning Breath on its own - 20%
Use Gel on its own - 20%
Use Water Cannon on its own - 40%

There is a chance of 0.2*0.2=0.04 (4%) that Gel and Burning Breath would trigger at the same time. If the order which skill checks first would be clear for example if burning breath would always (100% the time) be checked first then the chance of Gel to trigger would be
0.2-(0.2*0.2) = 0.16 (20%-4%=16%).
Brackets for better overview.
The chance of Burning Breath to trigger would remain 20%.

But hence we assume that the order is equaly random among same tier skills, such that it is 50/50 with 2 skills, 33/33/33 with 3 skills etc we must correct our formula for gel from
0.2-(0.2*0.2) = 0.16 (20%-4%=16%) to ->
0.2-((0.2*0.2)*0.5) = 0.18 (20%-2%=18%)

To calculate Burning Breath we would use the same formula as for gel in this case.

This leads us to the final chances to trigger for each skill while having all 3 of them skilled/active:

Use Gel - 18%
Use Burning Breath - 18%
Use Water Cannon - 64% x 40% = 25,6%
Use Basic Attack - 38,4%
(Hence Gel and Burning breath are using up 18% each from 100% because of their higher priority only 64% remains for watercannon)

Skilling Burning breath is not only decreasing the chance to use Gel from 20% to 18% it also decreases the chance to use Water Cannon from 32% to 25,6% in our example with made up probabilities.

Not to forget that it might also Overwrite your Best Assault. The chance for that is 0.2*0.18=0,036 (3,6%) in that example.
Everybody who skilled Mistral and Fights only 1 Enemy knows how annoying that is


Result is as it was said earlier that in most cases, its not clever to skill an A-Skill from a main element and keep it active.
