I think you got confused somewhere? I was *agreeing* with the statement that consumer power is their right to buy the product or not, no more. Somewhere over the last generation or two, a sense of entitlement has emerged, where consumers feel that they are, as 12agnar puts it, always right, and that companies selling a product must listen to their customers and act on their wishes.

This is just not the case. Businesses offer a product, either a good or a service, and the customer chooses whether to buy it or not. If it's a good product, that suits consumers needs, then more people will buy it, and may buy it over and over, and the company will make more money. If it's a bad product then less customers will buy it and those who do won't buy again and the business will likely fail. Hence customers hold the power in determining whether a business succeeds or fails.

However, that's where it ends, customers can't have it both ways. If they buy a product and continue to buy it, they can't then complain that the product isn't to their liking. On dinorpg for example, you can request a new feature, or complain about an existing one, then unless you (or players on aggregate) will spend more or less on the game as a result then there is no real incentive for MT to make the change. They are not obligated to make changes, even simple stuff like spelling things right, just because the existing customers want it. If you're not willing to quit the game or at least reduce your spending over an issue then really it is a non-issue for them.
