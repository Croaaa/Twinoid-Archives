POTAATO
I have to admit that I've won a few auctions using the last seconds method.

The problem is that it isn't an exploit or an MT problem. It is simply the nature of all open bid process. eBay, or your other local auction sites, works exactly the same way, whoever places the highest bid last, wins the auction. That's what a bidding system suppose to do.

What is missing from the current system are bidding wars, and that's a problem of not enough sellers with attractive items/prices, and not enough buyers wanting what's on sale.

If an on sale item is wanted by a lot of people, and there's a limited supply, then last second bidding won't work the same, as buyers can no longer be sure if his/her bid guarantees to be the last, so bids will have to come in earlier and earlier, thus a bidding race/war. But even then, last second bidding will still be here, for those who really wants the item, and doesn't care how much it costs.

I think what you (maybe a lot of other ppl as well?) want a simple market, an actual market where the seller simply sets the prices, and a buyer simply chooses the best deals in the market. This will leave the transaction up to fate (or to those who'll hound the market), whoever sees the item and puts in the offer first, wins the sale.

Is first better than last? idk ...
