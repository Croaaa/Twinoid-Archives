When you fight monsters, any surplus XP is lost when you level up (you just don't get what you normally get). When you complete missions, the surplus is carried over. If you can, complete missions as the last step before levelling up.

As for the order in which you do them, it does not matter: 1 + 2 + 3 = 3 + 1 + 2. You can benefit by doing things like Wolf Hunting to avoid Gluons.

The other consideration is the coins you get. More coins you get from a mission, the sooner you can buy another Dinoz.

Not much to add to what Potaato said, but one suggestion would be to do a mix of the two. You can save missions on a core set of Dinoz while going through missions quickly on the others. The Dinoz doing missions will support the core Dinoz with mission money, gathering, universal skills, quest items, etc., and continue being useful as moneymakers even after they've run out of missions. Only core Dinoz will (and should) hope to reach level 30+, so they'll be the ones in need of the saved anyway. It'll still take forever--or lots of money spent on Irmas--to level the core Dinoz, but it's a bit more manageable in terms of the options available.

It's pretty tough saving all the missions (and they're definitely useful for not losing on level-up), so as Karsh mentioned, a compromise would be to save only the 100+ missions. But all of these combined would net you a little over a level's worth of at 30+. Still, it's better than saving nothing at all. In the end, it's whatever you feel is most enjoyable.
