Who cares whether you trained your dino in 2 hours or in 2 years.
Fair warning: I'm an economist.

The point is that when you buy a Dino of higher level than 1, you are in essence benefiting from all the time (or money) spent leveling up that Dino - time and money YOU didn't invest, so you pay a premium to the original owner for their efforts invested in the Dino.

The question is, what is the value of the time necessary to build a Dino up to Level 18? Surly not 2.000 per level! Each level takes longer and is harder to obtain, so the default system of a 2.000 premium added for each level grossly undervalues the Dino the higher in level it is.

The best way to estimate a value on the time necessary to build a level 18 Dino is to simply build one instantly using Irmas and Meatpies - and to total the spent doing so. This simply establishes a minimum price that a rational seller should sell thier Dino for. This is NOT a maximum price. If the Dino is in high demand and there are plenty of players in the market with disposable income (at this point in the English game, this is probably not a fair assumption), the maximum price should be expected to be much higher!

The reason I say this is the minimum price that a rational seller should sell their Dino for is quite simply because this is the base amount of necessary to replace the Dino. This does NOT include the time and effort spent actually taking the Dino through game. In theory, the value of this effort will be determined by market forces.

One last note: If a rational seller is not thinking about straight replacement of a Dino he/she is selling AND if that Dino does not fit into the overall strategy of team-play for that seller - or is simply undesirable to the seller due to appearance - then it would be reasonable to sell for LESS than that minimum price to "dump" a Dino that the seller simply no longer wishes to play.

I assume that the minimum price that motion-twin sets on Dino is not as much a reflection of a Dino's value, but rather an anti-cheating/account swapping mechanism to prevent someone from running multiple accounts and then cheaply combining all their Dinoz into a single account for just a few . Also, this prevents out-of-game selling and provides an incentive for the actual market for buying and selling Dino to remain IN the game so that motion-twin can capture the profits from providing the legal tender necessary for the transaction to occur.
