well... at the end of the Kabuki quest you get a Kabuki egg for free, and then you are offered two more at 50,000 each... but by the looks of your account you are a long way off completing the Kabuki quest
you can also buy from the market, and I think Kabuki eggs go for around 120 - 160 these days (1= 1000)
your best chance of getting one in the near future is to spend real money on the game (you will need to spend at least 40 Euros to afford a Kabuki egg) and then buy one from the trainer market (make sure you buy , not ... can't be used to buy things at the market)
if you don't want to spend money on the game, you need to gradually buy more and more dinoz (save your money, don't buy Irma's, food or anything other than dinoz) and make sure they learn collecting skills (you need to collect ingredients to complete the Kabuki quest) and then try and complete the quest (this will take you 6 months - 1 year if you don't spend real money)

The gathering skills you will need are:
Smashroom Hunter (Get a Moueffe to follow this path: Burning Claws - unlock - Smashroom Hunter)
Search (Get a Gorilloz to follow this path: Endurance - unlock - Search)
- Apprentice Fisher (Get a Sirian to follow this path: Perception - unlock - Apprentice Fisherman)
Elemental Fission (Get a Glidwings to follow this path: Intelligence - unlock - Lightning Conductor - unlock - Elemental Fission)
Gathering (Get a Cloudoz to follow this path: Strategy - unlock - Gathering)

for now, you should focus on other things than getting a Kabuki, such as getting as many dinoz as you can and getting U skills (these skills help all your dinoz instead of just the one that learns them) and learning the mechanics of the game.
Joining a clan will also help you learn off other members, but make sure it is a clan with experienced members who know what they're doing... The Cookies might let you join
you can also learn the skill trees and other information from clan help pages, such as the ones on Death's Children's clan page

hope that helps and wasn't too much information... the short answer to your question is roughly 140
