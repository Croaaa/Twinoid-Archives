I guess some people here are too young to remember the old, "Play by Mail" type of games. In those games, the folks running the games would use paper mail to send you a sheet showing your current situation and a form for you to fill out for your next turn. You would then have to put that form in an envelope and mail it back. Once a week, They would run all of the information through a computer... one with a whopping 256K of RAM, then they would mail all of the player sheets back out.

One move per week was the standard, and if you didn't mail in your move in time for the weekly update, then you lost your turn for that week.

One move per day is fine by me, but I actually get two moves per day! Why? Because I saved up my game coins and bought a second Dino! Since then, I haven't spent game coins on Irma potions or healing food, and now I am almost at the point where I can purchase a third Dino!

With 3 Dinos, I'll be earning three times the gold per day, and soon I'll be able to buy a 4th one, then a 5th, then a 6th...

The folks who say, "Gimme! Gimme! Gimme! Now! Now! Now!" and spend all of their coins on Irma Potions are the ones who ultimately lose in a game like this. In the original Dinoparc, I started with a single Dino, and it took me about 30 seconds to use all of my turns. Now, I have 23 Dinos with an average level of more than 13, and I only bought game coins twice before I figured out the system. After 6 months, I'm now at the point where I can play for an hour each day on that site without spending a penny of real-world cash!

It's up to you... You can whine, or you can *WORK!* That's my virtual $0.02
