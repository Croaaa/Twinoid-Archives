Okay, here's the deal.

The first thing I would question is the assumption that everybody seems to be making which is that the ingredients are placed in the grid at its creation and then you click a square to try and 'find' them. As far as I know this has never been confirmed and I think it's just as likely that a random 'roll' is made every time you click on a square to see what you get. This was thought to be the case with the snowplough game and see no reason why it shouldn't apply to basic collecting too. If this is what happens then I'm sure you can all see that it makes absolutely no difference whether you mix and match your collectors or not.

However, let's assume for now that you're all right and that ingredients are fixed in the grid until you find them. Although it's harder to illustrate, it still makes no difference to your average results (you will not automatically 'find less stuff' as Blitzara suggests) and the only difference will be a slight increase in variance if you mix them up.

People who truly understand statistics, sampling and probability will understand this intuitively but for those who are skeptical, the best way might be to illustrate with an example. Let's say you have 2 locations for collecting energy in Dinoland and each contains 9 energy and 3 energy. Obviously this isn't particularly realistic but the numbers work well for the purpouses of illustration. Let's say you have 3 collectors, one of whom has Elemental Fision, the other 2 don't. If you keep them separate, the one with fission will clear a grid and get 9 and 3 evergy while the other two dinoz, in the same time, will clear the other grid twice and find 18 energy. Total yield is 27 and 3

So what if you just threw them altogether and cleared the same grid 3 times? Each clearance all 9 energy would be found, so the total yield would be the same 27 energy, but what about the :air As everyone is quick to point out, if an evergy is hit by a non-Fission dino it will be lost. But the key is there are three times as many energy to find and, on average, a third of them will be found by the 'right' dino. So, on average, 3 energy will still be found giving us an identical expected total yield.

"But," I hear you say, "there is no guarantee you will find any at all! What if all 9 are hit by the non-Fission dinoz?"

Yes, that is indeed a possibility, and this is what I meant at the start by an increase in variance. However what nobody seems to acknowledge, or at least is unwilling to point out, is that there is also a chance that you will find all 9 energy doing it this way! True it's a remote chance, but it contributes to it all averaging out in the long run.

So in short, the only reason to separate your collectors is if you absolutely have to find a more predictable number of advanced ingredients in a fixed time. And note that because the number of ingredients in each grid is not entirely predictable in real life like it is in our example, this is not really possible anyway. Inmost cases it simply doesn't matter at all, just search wherever you can and whenever you can.

There are, however, two factor that nobody has really mentioned yet in favour of mixing gatherers:

1. It gives you a lot more flexibility and less 'wasted turns'. Not all gatherers just sit in one place clicking search until the end of time. Sometimes they need to move between gathering locations and even do quests. While doing this you will have more opportunities to gather (and hence will gather more in the long term) if you are not rigidly sticking to 'advanced grids' and 'non-advanced grids'.

2. Collectors are crappy fighters and the more of them you have in a group, the less HP they will lose and the less downtime you will have. If you're smart this won't have much impact on your collecting, but will help them level faster.
