Since I couldn't find accurate infos about the relation between and grouping I decided it was payback time, and started a study on my own.
Every research needs founds so I invested some money and got 6 new dinos and moved them together, reforming groups to test.

I wanted to wait a bit more to publish this, but since I've seen some new posts on the argument in the last few days, I decided to anticipate it, and update it when I'll have new data to share.

First of all I'll share the general results of my research:

1) All calculations are done using floating point math, and are finally 'rounded' to assign actual (that explains some little glitches in figures)

2) is given on a 'per dinoz' base (each dinoz is calculated and THEN they are added to show group ).
This did let me astonished, since I assumed that, as in all the MUDs I played, is splitted between group's components, according to respective lvls.
This also means that there is no 'overflow' for lost when leveling

3) When grouped, dinoz gets 75% of the it'd get fighting same enemies solo.
Group size doesn't count (at least there is no difference between groups of 2 and groups of 3 dinos. I can't create a group of 4 or 5 yet, but I suspect it wont change a bit).
75% is a bit 'arbitrary'... it could be in the range 73% to 77%, but I'm assuming a human (the ideator, engineer, planner or programmer) did choose 75% instead of 74.265738%.

I don't know if you need to kill 2 smashrooms and you fight 4, will the other 2 count towards the other dinoz.


If when starting the fight, the case is 0/2 for leader and 0/2 for follower; and you kill 3 or 4 of the monsters, the results will be 2/2 & 1/2 and 2/2 & 2/2, respectively.

However, if the case is 1/2 for leader and 0/2 for follower; and you kill 4 of the monsters, the results will be 2/2 & 0/2.

So only in the case where you have not killed any of the monsters and kill more monsters than needed for the first dinoz, will the extra kills carry over to the next dinoz. After you have killed at least one of the monsters required for a group of monsters, the ertra kills will not carry over.

This has been my experience over and over again.

As for grouping, grouping the same level dino's is best even if just for sanity's sake.
Heres how group XP goes.

Each Dino gets XP for each monster they fight.
However because you generally fight more monsters in a group their is a XP penalty so that solo guys arent too misfortuned.

2-3 Dino Group = 75% of normal XP
4 Dino Group = I forget the exact number but its 60-65% ish.
5 Dino Group = 50% of normal XP

So you may ask, why even bother with groups at all or with groups bigger than 2-3. Well say you get 10XP per Green Giant, and solo you only find 1. When you group into 2-3 man groups you will fight 2 instead of 1. At 75% XP this is 15 XP per dino. Much better than solo. And in a 5 man group you may see 3 Green giants, at 50% = 15 XP still. It all really depends on what you face. If your very high in level a solo dino will see the same number of enemies as a group of any size. And in these circumstances XP is better solo.

So why pick a group if the XP is better solo or indeed in a lower size group. Well the simple reason is HP loss. 5 Dino's with 5 main attacks, most of the time you will take 0 damage from fights. Compared to solo where you take a beating nearly every fight. The gold you save from healing items and resting and indeed the extra gold bonus gained in some fights makes up for the losses in most cases.
