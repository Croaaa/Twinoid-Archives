At the moment everybody is politely ignoring the big elephant in the room that half the forum mods (including the two most active ones) have resigned. I PMd one of the remaining mods to ask if it was okay to start this thread, but didn’t receive a reply, so I’m going to assume it’s okay.

I feel that now might be a good time to discuss the forum and specifically the forum moderation. I’m sure we all have different ideas about how the forum has been moderated in the past and how it should be moderated in the future, but any discussion of this topic has been quickly silenced. Now most of the mods have gone, maybe we can discuss this without it being construed as being personal or some kind of attack on authority, but instead as a bona-fide suggestion on how to proceed in the future. I think it would be good if we kept individual names or incidents out of the discussion so that it can stay impersonal and as positive as possible. Posted below are just my thoughts and ideas, so if you disagree with all or part, then go ahead and say so or (better yet) post your own ideas. Also, following recent events, I guess I should point out that these views are entirely my own and not posted on behalf of my clan.

I think the fundamental problem with the forum as it exists now (or existed two weeks ago anyway) is that it is over-moderated. It is a forum that probably gets only about 100 new posts per day (that’s just a guess, as I don’t really visit the Chaos Tavern) but it is moderated like a forum that receives 2,000 posts per day, with too many moderators trying to enforce too many rules with too little leeway. This causes several problems such as:

1. Rules that aren’t really necessary for a forum this size being enforced religiously, without any real thought about whether they are sensible. For example, the main justification for locking threads that go off topic or are ‘low content’ is that they push more useful threads off the forum. But is that really a problem when threads currently take a week to even leave the first page?
2. New players (or older players new to the forum) see a bunch of locked threads and mod intervention in every other thread and it makes the forum look intimidating.
3. Mods are forced (probably largely subconsciously) to create work for themselves to justify their own existence, whether this is moderating threads, disciplining users or coming up with big ideas for new rules or reorganisations that aren’t really necessary.
4. The more laissez faire mods are effectively made redundant. If one mod decides a thread is fine and another mod decides it isn’t and locks/heavily moderates it, then the opinion/decision of the first mod is basically meaningless.

Obviously this isn’t entirely the fault of the moderators themselves. They didn’t invent the forum rules (well, most of the time ) so really all they are trying to do is enforce them as they best see fit. Neither is it their fault that we are stuck with extremely crappy forum software, I’m sure they were just as annoyed by that as we are. Nevertheless I think we should concentrate on the things we *can* do something about. So here are my proposals for the forum going forward.

1. Less mods! How many did we have before, six? That’s too many for a forum this size, I think three or four would be plenty.
2. A clarification of what a mod actually does. Mods should be there to perform a specific function and that function should be clearly defined. There shouldn’t be instances where mods are using their powers to do things that are not directly part of their job. For example, mods should never edit their own posts to remove something they regret saying in a regular discussion. And they shouldn’t create threads and then try to enforce new rules on that thread that are not part of the regular forum rules. In short, when not actually moderating, mods should obey the same rules as everybody else.
3. A generally more relaxed and tolerant attitude towards moderating (a lot of the ideas below stem from this).
4. Less rules on what can be posted on the Game Discussions forum. Start with the assumption that any thread is okay unless there is a strong argument for removing it, rather than the current policy, which seems to be to lock anything that doesn’t check a rather stringent set of boxes.
5. Rethink the “General Questions” and “Skills Thread” threads. I think these were created with the best of intentions, but the end result has been that most useful discussion on the forum has been squeezed into these giant cumbersome threads, where it is impossible to find anything and discussion can get confusing at times.
6. Rewrite the disciplinary guidelines. These are way too rigid and draconian and people are getting banned completely needlessly and for way too long. Two weeks for a first offence of ‘recidivist moaner’? Forum bans should be an absolute last resort, not something to be thrown around like confetti whenever someone breaks a rule.
7. A rethink on many of the forum rules. Some are unnecessary while others are impractical to enforce. Also there are some ‘rules’ that don’t actually seem to be in the rules themselves but have rather been invented at some point or creatively extrapolated from existing rules to give them new meanings.
8. Encourage the use of common sense in all aspects of moderating. Rather than a mindless enforcing of some rather sketchy rules, moderators should about think whether each decision they make is actually making the forum better or if it is just moderation for moderations sake. Moderators should have some leeway in the enforcement of the rules and should hence be accountable for that enforcement rather than being able to hide behind the rather tired “I am only following the rules”.

Anyway, as I said these are just my suggestions, maybe other people have different ones. And just to reiterate please try to keep this positive and impersonal. This is not about having a go at current or former mods, it is about trying to improve the way things are done in the future now that we basically have a 'fresh start'.

Fire away
