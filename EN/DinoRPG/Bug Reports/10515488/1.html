I have a suggestion aimed at helping clans keep track of the Dark World's portal focusing group.

Premise:

Imagine that you intend to send a group of 3 dinoz to the Dark World and one of your clanmates intends to provide the other 4 dinoz. Unfortunately, you are both unaware that someone in your clan is keeping 2 dinoz focusing. Your friend arrives at Bao's first and does his part.

As soon as you focus your first dino the party is closed. Now if you click for your dino to go thru the portal he is stranded separated from his groupmates, and you'll lose who knows how much time until the other 2 dinoz can join him.

How it is dealt with today:

My clan, and I suppose many others, keep a list of how many players want to start focusing on a short term (and how many dinoz each) on a private page. That is, however, an awful way to address the issue.

You have no way to know if you're good to focus, you can't change your planning because you're absolutely in the dark and if things go wrong you have no way of knowing who messed up.

Why it doesn't work:

Because there are players who never enter the clan's forum or don't care if they're screwing a clanmate's or even the whole clan's plan - I've seen it happen more than once. People (even well-meaning players) also forget to update the DW's queue or misunderstand its purpose.

Suggestion:

I propose a two-part solution.
1- There must be a button, a page, <something> within the clan that shows which dinoz are currently focusing (well, not necessarily the exact dino but the master's name and how many dinoz he has focusing instead).
2- Once a 7-dinoz DW party is closed there must be a log entry on the clan's history or such, stating who focused and how many dinoz there were. This way the DW queue doesn't depent on each clanmate to stay current.
