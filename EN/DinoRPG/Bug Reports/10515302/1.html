Don't think it is needed, although I had the chance to observe many fails at moving, reacting and understanding the coordinations from inexperienced players. Actually I think the system is quite easy to understand after you have seen 1 or 2 battles (or read the rules AND invested brain time thinking about them). The tricks can be learned from the more experienced players (for example how to execute an attack as safely as possible, reducing the chances of getting countered).
I think most fails at positioning etc. stem from
a) brain-afk / distracted people
b) not understanding the basics of the cotc system (not being able to read the possibilites of how and where you could attack or be attacked)
c) not being able to read (the chat)
The training system probably would only help with b), which can also be solved by reading the cotc basic rules and thinking about their consequences for some minutes. Generelly people, that would be motivated enough to fight a test battle to learn the cotc mechanics, will also manage to learn them without this sandbox mode.
