al-Aqsa
While more often than ot I'm against all types of forced limitations on players due to their power or experience I think Loamoats is correct in that giving total greenhorns the freedom of creating a Clan does more harm than good. More often than not people grow tired of their clans because people arent joining. Even if they make it to an actual server event the new Clan just trying their wings gets ganked by people whom consider destroying everything that moves great fun. Both of the scenarios usually lead to the player quitting the game altogether which is something the server cannot really afford.

I've always felt that this game should have some sort of tutorial to point out that things like wasting all of your coins on your first dino or creating a clan as soon as you can are in-fact Bad Ideas and will probably cause you to burn out before experiencing much of the game. DinoRPG is a fairly complicated game for flash at least and there are a lot of beginner traps.

Other option would be that Moderators PM'd every new master with some general tips and at least noted them of the forum community where people are more than happy to answer their questions. Now I realize this suggestion goes both way beyond the job description of a Moderator and would be largely useless because of the silly Stamp system needed for private conversations.

Of the actual restrictions posted here I like the point barrier the best. While Mialmeris suggestion of having a sliding cost of clan creation is interesting it might cause the most stubborn of the uninformed people to ruin their game totally. In general though, I wish there was a way to include the new players into the greater DinoRPG community more effectively.
