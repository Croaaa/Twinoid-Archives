MT is in a hard position here. And while it is certainly their own doing, it is none-the-less painful.

Games with complicated interactions of skills and abilities (say like World of Warcraft) have the ability to make massive changes to skills, refund everybody's skill points, and let them retool their skills. They even let you pay game currency to do it later if you want to try something different. While accusations of "you nerfed x skill" or "x class is useless now" will still be made, "dumb" moves can be fixed and recovered from because people can start over with relative pain-free ease.

That is not the case here. "Dumb" moves have been compounded and glossed over with gimmicks (a la poisonite) without much thought. Any proposed change will be greeted with "I spent lots of money making it this way, if it changes, I will take my ball and go home". Or, even if they HAVEN'T yet spent lots of money, they will complain that "but I was planning on doing the thing which you are now nerfing" and will take their ball and go home.

Essentially, the one aspect of it that is addicting (the permanence and randomness of the skill ups) is also its biggest weakness. If "ok everyone, we have screwed up so badly that we need to refund every sphere and wipe off all of the skills learned" can't ever be done, than all these changes will never work.

Basically, the only solution that I can think of that will actually work long term, is that there is a complete and dramatic rework of the skills/spheres/items, while praying that the logs of each dinoz have stored the sequence of levels up and letting players re-roll their skills in the order that they got them.

Otherwise, we are doomed to find that nothing will really change. They are failing to deal with the biggest design flaws in the game and are instead dealing with ancillary issues (bubble? really?) in pretty thoughtless ways.

We need bold leadership from MT in a "this is going to make the game more fun, for more people, for a longer time" type statement and let the chips fall where they may. I see in businesses all the time an attitude of fear to make changes that their "biggest customer" won't like and so they put off doing things or making changes that need to be made. Sometimes it is just better to do the hard thing and often times that is rewarded with more business with the right kind of customer. Or bankruptcy! The unknown is what makes it so exciting!
