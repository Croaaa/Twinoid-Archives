Piemaster
My number one more-important-that-all-else suggestion is to impliment the Swiss system of pairings.

This will get rid of 90% of the problems we had in this CotC in one swoop. Clans that are doing well will be paired against other clans that are doing well (so preventing the problem of clans getting loads of free wins from those that have given up). If you win you will usually get a harder match next round and if you lose you will usually get an easier match next round, but winning is still always preferable to losing and it is never advantageous to concede a match. This, in my opinion, is how a tournament *should* work.

Of course this won't solve the problem of 'uneven' fights. Personally I don't think there is a way to solve this problem completely because there is too much disparity between clans. However, I think it would be good if the power calculator in this CotC was used as a tiebreaker for pairings within the Swiss System (and exclusively for round 1). So you will always be paired against the clan closest to you in power who has the same number of wins as you (that you haven't already faced). This *might* mean that a strong clan can field an intentionally low-levelled team and win the first few rounds, but they will soon come unstuck when they have to face better opposition with their still low-levelled dinos.

The CotC is supposed to be a clan event, however, with the power level caps, often an entire clan cannot participate in a battle. This may make the matches a little less one-sided, but the CotC2 clearly indicated that the battles remain largely one-sided anyway. As such, I think the power level caps take away from the system (lower participation) more than they add (slight fairness).

I am inclined to think that registering individual dinoz is a poor idea, and registering participating players with each player having a power level would be better (to determine initial clan rankings). (Additionally, the Player Rankings should be changed to Power Levels so Clans would have better pre-CotC information for next time.) Level ups and Spheres should be allowed during the CotC.

Instead of limiting the maximum power level on the board, matches should be balanced by limiting the maximum power level of individual dinoz, based on the opponents dinoz maximum power level. (Based on does not mean equal to in this situation.) The number and power distribution of the opponent clans top dinoz should be used to calculate the maximum for your clan, which should generally exceed somewhat their opponents maximum, depending on how common that power level is. So a clan with a single level 45 dinoz might cap the opposing team at ~45, but a clan with dozens of level 45 dinoz might cap the opposing team at ~50. Still both teams should be allowed to fully populate their side of the field.

The Swiss System should be used, but the prize structure should be Rank Based. Prizes for the Number of Wins should also be awarded on top of the rank based prizes. This should give everyone a reason to compete throughout the entire CotC.

Moves on the board should be capped at 10, not 15 considering the size of the board. AP should be awarded per hour, and not half hour people need to be able to sleep during the CotC without forfeiting the battle. The length of a battle should be increased to at least 3 days. The algorithm for distributing obstacles should be modified to produce more strategically significant map designs.

This is another idea I have, which is based on the rules of the second CotC, but make it a long term event, with less time to consume, but more action in a short time, with rewards for the clan, and the members that actually participate.

The basic idea
- each clan fighting each other clan twice
- fast generation of AP
- short battles
- points for win/draw
- prizes for clans and participating members

In details
It is hard to find the point where to start, the details, but nevertheless I'll give it a try.

Legal Teams
A legal team consists of at least 30 dinoz and no more than 100 dinoz.
If one clan registers a non-legal team, the battle is canceled and it gets 0 points. If the other clan has registered a legal team, it will get 3 points, otherwise it also will get 0 points.

A battle between two clans comes with several phases:
- Announcement
- Time Picking
- Registration Phase
- Adjustment Phase (if required)
- Battle


Announcement
This is not actually a phase, but the beginning of the battle between two clans. Each clan will be informed against which clan it will fight next, and at which place the battle will take place. In addition the time picking clan is announced.

Time Picking
This should help to even the problem with different time zones. Since each clan faces each other clan twice, every clan will be able to fight the other clan at the time they chooses once.
During this phase, the time picking clan, chooses when at which time the battle will start. At the end of this phase, the time is locked, and the opposed clan is informed about the picked time.

Registration Phase
Members of each clan register their dinoz during this phase. The power value is calculated as it was in CotC2. The current calculated power value of each clan is known to each other. At the end of this phase, leveling and adding spheres is locked for registered dinoz, until the end of the battle
(Exception: see Adjustment Phase).
Both clans have to register [u]legal teams[/u] during this phase.

Adjustment Phase
If the power value of one clan is much higher than the power value of the opposing clan, this phase should help to even the power.
If the clan's power value beats the power value of the opposing clan times 1.1, it may adjust its power value by dropping and adding dinoz from the team, but within regulations:
- the team must be legal at any time
- it may not exceed the power value it had before this phase
- the power value may not drop below 90% of the opponent's power value
At the end of this phase, leveling and adding spheres is locked for registered dinoz, until the end of the battle. Dinoz that have been unregistered (and have not been registered again) during his phase, may level and add sphere again.

Battle
You may have wondered, why there is an adjustment phase at all. I decided, that the clan with the least power value should determine the how much power could be on the field. So if a clan, has a dramatically higher power value, it has the option to have less dinoz with higher power, risking to be outnumbered on the field, or to adjust its value to have an approximately equal power value.
Max. power points on battle field (MPP):
Clan A = Clan with the lower power value
Clan B = Clan with the higher power value
Pa = Sum of power points of the 30 dinoz, that have the highest power points within Clan A's team.
MPP = Pa * 1.55
This calculation should animate clans to register more than 30 dinoz

- A battle lasts for 3 hours.
- Battle Points are being calculated every 5 minutes.
- Each player may have up to 3 dinoz at the same time on the battle field.
- Each clan may have up up to 45 dinoz at the same time on the battle field. The sum of power points of the dinoz on the field may not exceed MPP.
- Each dino, entering the battlefield starts with 1 Action Point. It gains 1 Action Point every 150 seconds up to a maximum of 10 Action Points.
- Moving from on field to another costs 1 Action Point, as does attacking an opposing dino.
- If a dino dies on the field, it is removed and may not return on the battle field.

The clan with the most battle points at the end of the battle, gets 3 league points.
If both clans have equal battle points at the end of the battle, both get 1 league point.
If the clan of a participating player gains league points, that player gets the same amount of points for the CotC-Players-Ranking.

League
Total Ranking
Clans are being ranked by the amount of total league points they gained. Clans with equal points share the same rank. After each clan has fought twice against each other clan, the season ends.
Period Ranking
Clans are being ranked by the amount of league points they gained. Clans with equal points share the same rank. After 3 month, the Clan period rewards are handed out, and the league points for the period ranking are reset to 0.

CotC-Players-Ranking
Players are being ranked by the amount of points, they gained for the CotC-Players-Ranking. Players with equal points share the same rank. A player retains his or her points when changing the clan.
After 3 months, the Player's rewards are handed out, and the points for all players are reset to 0.

Rewards
Now this is tricky part. If each clan fights each other clan twice, it would take very long to actually get the prizes. You also may have wondered, why I choose to list the points for clans and players separately. Well first of all, I'd like to see this members being rewarded, that actually participated. With that in mind, this players can get rewards based on their activity.
Player's reward
Each player gains equal to his points gained for the CotC-Players-Ranking. In addition, the best 10 players gain an epic item.

Clan period rewards
A player is considered a member of a clan, it has been at least 8 weeks with during a reward period. In addition, the player must have participated with at least one battle of that clan. The player gains the reward of that clan he or she is considered a member.
Each member of the top ranked clan gains 1 golden napodino + epic golden cup.
Each member of a top 5 clan gains 50 demon tickets + epic silver cup.
Each member of a top 10 clan gains 50 demon tickets.
Each member of a top 20 clan gains 1 golden napodino + epic bronze cup.
Each member of a top 30 clan gains 50 .
Each member of a top 40 clan gains 25 + epic participation item.
Each member of a top 50 clan gains 25 .
If you qualify for more than on of that points, you get the reward of each point

Season Rewards
