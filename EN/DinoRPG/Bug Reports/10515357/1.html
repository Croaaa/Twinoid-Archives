Well, I was looking at how the attacks work and just thought of something...
That leafy dawn is pretty overpowered shouldn't be noted anymore, but I believe this is actually a fault in the programming... I don't think it should've been this strong.

If you look at other attacks, like Blazing for instance, it says:
Attacks all enemies with a power of Fire 6 + Lightning 6And we all know what kind of damage that gives...

Then another attack, Lightning Dance says:
Attacks 5 times with a Lightning power 3
Now leafy says:
Regenerates all Dinoz in the Group up to Lightning 2 + Wood 2 health points
But... if you look at how much hp leafy heals, it gets far above the damage one lightning dance-attack or even a blazing does, up to like... even 80 hp healed per dino? Compared to 30 hp damage at most with blazing, which should be far superior according to the numbers...

Now leafy does work in a different way, and this is (I guess) in the same way as precocious spring works:
All the other Dinoz in the Group recover health points up to the Wood element of the Dinoz
So the dino gets healed with 2x the element value + 2x the element value, which in result is far superior to precocious spring and really overpowered as a low-hp dino can get itself up to almost full hp again in just 2 leafy's...
Well, you all know that, but the thing that got me thinking now is... wasn't Leafy supposed to be calculated via the same way as attacks? This would make a Leafy heal kinda similar in strength to one Lightning Dance attack, so say up to 20 hp healed per dino, which makes it similar in strength to precocious spring and definitely not as overpowered as it is now...

So what do you think? Wouldn't this be a mistake in the programming?

Piemaster
I don't think you can generalise about 'Rock/Paper/Scissors' type metagames. I have used the phrase before and with hindsight I think it is a huge oversimplification.

If you have Strategy A, which is good against Strateies, D, E, H and L, weak against C, F, J and N and average aginst the rest and you can say something similar about all the other strategies A-N then that is a very healthy metagame even though you could describe it as just a more complex 'rock/paper/scissors/ type arrangement.

However the 'bad' type of rock/paper/scissors metagames arise from when you have a single dominant strategy and a single rather contrived, but effective, counter to that strategy. This leaves you with 3 basic strategies:

A. The dominant strategy
B. The counter to that dominant strategy
C. Other 'good' strategies that are neither of the above.

This leads to a straightforward B beats A beats C beats B, which is boring as hell.
