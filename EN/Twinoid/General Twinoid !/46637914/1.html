Bad Ass!

Made by deepnight in 48hrs for the Ludum Dare, and climbing to 2nd position out of more than 1300 games I would like to officially introduce you to Badass Inc.

Made with nostalgic 30 somethings in mind, Badass Inc. is a point and click action game that will take you back. You’re a contract killer; a sleek, stylish, feline predator that trades in scalps and briefcases full of cash. Your mission: eliminate a target for Ludum-Tek in an “unconventional way”.

Packing pixelart and a brooding soundtrack, Badass plunges you into a city wracked with desperation. Revel in the apathy of the protagonist as you work through situational puzzles, interspersed with cinematic action scenes and tense shootouts. It’s an in and out job, take out the target and get paid, right?

What the HELL!? Where's Dead Cells!?!

Don't worry we're still working on it! This extended version was made in a week and so didn't take deep off of DC or anything like that. Put down your pitchforks...

Our hope for this game is that we can "test the water" if you will with our community, to see if people are interested in this kind of game. If we get a good response then we'll get cracking on a full blown version with all the bells and whistles. At the moment the game is a very limited prototype that we're using to test the concept.

So we'd be really grateful if you could share the game on your facebooks/twitters/carrier pigeon networks/google stone thingy etc and spread the word. In the event that you don't like it, well you'll still have this little version to play for free for ever.

Where can I get me a copy of that wicked soundtrack!?

Volkor X provided the music as a favour to deepnight, so if you like it head over to his site here: volkorx.bandcamp.com and support another great independent artist!

I'm not saying I disagree with you here, but I'd really like to point how how often delays happen in game development. MT is far from the worst company when it comes to delaying games. I don't know if I can think of a single indie developer that hasn't delayed their fair share of games.

Sure a lot of times they only get delayed by a few months. A year delay is probably par for the course for most indie devs. But the unfortunate problem is I see a lot of developers move toward just not giving even an estimate of a release. A lot of times even neglecting to give very many previews of what they're working on altogether. And for someone like me who really does enjoy following game developers when they're working on something I really want to see, that gets annoying.

From my perspective, a lot of times it's because for a lot of people, "I can't WAIT to get my hands on this game" turns to "I'm getting SUPER impatient" and I'm not totally sure how devs even respond to that. Sure, for a large company like Nintendo or Microsoft delaying a game can be REALLY damaging. I feel like even delaying Zelda for Wii U made nintendo's lineup for 2015 SUPER weak, and I don't even like the 3D zelda games much at all. But for an indie dev, why is it so surprising when a game doesn't get finished on time? It happens all the time. It's not new.

Just yesterday a game I backed on kickstarter announced their release date for the game they had funded last year. The campaign originally announced a 2015 release date. And the date they announced was LATE december 2015. But I will bet good money they won't hit that release date.

That said, Twinoid IS an established company with a reputation to uphold. But in my opinion? Where Twinoid has really excelled is in keeping in contact with their community. And this game is just another case of them showing that off as a strong point. They are genuinely interested in seeing if we as a community would be interested in seeing them do something new with this game. Yeah, they have a few irons in the fire right now, and in reality it's totally possible we could see a game just not get finished (Kube). But they've managed to release several games that take very new and interesting angles and make them happen.

Also keep in mind that Twinoid is a group of developers, not just one or two, and as such in a lot of cases they're probably working most efficiently if they AREN'T just focused on one game at a time. Delays are annoying, yeah. But if you're finding a delay to be frustrating then maybe you're better off not following development of any one game or company too closely.

-

ANYWAY, as far as Badass Inc is concerned, I don't often like point and click adventures, but I see some potential here. I did find myself interested in the assassin character, which is important for story driven games. (She's just so much more colorful than most. Usually I feel like a character like that has to refuse to be funny or cheery at all, so I appreciated that.) And the combat was surprisingly tasteful for a game like this. I'd almost like to see the combat expanded upon depending on where this goes.

All in all: I'd play it. *shrug*
