I'd like to clarify a few things before people get too scared. Online fraud is a very common thing, anywhere there's real money involved you will most likely find people like this guy. They often exploit people through social engineering to get what they want, I think you're giving them too much credit by calling them hackers. This type of fraud should be easily preventable by taking a few precautions.

Do not click on random links unless you're absolutely sure the source is to be trusted. If you did click on a link and you downloaded something that looks like an .exe, image, screensaver etc. you shouldn't under any circumstances run the file. While clicking links should generally be harmless this is the easiest way to get infected with malware like keyloggers or trojans in which case you'll need to clean your computer with anti malware software.

Do not enter your personal information (username, password) on random sites unless you know that you are on the official site. This is called phishing and scammers can set up sites that look exactly the same as the one they're trying to target, even the URL could be similar (twinnoid.com) for the sole purpose of stealing other people's accounts.

Do not buy things from random people, if a deal is too good to be true it probably is. In this case MTPasses at half the price. When you buy something with PayPal, your personal details usually gets sent to the seller, this is so they can ship you the product.

It goes without saying but do not share your account with anyone. Not only is it against the TOS but you shouldn't trust anyone, especially when they seem like a great person who for some reason wants to gain access to your account.

Also just for the record, if they know your IP address they will most likely not be able to do anything with it, they would need much more than that to be able to hack you.

TL;DR: Use common sense, do not trust random people over the internet and you shouldn't have anything to worry about. This is something that isn't easily preventable by Twinoid admins since the scammers aren't actually hacking the site, they're exploiting people's trust or lack of knowledge with computers.
