This is a direct response to the thread named "[Sug] Non-hero access to forum", created on World Forums > Discussions of Die2Nite.

In that thread, a few people argued that residents should not have access to the World Forums and they defended that idea based on the thought that if residents gained access to the World Forums, they would spam them and post, basically, stupid stuff.

Well, I want to state my opinion, a resident's opinion, and I want to do it with resident's resources So since I cannot post in the World Forums of D2N, if I want to answer something there I am forced to write here, the Twinoid's Beta-Test Forum (this is not even D2N related). I know that I have .net too but I want to do it the "official" way.

I get the point of the WF as a business, nothing to argue there. The community there is great and MT knows that. I have even considered buying hero days just to post there to talk about the game I like, but I cannot, or I don't want, to pay (the reasons why people don't pay are irrelevant here). If World Forums were opened to everyone, heroes would lost a feature and that means less money to MT (at least in theory).

Now I am going to the point were someone basically said that residents posted crap on the WF. Well, you will excuse, but that is dumb. There is a relation between being a hero and knowing the game well (a newbie would difficult pay hero days), but that does not mean that residents could not know the game, or that heroes could not say stupid thing. I had to teach a Tech once how to make imprints and I met another hero who said that "day 2 is too soon to build a Factory". I just want to point out that there is no relation between being a resident or hero and knowing more about the game (or having the skills to think critically about stuff).

I find this last point an obvious fact. It is obvious too that residents will be the ones making newbie's questions. Hell, if you are paying for the game you should know how things works. Every noob is a resident, but not all residents are noobs.

Another fact is that stupid messages create more interest that intelligent ones, so if someone asks a funny or stupid doubt you will think that he is a resident and that will feed the prejudice some people have about residents.

tl;dr version: I find it pretty absurd that residents have to deal with communication problems because they chose not to pay. This is not even something in game or something "special". Free access to forums is something normal in the internet (even in other MT games like DinoRPG, when non paying users can access the forums and their forums are not chaos and spammers anarchy).

Edit: now that I mentioned DinoRPG, if we compare the situation with this game everything seems very paradoxical. DinoRPG is a game which not require all the communication that D2N requires, but it do have free forums for everyone. While D2N, a game based entirely on communication and communities, has all these problems. And the worse of all things is that this creates rivalry and segregation between residents and heroes (in some of them, obviously, I have seen the two faces of this and I have seen tolerant people).
