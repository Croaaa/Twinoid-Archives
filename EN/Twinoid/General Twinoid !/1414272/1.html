In other words, this system gives a simple advantage for players/users in the sense that, if someone sees a user giving misleading or confusing data but sees someone giving a best advice and this certain user has more rep. points because he's more trustworthy than anyone else, it helps other users to pick the best solution, and rating this other user accordingly to its level of helpfulness.

On the other hand, if a user has a reputation of being a troll, a bad user/player or a griefer in different games, you can rate him down thus warning other players to avoid him/her at all cost, saving mods the pain of sending warnings if they see a recidivist user doing bad deeds. This also is good as, if the user gets a reputation of -100 for example, or a 5 star warning (from mods), this user is automatically banned from the system (just for being that, a troll/griefer that makes the life of innocent players and mods hard to endure ).

Will it be a bad or a hard idea? This works in other boards/communities that are for gamming (will not cite names) and it is very helpful for users and mods alike to control the vast growing numbers of loyal minions--- I mean, loyal players/customers, yes


As for the "oh exploitable" part of this system, other communities made it possible just for 1 person to rate other person JUST ONCE per user, or ONCE PER WEEK, in order to prevent the rigging of the rep points (also, users can't rate themselves, obviously), or to abuse this system to unrightfully ban someone that doesn't deserve it (if the auto-ban system is implemented as well of course).
