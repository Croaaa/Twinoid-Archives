It is the near future.
The world is being overrun by the ice cold claws of the undead.
Today, however, is just a normal day for you. You wake up, work, eat, drink, sleep just like any other day, but there's something eerie that you can't quite explain. A sense of dread. A sense that soon, all you have known will be rocked violently by the coming events.
It is the call of the apocalypse.
It is the fall of humanity.

SETTING:

Hallowford - a large metropolitan city resting it's docks on the northern shores of the Pacific Ocean. The city is rich with the goods it gains from it's famously large docks, but it also is a haven for local drug trafficking and crime.

Lewisville - the small, quaint town just a few miles south east of Hallowford. It gains most of its revenue from the local farming trade and tourist attractions covering the highway which cuts straight through the city. It is also the location of the Lewisville Airport which services the entire valley.

The Valley - the cities rest within the thick forests of the valley. The forests are lined with rivers and streams and are usually separated by large farming fields or plantations. The beaches to the west are a very popular summer vacation destination. The area is known to have light snowstorms during winter and cool, breezy summers.

The Mountains - the rugged terrain surrounding the forest is filled with pine forests and lumbermills. It's also dotted with cottages that house hunters and visitors. A few ski resorts sit on the mountain slopes making it a very renown snowboarding and skiing location. During the winter months, it is common for the mountains to experience heavy snowfall and temperatures low enough to freeze zombies until they thaw in the spring.

ROLE-PLAY:

How to: Basic guide to roleplaying. It's mostly meant for Gmod, but it's the best I could find.

Rules: This is a realistic roleplay and so to keep this thread realistic, I encourage use of Google to do quick research on items like weapons or food. Like say you find a sub-machine gun off a dead police officer, then you should use Wikipedia to find the model and stats of a weapon that American (since this RP is based in America) police would use.
No over powered characters.
Your character must act like they would in a real zombie apocalypse.
Keep it clean. Gore and mild swear allowed however as long as the swearing doesn't get out of hand. Gore is good though.
Keep it realistic. Gun shots attract the horde, even measly cuts can get infected, more items slow you down, can't carry everything, food and water, etc.
Use detailed sentences and proper grammar. Sentences like "I went to zombie and killed it" will be ignored (typos excused).
Dice usage is encouraged for looting and zombie killing. When fighting other players it is mandatory. If you want to fight someone randomly, they can only be injured. If the fight is part of the plot however, then death can happen if a majority of each of the involved parties agrees.
Weather, time, and important events can only be made by the RP maker (me). You can message me though if say, you want it to start raining.
More rules may be included as the RP progresses.

I would like to explain how time works in this roleplay real quick. This might be a little confusing.

Each character has their separate timeline.
Whichever character is ahead of another in time decides what happens (like shooting first (unless with RP gadgets), getting out of the way of oncoming hazards, etc). There is no traveling back in time in this roleplay, so going backwards is one less variable to worry about. But one character cannot be constantly ahead or behind everyone else by too far. If a character doesn't do anything for too long and everyone else's timeline naturally progresses too far ahead, that character will have to choose an idle action they did (sleeping, patrolling, working, etc) while they weren't doing anything. If there is a break in a character's timeline too long and an important event happens that they're involved in, then someone else would have to temporarily control that character to continue the plot (no making them do something they seemingly wouldn't or killing them off unless that must happen/the 'owner' agrees).

There are multiple ways to link character's timelines together:
Talking to a character while each have the same conversation at once. One way of mixing the past and present during multiple person conversations is to leave choices for the other character(s) to respond to so that they can build off of it. (example of that here)
Stating events that happen with either past or present wording. You cannot choose future wording because of the lack of time travel as mentioned before. You can still predict things, but your character cannot already and exactly know what's going to happen.
Clocks. You might have seen this already earlier. These are sometimes hard to get to (especially once all the clocks begin to die), but they are the simplest. You can also state the time of day, but that's less accurate.
Any other creative way that you can think of as long as someone can easily comprehend it.

One example of different timelines happening right now is that BlackVoid is traversing this crowded, zombie-infested street at the same time Lyle is driving to pick up Lynda.

Another thing about new characters is that they should probably join at the most current timeline. If they have the ability to be independent of other characters for awhile, they can choose to start a little behind to give their characters more background.
