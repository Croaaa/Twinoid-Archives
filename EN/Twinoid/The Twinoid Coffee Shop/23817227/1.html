Well since at least one genre fan is floating around this out-land talking shop I might as well follow their lead on something I've been wondering recently:

What's with the emerging trend in lovable and/or empathetic zombies in media?

The first of these I can think of is, like, uh, a transition case in Shaun of the Dead where mostly the zombies remain unrelenting murderous brain fiends excepting one who is, like, your buddy.
Literally

By the time you get to Fido[1] we're pretty much all buddy and now with Warm Bodies we're really upping the potential for, um, love.
Not that there hasn't been a pile of "I was in love with a zombie" films before based on the sort of titles you see in the wiki's zombie comedy films category.
And now, this morning, an NFP I follow has released a video on transit issues in TO that totally makes me empathize with the shuffling undead (Heh. I think plenty of folks feel undead when doing that sort of shuffle).

You're not supposed to love the undead or see yourself in them you're supposed to kill them with a chainsaw!
Well, are not supposed to see yourself in them aside from that whole 1.5+ million hits on Google for 'zombies a critique of consumer culture' thing.
What other media is out there that tilts more toward 'love the zombie' than 'kill the zombie'? And, especially, which ones are more like Empathize with the Zombie? (Which, I think, is more challenging to do that Love the Zombie, which is easy to play for laughs).

And now, like a good bus transfer zombie, I need to shuffle off to work.
