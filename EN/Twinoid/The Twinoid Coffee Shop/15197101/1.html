When did you start playing the Elder Scrolls games?

Which one is your favourite? Why?

I started at Morrowind but was completely blown away and played it for years. Morrowind changed the way I looked at video games. It was pretty spot on what I had always wanted in a game. After Morrowind, I find it impossible to play other games from the same genre because they're just not good enough.

I adore the complete freedom to go wherever you want, whenever you want. Coupled with the possibility to interact with most things in the environment and the fact that you don't have to complete any quests - you can live your life and go with the flow.

I really dig the lore as well and became particularly enthusiastic about everything Argonian (and there isn't that much).
The Twin Lamp quest mod was simply magnificent.

I thoroughly enjoyed Oblivion. It was nice to explore the Imperial Province for a change, although a bit too 'normal' and flat after the wonders of Vvardenfell.
It doesn't quite compare with Morrowind as it got dumbed down in a major way. AlienSlof's mods were pretty awesome though.

Then Skyrim - again I was blown away and I adored it. I didn't even care about the land of Skyrim. It would definitely have been my LAST choice of land to explore in Tamriel, yet I had a blast exploring it and learning about all things Nord. I seriously don't give a damn about dragons either to be honest and felt they were just added to make the lowest common denominator in the fan base happy. Ah well.

It's understandable that they had to go with what sells. Between selling to a very wide audience or to a few RPG nuts, I suppose the choice is simple. Yet I would have loved if they continued more in the Morrowind vein, with mad places, tons of lore and genuinely challenging quests with places very hard to find.

I'm not sure that I'm terribly excited about the Elder Scrolls Online they are making but I have no doubt I'll be playing it far too much as well

The only complaint (and I'm not even sure if it is a complaint) is that they almost did it too well. The information, background, culture, etc. there is so much of it it just feels so dense it is hard to absorb it all. I mean I read every book or item I come across but there are so many places and dates and people mentioned, I feel like I am in history class all over again lol.


Slight tangent to back up my point: That is why I loved the game Lost Odyssey; you can tell they put a lot of effort into the story. If you haven't played the game you play a character who lost his memories (I know, original right?) but you lost your memories because you are actually immortal (you literally cannot die, after 3 turns you will come back to life, you lose if all your characters are KO at the same time). So with this game as you go throughout you see something that reminds you of a memory and you "dream" and recall something that happened to you before. However these dreams are completely optional, they are not necessary at all but they are extremely well written. I was actually looking forward to the next dream that I found. They would give the history of the character and the world but they did not overwhelm you with it by giving you 20 million dates and places. If someone were to ask me about the game itself I would say it is average; a completely typical Final Fantasy-like RPG. However the story and background is what made it a memorable game to me (also the way they handled being immortal and the leveling system but the background is my point lol). So the fact that they put in so much effort into something that the player can completely ignore if they wanted to speaks volumes to me.


That is the point I'm making with the Elder Scrolls series. You can completely ignore the universe if you so choose but even so they put a ton of work into it.
