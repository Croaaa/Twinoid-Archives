Can't say I'm a fan off the flippant remarks to Kiwis post.

Depression is such an internal struggle that outwardly you can seem fine. When someone asks "how are you?" I say good automatically. It's more of a hello than a question. When someone asks and can see that I'm not alright I play it off as something else, feel sick, lost in thought, ect. I've lived with depression for a long time. It comes and it goes but it's getting worse. This is just my experience but I think I can speak for others like me: I want help. I really do, but I can't find it. Friends and family tell you what you have in your life and that makes you more depressed. Yes I have so many great things, I have a home, food, a loving family. Yet I have an overwhelming desire to take my own life. Why? Well mental health. There is a chemical imbalance in my brain. I understand that. Knowing that does not change how I feel. I've googled suicide prevention many times and I hate the advice. It's obviously written by people that have no idea. It's one thing to understand depression, it's entirely different to live it. I tried to get help 8 yrs ago and got locked in a psych ward for my trouble. I kinda just deal with it now.

What I would love is a suicide hotline manned by suicidal people, the only ones that truly understand the feelings
