Haha you are putting in quite the effort. But it's fun.

You do see that if you have this superpower, it is either limited or unlimited.

If it is unlimited, that's awesome. You get to do whatever you want for no cost, no consequences.

If it is limited, that's also awesome. Equally awesome because if you had unlimited powers, everything bad that happens will be your fault.

All the world hunger, deaths, overpopulation, stupidity, wars, famine, boredom ... having unlimited powers puts all that on your shoulders because, hey, you can think them away. And you'd constantly have to keep track of everything because removing the negatives from one will affect the others.

(A baby died and you can save it, so you do; then you save all the babies in the world. Woops, overpopulation! Let's enlarge the size of the Earth. But now it's too hot so let's lower the temperature, realign the stars, etc.)

That's on a macro level. On a personal level it should be easy to see the consequences of unlimited powers as well. Like, for example, when do you let your best friend die?

Basically, limited powers (limited ability to give yourself convenience) gives you an excuse that having unlimited powers won't give you.

It's convenient to have both the excuse not to do the responsible things, and the ability to do everything with no cost. But you can't have both by nature.

So with the power to have everything convenient to you and the world twisting itself to accommodate your wishes, which do you get? It's inconvenient to not be convenient... so you should have both. But you can't.

That's the first contradiction.

That's the point I'm trying to disprove. It's not convenient to have a close and healthy relationship without fighting. The "cheap" way out is actually a fake way out, power or not. Look at it this way.

You and your sibling are of mindsets A and B right now, respectively. After you fight, you will be of mindsets C and D, respectively (you're maybe mad at them). In the long run, being in mindsets C and D will put you in mindsets E and F respectively. When you've reached E and F, you've reached a bond that is achieved through means that involve, but are not exclusive to, shared conflict.

If you fight, then your mindset goes from A to C, then to E eventually. Your sibling goes from B to D, then to F eventually. This is natural.

Now, taking the cheap way out, you will skip C and D. You jump from A to E right away, and the other person from B to F. There's no problem for the other person because they're under your control. But your mindset has been changed by your power. That's really important so I had to italicize that.

...

I wrote a ton of stuff to explain why that was important... I deleted it. Ultimately I decided to not explain it.

It should be easy to see without need of extensive proof why, given such a power, the fact that your own mind is not immune to your own power will lead to a contradiction and inconvenience further down the line. Essentially, you, right now, having this power, do not have control about what your actions will be in the future, because your mind is changeable.

Of course, the cheap way out is, "it's convenient to not have my mind be malleable by my own power." Contradiction, because it will invalidate "It would be convenient for you to have a close and health relationship without fighting," which we know is convenient.

And now we have run into a dilemma.

"It is convenient to have close and healthy relationships without fighting" allows you to bypass conflict altogether and be happy right away. However, as I showed above, it will require your power to manipulate your mind. You do not have perfect control of your actions.

"It's convenient to not have my mind be malleable by my own power" allows you to be in perfect control all the time, but it does not allow you to bypass conflict. You'll have to experience conflict to gain its benefits.

The cheap way out will be to say "it's convenient to be in perfect control at all times while automatically gaining the benefits from conflict."

Sadly, that's not possible. I've already shown it above. To be in perfect control at all times means you cannot automatically gain the benefits from conflict. To automatically gain the benefits from conflict means you can't be in control at all times.

Under the assumption of everything is convenient for you, we've run into a situation where any way we choose, it will always carry an inconvenience. There's the contradiction.
