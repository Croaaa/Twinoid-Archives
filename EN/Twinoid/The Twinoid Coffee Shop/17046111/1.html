Sand's Demo Version 1.3

Q&A

Recommended things to do in the sand's demo

Remember for the demo only, you will spawn with a great healing item, so if you have to heal use them, it is for demo purposes only. so don't expect it to be in the full game, the item it's self will be in the game, but you will not automatically spawn with it when you start the game in the finished product

If the boss of the demo if giving you trouble, the mutant, remember to save before putting the keycard in the slot, heal up, and use the flare gun,

If the partner A.I is bugging you, Ditch him, or use partner commands, how to use a partner command, be near him, not an easy task, click the action button, Z, and click follow, he will follow you in a earthbound fashion, at least until you go to another area that is, some times the follow command does not work in some areas, have no idea why sadly,

Yes sand's does work on mac, just follow the instructions provided in the folder mac help, it will be complex but if you do want to play sand's it is the only way, sadly i do not know if it work's for linux, witch sadly it probably doesn't

Ok, so I noticed the problem right from the moment I started downloading. The game's size is just too damn big and when I even finished downloading I found out the problem - you didn't reduce the sound quality. Seriously the sound and music files take up like 3/4 of the game's size, if you're trying to make an SNES like RPG at least try to make it about the size of one (Hint: it's 16 MB though i don't think any game used even half of that with the largest one's being about 6 MB). It might sound strange to reduce the quality of your audio but trust me, sounds that actually fits the game's graphics are a lot better and less jarring than what you currently have.

The second problem I noticed was immediately after I launched the game and that was that for me it ran in full 1080p glory which immediately made the backgrounds, sprites and portraits horribly low resolution and the text nigh unreadable (Though despite of that I have a weird suspicion that there might just be too much of it). So if at all possible try to make it windowed so that it wouldn't need to scale it's images (Your backdrops appear to be about 320 x 240 so you might wanna keep that in mind).

And finally I couldn't get out of the very first area because of how messy the level design is and combined with the inability to read most of the text I simply couldn't understand my objectives. So I would probably suggest for you try and keep the levels more linear (with some side paths for extra items of course) and objectives more intuitive (Hell you always seem to have a bloke besides you so you could add an ability for him to remind you your objectives when interacted with).

But anyway despite of all the criticism I still think that this is a pretty cool project so good luck with it.
