If you are making it an arctic winter sort of game then I have an idea that may be interesting:

Instead of all the players starting together they start seperate across the map.

_______________________________________

As the game gets harder as time passes (due to the increasing cold) people will want to join together to build bigger and bigger camp sites (eventually building permanent buildings).

The problem is you don't know the location of someone (or who else is playing) until you have met them them (or met someone whose met them) so you slowly group together across the map.

Wolves also grow in numbers around the map making it dangerous to scavenge, especially in the forests where the best firewood is.

____________________________________________

Here's how I see it working:
Day 1:
Wake up alone in the cold, find some firewood, explore a few zones, maybe walk into some wolves (not many around at the beginning), maybe find tracks of another player, build a fire and camp.

Day 2:
Find that player whose tract you spotted yesterday, maybe they've already found someone else, the 3 of you build a bigger fire and a little tent to camp in (and carry around with you)

Day 5:
By this point around 10 of you have found each other and you've started to build some permanent structures. Some people have died as they were unable to stay warm enough at night alone (couldn't find the resources to build a big enough fire and no one found them to help, or they were caught by the wolves and couldn't escape).

Other people begin to find the town and join but some people come and simply raid it for the firewood and other resources

Day 8:
Town has around 15 people now but there is another town of 10 people somewhere else on the map that sprung up separately. They don't seem to want to join your town but have started sending raiders. Your town has built permanent locks to protect the most vital resources from them and has invested in some big walls to protect.

Day 15:
Your people steal from the other town, stopping their ability to remain warm overnight. Some have joined your town, others you didnt allow in for their stealing previously and they died that night

Day 19:
It finally became to cold and people started to panic, hording blankets and firewood for their own tents and causing others to die of the cold

Day 21:
Despite your grand fire and mountain of blankets it's not enough to hold out from the cold and you die. But being the last person alive you are rewarded with a gift of some sort

I saw it as a single zone thing still but could be build by a lake or in the woods or in a cave

Basicly at the start of the game everyone has a lit campfire that will go out after nightfall (nighfall is when everyon needs to be snuggled up).

1. Campfire (to keep warm)
2. Chest (to hold items)
3. Tent (which you can carry with you)
4. Planners study (permanent building that unlocks other buildings)

Once the planners study is built that zone is considered a town. If that zone has no-one in it overnight and no fire lit then the town freezes and becomes useless. (Except for the items on the ground and buildings that can be torn down)

Entering a town is possible unless there is a gate. Once in a town you can use the bank, mess with the fire (putting it out if you want) and help with construction

JasperNL

PHP is slow, bulky and buggy. The app will be very database-heavy, so executing "open database" on every page load is terrible for the performance of the app, and scales in no way. With PHP you're going to need a monster-server because the server is so CPU-intensive.

The use of JQuery will only amplify this, as there are many pageloads.

Write your app in Java, NodeJS, Go or Scala. Can even be written in C++ if you use the right database. As I understood you prefer to use C, so that's great.

For 'dynamic' web applications, I would recommend going for NodeJS. Make use of some good framework for web handling (Express is the common choice) and the choice of the database does not matter.
The most important is that you can set the database open all the time, and query one and the same connection continuously.
