Exactly. All drugs have different efficacy and side-effects for different people. Everyone's body is different.

I'm not saying cannabis is entirely bad. It can certainly be beneficial for many people under many circumstances. But it's important to separate the facts from the sweeping generalizations of starry-eyed stoner propaganda.

When Sigmund Freud first discovered cocaine, he stated that everyone should be on it because it's fucking awesome. (I'm paraphrasing here.) But at that time, he didn't understand the rebound effects, tolerance, addictiveness and risk of deadly overdose. Now we know that cocaine usage has a tendency to destroy people's lives.

My point in saying that is that when people have a positive experience they want to preach the good word to the whole world. But we have to consider those perspectives with skepticism and rational objectivity. It's valid to say "This stuff is really interesting. Let's study it so we can fully understand the benefits and risks." But when you hear people say "Fuck yeah, this shit is a miracle!" that should raise some big red flags. Just tune that noise out.
