Chapter 1, NonHumble Begginings

In a faraway land, there is a village. This is the Village of Estwal, where heroes are few. But a few daring souls, the future heroes of the land, reside here. This land is peaceful, until one day evil monsters, warlords, and wizards decided to strike. The armies of the land are defending, but things are looking grim...

Welcome to Heroes Journey, where you will encounter many monsters, dragons, and such.
To start off, create a character and get to adventuring!

Turns
A player may take a turn every day, and can post again 24 hours after they post. Turns do not stack, however, and can consist of moving far, a battle, or something other of the sort.

Races
The game has a number of races, each with its own powers and abilities.
Human- A short lived creature compared to other species, but their lives are filled with glory, hope, and dreams. Can once per battle channel his or her rage and deal 2 additional damage on a hit. Is persuasive.

Elf- One of the most respectable creatures, they are very old and have long lives. Gains a +1 attack and damage bonus when using a bow. Is nimble.

Dwarf- Seen as a disgusting creatures by other races, dwarfs often serve as miners that often completely excavate entire mountains. Can once per battle deal +4 damage on a confirmed hit.

More coming soon!
Occupations- How you get paid, you have to learn a trade of some sort!

Hitman: Gets paid to kill a target.
Explorer: Gets paid to ap or explore newlands.
Inventor: Uses new technologies to invent things.
Veteranairian: Cares for animals.
BodyGuard: Paid to protect.
Doctor: Paid to heal the hurt.
More coming soon!

Character Creation
To create a character, fill this out:

Name:
Race:
Occupation:
Age:
Personality:
Weapon:

You begin with 10 gold, a weapon, 1 level, and a power based on your occupation.

Battle
Announce that you would like to fight, and a battle will begin. To attack, roll a d20 against the defence of the monster or player being attacked. If you hit, roll a d6 for damge or another dice depending on the weapon or power. If you hit, the monster misses. If you miss, the monster hits. This is to stop tedious dice rolling.

I am 100 percent sure rules will need to be changed or added, so go ahead and get a character!
