Colt1911

The Wheel of Time series by Robert Jordan.
The influence of Lord of the Rings is very strong in this one, but aside from the setting, Robert Jordan was able to bring into it such a skill in world building, you easily get lost in the intricacies of it, and that's not even counting how easy the plot flows.
There's tons of influences from many mythologies too, but personalized into the world. For example, to gain knowledge, Matt Cauthon was hung from a tree with a spear to his chest. Reminiscent of Odin.

The Hunger Games trilogy by Suzanne Collins.
My favorite series. Ever. EVER!
Set in a post apocalyptic time, this book explores themes of violence, poverty, big brother is watching you, and to what extent will people go for some entertainment. Very gritty.
The occasional futuristic technology aside, it feels like despite being post apocalyptic, it's already happening right now, making it a very real and heavy societal commentary in my eyes.

Love this thread as I am an avid book reader!

My favorite book hands down is Johnny Got His Gun by Dalton Trumbo. I read it ten times back to back and countless more since. I keep starting to type a brief synopsis but cannot. You have to just read it. Might be confusing at first but all will be revealed. Well worth your time!


A Clockwork Orange by Anthony Burgess. It's always a good read. It's written in a language that my sister once best described to me as "How we might speak if the Russians won the cold war" It's hard to understand at first but you pick up the meanings as you read. I loved learning the slang as I made my way through the book. As soon as you finish you want to start again with your new knowledge of the books language.

American Gods by Neil Gaiman. Recently read this one. Full of mythology. The idea is that the settlers brought their old god's with them when they came to America but belief in them has faded over time and so has their power. New gods have arisen and a war is coming. Much more to it than that I cannot do it justice. (Neil Gaiman also wrote my Favorite graphic novel series The Sandman which is...just...Wow!)
