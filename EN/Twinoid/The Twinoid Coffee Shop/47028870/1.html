Some time in the future, there is a single town that is attacked every night by a horde of zombies. They build up their defenses, then the zombies come and tear them apart. Then they do it again, every day and night. The last hope for humanity can't last much longer as the horde is getting stronger every night. They must survive by being quick witted and tough, but the zombies are becoming stronger, faster, and smarter.

Welcome to Zombie Game
In this game, you can play as either a human or a zombie. If you are a human, your job is to endure the hoard as long as possible. If you are a zombie, your job is to destroy the city in as few days as possible.

There will be separate private chats for both the humans and the zombies, so they can coordinate when needed. To begin the game, we will need 4-5 humans and 4-5 zombies.

Actions
During the Day, zombies can train and humans can build.
The human army, by default, starts with 1 Ballista, 50 soldiers, and 20 snipers. A wall is also pre-constructed on each side of the town, North, South, East, and West. Snipers are settled on the walls, and can kill zombies before they get to them. If a wall is destroyed, all snipers positioned on that wall are lost as well. Soldiers can be positioned inside or outside of each wall. If soldiers are positioned outside, they will die before the wall takes damage. If they are inside the wall, they will defend the town after the walls are destroyed.

List of Actions Per Day
Human Actions
Build- Build a new building. You may roll a d6 and put that many building points into a building, or a d10 if you are a builder.
List of Buildings
____________________
Wall- Provides 30 defence to the town. Takes 20 Builder Points (BP) to build. Zombies will know where all walls are built.
Ballista- Can shoot d10 zombies before they get to the gates during the attack. 15 BP.
Barracks- Generates d20 soldiers per day. 25 BP to build.
Catapult- Can kill d20 zombies per night- 30 BP to build.
Traps- Kills 10 of the first zombies to attack the town from one direction.
____________________
Reinforce- Repair a wall for 1d6 defence.
Zombie Actions
Train- Train a number of zombies.
List of Zombies
____________________
Normal Zombies- The bulk of the hoard. You may train d6 of these at time.
Giants- Can withstand 5 times as much as Normals, and deal 5 times as much damage. Are trained 1 at a time.
Sploders- Always attack walls first, each one that gets through deals 5 damage to the wall. Are trained in sets of d4.
____________________

The PCs
Each human PC plays the head guard on one of the walls, NESW. A fifth player is the mayor who decides which defenses go where.

Each zombie PC plays a zombie captain that leads the assault on one of the walls. They are special, they have 20 life, and deal 5 damage per attack. There is also a fifth zombie leader, which determines who attacks what gate.


The Attack
After every player has completed an action, the night will begin. The mayor of the town will determine where to put the soldiers, ballistas, and other defensive structures. They can be placed on the North, East, South, or West gates. The zombies won't know where what defences are, as this is decided in private human chat. Each night, the zombies choose which zombies to send at which gate. This way, the humans try to predict which zombies are coming in which direction.

NOTE
Zombies pick their attack numbers first, then the town gets a report for each wall on how many zombies there are. For example, if 20 zombies are coming at the North Wall, you may get, 15-30 zombies are coming from the North! These reports get more accurate if there is a scout in the town.

HUMAN CLASSES
Builder- See actions and building.
Scout- Zombie reports are more accurate.
Soldier- You will kill the first 5 zombies wherever you are stationed.
Doctor- You may heal d8 dead zombies from the dead and convert them to soldiers.

ZOMBIE CLASSES
Giant- You have 10 attack power and 10 defence.
Infector- You may raise d8 dead humans from your attack and convert them to basic zombies.
Shooter- You can shoot down snipers before they shoot.
