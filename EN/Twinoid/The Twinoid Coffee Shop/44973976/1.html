During the great technological revolution of 2089, when both economy and living quality increased, Earth had become the home to 24 billion people. Although most governments praised all forms of equality- based upon gender, race, sexual orientation, religion and other factors- humanity faced another issue. Human individuality.
Several governments took example of the internet, where every single person required a single, available username to use a website, and, in 2090, 6 governments (The republic of Sweden, The allied Baltic states, The Canadian Union, The Mediterranean isles, Republic of Korea and the Chinese-Russian alliance) joined in a cross-country law adjustment, demanding that in each of these countries every single person required an ‘unoccupied username’, meaning that each person born in said countries had to be given a name that no other citizen had.
The 6 governments, now collegially referred to as ‘The Username Convention (UC)’, allowed for, theoretically, any name to be given. This resulted in inappropriate names to be given, due to the generation giving these names out being the generation during which online powers turned to actual, physical powers. For those of you reading this further into the future, the following websites were actual countries for certain periods of time: 4chan, Facebook, tumblr, Google (Historians claim it could’ve become as powerful as the Chinese-Russian alliance, if it weren’t for certain mistakes and coincidences.), Yahoo and Reddit.
Due this issue, in 2091 the UC started a new intelligence agency- the A.D.M.I.N (Association for Democratic Moderators of Internet Names). This agency was responsible for observing and moderating names given to the citizens of the convention. If a violation was detected, the name was forcibly changed and the parents/guardians naming the child were punished accordingly.
In 2092, five more governments joined the UC: USA, Mexico, the Brazilian federation, Japan and The British Kingdoms. More governments joined from year 2093 to year 2107. In 2107 every country had officially become a member of UC.So, that's the very basic information on the world at hand. If it seems all nice and pretty- it sure isn't. Goals/Military Conflict:
The UC/A.D.M.I.N. have the goal of restoring peace in the region, but doing so by force. The A.D.M.I.N. were specifically sent to deal with the Anonymous and find out more information, but their surveillance and espionage abilities will come in handy.

The Anonymous have the main task of defending their towns, while doing their best to keep their ranks as intact as they can. They mainly face the Rebels, then the UC, and only then do they care for dealing with the civilians.

The Rebels are aiming to take down the Anonymous at their core, steal their equipment and plant the seed of rebellion into the area.

The civilians have no particular goal, although their alignment and general actions are determined by which area they come from.

Mechanics of play:
There are five modes to deal with -

1)Roleplay Mode: You'll communicate with other people in the region, find out information and generally do things that don't directly affect the way the game progresses - of course, handing information to a member of a different faction may change the way the game goes just slightly.
Enter this mode by simply typing something using the 'Roleplay' function on the forum.

2)Interaction Mode: You'll be using this mode a whole lot, most likely. Whenever you enter a town, you will be provided with the following basic options:
Rummage through trash
Search streets
Consult with civilian
Consult with soldier
Request Equipment (You may specify what kind or leave empty for a chance of general improvement. It may be difficult to pull off as a civilian, and pretty much impossible in hostile areas.)
Look for fight
Perform Espionage
Recommend action to people

You can, however, try and do a specific action not mentioned here. I'll do my best to make a reaction to it, although it may not have much of an effect.

Enter this mode by typing 'Interaction: [ACTION]' in your post., where [ACTION] is what you wish to do.

3)Combat Mode: What war is all about. Combat is decided via dice rolls. You can roll attack or defense. Which dice you roll depends on your weapon/equipment.
Enter this mode by encountering something via interaction mode or travel mod or by getting dragged into battle.

4)Travel Mode: You travel from place to place in this mode. Fairly simple. There are set distances between each two locations, and you spent one turn moving 1 instance. For example, the distance between #404 and Equality is 1, while a direct move from Raban to Kashalana is the combined instances from Raban to Kintar and from Kintar to Kashalana times 1.5[(RaKi+KiKa)x1.5).
You can stop at any moment and attempt the actions you can do in towns/villages, but there is very little chance of them succeeding in random places.
You can enter this mode by typing 'Travel: [LOCATION]', where [LOCATION] is your desired destination.

5)Battle Mode: This is the mode I'll be using the entire time, playing the roles of the war leaders/military groups/armies. Each army moves around the same way you do in travel mode, with the exception when the MCB moves, where the amount of instances is tripled.
When a battle begins and you are in it, you will be forced into combat mode. After finishing the fight, assuming you survive, you may choose to either continue combat, flee, tend your wounds, await further instructions or surrender. ake note, that while most fights from direct Combat Mode are going to be 1v1's (With exceptions, of course.), while war... isn't fair. You may end up against an entire group all by yourself, or vice versa.

Game ticks: Every week is a day. Every day you make 7 actions- one per day. This is the most efficient and easy way to have a consistent flow of gameplay.

Short look at combat: As stated before, each day counts as a tick and 7 ticks make on in-game-day. During combat, you may roll a combat dice to attack or defense dice to defend. You may not attack twice, but you can defend twice.
Certain combat or defensive advantages can take effect, such as better position, weapon better for close/long range, a well-protected location, so on and so forth.
Depending on the firing mode of weapons, you shoot different amounts of bullets, changing the multiplier:
Automatic - Full magazine/clip dice (Example: UC X30 FAR has 30 bullets. Since there is no d30, you must roll either 3 d10's or d10 and d20.)
Semi-Automatic - Half magazine/clip dice (Example: Class V Glock 32 has 24 bullets, you must roll d12.) or no multiplier.
Rapid-Fire - Quarter magazine/clip or no multiplier.
Single-Fire - No multiplier.

The damage done to the opponent is calculated like so:

(M (C) + A - D + Y) : 2 = X
Where:
M- Multiplier
C- Combat/Weapon roll value
A- Attacker's advantage
D- Defender's defense value
Y- Defender's advantage
X- Damage dealt to defender. (Negative numbers are ignored)

Don't worry, I'll be the one calculating this. It's just good for you to known how it comes to be.
