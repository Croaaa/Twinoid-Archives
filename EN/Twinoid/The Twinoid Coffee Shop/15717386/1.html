I'll just list some shows that I like and some shows that I think are decent since everyone has different tastes. No bad shows though lol. Actually with all the shows I am listing if you don't like ANY of the shows I put down then I can't help you because we apparently have completely different tastes

Walking Dead - Just saw this a few months ago in a marathon on tv. It isn't amazing but if you enjoy "realistic" zombie shows then this is a good one. Don't go into it expecting a ton of shooting and explosions, mostly the social and survival aspects (like Die2Nite lol)

Psych - About a guy who is hyper-observant and uses that to pretend to be a psychic and fools the police into believing it and hiring him for cases. Opens a psychic private investigator business with his best friend. Good show in general but the jokes flying between the two friends is what makes the show, pure comedy.

Warehouse 13 - In this show objects of famous people (famous as in Napoleon, not famous as in Lady Gaga lol) can sometimes get supernatural powers when that person dies. Like a famous psychiatrist's chair makes a person tell the truth whenever they sit in it or Edgar Allen Poe's pen can make people die horrific deaths that match up with some of his stories, etc. This show follows a secret government agency that tries to go around and collect these objects while covering it up and protecting people but don't think M.I.B. secret agents, more like a dysfunctional family going around. It is a good show and you really start to like the characters.

Leverage - A very good show following a group of thieves that through various circumstances end up working together and decide to start helping people (think Robin Hood). Each person has a job in the group, there is a mastermind, a hacker, a thief, a grifter, and the muscle but they are supposedly talented enough that they can't be completely pigeon holed like that, they take on each other's jobs as the situation requires. Very funny and begin to act almost like a dysfunctional family.

Sanctuary - I actually haven't seen much of this but from what I've seen it is a pretty good show. Another show with supernatural beings that the general public doesn't know about. There are werewolves, vampires, a highly intelligent caveman, and a lot of creatures that don't have a commonly known classification (at least that I know of, I think they create some of their own).

Alphas - Another show that I don't watch all the time but when I do it seems fairly interesting. Show is about how there are people who have "evolved" or have genetic defects that give them super human powers and they are called "Alphas" (the powers are highly varied so can't list them all but there are some interesting ones I never would have thought of). The show follows a group of "Alphas" that work for the government (sort of). There is no real "central" plot arc because it has changed so much (at one point they were trying to capture alphas, now they are helping some but not others...). Haven't seen enough of this to give a real good description but I feel like even if I did it would be hard to summarize lol.

Haven - Another good show from what I've seen but I haven't watched every episode. This is another one that is really hard to describe. So actually I won't even try this one lol. Here read this.

Lost Girl - This is a decent show from what I've seen. I still can't decide if this is a mature show or an immature show lol. What I mean by that is that there are sex scenes and other mature content, and sometimes they are done in a mature manner while other times it just seems like fanservice so some teenage guy can say "Yay bewbies!" Anyways this is another show that has supernatural creatures (werewolves, sirens, succubus, ogres, etc.) and they are called Fae (any non-human is called Fae) and they form almost like a secret government that controls the world partially that keeps the normal public from learning that they exist. However they are divided into 2 camps called "Light Fae" and "Dark Fae" but it isn't purely good vs evil. Really it is just Dark Fae don't care about killing humans if they need to eat them (some fae feed off humans to survive, some don't need to but they basically look at humans like cattle) while the Light Fae still feed off humans but they try to not do any real harm. The show follows a succubus who was an orphan who didn't learn what she was until later and she refuses to join the Light Fae or the Dark Fae so the story follows her walking the line between the two just trying to survive.

Eureka - This show just ended which makes me sad Good show about the government setting up a secret city that is populated entirely be geniuses (think of a town full of Albert Einsteins, even the kids). As such the town is advanced in technology and are always doing dangerous experiments that often go wrong. The town's sheriff (at one point his deputy is a realistic android) ends up stopping whatever disaster is happening with the help of the scientists because the sheriff is the only person in the town with a normal IQ. So basically the normal plot of an episode is: scientists accidentally cause disaster, scientists can't solve it in time, they dumb it down to the sheriff who is standing there, the sheriff thinks of some average joe analogy that somehow helps the scientists come up with some complicated solution that solves the problem and prevents the world from being destroyed. Also the sheriff is usually sent into some life threatening situation to do the physical aspect of the solution. This is a terrible summary of the show that doesn't do it justice so I highly recommend watching the show for yourself to see.

Merlin - You can imagine what this show is about. Merlin the famous wizard but instead of showing him being this all powerful wizard helping King Arthur control his kingdom, the show takes place when Merlin and King Arthur are still young so King Arthur is still a Prince and Merlin is just learning about his powers.

Falling Skies - A pretty good show about the world after an alien invasion. Aliens invaded and destroyed all of civilization with the exception of very small resistances, the story follows one group of people (well a battalion of people, some soldiers, a lot of civilians, but the story focuses on only a few as the leaders). The story is just them trying to survive and try and fight the aliens as best the can in the hopes that one day they leave. Think of it almost like the American Revolution with aliens A very small rebel army trying to fight a vastly superior invading army by using hit and run tactics and hiding out from the enemy while trying to find food and water to survive.

Shows continued:

Stargate SG-1 - Old show that ended a long time ago but still one of my favorite show growing up. Show about finding an alien device on Earth that allows you to travel instantaneously to planets light years away. Sort of like Star Trek (granted I have not seen a lot of Star Trek lol) but set in current times and has mostly a squad of 5 doing the fighting as opposed to giant spaceships (although the "evil alien race" has a lot of spaceships). Also there is a spin off Stargate Atlantis if you like this one. There is another spin off called Stargate Universe but while it takes place in the same universe (no pun intended) as the other stargate shows, the show itself is a different type. Stargate Universe has a large group of people getting accidentally stranded on an abandoned alien ship so they are trying to survive, learn how to control the ship, and find their way home.

Flashpoint - A decent show that follows a SWAT team doing their job (it is still fictional, it isn't like Cops lol). Pretty good because unlike other things I've seen (the movie SWAT.....) it actually tells the story of everyone involved (gunman, hostage, SWAT team, etc). Also shows the impact of the job on the SWAT team members (psychological damage). I think it only has like one season though so there aren't a ton of shows.

Supernatural - I watch this if there is nothing else on, not great but not terrible. Basically imagine a world where every supernatural or religious creatures really existed (vampires, werewolves, angels, demons, the Four Horsemen, Lucifer, Gabriel, etc). Two brothers travel around finding and killing these creatures (usually not angels but...) since they are usually killing humans. As the story goes on it becomes this huge "stop the apocalypse, save mankind" type story but near the beginning it is just a "find what creature is doing this and kill it". They go into a lot of old religion and folklore which can be interesting at times.

Faceoff - If you like competition or reality shows you may like this one. Honestly I hate them but if this is on I will tune in for the last 10 minutes of the show. Basically it is a competition between make-up/special effects people from movies. They give them some crazy theme and task they must do then give them a few days to do it. The show is entertaining at first just because it is interesting to see how movie make up is done (as in creating alien creatures, not adding eye-liner to some actress). However I stopped watching because they keep cutting to the contestants talking trash about other contestants and it just got annoying. The last 10 minutes or so is when they present their work which is the only part I still enjoy lol. The creatures they create are freaking amazing at times.

Monk - Another old show that has ended. Another crime show but instead of it being like CSI, it is about this one guy who at one point was an AMAZING detective but something happened causing him to have a mental breakdown and now he has a lot of problems (a major one being germophobic so he can't touch anything). Show just follows him as he tries to solve crimes without touching anything and freaking out when he does, pretty entertaining.

NCIS - Another CSI show, nothing special about it. I just like it because I like the characters.

I can only think of a few favorites at the moment.

LOST - A sci-fi drama revolving around a group of people who have to survive on an island after a plane-crash. Action, adventure, mystery, suspense. Well written. Great characters/actors. A very in-depth and involving story-line. 120 episodes. Some say it's hard to understand/follow; I personally didn't have a problem with it. You just have to think through it a bit. Definitely one of, if not my favorite, series. Once you get into it, you might not be able to leave your couch for a while...

Prison Break - A man is falsely accused of a murder and sent to prison where he awaits execution. His brother, an intellectual genius/structural engineer, is convinced of innocence and formulates plans to break him out. Another great series. It gets a bit slow halfway through the seasons, but picks back up.
You will find out early on that the brother gets something very helpful tattooed across his body...

Robin Hood - A series by BBC. Captures the fabled Robin Hood pretty nicely. Some decent medieval action. Overall a good series (not the best, but good) if you like the typical GoodvsBad conflict. Too bad it was canceled after Season 3.

White Collar - Just started watching this recently. From what I've seen so far, it's pretty good. Follows a man who is finally caught after several years of playing cat-and-mouse with the FBI. In exchange for some criminal information, he signs onto a work-release program in which he assists a FBI agent catch other criminals.
Unlike the previous three shows, White Collar is more like your typical crime-type TV show, in which the episodes don't really build into an actual story. In LOST for example, watching 1-2 episodes out of order could prove to be very confusing...

I know White Collar is still airing... Not sure if repeats of the previous 3 shows are aired. They are all on Netflix for sure. (Netflix>TV IMO )
