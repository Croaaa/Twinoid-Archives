After the failure of my last thread, I thought I'd start something a lot more passive. So I give you...

Roll to Dodge.

Concept is simple.

1.You decide on an action.
2.You use a d6.
3.The d6 determines the result of your action.

0 (Attainable with -1 modifier): Ouch- You suffer an instantaneous heart-attack and die on the spot.
1: Uber-failure- you don't just fuck up. You fuck up BAD. Odds are you will die or be seriously wounded. (-1 to next roll)
2: Failure-You fail at what you attempted to do.
3: Marginal success: You do succeed at what you were trying to do;However it is in a less favorable way.
4: Succeed-Simple enough
5: Extreme Succees-You do what you were trying to do REALLY well. (+1 to next roll)
6: CRITICAL!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!!-You do what you were trying to do really well, sometimes to the point of it being a bad thing.
7 (Attainable with +1 modifier): Chuck would be proud- Some huge coincidence leads to your action having major positive effects on the world.


Example: I eat some cake.
0: Heart-attack. You are dead.
1: Nope. The cake tries to eat YOU instead.
2: you weren't hungry enough to want cake today
3: It was kinda stale.
4: Tasty.
5: The cake was delicious.
6: You probably weren't meant to eat it all...
7: AS you eat the cake, a single crumb falls down the drain. That crumb flies into the ocean after going through the sewer system, and lands on a lump of algae. This hypercharges its growth, leading to it producing a giant tree who's leaves cure AIDS. Lucky you!

As well as this, if you launch a hostile action against another player, they have to Roll To Dodge. Assuming the attack you make is on target, they then roll for themselves. The results are:
0: Critical hit-Same as below
1: Critical hit- The attacker got a direct hit, killing the target instantly.
2: Hit- The attack hits, and the target is wounded. -1 to next roll. The battle continues.
3: Light Hit- The attack does hit, but lightly. The battle continues.
4: Miss- Simple
5: Compromising miss- The attacker misses, leaving themselves open for a counterattack. +1 to next roll for target.
6: Auto-counter- You do not roll the following turn; Instead you automatically get to launch another attack against the target.
7: Ya missed me...- you kill the attacker instantly with some badass counterattack.

I'll update this each day.


The city is a simple place, where the citizens of Twinoid live. But each of their lives carries several different actions, each of which seems to be governed by a diceroll, however odd that sounds. I am sure some big calamity will result from these actions, leading to some vague plot development (Nice fourthwall break, cold). Well, citizen. Good luck!
