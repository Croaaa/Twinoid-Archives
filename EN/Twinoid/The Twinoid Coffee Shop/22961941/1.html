I have been thinking of discarding the experiment Z.E.T.S. project, and writing a book called "Swarmers".
The swarmers are extra-terrestrial bugs, creatures similar to earths arachnids. They crawl in peoples brains, taking over they're bodies and multiplying.
In cold environment, they stay inside the warm human body, but when its hot enough outside, they start swarming around the person. They have hardened shells, so when they swarm around, it is almost impossible killing the person.
Melee is suicide, because they will start crawling on you.
Seconds after the first swarmer gets in your head, you will start seeing red, have nausea, cough up blood and peeling off your skin. Thru these peeled off holes, the swarmers crawl out in hot environment.
Personally, i think this idea is better than zombies.
What do you guys think?

PROLOUGE


It’s Year 2017. 70 years had passed since the Roswell incident. All the conspiracy theories have been disproven, the event was forgotten.
The event was caused by a Mogul, a classified US government Project, surveillance balloon crashing at Roswell, New Mexico. The balloons task was to detect sound waves from Soviet atomic bomb tests.

The government of US announced it as a weather balloon, and the event was forgotten up until the year 1978.
That year, physicist and ufologist Stanton T. Friedman brought the event back in to the light, by interviewing Major Jesse Marchel, who was involved in recovering the debris in 1947. The man expressed his belief, that military was covering up a retrieving of an alien spacecraft.

While the story was all false, it served a great purpose- hiding the research about what made that balloon crash…

While the story was all false, it served a great purpose- hiding the research about what made that balloon crash…

The scientists in Roswell have been studying debris samples, looking at the crash site and interviewing civilians ever since the event. The debris they collected was heavily radioactive. The reason behind that remains unknown.

Every 10 years a new team is issued to study the debris. The research is top secret, it goes on inside a top secret shelter under the crash site.

The main research object is a small, spherical rock. It isn’t like any meteor ever seen before. It seems to have something inside, but no matter what any team has tried past 70 years, nothing seems to work.

Will the next team succeed?
