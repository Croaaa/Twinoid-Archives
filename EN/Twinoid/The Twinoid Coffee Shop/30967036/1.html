Rant

Wrest ye not from this mortal coil, all that shan't be awarded to those who succumb to trepidation, but forego the sinful pleasures of lust and envy, for they are the trail from whence one can never truly return. The devil's playthings they are.

Blissful empty agony, whilst thou howl at the moon with me, lupine fiend? Join me on my podium, six footlengths underground, and stare at the stars, those precious few we can behold.

Stab at me, thrust thy blade into my back, for I have already fallen and will strive no more. Is it such, this life, that merely that which is of no consequence to others is freely available, and that which matters will be kept from you, lest you break through the walls put there by human unwill, malice and greed? Et tu, vite?

A poem I wrote for my girlfriend.

Tunes in Our Hearts
I imagine one day that I'll go to a store and get a guitar,
And I'll sing a song I wrote about how beautiful you are.
Sadly, I don't know how to play it,
And I cannot sing worth a shit.
But to us, the music will be the most beautiful art.
Because the music that we hear are the tunes in our hearts.

Back in high school, we had to write this monster balled/ode/etc.
Of course, my pushover of an English teacher changed the assignment to whatever short free-verse crap we could push out as soon as people started complaining.
And of course, she only changed it after I finished my 100-liner epic ballad.
And of course, she loved it so much she made me read it for the class.
And of course, she didn't let me change the pronouns when I read it so it didn't sound lesbian. No, that just wouldn't be authentic.
And this is only the second most awkward school presentation I did.

I believe I still have that goddamned balled on my computer somewhere. I'll post it if I can find it. Meanwhile enjoy this original poem: perfect for the angry breakup

Roses are red;
Violets aren't blue.
Poetry lies
And so do you.
