Mush a des chances d'être traduit, mais en attendant, les joueurs qui ne parlent pas français ne sont pas censés jouer (voir ici). Et c'est normal... c'est gênant pour eux parce qu'ils ne comprennent rien à ce qui se passe et ne peuvent pas communiquer, et pour les autres joueurs qui ne comprennent pas ce qu'ils disent.

SoItBegins, there was a problem about a month ago when a few Spanish speakers tried to play ; they didn't understand anything, and nobody could understand what they said. So they were forbidden to play.

Communication is essential in Mush. There's an in-game chat and private conversations where people give information and orders, ask for help, choose what projects or research they must do, investigate to find out who the Mush are...

It's also a complex game and there's a lot to read and understand (the advice given by the game or other players, all the information given in the Help section...) It's impossible to understand all this without speaking French, so it's impossible to play the game well. Otherwise you would play without knowing what's going on.

Sure, you can learn or use a translator, but it's not a simple game where you can read the rules once or twice and then play without needing to read or write anything. You need to read and write all the time.

Besides, if someone doesn't speak French, he may get reported by the other players and banned from the game.

So if I were you, I'd wait for Mush to be translated.
