Hi,

I and most of us don't know who is Kino, since we were not playing in the same "Monde" (world).

Regarding your question about potions, it only gives you the previous state of Health of the one you're currently in. That means that if you take a potion will being in "Bonne" health (good), it will give you an "Excellente" health (excellent). But if you wait for your health to be "Mauvaise" (bad), it will give you a "Bonne" health (good) and not excellent.

When you die, your kingdom disappear : that means that all of your territories become "sauvages" (wilds) without any troops on them. You have then an option giving you the possibility to join another "Monde" (world) and start again a new kingdom with new neighours

DeathLegion
Thank you, I hadn't seen that message

So, yes, when you die, you die completely, all that shall remain of your past glory is a rumor of battles in your mind and the ones of your enemies and friends.

Another way to save these acts of honor is to record them in the "Forum carte" (map forum), which you can access when you're looking at the world map : I myself write RPs about my current world's main events in our map forum, for fun

About your asks of "what's the point in playing if we know everything will be started again in the end ?", I'd answer that it is just the way games are supposed to be played. Let's imagine your Empire would last forever. Do you think new players would be OK to play with you, when you have dominated the whole world and have thousands of men to support your campaigns ?

I think the answer is : no. Everything must come to an end, so that every player has the opportunity to become a legend and rewrite history. It's vanquishers' duty to control the fate of their kingdom, and losers' one to be subordinate of the previous ones, but when you die, no matter if you were surrounded by a crazy Emperor or being that same Emperor, you just have the equal chances to expand in a new world.

Just to conclude, you're actually in a "debutant" level world (beginner => a soldier icon). After your first death, you'll probably go on a "normal" level world (an archer icon), and if you do well there, maybe you could go on a "moyenne" level map (medium => a balist icon). That means that you're next life will be with experimented players : it may be harder but it will be funnier and you'll have less chances to be with an inactive for "vassal".

Also, your health evolution is totally random. One could live for 80 years in good health and then die in a few years, while another one could live be in bad health at the age of 30 and then live for 20 years like this...
We think that each "tour" you play, you have a percentage of chances to change of health state, percentage that grows with your age : the older you get, the more you have chances to suffer from health decreasing. But still, lucky people will be able to challenge probabilities...

Nobody can say if your potion will be worthful. Some have bought potions and have seen their health drop only a few "tours" later, others have bought potions and have last in this state for ages...
